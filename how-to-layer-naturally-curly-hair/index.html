How To Layer Naturally Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Are you tired of your naturally curly hair looking flat and lacking definition? Layering your curls can add dimension and give your hair the bounce and volume it deserves. In this article, we will guide you on how to layer naturally curly hair and provide you with tips to maintain your curls' health.

Table of Contents

  • How to Layer Naturally Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Layering Naturally Curly Hair
  • FAQs

How to Layer Naturally Curly Hair

Layering naturally curly hair involves cutting the hair in a way that adds volume and shape to the curls. It is important to find a stylist who has experience in cutting curly hair to avoid any mishaps.

1. Determine the Length of Your Layers

The length of your layers depends on the length of your hair and the desired outcome. For shorter hair, the layers should start at the crown of your head, while for longer hair, the layers can start below the chin. It is recommended to start with longer layers and gradually work your way up to shorter ones to avoid cutting too much hair.

2. Decide on the Type of Layers

There are various types of layers that can be added to naturally curly hair, including:

  • Long layers: These add volume and shape to the hair while keeping the length intact.
  • Short layers: These create a more defined look and add bounce to the curls.
  • Face-framing layers: These layers start at the chin and frame the face, giving a more polished look.

3. Consider the Curl Pattern

The curl pattern is an important factor to consider when layering naturally curly hair. If you have tighter curls, shorter layers may result in a triangular shape. On the other hand, longer layers may weigh down looser curls. It is recommended to consult with a stylist to determine the best layering technique for your hair type.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to layer naturally curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair, and let it air dry.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Part your hair in the middle or on the side, depending on your preference.
  4. Start with longer layers and gradually work your way up to shorter ones.
  5. Cut the hair at an angle, instead of straight across, to ensure a natural look.
  6. Trim any split ends.
  7. Apply styling products to enhance your curls.
  8. Scrunch your hair with a towel or diffuser to bring out the curls.
  9. Avoid touching your hair too much to prevent frizz.
  10. Enjoy your bouncy, voluminous curls!

Tips for Layering Naturally Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering naturally curly hair:

  • Find a stylist who has experience in cutting curly hair.
  • Do not cut too much hair at once.
  • Start with longer layers and gradually work your way up to shorter ones.
  • Consider the curl pattern when deciding on the type of layers.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the curls.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Apply styling products to enhance your curls.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much to prevent frizz.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your layers.

FAQs

1. Can I layer my naturally curly hair at home?

We recommend that you visit a stylist who has experience in cutting curly hair to avoid any mishaps. However, if you choose to layer your hair at home, make sure to start with longer layers and gradually work your way up to shorter ones. Cut the hair at an angle, instead of straight across, to ensure a natural look.

2. How often should I get my hair trimmed?

It is recommended to get your hair trimmed every 8-12 weeks to maintain the shape of your layers and prevent split ends.

3. What styling products should I use for naturally curly hair?

Styling products that enhance curls include curl defining creams, gels, mousses, and leave-in conditioners. Choose products that work best for your hair type and apply them to damp hair.

Hair Care for Naturally Curly Hair

Maintaining the health of your naturally curly hair is important to prevent breakage and maintain the shape of your layers. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can strip away natural oils.
  • Use a microfiber towel or t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel to prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the curls.
  • Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as they can weigh down your curls.

Layering your naturally curly hair can give it the bounce and volume it deserves. Follow our guide on how to layer naturally curly hair, and use our tips to maintain your curls' health. With the right care and maintenance, your curls will be the envy of everyone!
