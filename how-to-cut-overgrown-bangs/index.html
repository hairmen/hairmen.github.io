How To Cut Overgrown Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have overgrown bangs, you might be wondering how to cut them yourself at home. Don't worry, it's easier than you think! In this article, we'll guide you through the process step-by-step, and provide some tips and tricks to make sure you get the perfect cut every time.

How to Cut Overgrown Bangs

If you're ready to take matters into your own hands and get rid of your overgrown bangs, here's what you need to do:

Step 1: Start with Dry Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is dry. Wet hair can be misleading and make it difficult to get an accurate cut. Blow-dry your hair or let it air dry before you start.

Step 2: Section Your Bangs

Section off your bangs by separating them from the rest of your hair. Use a comb to create a straight part in the middle of your forehead, then comb your bangs forward and clip them in place.

Step 3: Determine the Length

Decide how much length you want to take off. It's best to start with a small amount and work your way up, so you don't accidentally cut too much. Use your fingers to measure how much you want to cut, or use a ruler for a more precise measurement.

Step 4: Cut Your Bangs

Using sharp hair cutting scissors, start cutting your bangs. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once. Take your time and make small cuts until you reach the desired length.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Release your bangs from the clip and check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments by trimming any uneven pieces. Remember, it's always better to cut too little than too much.

Step 6: Style Your Bangs

Once you're happy with the length, style your bangs as desired. Use a round brush to blow-dry them straight, or use a flat iron for a sleek look. You can also add texture with a curling iron or use a hair straightener to create waves.

Tips for Cutting Overgrown Bangs

Cutting your own bangs can be intimidating, but these tips will help you get the perfect cut:

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to get a clean cut.
  • Start with small cuts. It's always better to cut too little than too much.
  • Don't cut straight across. Instead, cut at an angle to create a softer, more natural look.
  • Trim the ends of your bangs with thinning scissors to create texture.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so don't be afraid to take a break and come back to it later.

FAQs

How often should I cut my bangs?

It's best to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair?

Absolutely! Just make sure to cut them when they're dry and style them as usual.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Overgrown Bangs

Once you've cut your overgrown bangs, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a nourishing hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends from traveling up the hair shaft.

With these tips and tricks, you can confidently cut your overgrown bangs at home and keep your hair looking its best. Good luck!
