What To Do About Extreme Hair Loss A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 01, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're experiencing extreme hair loss, you're not alone. Hair loss affects millions of people every year, and it can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, stress, a poor diet, and certain medical conditions. In this guide, we'll take a look at what you can do to help prevent and treat extreme hair loss.

How to Prevent Extreme Hair Loss

Preventing extreme hair loss starts with taking care of your hair and scalp. Here are a few tips:

Eat a Healthy Diet

Your hair needs essential vitamins and minerals to grow and stay healthy. Make sure you're eating a well-balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as tight braids or ponytails, can cause hair loss over time. Try to wear your hair in loose styles that don't pull on your hair.

Protect Your Hair from Heat Damage

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can damage your hair and cause it to break and fall out. If you must use heat styling tools, make sure you're using a heat protectant spray and don't use the highest heat setting.

How to Treat Extreme Hair Loss

If you're already experiencing extreme hair loss, there are a few things you can do to help treat it:

Use Hair Growth Products

There are many hair growth products on the market, such as shampoos, conditioners, and serums, that can help stimulate hair growth and prevent further hair loss. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and caffeine.

Consider Hair Transplant Surgery

If you're experiencing extreme hair loss and nothing else seems to be working, you may want to consider hair transplant surgery. This is a surgical procedure where hair follicles are taken from one part of your scalp and transplanted to another part where hair is thinning or balding.

Try Scalp Micropigmentation

Scalp micropigmentation is a non-surgical procedure where tiny dots of pigment are tattooed onto your scalp to create the appearance of hair. This can be a good option if you're not a candidate for hair transplant surgery or if you're looking for a less invasive option.

FAQ About Extreme Hair Loss

What Causes Extreme Hair Loss?

Extreme hair loss can be caused by genetics, stress, a poor diet, certain medical conditions, and hormonal changes.

Is Extreme Hair Loss Permanent?

Extreme hair loss can be permanent, but it depends on the underlying cause of the hair loss. With proper treatment, some hair loss can be reversed.

Can Hair Loss Be Reversed?

Some hair loss can be reversed with proper treatment, such as using hair growth products, getting scalp micropigmentation, or undergoing hair transplant surgery.

Hair Care Tips to Help Prevent Hair Loss

Here are a few additional tips to help prevent hair loss:

Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle, which can lead to hair loss. Try to wash your hair no more than every other day.

Avoid Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments, such as perms and relaxers, can damage your hair and cause it to break and fall out. If you must use chemical treatments, make sure you're going to a professional stylist who knows how to properly apply them.

Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause it to break and fall out. Use a wide-toothed comb to gently detangle your hair.

By following these tips, you can help prevent and treat extreme hair loss. Remember, if you're experiencing extreme hair loss, it's important to talk to your doctor or a hair loss specialist to determine the underlying cause and the best course of treatment for you.
