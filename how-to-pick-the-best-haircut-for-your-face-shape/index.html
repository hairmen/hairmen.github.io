How To Pick The Best Haircut For Your Face Shape

Are you tired of getting haircuts that don't quite suit you? It might be because you haven't considered your face shape when picking a new hairstyle. By choosing a cut that complements your face shape, you can highlight your best features and enhance your overall look. In this article, we'll go over how to pick the best haircut for your face shape, step by step.

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding your perfect haircut is determining your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and examine your face in the mirror.

If your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width, you have an oval face shape. If your face is as wide as it is long, you have a round face shape. If your face is as long as it is wide and your jawline is square, you have a square face shape. If your face is widest at the temples and tapers down to a narrow chin, you have a heart-shaped face. If your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at the forehead and jawline, you have a diamond face shape. If your face is longer than it is wide and your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width, you have an oblong face shape.

Step 2: Explore Haircuts for Your Face Shape

Once you've determined your face shape, it's time to start exploring haircuts that will flatter your features. Here are some general guidelines for each face shape:

  • Oval: Oval faces can pull off most hairstyles, but look particularly good with long layers, side-swept bangs, and chin-length bobs.
  • Round: Round faces benefit from haircuts that add height and length, like layered pixie cuts, long waves, and asymmetrical bobs.
  • Square: Square faces look best with haircuts that soften the jawline, like long layers, side-swept bangs, and textured pixie cuts.
  • Heart: Heart-shaped faces look great with haircuts that balance the face shape, like long layers, side-swept bangs, and chin-length bobs.
  • Diamond: Diamond faces shine with haircuts that add width and volume to the forehead and chin, like side-swept bangs, layered bobs, and pixie cuts with volume on top.
  • Oblong: Oblong faces benefit from haircuts that add width and volume to the sides of the face, like blunt bobs, wavy shoulder-length cuts, and full bangs.

Step 3: Consider Your Hair Type and Lifestyle

Once you've found a few haircuts that suit your face shape, it's important to consider your hair type and lifestyle. If you have thick, curly hair, a blunt bob might not be the best choice for you. If you're constantly on the go and don't have time to style your hair every day, a high-maintenance cut might not be practical.

Talk to your stylist about your hair type and lifestyle, and ask for their recommendations. They can help you find a haircut that not only flatters your face shape, but also works with your hair texture and daily routine.

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing a new haircut:

  • Bring pictures to your stylist to show them exactly what you're looking for.
  • Consider your hairline and any cowlicks when choosing a haircut.
  • Think about how much time you're willing to spend styling your hair each day.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your New Haircut

Once you've found the perfect haircut, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are appropriate for your hair type.
  • Invest in quality styling products that will help you achieve the look you want.
  • Get regular trims to keep your haircut looking fresh.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine with a hat or swim cap.
