How To Cut Medium Shaggy Layers Yourself

Are you tired of paying a fortune to get your hair cut at the salon? Do you want to save money and try a new hairstyle at the same time? Then, why not try cutting your hair into medium shaggy layers yourself? It's easy, fun, and doesn't require any special skills. So, let's get started!

How to Cut Medium Shaggy Layers Yourself

Before we start, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also helpful to have clips to section off your hair.

Medium shaggy layers are a versatile hairstyle that can be adapted to any face shape or hair type. The layers frame the face and add volume and movement to the hair. To achieve this look, follow the step-by-step guide below.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair and ensure that you don't cut too much.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into three sections: top, middle, and bottom. Clip up the top section and leave the middle and bottom sections loose.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layers

Start with the bottom section and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and trim the ends. Make sure to cut at an angle to create a soft, layered look. Repeat this process with the middle section.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layers

Unclip the top section and comb it forward. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle to create shorter layers around the face. Repeat this process on both sides of the head.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Blend the layers by combing through the hair and cutting any uneven pieces. Make sure to cut at an angle to create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 6: Check the Length

When you're done, check the length of your hair. If it's too long, trim the ends until you achieve the desired length. If it's too short, wait a few weeks before trying again.

Tips for Cutting Medium Shaggy Layers

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a comb to guide your scissors and create a straight line.
  • Don't cut too much at once, as you can always go back and trim more if needed.
  • For a more dramatic look, cut shorter layers around the face.
  • For a softer look, cut longer layers that blend into each other.

FAQs

How often should I cut my medium shaggy layers?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style of your layers.

Can I cut my hair when it's wet?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may cause you to cut off too much.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

It's best to use hair-cutting scissors, as they are sharper and will give you a cleaner cut.

Hair Care Tips for Shaggy Layers

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Trim your layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style of your hair.

Now that you know how to cut medium shaggy layers yourself, it's time to give it a try! Remember, practice makes perfect, so don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and techniques. Good luck!
