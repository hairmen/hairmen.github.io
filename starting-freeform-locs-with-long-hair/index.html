Starting Freeform Locs With Long Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Aug 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone with long hair who wants to start freeform locs, you're in the right place. This comprehensive guide will take you through everything you need to know about starting freeform locs with long hair. From how to begin to frequently asked questions, we've got you covered. So let's dive in!

Description

Freeform locs are a popular hairstyle that is low-maintenance and versatile. Freeform locs are created by letting your hair naturally mat and lock over time, without the use of products or styling tools. This style is perfect for those who want a carefree and unique look. Starting freeform locs with long hair can be a bit intimidating, but it's definitely doable.

How to Start Freeform Locs with Long Hair

The first step to starting freeform locs with long hair is to stop using any chemical treatments or heat styling on your hair. Chemical treatments like relaxers or perms can weaken your hair and prevent it from locking naturally. Heat styling can also damage your hair and make it difficult for it to lock. Give your hair a break and let it be in its natural state.

Next, it's important to keep your hair clean and moisturized. Clean hair helps with the locking process, and moisture helps prevent breakage. Use a gentle shampoo to clean your hair, and follow up with a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized.

Once your hair is clean and moisturized, it's time to let it do its thing. Avoid combing or brushing your hair, as this can prevent it from locking. Instead, let your hair tangle and mat on its own. You can separate any large knots or mats with your fingers, but avoid pulling your hair apart too much.

Step-by-Step Guide to Starting Freeform Locs with Long Hair

1. Stop using chemical treatments and heat styling on your hair.

2. Clean your hair with a gentle shampoo.

3. Moisturize your hair with a leave-in conditioner or oil.

4. Let your hair tangle and mat on its own.

5. Separate any large knots or mats with your fingers, but avoid pulling your hair apart too much.

Tips for Starting Freeform Locs with Long Hair

- Be patient. Freeform locs take time to form, so don't get discouraged if you don't see progress right away.

- Avoid using any products that contain wax or heavy oils, as these can prevent your hair from locking.

- Wear a protective style like braids or twists to help your hair lock faster.

- Drink plenty of water and eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth.

- Embrace the process and enjoy the journey!

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to start freeform locs with long hair?

A: The time it takes to start freeform locs with long hair varies from person to person. It can take anywhere from a few months to a year or more for your hair to start locking. Be patient and enjoy the journey!

Q: Do I need to cut my hair to start freeform locs?

A: No, you don't need to cut your hair to start freeform locs. You can start the process with any length of hair.

Q: Can I wash my hair while starting freeform locs?

A: Yes, it's important to keep your hair clean while starting freeform locs. Use a gentle shampoo to clean your hair, and follow up with a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized.

Hair Care for Freeform Locs with Long Hair

Once your freeform locs are established, it's important to take care of them to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

- Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.

- Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized.

- Avoid using heavy styling products that can build up in your hair and prevent it from locking.

- Wear a satin or silk scarf or bonnet at night to protect your hair.

- Be gentle when separating your locs to prevent breakage.

Conclusion

Starting freeform locs with long hair is a fun and unique journey. With patience and proper care, you can achieve a beautiful and low-maintenance hairstyle. Remember to embrace the process and enjoy the journey!
