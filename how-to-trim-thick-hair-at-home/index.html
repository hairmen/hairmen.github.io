How To Trim Thick Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Nov 20, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to trim thick hair at home! If you're tired of spending money at the salon or just want to take matters into your own hands, we've got you covered. We'll go over everything from how to prepare your hair for trimming to the best tools to use, and we'll even answer some frequently asked questions. So grab your shears and let's get started!

How to Prepare Your Hair for Trimming

Before you start trimming, it's important to prepare your hair so that it's in its best possible condition. Here are a few steps you should take:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will remove any dirt or product buildup and make your hair easier to work with.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Once you've washed your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle it. This will help prevent any snags or tangles while you're trimming.

Step 3: Let Your Hair Dry

It's best to trim your hair when it's dry, so let it air-dry completely before you start. If you're in a hurry, you can use a blow dryer on a low or cool setting to speed up the process.

Tools You'll Need for Trimming Thick Hair

Now that your hair is ready, it's time to gather your tools. Here are the essentials:

Shears

The most important tool for trimming your hair is a good pair of shears. Look for ones that are specifically designed for cutting hair and have sharp, straight blades.

Comb

You'll also need a comb to help you section off your hair and make sure it's straight while you're cutting. A wide-tooth comb is best for thick hair.

Clips

Clips can be useful for holding sections of hair out of the way while you're trimming. Look for ones with a strong grip that won't slip or slide.

Tips for Trimming Thick Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you start trimming:

Start Small

It's always better to start with a small amount of hair and trim more later if you need to. This will help you avoid any major mistakes.

Cut in Sections

Rather than trying to cut all your hair at once, divide it into sections and work on one section at a time. This will make it easier to control and ensure that everything is even.

Cut at an Angle

To create layers or texture, you can cut your hair at an angle rather than straight across. This will give you a more natural look.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Trim My Own Hair if It's Really Thick?

Absolutely! With the right tools and preparation, anyone can trim their own hair.

How Often Should I Trim My Hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how damaged it is. As a general rule, you should trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy.

Should I Trim My Hair While It's Wet or Dry?

It's best to trim your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to work with and may shrink up as it dries.

Hair Care After Trimming

Once you've finished trimming your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

Use a Hair Mask

After trimming, your hair may be a bit dry or damaged. Use a hair mask once a week to help restore moisture and shine.

Avoid Heat Styling

To prevent further damage, try to avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons.

Get Regular Trims

To keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, make sure to get regular trims every 6-8 weeks.

And there you have it – our complete guide on how to trim thick hair at home! With a little practice and patience, you'll be able to achieve salon-worthy results in the comfort of your own bathroom.
