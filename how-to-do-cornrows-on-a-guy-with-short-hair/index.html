How To Do Cornrows On A Guy With Short Hair

Written by Oliver Nov 16, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and low-maintenance hairstyle, cornrows might be the perfect choice for you. Although they're traditionally seen on women, more and more men are embracing the trend. If you have short hair, you might be wondering if cornrows are even possible. The good news is that they are! In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to do cornrows on a guy with short hair.

Description

Cornrows are a type of braid where the hair is braided tightly against the scalp in narrow, straight rows. They can be done in a variety of patterns and styles, and are a popular choice for people who want a low-maintenance, long-lasting hairstyle. Cornrows can be done on hair of any length, but they're especially popular on short hair because they create a neat and tidy look.

How To

Before you start braiding, make sure your hair is clean and free of tangles. You'll also need a comb, hair ties, and possibly some hair gel or pomade to help keep the braids in place. Here's how to do cornrows on a guy with short hair:

Step 1: Section your hair

Using a comb, divide your hair into sections. The size of the sections will depend on how wide you want your cornrows to be. The smaller the sections, the more intricate the pattern will be. Use hair ties to secure the sections you're not working on.

Step 2: Start braiding

Working on one section at a time, take a small piece of hair from the front and divide it into three sections. Cross the right section over the middle section, then cross the left section over the middle section. As you cross each section over, add a small amount of hair from the section next to it. Continue braiding until you reach the end of the section.

Step 3: Repeat on the other sections

Once you've finished the first section, move on to the next one and repeat the process. Continue braiding each section until you've completed your desired pattern. You can braid all the way down to the ends of your hair, or you can stop the braids halfway and leave the rest of your hair loose.

Step 4: Secure the braids

When you've finished braiding, use a hair tie to secure the end of each braid. You can also use some hair gel or pomade to help smooth down any flyaways or stray hairs.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when doing cornrows on a guy with short hair:

- Start with clean, detangled hair to make the braiding process easier.

- Use a comb to create even sections and keep the braids neat.

- If you have trouble braiding your own hair, you can ask a friend or family member to help.

- Don't braid too tightly or you could risk damaging your hair or scalp.

- To keep your cornrows looking fresh, wrap your head with a satin scarf or bonnet at night to prevent frizz.

FAQ

Q: How long do cornrows last?

A: Cornrows can last anywhere from a week to a month, depending on how well you take care of them.

Q: Can I wash my hair with cornrows?

A: Yes, you can wash your hair with cornrows, but be gentle and use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy while wearing cornrows, make sure to moisturize your scalp and hair regularly. You can use a lightweight oil, like coconut or jojoba, to keep your scalp from getting too dry. Avoid using heavy products that can clog your pores or weigh down your hair. If you start to experience any discomfort or itching, take out your cornrows and give your hair and scalp a break.

With these tips and techniques, you can rock a stylish set of cornrows on your short hair. Whether you're going for a simple, classic pattern or an intricate design, cornrows are a versatile and low-maintenance hairstyle that can work for anyone.
