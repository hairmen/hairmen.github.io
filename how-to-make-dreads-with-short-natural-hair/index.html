How To Make Dreads With Short Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 23, 2023 · 4 min read

Dreadlocks have been a popular hairstyle for centuries, and they continue to be a favorite among people with all hair types. Whether you have short natural hair or long relaxed hair, you can achieve the dreadlock look with a little patience and effort. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to make dreads with short natural hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Make Dreads with Short Natural Hair
  3. Step by Step Guide
  4. Tips for Making Dreads with Short Natural Hair
  5. Frequently Asked Questions
  6. Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Description

Dreadlocks, also known as dreads or locks, are a hairstyle that involves the matting and twisting of hair into rope-like strands. The style is popular among people with natural hair, as it allows them to embrace their hair's natural texture and create a unique look. Making dreads with short natural hair can be challenging, but with the right technique, it is possible to achieve the look you desire.

How to Make Dreads with Short Natural Hair

The key to making dreads with short natural hair is to start with clean, dry hair. You will also need a few essential tools, including a comb, hair clips, and dread wax or gel. Here are the steps to follow:

Step by Step Guide

  1. Wash and dry your hair thoroughly. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oil from your scalp.
  2. Section your hair into small parts using a comb. Clip each section to keep it separate.
  3. Apply a small amount of dread wax or gel to the first section of hair. Rub it between your palms to warm it up before applying it to your hair.
  4. Starting at the root, twist the hair in a clockwise direction. Keep twisting until you reach the end of the section. Repeat this process for each section of hair.
  5. After twisting each section, use a hair clip to hold the dread in place. This will help it set and prevent it from unraveling.
  6. Leave the hair clips in place for at least two days to allow the dreads to set.
  7. After two days, remove the hair clips and gently separate the dreads using your fingers. Be careful not to pull too hard, as this can damage your hair.
  8. Use a crochet hook to tighten any loose spots in the dreads. Insert the hook at the base of the dread and pull it through to the end.
  9. Repeat this process for each dread, taking care not to damage your hair.
  10. Once you have tightened all the dreads, apply a small amount of dread wax or gel to your scalp to prevent itching and flaking.

Tips for Making Dreads with Short Natural Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you make dreads with short natural hair:

  • Be patient. It can take several months for your dreads to fully form, so don't be discouraged if they don't look perfect right away.
  • Avoid using too much product. Overusing dread wax or gel can cause buildup and prevent your dreads from matting properly.
  • Avoid using a hairdryer. Heat can cause damage to your hair, so it's best to let your dreads air dry.
  • Keep your scalp clean. Use a tea tree oil shampoo to prevent dandruff and other scalp issues.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some commonly asked questions about making dreads with short natural hair:

  • Can I make dreads with relaxed hair? Yes, you can make dreads with relaxed hair, but it may take longer for them to form.
  • How long does it take for dreads to form? It can take several months for dreads to fully form, but this varies depending on hair type and length.
  • How often should I wash my dreads? Wash your dreads once every two weeks to prevent buildup and keep your scalp clean.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Here are some tips for taking care of your dreads:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your dreads.
  • Use a crochet hook to remove any lint or debris that gets trapped in your dreads.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your dreads looking neat.

With these tips and techniques, you can make dreads with short natural hair and enjoy a unique and stylish look. Remember to be patient, take care of your hair, and have fun with your new style!
