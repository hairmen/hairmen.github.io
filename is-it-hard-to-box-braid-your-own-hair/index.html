Is It Hard To Box Braid Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Nov 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Box braids have been around for centuries and have become increasingly popular among women of all ages. Not only do they look fabulous, but they also provide a protective style for natural hair. However, one question that comes up often is, "Is it hard to box braid your own hair?" The answer is, it depends. With the right technique and some patience, you can successfully box braid your own hair. Here's how:

How to Box Braid Your Own Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start braiding your hair, make sure you have all the necessary supplies. You'll need:

  • Comb
  • Hair Clips
  • Rat Tail Comb
  • Small Rubber Bands
  • Braiding Hair
  • Scissors

Once you have everything you need, you're ready to start.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Dry your hair thoroughly and detangle it with a wide-tooth comb. Divide your hair into small sections using hair clips. Use the rat tail comb to part your hair into neat sections.

Step 3: Add Braiding Hair

Take a small section of braiding hair and fold it in half. Place the folded end of the braiding hair under the section of your hair, so the two ends of the braiding hair are on top of your hair. Take the braiding hair and your hair and start braiding it. Braid the hair until you reach the end of your hair. Secure the ends with a small rubber band.

Step 4: Start Braiding

Take three sections of hair, including the braiding hair, and start braiding. Make sure to braid tightly and evenly to prevent your braids from unraveling. As you braid, add small sections of hair to the braiding hair to make the braids bigger. Continue braiding until you reach the end of your hair.

Step 5: Secure the Ends

Once you've finished braiding, secure the ends with a small rubber band. Cut any excess braiding hair with scissors. Repeat this process until you've braided your entire head.

Tips for Box Braiding Your Own Hair

Box braiding your own hair can be challenging, but these tips can help:

  • Take breaks if you get tired. Box braiding can be time-consuming, so don't be afraid to take a break and come back to it later.
  • Use a detangling spray to make it easier to comb through your hair.
  • Braid your hair in sections to make it more manageable.
  • Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start braiding.
  • Don't braid too tightly, as it can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

FAQs

How long does it take to box braid your own hair?

The time it takes to box braid your own hair depends on the length and thickness of your hair. On average, it can take anywhere from 6 to 12 hours to complete.

How long do box braids last?

Box braids can last anywhere from 4 to 8 weeks, depending on how well you take care of them.

How do I take care of my box braids?

To take care of your box braids, make sure to:

  • Moisturize your scalp regularly.
  • Cleanse your scalp with a gentle shampoo.
  • Wear a satin scarf or bonnet when you sleep.
  • Avoid using heavy oils and products that can weigh down your braids.

Hair Care

Box braids are a great way to protect your natural hair, but it's important to take care of your hair while it's in braids. Make sure to moisturize your scalp and hair regularly, and avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your braids. When you're ready to take out your braids, be gentle and use a detangling spray to make it easier to comb through your hair. Taking care of your hair while it's in braids will help promote healthy hair growth and prevent damage.
