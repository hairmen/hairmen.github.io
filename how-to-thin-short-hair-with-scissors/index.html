How To Thin Short Hair With Scissors A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of your short hair looking too thick and bulky? Do you want to add some texture and movement to your locks? Thinning your hair with scissors is a great way to achieve a more natural and effortless look. In this article, we will show you how to thin short hair with scissors step by step. Follow our tips and tricks for a flawless and professional haircut.

What is Thinning Hair with Scissors?

Thinning hair with scissors is a technique used by hairstylists to remove excess bulk and weight from the hair. It involves cutting some of the hair strands shorter than others, so they blend seamlessly with the rest of the hair. Thinning is especially useful for people with thick, coarse, or curly hair, as it creates a more manageable and lightweight hairstyle.

How to Thin Short Hair with Scissors: Step by Step

Before you start thinning your hair, make sure you have the right tools and equipment. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and some hair clips. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Dry your hair with a towel or a hairdryer until it is 80% dry. Do not over-dry your hair, as it can cause frizz and damage.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into several sections using hair clips. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up. This will make it easier to thin your hair evenly and prevent cutting too much at once.

Step 3: Comb the Hair

Using a fine-toothed comb, comb through each section of your hair to remove any tangles and knots. This will also help you see the natural fall of your hair and determine which areas need to be thinned.

Step 4: Start Thinning

Take a small section of hair between your fingers and hold it at a 45-degree angle away from your head. Using the scissors, cut into the hair vertically and gently close the blades. Do not cut too much at once, as it can create uneven layers and gaps. Repeat the process on the other sections of your hair, focusing on the areas that need to be thinned the most.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After thinning each section, comb through the hair again to check if it looks even and balanced. If you notice any uneven spots or gaps, go back and trim them with the scissors.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you finish thinning your hair, you can style it as desired. You can use a variety of hair products, such as mousse, wax, or hairspray, to add volume, texture, and hold.

Tips for Thinning Short Hair with Scissors

Thinning your hair with scissors can be tricky, especially if you are doing it for the first time. Here are some tips to help you achieve a smooth and successful haircut:

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid split ends and breakage.
  • Take small sections of hair and cut less than you think you need. You can always go back and cut more later.
  • Do not thin the hair too close to the roots, as it can create a choppy and unnatural look.
  • Thinning is not recommended for people with very fine or thinning hair, as it can make the hair look even thinner.
  • If you are not confident in your hair cutting skills, consider going to a professional hairstylist for a consultation.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions and answers about thinning short hair with scissors:

Is thinning hair with scissors damaging?

No, thinning hair with scissors is not damaging if done correctly. It can actually help reduce the weight and stress on the hair and make it more manageable.

How often should I thin my hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Generally, you can thin your hair every 6-12 weeks, or when it starts to feel too heavy and bulky.

Can I thin my hair with regular scissors?

No, it is not recommended to thin your hair with regular scissors, as they can cause split ends and uneven layers. Use only hair cutting scissors designed for this purpose.

Hair Care Tips for Thinning Short Hair

After thinning your hair with scissors, it is important to take good care of it to maintain its health and beauty. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and make it dry and brittle.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals, such as iron, zinc, and biotin, to promote hair growth and strength.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as they can strip away its natural oils and cause damage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a perfectly thinned hairstyle that looks natural and effortless. Remember to be patient and take your time, and do not hesitate to ask for help if you need it. With some practice and confidence, you can become a pro at thinning short hair with scissors.
