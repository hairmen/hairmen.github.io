How To Bleach Hair At Home Asian

Written by Daniel Jun 20, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you an Asian who wants to achieve a lighter hair color? Bleaching your hair at home is a cost-effective way to achieve your desired look. However, it can also be risky if not done properly. In this article, we will guide you on how to bleach hair at home Asian safely and effectively.

How to

Before starting the bleaching process, you must prepare everything you need. Here are the materials you will need:

  • Bleach powder
  • Developer (20 or 30 volume)
  • Gloves
  • Plastic bowl
  • Applicator brush
  • Aluminum foil
  • Shampoo and conditioner

Once you have everything you need, follow these steps:

Step by Step

  1. Put on gloves to protect your hands.
  2. Mix the bleach powder and developer in a plastic bowl according to the instructions on the package. The mixture should be thick and creamy.
  3. Divide your hair into sections using clips.
  4. Apply the bleach mixture to your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  5. Wrap each section of hair with aluminum foil to prevent the bleach from drying out.
  6. Leave the bleach mixture on your hair for the recommended time on the package. Do not exceed the recommended time to avoid hair damage.
  7. Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water until the water runs clear.
  8. Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner to restore moisture.

Tips

  • Do a strand test before applying the bleach mixture to your entire hair to check for any allergic reactions or hair damage.
  • Use a lower volume developer if you have sensitive scalp or thin hair.
  • Avoid overlapping the bleach mixture on previously bleached hair to prevent breakage.
  • Apply coconut oil or hair mask to your hair to restore moisture after bleaching.

FAQ

Q: Can I bleach my hair at home if it has been previously colored?

A: Yes, but you must wait for at least two weeks after the previous coloring to avoid hair damage.

Q: How often can I bleach my hair?

A: It is recommended to wait for at least six weeks before bleaching your hair again to give it time to recover from the previous bleaching.

Q: Can I bleach my hair if it has been chemically treated?

A: It depends on the type of chemical treatment. Consult a professional hairstylist before bleaching your hair to avoid hair damage.

Hair Care

Bleaching can cause hair damage and dryness. To maintain healthy and shiny hair, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the natural oils from your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as blow dryer and flat iron frequently to prevent further hair damage.
  • Apply hair mask or leave-in conditioner regularly to restore moisture.

Now that you know how to bleach hair at home Asian, you can achieve your desired hair color without spending too much money. Just remember to follow the steps and tips carefully to avoid hair damage. Good luck!
