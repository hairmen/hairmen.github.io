What Is A Fade Haircut In French

Written by Bardi Oct 31, 2023 · 4 min read

A fade haircut is a trendy and stylish hairstyle that has gained popularity in recent years. It involves cutting the hair short at the sides and gradually tapering it towards the top. This creates a fade effect, hence the name. In French, a fade haircut is known as "coupe dégradée." In this article, we will discuss what a fade haircut is in French, how to achieve it, and tips for maintaining it.

How to Get a Fade Haircut in French?

The first step in getting a fade haircut is to decide on the length of your hair. You can choose to keep it short or leave some length on top. Once you have decided on the length, you need to choose the type of fade you want. There are three types of fades: low, mid, and high. The low fade starts at the hairline and gradually tapers down towards the ears. The mid fade starts midway up the sides of the head and the high fade starts at the temples.

Once you have chosen the type of fade, you can start cutting your hair. You can either do it yourself or go to a professional hairstylist. If you are doing it yourself, make sure you have the right tools, including clippers, scissors, and a comb. Start by cutting the hair short at the sides and gradually tapering it towards the top. Use the comb to blend the hair and create a smooth transition between the different lengths.

Once you have finished cutting your hair, you can use a styling product to create the desired look. You can use a pomade, wax, or gel to add texture and hold. Apply the product to your hair and style it according to your preference.

Step by Step Guide to Get a Fade Haircut in French

Here is a step by step guide to getting a fade haircut in French:

Step 1: Decide on the Length of Your Hair

Decide on the length of your hair and how short you want it to be. You can choose to keep it short or leave some length on top.

Step 2: Choose the Type of Fade

Choose the type of fade you want. There are three types of fades: low, mid, and high. The type of fade you choose will depend on your hair type and the look you want to achieve.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Cut your hair short at the sides and gradually taper it towards the top. Use clippers, scissors, and a comb to create a smooth transition between the different lengths.

Step 4: Apply Styling Product

Apply a styling product to your hair to add texture and hold. You can use a pomade, wax, or gel to create the desired look.

Tips for Maintaining a Fade Haircut in French

Here are some tips for maintaining your fade haircut:

  • Visit a stylist regularly to maintain the shape of your haircut.
  • Cleanse and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and shiny.
  • Use a styling product to add texture and hold.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before styling.

FAQ about Fade Haircut in French

Q: How often do I need to get a fade haircut?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your haircut. Generally, it is recommended to get a fade haircut every 2-3 weeks.

Q: Can I do a fade haircut at home?

A: Yes, you can do a fade haircut at home. However, it is recommended to go to a professional hairstylist if you are not confident in your skills.

Q: What styling products can I use for a fade haircut?

A: You can use a pomade, wax, or gel to add texture and hold to your hair.

Hair Care Tips for a Fade Haircut in French

Here are some hair care tips for maintaining a fade haircut:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your haircut.

In conclusion, a fade haircut is a trendy and stylish hairstyle that can be achieved by cutting the hair short at the sides and gradually tapering it towards the top. In French, a fade haircut is known as "coupe dégradée." By following the steps and tips outlined above, you can achieve and maintain a perfect fade haircut.
