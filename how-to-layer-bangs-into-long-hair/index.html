How To Layer Bangs Into Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you thinking of adding some bangs to your long hair? Layered bangs can add a trendy and chic touch to any hairstyle. Not to mention, they can be a great way to frame your face and accentuate your features. However, getting the perfect layered bangs can be a daunting task, especially if you're doing it for the first time. Don't worry, though - we've got you covered. In this article, we'll show you how to layer bangs into long hair in a relaxed, easy-to-follow language.

Description

Layered bangs are a type of fringe that's cut at different lengths to create layers. This style is perfect for those who want a little bit of texture and volume in their hair. Layered bangs can be styled in different ways, depending on your preference. You can go for a messy, tousled look, or a sleek and polished one. Either way, layered bangs can add a touch of glamour to your hairstyle.

How to Layer Bangs into Long Hair

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting your bangs, you need to prepare your hair. Make sure it's clean and dry, and brush it thoroughly. You can apply some hair oil or serum to make it easier to work with. Also, make sure you have all the necessary tools, such as scissors, clips, and a comb.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, you need to section your hair to make it easier to work with. Use a comb to create a straight line from the crown of your head to your temples. Then, clip the rest of your hair away, leaving only the section you want to cut.

Step 3: Determine the Length

Now, it's time to determine the length of your bangs. You can use a ruler or a tape measure to make sure they're even. Keep in mind that layered bangs should be cut at different lengths to create texture and volume. Start with a longer length and gradually work your way up to a shorter one.

Step 4: Cut Your Bangs

Using a pair of sharp scissors, start cutting your bangs. Cut them at an angle, starting from the longer side and working your way up to the shorter one. Make sure you're cutting the hair at an angle and not straight across. This will create a more natural and textured look.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've cut your bangs, check your work in the mirror. Make sure they're even and at the right length. If you need to make any adjustments, do so carefully. Remember, it's always better to cut less hair than too much.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering bangs into long hair:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually work your way up to a shorter one.
  • Cut your bangs at an angle, not straight across.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Check your work in the mirror to make sure your bangs are even.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once - it's always better to take it slow.

FAQ

Q: Can I layer my bangs if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can still layer your bangs if you have curly hair. However, it's important to cut them when your hair is dry and fully stretched, so you can see the natural curl pattern.

Q: How often should I trim my bangs?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look unkempt. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

To keep your layered bangs looking their best, here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage it.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to keep them looking neat and tidy.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you should be able to layer bangs into long hair with ease. Remember to take your time and be patient - after all, practice makes perfect. Good luck!
