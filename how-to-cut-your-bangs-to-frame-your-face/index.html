How To Cut Your Bangs To Frame Your Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Nov 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your boring hairstyle? Do you want to add some glam to your look? Cutting your bangs can give you an instant makeover. However, it can be intimidating to cut your own hair, especially if you've never done it before. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll guide you on how to cut your bangs to frame your face.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Your Bangs to Frame Your Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Bangs
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care after Cutting Your Bangs

Description

Before we get started, let's talk about what it means to frame your face. Framing your face means cutting your bangs in a way that accentuates your facial features. It can make your face appear slimmer, highlight your cheekbones, and draw attention to your eyes.

When cutting your bangs, you'll want to consider your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. You can choose from different types of bangs, such as blunt, side-swept, or wispy. The key is to find a style that complements your face shape and enhances your natural beauty.

How to Cut Your Bangs to Frame Your Face

Now, let's get into the nitty-gritty of cutting your bangs. Here are the steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section off the part of your hair that you want to cut into bangs. You can use a comb to create a triangle shape, with the point at the top of your forehead and the base at your temples.
  3. Use sharp scissors to cut your bangs. Make sure to cut slowly and in small sections, so you don't accidentally cut off too much hair.
  4. Keep checking your progress in the mirror. You can use hair clips to hold the rest of your hair back while you cut.
  5. Once you've cut your bangs to the desired length, use a comb to check for any uneven spots. You can trim any stray hairs with scissors.
  6. Style your bangs as desired. You can blow dry them straight, curl them with a curling iron, or leave them natural.

Tips for Cutting Your Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your bangs:

  • Start with longer bangs and gradually cut shorter, so you don't accidentally cut off too much hair.
  • Use sharp scissors, as dull ones can create split ends and make your hair frizzy.
  • Don't cut your bangs when they're wet, as they'll look shorter when they dry.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own bangs, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQ

Now, let's answer some common questions about cutting bangs:

1. How often should you cut your bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your bangs to be. On average, you can trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their length and shape.

2. What if I mess up my bangs?

If you accidentally cut your bangs too short or unevenly, don't panic. You can use hair extensions or hair clips to blend in the bangs with the rest of your hair. Alternatively, you can wear your hair in a ponytail or updo until your bangs grow out.

3. Can I cut my bangs with kitchen scissors?

No, it's not recommended to cut your hair with kitchen scissors. They're not sharp enough and can damage your hair.

Hair Care after Cutting Your Bangs

After cutting your bangs, you'll want to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to prevent split ends.

And there you have it, a complete guide on how to cut your bangs to frame your face. With these tips and tricks, you can give yourself a stylish new look from the comfort of your own home. Happy cutting!
