How To Use Thinner Scissors For Hair Cutting In 2023

Written by Oliver Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you want to add texture to your hair or thin out some of the bulk, then using thinner scissors is an excellent option. However, using thinner scissors can be a bit intimidating, especially if you're new to hair cutting. In this blog post, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to use thinner scissors, as well as tips and advice for maintaining your hair's health.

How to Use Thinner Scissors

Thinner scissors are designed to remove some of the bulk and add texture to your hair. They are also known as texturizing scissors or thinning shears. Thinner scissors have teeth on one blade and a straight edge on the other. The teeth of the scissors remove some of the hair while leaving some behind, creating a more textured look.

When to Use Thinner Scissors

Thinner scissors are great for thinning out thick hair or adding texture to fine hair. They can also be used to blend layers or soften harsh lines. You can use thinner scissors on wet or dry hair, but they work best on dry hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Separate the hair you want to cut from the rest of your hair. Use a hair clip to hold the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 2: Hold the Thinner Scissors Correctly

Hold the thinner scissors with the teeth facing up. The straight edge of the scissors should be facing down. Make sure your fingers are comfortable and secure in the scissors' holes.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Hold a section of hair between your fingers and cut into the hair with the thinner scissors. Start at the ends of the hair and work your way up. Avoid cutting too much hair at once. It's better to make multiple small cuts than one big one.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

After you've cut the hair, blend it by using your fingers to comb through it. This will help to remove any blunt lines and create a more natural look.

Tips for Using Thinner Scissors

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinner scissors:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up.
  • Use the thinner scissors to blend layers and soften harsh lines.
  • Thinner scissors work best on dry hair.
  • Comb through the hair after cutting to blend and remove any blunt lines.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to make multiple small cuts than one big one.

FAQs

Q: Can thinner scissors be used on all hair types?

A: Thinner scissors can be used on all hair types, but they work best on thick and coarse hair.

Q: How often should I use thinner scissors?

A: Thinner scissors can be used as often as needed to maintain your desired hair style. However, it's important to avoid cutting too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven and choppy results.

Hair Care After Using Thinner Scissors

After using thinner scissors, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and texture. Here are some tips:

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  • Get regular haircuts to maintain your desired style and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Using thinner scissors can be a great way to add texture and dimension to your hair. By following these tips and guidelines, you can achieve a professional-looking cut at home while maintaining the health of your hair.
