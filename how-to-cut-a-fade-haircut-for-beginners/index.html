How To Cut A Fade Haircut For Beginners

Written by Daniel Jun 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a beginner looking to learn how to cut a fade haircut, you've come to the right place. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to create a clean and stylish fade haircut at home. Whether you're cutting your own hair or someone else's, we've got you covered. Let's get started with the basics.

Description

A fade haircut is a popular hairstyle that involves gradually tapering the hair from short to long. It's a versatile style that can be worn by anyone, and it's easy to maintain. Fades can be high, medium, or low, depending on how high up the hairline the fade begins. The key to a good fade is to blend the hair seamlessly, so there are no harsh lines or abrupt transitions.

How to

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools: clippers, a comb, scissors, and a spray bottle. It's also important to have a clear idea of what kind of fade you want to create. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Wash and dry the hair

Start by washing the hair and towel-drying it. Wet hair is easier to cut, but you don't want it to be dripping wet. Use a spray bottle to dampen the hair if necessary.

Step 2: Create a guideline

Use the clippers to create a guideline around the head, about an inch above the hairline. This will be the shortest part of the fade. Use a comb to lift the hair and make sure the guideline is straight all the way around.

Step 3: Begin the fade

Start with the longest guard on the clippers (usually #8 or #10) and begin cutting the hair above the guideline. Use an upward motion and move the clippers in a circular motion around the head, gradually lowering the guard size as you go. Make sure to blend the hair as you go, using the comb to lift the hair and create a seamless transition.

Step 4: Create the taper

Once you've reached the desired length, switch to a smaller guard size (#4 or #6) and begin cutting the hair below the guideline. Use the same upward and circular motion, but focus on blending the hair even more. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs and create a natural-looking taper.

Step 5: Clean up the edges

Use the clippers without a guard to create a clean edge around the hairline and the back of the neck. Be careful not to cut too high up the hairline, or you'll ruin the fade.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results:

  • Use a mirror to check your work from all angles.
  • Take your time and don't rush.
  • Practice on a mannequin head or a willing friend before trying the cut on yourself.
  • Invest in high-quality clippers and scissors for the best results.
  • Watch tutorials online to learn different techniques and styles.

FAQ

Can I cut a fade haircut by myself?

Yes, you can cut a fade haircut by yourself, but it's recommended to have someone else help you with the back and sides.

What's the best guard size to use for a fade?

The best guard size to use for a fade depends on how high you want the fade to start and how short you want the hair to be. Generally, you'll want to start with a longer guard size and work your way down to a shorter one.

How often should I get a fade haircut?

How often you should get a fade haircut depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain the style. On average, most people get a fade every 2-4 weeks.

Hair Care

To maintain your fade haircut, you'll want to wash and condition your hair regularly and use styling products to keep it looking sharp. Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage your hair. Regular trims will also help keep your fade looking fresh.

Now that you know how to cut a fade haircut for beginners, it's time to give it a try. With practice and patience, you'll soon be creating stylish fades like a pro.
