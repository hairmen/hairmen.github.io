How To Sleep With Curls Without Messing Them Up

Written by Oliver Nov 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Sleeping with curly hair can be a struggle. You spend hours perfecting your curls only to wake up with a frizzy mess. But don't worry, there are ways to sleep with curls without messing them up. In this article, we'll give you some tips and tricks to keep your curls looking great all night long.

Table of Contents

  • How to Sleep with Curls
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Sleep with Curls

The first step to sleeping with curls without messing them up is to avoid sleeping on them. This can be easier said than done, especially if you're a restless sleeper. But there are a few ways to avoid crushing your curls while you sleep:

1. Use a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

Silk and satin pillowcases are great for curly hair because they create less friction than cotton pillowcases. This means your curls are less likely to get tangled and frizzy while you sleep. Plus, silk and satin pillowcases are gentler on your skin, too!

2. Pineapple Your Hair

Pineappling is a technique where you loosely gather your curls at the top of your head, like a pineapple. This keeps your curls from getting flattened while you sleep. To pineapple your hair, follow these steps:

  1. Flip your head over and gather your curls at the top of your head.
  2. Secure your hair with a soft scrunchie or hair tie.
  3. Flip your head back up and adjust any loose curls.

3. Use a Buff or Bonnet

A buff or bonnet is a great way to protect your curls while you sleep. You can wear it over your pineapple, or just on its own. Buffs and bonnets are made from soft, stretchy material that won't tug on your curls or leave marks on your skin.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're new to sleeping with curls, it can be helpful to have a step-by-step guide. Here's how to sleep with curls without messing them up:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Apply your styling products, such as mousse, gel, or cream.
  3. Flip your head over and scrunch your curls to encourage definition.
  4. Pineapple your hair, making sure to leave some curls out around your face.
  5. Put on a buff or bonnet to protect your curls.
  6. Go to sleep!
  7. In the morning, take off your buff or bonnet and shake out your curls.
  8. If needed, apply a little more styling product to refresh your curls.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you sleep with curls without messing them up:

1. Refresh Your Curls in the Morning

If your curls are looking a little flat in the morning, don't worry. You can refresh them with a little water and styling product. Just spray your curls with water, scrunch them, and apply a little mousse or gel.

2. Use a Silk or Satin Scarf

If you don't want to wear a buff or bonnet, you can wrap your hair in a silk or satin scarf instead. This will protect your curls from rubbing against your pillowcase.

3. Don't Touch Your Hair Too Much

While you're sleeping, try not to touch your hair too much. This can cause frizz and disrupt your curls. If you need to adjust your hair, do it gently.

FAQs

1. How can I keep my curls from flattening while I sleep?

The best way to keep your curls from flattening while you sleep is to pineapple your hair and wear a buff or bonnet. You can also try sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase.

2. How often should I wash my curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and how much product you use. Some people with curly hair can go a week or more without washing, while others prefer to wash every other day. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Finally, it's important to take care of your curls to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for curly hair care:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's important to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are sulfate-free and contain ingredients like coconut oil or shea butter.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage curly hair and cause frizz. Try to avoid using hot tools like curling irons and blow dryers. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.

3. Use a Deep Conditioner

Once a week, use a deep conditioner to hydrate and nourish your curls. Leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing out.

With these tips and tricks, you can sleep with curls without messing them up. Remember to take care of your curls and they'll look great all day, every day!
