What Do I Look Like With Bleached Hair

Written by Aprilia Aug 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you thinking of bleaching your hair but are unsure of how it will look on you? Bleached hair is a bold and daring look that can completely transform your appearance. In this blog post, we will explore what you can expect to look like with bleached hair, as well as provide tips and advice for taking care of your newly bleached locks.

Description

Bleached hair is a hair color that has been stripped of its natural pigment using bleach. This process leaves hair looking lighter and more vibrant. Depending on the color of your natural hair, bleached hair can range from a pale blonde to a platinum white. Bleached hair can be a dramatic change from your natural color, so it’s important to consider if it’s the right choice for you.

How to Bleach Your Hair

If you’re interested in bleaching your hair, it’s important to do so safely and correctly. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Gather your supplies

You will need bleach powder, developer, a mixing bowl, a tint brush, gloves, and a plastic cap.

Step 2: Mix the bleach

Follow the instructions on the bleach powder and developer to mix them together in the mixing bowl.

Step 3: Apply the bleach

Wear gloves to protect your hands and use the tint brush to apply the bleach mixture to your hair. Be sure to cover your hair evenly and completely.

Step 4: Cover your hair

Once your hair is covered in bleach, put on the plastic cap to help the bleach process evenly. Leave the bleach on for the recommended amount of time.

Step 5: Rinse and condition

Once the bleach has processed, rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water. Use a deep conditioning treatment to help restore moisture to your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Bleached Hair

Now that you’ve bleached your hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain the color and health of your hair. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Use a purple shampoo

Purple shampoo can help keep your bleached hair looking vibrant and prevent it from turning yellow or brassy.

Tip 2: Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools can damage bleached hair, so it’s best to avoid them as much as possible. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray.

Tip 3: Get regular trims

Bleached hair can become damaged more easily, so it’s important to get regular trims to prevent split ends.

FAQs

Q: Will bleaching my hair damage it?

A: Bleaching your hair can cause damage, but if done correctly and with proper care, you can minimize the damage.

Q: Can I bleach my hair at home?

A: Yes, you can bleach your hair at home, but it’s important to follow the instructions carefully and take proper safety precautions.

Q: How often should I wash my bleached hair?

A: It’s best to wash bleached hair no more than two to three times per week to prevent stripping it of its natural oils.

Hair Care for Bleached Hair

When caring for bleached hair, it’s important to use products specifically designed for colored hair. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are formulated to add moisture and protect hair color. Additionally, use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore moisture to your hair. Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and always use a heat protectant spray if you must use them. Finally, make sure to get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

Overall, bleached hair can be a fun and daring look, but it’s important to consider the potential damage and care for your hair properly. Follow these tips and advice to maintain healthy and vibrant bleached hair.
