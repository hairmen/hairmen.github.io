Does Short Hair Make You Look Fatter Or Skinnier

Written by Eveline Aug 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you thinking about getting a short haircut but worried about how it will affect your appearance? One common concern is whether short hair makes you look fatter or skinnier. In this article, we'll explore this topic and provide some tips on how to choose the right haircut for your face and body shape.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Determine Your Face Shape
  3. Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing the Right Haircut
  4. Tips for Choosing the Right Haircut

Description

Short hair can make you look either fatter or skinnier, depending on your face shape and body type. For example, if you have a round face or a fuller body, certain short hairstyles can make you look wider or heavier. On the other hand, if you have a long face or a slimmer body, some short hairstyles can make you look more proportionate and elongate your features.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before you choose a short haircut, it's important to determine your face shape. This will help you choose a hairstyle that flatters your features and complements your natural beauty. Here are some basic steps to determine your face shape:

  1. Stand in front of a mirror and tie your hair back.
  2. Use a measuring tape or ruler to measure the widest part of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline.
  3. Compare your measurements to the following face shapes:
    • Round: forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width
    • Oval: forehead is wider than the jawline, and the face tapers to a narrow chin
    • Square: forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width, with a strong jawline
    • Heart: forehead is wider than the jawline, and the face tapers to a narrow chin
    • Diamond: cheekbones are the widest part of the face, with a narrow forehead and jawline
    • Rectangle: forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width, with a longer face shape

Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing the Right Haircut

Once you know your face shape, you can use this guide to choose a short haircut that flatters your features:

  1. Round face: opt for a short haircut with layers and volume at the crown to elongate your face. Avoid blunt cuts and heavy bangs that can make your face look wider.
  2. Oval face: you can pull off almost any short haircut, from a pixie to a bob. Consider adding texture or bangs to soften your features.
  3. Square face: choose a short haircut with soft layers and a side-swept fringe to balance your strong jawline. Avoid blunt cuts and center parts that can make your face look boxy.
  4. Heart face: try a short haircut with volume at the crown and layers around the face to balance your narrow chin. Avoid heavy bangs and short hairstyles that cling to the face.
  5. Diamond face: choose a short haircut with volume at the crown and soft layers around the face to balance your wide cheekbones. Avoid short hairstyles that make your face look too angular.
  6. Rectangle face: opt for a short haircut with volume at the sides and a side-swept fringe to add width to your face. Avoid short hairstyles that make your face look too long.

Tips for Choosing the Right Haircut

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing a short haircut:

  • Consider your body type: if you have a fuller body, avoid short hairstyles that make your face look wider. Instead, choose a haircut that balances your features and elongates your neck.
  • Consult with a hairstylist: a professional hair stylist can help you choose a short haircut that flatters your face shape and suits your lifestyle.
  • Experiment with texture: adding texture to your short haircut can make it look more dynamic and suit your face shape.
  • Accessorize with earrings: wearing earrings that complement your face shape can draw attention to your features and balance your overall appearance.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about short hair and its effect on your appearance:

Q: Does short hair make you look older?

A: Short hair can make you look older or more mature, especially if you choose a drastic haircut that doesn't suit your face shape or hair texture. However, a well-chosen short haircut can also make you look more youthful and vibrant.

Q: Does short hair make your face look wider?

A: Short hair can make your face look wider or narrower, depending on the cut and your face shape. If you have a round face or a fuller body, certain short hairstyles can make your face look wider. On the other hand, if you have a long face or a slimmer body, some short hairstyles can make your face look more proportionate and elongate your features.

Q: How often should I trim my short hair?

A: Short hair requires more frequent trims than long hair, as the ends can become damaged and split more easily. Depending on your hair texture and style, you may need to trim your short hair every 4-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health.

Hair Care and Styling Tips

Here are some tips to keep your short hair looking healthy and stylish:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to protect your hair from heat damage and add shine.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before blow-drying or styling your hair with hot tools.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, especially when it's wet.
  • Try different styling products, such as mousse, gel, or wax, to add texture and hold to your hair.

In conclusion, short hair can make you look fatter or skinnier, depending on your face shape and body type. By following these tips and consulting with a hairstylist, you can choose a short haircut that flatters your features and complements your natural beauty.
