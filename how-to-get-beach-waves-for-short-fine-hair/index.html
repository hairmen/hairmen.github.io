How To Get Beach Waves For Short Fine Hair

Written by Petter Aug 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your short fine hair looking flat and lifeless? Do you want to achieve those beautiful beachy waves that everyone seems to have? Well, you're in luck! In this article, we'll show you how to get beach waves for short fine hair. We'll cover everything from how to prep your hair to the best products to use. Let's dive in!

Table of Contents

  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Products to Use
  • The Technique
  • Tips for Success
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Prepping Your Hair

The key to getting beach waves for short fine hair is to start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is dirty or oily, the waves won't hold as well. So, shampoo and condition your hair as usual, then blow dry it straight. If you have any natural wave or curl, use a straightening iron to smooth it out. This will create a clean base for the waves to form.

Products to Use

Next, you'll need to use some products to help create the waves. Here are some of the best products for short fine hair:

  • Sea salt spray - this will give your hair that beachy texture and hold
  • Mousse - use a volumizing mousse to add body to your hair
  • Hairspray - use a flexible hold hairspray to keep the waves in place

The Technique

Now it's time to create the waves! Here's how:

  1. Section your hair - divide your hair into 2-3 inch sections
  2. Spray with sea salt spray - spray each section with sea salt spray, focusing on the mid-lengths to ends
  3. Twist and scrunch - twist each section away from your face, then scrunch it up towards your scalp
  4. Repeat - continue twisting and scrunching each section until you've done your whole head
  5. Hairspray - spray your hair with a flexible hold hairspray to keep the waves in place
  6. Let it set - let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on low heat to dry it
  7. Fluff and go - once your hair is dry, gently fluff it with your fingers to loosen the waves, then you're ready to go!

Tips for Success

Here are some additional tips to help you get the best beach waves for short fine hair:

  • Use a diffuser - this will help add volume and texture to your waves
  • Don't overdo it - too much product or twisting can make your hair look crunchy or stiff
  • Experiment with placement - try twisting and scrunching your hair in different directions for a more natural look

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting beach waves for short fine hair:

How long will the waves last?

The waves should last all day, but if you want them to last longer, use a stronger hold hairspray or refresh them with some sea salt spray throughout the day.

Can I use a curling iron instead of twisting my hair?

Yes, you can use a curling iron to create beachy waves, but it can be more time-consuming and may not look as natural as the twisting and scrunching method.

Will this work on all hair types?

This method is best suited for short fine hair, but it can work on other hair types as well. Just adjust the amount of product and twisting based on your hair's texture and thickness.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a heat protectant - if you use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage
  • Trim your hair regularly - regular trims will help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy
  • Avoid over-washing - washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and make it dry and brittle

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to get beautiful beach waves for your short fine hair in no time! Give it a try and see how it works for you.
