How To Make Bangs Look Good With Curly Hair

Written by Bardi Jun 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you thinking about getting bangs but worried about how they'll look with your curly hair? Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll discuss how to make bangs look good with curly hair and provide tips on how to achieve the perfect style.

Description

Bangs can be a great way to change up your look and add some personality to your hairstyle. However, if you have curly hair, you may be hesitant to try them out. Curly hair can be unpredictable, and bangs can easily turn into a frizzy mess if not styled correctly. But fear not, we have some tips and tricks to help you rock your new bangs with confidence.

How To

The key to making bangs look good with curly hair is to embrace your natural texture and work with it instead of against it. Here's how:

Step 1: Choose the Right Bangs

Not all bangs are created equal, and when it comes to curly hair, some styles work better than others. If you have tight curls, opt for longer, side-swept bangs that blend seamlessly with the rest of your hair. If you have looser curls, you can get away with shorter, blunt bangs that add some edge to your look.

Step 2: Cut Them When Dry

Curly hair can shrink up to 50% when it dries, so it's essential to cut your bangs when they're dry to ensure you get the length you want. Dry cutting also allows your stylist to see how your curls will fall and create a shape that works with your natural texture.

Step 3: Style Them When Wet

When it comes to styling your bangs, it's best to work with them when they're wet. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls and use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry them gently. Avoid brushing or combing your bangs, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your curl pattern.

Step By Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to styling your curly bangs:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products formulated for curly hair. Be sure to detangle your hair gently to avoid causing breakage or frizz.

Step 2: Apply Your Styling Product

Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair, focusing on your bangs. Be sure to distribute the product evenly to ensure your curls are defined and moisturized.

Step 3: Dry Your Hair with a Diffuser

Attach a diffuser to your hairdryer and use it to dry your hair gently. Scrunch your hair with your hands as you dry it to encourage your curls to form.

Step 4: Define Your Bangs

Once your hair is dry, use your fingers to define your curls and shape your bangs. Be gentle and avoid using a brush or comb, as this can cause frizz.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you make your bangs look good with curly hair:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Refresh your curls with a spray bottle of water and a bit of leave-in conditioner throughout the day.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about making bangs look good with curly hair:

Can I straighten my curly bangs?

While it's possible to straighten your bangs with a flat iron, it's not recommended for curly hair. Heat styling can damage your curls and cause breakage, so it's best to work with your natural texture instead.

How often should I cut my bangs?

How often you cut your bangs will depend on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your curls lose their shape. It's best to consult with your stylist to determine the best schedule for you.

What should I do if my bangs look too short?

If your bangs look too short after they've dried, try stretching them gently with your fingers. You can also use a bit of leave-in conditioner to help loosen them up.

Hair Care

Curly hair requires a bit more TLC than straight hair, but with the right products and techniques, it can look amazing. Here are some tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Protect your hair from heat styling by using a heat protectant spray.

With these tips and tricks, you can confidently rock bangs with your curly hair. Remember to embrace your natural texture, work with it, and have fun experimenting with different styles!
