How To Figure Out Your Natural Hair Type A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Understanding your natural hair type is crucial in determining the right hair care regimen that works for you. With so many hair types and textures, it can be overwhelming to identify your hair type. However, with the right information, it can be an easy and straightforward process. In this guide, we will walk you through how to figure out your natural hair type.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Know the Different Hair Types
  • Step 2: Observe Your Hair
  • Step 3: Understand Your Hair's Characteristics
  • Step 4: Determine Your Hair Type
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Type
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Products for Your Hair Type

Step 1: Know the Different Hair Types

Before identifying your hair type, it's essential to know the different types of hair. There are four primary hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Straight hair is naturally smooth and lacks any curls or waves. Wavy hair has a slight s-shaped pattern, while curly hair has a more defined curl pattern. Coily hair, also known as kinky hair, has a tight curl pattern and is often referred to as afro-textured hair.

Step 2: Observe Your Hair

Take a closer look at your hair. Is it naturally straight, wavy, curly, or coily? Look at your hair's shape, texture, and pattern. Is it thick or thin? Does it have a lot of volume or is it limp? These observations can help determine your hair type.

Step 3: Understand Your Hair's Characteristics

Each hair type has its unique characteristics. Straight hair is often shiny, oily, and prone to getting greasy quickly. Wavy hair is relatively easy to style, but it can be prone to frizz. Curly hair is often dry and prone to breakage, and coily hair is fragile and can easily become tangled. Understanding your hair's characteristics can help you identify your hair type.

Step 4: Determine Your Hair Type

Based on your observations and understanding of your hair's characteristics, you can determine your hair type. Here are some guidelines:

  • Straight hair: If your hair is naturally straight, it's likely that you have a type 1 hair type.
  • Wavy hair: If your hair has a slight s-shaped pattern, you likely have a type 2 hair type. Type 2 hair can range from a loose wave to a more defined wave pattern.
  • Curly hair: If your hair has a defined curl pattern, you likely have a type 3 hair type. Type 3 hair can range from loose curls to tight curls.
  • Coily hair: If your hair has a tight curl pattern, you likely have a type 4 hair type. Type 4 hair can range from a tight coil to a zig-zag pattern.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Type

Once you've identified your hair type, it's crucial to maintain it. Here are some tips:

  • Use products specifically designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly.
  • Protect your hair while sleeping with a satin or silk bonnet or pillowcase.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals, including relaxers and dyes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some commonly asked questions about natural hair types:

  • Can your hair type change? Your hair type can change due to genetics, aging, and lifestyle factors.
  • Can you have multiple hair types? Yes, it's possible to have multiple hair types on your head. For example, you may have type 3 hair on the crown of your head and type 4 hair at the nape of your neck.
  • How often should you wash your hair? It depends on your hair type and texture. Generally, it's recommended to wash your hair once a week or every two weeks.

Hair Care Products for Your Hair Type

Here are some recommended hair care products for each hair type:

  • Straight hair: Use a light shampoo and conditioner to prevent weighing down your hair. Try using a volumizing mousse or spray to add volume.
  • Wavy hair: Use a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner to define your waves. Use a lightweight curl cream or gel to keep your waves defined.
  • Curly hair: Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated. Use a leave-in conditioner and curl cream to define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Coily hair: Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. Use a leave-in conditioner and a hair butter to keep your coils defined and moisturized.

Figuring out your natural hair type may seem daunting, but with the right information, it can be a simple and straightforward process. Remember to observe your hair's shape, texture, and pattern and understand your hair's characteristics. Use the tips and recommended hair care products to maintain your hair type and keep your hair healthy and beautiful.
