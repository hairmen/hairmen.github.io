How To Cut Your Own Hair In Layers Step By Step

Written by Frank Jul 18, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, cutting your own hair in layers can give you a fresh, new look. While it may seem intimidating, with the right tools and technique, you can successfully cut your own hair in layers. In this article, we'll go through the steps to cut your own hair in layers step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair in Layers
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

How to Cut Your Own Hair in Layers

Cutting your own hair in layers can be done with just a few tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section your hair. It's important to make sure your hair is clean and dry before starting to cut.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own hair in layers:

  1. Start by sectioning your hair. Divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section out of the way.
  2. Take one section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers at the length you want to cut it. Cut the hair in a straight line.
  3. Release another section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers at the length of the first section. Cut the hair in a straight line.
  4. Continue to release and cut each section of hair in the same length. This will create the base layer of your haircut.
  5. Next, release a small section of hair from the top layer. Comb it straight up and hold it between your fingers at the desired length. Cut the hair in a straight line. This will create the first layer of your haircut.
  6. Continue to release small sections of hair from the top layer and cut them in the desired length. This will create the layers of your haircut.
  7. Repeat steps 5 and 6 on the other side of your head.
  8. Once you've finished cutting all the layers, blend them together by combing the hair and cutting any uneven ends.
  9. Check your hair in the mirror to make sure both sides are even and adjust any areas that need it.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair in layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take breaks if you feel tired or overwhelmed.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head while cutting.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time to ensure a good result.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair in layers:

  • How often should I cut my hair? It's recommended to cut your hair every six to eight weeks to maintain healthy hair.
  • Can I cut my hair wet or dry? It's best to cut your hair when it's dry to ensure accuracy.
  • What if I make a mistake? If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim the hair or go to a professional for help.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair in layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and use a heat protectant spray when styling. Get regular trims every six to eight weeks to maintain your haircut.

Cutting your own hair in layers can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a stylish new look. Follow these step by step instructions and tips for a successful haircut.
