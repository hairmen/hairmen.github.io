What Does The Quran Say About Facial Hair

Written by Robby Jul 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are someone who likes to take care of their facial hair, you might have wondered what Islam's holy book, the Quran, says about it. This article will cover what the Quran says about facial hair, including the reasons behind it, how to take care of it, and some frequently asked questions about the topic.

Table of Contents

  1. Reasons Behind Facial Hair
  2. How to Care for Facial Hair
  3. Types of Facial Hair
  4. Frequently Asked Questions

Reasons Behind Facial Hair

The Quran mentions that facial hair is a natural part of a man's appearance, and it is something that should not be neglected. It is mentioned that men should grow a beard and keep it trimmed, and this is considered to be a sunnah (a practice of the Prophet Muhammad). The Quran also mentions that men should not shave their beards, as this is something that is associated with non-believers.

Facial hair is also seen as a sign of masculinity and maturity, and it is something that is respected in many Muslim cultures. It is believed that by growing a beard, a man is following in the footsteps of the Prophet and is showing his devotion to Islam.

How to Care for Facial Hair

If you want to follow the Quran's teachings and grow a beard, there are a few things you need to keep in mind when it comes to caring for it. First of all, it is important to keep your beard clean and well-groomed. This means washing it regularly with shampoo and conditioner, and using a beard oil or balm to keep it soft and moisturized.

You should also invest in a good quality beard trimmer or scissors, as this will make it easier to keep your beard looking neat and tidy. It is recommended that you trim your beard at least once a week, depending on how quickly it grows.

Step-by-Step Guide to Trimming Your Beard

Here is a step-by-step guide to trimming your beard:

  1. Wash your beard with shampoo and conditioner, and then dry it thoroughly.
  2. Comb your beard in the direction of the hair growth, using a fine-toothed comb.
  3. Decide on the length you want your beard to be, and adjust your trimmer or scissors accordingly.
  4. Start trimming from the bottom of your beard, and work your way up towards your chin.
  5. Use a pair of scissors to trim any stray hairs that your trimmer might have missed.
  6. Use a beard oil or balm to moisturize your beard and keep it looking healthy.

Types of Facial Hair

There are many different types of facial hair that you can grow, depending on your personal style and preferences. Some of the most popular styles include:

  • The full beard: This is a long, bushy beard that covers the entire lower half of your face.
  • The goatee: This is a small beard that only covers the chin and the area immediately surrounding it.
  • The mustache: This is a strip of hair that grows above the upper lip.
  • The soul patch: This is a small patch of hair that grows under the lower lip.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is it mandatory for Muslim men to grow a beard?

A: While it is recommended for Muslim men to grow a beard, it is not mandatory.

Q: Can Muslim men shave their beard?

A: Muslim men are discouraged from shaving their beards, as it is considered to be a practice of non-believers. However, if there is a valid reason for shaving (such as for a medical procedure), then it is permissible.

Q: Can Muslim women have facial hair?

A: Yes, it is natural for women to have facial hair, and there is no religious requirement for them to remove it. However, many women choose to remove their facial hair for personal or cultural reasons.

Q: How long does it take to grow a beard?

A: The length of time it takes to grow a beard varies from person to person, but on average, it takes about two to three months to grow a full beard.

Q: How can I prevent my beard from becoming itchy?

A: Itchy beards are often caused by dry skin, so it is important to keep your beard moisturized with a beard oil or balm. You should also avoid using harsh soaps or shampoos on your beard, as these can strip away the natural oils that keep it soft and healthy.

Overall, facial hair is an important part of Muslim culture, and it is something that is respected and admired by many. By following the Quran's teachings and taking good care of your beard, you can show your devotion to Islam and look great at the same time.
