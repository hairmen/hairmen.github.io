Description

For those with short mixed hair, starting dreadlocks may seem like a daunting task. However, with the right approach and techniques, anyone can start dreads regardless of hair type or length. This article will provide a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with short mixed hair, as well as tips and hair care advice to keep your dreads looking healthy and vibrant.

How to Start Dreads with Short Mixed Hair

The first step in starting dreads with short mixed hair is to ensure that your hair is clean and free of any products or residue. This will help your hair lock and knot more easily, which is essential for creating dreads. Once your hair is clean, you can begin the process of sectioning your hair into small, even sections. You can use clips or rubber bands to help keep the sections in place.

Tools Needed:

  • Comb
  • Clips or rubber bands
  • Dread wax or gel (optional)
  • Patience

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to start dreads with short mixed hair:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into small, even sections. You can use clips or rubber bands to help keep the sections in place. The size of the sections will depend on your hair type and the desired thickness of your dreads.

Step 2: Backcomb Your Hair

Starting at the roots, use a comb to backcomb each section of hair. This involves combing the hair backwards towards the scalp, which will create knots and tangles in the hair. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 3: Twist Your Hair

Once you have backcombed each section of hair, twist each section tightly in the same direction. This will help the hair lock and knot together, which is essential for creating dreads.
