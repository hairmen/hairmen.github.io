How To Cut Long Hair Layers Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 26, 2023 · 3 min read

Long hair layers are a popular hairstyle that adds volume and movement to your hair. If you're looking to give your long hair a fresh look, cutting layers is a great option. However, cutting hair layers can be tricky, especially if you're doing it yourself. In this blog post, we'll show you a step-by-step guide on how to cut long hair layers that you'll love.

How to Cut Long Hair Layers

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and sectioning clips. Here's how to cut long hair layers:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair and let it air dry, or blow dry it straight. This will make it easier to see the natural texture of your hair and where to cut the layers.

Step 2: Part Your Hair

Using a comb, part your hair down the middle. Then, section your hair into four parts: two on the top and two on the bottom. Use sectioning clips to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting with the bottom section, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this process on each section of hair, making sure to cut at an angle to create layers.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the sections, blend the layers by combing through your hair. This will help to create a natural look and prevent any harsh lines.

Step 5: Trim the Ends

Finally, trim the ends of your hair to make sure they're even. This will give your hair a clean and polished look.

Tips for Cutting Long Hair Layers

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect long hair layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting off too much at once.
  • Cut at an angle to create layers that blend naturally.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Comb through your hair frequently to check for any unevenness.

FAQ

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting long hair layers:

How often should I cut my long hair layers?

It's recommended to cut your long hair layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own long hair layers?

Yes, you can cut your own long hair layers, but it's important to be careful and start with small sections of hair. Consider getting a friend or family member to help you with the back of your head.

Hair Care

After cutting your long hair layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often to prevent damage.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to add moisture and nourishment.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut long hair layers, it's time to give it a try. Remember to start with small sections of hair and cut at an angle to create natural-looking layers. With practice and patience, you'll be able to achieve the perfect long hair layers that you'll love.
