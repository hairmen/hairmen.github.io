Should I Do Prp For Hair Loss

Written by Luffy Aug 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you struggling with hair loss and wondering if PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) therapy is worth a try? In this article, we will discuss what PRP is, how it works, and answer some frequently asked questions about PRP for hair loss.

What is PRP?

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy is a non-surgical treatment that uses the patient’s own blood to promote healing and regeneration. Blood is drawn from the patient and spun in a centrifuge to separate the platelets from the red and white blood cells. The concentrated platelets, which contain growth factors, are then injected into the scalp to stimulate hair growth.

How Does PRP Work for Hair Loss?

PRP works by stimulating the hair follicles to enter into the anagen (growth) phase and promoting the growth of new hair. The growth factors in the platelets activate the stem cells in the hair follicle, which then differentiate into hair cells. PRP also improves blood flow to the scalp, which is essential for healthy hair growth.

Who is a Good Candidate for PRP?

PRP is a safe and effective treatment for both men and women who are experiencing hair thinning or hair loss. It is best suited for individuals in the early stages of hair loss, as it may not be as effective for those with advanced hair loss. It is also not recommended for individuals with certain medical conditions, such as blood disorders or autoimmune diseases.

How is PRP Done for Hair Loss?

The PRP procedure for hair loss involves the following steps:

  1. Blood is drawn from the patient.
  2. The blood is spun in a centrifuge to separate the platelets from the red and white blood cells.
  3. The concentrated platelets are then injected into the scalp using a fine needle.

What are the Results of PRP for Hair Loss?

PRP has been shown to improve hair thickness, density, and growth in individuals with hair loss. Results vary from person to person, and multiple treatments may be needed to achieve optimal results.

What are the Risks and Side Effects of PRP?

PRP is a safe and minimally invasive procedure, and side effects are rare. Some patients may experience mild pain or discomfort at the injection site, but this typically resolves within a few days. There is also a small risk of infection or bleeding.

How Many Treatments are Needed?

The number of treatments needed varies depending on the individual’s hair loss and response to treatment. Typically, patients receive 3-4 treatments spaced 4-6 weeks apart, followed by maintenance treatments every 6-12 months.

How to Prepare for PRP?

Prior to the PRP procedure, patients should avoid blood-thinning medications and supplements, such as aspirin and fish oil, for at least a week. They should also eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote optimal healing and recovery.

How to Care for Your Hair After PRP?

After the PRP procedure, patients should avoid washing their hair for at least 24 hours and avoid hair treatments, such as coloring or perming, for at least 48 hours. They should also avoid strenuous exercise and activities that may cause sweating for at least 48 hours to minimize the risk of infection.

FAQ

Is PRP painful?

Most patients experience mild pain or discomfort at the injection site, but this typically resolves within a few days.

How much does PRP for hair loss cost?

PRP for hair loss typically costs between $500 and $2,000 per treatment, depending on the clinic and location.

When will I see results from PRP?

Results from PRP for hair loss may take several weeks to months to become apparent, and multiple treatments may be needed to achieve optimal results.

Is PRP for hair loss covered by insurance?

PRP for hair loss is considered a cosmetic procedure and is typically not covered by insurance.

Is PRP for hair loss effective?

PRP has been shown to be effective in improving hair thickness, density, and growth in individuals with hair loss.

Overall, PRP is a safe and effective treatment option for individuals struggling with hair loss. If you are considering PRP for hair loss, it is important to consult with a qualified healthcare provider to determine if it is the right treatment option for you.
