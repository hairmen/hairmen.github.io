Can You Start Dreads With Damaged Hair

Written by William Jul 16, 2023 · 3 min read

If you have damaged hair and you're considering starting dreadlocks, you may be wondering if it's possible. The short answer is yes, you can start dreads with damaged hair. However, there are some things you need to know before you make the decision to start your journey to dreadlocks with damaged hair.

Description

Before we dive into the details, let's define what we mean by "damaged hair." Damaged hair can refer to hair that has been chemically treated, such as relaxed hair, or hair that has been physically damaged, such as hair that has been straightened using heat. Damaged hair can also refer to hair that is dry, brittle, and prone to breakage.

How to

If you have damaged hair and you want to start dreadlocks, here's what you need to do:

Step 1: Assess the Damage

The first step is to assess the damage to your hair. If your hair is severely damaged, it may not be a good idea to start dreadlocks. You may need to give your hair time to heal before you start the process.

Step 2: Choose a Method

Next, you'll need to choose a method for starting your dreadlocks. There are several methods to choose from, including backcombing, twisting, and using a crochet hook. Each method has its pros and cons, so do your research and choose the method that's best for your hair type and texture.

Step 3: Start the Process

Once you've chosen a method, it's time to start the process. Follow the instructions carefully, and be gentle with your hair. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause further damage.

Step 4: Be Patient

Starting dreadlocks takes time, especially if you have damaged hair. Be patient and give your hair time to adjust to the process. It may take several months or even a year before your dreadlocks are fully formed.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind if you're starting dreadlocks with damaged hair:

  • Be gentle with your hair
  • Avoid using heat on your hair
  • Moisturize your hair regularly
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Protect your hair while you sleep

FAQ

Can I start dreadlocks with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start dreadlocks with relaxed hair. However, it's important to note that relaxed hair is more prone to breakage, so you'll need to be extra gentle with your hair throughout the process.

Can I start dreadlocks with heat-damaged hair?

Yes, you can start dreadlocks with heat-damaged hair. However, it's important to avoid using heat on your hair while you're starting the process. Heat can cause further damage to your hair and make it more difficult to form dreadlocks.

How can I prevent further damage to my hair?

To prevent further damage to your hair, be gentle with your hair throughout the process. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, and avoid using heat or harsh chemicals.

Hair Care

Once your dreadlocks are fully formed, it's important to take care of them to prevent further damage. Here are some tips for caring for your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  • Avoid using heavy products on your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun
  • Retwist your dreadlocks regularly to maintain their shape
  • Trim any loose or frayed hairs to prevent breakage

In conclusion, it is possible to start dreadlocks with damaged hair, but it's important to be gentle with your hair throughout the process and take care of your dreadlocks once they're formed.
