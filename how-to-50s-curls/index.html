How To Get 50S Curls A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

The 1950s was an iconic era for fashion, beauty and hairstyles. One of the most popular hairstyles from the '50s is the classic curls. These curls are still in trend and can be seen on many red carpets today. If you want to achieve this timeless look, read on for our step-by-step guide on how to 50s curls.

Description

50s curls are a type of hairstyle that is characterized by curls that are big, bouncy and full of volume. It is a look that is very feminine and glamorous. The curls are usually created using hot rollers or a curling iron, and the hair is styled to have a lot of body and movement.

How to

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start curling your hair, it's important to prepare it properly. Start by washing your hair and applying a volumizing mousse or spray to add body and texture. Blow dry your hair until it's about 80% dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into sections. You can use clips or hair ties to keep each section in place. It's important to work on one section at a time to ensure that the curls are evenly distributed.

Step 3: Curl Your Hair

Now it's time to start curling your hair. You can use either hot rollers or a curling iron to create the curls. If you're using hot rollers, roll each section of hair around the roller and secure it with a clip. Leave the rollers in for about 15-20 minutes before removing them. If you're using a curling iron, wrap each section of hair around the barrel and hold it in place for a few seconds before releasing it.

Step 4: Set the Curls

Once you've finished curling your hair, it's important to set the curls in place. You can use a hairspray or a setting spray to ensure that the curls stay in place throughout the day.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair by brushing it out gently to create soft, bouncy curls. You can also add some hair accessories like a headband or a scarf to give your hair a vintage '50s look.

Tips

  • Use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from heat damage.
  • Don't brush your hair too vigorously after curling it as this can cause the curls to fall out.
  • For extra volume, tease your hair at the roots before curling it.
  • If you have naturally straight hair, you can use a perm to create long-lasting curls.

FAQ

How long do 50s curls last?

With proper care and styling, 50s curls can last for up to three days.

Can I create 50s curls on short hair?

Yes, you can create 50s curls on short hair using a curling iron or hot rollers. However, the curls may not be as voluminous as they would be on longer hair.

How do I maintain my 50s curls?

To maintain your 50s curls, avoid washing your hair too frequently as this can cause the curls to fall out. Instead, use dry shampoo to absorb any excess oil and keep your curls looking fresh. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent frizz.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair to ensure that it stays healthy and strong. To keep your hair in good condition, use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type. Avoid using too many styling products as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy. Additionally, try to limit your use of hot tools as excessive heat can cause damage to your hair.

Now that you know how to 50s curls, it's time to try out this classic look for yourself. With a little practice and patience, you'll be able to create the perfect vintage curls that will make you feel like a Hollywood starlet.
