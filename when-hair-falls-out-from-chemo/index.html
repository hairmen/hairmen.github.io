When Hair Falls Out From Chemo What You Need To Know

Written by Christine Sep 12, 2023 · 3 min read

If you or a loved one is about to undergo chemotherapy, one of the most common side effects you may experience is hair loss. This can be a difficult and emotional experience, but it’s important to understand what to expect and how to care for your hair during and after treatment. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know about when hair falls out from chemo.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Chemotherapy is a type of cancer treatment that uses drugs to kill cancer cells. While chemotherapy can be effective in treating cancer, it can also have side effects on the body, including hair loss. Hair loss from chemotherapy can occur gradually or all at once, depending on the type and dosage of drugs used.

Chemotherapy drugs target rapidly dividing cells, which includes cancer cells but also hair follicle cells. As a result, hair follicles can become damaged and hair can fall out. Hair loss can occur on the scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, and other areas of the body.

How to

If you’re about to undergo chemotherapy and are concerned about hair loss, there are a few things you can do to prepare:

  • Consider cutting your hair short before treatment to make the hair loss less noticeable
  • Invest in a wig or hairpiece
  • Experiment with hats, scarves, and other head coverings
  • Talk to your doctor about using a cooling cap during treatment to reduce hair loss

Step by step

While there’s no way to completely prevent hair loss from chemotherapy, there are steps you can take to minimize the impact:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools
  • Brush or comb your hair gently
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on the hair

After treatment, your hair will begin to grow back. However, it may be a different color or texture than before. It’s important to be patient and gentle with your hair as it grows back.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help you care for your hair during and after chemotherapy:

  • Protect your scalp from the sun with a hat or scarf
  • Avoid using hot water on your scalp in the shower
  • Consider using a scalp oil to promote healthy hair growth
  • Talk to your doctor about taking supplements or vitamins to support hair growth

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about hair loss from chemotherapy:

When does hair loss from chemotherapy occur?

Hair loss can occur within a few weeks of starting treatment or it may not occur until several months in.

Will all of my hair fall out?

Not everyone will experience complete hair loss from chemotherapy. The amount of hair loss can vary depending on the type and dosage of drugs used.

Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy?

Yes, most people will experience hair regrowth after chemotherapy. However, it may take several months for hair to grow back fully.

Hair Care

While experiencing hair loss from chemotherapy can be difficult, it’s important to remember that it’s a temporary side effect. In the meantime, taking care of your scalp and any remaining hair can help you feel more comfortable and confident. Be gentle with your hair, use mild products, and protect your scalp from the sun. With time and patience, your hair will begin to grow back.
