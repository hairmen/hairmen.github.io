How To Keep Bleached African American Hair Healthy

Written by Frank Nov 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have bleached African American hair, you may already know that it requires extra care and attention to keep it healthy. Bleached hair is more vulnerable to damage and breakage, which is why it's important to take good care of it. In this article, we'll share some tips on how to keep your bleached African American hair healthy and looking its best.

How to Bleach African American Hair

Bleaching African American hair is a process that involves stripping the hair of its natural color to achieve a lighter shade. This process can be damaging to the hair if not done properly. Here are some steps to follow when bleaching African American hair:

  1. Consult with a professional stylist who has experience with bleaching African American hair. They can assess the condition of your hair and determine the best approach for your hair type.
  2. Prepare your hair by washing it with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue.
  3. Apply a protective oil or cream to your hairline, ears, and neck to prevent irritation from the bleach.
  4. Mix the bleach according to the manufacturer's instructions and apply it to your hair in sections, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  5. Check the color of your hair every few minutes and rinse the bleach out when you've reached the desired shade.
  6. Deep condition your hair to restore moisture and prevent damage.

Step-by-Step Guide to Keep Bleached African American Hair Healthy

Once you've bleached your African American hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy. Here's a step-by-step guide to keeping your bleached hair looking its best:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair with a Moisturizing Shampoo

Use a moisturizing shampoo that's designed for bleached hair to cleanse your hair without stripping it of its natural oils. Avoid using hot water, which can further dry out your hair.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair with a Deep Conditioner

Use a deep conditioner once a week to help restore moisture and prevent breakage. Apply the conditioner to your hair and let it sit for at least 10 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Protect Your Hair from Heat

Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers and flat irons, which can further damage your hair. If you must use heat, apply a heat protectant to your hair first.

Step 4: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks can help prevent split ends and breakage, keeping your hair looking healthy and strong.

Step 5: Protect Your Hair While Sleeping

Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent your hair from snagging and breaking while you sleep. You can also protect your hair by wearing a silk or satin bonnet or scarf.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Bleached Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining healthy, bleached African American hair:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, like braids and weaves.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair and add moisture.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap.

Frequently Asked Questions About Bleached African American Hair

Q: Can I bleach my African American hair at home?

A: While it's possible to bleach your hair at home, we recommend consulting with a professional stylist who has experience with bleaching African American hair. Bleaching can be damaging to your hair if not done properly.

Q: How often should I wash my bleached hair?

A: We recommend washing your hair no more than 2-3 times per week to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Q: Can I still use hair dye on my bleached hair?

A: Yes, but be sure to use a hair dye that's specifically designed for bleached hair and follow the instructions carefully.

Hair Care Products for Bleached African American Hair

Here are some hair care products that are specifically designed for bleached African American hair:

  • Moisturizing Shampoo
  • Deep Conditioner
  • Leave-In Conditioner
  • Heat Protectant
  • Hair Oil

By following these tips and using the right hair care products, you can keep your bleached African American hair healthy and looking its best.
