How To Thin Out Long Thick Curly Hair

Written by Luffy Jul 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have long, thick, curly hair, you probably know how difficult it can be to manage. One of the best ways to make your hair more manageable is by thinning it out. In this blog post, we will go through the steps to thin out long thick curly hair.

Description

Thinning out your long, thick, curly hair can help you manage it better, reduce the amount of time you spend styling it, and make it look healthier. Thinning out your hair involves removing some of the bulk and weight from your hair, which can result in more defined curls and less frizz.

How to Thin Out Long Thick Curly Hair

Thinning out your long, thick, curly hair can be done in a few different ways. Here are some of the most common methods:

Method 1: Razor Cut

A razor cut is a technique where a stylist uses a razor to cut your hair instead of scissors. This technique can be used to thin out your hair while also adding texture and movement.

Method 2: Point Cutting

Point cutting is a technique where the stylist cuts into the hair at an angle, rather than straight across. This technique can be used to thin out your hair while also adding texture and definition to your curls.

Method 3: Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are scissors that have teeth on one blade, which can be used to remove some of the bulk from your hair without removing length. This technique can be used to thin out your hair while also adding texture and movement.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to thin out your long, thick, curly hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair.
  2. Towel dry your hair until it is damp.
  3. Section your hair into manageable sections.
  4. Decide which method you want to use to thin out your hair (razor cut, point cutting, or thinning shears).
  5. Use the chosen method to thin out your hair, working in small sections.
  6. Once you have finished thinning out your hair, style it as desired.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when thinning out your long, thick, curly hair:

  • Make sure to choose a stylist who is experienced in thinning out curly hair.
  • Communicate clearly with your stylist about the amount of hair you want to remove.
  • Avoid thinning out your hair too much, as this can result in a choppy and uneven look.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to help define your curls.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

FAQ

Can I thin out my own hair at home?

It is not recommended to thin out your own hair at home, as it can be difficult to achieve the desired results and can result in an uneven and choppy look. It is best to leave this technique to a professional stylist.

Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner?

Thinning out your hair will not make it look thinner, but it will remove some of the bulk and weight from your hair, making it more manageable and easier to style.

Hair Care

To keep your long, thick, curly hair looking its best after thinning it out, make sure to follow a good hair care routine. This should include regular washing and conditioning, using a deep conditioning treatment once a week, and avoiding heat styling as much as possible.

In conclusion, thinning out your long, thick, curly hair can help you manage it better, reduce the amount of time you spend styling it, and make it look healthier. By following the steps and tips outlined in this blog post, you can achieve the best results and enjoy your new, more manageable hairstyle.
