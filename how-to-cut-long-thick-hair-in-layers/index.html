How To Cut Long Thick Hair In Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Nov 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Long thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it's gorgeous and versatile. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. One way to make long thick hair more manageable and stylish is by cutting it in layers. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut long thick hair in layers.

Description

Layered hair is a popular hairstyle that can add volume, texture and movement to long thick hair. Layers are cut at different lengths to create depth and dimension. This style works well for all face shapes and hair types, but it's especially flattering for those with long, thick hair.

How To Cut Long Thick Hair In Layers

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a picture of the layered hairstyle you want to achieve for reference.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to section and cut. Comb out any tangles and knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, divide your hair into sections. Start at the nape of your neck and work your way up. Clip each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom section, comb out any tangles and hold the hair straight down. Use your scissors to cut the ends straight across. This will be your base layer.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the next section of hair and comb it out. Hold the hair straight up and cut it at an angle. The angle should be determined by the length you want the layer to be. Repeat this step for all the middle sections.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top section of hair and comb it out. Hold the hair straight up and cut it at an angle. The angle should be steeper than the middle layer to create more movement and texture. Repeat this step for all the top sections.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back.
  • Make sure your cuts are even. Use a mirror or ask a friend to check for you.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting long thick hair can be tiring and time-consuming.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my own hair in layers?

A: Yes, you can cut your own hair in layers. Just make sure you have the right tools and take your time.

Q: How often should I get my hair cut in layers?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how much maintenance you're willing to do. Generally, getting a trim every 6-8 weeks is recommended.

Q: Will cutting my hair in layers damage it?

A: No, cutting your hair in layers will not damage it. In fact, it can make it healthier by removing split ends and promoting new growth.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair in layers, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's formulated for your hair type. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Cutting long thick hair in layers can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and manageable hairstyle that works for you. Follow these steps and tips for a successful layered haircut.
