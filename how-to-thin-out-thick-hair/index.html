How To Thin Out Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Sep 22, 2023 · 3 min read

Having thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can give you a full, voluminous look, it can also be difficult to manage and style. One solution to this problem is to thin out your hair. In this article, we’ll give you a step-by-step guide on how to thin out thick hair, along with tips on how to care for your hair after thinning it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Out Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair
  • FAQ

How to Thin Out Thick Hair

Thinning out your hair involves removing some of the bulk to make it more manageable. This can be done in a number of ways, including:

  • Using thinning shears
  • Using a razor
  • Getting a layered haircut

Each of these methods has its pros and cons, and the best method for you will depend on your hair type and personal preferences.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Decide on the Method

The first step in thinning out your hair is to decide on the method you want to use. If you’re not sure which method is best for you, consult with a hairstylist.

2. Wash and Dry Your Hair

Next, wash and dry your hair so that it’s clean and free of any product buildup. This will make it easier to see where you need to thin out your hair.

3. Section Your Hair

Section your hair into 4-6 sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you thin out your hair evenly.

4. Start Thinning Out Your Hair

Using your chosen method, start thinning out your hair section by section. Be sure to work slowly and carefully, taking small amounts of hair at a time. It’s better to remove too little hair than too much.

5. Check Your Progress

After you’ve thinned out each section, check your progress in the mirror. If you need to thin out more, repeat the process until you’re satisfied with the results.

6. Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired using your favorite styling products.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up
  • Use sharp scissors or a razor to ensure a clean cut
  • Don’t thin out your hair too much, as it can lead to breakage and damage
  • Consult with a hairstylist if you’re not sure which method to use

FAQ

How often should I thin out my hair?

It’s best to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows and how much you need to thin it out.

Will thinning out my hair make it grow back thicker?

No, thinning out your hair will not make it grow back thicker. It simply removes some of the bulk to make it more manageable.

Hair Care After Thinning Out Thick Hair

After thinning out your hair, it’s important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Condition your hair regularly
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and healthy

Thinning out your hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and easier to style. By following these tips and using the right method, you can achieve a beautiful, healthy-looking head of hair.
