What Is A Fade Haircut In Spanish

Written by Robby Sep 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a new haircut but not sure what style to go for? One popular style that has been around for decades is the fade haircut. In Spanish, it is known as "corte degradado". In this article, we will explain what a fade haircut is, how to get one, and some tips and tricks to keep it looking fresh. Let's dive in!

Description

A fade haircut is a men's haircut that gradually fades from short to longer hair. The hair on the top of the head is typically longer and styled in various ways, while the sides and back are shaved or buzzed down to the skin. The fade can be low, medium, or high, depending on how high up the head it starts. It is a versatile haircut that can be customized to fit any face shape or hair texture, making it a popular choice for men of all ages and backgrounds.

How to Get a Fade Haircut

If you're interested in getting a fade haircut, the first step is to find a reputable barber or hair stylist who has experience with this style. You can ask friends, family, or co-workers for recommendations, or search online for reviews and photos of their work. Once you've found a stylist you trust, schedule an appointment and bring in some reference photos of the type of fade you want.

During the appointment, the stylist will start by determining the length and style of the hair on top, then use clippers or shears to cut the sides and back to the desired length. They will then use a series of guards or blades to gradually fade the hair from short to longer, creating a seamless transition between the two lengths. The process can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the complexity of the style.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to getting a fade haircut:

  1. Find a reputable stylist.
  2. Bring in reference photos of the type of fade you want.
  3. Determine the length and style of the hair on top.
  4. Cut the sides and back to the desired length.
  5. Gradually fade the hair from short to longer using clippers or shears.
  6. Blend the fade for a seamless transition.
  7. Add any finishing touches, such as a line-up or beard trim.
  8. Style the hair on top using pomade or other styling products.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep your fade looking fresh:

  • Get regular touch-ups every 2-4 weeks to maintain the shape and length of the fade.
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Apply a small amount of pomade or other styling product to the hair on top to keep it in place and add shine.
  • Avoid using too much product or applying it to the sides and back, as this can make the fade look greasy or weighed down.
  • Consider adding a beard or mustache to complement the look of the fade.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fade haircuts:

Is a fade haircut suitable for all hair types?

Yes, a fade haircut can be customized to fit any hair type, from straight to curly, thick to thin. However, the length and style of the hair on top may need to be adjusted to work with the natural texture of the hair.

How do I know which type of fade to get?

It's best to consult with your stylist and bring in reference photos of the type of fade you want. They can assess your hair type and face shape and recommend a style that will work best for you.

Can I get a fade with a longer beard?

Absolutely! A fade can be paired with any length of beard or mustache, depending on your personal style preference.

Hair Care

To keep your hair and scalp healthy, it's important to follow a regular hair care routine. This includes washing your hair every 2-3 days with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoiding excessive heat styling or chemical treatments, and protecting your hair from the sun and cold weather. If you have any specific concerns or issues, such as dandruff or hair loss, consult with a dermatologist or hair care professional for personalized advice.

Conclusion

So there you have it - a complete guide to what a fade haircut is in Spanish, how to get one, and how to take care of it. Whether you're looking for a classic or modern style, a fade can be customized to fit your individual preferences and personality. So why not give it a try and see how it can transform your look?
