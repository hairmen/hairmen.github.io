How To Give Yourself A Short Layered Haircut Step By Step Guide

If you're tired of your long locks and want to switch up your hair game, why not try a short layered haircut? Not only is it trendy, but it's also low-maintenance and easy to style. Plus, you can do it yourself at home! In this article, we'll go over the steps to give yourself a short layered haircut, as well as some tips and FAQs to make sure you get the best results possible.

How to Give Yourself a Short Layered Haircut

Before we get into the nitty-gritty, it's important to know the basics of a layered haircut. A layered haircut involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create texture, volume, and movement. Short layered haircuts are perfect for those who want a stylish and modern look without having to spend hours styling their hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now, let's get started on how to give yourself a short layered haircut:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can be difficult to cut and can cause your hair to look shorter than intended.
  2. Section your hair. Use hair clips to section your hair into manageable parts. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up.
  3. Decide on the length. Determine how short you want your hair to be and use that as a guide for cutting the rest of your hair. Remember, you can always go shorter, but you can't go longer once you've cut it!
  4. Start cutting. Holding your scissors at an angle, cut small sections of your hair at a time. Snip the ends at a slight angle to create layers. Continue cutting each section of your hair until you've reached the top layer.
  5. Blend the layers. Once you've cut all the layers, blend them together by snipping the ends of the top layer to match the rest of your hair.
  6. Check for symmetry. Make sure both sides of your hair are even and adjust as needed.

And there you have it! You've given yourself a short layered haircut.

Tips for a Perfect Short Layered Haircut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when giving yourself a short layered haircut:

  • Invest in good quality hair scissors. Blunt or dull scissors can cause split ends and damage to your hair.
  • Take your time. Rushing through the process can lead to uneven layers and a choppy haircut.
  • Use a mirror. It's important to see the back of your head to ensure symmetry and even layers.
  • Start with a longer length than you want. You can always go shorter, but it's difficult to add length once it's been cut.
  • Practice makes perfect. Don't be discouraged if your first attempt doesn't turn out as planned. Keep practicing and you'll master the technique in no time.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about short layered haircuts:

1. Will a short layered haircut work for my hair type?

Short layered haircuts work well for all hair types, including straight, wavy, curly, and thick hair.

2. How often should I get a short layered haircut?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and maintain your hairstyle.

3. Can I still style my hair after a short layered haircut?

Absolutely! Short layered haircuts are versatile and can be styled in many ways, including straight, curly, or wavy.

Hair Care After a Short Layered Haircut

After you've given yourself a short layered haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent your hair from drying out.
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your hairstyle and prevent split ends.

And there you have it! A complete guide on how to give yourself a short layered haircut. Remember to take your time, invest in good quality scissors, and practice often to master the technique. Good luck!
