Big Chop Natural Hair Journey A Guide To Starting Your Natural Hair Journey

Written by Frank Jul 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of the constant upkeep and maintenance of your relaxed hair? Are you ready to embrace the natural texture of your hair? If so, then a big chop natural hair journey may be right for you. In this article, we will guide you through the process of starting your natural hair journey, from the big chop to hair care tips.

Table of Contents

  1. What is a Big Chop?
  2. How to Prepare for a Big Chop
  3. The Big Chop Process
  4. Caring for Your Natural Hair
  5. Tips for Transitioning to Natural Hair
  6. Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Big Chop?

A big chop is the act of cutting off all of your relaxed, chemically-treated hair to start fresh with your natural hair texture. This process can be liberating for many women who are ready to embrace their natural hair journey.

How to Prepare for a Big Chop

Before you take the plunge and chop off your hair, there are a few things you should do to prepare:

1. Research natural hair styles

Take some time to research different natural hair styles that you may want to try after your big chop. This will give you an idea of what to expect and can help you feel more confident about the process.

2. Find a stylist

Look for a stylist who specializes in natural hair care. They can guide you through the big chop process and help you choose a natural hair style that fits your face shape and lifestyle.

3. Prepare your hair

Before your big chop, make sure to deep condition your hair to improve its strength and elasticity. You should also avoid using heat styling tools and chemical treatments in the weeks leading up to your big chop.

The Big Chop Process

The big chop process can be done in a salon or at home, depending on your preference. Here are the steps to follow:

1. Section your hair

Section your hair into four or more sections and secure with hair clips.

2. Cut off the relaxed hair

Using sharp hair shears, cut off the relaxed hair from each section, leaving only your natural hair.

3. Style your natural hair

Once your hair is cut, you can choose to style it in any natural hair style that you prefer, such as a TWA (teeny weeny afro) or a protective style like braids or twists.

Caring for Your Natural Hair

Now that you have made the big chop, it is important to care for your natural hair properly. Here are some tips:

1. Moisturize regularly

Natural hair is prone to dryness, so it is important to moisturize your hair regularly with a water-based moisturizer.

2. Protect your hair at night

Before going to bed, wrap your hair with a satin scarf or use a satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage.

3. Avoid heat styling tools

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your natural hair.

Tips for Transitioning to Natural Hair

If you are not ready to make the big chop, there are other ways to transition to natural hair:

1. Protective styles

Wear protective styles like braids, twists, and weaves to protect your natural hair and promote healthy growth.

2. Trim your relaxed hair

Gradually trim off your relaxed hair over time, until you are left with only your natural hair.

3. Use natural hair products

Start using natural hair products that are free of harsh chemicals and sulfates.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Will my hair grow back after the big chop?

Yes, your hair will grow back after the big chop. However, it may take some time for your hair to grow back to its original length.

2. How often should I deep condition my natural hair?

You should deep condition your natural hair at least once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

3. Can I use heat on my natural hair?

It is best to avoid using heat styling tools on your natural hair, as they can cause damage and breakage.

Starting your big chop natural hair journey can be an exciting and liberating experience. With proper care and maintenance, your natural hair will grow healthy and strong. Remember to research natural hair styles, find a stylist, and prepare your hair before the big chop. Good luck on your natural hair journey!
