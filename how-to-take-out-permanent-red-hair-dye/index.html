How To Take Out Permanent Red Hair Dye

Written by Daniel Sep 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've recently dyed your hair a permanent red color and now want to get rid of it, you may be wondering how to do so without damaging your hair. In this article, we'll discuss the steps you can take to safely and effectively remove permanent red hair dye. Let's get started!

Description

Permanent hair dye is a long-lasting hair color that can be difficult to remove. Red hair dye, in particular, can be challenging to get rid of because it tends to cling to the hair shaft. However, with the right techniques and products, you can safely and effectively remove permanent red hair dye from your hair.

How to

The following steps will guide you through the process of taking out permanent red hair dye:

Step 1: Clarify Your Hair

The first step in removing permanent red hair dye is to clarify your hair. Clarifying shampoos are designed to remove buildup and impurities from the hair, including hair dye. Use a clarifying shampoo to wash your hair and rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Apply a Hair Color Remover

Next, apply a hair color remover to your hair. Hair color removers are designed to break down the hair dye molecules so they can be washed out of the hair. Follow the instructions on the product packaging to apply the hair color remover to your hair.

Step 3: Rinse Your Hair

After applying the hair color remover, rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water. Be sure to rinse your hair for at least 5-10 minutes to ensure that all of the product is washed out of your hair.

Step 4: Apply a Deep Conditioning Treatment

After rinsing out the hair color remover, apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair. Hair color removers can be drying to the hair, so it's important to replenish moisture and nutrients to your hair. Leave the deep conditioner on your hair for 5-10 minutes, then rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 5: Repeat If Necessary

If the red hair dye is still visible after one treatment, repeat the above steps until the hair dye is completely removed.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when removing permanent red hair dye:

  • Be patient. It may take several treatments to completely remove the red hair dye.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as this can open up the hair cuticles and cause the hair dye to set in further.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as these can further damage your hair.
  • Consider visiting a professional hairstylist if you're unsure about removing the red hair dye yourself.

FAQ

Can I use bleach to remove red hair dye?

Bleach can be used to remove red hair dye, but it can be very damaging to your hair. If you choose to use bleach, be sure to follow the instructions carefully and use a deep conditioning treatment afterwards.

How long will it take to remove the red hair dye?

The length of time it takes to remove the red hair dye will depend on the type of product you use and the condition of your hair. It may take several treatments to completely remove the hair dye.

Will removing the red hair dye damage my hair?

Using hair color removers can be drying to your hair, so it's important to use a deep conditioning treatment afterwards to replenish moisture and nutrients to your hair. If you're concerned about damaging your hair, consider visiting a professional hairstylist.

Hair Care

After removing the red hair dye, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, or use a heat protectant spray if you must use them.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Consider using a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to protect your hair color.

With these tips and techniques, you can safely and effectively remove permanent red hair dye from your hair. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and take care of it afterwards to prevent further damage.
