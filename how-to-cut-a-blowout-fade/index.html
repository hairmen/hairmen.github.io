How To Cut A Blowout Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Oct 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a trendy and stylish haircut, then a blowout fade is one of the best options out there. This hairstyle is a combination of a blowout and a fade, and it's perfect for men who want to look sharp and put-together. In this article, we will teach you how to cut a blowout fade in a relaxed English language. We will cover everything from the tools you need to the step-by-step process, as well as tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Tools You Need

Before you start cutting a blowout fade, you need to ensure that you have the right tools. Here's a list of the tools you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Clipper guards of different sizes
  • A comb
  • A blow dryer
  • A mirror
  • A cape or towel to keep the hair off the client's clothes

Preparation

Once you have all the necessary tools, it's time to prepare for the haircut. Here's what you need to do:

  • Wash the client's hair and towel-dry it until it's damp
  • Section the hair into four parts: the top, the sides, and the back
  • Clip the top section of the hair to keep it out of the way
  • Attach the appropriate clipper guard to your clippers

Blowout

The blowout is the first step in achieving a blowout fade. Here's how to do it:

  • Unclip the top section of the hair and use a comb to lift it up
  • Use a blow dryer to blow the hair in an upward direction
  • Use the comb to lift the hair up and away from the scalp while blowing it with the dryer
  • Repeat this process until the top section of the hair is dry and styled

Fade

The next step is to create a fade. Here's how to do it:

  • Start with the clipper guard that's a size smaller than the one you used for the blowout
  • Use the clippers to make a guideline around the back and sides of the head
  • Use a higher clipper guard to fade the hair from the guideline to the top section of the hair
  • Blend the hair between the different clipper guard sizes to create a smooth transition

Lines and Detailing

Once you've created the fade, it's time to add some lines and detailing to the haircut. Here's how to do it:

  • Use the clippers to create a line around the perimeter of the haircut
  • Use scissors to blend the hair around the ears and neckline
  • Use a trimmer to create more defined lines and detailing around the hairline and sideburns

Clean-up

The final step is to clean up any loose hairs and give the client a finished look. Here's how to do it:

  • Use a comb and scissors to trim any stray hairs that are sticking out
  • Dust off the client's neck and shoulders to remove any loose hairs
  • Show the client the finished haircut and ask if they would like any adjustments

Tips for Cutting a Blowout Fade

Here are some tips to keep in mind while cutting a blowout fade:

  • Start with a larger clipper guard and work your way down to create a smooth fade
  • Use a comb to lift the hair up and away from the scalp while blow-drying it for a more voluminous look
  • Be patient and take your time while creating the fade to ensure that it looks even and polished

FAQs

How long does a blowout fade last?

A blowout fade can last anywhere from 2-4 weeks, depending on how fast the hair grows.

Can I cut a blowout fade on my own?

While it's possible to cut a blowout fade on your own, we recommend going to a professional barber or stylist for the best results.

What products should I use to style a blowout fade?

You can use a variety of products to style a blowout fade, including pomade, wax, or gel. Choose a product that works well with your hair type and provides the hold and shine you're looking for.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your blowout fade looking great, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage the strands
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your blowout fade

Conclusion

Now that you know how to cut a blowout fade, you can give your clients a trendy and stylish haircut that they'll love. Remember to use the right tools, take your time, and follow the step-by-step process to create a polished and even fade. With the right care and maintenance, your blowout fade will look great for weeks to come.
