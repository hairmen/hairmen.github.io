Should You Use Thinning Shears On Thick Hair

Written by Frank Sep 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Thinning shears are a popular tool used by hairstylists to remove bulk from thick hair. They work by snipping out sections of hair, creating a softer, more natural-looking finish. But, should you use thinning shears on thick hair? In this post, we’ll explore the pros and cons of using thinning shears on thick hair, how to use them correctly, and provide tips and tricks for maintaining healthy hair.

Table of Contents

  • Pros and Cons of Using Thinning Shears on Thick Hair
  • How to Use Thinning Shears on Thick Hair
  • Tips and Tricks for Using Thinning Shears on Thick Hair
  • FAQs About Using Thinning Shears on Thick Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Pros and Cons of Using Thinning Shears on Thick Hair

There are both pros and cons to using thinning shears on thick hair. Let’s take a look at some of them:

Pros

  • Removes bulk from thick hair, creating a softer, more natural-looking finish
  • Helps to blend layers and textures
  • Can be used to create a variety of styles, from blunt cuts to wispy, textured looks

Cons

  • Can cause damage to hair if not used correctly
  • May not be suitable for all hair types
  • Can create an uneven finish if not used correctly

Overall, the decision to use thinning shears on thick hair will depend on the individual hair type and style desired. It’s important to consult with a hairstylist before making any drastic changes to your hair.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Thick Hair

When using thinning shears on thick hair, it’s important to follow these steps:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair
  2. Section off the hair into manageable sections
  3. Hold the thinning shears at a slight angle and snip small sections of hair
  4. Work slowly and methodically, being careful not to cut too much hair at once
  5. Blend the cut sections into the rest of the hair using a comb
  6. Repeat as necessary, being careful not to overuse the thinning shears

It’s important to note that thinning shears should not be used on wet or damp hair, as this can lead to uneven cutting and damage to the hair.

Tips and Tricks for Using Thinning Shears on Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips and tricks for using thinning shears on thick hair:

  • Use high-quality thinning shears to ensure a clean, even cut
  • Consult with a hairstylist before using thinning shears on your hair
  • Only use thinning shears on the ends of the hair, not the roots
  • Work slowly and methodically, being careful not to cut too much hair at once
  • Use a comb to blend the cut sections into the rest of the hair

FAQs About Using Thinning Shears on Thick Hair

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

Thinning shears can be used on a variety of hair types, but it’s important to consult with a hairstylist before using them on your hair.

Will using thinning shears damage my hair?

Thinning shears can cause damage to hair if not used correctly. It’s important to use high-quality thinning shears and work slowly and methodically.

How often should I use thinning shears on my hair?

It’s important not to overuse thinning shears on your hair. Consult with a hairstylist to determine how often you should use them.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Using thinning shears on thick hair can help create a softer, more natural-looking finish. However, it’s important to also take care of your hair to maintain its health and vitality. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength

By following these tips and using thinning shears correctly, you can create a beautiful, healthy head of hair.
