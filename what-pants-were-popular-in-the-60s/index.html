What Pants Were Popular In The 60S

Written by Christine Jul 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our blog post about what pants were popular in the 60s. In this article, we will be discussing the different types of pants that were popular during this era, and how you can incorporate them into your modern wardrobe. Whether you're a fan of the classic bell bottoms or the sleek cigarette pants, we've got you covered. Read on to find out more!

What Were the Most Popular Pants in the 60s?

The 60s was a decade of change, and this was reflected in the fashion of the time. One of the most iconic styles of pants from this era was the bell bottom. These pants were tight around the thighs and flared out towards the bottom, creating a distinctive bell shape. Bell bottoms were often worn with platform shoes, and were a favorite of hippies and musicians alike.

Another popular style of pants in the 60s was the cigarette pant. These pants were slim-fitting and often made of a stretchy material like spandex. They were typically worn with high heels, and were a favorite of fashion-forward women who wanted to show off their curves.

How Can You Incorporate 60s Style Pants into Your Wardrobe?

If you're looking to add some 60s flair to your wardrobe, there are a few different ways you can do it. One option is to invest in a pair of vintage bell bottoms or cigarette pants. These can be found at thrift stores and online vintage retailers, and can be paired with modern tops and shoes for a retro-inspired look.

Another option is to look for modern pants that are inspired by 60s styles. For example, you could try a pair of high-waisted wide-leg pants, which are similar to bell bottoms but with a more modern twist. Alternatively, you could try a pair of cropped cigarette pants, which are a great option for work or dressier occasions.

Step-by-Step Guide to Wearing 60s Style Pants

1. Choose your style: Decide whether you want to go for a more traditional 60s look with bell bottoms, or a more modern take with cigarette pants or wide-leg trousers.

2. Choose your fabric: Look for pants made of stretchy materials like spandex or denim for a more authentic 60s look.

3. Choose your footwear: Bell bottoms look great with platform shoes or boots, while cigarette pants look best with high heels or flats.

4. Choose your top: Pair your pants with a simple, fitted top to balance out the volume of the pants.

5. Accessorize: Add some 60s-inspired accessories like a headband or scarf to complete your look.

Tips for Wearing 60s Style Pants

- Keep your outfit simple: Let your pants be the focus of your outfit by pairing them with a simple top and minimal accessories.

- Choose the right length: Make sure your pants are the right length for your height and the style you're going for.

- Experiment with different styles: Don't be afraid to try different types of 60s-inspired pants to find the one that suits you best.

FAQs

Q: Can men wear 60s-inspired pants?

A: Absolutely! Men can wear bell bottoms or wide-leg trousers for a retro-inspired look.

Q: What should I wear with bell bottoms?

A: Bell bottoms look great with platform shoes or boots, and can be paired with a fitted top or a flowy blouse.

60s-Inspired Hair Care

No 60s-inspired outfit is complete without the perfect hairstyle. If you're going for a retro look, try a bouffant or a beehive. For a more modern take, try a sleek bob or a messy bun.

To achieve a bouffant, backcomb the crown of your hair and smooth the top layer over it. Pin the sides back and add a hair accessory like a scarf or a headband.

For a sleek bob, use a straightening iron to smooth out your hair and add shine. Part your hair down the middle and tuck it behind your ears.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock 60s-inspired pants with confidence and style. Whether you're going for a retro look or a modern twist, these pants are sure to make a statement. Happy shopping!
