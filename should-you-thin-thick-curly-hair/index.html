Should You Thin Thick Curly Hair

Written by Luffy Oct 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it can look absolutely gorgeous, it can also be thick and unmanageable. If you have thick curly hair, you may be wondering if you should thin it out. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of thinning thick curly hair, and provide tips on how to do it properly.

Pros and Cons of Thinning Thick Curly Hair

Thinning thick curly hair can have both advantages and disadvantages. Here are some pros and cons to consider before you decide to thin your hair:

Pros:

  • Reduce Volume: Thinning your hair can help to reduce the volume, making it easier to manage and style.
  • Reduce Frizz: Thick curly hair can often be prone to frizz, thinning it can help to reduce the amount of frizz you experience.
  • Improve Definition: Thinning your hair can help to improve the definition of your curls, making them more defined and less weighed down.

Cons:

  • Loss of Volume: Thinning your hair can also result in a loss of volume, which may not be desirable if you have thin hair.
  • Damaged Hair: Thinning your hair can cause damage, especially if it's done improperly or too often.
  • Uneven Results: Thinning your hair can result in uneven results, which can be difficult to fix.

How to Thin Thick Curly Hair

If you've decided to thin your thick curly hair, it's important to do it properly to avoid damage. Here are some tips on how to thin thick curly hair:

  • Use Thinners: Use thinning shears or texturizing scissors to thin your hair. These tools are specifically designed to thin hair without causing damage.
  • Start Slow: It's important to start slow when thinning your hair. Thin small sections at a time to avoid over-thinning your hair.
  • Consult with a Professional: If you're unsure about how to thin your hair, consult with a professional hairstylist. They can provide guidance and ensure that your hair is thinned properly.

Step-by-Step Guide to Thinning Thick Curly Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide to thinning thick curly hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section your hair and clip it up, leaving one section down.
  3. Use thinning shears to thin small sections of hair, starting at the bottom of the section and working your way up.
  4. Repeat on each section until you've thinned your hair to your liking.
  5. Style your hair as usual.

Tips for Thinning Thick Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for thinning thick curly hair:

  • Don't thin too much: It's important not to over-thin your hair, as this can result in damaged hair and uneven results.
  • Thin in layers: Thin your hair in layers to avoid an uneven look.
  • Use the right tools: Use thinning shears or texturizing scissors to thin your hair, as regular scissors can cause damage.
  • Consult with a professional: If you're unsure about how to thin your hair, consult with a professional hairstylist.

FAQs

Can thinning thick curly hair cause damage?

Thinning your hair can cause damage if it's done improperly or too often. It's important to use the right tools and start slow when thinning your hair to avoid damage.

How much hair should I thin?

The amount of hair you should thin depends on your personal preference and hair type. It's important not to over-thin your hair, as this can result in damaged hair and uneven results.

How often should I thin my hair?

It's not recommended to thin your hair too often, as this can cause damage. It's best to consult with a professional hairstylist to determine how often you should thin your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Curly Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for thick curly hair:

  • Use the right products: Use products specifically designed for thick curly hair to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling can cause damage to your hair, so it's best to avoid it if possible.
  • Deep condition regularly: Deep conditioning your hair regularly can help to keep it healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair: Brushing your hair can cause frizz and damage, so it's best to avoid it if possible.

Thinning thick curly hair can be a great way to reduce volume and improve definition. However, it's important to do it properly to avoid damage. Use the right tools, start slow, and consult with a professional hairstylist if you're unsure. With these tips, you can achieve beautiful, healthy, and manageable hair.
