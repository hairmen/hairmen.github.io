How Can I Tell If Short Hair Will Suit Me

Written by Aprilia Sep 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Short hair can be a trendy and low-maintenance option for those looking to switch up their hairstyle. However, it's important to consider whether or not short hair will suit your face shape and features before making the chop. Here are some tips to help you determine if short hair is the right choice for you.

Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in determining whether or not short hair will suit you is to determine your face shape. There are typically four main face shapes: round, square, oval, and heart-shaped. Different face shapes lend themselves to different hairstyles, so it's important to know which one you have before making any decisions about your hair. If you're not sure what your face shape is, there are plenty of online resources and guides to help you figure it out.

Consider Your Features

Another important factor to consider when deciding if short hair will suit you is your facial features. If you have prominent cheekbones, a strong jawline, or other features that you want to highlight, short hair may be a good choice. On the other hand, if you have a larger forehead or a more prominent nose, longer hair may help balance out your features better.

Think About Your Hair Texture

Your hair texture can also play a role in how well short hair will suit you. If you have thick, coarse hair, short hair can help tame it and make it more manageable. However, if you have fine or thin hair, short hair may not be the best choice as it can make your hair appear even thinner.

Start with a Longer Length

If you're considering short hair but aren't quite ready to commit to a super-short cut, start with a longer length. A bob or lob can give you a taste of shorter hair without going all the way. This can help you get used to the idea of having less hair and also allow you to experiment with different styles before going for a more drastic cut.

Try Wigs or Virtual Makeovers

If you're still unsure whether or not short hair will suit you, try experimenting with wigs or virtual makeovers. This can give you a better idea of what you would look like with shorter hair without having to make any permanent changes. There are plenty of apps and online tools that allow you to upload a photo of yourself and try on different hairstyles virtually.

Ask Your Stylist

Your stylist is also a great resource when it comes to deciding whether or not short hair will suit you. They can take into account your face shape, features, and hair texture to help you make an informed decision. They can also recommend styles that will work well with your hair type and face shape.

Factor in Maintenance

Short hair can be low-maintenance, but it still requires some upkeep. If you're used to having longer hair, you may need to adjust your hair care routine to accommodate shorter locks. You may also need to get more frequent haircuts to maintain your style. Consider whether or not you're willing to put in the effort to keep your short hair looking its best.

Confidence is Key

Ultimately, the most important factor in deciding whether or not short hair will suit you is confidence. If you feel confident and comfortable with shorter hair, it will show. Don't be afraid to take the plunge and try something new!

FAQ

Q: Will short hair suit me if I have a round face?

A: Yes! Short hair can actually look great on those with round faces. The key is to choose a style that adds height and volume on top to elongate the face.

Q: Can I still style short hair?

A: Absolutely! Short hair can be styled in a variety of ways, from sleek and straight to tousled and textured.

Q: Will short hair make my face look wider?

A: It depends on the style you choose. A bob or lob that hits at the jawline can actually help slim the face, while a super-short cut that is tapered at the sides may widen the face.

Hair Care Tips

Whether you decide to go for short hair or not, it's important to take care of your locks. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush and comb to prevent tangles and breakage.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain your style.
