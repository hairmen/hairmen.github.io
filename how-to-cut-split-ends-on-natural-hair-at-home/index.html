How To Cut Split Ends On Natural Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 01, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Split ends are a common problem that many people face, especially those with natural hair. They can be caused by a variety of factors, such as heat styling, chemical treatments, and environmental stressors. If left untreated, split ends can lead to further damage and breakage, which can be frustrating for those who are trying to grow their hair. In this post, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut split ends on natural hair at home.

How to

Before we dive into the actual process of cutting split ends, it is important to note that prevention is key. Here are a few tips to help prevent split ends from occurring in the first place:

Tips

  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to avoid pulling and tugging on the hair
  • Moisturize your hair regularly
  • Protect your hair from environmental stressors, such as sun and wind
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends from traveling up the hair shaft

Now that you know how to prevent split ends, let's move on to how to actually cut them.

Step-by-Step

Step 1: Wash and detangle your hair. It is important to start with clean, detangled hair to make the cutting process easier.

Step 2: Divide your hair into small sections. This will make it easier to identify and cut split ends.

Step 3: Take a small section of hair, and twist it tightly. Any split ends will stick out, making them easier to identify.

Step 4: Use a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors to snip off the split ends. It is important to use hair cutting scissors, as regular scissors can cause more damage to the hair.

Step 5: Repeat steps 3 and 4 on all sections of your hair.

Step 6: Once you have cut all of the split ends, wash and condition your hair again.

Step 7: Style your hair as desired.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my split ends?

A: It is recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends from traveling up the hair shaft.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to cut split ends?

A: It is not recommended to use regular scissors, as they can cause more damage to the hair. It is important to use hair cutting scissors.

Q: Can I cut my split ends at home?

A: Yes, you can cut your split ends at home as long as you use the proper tools and techniques.

Hair Care

In addition to cutting split ends, it is important to take care of your hair on a regular basis to prevent further damage. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo
  • Deep condition your hair at least once a week
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Protect your hair from environmental stressors, such as sun and wind
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair

By following these tips and cutting your split ends regularly, you can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent further damage.
