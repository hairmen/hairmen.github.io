Is It Normal To Have Bald Spots After Microblading

Written by Robby Nov 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you are someone who has undergone microblading, you might be familiar with the post-procedure phase where your eyebrows might appear slightly different from what you expected. While some people may experience no issues, others might notice bald spots or gaps in their newly microbladed eyebrows. If you are someone who is experiencing such a concern, you might be wondering if it is normal to have bald spots after microblading. In this blog post, we will discuss this issue in detail and provide you with some tips on how to deal with it.

Description

Microblading is a cosmetic tattooing procedure that involves creating hair-like strokes in the eyebrow area using a handheld tool. The purpose of this procedure is to give the eyebrows a fuller, natural-looking appearance. However, just like any other cosmetic procedure, microblading also has its risks and side effects. One of the most common concerns that people have after microblading is the appearance of bald spots or gaps in their eyebrows.

How to

If you are experiencing bald spots or gaps in your newly microbladed eyebrows, here are some tips on how to deal with them:

1. Wait for the Healing Process to Complete

It is important to understand that microblading is a semi-permanent procedure that requires some time to heal. During this period, your eyebrows might appear slightly different from what you expected. The healing process can take up to four weeks, and during this time, you might notice some scabbing, flaking, or peeling. It is crucial to avoid picking at the scabs or flakes as this can lead to further damage to your skin.

2. Schedule a Touch-Up Appointment

If you notice bald spots or gaps in your eyebrows after the healing process is complete, you can schedule a touch-up appointment with your microblading artist. A touch-up appointment is a follow-up appointment that is usually scheduled four to six weeks after the initial procedure. During this appointment, your artist will fill in any gaps or bald spots and make any necessary adjustments to your eyebrows.

3. Apply Castor Oil

Castor oil is a natural remedy that can help promote hair growth. Applying castor oil to your eyebrows can help stimulate hair growth and fill in any bald spots or gaps. You can apply castor oil to your eyebrows using a clean mascara wand or a cotton swab. However, it is important to avoid applying too much castor oil as this can lead to clogged pores or irritation.

Step by Step

Here are some steps on how to apply castor oil to your eyebrows:

Step 1: Cleanse Your Face

Before applying castor oil, it is important to cleanse your face and eyebrows. Use a gentle cleanser to remove any dirt, oil, or makeup from your skin.

Step 2: Apply Castor Oil

Dip a clean mascara wand or a cotton swab into the castor oil and apply it to your eyebrows. Make sure to apply the oil evenly and avoid applying too much.

Step 3: Massage Your Eyebrows

Use your fingertips to massage the castor oil into your eyebrows in a circular motion. This will help stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth.

Step 4: Leave the Castor Oil Overnight

Leave the castor oil on your eyebrows overnight and wash it off in the morning. You can repeat this process every night until you see an improvement in your eyebrows.

Tips

Here are some additional tips on how to deal with bald spots or gaps in your microbladed eyebrows:

1. Avoid Sun Exposure

It is important to avoid sun exposure during the healing process as this can lead to further damage to your skin. Make sure to wear a hat or use a sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher to protect your skin.

2. Avoid Sweating

During the healing process, it is important to avoid sweating as this can lead to infection or irritation. Avoid strenuous activities, hot showers, or saunas during this period.

3. Follow Your Aftercare Instructions

Make sure to follow your aftercare instructions carefully to ensure proper healing. This includes avoiding makeup, touching your eyebrows, or getting them wet for at least 24 hours after the procedure.

FAQ

Q. Is it normal to have bald spots after microblading?

A. Yes, it is normal to have bald spots or gaps in your eyebrows after microblading. This can happen due to various reasons, including the natural shedding of the skin or improper healing.

Q. Will my eyebrows look different after the healing process?

A. Yes, your eyebrows might look slightly different from what you expected after the healing process. However, this is normal, and you can schedule a touch-up appointment with your microblading artist to make any necessary adjustments.

Q. How long does it take for the eyebrows to heal after microblading?

A. The healing process can take up to four weeks, and during this time, you might notice some scabbing, flaking, or peeling. It is important to avoid picking at the scabs or flakes as this can lead to further damage to your skin.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to ensure healthy hair growth. Here are some tips on how to take care of your hair:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to include foods such as eggs, nuts, fish, and leafy greens in your diet.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Excessive heat styling can damage your hair and lead to hair loss. Avoid using hot tools such as curling irons or straighteners on a daily basis.

3. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Using a gentle shampoo can help prevent damage to your hair and scalp. Make sure to choose a shampoo that is free of harsh chemicals and sulfates.

In conclusion, experiencing bald spots or gaps in your eyebrows after microblading is normal, and there are several ways to deal with this issue. Make sure to follow the aftercare instructions carefully, avoid sun exposure and sweating, and schedule a touch-up appointment with your microblading artist if necessary. With proper care and patience, you can achieve the full, natural-looking eyebrows that you desire.
