How To Recover From Pcos Hair Loss A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're one of the millions of women who suffer from polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), you may be familiar with the unpleasant side effects it can cause, such as weight gain, irregular periods, and acne. However, one of the most distressing symptoms of PCOS is hair loss. If you're struggling with thinning hair, bald spots or general hair loss, you're not alone. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to recover from PCOS hair loss:

Description

PCOS is a hormonal disorder that affects women of reproductive age. It causes a range of symptoms, including hair loss, due to elevated levels of androgens (male hormones) such as testosterone in the body. Hair loss related to PCOS can be particularly difficult to deal with, but there are several things you can do to help manage the condition and promote regrowth.

How to Recover from PCOS Hair Loss

Step 1: Consult with a Doctor

The first step in recovering from PCOS hair loss is to consult with a doctor. They can help identify the underlying causes of your hair loss and recommend an appropriate treatment plan. Your doctor may also refer you to a dermatologist or endocrinologist, depending on the severity of your symptoms.

Step 2: Manage Your PCOS Symptoms

Managing your PCOS symptoms can help reduce hair loss. This may involve lifestyle changes such as exercising regularly, eating a healthy diet, and managing stress. Your doctor may also recommend medications such as birth control pills or metformin to help regulate your hormones and manage your PCOS symptoms.

Step 3: Use Hair Growth Products

There are several over-the-counter hair growth products available that can help promote hair regrowth. Look for products that contain ingredients such as minoxidil, biotin, and keratin. These ingredients can help stimulate hair follicles and promote healthy hair growth.

Step 4: Consider Hair Restoration Treatments

If your hair loss is severe, you may want to consider hair restoration treatments such as hair transplants or scalp micropigmentation. These treatments can help restore the appearance of your hair and improve your self-confidence.

Tips for Recovering from PCOS Hair Loss

Here are a few additional tips that can help you recover from PCOS hair loss:

  • Be gentle with your hair. Avoid harsh chemicals and treatments that can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to avoid pulling on your hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids and ponytails that can cause hair breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a protective hair product.

FAQs

Can PCOS hair loss be reversed?

While PCOS hair loss can be difficult to manage, it is possible to reverse it with the right treatment plan. Working with your doctor to manage your PCOS symptoms and using hair growth products can help promote regrowth.

What causes PCOS hair loss?

PCOS hair loss is caused by elevated levels of androgens (male hormones) such as testosterone in the body. These hormones can cause hair follicles to shrink, leading to hair loss.

How long does it take to recover from PCOS hair loss?

Recovery time for PCOS hair loss can vary depending on the severity of your symptoms and the treatment plan you're following. It may take several months to see significant regrowth, but with patience and persistence, it is possible to recover from PCOS hair loss.

Hair Care for PCOS

Finally, taking care of your hair can help promote healthy regrowth. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Avoid heat styling tools such as straighteners and curling irons.
  • Use a deep conditioning hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair health, such as vitamins A, C, and E, biotin, and iron.

Remember, recovering from PCOS hair loss is a process that takes time and patience. Working with your doctor and taking care of your hair can help promote healthy regrowth and improve your self-confidence.
