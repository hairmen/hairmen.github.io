How To Style Your Hair 1950S A Guide To Achieving The Perfect Retro Look

Written by Robby Sep 23, 2023 · 4 min read

The 1950s was an iconic era for fashion and beauty. From the clothing to the makeup, everything had a specific style that was unique to that decade. One of the most popular hair trends during this time was the classic pin-up style, which is still widely popular to this day. In this blog post, we will be discussing how to style your hair 1950s and achieve that perfect retro look. Read on to learn more.

Description

The 1950s was a time of glamour and sophistication. Women would often wear their hair in a variety of styles, from short bobs to intricate updos. The pin-up style was particularly popular, and it involved creating voluminous curls and waves that framed the face. This style was often achieved using hot rollers, curling irons, and hairspray.

How to Style Your Hair 1950s: Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start styling your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the hot tools you will be using. If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, you can skip this step.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward. This will ensure that you don't miss any sections and that your curls are even throughout your hair.

Step 3: Curl Your Hair

Using a curling iron or hot rollers, curl each section of hair. Make sure to curl your hair away from your face to achieve that classic 1950s look. Once you have curled each section, clip the curls in place and let them cool for at least 10 minutes.

Step 4: Brush Out Your Curls

Once your curls have cooled, remove the clips and gently brush out your curls using a paddle brush. This will create soft, voluminous waves that are perfect for the pin-up look.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Use a comb to create a deep side part, and tuck one side of your hair behind your ear. You can also create a small bouffant at the crown of your head to add some extra volume. Finish off your look with a light mist of hairspray to keep your curls in place.

Tips for Styling Your Hair 1950s

- If you have shorter hair, you can still achieve the pin-up look by creating small curls and waves with a curling iron.
- When curling your hair, use a smaller barrel to create tighter curls, and a larger barrel to create loose waves.
- Don't be afraid to tease your hair at the crown to create extra volume.
- Use bobby pins to secure any loose pieces of hair, and hairspray to keep your curls in place.
- If you want to switch up your look, try adding a bandana or a ribbon to your hair for a pop of color.

FAQs

Q: Can I achieve the pin-up look with naturally straight hair?

A: Yes, you can achieve the pin-up look with straight hair. Just use a curling iron or hot rollers to create the curls and waves.

Q: Can I achieve the pin-up look with short hair?

A: Yes, you can achieve the pin-up look with short hair by creating smaller curls and waves with a curling iron.

Q: How long does it take to achieve the pin-up look?

A: It can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour to achieve the pin-up look, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Hair Care Tips

- Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools on your hair to prevent damage.
- Don't overuse hot tools, as this can cause damage and breakage.
- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
- Limit your use of hairspray, as it can dry out your hair and make it brittle.
- Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to style your hair 1950s, you can channel your inner pin-up girl and rock this retro look. Remember to take care of your hair and use proper hair care techniques to keep your locks healthy and beautiful.
