How To Make Dreadlocks With Natural Hair At Home

Written by Aprilia Jul 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in creating dreadlocks with your natural hair? This guide will provide you with everything you need to know to make dreadlocks at home. From the materials you need to the step-by-step process, we've got you covered!

Table of Contents

  • Materials Needed
  • Preparation
  • Making the Dreadlocks
  • Aftercare
  • Tips for Making Dreadlocks at Home
  • FAQ

Materials Needed

To make dreadlocks at home, you will need the following materials:

  • Comb
  • Dread wax or natural oils such as coconut or olive oil
  • Rubber bands or hair ties
  • Scissors (optional)
  • Patience and time

Preparation

Before you start making your dreadlocks, it's important to prepare your hair. Here are the steps to take:

  1. Wash your hair thoroughly to remove any dirt or product buildup.
  2. Dry your hair completely.
  3. Separate your hair into small sections using a comb.
  4. Apply the dread wax or natural oil to each section to make it easier to form the dreadlocks.

Making the Dreadlocks

Now that your hair is prepped, it's time to start making your dreadlocks:

  1. Take one section of hair and twist it tightly from root to tip.
  2. Roll the twisted section of hair between your palms to create a cylindrical shape.
  3. Use rubber bands or hair ties to secure the dreadlock in place.
  4. Repeat this process with each section of hair until your head is full of dreadlocks.
  5. If desired, use scissors to trim any loose or stray hairs from the dreadlocks.

Aftercare

Once you have made your dreadlocks, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking great. Here are some tips for aftercare:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Retwist your dreadlocks every few weeks to maintain their shape.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your dreadlocks.

Tips for Making Dreadlocks at Home

Making dreadlocks at home can be a time-consuming process, but the end result is worth it. Here are some tips to make the process smoother:

  • Start with clean, dry hair.
  • Use a dread wax or natural oil to make it easier to form the dreadlocks.
  • Take your time and be patient.
  • Experiment with different sizes and shapes of dreadlocks to find what works best for you.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member.

FAQ

How long does it take to make dreadlocks?

The length of time it takes to make dreadlocks depends on the length and thickness of your hair, as well as the size and number of dreadlocks you want. It can take anywhere from a few hours to a full day to make dreadlocks at home.

Do I need to use dread wax to make dreadlocks?

No, you can use natural oils such as coconut or olive oil to make it easier to form the dreadlocks. However, using a dread wax can help the dreadlocks stay in place and maintain their shape.

Can I make dreadlocks with short hair?

Yes, you can make dreadlocks with short hair. However, the shorter your hair is, the smaller your dreadlocks will be.

Can I undo my dreadlocks?

Yes, you can undo your dreadlocks by cutting them off or using a special tool called a dread comb to comb them out. Keep in mind that undoing dreadlocks can be time-consuming and may cause some damage to your hair.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair, especially when you have dreadlocks. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Moisturize your hair with natural oils such as coconut or olive oil.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
