How To Layer Your Hair At Home With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your plain, one-length hair? Do you want to add some dimension and movement to your locks? Layering your hair is a great way to achieve this look. In this article, we'll show you how to layer your hair at home with scissors.

How To

Before we get started, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and clips to section your hair.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it. It's important that your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, part your hair down the middle and then divide it into two sections. Clip each section to keep it out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors vertically and make small snips into the hair. This will give you a more textured, layered look. Move on to the next section, working your way around the bottom layer of your hair.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Unclip the top section of your hair and repeat the process on the middle layer. Remember to take small sections and make vertical snips with your scissors.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, unclip the top layer of your hair and cut it in the same way as the previous layers.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all three layers, it's time to blend them together. Take a comb and run it through your hair, making sure the layers are smooth and seamless.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Stand in front of a mirror and check your hair from all angles. If you see any uneven spots, take your scissors and make small adjustments.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when layering your hair at home:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can appear longer than it really is, so make sure it's completely dry before you start cutting.
  • Be patient. Layering your hair can take some time, especially if you're doing it for the first time.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about layering your hair:

Is it easy to layer your hair at home with scissors?

Yes, layering your hair at home with scissors is relatively easy, especially if you follow our step-by-step guide.

Can I layer my hair if it's curly?

Absolutely! Layering can actually help enhance your natural curls and give them more bounce.

How often should I layer my hair?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much you want to change your look. As a general rule, you should aim to layer your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care

Layering your hair can give it a fresh, new look, but it's important to take care of your locks afterwards. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and shiny:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner that's right for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair. If you must use a blow dryer or flat iron, use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals. Your hair needs nutrients to grow strong and healthy.

Layering your hair at home with scissors is a great way to add some dimension and movement to your locks. With the right tools and our step-by-step guide, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home.
