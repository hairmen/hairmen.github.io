What To Do If Your Hair Falls Out After Bleaching

Written by Luffy Jul 12, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you have bleached your hair, you know it can be a risky move. Bleaching can damage your hair and cause it to fall out. While it may be tempting to try it at home, it is always best to leave it to the professionals. However, if you have already bleached your hair and are experiencing hair loss, don't worry. In this article, we will discuss what to do if your hair falls out after bleaching.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

If you have noticed hair loss after bleaching your hair, the first step is to stop any further damage. This means no more bleaching or coloring your hair until it has fully recovered. You should also avoid using any heat styling tools and minimize any pulling or tugging on your hair.

The next step is to assess the damage. If you have only experienced mild hair loss, you may be able to treat it at home. However, if you have large patches of hair loss or your hair is severely damaged, it is best to consult a professional.

Step by Step

Here are some steps to follow if you have experienced hair loss after bleaching:

  1. Stop any further damage by avoiding bleaching, coloring, and heat styling
  2. Assess the damage and determine if you need professional help
  3. If treating at home, use a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment to nourish your hair
  4. Avoid tight hairstyles or ponytails that can cause further damage
  5. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid any further damage
  6. Take supplements or eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth

Tips

Here are some tips to help you recover from hair loss after bleaching:

  • Be patient - hair growth takes time
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and treatments on your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions
  • Use a silk pillowcase to avoid any pulling or tugging on your hair
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair

FAQ

Can hair loss after bleaching be reversed?

Yes, hair loss after bleaching can be reversed with proper care and treatment.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after bleaching?

It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months for hair to grow back after bleaching, depending on the severity of the damage.

Is it safe to bleach your hair at home?

No, it is not recommended to bleach your hair at home. It is always best to leave it to the professionals to avoid any damage or hair loss.

Hair Care

To prevent hair loss after bleaching, it is important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals and treatments on your hair
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment or hair mask once a week
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Eat a healthy diet and take supplements if needed

Remember, if you experience hair loss after bleaching, it is important to stop any further damage and assess the severity of the damage. If in doubt, consult a professional for the best course of action.
