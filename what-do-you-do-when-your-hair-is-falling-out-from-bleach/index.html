What To Do When Your Hair Is Falling Out From Bleach A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've recently bleached your hair and notice that it's falling out, don't panic. Hair damage from chemical treatments is not uncommon, and there are steps you can take to repair the damage and prevent further hair loss. In this article, we'll explore the causes of hair loss from bleach, how to treat it, and how to care for your hair to prevent future damage.

Table of Contents

  • Causes of Hair Loss from Bleach
  • Treatment for Hair Loss from Bleach
  • Hair Care Tips to Prevent Future Damage
  • FAQs

Causes of Hair Loss from Bleach

Bleaching your hair can cause damage to the hair shaft, which can lead to breakage and hair loss. When you bleach your hair, the bleach penetrates the hair shaft and removes the natural color from the hair. This process can weaken the hair shaft, making it more prone to breakage and hair loss.

Other factors that can contribute to hair loss from bleach include leaving the bleach on for too long, using a bleach that is too strong for your hair type, and not properly conditioning your hair after bleaching.

Treatment for Hair Loss from Bleach

If you're experiencing hair loss from bleach, there are several steps you can take to repair the damage and prevent further hair loss.

1. Stop Bleaching Your Hair

The first step in treating hair loss from bleach is to stop bleaching your hair. Continuing to bleach your hair can cause further damage and hair loss. Give your hair a break from chemical treatments and allow it to recover.

2. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning can help repair the damage to your hair and prevent further breakage. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore moisture to your hair. Look for a conditioning treatment that contains keratin or protein, as these ingredients can help strengthen the hair shaft.

3. Trim Your Hair

If your hair is damaged from bleach, it may be necessary to trim your hair to remove the damaged ends. This can help prevent further breakage and hair loss.

4. Take Hair Supplements

Taking hair supplements can help promote hair growth and strengthen the hair shaft. Look for supplements that contain biotin, vitamin C, and vitamin E, as these nutrients are essential for healthy hair.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Future Damage

Preventing future damage to your hair is important to maintain healthy hair. Here are some tips to help prevent further hair loss from bleach:

1. Use a Lower Volume Developer

If you're going to bleach your hair, use a lower volume developer to prevent damage to the hair shaft. A 20 or 30 volume developer is less damaging than a 40 volume developer.

2. Don't Leave the Bleach on Too Long

Leaving bleach on your hair for too long can cause damage to the hair shaft. Follow the instructions on the bleach kit and don't leave the bleach on for longer than recommended.

3. Use a Hair Mask

Using a hair mask once a week can help moisturize and strengthen your hair. Look for a hair mask that contains natural ingredients like avocado oil or coconut oil.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause further damage to your hair. Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons, or use them on a lower heat setting.

FAQs

Q: Can hair loss from bleach be reversed?

A: Yes, hair loss from bleach can be reversed with proper treatment and hair care.

Q: How long does it take for hair to recover from bleach?

A: It can take several months for hair to fully recover from bleach. The amount of time it takes to recover depends on the severity of the damage.

Q: Is it safe to bleach your hair again after experiencing hair loss?

A: It's best to wait until your hair has fully recovered before bleaching your hair again. Bleaching your hair too soon can cause further damage and hair loss.

In conclusion, hair loss from bleach is a common problem, but it can be treated and prevented with proper hair care. If you're experiencing hair loss from bleach, follow the steps outlined in this article to repair the damage and prevent further hair loss.
