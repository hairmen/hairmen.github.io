How to Wear Your Hair Up with a Round Face

Firstly, it's essential to understand that not every hairstyle suits every face shape. Round faces demand specific hairstyles that help to elongate and slim the face. Wearing your hair up draws focus to your face shape and can be challenging for round faces. However, we've put together some hairstyles that will work well for you.

Wearing your hair up is an excellent way to show off your face shape and highlight your features. The trick is to find the right style that provides balance and structure to your face. Here are some hairstyles that work well for round faces:

1. High Bun

A high bun is an excellent way to elongate your face shape and create an illusion of a more prominent forehead. It also draws focus to your eyes and cheekbones, giving you a more defined look. To achieve this style, gather your hair and tie it up into a high ponytail. Next, twist the ponytail and wrap it around the base to create a bun. Secure the bun with hairpins and hairspray.

2. Half-Up, Half-Down

The half-up, half-down hairstyle is perfect for round faces. It provides balance and structure to your face and highlights your features. To achieve this style, divide your hair into two sections, the top and bottom. Take the top section and tie it up into a high ponytail. Next, curl the bottom section and let it fall freely. This style is ideal for any occasion, from casual to formal.

3. Side Ponytail

A side ponytail is a classic hairstyle that looks great on round faces. It draws focus to your eyes and cheekbones and provides balance to your face. To achieve this style, gather your hair to one side and tie it up into a low ponytail. You can also add some curls to the ends of your ponytail for a more feminine look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to wearing your hair up with a round face:

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

Before we dive into hairstyles, it's essential to determine your face shape. Stand in front of a mirror and examine your face shape. Round faces have a circular shape with soft curves and no sharp angles.

Step 2: Choose Your Hairstyle

Choose a hairstyle that provides balance and structure to your face shape. Opt for hairstyles that elongate your face and draw focus to your eyes and cheekbones.

Step 3: Gather Your Hair

Gather your hair and tie it up into a ponytail or bun. Ensure that the style you choose is comfortable and secure.

Step 4: Add Accessories

Accessorize your hairstyle with hairpins, clips, or headbands to add some flair to your look.

Step 5: Hairspray

Finish off your hairstyle with hairspray to hold it in place and prevent flyaways.

Tips for Wearing Your Hair Up with a Round Face

Here are some tips to keep in mind when wearing your hair up with a round face:

  • Opt for hairstyles that provide volume at the crown of your head.
  • Avoid hairstyles that add width to your face, such as blunt bangs.
  • Accessorize your hairstyle with a headband or hairpins to add some flair to your look.
  • Experiment with different styles and find what works best for you.

FAQs

Q. Can I wear my hair up if I have a round face?

A. Yes, you can wear your hair up if you have a round face. The trick is to find the right style that provides balance and structure to your face.

Q. What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a round face?

A. Avoid hairstyles that add width to your face, such as blunt bangs or a bob that ends at chin level.

Hair Care for Round Face

Maintaining healthy hair is essential to any hairstyle. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Opt for sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners to prevent hair damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on a daily basis.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair growth.

There you have it, a complete guide on how to wear your hair up with a round face. Follow these tips, tricks, and steps to achieve the perfect hairstyle that complements your face shape. Remember, the key is to find the right style that provides balance and structure to your face.
