How To Loosen Tight Naturally Curly Hair

Written by Daniel Oct 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. On one hand, your curls are unique and beautiful, but on the other hand, they can be difficult to manage and style. One common problem for people with curly hair is tight, coiled curls that can be hard to loosen up. If you're struggling with tight curls, don't worry â there are plenty of natural techniques you can use to relax your hair without resorting to harsh chemicals or heat styling tools.

Description: What Causes Tight, Coiled Curls?

Tight, coiled curls are caused by a combination of genetics, hair care practices, and environmental factors. If you have naturally curly hair, you may be predisposed to tight curls due to your genes. However, certain hair care practices can exacerbate this tendency, such as using harsh shampoos or heat styling tools. Environmental factors like humidity can also contribute to tight curls by causing the hair to absorb moisture and shrink up.

How to Loosen Tight Naturally Curly Hair

If you're looking to loosen up your tight curls, there are several natural techniques you can try:

1. Use a Deep Conditioner

One of the best ways to relax tight curls is to use a deep conditioner. Look for a conditioner that is specifically designed for curly hair, and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out. This will help to moisturize and soften your hair, making it easier to manage.

2. Try a Hair Mask

Hair masks are another great way to relax tight curls. Look for a mask that contains natural ingredients like coconut oil or avocado, which are known for their moisturizing properties. Apply the mask to your hair and leave it on for at least 20 minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When you're styling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. This will help to prevent breakage and damage, which can make your curls even tighter. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up, gently detangling each section as you go.

4. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can be damaging to curly hair, and can make your curls even tighter. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, and opt for natural styling techniques instead.

Step by Step: How to Loosen Tight Naturally Curly Hair

If you're ready to try some natural techniques for relaxing your tight curls, here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo. Rinse thoroughly and apply a deep conditioner. Leave the conditioner on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 2: Apply a Hair Mask

After rinsing out your conditioner, apply a hair mask to your damp hair. Leave the mask on for at least 20 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Once you've rinsed out your hair mask, gently detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up, being careful not to pull or tug on your hair.

Step 4: Air Dry Your Hair

Instead of using a blow dryer, let your hair air dry naturally. This will help to prevent damage and breakage, which can make your curls even tighter.

Step 5: Style Your Hair Naturally

Once your hair is dry, style it naturally using techniques like twist outs or bantu knots. These styles will help to stretch out your curls and create a softer, looser look.

Tips for Loosening Tight Naturally Curly Hair

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind as you work to relax your tight curls:

  • Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Protect your hair from humidity by using a moisture-sealing product like a hair oil or serum
  • Avoid using tight hair accessories like elastic bands or scrunchies, which can create tension and make your curls tighter
  • Be patient â it may take some time to see results, but with consistent care and attention, you can loosen up your tight curls naturally

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use heat styling tools to loosen my tight curls?

A: While heat styling tools can temporarily loosen your curls, they can also be damaging to your hair. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, and opt for natural styling techniques instead.

Q: How often should I deep condition my hair?

A: It's best to deep condition your hair once a week, or as needed based on your hair's level of dryness or damage.

Q: Will using a hair mask make my hair greasy?

A: As long as you rinse out the hair mask thoroughly, it should not make your hair greasy. In fact, hair masks can help to moisturize and nourish your hair, leaving it looking soft and healthy.

Hair Care for Naturally Curly Hair

If you have naturally curly hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips for maintaining healthy, beautiful curls:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Deep condition your hair regularly
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Protect your hair from humidity with a moisture-sealing product
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Be patient â it may take some time to find the right hair care routine for your curls, but with persistence and care, you can achieve healthy, beautiful hair.

Loosening up tight, naturally curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and a little patience, it's possible to achieve a softer, more relaxed look. By using natural techniques like deep conditioning, hair masks, and gentle styling techniques, you can maintain healthy, beautiful curls that are easy to manage and style.
