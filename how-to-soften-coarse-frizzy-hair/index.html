How To Soften Coarse Frizzy Hair

Written by Oliver Jun 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have coarse, frizzy hair that always seems to be unmanageable? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people struggle with this type of hair, but there are ways to soften it and make it more manageable. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you achieve soft, silky hair.

Table of Contents

  • How To Soften Coarse Frizzy Hair
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Description

Coarse, frizzy hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, humidity, and heat damage. This type of hair is typically dry and lacks moisture, which makes it difficult to manage and style. Softening coarse frizzy hair involves adding moisture, reducing heat damage, and using the right products to tame frizz.

How to Soften Coarse Frizzy Hair

Softening coarse frizzy hair can be achieved through a variety of methods, including:

1. Deep Conditioning

Deep conditioning is an essential step in softening coarse frizzy hair. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to add moisture and restore your hair's natural oils. Apply the treatment to damp hair, leave it on for 10-15 minutes, and then rinse it out with cool water.

2. Avoid Heat Damage

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Avoid using these tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray before styling. Air-drying your hair is also a great way to avoid heat damage.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help keep your hair moisturized throughout the day. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to damp hair after washing, and then style as usual.

4. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Moisturizing shampoos and conditioners can help add moisture to your hair and reduce frizz. Look for products that contain natural oils like argan oil or coconut oil.

Step by Step

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo. Be sure to rinse thoroughly to remove any product buildup.

Step 2: Apply Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your damp hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the treatment on for 10-15 minutes, and then rinse it out with cool water.

Step 3: Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your damp hair, focusing on the ends. Comb through your hair to distribute the product evenly.

Step 4: Air-Dry Your Hair

Air-drying your hair is the best way to avoid heat damage. If you need to use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment to reduce frizz.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you soften coarse frizzy hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Try using a hair mask once a week to add extra moisture to your hair.

FAQ

How often should I deep condition my hair?

It's recommended to deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

Can I still straighten my hair if it's coarse and frizzy?

Absolutely! Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using a high heat setting.

What's the best way to reduce frizz?

The best way to reduce frizz is to keep your hair moisturized and avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.

Hair Care

Softening coarse frizzy hair requires a good hair care routine. Be sure to use moisturizing shampoos and conditioners, deep condition once a week, and use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized throughout the day. Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors. With the right care, you can achieve soft, silky hair that's easy to manage.
