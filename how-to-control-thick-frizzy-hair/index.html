How To Control Thick Frizzy Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Jun 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read
If you have thick, frizzy hair, you know how hard it can be to manage. It's a constant battle to keep your hair looking sleek and smooth, and it can be frustrating when nothing seems to work. Luckily, there are some tips and tricks that can help you control your hair and achieve the look you want. In this article, we'll share some of our favorite tips for managing thick, frizzy hair.

Description

Thick frizzy hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a sleek, smooth look. In this article, we'll share tips for controlling frizz, taming thick hair, and achieving a beautiful, healthy mane.

How to Control Thick Frizzy Hair

If you have thick, frizzy hair, you may feel like you're fighting a losing battle. But don't give up! With the right techniques and products, you can achieve a sleek, smooth look. Here are some tips for controlling your hair:

1. Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

The right shampoo and conditioner can make a big difference in the appearance of your hair. Look for products that are designed to fight frizz and tame thick hair. Avoid products that contain sulfates, as these can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

2. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help keep your hair moisturized and prevent frizz. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing and conditioning, and comb it through with a wide-toothed comb.

3. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and frizzy. Try washing your hair every other day or every three days, and use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

4. Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

A wide-toothed comb is gentler on your hair than a brush, and it can help prevent breakage and frizz. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair after washing, and comb it through again before styling.

5. Use a Heat Protectant

If you use heat styling tools like a flat iron or curling wand, be sure to use a heat protectant spray or cream. This will help protect your hair from damage and prevent frizz.

6. Avoid Touching Your Hair Too Much

The more you touch your hair, the more you'll disrupt the natural oils and cause frizz. Avoid touching your hair too much, and try not to brush or comb it throughout the day.

7. Use a Microfiber Towel

A microfiber towel is gentler on your hair than a regular towel, and it can help prevent frizz. Use a microfiber towel to gently blot your hair after washing, and avoid rubbing it vigorously.

8. Try a Hair Mask

A hair mask can help nourish and moisturize your hair, leaving it soft and smooth. Look for a hair mask that's designed for thick, frizzy hair, and use it once a week or as needed.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to controlling thick frizzy hair:
  1. Wash your hair with a frizz-fighting shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing and conditioning.
  3. Detangle your hair with a wide-toothed comb.
  4. Blot your hair gently with a microfiber towel.
  5. Apply a heat protectant spray or cream if you plan to use heat styling tools.
  6. Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day.
  7. Use a hair mask once a week or as needed to nourish and moisturize your hair.

Tips for Managing Thick Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing thick frizzy hair:
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as these can dry out your hair.
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal parts, as these can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Try sleeping on a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

FAQs

Q. What causes frizzy hair?

A. Frizzy hair is caused by a lack of moisture in the hair. This can be due to genetics, environmental factors, or the use of harsh hair products.

Q. How can I prevent frizz?

A. To prevent frizz, use a frizz-fighting shampoo and conditioner, apply a leave-in conditioner after washing, and avoid using hot water when washing your hair. Use a microfiber towel to blot your hair dry, and avoid touching it too much throughout the day.

Q. Can I still use heat styling tools on my hair?

A. Yes, you can still use heat styling tools on your hair, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray or cream to protect your hair from damage.

Hair Care for Thick Frizzy Hair

If you have thick frizzy hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for hair care:
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh hair products that can dry out your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid harsh chemical treatments like perms and relaxers.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.

Conclusion

Controlling thick frizzy hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a sleek, smooth look. Use a frizz-fighting shampoo and conditioner, apply a leave-in conditioner, and avoid using hot water when washing your hair. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, and blot it dry with a microfiber towel. Use a hair mask once a week or as needed to nourish and moisturize your hair. And remember to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
