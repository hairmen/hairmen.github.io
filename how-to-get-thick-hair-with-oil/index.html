How To Get Thick Hair With Oil A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're someone who struggles with thin or limp hair, you've probably tried countless products and treatments in search of a solution. However, one of the most effective ways to boost hair thickness and overall health is by incorporating oils into your hair care routine. In this article, we'll explore the top oils for thickening hair, how to use them, and other tips for achieving luscious locks.

  • Types of Oils for Thick Hair
  • How to Use Oil for Thick Hair
  • Additional Tips for Thickening Hair

Types of Oils for Thick Hair

There are several types of oils that are particularly effective for thickening hair:

Coconut Oil

Coconut oil is a popular choice for hair care due to its ability to penetrate the hair shaft and strengthen strands from within. It also contains lauric acid, which helps to prevent breakage and split ends.

Castor Oil

Castor oil is a thick, viscous oil that is rich in nutrients like vitamin E and fatty acids. It's particularly effective for promoting hair growth and thickening hair.

Argan Oil

Argan oil is a lightweight oil that is packed with antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals. It helps to protect hair from damage and breakage, while also adding shine and moisture.

Jojoba Oil

Jojoba oil is similar in composition to the natural oils produced by the scalp, making it a great choice for those with oily hair. It helps to unclog hair follicles and promote healthy hair growth.

How to Use Oil for Thick Hair

Now that you know which oils to use, it's important to know how to use them for the best results:

Step 1: Choose Your Oil

First, choose the oil(s) that are best suited to your hair type and concerns. You can use a single oil, or mix and match oils for a customized blend.

Step 2: Apply the Oil

Next, apply the oil to your hair and scalp. Use your fingers to massage it in, focusing on the roots and ends. Be sure to coat your hair evenly, but avoid applying too much oil, as this can weigh down your hair.

Step 3: Leave the Oil In

Leave the oil in your hair for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for a more intense treatment. You can cover your hair with a shower cap or towel to prevent the oil from getting on your pillow.

Step 4: Rinse and Shampoo

Finally, rinse the oil out of your hair with warm water and shampoo as usual. You may need to shampoo twice to fully remove the oil.

Additional Tips for Thickening Hair

In addition to using oils, there are other things you can do to promote thicker, healthier hair:

Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals is crucial for healthy hair. Be sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, lean protein, and healthy fats.

Reduce Heat Styling

Excessive heat styling can damage hair and lead to breakage. Try to limit your use of hot tools and always use a heat protectant spray.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails can cause tension on the hair follicles, leading to hair loss over time. Opt for looser styles whenever possible.

FAQs

Can I use oil on my hair every day?

While using oil on your hair every day is not necessary, it can be beneficial for some people. If you have dry or damaged hair, you may want to use oil more frequently to help moisturize and nourish your strands.

How long does it take to see results from using oil?

Results can vary depending on the individual and the oil used. However, with consistent use over time, you should start to see improvements in hair thickness and overall health within a few weeks to a few months.

Hair Care Routine for Thick Hair

To maintain thick, healthy hair, it's important to have a consistent hair care routine. Here are some tips for a hair care routine that promotes thickness and overall hair health:

Wash your hair regularly

Wash your hair regularly with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to remove buildup and keep your scalp healthy.

Condition your hair

Use a conditioner that is specifically formulated for your hair type to nourish and moisturize your strands.

Use a wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to prevent breakage.

Avoid brushing wet hair

Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so avoid brushing your hair when it's wet. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle.

Protect your hair from the sun

UV rays can damage hair and lead to breakage, so be sure to wear a hat or use a hair protectant spray when spending time outdoors.

By following these tips and incorporating oils into your hair care routine, you can achieve thicker, healthier hair in no time!
