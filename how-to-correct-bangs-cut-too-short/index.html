How To Correct Bangs Cut Too Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've ever taken a pair of scissors to your own hair, you know how easy it is to get carried away. And if you've ever cut your own bangs, you know that a mistake can be particularly unforgiving. But don't worry - if you've cut your bangs too short, there are a few things you can do to fix the problem.

Description

When bangs are cut too short, they can be a major source of stress and embarrassment. But there's no need to panic - there are plenty of ways to fix the problem. Whether you want to grow your bangs out or simply style them differently, there are steps you can take to get your hair looking the way you want it to.

How to Fix Bangs Cut Too Short

Step 1: Assess the Damage

The first step in fixing short bangs is to assess the damage. Take a look at your bangs in the mirror and determine just how short they are. If they're only slightly shorter than you intended, you may be able to style them in a way that hides the mistake. If they're significantly shorter, you may need to grow them out.

Step 2: Style Your Bangs

If your bangs are only slightly too short, you may be able to style them in a way that hides the mistake. Try blow-drying your bangs with a round brush, sweeping them to the side, or using a hair straightener to create a sleek look. You can also try adding volume to your bangs with a volumizing spray or mousse.

Step 3: Grow Your Bangs Out

If your bangs are significantly shorter than you intended, you may need to grow them out. This can be a frustrating process, but it's the best way to get your hair back to its original length. In the meantime, you can try using bobby pins or headbands to keep your bangs out of your face.

Step 4: Get a Haircut

If you're really unhappy with your bangs, you can always get a haircut. A stylist can help you find a new style that works with your short bangs, or they can cut your hair to a length that allows your bangs to blend in with the rest of your hair.

Tips for Growing Out Bangs

Growing out your bangs can be a long and frustrating process, but there are a few things you can do to make it easier:

  • Use hair accessories like headbands and bobby pins to keep your bangs out of your face.
  • Try parting your hair differently to take the focus off your bangs.
  • Use hair products like mousse or gel to add volume to your hair and help it blend with your bangs.
  • Be patient - growing out your bangs takes time, but it's worth it in the end.

FAQ

Can I fix my bangs myself?

If your bangs are only slightly too short, you may be able to style them in a way that hides the mistake. But if they're significantly shorter, you may need to grow them out or get a haircut. If you're unsure, it's best to consult a stylist.

How long does it take to grow out bangs?

It depends on how short your bangs are and how quickly your hair grows. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month. So if your bangs are an inch too short, it may take around two months to grow them out.

Can I speed up the process of growing out my bangs?

There's no surefire way to make your hair grow faster, but there are a few things you can do to keep your hair healthy and promote growth. Eating a healthy diet, getting enough sleep, and avoiding heat styling tools can all help your hair grow faster.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Use a heat protectant spray if you do use heat styling tools.
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to fix your short bangs and keep your hair looking its best.
