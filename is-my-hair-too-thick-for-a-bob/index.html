Is My Hair Too Thick For A Bob

Written by Frank Jul 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're considering getting a bob haircut but have thick hair, you may be wondering if your hair is too thick for this style. While a bob can be flattering on anyone, it's important to consider your hair type and texture before making a drastic change to your hair. In this article, we'll explore how to determine if your hair is too thick for a bob, and provide tips for getting the perfect bob for your hair type.

Description: Understanding Hair Texture and Thickness

Before we dive into whether or not your hair is too thick for a bob, it's important to understand hair texture and thickness. Hair texture refers to the diameter of each individual strand of hair, while hair thickness refers to the number of hair strands on your head. It's possible to have thick hair with fine texture, or thin hair with coarse texture. Knowing your hair type and texture can help you make informed decisions about your hair care and styling.

How to Determine if Your Hair is Too Thick for a Bob

If you're still unsure whether or not your hair is too thick for a bob, there are a few ways to determine your hair's thickness. One simple way is to gather all of your hair into a ponytail and measure the circumference of the ponytail. If your ponytail is thicker than a certain measurement, a bob may not be the best option for you. Another way to determine your hair's thickness is to talk to your stylist, who can assess your hair and recommend the best haircut for your hair type.

Step by Step Guide to Getting a Bob with Thick Hair

If you've determined that your hair is not too thick for a bob, here's a step-by-step guide to getting the perfect bob for thick hair:

Step 1: Consult with Your Stylist

Before getting a bob, consult with your stylist to determine the best length and style for your hair type and face shape. Your stylist can also recommend products and techniques to help make your hair more manageable.

Step 2: Consider Layering

Layering can help thin out thick hair and make it more manageable. Ask your stylist to add layers to your bob to help reduce bulk and add movement.

Step 3: Use the Right Products

Using the right products can make a big difference in managing thick hair. Look for products that are designed for thick hair, such as volumizing shampoos and conditioners, and use a deep conditioning treatment regularly to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Step 4: Style Your Bob

Once you've gotten your bob, it's important to style it properly to keep it looking great. Use a round brush and blow dryer to add volume and shape to your hair, and consider using a flat iron or curling iron to add texture and dimension.

Tips for Maintaining a Bob with Thick Hair

Maintaining a bob with thick hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, it can be done. Here are a few tips for keeping your bob looking great:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove buildup and keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it more difficult to manage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help smooth and tame frizz.
  • Get regular trims to keep your bob looking neat and tidy.

FAQ: Common Questions about Bobs and Thick Hair

Q: Can I get a bob if I have curly hair?

A: Yes! Bobs can look great on curly hair. Just make sure to work with a stylist who is experienced with cutting curly hair, and ask for layers to help reduce bulk.

Q: Will a bob make my thick hair look even thicker?

A: It's possible, but with the right cut and styling techniques, a bob can actually help thin out thick hair and make it more manageable.

Q: How often should I get my bob trimmed?

A: It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your bob looking neat and tidy.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Regardless of whether or not you decide to get a bob, taking care of thick hair requires some extra effort. Here are a few tips for keeping your hair healthy and manageable:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to avoid tugging and pulling on your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment regularly to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

In conclusion, getting a bob with thick hair is possible, but it's important to consider your hair type and texture before making a drastic change to your hair. By consulting with your stylist, using the right products and techniques, and maintaining a healthy hair care routine, you can achieve a stylish and manageable bob that flatters your features.
