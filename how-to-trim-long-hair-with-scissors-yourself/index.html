How To Trim Long Hair With Scissors Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon every time you need a trim? Trimming long hair can seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and technique, it's actually quite simple. In this article, we'll show you how to trim long hair with scissors yourself, so you can save money and keep your locks looking healthy and beautiful.

Description

Long hair may be beautiful, but it requires regular maintenance to keep it healthy and looking its best. Regular trims are essential to prevent split ends and breakage, but salon appointments can be expensive and time-consuming. By learning how to trim long hair with scissors yourself, you can save money and time while still maintaining your hair's health and beauty.

How-To: Trim Long Hair with Scissors Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, gather the tools you'll need for your DIY haircut. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips or elastics to section your hair. Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean, as dull or dirty scissors can damage your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see any split ends or damaged areas that need to be trimmed. Brush your hair thoroughly to remove any tangles, and part it in the middle or wherever you normally wear your part.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, create sections in your hair that are about an inch wide. Use hair clips or elastics to hold each section in place while you work on the others.

Step 4: Start Trimming

Take one section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and slide your fingers down to the point where you want to trim. Use your scissors to cut off the desired length, being careful not to cut too much at once.

Repeat this process with each section of hair, working your way around your head. Remember to take small sections and cut slowly, as it's easier to trim more hair later than to fix a mistake.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've finished trimming, check your work in a mirror to make sure everything looks even. If you spot any uneven areas, use your scissors to make small adjustments until everything looks just right.

Tips for Trimming Long Hair with Scissors Yourself

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the perfect DIY haircut:

  • Start by trimming less than you think you need. You can always trim more later, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Take breaks if you need them. Trimming your own hair can be tiring and time-consuming, so don't be afraid to take a break and come back to it later.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair, so make sure your scissors are sharp and clean before you start.
  • Trim your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can be deceiving, as it appears longer than it really is. By trimming your hair when it's dry, you can get a more accurate idea of how much you need to cut.
  • Don't trim too much at once. It's better to trim a little bit at a time than to cut off too much and regret it later.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about trimming long hair with scissors yourself:

Is it easy to trim long hair with scissors yourself?

With the right tools and technique, trimming long hair with scissors is relatively easy. It may take a bit of practice to get it just right, but once you've got the hang of it, you'll be able to save time and money by doing your own trims.

What kind of scissors should I use?

It's important to use a pair of sharp, clean scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair. Don't use regular scissors, as they can damage your hair and cause split ends.

How often should I trim my long hair?

It's recommended that you trim your long hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage. However, the frequency of your trims may depend on your hair type and lifestyle.

Hair Care Tips for Long Hair

Trimming your hair is just one part of maintaining its health and beauty. Here are a few more tips for caring for your long hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back too tightly, as this can cause breakage and damage.

By following these tips and learning how to trim long hair with scissors yourself, you can keep your locks looking beautiful and healthy without breaking the bank.
