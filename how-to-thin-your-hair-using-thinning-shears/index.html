How To Thin Your Hair Using Thinning Shears

Written by William Oct 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning shears are an excellent tool for those who have thick hair and want to reduce the volume without losing the length. In this article, we will guide you on how to thin your hair using thinning shears. We will cover everything from how to use them to various tips and tricks for a perfect outcome.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors with teeth that are designed to thin out hair without removing length. They have a unique blade that cuts some hair strands while leaving others unaffected, creating a natural-looking result. Thinning shears are ideal for people with thick hair who want to reduce the volume or for those who have a bulky hairstyle that needs taming.

How To

Before you start thinning your hair, make sure that it is clean and dry. Wet hair tends to stretch, making it difficult to judge how much hair you are removing. Follow these steps to thin your hair using thinning shears:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into manageable parts. Use hair clips to separate the hair that you want to thin out from the rest. This will make it easier to work on one section at a time.

Step 2: Hold the Thinning Shears Correctly

Hold the thinning shears at a slight angle and position them about an inch or two away from your scalp. Make sure that you are holding them correctly to avoid any accidents. The teeth of the shears should be facing upwards, and the blades should be pointing downwards.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Start cutting the hair by opening and closing the shears while moving them downwards. Make sure that you are only cutting a few strands at a time. It's better to cut less hair initially and go back later if you need to cut more. You can always trim more hair, but you can't add it back once it's cut.

Step 4: Repeat the Process

Repeat the process on each section of your hair until you've achieved the desired level of thinning. Be careful not to overdo it, as thinning shears can take out a lot of hair quickly. It's a good idea to take breaks in between sections to assess the progress and avoid over-thinning.

Tips

Here are some tips to get the best results when using thinning shears:

  • Use sharp thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair
  • Don't use thinning shears on wet hair
  • Be patient and take your time
  • Use a mirror to check the progress from different angles
  • Experiment with different techniques to find the one that works best for you

FAQ

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

Thinning shears are ideal for thick hair, but they can be used on all hair types. However, if you have thin hair, be careful not to over-thin it, as it can make it look even thinner.

Do thinning shears damage hair?

If used correctly, thinning shears won't damage your hair. However, it's crucial to use sharp shears and not to overdo it. Thinning shears can be damaging if they're dull or used excessively.

How often should you thin your hair?

It's recommended to thin your hair every six to eight weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows and how much volume you want to reduce.

Hair Care

After thinning your hair, make sure to take good care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type. Avoid using heat styling tools frequently, as they can damage your hair. Apply a hair mask regularly to nourish your hair and keep it moisturized.

Thinning your hair using thinning shears can give you a more manageable and natural-looking hairstyle. Follow these steps, tips, and tricks to achieve the best results. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful.
