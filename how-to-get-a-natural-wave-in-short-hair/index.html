How To Get A Natural Wave In Short Hair

Written by Luffy Jun 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of having the same straight, boring short hair? Do you want to add some texture and volume to your locks without using heat tools or chemicals? Luckily, getting a natural wave in short hair is easier than you think. In this article, we'll guide you through some simple steps and tips on how to achieve a beautiful, effortless wave in your short hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get a Natural Wave in Short Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Natural Wave
  • FAQs

How to Get a Natural Wave in Short Hair

The key to getting a natural wave in short hair is to work with what you have. You don't need to have long hair or use heat tools to achieve a beautiful wave. Here's how to do it:

1. Start with clean, damp hair

Before styling your hair, make sure it's clean and damp. You can either wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner or just wet it with water. Don't towel dry your hair too much, as you want it to be damp but not dripping wet.

2. Apply a wave-enhancing product

Using a wave-enhancing product will help enhance your natural waves and add texture to your hair. You can use a mousse, gel, or sea salt spray. Apply a dime-sized amount of the product to your palms and work it through your hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends.

3. Create waves with your fingers

Using your fingers, scrunch your hair in an upward motion to create waves. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends. Make sure to scrunch your hair in small sections to create more defined waves.

4. Let your hair air dry

Once you've created your waves, let your hair air dry. Avoid using a hair dryer or any heat styling tools, as this can damage your hair and make it look frizzy. If you're in a hurry, you can use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer, but make sure to use low heat and speed settings.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get a natural wave in short hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner or wet it with water.

Step 2: Apply a wave-enhancing product to your damp hair.

Step 3: Scrunch your hair in an upward motion to create waves.

Step 4: Let your hair air dry.

Tips for Maintaining Your Natural Wave

Now that you've created your natural wave, here are some tips on how to maintain it:

  • Don't brush or comb your hair too much, as this can break up your waves and create frizz.
  • Avoid using hair styling products with alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and make it look dull.
  • Sleep with your hair in a loose bun or braid to maintain your waves overnight.
  • If your waves start to fall flat, you can use a sea salt spray or wave-enhancing product to refresh them.

FAQs

Q: Can I get a natural wave in short hair without using any products?

A: Yes, you can. However, using a wave-enhancing product will help enhance your natural waves and add more texture to your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my hair if I have natural waves?

A: It depends on your hair type and how oily your scalp gets. Generally, you can wash your hair every 2-3 days to maintain your natural waves.

Hair Care for Natural Waves

To keep your natural waves looking their best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from getting too dry.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and make it look frizzy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep your hair healthy.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote hair growth and keep your hair looking shiny and healthy.

With these simple steps and tips, you can easily get a natural wave in your short hair. Embrace your natural hair texture and experiment with different wave-enhancing products to find what works best for you. Happy styling!
