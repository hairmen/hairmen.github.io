Are Layers A Good Idea For Thick Hair

Written by Aprilia Nov 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Thick hair can be both a blessing and a curse. On one hand, you have a lot of volume and body to work with. On the other hand, it can be challenging to style and maintain. One solution that many people turn to is layers. But are layers a good idea for thick hair? Let's explore.

  2. How To Get Layers In Thick Hair
  3. Step By Step Guide

Description

Layers can be a great way to add texture and movement to thick hair. By removing some of the weight and bulk, layers can make it easier to style and manage your hair. They can also give the illusion of more volume and help create a more flattering shape.

However, layers may not be the best choice for everyone. If your hair is very curly or prone to frizz, layers may exacerbate these issues. Additionally, if you have a lot of natural volume or a strong wave pattern, layers may cause your hair to look too poofy or triangular.

How To Get Layers In Thick Hair

If you've decided that layers are a good option for your thick hair, there are a few different ways to achieve the look. Here are some of the most common methods:

1. Long Layers

Long layers are a good choice if you want to maintain as much length as possible while still adding some texture. Your stylist will typically cut the layers starting around chin-length or lower, and gradually blend them into the rest of your hair.

2. Short Layers

Short layers are a more dramatic option that can add a lot of movement and volume to your hair. Your stylist will cut the layers closer to the crown of your head and create a more graduated, stacked effect.

3. Face-Framing Layers

Face-framing layers are a great way to add dimension and draw attention to your features. Your stylist will cut shorter layers around your face, which can help soften your features and create a more flattering shape.

Step By Step Guide

If you're planning to get layers in your thick hair, here's what you can expect during the process:

1. Consultation

Your stylist will start by discussing your hair goals and preferences with you. They may ask to see pictures of styles you like, or recommend options based on your face shape and hair texture.

2. Shampoo and Condition

Your stylist will shampoo and condition your hair to prepare it for cutting. They may also use a detangling spray or leave-in conditioner to make your hair more manageable.

3. Section Hair

Your stylist will divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to cut the layers evenly and avoid any choppy or uneven spots.

4. Cut Layers

Your stylist will use scissors or a razor to cut the layers into your hair. They will typically start with a small section at the back of your head and work their way forward, checking to make sure the layers are even as they go.

5. Style and Finish

Once the layers are in place, your stylist will style your hair using a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron. They may also apply a finishing spray or serum to add shine and hold.

Tips For Maintaining Layers In Thick Hair

Once you have layers in your thick hair, there are a few things you can do to keep them looking great:

1. Use the Right Products

Look for styling products that are lightweight and won't weigh down your hair. Avoid heavy oils or creams that can make your hair look greasy or flat.

2. Blow Dry Properly

When blow drying your hair, use a round brush to add volume and lift at the roots. Aim the nozzle of your blow dryer downward to help smooth the hair cuticle and reduce frizz.

3. Schedule Regular Trims

To keep your layers looking fresh, it's important to get regular trims every 6-8 weeks. This will help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and full.

FAQs

Q. Will layers make my hair look thinner?

A. Layers can actually make your hair look thicker by adding volume and texture. However, if your hair is already very fine or thin, too many layers may make it look stringy or wispy.

Q. Can I still wear my hair straight with layers?

A. Absolutely! Layers can actually make your hair easier to straighten by removing some of the bulk and weight. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray and a flat iron with adjustable temperature settings.

Q. Will layers work with any face shape?

A. Layers can be customized to flatter any face shape. Your stylist can help you choose the right length and placement to enhance your features and complement your bone structure.

Hair Care Tips For Thick Hair

In addition to getting layers, there are a few other things you can do to care for your thick hair:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Thick hair can be prone to dryness and damage, so it's important to use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.

2. Avoid Over-Shampooing

While it's important to keep your hair clean, washing it too often can strip away natural oils and leave it dry and brittle. Aim to shampoo every 2-3 days, or use a dry shampoo in between washes.

3. Protect Your Hair From Heat Damage

When using hot styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage and breakage. You can also air dry your hair whenever possible to reduce the amount of heat you expose it to.

In conclusion, layers can be a great option for adding texture and manageability to thick hair. Just be sure to consult with your stylist to find the right length and placement for your hair type and face shape.
