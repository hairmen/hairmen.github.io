How To Give Asian Hair Texture A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 07, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have straight, thick Asian hair, you may be looking for ways to add texture and definition to your locks. Fortunately, there are several techniques and products that can help you achieve the desired look. In this article, we’ll explain how to give Asian hair texture, including the best products to use, how to style your hair, and how to care for it properly.

Table of Contents

  • How to Give Asian Hair Texture
  • Best Products for Asian Hair Texture
  • Styling Tips for Asian Hair
  • Hair Care for Asian Hair
  • FAQs

How to Give Asian Hair Texture

The key to adding texture to Asian hair is to create movement and dimension. This can be achieved in several ways:

1. Layering

Layering your hair can help create the illusion of movement and volume. A skilled hairstylist can add layers that complement your face shape and hair texture. Opt for long layers to add volume and movement without sacrificing length.

2. Texturizing

Texturizing involves using scissors or a razor to cut small pieces of hair at different lengths, creating a choppy or tousled look. This technique can add texture and definition to straight, thick hair.

3. Adding Waves or Curls

Adding waves or curls to your hair can help create the illusion of texture and movement. You can use a curling iron or hair rollers to create loose waves or curls. Alternatively, you can braid your hair while it’s damp and let it dry overnight for natural-looking waves.

Best Products for Asian Hair Texture

To achieve textured, defined hair, you’ll need the right products. Here are some of the best products for Asian hair texture:

1. Sea Salt Spray

Sea salt spray can help add texture and volume to your hair. Simply spritz it onto damp hair and scrunch your hair with your hands to create natural-looking waves.

2. Texturizing Spray

Texturizing spray can help create a tousled, messy look. Spray it onto dry hair and scrunch your hair with your hands to create texture.

3. Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo can help add volume and texture to limp or oily hair. Spray it onto your roots and massage it in with your fingers to absorb excess oil and add volume.

Styling Tips for Asian Hair

Once you’ve layered and texturized your hair, it’s time to style it. Here are some tips for styling Asian hair:

1. Blow-Dry Your Hair

Blow-drying your hair can help create volume and movement. Use a round brush to lift your roots and create volume, and blow-dry your hair in the opposite direction of your natural part to create more volume.

2. Use a Styling Product

Once your hair is dry, use a styling product to create texture and definition. Apply a small amount of texturizing cream or pomade to your fingertips and work it through your hair.

3. Experiment with Braids

Braids can help create natural-looking waves and texture. Braid your hair while it’s damp and let it dry overnight for beachy waves. Alternatively, braid your hair and use a flat iron to create defined, textured waves.

Hair Care for Asian Hair

To maintain healthy, textured hair, it’s important to take good care of it. Here are some tips for caring for Asian hair:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Asian hair tends to be thicker and coarser than other hair types, which can make it prone to dryness and breakage. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

2. Avoid Overwashing Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and damage. Try to wash your hair every other day or every third day, depending on your hair type and lifestyle.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat Damage

Using heat styling tools like curling irons and flat irons can damage your hair over time. To protect your hair from heat damage, use a heat protectant spray before styling your hair, and avoid using high heat settings.

FAQs

Q: Can I add texture to my Asian hair without cutting it?

A: Yes! You can add texture to your hair by adding waves or curls, using styling products, or experimenting with braids.

Q: What kind of haircut is best for adding texture to Asian hair?

A: A layered haircut with long layers is best for adding volume and movement to Asian hair.

Q: How often should I get my hair cut to maintain texture?

A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, you should get your hair cut every 6-8 weeks to maintain your desired length and shape.

Q: What’s the best way to style Asian hair for a textured, tousled look?

A: Use a texturizing spray or sea salt spray to create volume and texture, then scrunch your hair with your hands to create a tousled, messy look.

Q: Can I use hair extensions to add texture to my Asian hair?

A: Yes! Hair extensions can be a great way to add volume and length to your hair, and there are many options available for adding texture and definition to your locks.

By following these tips and techniques, you can add texture and definition to your Asian hair for a fresh, modern look. Remember to use the right products, style your hair properly, and care for it regularly to maintain healthy, beautiful locks.
