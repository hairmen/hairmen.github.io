Revive Your Old Wig A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jun 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do you have an old wig that you haven't worn in a while? Are you wondering how to bring it back to life? Look no further! In this article, we'll show you how to revive your old wig and make it look brand new again. We'll cover everything from cleaning and styling to storage and maintenance. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Cleaning Your Wig
  • Detangling Your Wig
  • Restyling Your Wig
  • Storing Your Wig
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips

Cleaning Your Wig

The first step in reviving your old wig is to clean it. Over time, wigs can accumulate dirt, oil, and other debris, which can cause them to look dull and lifeless. To clean your wig, you'll need a few basic supplies:

  • Wig shampoo
  • Conditioner
  • Detangling brush
  • Cool water

Start by filling a sink or basin with cool water. Add a small amount of wig shampoo and gently swish your wig around in the water. Be careful not to scrub or rub the hair too hard, as this can cause damage. Rinse the wig thoroughly with cool water, making sure to remove all the shampoo.

Next, apply a small amount of conditioner to the wig, avoiding the roots. Let the conditioner sit for a few minutes, then rinse it out with cool water. Use your detangling brush to gently comb through the hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Once your wig is clean and detangled, gently squeeze out any excess water and lay the wig flat to dry on a towel. Never use a hair dryer or other heat source to dry your wig, as this can cause damage.

Detangling Your Wig

If your wig is particularly tangled, you may need to spend some extra time detangling it. Start by using your fingers to gently separate any large knots or tangles. Then, use your detangling brush to carefully comb through the hair, working from the ends to the roots.

If you encounter a stubborn tangle, try spraying a small amount of detangling spray onto the knot and gently working it out with your fingers or brush. Be patient and gentle, as forcing the tangle can cause damage to the hair.

Restyling Your Wig

Once your wig is clean and detangled, you may want to restyle it to give it a fresh look. Depending on the type of wig you have, there are different ways to restyle it.

If you have a synthetic wig, you can use a low heat setting on a hair dryer to reshape the hair. Simply hold the hair dryer a few inches away from the wig and use a round brush to style the hair as desired.

If you have a human hair wig, you can style it just like your own hair. Use a curling iron or flat iron to create waves or straighten the hair, or use rollers to add volume and body.

Storing Your Wig

Proper storage is key to keeping your wig looking its best. When you're not wearing your wig, store it on a wig stand or mannequin head to help maintain its shape. Avoid storing your wig in direct sunlight or near sources of heat, as this can cause the hair to dry out and become damaged.

If you're traveling with your wig, use a wig travel case or box to protect it from damage. Pack the wig carefully and avoid crushing or bending the hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I clean my wig?

It's generally recommended to clean your wig every 4-6 weeks, depending on how often you wear it and how much product you use. If your wig starts to look dull or dirty, it's time for a cleaning.

Can I use regular shampoo on my wig?

No, regular shampoo can be too harsh for wigs and can cause damage. Use a wig-specific shampoo that's designed to gently clean the hair without causing damage.

Can I use heat styling tools on my wig?

If you have a synthetic wig, be careful when using heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair. If you have a human hair wig, you can use heat styling tools just like you would on your own hair.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your wig looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Avoid using too much product, as this can cause buildup and make the hair look dull.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or detangling brush to gently comb through the hair.
  • Protect your wig from direct sunlight and heat sources.
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to help maintain its shape.

With these tips and tricks, you can revive your old wig and make it look brand new again. Whether you're wearing your wig for a special occasion or just for fun, taking good care of it will ensure that it looks great every time you wear it.
