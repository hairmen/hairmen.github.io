Why Is My Hair Getting Thicker As I Age

Written by Oliver Nov 11, 2023 Â· 3 min read

As we age, we often notice changes in our body that we never expected. One of the most surprising changes for many people is the fact that their hair is getting thicker. This can be confusing, especially if you've always believed that hair naturally thins out as you get older. In this article, we'll explore why your hair may be getting thicker and what you can do about it.

What Causes Hair to Thicken as You Age?

There are several reasons why your hair may be getting thicker as you age. One of the main reasons is hormonal changes. As we get older, our bodies produce less estrogen and testosterone. This can cause hair to become thinner and more brittle. However, in some cases, the opposite can happen. For example, some women may experience an increase in testosterone levels as they age. This can cause their hair to become thicker and more resilient.

Another possible reason for hair thickening is changes in your diet. As we age, our bodies become less efficient at absorbing nutrients from food. This can lead to deficiencies in certain vitamins and minerals that are essential for healthy hair growth. In some cases, taking supplements or changing your diet can help to improve the health of your hair.

How to Care for Thickening Hair

If you've noticed that your hair is getting thicker as you age, there are several things you can do to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

Use a Gentle Shampoo

When washing your hair, use a gentle shampoo that won't strip away natural oils. Look for a product that is specifically designed for thick or coarse hair.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can damage hair and make it more prone to breakage. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using heat-free styling methods.

Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean protein can help to provide your hair with the nutrients it needs to stay healthy and strong.

FAQs

Is it normal for hair to get thicker as you age?

Yes, it is normal for hair to get thicker as you age. This is often due to hormonal changes that occur in the body.

Can stress cause hair to thicken?

No, stress does not typically cause hair to thicken. In fact, stress is more likely to cause hair loss or thinning.

Conclusion

If you've noticed that your hair is getting thicker as you age, don't panic. This is a natural part of the aging process for many people. However, if you're concerned about the health of your hair, there are steps you can take to keep it strong and healthy. By using a gentle shampoo, avoiding heat styling, and eating a balanced diet, you can help to promote healthy hair growth and keep your locks looking their best.
