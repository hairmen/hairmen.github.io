What To Say When Someone Gets A New Haircut Tips And Faqs

Written by Bardi Sep 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Getting a new haircut can be a big deal for some people. It can give them a boost of confidence, make them feel refreshed, and even change their outlook on life. As a friend, family member, or colleague, it's important to know what to say when someone gets a new haircut. In this article, we'll give you tips and FAQs on how to compliment someone's new 'do without sounding awkward or insincere.

How to Compliment Someone's New Haircut

When someone gets a new haircut, it's always nice to acknowledge and compliment it. Here are some ways to express your admiration:

1. Be Specific

Instead of just saying "I like your haircut," be specific about what you like. For example, "Your new haircut really highlights your cheekbones" or "I love how the layers give your hair more volume."

2. Use Positive Adjectives

Use positive adjectives to describe their haircut, such as "chic," "stylish," "trendy," or "modern."

3. Show Enthusiasm

Show enthusiasm about their new haircut by using an excited tone of voice and facial expression. Let them know that you appreciate and notice their efforts to change their appearance.

4. Ask Questions

Ask questions about their new haircut, such as where they got it done or what inspired the change. This shows that you're interested and engaged in their life.

Step by Step Guide: What to Say When Someone Gets a New Haircut

Here's a step-by-step guide on what to say when someone gets a new haircut:

Step 1: Acknowledge Their New Haircut

Start by acknowledging their new haircut. You can say something like, "Wow, you got a new haircut! I love it."

Step 2: Be Specific

Be specific about what you like about their new haircut. For example, "I love the way your bangs frame your face" or "The color really suits your skin tone."

Step 3: Use Positive Adjectives

Use positive adjectives to describe their new haircut, such as "amazing," "gorgeous," or "stunning."

Step 4: Show Enthusiasm

Show enthusiasm about their new haircut by using an excited tone of voice and facial expression. Let them know that you appreciate and notice their efforts to change their appearance.

Step 5: Ask Questions

Ask questions about their new haircut, such as where they got it done or what inspired the change. This shows that you're interested and engaged in their life.

Tips for Complimenting Someone's New Haircut

Here are some additional tips for complimenting someone's new haircut:

1. Be Sincere

Make sure your compliments are sincere and genuine. People can tell when you're being insincere or fake.

2. Avoid Criticism

Avoid criticizing their previous hairstyle or making negative comments about their new haircut. Focus on the positive and let them enjoy their new look.

3. Be Respectful

Be respectful of their personal style and taste. Just because you may not like their new haircut doesn't mean you should say anything negative about it.

FAQs: What to Say When Someone Gets a New Haircut

Q: What if I don't like their new haircut?

A: It's okay if you don't like their new haircut, but it's important to keep your opinions to yourself. Remember, it's their hair and their choice.

Q: How do I compliment someone if I'm not good with words?

A: You can always use nonverbal cues, such as a smile, nod, or thumbs up. These gestures can convey your approval and appreciation without saying anything.

Q: What if they don't like their new haircut?

A: If they express dissatisfaction with their new haircut, you can offer words of encouragement and support. Let them know that hair grows back and that they can always change it again if they want to.

Hair Care Tips

Getting a new haircut is just the first step in maintaining healthy and beautiful hair. Here are some hair care tips to keep your hair looking its best:

1. Use the Right Products

Use products that are specifically designed for your hair type, whether it's oily, dry, curly, or straight. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent damage.

2. Don't Overwash

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days or as needed.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can damage your hair and cause split ends. Try to limit your use of these tools and use heat protectant products when you do use them.

4. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Now that you know what to say when someone gets a new haircut, you can confidently compliment your friends, family, and colleagues on their new look. Remember to be sincere, specific, and enthusiastic, and you'll make their day.
