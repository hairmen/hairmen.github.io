How To Become A Curly Hair Stylist Guide To Mastering Curls

Written by Luffy Sep 19, 2023 · 4 min read

Curls are a unique hair type that require a special set of skills to style and maintain. As a curly hair stylist, you have the opportunity to help clients embrace their natural texture and feel confident in their curls. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to becoming a curly hair stylist and provide tips on how to master curls.

How to Become a Curly Hair Stylist

Becoming a curly hair stylist requires a combination of education, practice, and experience. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Get a Cosmetology License

The first step to becoming a curly hair stylist is to get a cosmetology license. This requires attending a cosmetology school and passing a state licensing exam. Look for a school that includes curly hair education in their curriculum.

Step 2: Attend Curly Hair Workshops and Classes

After getting your cosmetology license, attend workshops and classes that focus specifically on curly hair. These will teach you techniques for cutting, styling, and caring for curly hair. Look for classes offered by companies that specialize in curly hair, such as DevaCurl and Ouidad.

Step 3: Practice, Practice, Practice

Practice styling curly hair as much as possible. Offer discounted services to friends and family to build your portfolio and gain experience. Take before-and-after photos of your work to showcase your skills to potential clients.

Step 4: Build Your Clientele

Once you feel confident in your skills, it’s time to start building your clientele. Offer special promotions to attract curly-haired clients, and ask for referrals from satisfied customers. Use social media to showcase your work and attract new clients.

Tips for Mastering Curls

Here are some tips to help you master curly hair:

Tip 1: Use the Right Products

Curls require moisture to stay healthy and defined. Use products specifically designed for curly hair, such as sulfate-free shampoos, conditioners, and styling products.

Tip 2: Cut Curls Dry

Curly hair should always be cut dry. Wet hair stretches, which can lead to uneven cuts. Cutting curls dry allows you to see the natural shape of the curls and create a customized cut.

Tip 3: Use the Right Cutting Technique

Cutting curly hair requires a specific technique to avoid creating frizz and uneven layers. Use a technique called “twist cutting” to create natural-looking layers that enhance the curls.

Tip 4: Embrace the Curls

As a curly hair stylist, it’s important to help your clients embrace their natural texture. Encourage them to avoid using heat tools and harsh chemicals, and instead embrace their curls with natural styling techniques.

FAQ About Becoming a Curly Hair Stylist

Q: Do I need special training to become a curly hair stylist?

A: Yes, it’s important to attend workshops and classes that focus specifically on curly hair to learn the techniques and skills needed to style and maintain curls.

Q: Can I specialize in curly hair as a cosmetologist?

A: Yes, you can specialize in any area of cosmetology, including curly hair. Attending classes and workshops and building a portfolio of curly hair styles will help you attract curly-haired clients.

Q: What products should I use on curly hair?

A: Use products specifically designed for curly hair, such as sulfate-free shampoos, conditioners, and styling products. Look for products that provide moisture and definition to enhance the curls.

Curly Hair Care

Curly hair requires special care to stay healthy and defined. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Don’t Over-Wash

Curly hair should not be washed every day. Over-washing can strip the hair of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz. Aim to wash curly hair every 2-3 days.

Tip 2: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Curly hair should be combed with a wide-tooth comb to avoid breaking and damaging the curls. Start at the ends and work your way up to detangle the hair.

Tip 3: Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent frizz and breakage. Silk is gentle on the hair and helps retain moisture.

Becoming a curly hair stylist takes dedication and practice, but it’s a rewarding career that allows you to help clients embrace their natural curls. Follow these steps and tips to become a master of curls.
