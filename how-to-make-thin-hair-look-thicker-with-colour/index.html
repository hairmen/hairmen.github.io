How To Make Thin Hair Look Thicker With Color

Written by Frank Sep 01, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have thin hair, you may be wondering how to make it look thicker. Fortunately, there are several ways to achieve the appearance of thicker hair, and one of them is with color. In this article, we will discuss how to make thin hair look thicker with color, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Make Thin Hair Look Thicker with Color
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Making Thin Hair Look Thicker with Color

How to Make Thin Hair Look Thicker with Color

The key to making thin hair look thicker with color is to add dimension and depth. This can be achieved with highlights, lowlights, or both. By adding lighter and darker shades to your hair, you create the illusion of depth, making your hair appear fuller and thicker.

The best way to achieve this is to visit a professional hair colorist. They can assess your hair and recommend the best color and technique to achieve the look you desire. However, if you prefer to color your hair at home, there are several options available.

Highlights

Highlights are a great way to add dimension to your hair. You can choose to add highlights throughout your hair or just in certain areas, such as around your face. If you have blonde or light brown hair, lighter highlights will create the illusion of depth. If you have darker hair, caramel or honey-colored highlights will work well.

Lowlights

Lowlights are the opposite of highlights. Instead of adding lighter shades to your hair, you add darker shades. This can create the appearance of depth and thickness, especially if you have lighter hair. Lowlights can be added throughout your hair or just in certain areas.

Both

Combining highlights and lowlights can create a more natural-looking result. This can be achieved with balayage or ombre techniques, which blend the colors together seamlessly. This technique works well for all hair colors and can create a more dramatic effect.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you want to color your hair at home, follow these steps:

  1. Choose the right color: Select a color that complements your skin tone and natural hair color. If you're unsure, consult with a professional or do a strand test first.
  2. Prepare your hair: Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before coloring. Don't use any styling products or conditioner.
  3. Protect your skin: Apply petroleum jelly or a barrier cream around your hairline and ears to prevent staining.
  4. Mix the color: Follow the instructions on the box to mix the color. Wear gloves to avoid staining your hands.
  5. Apply the color: Start at the roots and work your way down, making sure to saturate all the strands. Use a comb to distribute the color evenly.
  6. Wait: Leave the color on for the recommended time, then rinse it out thoroughly with lukewarm water.
  7. Condition: Apply the conditioner that comes with the color kit to your hair, and leave it on for the recommended time.
  8. Rinse and style: Rinse your hair thoroughly, then blow-dry and style as usual.

Tips for Making Thin Hair Look Thicker with Color

Here are some additional tips for making thin hair look thicker with color:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Blow-dry your hair upside down to create volume at the roots.
  • Avoid heat styling as much as possible, as it can damage your hair and make it look thinner.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using extensions for a more dramatic effect.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I color my hair if it's already damaged?

If your hair is already damaged, it's best to avoid coloring it until it's healthy again. Coloring can further damage your hair and make it look thinner. Focus on repairing your hair first with deep conditioning treatments and regular trims.

How often should I color my hair?

It's best to wait at least four to six weeks between coloring sessions to allow your hair to recover. Coloring too often can damage your hair and make it look thinner.

What if I don't want to color my hair?

If you don't want to color your hair, there are other ways to make it look thicker. Consider using volumizing products, getting a layered haircut, or using a texturizing spray.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for thin hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause damage.
  • Consider taking supplements, such as biotin, to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and techniques, you can make your thin hair look thicker and more voluminous. Whether you choose to color your hair or not, taking good care of your hair is essential for maintaining its health and appearance.
