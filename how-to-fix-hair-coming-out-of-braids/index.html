How to Fix Hair Coming Out of Braids - Tips and Tricks

Braids are a popular hairstyle that can last for weeks. However, one of the most frustrating things about braids is when the hair starts to come out of them. This can happen for a variety of reasons, including improper braiding techniques, using the wrong type of hair, or simply due to normal wear and tear. In this article, we will discuss how to fix hair coming out of braids and provide some tips and tricks to ensure your braids last as long as possible.

How to

There are a few different methods you can use to fix hair coming out of braids. Here are some of the most effective:

Method 1: Re-braid the Loose Hair

If you notice that only a small section of hair is coming out of your braids, you can try re-braiding that section. This will help to secure the hair back in place and prevent it from falling out completely.

To do this, start by separating the loose section of hair from the rest of the braid. Then, take a small amount of hair from the section and add it to the adjacent braid. Repeat this process until you have re-braided the entire section of loose hair.

Method 2: Use Hair Ties

If you have a larger section of hair coming out of your braids, you can use hair ties to secure it back in place. Start by gathering the loose hair together and twisting it tightly. Then, wrap a hair tie around the twisted section of hair to hold it in place. You can also use bobby pins to secure the hair tie in place.

Method 3: Use a Hot Tool

If you have a lot of loose hair coming out of your braids, you can use a hot tool, such as a flat iron or curling iron, to help secure it back in place. Start by twisting the loose hair tightly and then run the hot tool over the twisted section. This will help to smooth out the hair and hold it in place.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions for using the methods above:

Method 1: Re-braid the Loose Hair

  1. Separate the loose section of hair from the rest of the braid.
  2. Take a small amount of hair from the section and add it to the adjacent braid.
  3. Repeat this process until you have re-braided the entire section of loose hair.

Method 2: Use Hair Ties

  1. Gather the loose hair together and twist it tightly.
  2. Wrap a hair tie around the twisted section of hair to hold it in place.
  3. Use bobby pins to secure the hair tie in place, if necessary.

Method 3: Use a Hot Tool

  1. Twist the loose hair tightly.
  2. Run a hot tool, such as a flat iron or curling iron, over the twisted section.
  3. Smooth out the hair and hold it in place.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent hair from coming out of your braids:

  • Make sure your hair is clean and free of any tangles before braiding.
  • Avoid using too much tension when braiding your hair.
  • Use the right type of hair for your braids. Synthetic hair is often more durable and less likely to come out of braids than natural hair.
  • Avoid sleeping on your braids or pulling them too tightly.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent hair breakage.

FAQ

Q: Why does hair come out of braids?

A: Hair can come out of braids for a variety of reasons, including improper braiding techniques, using the wrong type of hair, or simply due to normal wear and tear.

Q: How long do braids typically last?

A: Braids can last anywhere from a few weeks to a few months, depending on how well they are cared for.

Q: Can I fix hair coming out of braids without taking them out?

A: Yes, you can use the methods described above to fix hair coming out of braids without taking them out.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy braids. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair regularly to remove dirt and oil buildup.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your braids.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help prevent hair breakage.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can fix hair coming out of braids and keep your hair looking great for weeks or even months to come.
