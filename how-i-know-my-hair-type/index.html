How I Know My Hair Type A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Knowing your hair type is essential when it comes to picking the right products and styling your hair. There are several hair types, such as straight, wavy, curly, and coily, and each requires unique care and attention. In this article, we'll discuss how to determine your hair type and offer some tips on how to care for it.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

The first step in determining your hair type is to look at the shape of your hair strands. Hair strands can either be straight, wavy, curly, or coily. To determine your hair type, take a strand of hair and examine it closely.

Straight Hair

Straight hair is typically shiny and reflects light easily. It is also the most resilient hair type and can withstand heat styling and chemical treatments. If your hair falls flat and doesn't have much texture, then you most likely have straight hair.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair is characterized by an "S" shape and has more texture than straight hair. If your hair has a slight curve and has a noticeable wave pattern, then you most likely have wavy hair.

Curly Hair

Curly hair has a spiral or corkscrew shape and can range from loose to tight curls. If your hair has a distinct curl pattern and tends to frizz easily, then you most likely have curly hair.

Coily Hair

Coily hair has a tight curl pattern and can range from small coils to larger coils. If your hair has a zig-zag pattern and is prone to shrinkage, then you most likely have coily hair.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair Type

Once you've determined your hair type, it's essential to care for it properly. Here are some tips for caring for each hair type:

Straight Hair

Straight hair can become oily quickly, so it's essential to wash it regularly with a gentle shampoo. Avoid using heavy conditioners, as they can weigh down your hair. Use a lightweight conditioner instead and avoid using too many styling products.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair needs moisture to prevent frizz and maintain its shape. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner and avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair. Use a diffuser attachment when blow-drying your hair to enhance your waves.

Curly Hair

Curly hair needs plenty of moisture to prevent frizz and maintain its shape. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner and apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing it. Avoid using a brush on your hair, as it can cause frizz. Instead, use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Coily Hair

Coily hair needs plenty of moisture to prevent breakage and maintain its shape. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner and apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing it. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair. Instead, air dry your hair or use a diffuser attachment when blow-drying it.

FAQ

What if my hair falls between two hair types?

If your hair falls between two hair types, then it's considered a combination hair type. In this case, you'll need to care for your hair based on the characteristics of each hair type.

Can my hair type change over time?

Yes, your hair type can change over time due to hormonal changes, age, or chemical treatments. It's essential to reevaluate your hair type regularly to ensure you're using the right products and caring for your hair properly.

Hair Care

Knowing your hair type is essential for maintaining healthy, beautiful hair. By following the tips above and using the right products, you can keep your hair looking its best. Remember to reevaluate your hair type regularly and adjust your hair care routine accordingly.
