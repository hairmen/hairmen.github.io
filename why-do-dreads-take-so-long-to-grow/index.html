Why Do Dreads Take So Long To Grow

Written by William Oct 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered why dreadlocks take so long to grow? Many people who decide to start a journey towards growing dreadlocks are often surprised at just how long it takes to achieve the desired length. In this article, we will explore the reasons why dreads take so long to grow and provide tips on how to care for your hair to promote healthy and faster hair growth.

Description

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are formed by allowing hair to mat and tangle together to form long, cylindrical ropes. While dreads can be achieved through various methods such as backcombing, twisting, and braiding, the process of growing them out naturally can take several years.

How to

If you are interested in growing dreadlocks, it is important to understand that it is a commitment that requires patience and dedication. Here are some tips for growing healthy dreads:

1. Start with healthy hair

Healthy hair is essential for growing strong and healthy dreadlocks. Make sure your hair is well-nourished and moisturized before starting the locking process. Use natural oils such as coconut oil or jojoba oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

2. Keep your scalp clean

Clean hair and scalp promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to wash your hair regularly to prevent buildup and maintain a clean scalp. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any excess oils or product buildup.

3. Be patient

Dreadlocks take time to grow, so it is important to be patient. Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage your hair and slow down the locking process. Instead, embrace your natural hair texture and let it grow out naturally.

4. Use aloe vera

Aloe vera is a natural plant that has been used for centuries to promote hair growth. Apply fresh aloe vera gel to your scalp and hair to nourish and moisturize your hair. This will help to promote healthy hair growth and prevent breakage.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to follow for growing dreadlocks:

1. Wash and condition your hair

Start with clean hair that is free of any product buildup. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair nourished and healthy.

2. Section your hair

Divide your hair into small sections using a comb. The smaller the sections, the tighter the dreadlocks will be.

3. Twist or backcomb your hair

Twist or backcomb each section of hair to encourage the strands to mat and tangle together. You can use a dread wax or locking gel to help hold the hair in place.

4. Maintain your dreads

Once your dreadlocks have formed, it is important to maintain them by washing and re-twisting them regularly. Use natural oils to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for growing healthy dreadlocks:

1. Protect your hair while sleeping

Use a silk or satin pillowcase to protect your hair while sleeping. This will help to prevent breakage and maintain the integrity of your dreadlocks.

2. Avoid using harsh chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals or hair products that can damage your hair and slow down the locking process. Stick to natural oils and products that are gentle on your hair.

3. Be gentle with your hair

Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage and damage to your dreadlocks. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to grow dreadlocks?

A: It can take several years to grow long, healthy dreadlocks. The length of time depends on various factors such as hair texture, thickness, and growth rate.

Q: Can I speed up the dreadlock process?

A: While there is no guaranteed way to speed up the dreadlock process, maintaining healthy hair and scalp can help to promote faster hair growth.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for growing healthy dreadlocks. Make sure to wash and condition your hair regularly and use natural oils to keep your hair moisturized and healthy. Be gentle with your hair and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage your hair. With patience and dedication, you can achieve long, healthy dreadlocks that you can be proud of.
