How To Cut Layered Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Layered bangs are a popular hairstyle that can add volume, texture, and dimension to your hair. If you're looking to switch up your look and want to try cutting your own bangs, then keep reading. We've put together a comprehensive guide on how to cut layered bangs at home.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Layered Bangs
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layered Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Layered Bangs

Description

Layered bangs are a versatile hairstyle that can be worn by anyone. They can be short, long, wispy, or thick, depending on the desired look. Layered bangs are cut in a way that creates different lengths throughout the bangs, giving them a layered, textured appearance. They are a great way to add interest and style to your hair without making a drastic change.

How to Cut Layered Bangs

Before you start cutting your own layered bangs, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to dampen your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own layered bangs:

  1. Start by sectioning off your bangs. Use a comb to separate the hair you want to cut from the rest of your hair.
  2. Decide on the length you want your bangs to be. Use your fingers to measure the length you want, and then mark it with your scissors.
  3. Begin cutting your bangs. Start at the center of your forehead and work your way outwards, cutting small sections at a time.
  4. Use your comb to lift and separate the hair as you cut. This will help you create a layered look.
  5. Repeat the process on the other side of your head. Make sure to cut the hair to the same length as the first side.
  6. Once you've cut the length you want, blend the layers by cutting small sections of hair at an angle. This will help create a natural, textured look.
  7. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs.
  8. Style your bangs as desired. You can blow dry them straight or use a curling iron to add some waves.

Tips for Cutting Layered Bangs

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own layered bangs:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's better to cut too little than too much.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Don't cut your bangs when they're wet. Wet hair is longer than dry hair, so you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be stressful, so it's important to take breaks and step away if you're feeling overwhelmed.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting layered bangs:

Can I cut my own bangs?

Yes, you can cut your own bangs. However, it's important to have the right tools and follow the proper techniques to avoid damaging your hair.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look overgrown. On average, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your bangs if you have curly hair. However, it's best to cut them when your hair is dry so you can see how the curls will fall.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Bangs

Now that you've cut your own layered bangs, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some hair care tips for layered bangs:

  • Use a heat protectant when styling your bangs with heat tools.
  • Don't wash your hair too often. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, leading to dry, brittle hair.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to keep them looking fresh.
  • Avoid using too much product on your bangs. This can weigh them down and make them look greasy.

Cutting your own layered bangs can be a fun and easy way to update your hairstyle. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-quality look from the comfort of your own home.
