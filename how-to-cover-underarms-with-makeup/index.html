How To Cover Underarms With Makeup A Comprehensive Guide

If you're someone who struggles with dark underarms or wants to hide them for a special occasion, you might have found yourself wondering if there's a way to cover them with makeup. The good news is, there is! In this article, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about how to cover underarms with makeup. From choosing the right products to application techniques, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cover Underarms with Makeup
  • Step-by-Step Guide

  • FAQ

How to Cover Underarms with Makeup

Before we dive into the steps for covering underarms with makeup, it's important to note that this technique is best used for special occasions or photoshoots, and not for everyday wear. It's also important to choose the right products to avoid irritation or discomfort. Here's what you'll need:

  • Primer
  • Concealer or Corrector
  • Foundation
  • Setting Powder
  • Setting Spray

Step 1: Prep Your Skin

Start by exfoliating your underarms to remove any dead skin cells or buildup that can make the area look darker. After exfoliating, wash your underarms with a gentle cleanser and pat them dry. Apply a moisturizer to keep your skin hydrated.

Step 2: Apply Primer

Apply a thin layer of primer to your underarms. This will help the makeup adhere better and last longer. Use a primer that's specifically formulated for the underarm area, as regular face primers may not work as well.

Step 3: Apply Corrector or Concealer

Choose a corrector or concealer that matches your skin tone or is slightly lighter. Apply it to your underarms using a small, flat brush or your fingers. Blend it well and make sure to cover the entire area evenly. If you have particularly dark underarms, you may want to use a corrector first to neutralize the darkness before applying the concealer.

Step 4: Apply Foundation

Once your corrector or concealer has been blended, apply a thin layer of foundation over your underarms. Use a brush or sponge to apply the foundation and blend it well. Make sure to choose a foundation that matches your skin tone and is long-wearing.

Step 5: Set with Powder

To set your underarm makeup and prevent it from rubbing off, apply a thin layer of setting powder over your underarms. Use a large, fluffy brush to apply the powder and make sure to cover the entire area. Be sure to choose a powder that's translucent or matches your skin tone.

Step 6: Finish with Setting Spray

To ensure your underarm makeup lasts all day and doesn't transfer onto your clothing, finish with a setting spray. Hold the spray about 6 inches away from your underarms and mist lightly. Allow the spray to dry completely before getting dressed.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a quick recap of the steps for covering underarms with makeup:

  1. Prep your skin
  2. Apply primer
  3. Apply corrector or concealer
  4. Apply foundation
  5. Set with powder
  6. Finish with setting spray

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you cover your underarms with makeup:

  • Choose products that are long-wearing and sweat-resistant
  • Use a brush or sponge to apply your makeup for a more even finish
  • Blend your makeup well to avoid any harsh lines or uneven patches
  • Avoid applying too much makeup, as this can look cakey and unnatural
  • If you're worried about the makeup transferring onto your clothing, wear a sleeveless top or dress that won't rub against your underarms

FAQ

Can I use regular makeup products on my underarms?

While you can technically use regular makeup products on your underarms, it's best to choose products that are specifically formulated for this area. Regular face makeup may not work as well and can cause irritation or discomfort.

Will covering my underarms with makeup cause any skin issues?

If you choose the right products and apply them correctly, covering your underarms with makeup shouldn't cause any skin issues. However, it's important to avoid using too much makeup or products that can cause irritation or clog your pores.

How long will the underarm makeup last?

The longevity of your underarm makeup will depend on the products you use and how well you apply them. Generally, if you choose long-wearing and sweat-resistant products, your makeup should last for several hours.

Hair Care for Underarms

Finally, it's important to take care of your underarm hair to keep the area looking smooth and even. Here are some tips:

  • Exfoliate your underarms regularly to prevent ingrown hairs
  • Shave or wax your underarms regularly to remove hair
  • Use a gentle, moisturizing shaving cream or wax to avoid irritation
  • Avoid using deodorants or antiperspirants immediately after hair removal, as this can cause irritation

By following these tips and techniques, you should be able to cover your underarms with makeup for any special occasion or photoshoot. Just remember to choose the right products, prep your skin properly, and apply your makeup with care.
