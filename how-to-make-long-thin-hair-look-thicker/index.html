How To Make Long Thin Hair Look Thicker

Written by Robby Nov 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of your long thin hair looking lifeless and flat? Don't worry, there are plenty of ways to add volume and thickness to your hair without having to resort to drastic measures like cutting it all off. In this article, we'll be sharing our top tips and tricks for achieving thicker-looking hair that will have you feeling confident and beautiful. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Long Thin Hair Look Thicker
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Top Tips for Thicker Hair
  • FAQs

How to Make Long Thin Hair Look Thicker

The first step in achieving thicker-looking hair is to start with a good haircut. A blunt cut that hits just below the shoulders is a great option for those with thin hair, as it creates the illusion of fullness and volume. Layers can also help add movement and dimension to your hair, but be sure to avoid thinning shears, as they can make your hair look even thinner.

Another great way to add thickness to your hair is by using volumizing products. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are specifically formulated for thin hair, as they will help plump up your strands and create a fuller look. You can also try using a volumizing mousse or root-lifting spray for added lift and body.

1. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow drying your hair upside down is a simple yet effective way to add volume and thickness. Start by flipping your hair over and blow drying it from the roots upwards, using your fingers to lift and separate your strands. Once your hair is about 80% dry, flip it back over and use a round brush to smooth out any remaining kinks and add some curl to the ends.

2. Use Velcro Rollers

If you want to add some extra bounce and volume to your hair, try using velcro rollers. Start by blow drying your hair until it's about 80% dry, then divide it into sections and wrap each section around a velcro roller. Leave the rollers in for about 15-20 minutes, then carefully remove them and use your fingers to gently tousle your hair.

3. Try a Half-Up Half-Down Style

A half-up half-down hairstyle is a great way to add volume and interest to your hair. Start by sectioning off the top half of your hair and pulling it back into a ponytail or bun. Then, use a curling iron to add some loose waves to the bottom half of your hair, and finish off the look with a spritz of hairspray for added hold.

Step-by-Step Guide

Ready to achieve thicker-looking hair? Follow these simple steps:

  1. Start with a good haircut that creates the illusion of fullness and volume.
  2. Use volumizing products like shampoos, conditioners, mousses, and root-lifting sprays to add thickness and body to your hair.
  3. Blow dry your hair upside down to add lift and volume, and use velcro rollers to add bounce and curl.
  4. Try a half-up half-down hairstyle to add interest and dimension to your hair.

Top Tips for Thicker Hair

Here are some additional tips and tricks for achieving thicker-looking hair:

  • Avoid using heavy products like oils and serums, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look even thinner.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and leave it looking dry and lifeless.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons on a daily basis, as they can damage your hair and make it look even thinner.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients that promote healthy hair growth, like biotin, vitamin C, and iron.

FAQs

Q: Can I still have long hair if I have thin hair?

A: Absolutely! With the right haircut and styling techniques, you can make long thin hair look thick and voluminous.

Q: Will using volumizing products make my hair look greasy?

A: Not necessarily. Look for lightweight, oil-free products that won't weigh down your hair or leave it looking greasy.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle, but most people can get away with washing their hair every 2-3 days.

Hair Care and Maintenance

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and full. Here are some tips for maintaining your thick, voluminous locks:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails, as they can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using hot tools, and avoid using them on the highest heat setting.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep it looking healthy.

With these tips and tricks, you can make long thin hair look thicker and more voluminous. So go ahead and try them out, and get ready to rock your new, full-bodied locks!
