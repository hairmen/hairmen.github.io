How To Cut Pixie At Home With Clippers

Written by Eveline Nov 19, 2023 · 3 min read

Cutting your hair at home can be a daunting task, especially when you’re considering a pixie cut. However, with the right tools and some basic knowledge, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. In this post, we’ll guide you through how to cut a pixie at home with clippers step-by-step.

How to Cut Pixie at Home with Clippers

Before we dive in, here’s a quick list of the tools you’ll need:

  • Clippers
  • Clipper guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape to protect clothing

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by washing your hair and towel drying it.

2. Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

3. Begin by using the largest clipper guard on the sides and back of your head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using upward strokes.

4. Repeat step 3 with a smaller clipper guard, gradually increasing in size as you work your way up.

5. Once you’ve finished the sides and back, move on to the top section. Use scissors to cut the top section to your desired length, leaving the front section longer than the back.

6. Blend the top and sides using scissors or clippers.

7. Use clippers to create a clean line around the nape of your neck.

8. Use scissors to trim any longer pieces or to add texture to your hair.

9. Remove the hair ties or clips and style your hair as desired.

Tips

- Use a light hand when cutting your hair. It’s always better to cut less than to cut too much.

- Invest in a good pair of scissors. They’ll make a big difference in the quality of your cut.

- Take your time and be patient. Cutting your hair at home can take longer than a salon visit, but the results can be just as good.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my pixie cut?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. On average, you should plan to cut your pixie cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape.

Q: Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

A: It’s best to cut your hair dry to achieve a more accurate cut. Wet hair can be deceiving, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.

Hair Care

Now that you’ve successfully cut your pixie at home with clippers, it’s important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shape. Here are some tips:

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

- Avoid using heat tools on your hair as much as possible to prevent damage.

- Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your cut.

Cutting a pixie at home with clippers can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and knowledge, you can achieve a professional-looking cut that you’ll be proud to show off. Happy cutting!
