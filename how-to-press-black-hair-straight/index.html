How To Press Black Hair Straight A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your curly or wavy black hair and want to achieve a sleek, straight look? Pressing your hair straight can be a great option, but it's important to do it correctly to avoid damaging your hair. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to press black hair straight, as well as provide tips and frequently asked questions to help you achieve the look you want.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Press Black Hair Straight
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Pressing Black Hair Straight
  4. Frequently Asked Questions
  5. Hair Care Tips for Pressed Hair

How to Press Black Hair Straight

Pressing black hair straight involves using a hot comb and a pressing comb to smooth out the hair. This method should not be used on hair that has been recently relaxed or chemically treated, as it can cause damage. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Cleanse and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or product residue. Follow up with a deep conditioner to moisturize your hair and prepare it for the pressing process.

Step 2: Blow Dry Your Hair

Use a blow dryer and a round brush to blow dry your hair straight. Make sure your hair is completely dry before moving on to the next step.

Step 3: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant product to your hair to prevent heat damage. Make sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Heat Up Your Hot Comb

Use a stove or a hot plate to heat up your hot comb. Test the temperature on a small section of hair before using it on your entire head.

Step 5: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using hair clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any spots.

Step 6: Press Your Hair

Starting at the nape of your neck, use the hot comb to press your hair straight. Follow up with a pressing comb to smooth out any kinks. Repeat this process on each section of your hair until it's all straight.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is straight, you can style it as desired. You can use a flat iron to make it even straighter, or add some curls or waves with a curling iron.

Tips for Pressing Black Hair Straight

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when pressing your black hair straight:

  • Use a high-quality hot comb and pressing comb to minimize damage to your hair.
  • Don't press your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a silk or satin scarf or bonnet to protect your hair while you sleep.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair and make it greasy.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized and healthy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I press my hair if it's been recently relaxed?

No, it's not recommended to press your hair if it's been recently relaxed or chemically treated. This can cause damage and breakage, and you should wait at least 2-3 weeks before attempting to press your hair.

How often should I press my hair?

You should only press your hair when necessary, as excessive heat can cause damage and breakage. Aim to press your hair no more than once a month.

How can I prevent my hair from reverting back to its natural state?

Use a high-quality heat protectant product and avoid getting your hair wet or sweating excessively. You can also use a silk or satin scarf or bonnet to protect your hair while you sleep.

Hair Care Tips for Pressed Hair

Here are some tips for caring for your pressed hair:

  • Avoid excessive heat styling, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair and make it greasy.

By following these tips and steps, you can achieve a sleek, straight look for your black hair without causing damage. Remember to take care of your hair and moisturize it regularly to keep it healthy and strong.
