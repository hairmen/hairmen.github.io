How To Cut An Undercut Bob

Written by Luffy Sep 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you thinking of changing up your hairstyle and want to try an undercut bob? This edgy and trendy haircut is perfect for those who want to create a bold statement with their hair. But before you take the plunge, it's important to know the proper technique for cutting an undercut bob. In this article, we'll go through the step-by-step process and provide some tips and tricks for achieving the perfect haircut.

Description

The undercut bob is a hairstyle that features shorter hair on the sides and longer hair on top. This style creates a contrast between the two lengths and adds dimension to the hair. The undercut section can be shaved or cut short, while the top section can be styled in a variety of ways, from straight and sleek to wavy and tousled. This hairstyle is popular among women and men and can be customized to suit different hair types and face shapes.

How to

To cut an undercut bob, you'll need a few tools, including scissors, clippers, and a comb. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair into two parts: the top section and the undercut section. Use a comb to create a straight part from one ear to the other, separating the hair on top from the hair on the sides and back.

Step 2: Cut the Undercut

Using clippers or scissors, cut the hair on the sides and back short, leaving about 1/4 inch to 1/2 inch of hair. You can create a gradual fade or a blunt cut, depending on your preference. Blend the hair at the top of the undercut section with the longer hair on top.

Step 3: Cut the Top Section

With the top section of hair, determine the length you want and cut it straight across. You can use scissors or a razor to create texture and movement. If you want a more dramatic look, you can add layers or angles to the top section.

Step 4: Blend the Two Sections

Blend the hair at the top of the undercut section with the longer hair on top by using scissors to cut the hair at an angle. This will create a seamless transition between the two lengths.

Step 5: Style the Hair

Once the haircut is complete, you can style the hair in a variety of ways. Use a straightener for a sleek look or add some texture with a curling iron or sea salt spray. You can also add some volume by teasing the hair at the crown.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting an undercut bob:

  • Start with clean, dry hair to make it easier to section and cut.
  • Use sharp scissors and clippers for a clean and precise cut.
  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time to avoid mistakes.
  • Consult with your client or stylist to determine the best length and style for their face shape and hair type.
  • Experiment with different lengths and angles to create a unique and personalized look.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about undercut bobs:

What face shapes are best for an undercut bob?

An undercut bob can work for many face shapes, but it's best for those with oval, round, or heart-shaped faces. Those with square or long faces may want to avoid this style.

How often do you need to get an undercut bob trimmed?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain the style. On average, you may need to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to maintain the length and shape.

Can you dye an undercut bob?

Yes, you can dye an undercut bob in a variety of colors and shades. Keep in mind that the undercut section may require more frequent touch-ups as the hair grows.

Hair Care

To keep your undercut bob looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid drying it out.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to protect the hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on the hair and use a heat protectant spray if necessary.
  • Trim the hair regularly to maintain the length and shape.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish the hair.

Now that you know how to cut an undercut bob, it's time to try it out for yourself or your clients. With the right technique and tools, you can create a bold and stylish haircut that's sure to turn heads.
