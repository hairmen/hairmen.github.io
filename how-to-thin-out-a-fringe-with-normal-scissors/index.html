How To Thin Out A Fringe With Normal Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've ever had a fringe that feels too heavy or thick, you might be wondering how to thin it out without having to go to the salon. Luckily, all you need are a pair of normal scissors and some patience. In this article, we'll show you how to thin out a fringe step-by-step, along with some tips, FAQs and hair care advice.

How to Thin Out a Fringe with Normal Scissors

Before we get started, it's important to note that thinning out a fringe is not the same as trimming it. Trimming involves cutting the ends of the hair to a specific length, while thinning out involves removing some of the hair's thickness and volume. Here's what you'll need:

  • Normal scissors
  • A comb
  • Clips (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to thin out your fringe:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your fringe forward so that it's covering your forehead.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the middle of your fringe. Hold it between your fingers and comb through it to remove any tangles.
  3. Starting at the middle of the section, use your scissors to cut vertically into the hair. This will create small, staggered cuts that remove some of the hair's thickness.
  4. Continue cutting through the section, moving towards the ends. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.
  5. Repeat this process with small sections of hair, working from the middle of your fringe outwards. Use your comb to keep the hair taut as you cut.
  6. Check your progress regularly by combing your fringe back into place and looking at it in the mirror. You want to create a natural, gradual thinning effect, so don't go overboard with the cutting.
  7. Once you're happy with the overall look of your fringe, comb it back into place and style as desired.

Tips for Thinning Out a Fringe

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way outwards. It's easier to remove more hair later than it is to add it back if you cut too much.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut too close to the roots, as this can create a choppy, uneven look.
  • Be patient and take your time. Thinning out a fringe can be a time-consuming process, but the results are worth it.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning out fringes:

Can I thin out my fringe with thinning shears?
Yes, you can use thinning shears to thin out your fringe. However, if you don't have access to thinning shears, normal scissors will work just as well. How often should I thin out my fringe?
This will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how thick you want your fringe to be. As a general rule of thumb, you can thin out your fringe every 4-6 weeks. Can I thin out my fringe if it's wet?
We recommend thinning out your fringe when it's dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to work with and may result in uneven cuts.

Hair Care Advice for Fringes

Here are some tips to help you care for your newly thinned out fringe:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your fringe looking fresh and clean.
  • Blow-dry your fringe with a round brush to create volume and texture.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your fringe, as this can cause damage and split ends.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your fringe soft and shiny.

Thinning out a fringe is a simple process that can be done at home with just a pair of normal scissors. By following our step-by-step guide and keeping our tips and advice in mind, you can achieve a natural, flattering look that frames your face perfectly.
