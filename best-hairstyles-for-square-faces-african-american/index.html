Best Hairstyles For Square Faces African American

Written by Eveline Jul 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have a square face shape and you're African American, you may be wondering what hairstyles are best for you. Having a square face means that your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all roughly the same width, and you may also have a strong jawline. With the right hairstyle, you can accentuate your best features and balance out your face shape. In this article, we'll share some of the best hairstyles for square faces African American, along with tips on how to style and care for your hair.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before we dive into the best hairstyles for square faces African American, it's important to determine your face shape. Here's how:

  1. Stand in front of a mirror with your hair pulled back.
  2. Use a washable marker or lipstick to draw an outline of your face on the mirror.
  3. Step back and look at the outline. Is your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline roughly the same width? If so, you have a square face shape.

Now that you know your face shape, let's explore some of the best hairstyles for square faces African American.

Best Hairstyles for Square Faces African American

When it comes to choosing a hairstyle for a square face, the key is to add height and volume to the crown of the head and soften the jawline. Here are some of the best hairstyles for square faces African American:

1. Long Layers

Long layers can help to soften the jawline and add movement to your hair. If you have natural curls or waves, long layers can also help to define your curls and add volume to your hair.

2. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs can help to balance out a strong jawline and draw attention to your eyes. Opt for longer, wispy bangs that blend in with the rest of your hair for a more natural look.

3. Pixie Cut

A pixie cut can be a bold and edgy option for a square face shape. Keep the sides and back short and add volume to the crown of the head to balance out your face shape.

4. Bob Cut

A bob cut can be a versatile option for a square face shape. Opt for a chin-length bob with layers or curls to soften the jawline and add movement to your hair.

5. Updo

An updo can be a chic and sophisticated option for a square face shape. Opt for a high bun or topknot to add height to the crown of the head and elongate the face.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Hair

Now that you've chosen a hairstyle, here's a step-by-step guide to styling your hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair using products that are designed for your hair type.
  2. Apply a heat protectant to your hair to prevent damage from heat styling tools.
  3. Blow dry your hair using a round brush to add volume to the crown of the head.
  4. Use a curling iron or flat iron to add curls or waves to your hair.
  5. Finish with a hairspray or other styling product to hold your hairstyle in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hairstyle

Here are some tips for maintaining your hairstyle:

  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant and avoiding excessive heat styling.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your hairstyle.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a square face?

Avoid hairstyles that are too short or too blunt, as these can emphasize the squareness of your face. Also avoid hairstyles that add width to the sides of your face, such as a blunt bangs or a wide, voluminous hairstyle.

Can I wear my hair straight if I have a square face?

Absolutely! Straight hair can be a great option for a square face shape. Just be sure to add volume to the crown of the head to balance out your face shape.

What hair color is best for a square face?

When it comes to hair color, there's no one-size-fits-all answer. However, highlights or ombre can help to soften the jawline and add dimension to your hair.

Hair Care Tips for African American Hair

Here are some hair care tips specifically for African American hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can lead to heat damage and breakage.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.

With the right hairstyle and hair care routine, you can accentuate your best features and feel confident in your appearance. Try out some of these best hairstyles for square faces African American and see which one works best for you!
