Men s Haircut 40S A Guide To Classic Style

Written by Luffy Jul 24, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you looking for a classic, timeless haircut that will never go out of style? Look no further than the men's haircut 40s. This iconic style is characterized by short, slicked-back hair on top and trimmed sides and back. In this article, we'll show you how to achieve this classic look, step by step, and provide tips for maintaining it.

How to Achieve the Men's Haircut 40s

Step 1: Choose the right length. The key to the men's haircut 40s is short hair on top and sides. For the top, aim for a length of about 2 to 3 inches, and for the sides and back, go for a clipper setting of 2 or 3.

Step 2: Wet your hair. Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is wet. This will make it easier to cut and style.

Step 3: Cut the sides and back. Use your clippers to trim the sides and back of your head, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Use a comb to hold your hair in place as you cut.

Step 4: Cut the top. Use scissors to trim the hair on top of your head, starting at the front and working your way back. Aim for a slightly longer length in the front and shorter length in the back.

Step 5: Style your hair. Apply a small amount of hair wax or pomade to your hair and use a comb to slick it back.

Tips for Maintaining the Men's Haircut 40s

Tip 1: Get regular trims. To keep your men's haircut 40s looking sharp, you'll need to get regular trims every 4-6 weeks.

Tip 2: Use the right products. Use a high-quality hair wax or pomade to style your hair and keep it in place. Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Tip 3: Avoid over-washing your hair. Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, making it look dull and lifeless. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or as needed.

FAQ

Q: Is the men's haircut 40s suitable for all hair types?

A: Yes, the men's haircut 40s can be adapted to suit all hair types, from straight to curly. However, if you have very thick or coarse hair, you may need to use a stronger hold product to keep it in place.

Q: Can I style my men's haircut 40s differently?

A: Yes, you can adapt the men's haircut 40s to suit your personal style. For example, you could try a side parting or add some texture to the top of your hair.

Hair Care

To keep your men's haircut 40s looking its best, it's important to take good care of your hair. This includes using a high-quality shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools, and protecting your hair from the sun and wind.

In conclusion, the men's haircut 40s is a classic style that will never go out of fashion. By following these simple steps and tips, you can achieve this timeless look and keep it looking sharp for years to come.
