Why Does Only One Side Of My Head Curl

Written by Robby Sep 24, 2023 · 6 min read

Have you ever noticed that only one side of your head seems to curl easily while the other side struggles to hold a curl? This can be frustrating, especially if you’re trying to achieve a specific hairstyle. In this article, we’ll explore the reasons why this happens and provide tips on how to overcome this issue.

Table of Contents

  • Reasons why only one side of your head curls
  • How to achieve curls on both sides of your head
  • Step-by-step guide to curling your hair
  • Tips to help your curls last longer
  • Frequently asked questions
  • Hair care tips for healthy curls

Reasons why only one side of your head curls

There are several reasons why only one side of your head may curl easily:

1. Natural hair texture

Everyone’s hair is different, and some people naturally have hair that is easier to curl on one side than the other. This can be due to differences in hair texture, thickness, or density.

2. Sleeping position

If you sleep on one side of your head more than the other, this can cause your hair to become flattened or bent in a way that makes it harder to curl. Try sleeping with your hair in a loose bun or braid to minimize the impact of your sleeping position on your curls.

3. Heat damage

If you frequently use heat styling tools like curling irons or flat irons, you may be causing damage to your hair that makes it harder to curl. This damage can be worse on one side of your head than the other, depending on how you hold the styling tool and how often you use it.

4. Product buildup

If you use a lot of hair products like hairspray, mousse, or gel, this can leave buildup on your hair that makes it harder to curl. This buildup can be worse on one side of your head than the other, depending on how you apply the product and how often you use it.

How to achieve curls on both sides of your head

If you’re struggling to achieve curls on one side of your head, there are several things you can try:

1. Use a different curling iron

If you’re using a curling iron that is too big or too small for your hair, this can make it harder to achieve curls on one side of your head. Try using a different size curling iron or a wand to see if this makes a difference.

2. Change your technique

If you’re curling your hair in the same direction on both sides of your head, this could be causing the uneven curls. Try curling your hair in different directions or using a different technique on the side that is harder to curl.

3. Use hair products

If your hair is resistant to curling, you may need to use hair products to help hold the curl. Try using a heat protectant spray, a curl-enhancing mousse, or a hairspray designed for curl retention.

Step-by-step guide to curling your hair

If you’re new to curling your hair, here’s a basic step-by-step guide:

1. Prep your hair

Wash and dry your hair, then apply a heat protectant spray. Brush your hair to remove any tangles and divide it into sections.

2. Heat up your curling iron

Turn on your curling iron and let it heat up for a few minutes. The temperature will depend on your hair type and the curling iron you’re using.

3. Start curling

Take a small section of hair and hold it near the end. Place the curling iron near the roots and wrap the hair around the barrel, holding the ends with your fingers. Hold for a few seconds, then release the curl.

4. Repeat

Continue curling sections of hair until you’ve curled all of it. If you want looser curls, use a larger barrel or curl larger sections of hair.

Tips to help your curls last longer

Once you’ve achieved curls on both sides of your head, you want them to last as long as possible. Here are some tips to help your curls stay in place:

1. Use hairspray

Spray your curls with a light mist of hairspray to help them hold their shape. Don’t use too much, or your hair will become stiff and crunchy.

2. Avoid touching your hair

The oils from your hands can make your hair look greasy and cause your curls to fall out faster. Try to avoid touching your hair as much as possible.

3. Sleep with your hair in a loose bun

If you want your curls to last overnight, sleep with your hair in a loose bun or braid. This will help protect your curls and prevent them from getting flattened while you sleep.

Frequently asked questions

1. Can I curl my hair if it’s wet?

No, you should never curl your hair when it’s wet. This can cause damage to your hair and make it harder to achieve curls.

2. How long will my curls last?

The length of time your curls will last depends on your hair type, the products you use, and how you style your hair. On average, curls will last for a few hours to a full day.

3. Can I curl my hair without heat?

Yes, there are several ways to curl your hair without heat, including using braids, twists, or foam rollers. These methods are gentler on your hair and can be a good option if you’re trying to avoid heat damage.

Hair care tips for healthy curls

If you want to maintain healthy, bouncy curls, it’s important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo that is gentle on your hair and scalp.

2. Deep condition regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair once a week and leave it on for at least 10 minutes before rinsing.

3. Avoid heat styling as much as possible

Heat styling tools can cause damage to your hair over time. Try to limit your use of these tools and use a heat protectant spray when you do use them.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve beautiful curls on both sides of your head and maintain healthy, bouncy hair.
