Can I Perm My Naturally Curly Hair

Written by Christine Nov 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Perming naturally curly hair may seem like an unnecessary task, but it can actually help enhance your curls and give them more definition. If you're considering perming your naturally curly hair, you may have some questions about the process. In this article, we'll answer some frequently asked questions, provide tips for success, and share some hair care tips to keep your newly permed curls looking their best.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Perm Naturally Curly Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Success
  5. Frequently Asked Questions
  6. Hair Care Tips

Description

Perming your naturally curly hair involves using chemicals to change its texture. The process typically involves applying a solution to your hair that breaks down the bonds that give your hair its natural shape. Once the bonds are broken down, your hair is then wrapped around perm rods and a neutralizing solution is applied to reform the bonds in the new shape.

Perming can be a great option for those who want more defined curls or want to add volume to their hair. However, it's important to note that perming can be damaging to your hair if not done correctly. It's important to follow proper techniques and care for your hair post-perm to minimize damage and maintain healthy hair.

How to Perm Naturally Curly Hair

Before perming your hair, it's important to select the right type of perm solution for your hair type. It's recommended to use a perm solution that is specifically designed for naturally curly hair. These solutions typically have a lower pH level and are gentler on the hair.

It's also important to have a professional perm your hair to ensure that the process is done correctly and safely. Attempting to perm your hair at home can result in damage and uneven curls.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the general steps involved in perming naturally curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup and prepare your hair for perming.
  2. Section your hair and wrap it around perm rods. The size of the rods will depend on the desired curl size.
  3. Apply the perm solution to your hair, making sure to saturate each section evenly.
  4. Cover your hair with a plastic cap and let it process for the recommended time (usually around 15-20 minutes).
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water to remove the perm solution.
  6. Apply the neutralizing solution to your hair and let it sit for a few minutes.
  7. Remove the perm rods and rinse your hair again with warm water.
  8. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair to help restore moisture and prevent damage.

Tips for Success

Here are some tips to keep in mind when perming naturally curly hair:

  • Consult with a professional stylist to determine the best perm solution and rod size for your hair type and desired outcome.
  • Be sure to follow the instructions on the perm solution carefully to avoid over-processing or under-processing your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to maintain moisture and prevent damage.
  • Minimize heat styling and avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair post-perm.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some commonly asked questions about perming naturally curly hair:

  • Can I perm my naturally curly hair if it's already damaged? It's not recommended to perm damaged hair as it can further weaken the hair and cause breakage.
  • How long will a perm last on naturally curly hair? A perm can last anywhere from 2-6 months depending on how well you care for your hair post-perm.
  • Can I color my hair after perming it? It's recommended to wait at least a week after perming before coloring your hair to avoid over-processing and damage.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind post-perm:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to maintain moisture and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or relaxers, as they can weaken the hair and cause breakage.

Perming your naturally curly hair can be a great way to enhance your curls and add volume to your hair. However, it's important to approach the process with caution and care for your hair post-perm to maintain healthy, beautiful curls.
