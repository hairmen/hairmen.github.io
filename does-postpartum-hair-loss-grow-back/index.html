Postpartum hair loss is a common phenomenon that affects most new moms. During pregnancy, the body experiences hormonal changes that result in thicker, fuller hair. However, after giving birth, the hormone levels return to normal, and many women experience hair loss. This can be a stressful and frustrating experience, but the good news is that postpartum hair loss does grow back. In this article, we will discuss how to deal with postpartum hair loss and the steps you can take to promote hair growth.

What Causes Postpartum Hair Loss?

Postpartum hair loss is caused by hormonal changes in the body. During pregnancy, the hormone estrogen increases, which prolongs the growing phase of the hair cycle. This results in thicker, fuller hair. However, after giving birth, estrogen levels drop, and the hair cycle returns to normal. As a result, many women experience hair loss, which can be a distressing experience.

How to Deal with Postpartum Hair Loss

Postpartum hair loss can be a stressful experience, but there are steps you can take to deal with it. Here are some tips:

FAQs

How Long Does Postpartum Hair Loss Last?

Postpartum hair loss usually lasts for a few months, and hair growth typically returns to normal within a year after giving birth.

Is Postpartum Hair Loss Permanent?

No, postpartum hair loss is not permanent. Hair growth typically returns to normal within a year after giving birth.

Can Breastfeeding Cause Postpartum Hair Loss?

Yes, breastfeeding can cause postpartum hair loss. This is because breastfeeding can cause hormonal changes in the body, which can result in hair loss.

Hair Care Tips for New Moms

Here are some additional hair care tips for new moms:

1. Keep Your Hair Clean

Keeping your hair clean is essential for promoting hair growth. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much product as this can weigh hair down and make it look greasy.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails, as these can pull on the hair and cause breakage. Instead, opt for looser styles that donât put too much tension on the hair.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product that contains SPF. The sun can damage hair, causing it to become dry and brittle.
