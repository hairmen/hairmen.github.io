Mens Fade Haircut Measurements A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Sep 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for a new haircut that's stylish and low-maintenance? The mens fade haircut might be just what you need. This popular style is characterized by short hair on the sides and back that gradually gets longer toward the top. In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about mens fade haircut measurements.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

The mens fade haircut can be customized to suit any face shape and hair type. The key to getting the perfect fade is knowing the right measurements to use. In general, a fade is created by using different clipper guard sizes to gradually blend the hair from short to long. The length of the fade can vary depending on personal preference and the overall look you're going for.

How to

To get a mens fade haircut, you'll need to visit a barber or hairstylist who is experienced with this style. Here are the general steps they'll follow:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair
  2. Determine the desired length of the fade and select the appropriate clipper guard size
  3. Create a guideline around the head using the clipper guard
  4. Gradually blend the hair from shorter to longer by switching to a larger clipper guard size
  5. Use scissors or clippers to blend any uneven areas
  6. Trim the hair on top to the desired length and style as desired

Step by step

Here's a more detailed breakdown of the steps involved in getting a mens fade haircut:

  1. Wash and dry your hair to remove any product or oils
  2. Discuss your desired look with your barber or hairstylist, including the length of the fade and the style you want on top
  3. Decide on the starting point for the fade, which is usually just above the ear
  4. Begin with a higher clipper guard size (such as a #4) and create a straight line around the head at the starting point
  5. Move down to a #3 guard and create another line about 1 inch below the first line
  6. Switch to a #2 guard and create another line about 1 inch below the previous line
  7. Use a #1 guard to blend the hair between the second and third lines
  8. Use a clipper without a guard to create a bald fade at the bottom of the hairline
  9. Blend any uneven areas with scissors or clippers
  10. Trim the hair on top to the desired length and style as desired

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting a mens fade haircut:

  • Bring a picture of the style you want to show your barber or hairstylist
  • Be specific about the length of the fade you want
  • Communicate clearly if you want a specific style on top (such as a pompadour or quiff)
  • Ask your barber or hairstylist for styling advice and product recommendations
  • Maintain your haircut by getting regular trims every 4-6 weeks

FAQ

What is the difference between a low fade and a high fade?

A low fade starts just above the ear and gradually gets shorter toward the bottom of the hairline, while a high fade starts at the temple and gradually gets shorter toward the top of the head. The length of the fade can vary depending on personal preference.

How long does a mens fade haircut last?

A mens fade haircut can last anywhere from 2-4 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain the style. It's important to get regular trims to keep the fade looking sharp.

What products should I use to style my mens fade haircut?

The products you use will depend on your hair type and the style you want to achieve. Pomades, waxes, and gels can all be used to add texture and hold to your hair. Ask your barber or hairstylist for recommendations.

Hair Care

To keep your mens fade haircut looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo every 2-3 days
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and healthy
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can damage it
  • Get regular trims every 4-6 weeks to maintain the style

Now that you know everything about mens fade haircut measurements, you're ready to try this stylish and low-maintenance look for yourself. Remember to communicate clearly with your barber or hairstylist, and to maintain your haircut by getting regular trims and using the right hair care products.
