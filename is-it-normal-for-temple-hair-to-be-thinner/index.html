Is It Normal For Temple Hair To Be Thinner

Description

Temple hair, also known as the hair around the hairline, can be naturally thinner than the rest of the hair on the head. However, if you have relaxed hair, you may notice that your temple hair is even thinner than usual.

In this article, we'll explore whether it's normal for temple hair to be thinner in relaxed hair and what you can do to care for it.

How to

If you have relaxed hair and are concerned about your temple hair being thinner than the rest of your hair, there are several things you can do.

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles such as braids, weaves, and ponytails can put stress on the hairline and cause traction alopecia. This condition can lead to hair loss and thinning around the temple area. To avoid this, opt for looser hairstyles or styles that don't put too much pressure on the hairline.

2. Moisturize Regularly

Relaxed hair is more prone to dryness, and dry hair can lead to breakage and thinning. To combat this, make sure to moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil. Pay extra attention to the temple area and massage the product gently into the scalp.

3. Protect Your Hair at Night

Friction from your pillowcase can cause breakage and thinning around the temple area. To prevent this, wrap your hair in a silk or satin scarf at night or use a silk or satin pillowcase.

Step by step

Here are some step-by-step tips for caring for your temple hair:

1. Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo. Be sure to massage the scalp gently to remove any buildup or dirt.

2. Condition Your Hair

After washing, apply a moisturizing conditioner to your hair, paying extra attention to the temple area. Leave the conditioner in for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your hair, paying extra attention to the temple area. Massage the product into your scalp gently.

4. Style Your Hair

Style your hair in a way that doesn't put stress on the temple area. Consider wearing your hair down or in a loose bun or ponytail.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for your temple hair:

  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons on your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as relaxers or hair dyes.

FAQ

Is it normal for temple hair to be thinner in relaxed hair?

Yes, it is normal for temple hair to be thinner in relaxed hair. Relaxers can weaken the hair and make it more prone to breakage and thinning.

Can temple hair become thicker?

While it's difficult to make hair thicker, you can take steps to prevent further thinning and breakage. Follow a healthy hair care routine and avoid harsh chemicals and tight hairstyles.

Hair Care

Overall, caring for your temple hair is an important part of maintaining healthy hair. By following these tips and adopting a healthy hair care routine, you can help prevent further thinning and breakage and keep your hair looking its best.
