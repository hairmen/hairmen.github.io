Does Pcos Cause Sudden Hair Loss

Written by Aprilia Sep 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder that affects women of reproductive age. One of the many symptoms of PCOS is hair loss or thinning of hair. In this article, we will discuss the link between PCOS and sudden hair loss, how to identify it, and what you can do to address it.

What is PCOS?

PCOS is a condition that affects a woman's hormones. Women with PCOS have higher levels of androgens (male hormones) than normal. This hormonal imbalance can lead to a range of symptoms, including irregular periods, acne, weight gain, and hair loss.

How Does PCOS Cause Hair Loss?

The exact mechanism by which PCOS causes hair loss is still not fully understood. However, it is believed that the excess androgens in women with PCOS can cause hair follicles to shrink, leading to hair loss or thinning of hair. Additionally, insulin resistance, which is common in women with PCOS, can also contribute to hair loss.

How to Identify PCOS-Related Hair Loss?

The first step in identifying PCOS-related hair loss is to look for the other symptoms of PCOS, such as irregular periods, acne, and weight gain. If you have these symptoms, it is important to speak with your doctor, who may order a blood test to check your hormone levels. Your doctor may also refer you to a dermatologist who specializes in hair loss for further evaluation.

The pattern of hair loss can also be a clue to whether it is related to PCOS. In women with PCOS, hair loss often occurs on the top of the scalp, while the hairline and temples remain intact.

What Can You Do to Address PCOS-Related Hair Loss?

There are several things you can do to address PCOS-related hair loss:

1. Treat the Underlying PCOS

The most effective way to address PCOS-related hair loss is to treat the underlying PCOS. This may involve medications to regulate your hormones, such as birth control pills or spironolactone. Lifestyle changes, such as losing weight and exercising regularly, can also be helpful in controlling PCOS symptoms.

2. Use Hair Growth Products

There are several hair growth products available over the counter that can help stimulate hair growth and prevent further hair loss. Look for products that contain minoxidil, which is the only FDA-approved ingredient for hair loss treatment.

3. Consider Hair Transplantation

If you have significant hair loss, hair transplantation may be an option. During this procedure, hair follicles are taken from other areas of your scalp and transplanted to the areas where hair loss has occurred. This can be a costly procedure and may not be covered by insurance.

FAQs

Can PCOS-related hair loss be reversed?

Yes, PCOS-related hair loss can be reversed with proper treatment. However, it may take some time for hair to regrow and become thicker.

Will hair loss stop once PCOS is treated?

Yes, once PCOS is treated, hair loss should stop, and hair may begin to regrow.

Hair Care Tips for Women with PCOS

Here are some hair care tips for women with PCOS:

1. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid harsh hair treatments, such as chemical relaxers or hot irons, which can damage hair and make it more prone to breakage.

2. Use Gentle Shampoos

Use gentle shampoos that are free from sulfates and other harsh chemicals that can strip hair of its natural oils.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to include plenty of fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats in your diet.

4. Manage Stress

Stress can exacerbate PCOS symptoms and lead to hair loss. Try to manage stress through activities such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises.

Conclusion

PCOS can cause sudden hair loss in women, but it is treatable. If you are experiencing hair loss or thinning of hair, speak with your doctor to determine if it is related to PCOS. Treating the underlying PCOS, using hair growth products, and considering hair transplantation are all options for addressing PCOS-related hair loss. Additionally, taking care of your hair through gentle hair care practices, a healthy diet, and stress management can help promote healthy hair growth.
