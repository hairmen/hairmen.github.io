How To Put Layers In Men s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Layers can add texture and dimension to men's hairstyles, making them look more modern and stylish. If you're interested in putting layers in your hair, this guide will show you how to do it step by step. We'll cover everything from the tools you'll need to the best techniques for layering men's hair.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Blending Your Layers
  • Tips for Layering Men's Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Tools You'll Need

In order to put layers in men's hair, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Hair dryer (optional)

Make sure your scissors are sharp and in good condition, as dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut cleanly.

Prepping Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to prep it by washing and drying it thoroughly. This will help to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup that could make it harder to cut your hair accurately.

If you're planning to use clippers to cut your hair, make sure it's dry and tangle-free before you start. Wet hair can be more difficult to clip, and tangles can cause the clippers to pull and snag your hair.

Sectioning Your Hair

Once your hair is prepped, you'll need to section it off into layers. Start by dividing your hair into two sections: the top and the bottom. Use a comb to create a horizontal parting at the back of your head, and clip the top section out of the way.

Next, create a vertical parting on one side of your head, about an inch away from your hairline. Comb the hair on the back side of the parting upward and clip it out of the way. This will be your first layer.

Repeat this process on the other side of your head, creating another vertical parting about an inch away from your hairline. Comb the hair upward and clip it out of the way. This will be your second layer.

Cutting Your Hair

With your first layer sectioned off, it's time to start cutting. Using your scissors, cut the hair at a slight angle, starting at the back and working your way to the front. This will create a gradual, layered effect.

For shorter layers, hold the hair straight up and cut the ends straight across. For longer layers, hold the hair at a 45-degree angle and cut the ends at an angle. This will create a more blended, natural look.

Once you've cut the first layer, unclip the top section of your hair and repeat the process, layering the hair on top of the first layer. Continue working your way up your head, section by section, until all of your hair is layered.

Blending Your Layers

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to blend the layers together. Use your scissors to trim any stray ends or uneven spots, making sure that the layers flow smoothly and seamlessly into each other.

If you notice any areas that look too choppy or uneven, you can use your scissors to soften them up by cutting into the hair at an angle. This will create a more natural, textured look.

Tips for Layering Men's Hair

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean, precise cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to, especially if you're new to cutting hair.
  • Be patient and take your time to ensure a great result.

FAQs

Can I layer my own hair?

Yes, you can layer your own hair if you feel comfortable doing so. However, it can be helpful to have someone else assist you, especially if you're new to cutting hair.

How often should I get my hair layered?

This will depend on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, most men should get their hair layered every 6-8 weeks to maintain their style and keep their hair looking healthy.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Once you've layered your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and stylish. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair soft and shiny.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and neat.

With these tips and techniques, you can put layers in men's hair like a pro, creating a stylish and modern look that's sure to turn heads. Whether you're cutting your own hair or working with a stylist, take your time and be patient to achieve the best possible results.
