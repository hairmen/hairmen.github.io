How to Get Rid of Eczema on the Scalp Naturally

Eczema on the scalp is a type of dermatitis that can cause redness, flaking, itching, and even hair loss. While there are many prescription and over-the-counter treatments available, natural remedies can be just as effective and without the side effects. Here are some natural ways to get rid of eczema on the scalp:

1. Tea Tree Oil

Tea tree oil is a natural antiseptic and anti-inflammatory that can help reduce the inflammation and itchiness associated with eczema. To use, mix a few drops of tea tree oil with a carrier oil such as coconut oil or olive oil, and massage into the scalp. Leave on for at least 30 minutes before washing out.

2. Aloe Vera

Aloe vera is a natural moisturizer that can help soothe and heal the skin. Apply fresh aloe vera gel directly to the scalp and leave on for 30 minutes before washing out. Alternatively, look for shampoos and conditioners that contain aloe vera.

3. Apple Cider Vinegar

Apple cider vinegar is a natural disinfectant that can help kill bacteria and fungi that may be causing eczema on the scalp. Mix equal parts apple cider vinegar and water and apply to the scalp with a cotton ball. Leave on for 10-15 minutes before washing out.

4. Coconut Oil

Coconut oil is a natural moisturizer that can help reduce inflammation and itching associated with eczema. Warm up a small amount of coconut oil and massage into the scalp. Leave on for at least 30 minutes before washing out.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to use natural remedies to get rid of eczema on the scalp:

  1. Choose a natural remedy such as tea tree oil, aloe vera, apple cider vinegar, or coconut oil.
  2. Mix the natural remedy with a carrier oil such as coconut oil or olive oil.
  3. Massage the mixture into the scalp and leave on for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Rinse out the mixture with warm water and shampoo.
  5. Repeat the treatment 2-3 times per week until symptoms improve.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help manage eczema on the scalp:

  • Avoid using harsh shampoos and conditioners that can dry out the scalp.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for sensitive skin.
  • Avoid scratching the scalp as this can make the symptoms worse.
  • Wear a hat or use sunscreen on the scalp when spending time in the sun.
  • Consult with a dermatologist if symptoms persist or worsen.

Frequently Asked Questions

What causes eczema on the scalp?

Eczema on the scalp can be caused by a variety of factors such as genetics, stress, allergies, and harsh hair products.

Is eczema on the scalp contagious?

No, eczema on the scalp is not contagious.

Can diet affect eczema on the scalp?

Yes, some foods such as dairy, gluten, and sugar can trigger eczema flare-ups. It is important to maintain a healthy and balanced diet to help manage eczema.

Hair Care for Eczema on the Scalp

When dealing with eczema on the scalp, it is important to use gentle hair care products that won't irritate the skin. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are specifically designed for sensitive skin and avoid using harsh styling products such as gels and hairsprays.

Additionally, try to avoid washing your hair every day as this can dry out the scalp. Instead, opt for washing every 2-3 days and use dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

In conclusion, eczema on the scalp can be a frustrating condition to deal with, but natural remedies can be just as effective as prescription treatments. Try using tea tree oil, aloe vera, apple cider vinegar, or coconut oil to help alleviate the symptoms of eczema on the scalp. Remember to be gentle with your hair and avoid using harsh hair products that can irritate the skin.
