How To Know If You Look Good With Blonde Hair

Written by Bardi Sep 11, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're considering changing your hair color to blonde, it's important to know if it will suit you. Blonde hair can be a bold statement, but it can also wash out some skin tones or clash with certain features. In this article, we'll go over how to determine if blonde hair is right for you, and if so, how to choose the right shade.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Know if Blonde Hair is Right for You
  2. Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing the Right Shade of Blonde
  3. Tips for Maintaining Your Blonde Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Tips for Blonde Hair

How to Know if Blonde Hair is Right for You

Blonde hair can look great on many people, but it's not a one-size-fits-all color. Here are some factors to consider when deciding if blonde hair is right for you:

Skin Tone

Blonde hair tends to look best on people with cool or neutral skin tones. If you have warm undertones, blonde hair may wash you out. To determine your skin tone, look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear blue, you likely have cool undertones. If they appear green, you likely have warm undertones. If you can't tell, you may have neutral undertones.

Eye Color

Blonde hair can bring out the best in certain eye colors. If you have blue or green eyes, blonde hair can make them pop. If you have brown eyes, blonde hair can create a striking contrast. If you have hazel eyes, blonde hair can bring out the green or gold tones.

Natural Hair Color

Your natural hair color can affect how well blonde hair suits you. If you have very dark hair, going blonde can be a drastic change that may not suit you. If you have light brown or blonde hair, going blonde may be an easy transition.

Lifestyle

Consider your lifestyle when deciding if blonde hair is right for you. If you spend a lot of time outdoors, blonde hair can be more susceptible to damage and fading from the sun. If you don't have a lot of time for maintenance, blonde hair may not be the best choice as it requires more upkeep than other hair colors.

Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing the Right Shade of Blonde

Once you've determined that blonde hair is right for you, it's time to choose the right shade. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

As mentioned earlier, your skin tone plays a big role in which shade of blonde will look best on you. If you have cool undertones, choose a cool blonde like ash or platinum. If you have warm undertones, choose a warm blonde like honey or golden. If you have neutral undertones, you can choose either cool or warm blondes.

Step 2: Consider Your Natural Hair Color

Your natural hair color can also affect which shade of blonde will suit you. If you have very dark hair, consider a warm blonde that won't make your hair look brassy. If you have light brown or blonde hair, you have more flexibility in choosing a shade.

Step 3: Think About Your Eye Color

Your eye color can help you choose a shade of blonde that will complement it. If you have blue or green eyes, consider a cooler blonde. If you have brown eyes, consider a warmer blonde. If you have hazel eyes, you can choose either a warm or cool blonde.

Step 4: Consult with a Professional

Choosing the right shade of blonde can be tricky, so it's always a good idea to consult with a professional stylist. They can recommend shades that will complement your skin tone, natural hair color, and eye color.

Tips for Maintaining Your Blonde Hair

Blonde hair requires more maintenance than other hair colors. Here are some tips to keep your blonde hair looking its best:

Use a Purple Shampoo

Blonde hair can easily turn brassy, especially if you have warm undertones. To keep your hair looking cool and bright, use a purple shampoo once a week. Purple cancels out orange and yellow tones, leaving your hair looking fresh.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Blonde hair is more susceptible to damage and fading from the sun. Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray to keep your hair looking its best.

Use a Deep Conditioner

Blonde hair can become dry and brittle, especially if you bleach it. Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

FAQs

Q: Will blonde hair suit me?

A: It depends on your skin tone, eye color, and natural hair color. If you have cool or neutral undertones, blue or green eyes, and light brown or blonde hair, blonde hair may suit you.

Q: What shade of blonde should I choose?

A: It depends on your skin tone, natural hair color, and eye color. Consult with a professional stylist to determine which shade will suit you best.

Q: How often do I need to touch up my roots?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how obvious the roots are. Generally, you should touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Blonde Hair

Blonde hair requires special care to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

Use a Gentle Shampoo

Blonde hair can be fragile, so it's important to use a gentle shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

Avoid Heat Styling

Blonde hair can become damaged and brittle from heat styling. Try to air dry your hair whenever possible, and use a heat protectant spray when you do use heat styling tools.

Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Use a Hair Mask

Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy. Look for a mask specifically designed for blonde hair.

With these tips, you can determine if blonde hair is right for you and keep it looking its best. Remember to consult with a professional stylist if you're not sure which shade to choose, and always use products specifically designed for blonde hair.
