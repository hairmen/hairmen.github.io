Fade Haircut Styles For Black Guys A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Oct 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a black guy looking for a stylish and versatile haircut, you can't go wrong with a fade. Fades have been a popular haircut choice for black men for decades, and for good reason. They're low-maintenance, versatile, and can be customized to suit any individual style. In this guide, we'll walk you through different types of fades, how to get them, and how to care for your hair afterwards.

Types of Fades

There are several types of fades to choose from, depending on your personal style and the length of your hair:

Low Fade

A low fade is a popular choice for black guys, as it blends well with their hair texture. This fade is tapered at the back and sides, and can be cut to any length. It's a great choice for those who want a subtle, low-maintenance haircut.

High Fade

A high fade is a more dramatic look, with a sharp contrast between the longer hair on top and the shaved sides and back. This style is great for those who want a bold, edgy look.

Temp Fade

A temp fade, also known as a temple fade, is a subtle fade that starts at the temples and fades into the rest of the hair. This style is great for guys who want a low-maintenance haircut that's still stylish.

Bald Fade

A bald fade, also known as a skin fade, is a dramatic fade that goes from a longer length on top to a shaved head. This style is great for those who want a bold, edgy look.

How to Get a Fade Haircut

If you're looking to get a fade haircut, it's best to visit a professional barber. They'll be able to give you a precise cut that suits your face shape and hair type. Before you go to the barber, it's a good idea to have a few pictures of the style you want, so you can communicate your vision clearly.

Step-by-Step Guide to Fading Your Hair

If you're feeling adventurous, you can try fading your hair at home. Here's a step-by-step guide to get you started:

  1. Start with a high-quality clipper with adjustable blades.
  2. Choose the length you want for the top of your hair and start clipping.
  3. Next, switch to a shorter clipper blade to fade the sides and back of your hair.
  4. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up, blending the short hair with the longer hair on top.
  5. Take your time and be patient. Fading your hair takes practice, so don't get discouraged if it doesn't look perfect on the first try.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fade

Once you have your fade, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking fresh. Here are a few tips:

  • Visit your barber regularly for touch-ups.
  • Wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Use a good quality conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQs About Fade Haircuts for Black Guys

1. Can I get a fade if I have relaxed hair?

Yes, you can get a fade with relaxed hair. Just make sure to communicate your vision clearly with your barber, as relaxed hair can be more fragile and require special care.

2. How often should I get a touch-up?

It's best to visit your barber for touch-ups every 2-3 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows.

3. Can I style my hair after getting a fade?

Yes, you can style your hair after getting a fade. Just make sure to use quality hair products that won't damage your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Black Guys with Fades

Here are a few additional hair care tips for black guys with fades:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, as they can damage the hair and scalp.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent breakage and keep your hair looking smooth.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles, as they can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote healthy hair growth.

With this guide, you should be able to find the perfect fade haircut style for your individual taste and hair type. Remember to communicate clearly with your barber, take good care of your hair, and experiment with different styles until you find the one that works best for you.
