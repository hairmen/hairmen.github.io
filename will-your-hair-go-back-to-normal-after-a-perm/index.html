Will Your Hair Go Back to Normal After a Perm?

If you have recently gotten a perm, you may be wondering if your hair will ever go back to its original state. Perms can be a great way to add texture and volume to your hair, but they can also damage your hair if not done properly. So, will your hair go back to normal after a perm? The answer is yes, but it will take some time and patience.

How to

If you want to get your hair back to its natural state after a perm, you will need to follow some steps. Here are the things you can do:

Step by step

  1. Wait for at least 3 months before taking any action.
  2. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup on your hair.
  3. Use a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture to your hair.
  4. Trim your hair regularly to get rid of any damaged ends.
  5. Avoid using heat styling tools and chemical treatments on your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help your hair go back to normal after a perm:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dye or bleach, until it has fully recovered.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as they can further damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can cause breakage.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about whether your hair will go back to normal after a perm:

  • How long does it take for hair to go back to normal after a perm? It can take several months for your hair to fully recover from a perm.
  • Can you speed up the process of getting your hair back to normal after a perm? No, you cannot speed up the process of getting your hair back to normal after a perm. It takes time and patience.
  • Will your hair be the same as it was before the perm? Your hair may not be exactly the same as it was before the perm, but it will be close.

Hair Care

After getting a perm, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to restore moisture to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair.

In conclusion, if you are wondering if your hair will go back to normal after a perm, the answer is yes. However, it will take time and patience. Follow the steps and tips outlined above to help your hair recover from a perm and prevent further damage in the future.
