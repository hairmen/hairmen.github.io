Is It Bad To Wax Hair The Truth Revealed

Written by Aprilia Jul 10, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of shaving every other day and want a long-lasting solution for hair removal? Waxing seems like the perfect answer, right? But before you book your appointment, you need to know - is it bad to wax hair?

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Wax Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Waxing
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Waxing

Description

Waxing is a popular hair removal method that involves applying hot or cold wax on the skin and removing it with a strip of cloth or paper. The process pulls out hair from the root, leaving the skin smooth and hair-free for weeks.

However, waxing can be painful, especially for those with sensitive skin. It can also cause ingrown hairs, redness, and irritation. So, is it bad to wax hair? The answer is - it depends.

How to Wax Hair

Waxing can be done at home or at a salon. If you decide to do it yourself, you will need waxing strips, wax, and a wax warmer. If you are new to waxing, it is recommended to visit a salon first to get the hang of the process.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to wax hair:

  1. Clean the area to be waxed with soap and water.
  2. Apply powder to the area to absorb any moisture.
  3. Heat the wax in a wax warmer according to the instructions.
  4. Apply a thin layer of wax on the skin in the direction of hair growth.
  5. Place a waxing strip on the wax and press it down firmly.
  6. Pull the strip in the opposite direction of hair growth in one swift motion.
  7. Repeat the process until all the hair is removed.
  8. Apply a soothing lotion or oil to the skin to reduce redness and irritation.

Tips for Waxing

To minimize the risk of irritation and ingrown hairs, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Exfoliate the skin before waxing to remove dead skin cells and allow the hair to come out easily.
  • Trim the hair to a quarter of an inch before waxing to avoid breakage.
  • Apply a numbing cream or spray to reduce pain and discomfort.
  • Avoid waxing during menstruation as the skin is more sensitive at this time.
  • Do not wax over sunburned, irritated, or broken skin.

FAQs

1. Is it bad to wax hair?

Waxing can cause pain, redness, and irritation, especially for those with sensitive skin. However, it is generally safe if done correctly.

2. Can waxing cause ingrown hairs?

Yes, waxing can lead to ingrown hairs if the hair breaks during the process or if the skin is not exfoliated properly before waxing.

3. How long does waxing last?

Waxing can last up to four weeks, depending on the hair growth cycle and the area of the body being waxed.

Hair Care After Waxing

After waxing, it is essential to take care of the skin to avoid further irritation or infection. Here are some tips to follow:

  • Avoid hot showers or baths for at least 24 hours after waxing.
  • Avoid tight clothing or synthetic fabrics that can cause friction on the skin.
  • Avoid exposure to sun or tanning beds for at least 24 hours after waxing.
  • Apply an antiseptic cream or lotion to the skin to prevent infection.

So, is it bad to wax hair? The answer is no, as long as you follow the proper procedure and take care of your skin after waxing. If you have sensitive skin or are prone to ingrown hairs, it is recommended to consult a dermatologist before waxing.
