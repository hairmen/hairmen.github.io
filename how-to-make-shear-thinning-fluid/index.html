Description

Shear thinning fluid is a type of non-Newtonian fluid that becomes thinner and less viscous when subjected to shear stress. This means that when you apply a force to it, such as stirring or shaking, it will become more liquid-like and easier to work with. Shear thinning fluids are commonly used in a variety of industries, including cosmetics, food processing, and oil drilling.

How to Make Shear Thinning Fluid

Making your own shear thinning fluid is surprisingly easy and only requires a few simple ingredients. Here's what you'll need:

  • Water
  • Cornstarch or potato starch
  • Food coloring (optional)

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to make your own shear thinning fluid:

  1. Mix 1/4 cup of cornstarch or potato starch with 1/2 cup of water in a bowl.
  2. Add a few drops of food coloring if desired.
  3. Stir the mixture until it's smooth and consistent.
  4. Add another 1/2 cup of water and stir again.
  5. You should now have a mixture that's thick and gooey. If it's too thick, add a little more water until you reach the desired consistency.
  6. Test your shear thinning fluid by placing some in your hand and squeezing it tightly. It should feel solid but turn to liquid when you release your grip.
  7. Your shear thinning fluid is now ready to use!

Tips for Using Shear Thinning Fluid

Here are a few tips for using your shear thinning fluid:

  • Use a whisk or blender to mix your shear thinning fluid for best results.
  • Experiment with different ratios of cornstarch or potato starch to water to achieve different consistencies.
  • Shear thinning fluids can be messy, so be sure to work on a protected surface and wear gloves if necessary.
  • Store your shear thinning fluid in an airtight container in the fridge when not in use.

FAQs

What is shear thinning fluid?

Shear thinning fluid is a type of non-Newtonian fluid that becomes thinner and less viscous when subjected to shear stress.

What are some common uses for shear thinning fluid?

Shear thinning fluids are commonly used in a variety of industries, including cosmetics, food processing, and oil drilling.

Can I use something other than cornstarch or potato starch?

Yes, other common ingredients used to make shear thinning fluid include arrowroot powder, tapioca starch, and even flour.

Hair Care Benefits of Shear Thinning Fluid

Did you know that shear thinning fluid can also be used in hair care products? When used in shampoos and conditioners, shear thinning fluids can help to create a more luxurious and silky texture. They can also help to thicken products without making them too heavy or greasy. So, if you're looking for a way to upgrade your hair care routine, consider trying out some shear thinning fluid-based products!
