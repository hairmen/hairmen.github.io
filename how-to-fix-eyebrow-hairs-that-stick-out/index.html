How To Fix Eyebrow Hairs That Stick Out A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with eyebrow hairs that just won't stay in place? Whether you have naturally unruly brows or you're dealing with post-waxing regrowth, there are several ways to tame those stray hairs and achieve a sleek, polished look. In this article, we'll walk you through how to fix eyebrow hairs that stick out, from simple grooming techniques to more advanced styling tips.

How to Fix Eyebrow Hairs that Stick Out: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Brush Your Brows

The first step in taming unruly eyebrow hairs is to brush them into place. Using a spoolie brush or a clean mascara wand, gently brush your brows in the direction of hair growth. This will help to separate any hairs that are clumped together and make it easier to see which hairs need to be trimmed or styled.

Step 2: Trim Stray Hairs

If you have long eyebrow hairs that are sticking out, you may need to trim them to achieve a more polished look. Using a pair of sharp eyebrow scissors, carefully trim any hairs that are longer than the rest. Be sure to only trim a little bit at a time, as it's easy to overdo it and end up with uneven brows.

Step 3: Fill in Sparse Areas

If you have sparse areas in your brows, you can use an eyebrow pencil or powder to fill them in and create a more defined shape. Choose a shade that matches your natural brow color and use short, feathery strokes to mimic the look of natural hair. Be sure to blend the product well to avoid any harsh lines.

Step 4: Use Brow Gel

To keep your brows in place all day, finish off your look with a brow gel. Choose a clear or tinted formula depending on your preference and apply it in the direction of hair growth. This will help to hold your hairs in place and prevent them from sticking out throughout the day.

Tips for Taming Unruly Eyebrow Hairs

Here are some additional tips for fixing eyebrow hairs that stick out:

  • Don't overpluck your brows - this can lead to sparse areas that are difficult to fill in
  • Use a magnifying mirror to get a closer look at individual hairs
  • Consider using a brow pomade for a more defined look
  • Experiment with different brow shapes to find the one that flatters your face the most

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use hair gel to tame my eyebrow hairs?

A: While you could technically use hair gel on your brows, it's not recommended. Hair gel is formulated for the thicker, coarser hair on your head and may be too heavy for the delicate hairs in your brows. Instead, opt for a product specifically designed for eyebrows, like a brow gel or wax.

Q: What if I accidentally trim too much off my eyebrows?

A: If you accidentally trim too much off your eyebrows, don't panic! You can use an eyebrow pencil or powder to fill in the sparse areas until the hair grows back. In the meantime, try to resist the urge to keep trimming - it's better to have a few long hairs than no hairs at all.

Hair Care for Perfect Eyebrows

In addition to grooming your eyebrows regularly, there are a few things you can do to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid overplucking or waxing your brows too frequently
  • Use a gentle cleanser to remove makeup and debris from your brows
  • Avoid exposing your brows to harsh chemicals or excessive heat
  • Consider using a brow serum or oil to promote healthy hair growth

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve perfectly groomed eyebrows that stay in place all day long. Whether you prefer a natural look or a more defined shape, there's a solution out there for you. So go ahead and tackle those unruly brow hairs with confidence!
