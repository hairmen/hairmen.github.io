How To Get Rid Of Temporary Red Hair Dye

Written by Luffy Jul 08, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Temporary hair dye can be a fun and exciting way to switch up your look. However, sometimes the color doesn't turn out as expected, or you may need to remove it quickly for work or other events. In this post, we'll go over how to get rid of temporary red hair dye.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

Before starting the removal process, it's important to know that temporary hair dye typically lasts anywhere from 1-6 washes. If you're looking to speed up the process, there are a few ways to do so:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo to strip the color from your hair
  • Wash your hair with hot water
  • Try a DIY vinegar rinse

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get rid of temporary red hair dye:

  1. Wet your hair thoroughly
  2. Apply a clarifying shampoo to your hair and work it in thoroughly
  3. Leave the shampoo in for 5-10 minutes
  4. Rinse your hair thoroughly with hot water
  5. If the color is still present, mix equal parts white vinegar and water and apply it to your hair
  6. Leave the mixture in for 10-15 minutes
  7. Rinse your hair thoroughly with hot water
  8. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and leave it in for 10-15 minutes
  9. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water
  10. Repeat this process as needed until the color is completely removed

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when removing temporary hair dye:

  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair until the color is completely removed
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent further damage to your hair
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can dry it out and lead to further damage
  • If you have sensitive skin, be cautious when using vinegar as it can cause irritation

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about removing temporary hair dye:

How long does temporary hair dye last?

Temporary hair dye typically lasts anywhere from 1-6 washes.

Can I remove temporary hair dye with just hot water?

While hot water can help remove temporary hair dye, it's typically not enough on its own.

Will removing temporary hair dye damage my hair?

As long as you follow the proper steps and use a deep conditioner afterwards, removing temporary hair dye shouldn't cause significant damage to your hair.

Hair Care

After removing temporary hair dye, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair frequently
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

Overall, removing temporary hair dye can be a simple process as long as you follow the proper steps and take care of your hair afterwards. If you're unsure about any of the steps, consult a professional stylist for advice.
