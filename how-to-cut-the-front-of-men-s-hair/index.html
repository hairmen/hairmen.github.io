How To Cut The Front Of Men s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to freshen up your style by trimming the front of your hair? This guide will walk you through the process of cutting the front of men's hair. From tools and techniques to tips and tricks, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You Will Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Blending Your Hair
  • Cleaning Up
  • Tips and Tricks

Tools You Will Need

Before you begin cutting the front of your hair, you will need a few tools:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Hair spray (optional)

Preparation

Before you begin cutting your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut, and you may end up cutting more than you intended. Comb your hair thoroughly to remove any tangles, and decide on the length you want to cut your hair to.

Sectioning Your Hair

Using a comb, part your hair down the middle, creating two equal sections. Then, using the comb, create a part from the top of one ear to the top of the other ear, dividing your hair into four equal sections. Clip the top sections up and out of the way, leaving the front sections down.

Cutting Your Hair

Using your scissors, start at the side of your head and cut a small amount of hair at a time. Be sure to keep your scissors angled downward to create a natural-looking cut. Use your comb to lift the hair up and away from your head, making it easier to cut.

Be sure to cut both sides equally, and take your time. It's better to cut small amounts of hair at a time rather than cutting too much and having to start over.

Blending Your Hair

Once you have finished cutting the front of your hair, it's time to blend it with the rest of your hair. Unclip the top sections of your hair and use your scissors to blend the front sections with the top sections. Again, take your time and cut small amounts of hair at a time. Use your comb to lift the hair up and away from your head, making it easier to cut.

Cleaning Up

Once you have finished cutting and blending your hair, use your clippers (if you have them) to clean up the back and sides of your head. Be sure to trim any stray hairs and create a clean, even look.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with small amounts of hair and cut slowly to avoid cutting too much.
  • Use your comb to lift the hair up and away from your head, making it easier to cut.
  • Take your time and be patient.
  • If you're not confident in your cutting abilities, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair, but be sure to take your time and start with small amounts of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.

Do I need clippers to cut my hair?

No, clippers are optional. You can use scissors to cut your hair, but clippers can make cleaning up the back and sides easier.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your personal preference and hair type. Some people prefer to cut their hair every 4-6 weeks, while others may go longer between haircuts.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, be sure to wash and style it as usual. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair looking healthy and shiny. If you used hair spray during the cutting process, be sure to wash it out thoroughly to avoid buildup.

Now that you know how to cut the front of men's hair, you can give yourself a fresh new look anytime. Just remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll have great-looking hair in no time!
