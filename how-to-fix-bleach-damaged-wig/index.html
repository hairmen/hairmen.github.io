How To Fix Bleach Damaged Wig A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 14, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’ve recently bleached your wig and ended up with a disaster, don’t panic! Bleaching wigs can be tricky, and sometimes even the most experienced wig wearers end up with damaged hair. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to fix bleach damaged wig and restore it to its former glory. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of fixing bleach damaged wig, step-by-step.

Description

When we bleach our wig, we strip it of its natural color and this can cause a lot of damage to the hair. The bleach can cause the wig to become brittle, dry, and damaged. If you’re noticing that your wig is dry, frizzy, and breaking, then it’s likely that it’s been damaged by bleach. You can fix bleach damaged wig at home with a few simple steps.

How to Fix Bleach Damaged Wig

Step 1: Wash Your Wig

The first step to fixing bleach damaged wig is to wash it thoroughly. Use a gentle shampoo and warm water to get rid of any build-up or residue on the hair. Rinse your wig with cool water until all the shampoo is gone.

Step 2: Deep Condition Your Wig

After washing your wig, it’s important to deep condition it. Use a hair mask or deep conditioner that’s specifically designed for damaged hair. Apply the conditioner generously to the hair and let it sit for at least 30 minutes. Rinse your wig with cool water until all the conditioner is gone.

Step 3: Detangle Your Wig

Once you’ve deep conditioned your wig, it’s time to detangle it. Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to gently comb through the hair. Be gentle and take your time, as the hair may be fragile and prone to breakage.

Step 4: Apply a Protein Treatment

If your wig is still feeling weak and brittle, you can apply a protein treatment to strengthen the hair. Look for a protein treatment that’s specifically designed for damaged hair, and follow the instructions on the packaging.

Step 5: Rinse Your Wig

After applying the protein treatment, rinse your wig with cool water until all the product is gone. This will help to seal in the protein and restore the hair’s strength and elasticity.

Step 6: Air Dry Your Wig

When it comes to drying your wig, it’s best to let it air dry naturally. Avoid using heat tools or blow dryers, as these can further damage the hair. Gently squeeze out any excess water with a towel and then lay the wig on a clean, dry towel to air dry.

Tips

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid further damage to your wig.
  • Be gentle when detangling your wig to avoid breakage.
  • Apply a protein treatment to help strengthen the hair.
  • Avoid using heat tools or blow dryers on your wig.
  • Store your wig properly to avoid tangling and damage.

FAQ

Can I fix bleach damaged wig at home?

Yes, you can fix bleach damaged wig at home with a few simple steps. Wash your wig, deep condition it, detangle it, apply a protein treatment, and air dry it.

How can I prevent my wig from becoming damaged?

To prevent your wig from becoming damaged, avoid over-processing it with bleach or other chemicals. Use gentle hair care products and store your wig properly when you’re not wearing it.

Hair Care

After fixing bleach damaged wig, it’s important to take care of it properly to prevent further damage. Use gentle hair care products, avoid heat tools and blow dryers, and store your wig properly when you’re not wearing it.

Fixing bleach damaged wig may seem like a daunting task, but with a little patience and care, you can restore your wig to its former glory. Follow these steps and tips to fix bleach damaged wig at home, and enjoy beautiful, healthy hair once again!
