How To Cut Hair At Home Boy With Scissors

Written by Daniel Nov 22, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to save money on haircuts or can't get to a barber, cutting your son's hair at home with scissors is a great option. In this guide, we'll walk you through step-by-step how to cut hair at home boy with scissors.

Description

Cutting hair at home can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be an easy and cost-effective way to keep your son's hair looking sharp. Whether you're looking to give him a simple trim or a more complex haircut, we've got you covered.

How to Cut Hair at Home Boy with Scissors

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather a few essential tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Scissors (hair-cutting scissors are best, but regular scissors will work)
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A towel or cape to drape over your son's shoulders

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Son's Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your son's hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to cut and ensure a more even result.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Using your comb, divide your son's hair into sections. Start by parting the hair down the middle, and then divide each side into two more sections. Clip each section out of the way with a hair clip or elastic band.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with the back of your son's head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut with your scissors along the length of your fingers. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way around the head.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you've finished cutting each section, use your scissors to blend the hair together. Hold the scissors vertically and make small snips into the hair to blend the different sections together. Be careful not to cut too much or create any choppy lines.

Step 6: Trim the Bangs

If your son has bangs, trim them last. Comb the bangs straight down and cut them straight across. Start with a small amount and trim more if needed. Remember, it's always better to err on the side of caution and cut less than to cut too much.

Tips

  • Work slowly and carefully, taking small sections of hair at a time.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp, making it easier to cut.
  • Don't be afraid to ask your son what he wants or to show you pictures of haircuts he likes.
  • If you make a mistake, don't panic. Hair grows back, and you can always take him to a barber to fix any mistakes.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my son's hair?
A: It depends on the length and style of his hair. As a general rule, most boys' hair should be cut every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to cut hair?
A: While hair-cutting scissors are best, regular scissors can work in a pinch. Just make sure they're sharp and clean.

Hair Care

After you've finished cutting your son's hair, it's important to take care of it to ensure it stays healthy and looks great. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash his hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep his hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down the hair.
  • Trim his hair regularly to prevent split ends and damage.

Cutting hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to keep your son's hair looking great without ever leaving the house. Happy cutting!
