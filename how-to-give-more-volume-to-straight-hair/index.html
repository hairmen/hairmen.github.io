How To Give More Volume To Straight Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Jun 23, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of your straight hair looking limp and lifeless? Do you want to add more volume and bounce to your locks? You're in luck! In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to give more volume to straight hair. From hair care tips to styling techniques, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care Tips
  • Blow Drying Techniques
  • Teasing or Backcombing
  • Using Products
  • Getting the Right Haircut
  • FAQs

Hair Care Tips

The key to voluminous hair is healthy hair. Before we jump into styling techniques, let's talk about hair care tips that can help give your hair more body and volume.

1. Shampoo Less Often

Over-washing your hair can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and flat. Try shampooing your hair every other day or use a dry shampoo in between washes.

2. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Look for products that are specifically designed to add volume to hair. These products contain ingredients that can help lift your hair from the roots and create the appearance of more body.

3. Apply a Lightweight Leave-In Conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner can help add moisture to your hair without weighing it down. Look for a lightweight formula that won't leave your hair feeling greasy or limp.

4. Rinse with Cool Water

After conditioning your hair, rinse with cool water to close the hair cuticles and help your hair retain moisture. This can also help create more shine and bounce.


Blow Drying Techniques

Blow drying your hair can be a great way to add volume and shape. Here are some tips to get the most out of your blow drying routine.

1. Use a Round Brush

When blow drying your hair, use a round brush to create lift at the roots. Start at the crown of your head and work your way down, using the brush to pull your hair away from your scalp.

2. Flip Your Hair Upside Down

Flipping your hair upside down while blow drying can help create more volume at the roots. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to avoid frizz and enhance your natural texture.

3. Finish with a Blast of Cool Air

After blow drying your hair, finish with a blast of cool air to set your style and add extra volume. This can help lock in the shape of your hair and create a longer-lasting style.


Teasing or Backcombing

Teasing or backcombing your hair can be a great way to add volume and texture. Here's how to do it:

1. Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into small sections. Use clips or hair ties to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.

2. Tease Your Hair

Using a fine-tooth comb, gently backcomb your hair at the roots. Start at the crown of your head and work your way down, teasing small sections at a time. Be careful not to overdo it, as this can damage your hair.

3. Smooth the Top Layer

After teasing your hair, use a soft-bristled brush to smooth the top layer. This can help create a more polished look and hide any teasing that may be visible.


Using Products

There are many products available that can help add volume and texture to your hair. Here are some of our favorites:

1. Mousse

Mousse is a great product for adding volume and hold to your hair. Apply a small amount to damp hair and distribute evenly, focusing on the roots.

2. Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo is a lifesaver for those days when you don't have time to wash your hair. It can help absorb excess oil and add volume to your roots.

3. Texturizing Spray

Texturizing spray can help add texture and body to your hair. Spray it on dry hair and use your fingers to scrunch and tousle your hair for a messy, beachy look.


Getting the Right Haircut

The right haircut can make all the difference when it comes to adding volume to straight hair. Here are some things to keep in mind:

1. Layers

Adding layers to your hair can help create movement and body. Ask your stylist for long layers that start at your chin or collarbone.

2. Blunt Ends

Blunt ends can make your hair look thicker and fuller. Ask your stylist for a blunt cut that falls just below your shoulders.

3. Bangs

Adding bangs to your hairstyle can create the illusion of more volume. Opt for side-swept bangs that start at the crown of your head.


FAQs

1. Can I add volume to my hair without using heat?

Yes! You can try using a volumizing shampoo, applying a lightweight leave-in conditioner, and using a texturizing spray to add volume to your hair without using heat.

2. How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it more often. If you have dry hair, you can probably get away with washing it less often. Aim to shampoo your hair every two to three days.

3. How can I prevent my hair from falling flat throughout the day?

Try using a hairspray or texturizing spray to hold your style in place. You can also try backcombing your hair at the roots for added lift.


Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential for maintaining its health and appearance. Here are some additional tips to keep your hair looking its best:

1. Protect Your Hair

Use a heat protectant spray before using any hot tools on your hair. This can help prevent damage and breakage.

2. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to include plenty of protein, iron, and vitamin C in your diet.

By following these tips and techniques, you can add more volume and body to your straight hair. Experiment with different styling methods and products to find what works best for you. With a little bit of effort, you can have the voluminous, bouncy hair of your dreams!
