Change Your Hair Type Surgery Everything You Need To Know

Written by Robby Nov 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've ever wished for a different hair type, you're not alone. Many people struggle with hair that is too curly, too straight, too frizzy, or too thin. But what if you could change your hair type with surgery? It may sound too good to be true, but hair type surgery is a real thing. In this article, we'll explore everything you need to know about this procedure, including how it works, what to expect, and how to care for your new hair.

Description

Change your hair type surgery is a cosmetic procedure that changes the texture of your hair. It is typically performed by a plastic surgeon or a dermatologist who specializes in hair restoration. The surgery can be used to make curly hair straight or vice versa, to add volume and thickness to thin hair, or to reduce frizz and flyaways. The procedure involves altering the structure of the hair follicle, either by breaking down the bonds that create curls or by creating new ones.

How to

The first step in getting hair type surgery is to find a qualified surgeon or dermatologist who specializes in the procedure. You'll want to do your research and read reviews to make sure you choose a reputable provider. Once you've found a doctor, you'll have a consultation to discuss your hair goals and determine whether you're a good candidate for the surgery. You'll also discuss the risks and benefits of the procedure and any pre- and post-operative care that will be required.

If you decide to move forward with the surgery, you'll schedule a date for the procedure. Depending on the type of surgery you're having, the procedure may take several hours. You'll be given anesthesia to keep you comfortable during the surgery. After the surgery, you'll need to follow your doctor's instructions for caring for your hair and scalp as you heal.

Step by Step

Step 1: Find a Qualified Provider

Research plastic surgeons or dermatologists who specialize in hair type surgery. Read reviews and check their credentials to ensure they are qualified to perform the procedure.

Step 2: Schedule a Consultation

Meet with the provider to discuss your hair goals and determine whether you're a good candidate for the surgery. You'll also discuss the risks and benefits of the procedure and any pre- and post-operative care that will be required.

Step 3: Schedule the Procedure

If you decide to move forward with the surgery, schedule a date for the procedure. Depending on the type of surgery you're having, the procedure may take several hours.

Step 4: Follow Post-Operative Care

After the surgery, follow your doctor's instructions for caring for your hair and scalp as you heal. This may include avoiding heat styling, using special shampoos and conditioners, and avoiding certain activities.

Tips

  • Research providers carefully to find a qualified surgeon or dermatologist
  • Be honest with your provider about your hair goals and any concerns you have
  • Follow all pre- and post-operative instructions carefully to ensure the best possible outcome
  • Be realistic about what the surgery can and cannot achieve
  • Consider the cost of the procedure and whether it fits within your budget

FAQ

Is hair type surgery safe?

Like any surgery, hair type surgery carries some risks. These may include infection, scarring, and changes in hair texture or growth. However, when performed by a qualified provider, the risks are generally low.

How long does it take to recover from hair type surgery?

Recovery times vary depending on the type of surgery you have. In general, you can expect to return to work and normal activities within a few days to a week. You'll need to follow your doctor's instructions for caring for your hair and scalp during the recovery period.

Will my new hair type be permanent?

The results of hair type surgery are generally long-lasting, but not always permanent. Your hair may eventually revert to its original texture over time. However, you can prolong the results by following a healthy hair care routine and avoiding damage to your hair.

Hair Care

After hair type surgery, it's important to take care of your new hair to ensure the best possible results. Your doctor will give you specific instructions for caring for your hair during the recovery period. In general, you should avoid heat styling, harsh chemicals, and tight hairstyles that can damage your hair. Use gentle shampoos and conditioners that are designed for your new hair type, and avoid over-washing or over-brushing your hair. Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

Change your hair type surgery can be a life-changing procedure for those who are unhappy with their natural hair texture. By finding a qualified provider, following pre- and post-operative instructions carefully, and taking care of your new hair, you can achieve the hair of your dreams.
