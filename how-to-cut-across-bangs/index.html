How To Cut Across Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Oct 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your current hairstyle and looking for a change? One way to switch up your look is by cutting across bangs. Not only is this a popular trend, but it’s also a great way to frame your face and add some dimension to your hair. In this guide, we’ll go over the step-by-step process of cutting across bangs, as well as some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

How to Cut Across Bangs

Before we get started, it’s important to note that cutting your own hair can be risky. We recommend seeing a professional stylist if you’re unsure about your abilities. However, if you’re feeling confident and want to give it a try, here’s how to cut across bangs:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. It’s important to have clean, dry hair before you start cutting.

Next, use a comb to separate the hair you want to cut into a triangle shape. The base of the triangle should be at your hairline, and the tip should be at the middle of your forehead.

Clip the rest of your hair back to keep it out of the way.

Step 2: Cut Your Bangs

Using sharp hair cutting scissors, start cutting your bangs. It’s important to cut your hair at an angle, not straight across. This will create a more natural-looking finish.

Start by cutting a small amount of hair at a time – you can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s been cut.

As you cut, comb your hair down to check the length and make sure it’s even.

Once you’ve cut your bangs to the desired length, use the tip of your scissors to create some texture by snipping into the ends of your hair.

Step 3: Style Your Bangs

Once you’ve finished cutting, it’s time to style your new bangs. Use a blow dryer and a round brush to create volume and shape.

You can also use a straightener or curling iron to add some waves or curls.

Tips for Cutting Across Bangs

Here are some additional tips to help you get the perfect cut:

Tip 1: Use the Right Tools

Make sure you have a sharp pair of hair cutting scissors – using regular scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.

It’s also helpful to have a comb and clips to keep your hair in place.

Tip 2: Start Small

It’s better to cut a little bit of hair at a time than to cut too much all at once.

Remember, you can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s been cut.

Tip 3: Cut at an Angle

Cutting your hair at an angle will create a more natural-looking finish.

Start by cutting your hair longer than you want it, and then gradually work your way to the desired length.

FAQ

How often should I cut my bangs?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how often you want to maintain your bangs.

On average, most people need to trim their bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your bangs if you have curly hair.

However, keep in mind that curly hair can be more difficult to cut evenly. We recommend seeing a professional stylist if you’re unsure about your abilities.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my bangs?

If you make a mistake while cutting your bangs, don’t panic.

You can always adjust the length or style them differently to hide any mistakes.

If you’re really unhappy with the results, consider seeing a professional stylist to fix the problem.

Hair Care

Now that you’ve cut your bangs, it’s important to take care of them to keep them looking their best.

Tip 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.

Use a shampoo and conditioner that’s appropriate for your hair type.

Tip 2: Use Heat Protectant

If you use heat tools to style your hair, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

Tip 3: Trim Your Bangs

To maintain your bangs, trim them every 4-6 weeks.

This will keep them looking fresh and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut across bangs, it’s time to give it a try! Remember to take your time, start small, and use the right tools. With a little practice, you’ll be a pro at cutting your own hair in no time.
