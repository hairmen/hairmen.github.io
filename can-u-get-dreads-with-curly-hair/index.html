Can You Get Dreads With Curly Hair

If you have curly hair, you may be wondering if you can get dreads. The answer is yes! While it may be more difficult to start dreadlocks with curly hair, it is possible with the right techniques and hair care.

How To Get Dreads with Curly Hair

Step 1: Decide on the size and shape of your dreads. You can choose to have thick or thin dreads, as well as the shape of your dreadlocks.

Step 2: Section your hair into the desired size of your dreads. Use a comb to create even sections and secure each section with a hair tie or clip.

Step 3: Use a dread wax or gel to help your hair lock together. Apply a small amount of the product to each section of hair, and use your fingers to twist and roll the hair to form the dreadlocks.

Step 4: Use a crochet hook to tighten the dreads. This will help to make the dreads more secure and prevent them from unraveling.

Step 5: Continue to maintain your dreads by washing them regularly with a residue-free shampoo and re-twisting them as needed.

Tips for Getting Dreads with Curly Hair

1. Use a dread wax or gel specifically designed for curly hair to help your hair lock together.

2. Be patient! It may take longer for your curly hair to lock into dreads compared to straight hair, but with time and proper care, it will happen.

3. Consider getting professional help to start your dreadlocks. A stylist who specializes in dreadlocks can help you achieve the look you want.

FAQ

Q: Will my curly hair get damaged from getting dreads?

A: No, as long as you take proper care of your dreads and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools, your curly hair should not get damaged.

Q: Can I still wash my hair with dreads?

A: Yes, it's important to wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to keep your dreads clean and healthy.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads with Curly Hair

1. Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your dreads.

2. Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair regularly.

3. Re-twist your dreads as needed to keep them looking neat and tidy.

4. Wear a silk or satin scarf or bonnet at night to prevent frizz and breakage.

In conclusion, yes, you can get dreads with curly hair! It may take a bit more time and effort, but with the right techniques and hair care, you can achieve the look you want. Be patient and don't be afraid to seek professional help if needed. With proper care, your dreads will look amazing for years to come!
