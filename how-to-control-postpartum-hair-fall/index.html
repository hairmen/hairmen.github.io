How To Control Postpartum Hair Fall

Written by Robby Aug 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Postpartum hair fall is a common problem that new mothers face. Losing hair after giving birth can be alarming, but it’s a normal part of the postpartum process. The good news is that there are ways to control postpartum hair fall. In this article, we’ll show you how to take care of your hair during this phase and keep it healthy and strong.

Table of Contents

  • How to Control Postpartum Hair Fall
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Postpartum Hair Fall
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care During Postpartum Period

How to Control Postpartum Hair Fall

Postpartum hair fall usually starts around 3-6 months after giving birth. It’s caused by hormonal changes in the body, and it’s completely normal. However, there are things you can do to minimize hair fall and promote hair growth:

  • Eat a healthy diet
  • Take supplements
  • Manage stress levels
  • Get enough sleep
  • Massage your scalp
  • Use gentle hair care products

Step by Step Guide

Here’s a step by step guide on how to control postpartum hair fall:

  1. Eat a healthy diet – Make sure you’re getting enough protein, vitamins, and minerals in your diet. Eat foods that are rich in nutrients that promote hair growth, such as eggs, spinach, and sweet potatoes.
  2. Take supplements – Talk to your doctor about taking supplements that promote hair growth, such as biotin, iron, and vitamin D.
  3. Manage stress levels – Stress can contribute to hair fall, so make sure you’re managing your stress levels. Try relaxation techniques such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises.
  4. Get enough sleep – Lack of sleep can also contribute to hair fall. Make sure you’re getting enough sleep every night.
  5. Massage your scalp – Massaging your scalp can improve blood circulation and promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes every day.
  6. Use gentle hair care products – Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes or bleach. Use gentle, natural hair care products that are free from sulfates and parabens.

Tips for Preventing Postpartum Hair Fall

Here are some additional tips for preventing postpartum hair fall:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles – Tight hairstyles such as braids, ponytails, and buns can put stress on your hair and contribute to hair fall. Opt for looser hairstyles instead.
  • Avoid heat styling – Heat styling tools such as curling irons and straighteners can damage your hair and contribute to hair fall. Avoid using them as much as possible.
  • Be gentle with your hair – Don’t brush or comb your hair too aggressively, as this can cause hair breakage and contribute to hair fall. Use a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about postpartum hair fall:

  • When does postpartum hair fall start? Postpartum hair fall usually starts around 3-6 months after giving birth.
  • How long does postpartum hair fall last? Postpartum hair fall can last for several months, but it usually resolves on its own.
  • Is postpartum hair fall normal? Yes, postpartum hair fall is a normal part of the postpartum process.

Hair Care During Postpartum Period

During the postpartum period, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips for hair care during this phase:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Condition your hair after every wash
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair
  • Avoid using hair dryers or other heat styling tools
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind

By following these tips, you can control postpartum hair fall and keep your hair healthy and strong. Remember to be patient, as it may take some time for your hair to recover from the hormonal changes of pregnancy and childbirth. With a little bit of care and attention, you can have beautiful, healthy hair even after giving birth.
