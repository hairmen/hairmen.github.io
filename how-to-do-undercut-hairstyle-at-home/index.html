How To Do Undercut Hairstyle At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Want to try out an edgy undercut hairstyle but don't want to spend a fortune at the salon? You're in luck! With a few tools and some patience, you can easily achieve this trendy look at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of doing an undercut hairstyle step by step.

Materials You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few tools:

  • Clippers with guards
  • A comb
  • A pair of scissors
  • A handheld mirror or a friend to help you see the back of your head

Preparing Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. You want it to be clean and free of any styling products. Then, decide on the length and style of your undercut. You may want to look up some inspiration photos or consult with a stylist beforehand.

Selecting the Right Clippers

When it comes to clippers, you want to choose a high-quality set that will last you for many haircuts to come. Look for clippers with adjustable blades and multiple guard sizes.

Choosing the Right Clipper Guards

The guard you choose will determine the length of your undercut. Start with a longer guard, such as a #4, and work your way down to shorter guards if you want a shorter, more dramatic undercut.

Using the Clippers: Technique

Begin by sectioning off the hair on top of your head with a clip or hair tie. Then, using your clippers and the guard of your choice, start at the nape of your neck and work your way up to the point where you want your undercut to end. Use an upward motion with the clippers, being careful not to go too high or too far into the longer hair on top.

Once you've finished the back, move on to the sides, using the same technique. Take your time and use the comb to guide the clippers, ensuring a smooth, even cut.

Cleaning Up Your Undercut

Once you've finished cutting your undercut, use your scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers may have missed. Then, use your clippers without a guard to clean up the hair around the edges and back of your neck.

Styling Your Undercut

Once your undercut is complete, you can style it in a variety of ways. Some popular options include slicking it back with pomade or gel, spiking it up with hair wax, or leaving it messy and tousled.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much too soon.
  • Use a handheld mirror or ask a friend to help you see the back of your head.
  • Take your time and use the comb to guide the clippers for a more precise cut.
  • Trim any longer hairs with scissors after using the clippers.

FAQs

Q: Can I do an undercut on myself?

A: Yes, but it may be easier to have a friend help you.

Q: How often do I need to touch up my undercut?

A: This will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how short you want to keep your undercut. Generally, you'll need to touch it up every 4-6 weeks.

Taking Care of Your Undercut

To maintain your undercut, you'll want to wash and condition your hair regularly. Use a styling product to keep it in place and avoid touching it too much throughout the day. You may also want to schedule regular appointments with a stylist to keep it looking sharp.

With this step-by-step guide, you can easily achieve a trendy undercut hairstyle from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, choose the right tools, and have fun with your new look!
