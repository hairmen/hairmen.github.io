How to Cut Fine Straight Hair for Volume

The key to cutting fine straight hair for volume is to create layers that add dimension and lift. Layers can help create the illusion of volume, making your hair look thicker and fuller. Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting fine straight hair for volume:

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Use sharp scissors
  • Cut at an angle to create layers and texture
  • Trim the ends to prevent split ends and breakage
  • Consider adding bangs or face-framing layers for extra volume

Step-by-Step Guide

Hereâs a step-by-step guide on how to cut fine straight hair for volume:

  1. Start by sectioning your hair. Divide it into four sections: two in the front and two in the back.
  2. Take one of the back sections and comb it straight down.
  3. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the back and moving towards the front. The angle should be slightly diagonal, so that the hair is shorter in the back and longer in the front.
  4. Repeat this process on the other back section.
  5. Move on to the front sections. Comb them straight down and cut them at an angle, starting from the front and moving towards the back.
  6. Be sure to blend the layers so that they look natural and seamless.
  7. Trim the ends to prevent split ends and breakage.
  8. If you want to add bangs, cut them at an angle and make sure they blend well with the rest of your hair.
  9. Style your hair as desired.
  10. Enjoy your new, voluminous look!
