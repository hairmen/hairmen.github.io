What Clothes Were Popular In The 60S

Written by Eveline Jul 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on the popular clothes of the 60s. This era was known for its bold and vibrant fashion style, which still inspires designers today. In this article, we'll take a look at what clothes were popular in the 60s, including dresses, pants, shoes, and accessories.

Table of Contents

  1. Dresses
  2. Pants
  3. Shoes
  4. Accessories
  5. Hair Care

Dresses

Dresses were a popular fashion item in the 60s. Women wore dresses for all occasions, from casual outings to formal events. The most popular styles were shift dresses, mini dresses, and maxi dresses. Shift dresses were simple, straight-cut dresses that hung loosely from the shoulders with little or no waistline. Mini dresses were short dresses that usually ended above the knee, while maxi dresses were long dresses that reached the ankle.

Popular fabrics for dresses in the 60s were cotton, wool, and synthetic materials like polyester. Patterns such as paisley, floral, and geometric prints were also popular. Accessorizing dresses with statement jewelry, like long necklaces and dangling earrings, was also common.

Pants

Pants were not commonly worn by women in the 60s, but they became more popular towards the end of the decade. Bell-bottom pants, a style that flared out at the bottom, were a popular choice. High-waisted pants, which sat above the belly button, were also popular. Women often wore pants with blouses or turtlenecks.

Men's pants in the 60s were typically straight-legged and made of materials like corduroy or denim. Colors like brown, beige, and olive green were popular. Men often wore pants with button-down shirts or turtlenecks.

Shoes

Shoes in the 60s were all about making a statement. Women wore high-heeled shoes with colorful patterns, like polka dots or stripes. Boots were also popular, especially ankle boots and knee-high boots. Men's shoes were typically lace-up dress shoes or loafers in black or brown.

Sneakers were also becoming more popular in the 60s, especially among young people. Brands like Adidas and Puma were gaining popularity, and Converse Chuck Taylors were a staple among teenagers.

Accessories

Accessories were a key part of 60s fashion. Women often wore large, oversized sunglasses and colorful scarves. Hats, like berets and fedoras, were also popular. Jewelry was bold and colorful, with large statement pieces like chunky bracelets and large earrings.

For men, accessories were more understated. Watches and belts were common, and men sometimes wore hats like fedoras or newsboy caps.

Hair Care

Hair was a big part of 60s fashion, especially for women. The beehive hairstyle, which involved teasing hair into a tall, conical shape, was a popular choice for formal events. The bouffant, a more subtle version of the beehive, was also popular. Long, straight hair was also in fashion, with many women using flat irons to achieve the look.

For men, short hair was the norm, with styles like the crew cut and the flat top being popular. The Beatles' shaggy haircut also gained popularity towards the end of the decade.

Tips for Wearing 60s Fashion Today

The 60s were all about bold colors and patterns, so don't be afraid to mix and match different prints and colors. Accessories are also key to achieving the look of the era, so experiment with statement jewelry and hats.

Frequently Asked Questions

What were the most popular colors in 60s fashion?

The most popular colors in 60s fashion were bold and bright, like orange, pink, and turquoise. Earth tones like brown, beige, and olive green were also popular.

What fabrics were popular in 60s fashion?

Popular fabrics in 60s fashion included cotton, wool, and synthetic materials like polyester. Velvet and suede were also popular for jackets and skirts.

What shoes were popular in 60s fashion?

Women's shoes in the 60s were often high-heeled with bold patterns, while men's shoes were typically dress shoes or loafers. Sneakers were also becoming more popular among young people.

That's a wrap on our guide to 60s fashion. We hope you found it helpful and inspiring for your own wardrobe. Don't be afraid to experiment with bold colors and patterns to achieve the look of the era. Happy styling!
