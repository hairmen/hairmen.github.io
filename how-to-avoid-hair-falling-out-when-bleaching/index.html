How To Avoid Hair Falling Out When Bleaching A Complete Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 24, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're someone who loves coloring your hair, chances are you've tried bleaching at least once in your life. Bleaching is a great way to achieve that perfect blonde shade, but it can also be a nightmare for your hair. One of the most common issues people face when bleaching their hair is hair fall. In this blog post, we'll be discussing how to avoid hair falling out when bleaching.

How to Avoid Hair Falling Out When Bleaching

The first step to avoid hair falling out when bleaching is to understand the process. Bleaching involves using chemicals to strip the natural color from your hair. This process can weaken the hair shaft, making it more prone to breakage and hair fall. Here are a few things you can do to avoid hair falling out when bleaching:

1. Choose a Professional

The best way to avoid hair falling out when bleaching is to get it done by a professional. They have the right knowledge and experience to bleach your hair without causing any damage. If you're not sure about the process, it's always better to leave it to the experts.

2. Use High-Quality Products

When it comes to bleaching, the quality of the products you use matters. Look for high-quality products that are specifically designed for bleaching. These products are less likely to cause damage to your hair and will help you achieve the desired results without hair fall.

3. Avoid Overlapping

One of the biggest mistakes people make when bleaching their hair is overlapping. Overlapping is when you apply bleach to hair that's already been bleached. This can cause significant damage to your hair and lead to hair fall. Make sure to apply the bleach only to the areas that need it.

4. Don't Overdo It

Another common mistake people make when bleaching their hair is overdoing it. Don't bleach your hair too often or leave the bleach on for too long. This can cause significant damage to your hair and lead to hair fall.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to avoid hair falling out when bleaching:

Step 1: Do a Strand Test

Before you start bleaching your hair, it's essential to do a strand test. This will help you understand how your hair will react to the bleach and avoid any potential damage.

Step 2: Use High-Quality Products

Use high-quality products that are specifically designed for bleaching. Don't compromise on the quality of the products you use.

Step 3: Apply the Bleach Carefully

Apply the bleach carefully, making sure to avoid overlapping. Don't leave the bleach on for too long.

Step 4: Rinse Your Hair Thoroughly

Rinse your hair thoroughly with cold water after bleaching. This will help remove any residual bleach and prevent hair fall.

Step 5: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

After bleaching, use a deep conditioning treatment to nourish and hydrate your hair. This will help prevent hair fall and keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Tips to Avoid Hair Falling Out When Bleaching

Here are a few tips to avoid hair falling out when bleaching:

1. Keep Your Hair Healthy

The healthier your hair is, the less likely it is to fall out when bleaching. Make sure to keep your hair well-nourished and hydrated by using high-quality hair care products.

2. Don't Bleach Your Hair Too Often

Bleaching your hair too often can cause significant damage and lead to hair fall. Avoid bleaching your hair more than once every six weeks.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid heat styling your hair after bleaching. Heat styling can cause further damage to your hair and lead to hair fall.

FAQs

Q. Can bleaching cause hair fall?

A. Yes, bleaching can cause hair fall. The chemicals in the bleach can weaken the hair shaft and make it more prone to breakage.

Q. How long should I wait to bleach my hair again?

A. It's best to wait at least six weeks before bleaching your hair again.

Q. Can I bleach my hair at home?

A. While it's possible to bleach your hair at home, it's always better to get it done by a professional. They have the right knowledge and experience to bleach your hair without causing any damage.

Hair Care After Bleaching

After bleaching, it's essential to take good care of your hair. Here are a few tips:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment at least once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid heat styling your hair as much as possible. If you need to use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can avoid hair falling out when bleaching. Remember to always use high-quality products and get it done by a professional if you're not sure about the process.
