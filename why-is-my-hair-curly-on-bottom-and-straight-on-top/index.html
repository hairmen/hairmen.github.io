Why Is My Hair Curly On Bottom And Straight On Top

Written by Petter Nov 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're one of the many people who have curly hair on the bottom and straight hair on the top, you may be wondering what's going on. This can be a frustrating issue to deal with, especially if you're not sure how to style it. Fortunately, there are a few reasons why this might be happening, and there are ways to work with your hair to make it look its best. In this article, we'll explore the causes of curly hair on the bottom and straight hair on top, and provide you with tips on how to manage it.

Description

Curly hair on the bottom and straight hair on top is a common hair issue that many people experience. This phenomenon is known as hair pattern disorder, or HDP. HDP is a condition where the hair on the top of the head is straight, while the hair on the sides and back is curly or wavy. This can be a frustrating issue to deal with, especially if you're not sure how to style it.

How to

The first thing to consider is your hair type. If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, it's likely that your hair will be curly on the bottom and straight on top. This is because the weight of your hair pulls the curls down, making them more pronounced at the bottom.

If you have straight hair, you may have HDP due to damage or heat styling. Overusing heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, can damage the hair and cause it to lose its natural texture. This can result in hair that is straight on top and curly on the bottom.

Step by Step

If you're dealing with HDP, there are a few steps you can take to manage it.

  1. Use a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner to help define your curls and waves.
  2. Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, and use a heat protectant spray when you do use them.
  3. Consider getting a haircut that is specifically designed for curly hair, such as a layered cut or a Deva cut.
  4. Experiment with different styling products to find the ones that work best for your hair type.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help you manage HDP:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Consider using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help enhance your curls.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

FAQ

Q: Can HDP be fixed?

A: While HDP can't be fixed, there are ways to manage it. Using the right products and styling techniques can help you make the most of your hair's natural texture.

Q: Will cutting my hair make it less curly on the bottom?

A: No, cutting your hair won't change its natural texture. However, getting a haircut that is designed for curly hair can help enhance your curls and make them more defined.

Hair Care

When dealing with HDP, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can cause dryness and damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can cause damage and dryness.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can manage HDP and make the most of your natural texture. Remember, everyone's hair is different, so it may take some trial and error to find the products and techniques that work best for you.
