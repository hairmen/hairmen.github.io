How To Cut Short Women s Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a bit daunting, especially if you’re new to it. But with the right tools, techniques, and a little bit of practice, cutting your short hair can be a breeze. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to cutting short women’s hair yourself. From choosing the right tools to finishing touches, we’ve got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Finishing Touches

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Clipper with guards (optional)
  • Comb
  • Flat iron or curling iron (optional)
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Handheld mirror or vanity mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp and designed for hair cutting. Dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts, so it's worth investing in a good pair. Clippers with guards can help you achieve an even cut, but they're not necessary if you're comfortable using scissors alone.

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, wash and dry it thoroughly. Make sure your hair is tangle-free and easy to work with. If you have curly hair, it's best to cut it dry, so you can see how the curls fall. Straight hair can be cut wet or dry.

Decide on the length you want to cut your hair. Use a handheld mirror or vanity mirror to check the back of your head and make sure you can see all angles of your hair before you start cutting.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair can help you achieve an even cut. Start by dividing your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section, so they don't get in the way while you're cutting.

Start with the back sections and work your way to the front. Use a comb to comb out a small section of hair, then hold it between your fingers and use your scissors to cut the desired length. Repeat this process for each section, making sure to cut evenly on both sides.

Cutting Your Hair

When cutting your hair, it's important to cut small sections at a time. This will help you achieve an even cut and avoid any mistakes. Use your comb to hold the hair taut, then cut across the hair in a straight line. If you want a layered look, cut at an angle to achieve the desired length.

If you're using clippers, attach the appropriate guard and use them to cut your hair in a straight line. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward, checking your work in the mirror as you go.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to add finishing touches. Use your scissors to trim any uneven pieces or flyaways. If you want to add some texture, use your flat iron or curling iron to create waves or curls.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

To make your haircut a success, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp and designed for hair cutting.
  • Use sectioning clips to keep your hair organized and out of the way.
  • Work in a well-lit area with a clean surface.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and be patient.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

Regular scissors are not designed for hair cutting and can cause split ends and uneven cuts. It's best to invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors.

Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

Straight hair can be cut wet or dry, but curly hair should be cut dry so you can see how the curls fall.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Your Haircut

After your haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shape. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims will also help to maintain your haircut and prevent split ends.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
