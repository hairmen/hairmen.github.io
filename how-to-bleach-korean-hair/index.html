How To Bleach Korean Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 07, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to achieve the popular Korean hair trend of a bright, bleach blonde look, then you'll need to bleach your hair. Bleaching can be intimidating, but with the right steps and precautions, you can safely achieve the look you want. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of how to bleach Korean hair.

Description

Bleaching your hair involves stripping your hair of its natural color and pigment, which can be damaging to your hair. This process can cause dryness, breakage, and split ends, so it's important to take care of your hair before and after bleaching. Korean hair is typically dark and thick, so it may take more time and care to achieve the desired level of lightness.

How To

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start bleaching, it's important to prepare your hair. Wash your hair a day or two before bleaching so that your scalp's natural oils can protect your hair. Avoid using any styling products, as they can interfere with the bleaching process. You'll also want to protect your skin and clothing by wearing gloves and a protective cape or towel.

Step 2: Mix Your Bleach

Choose a high-quality bleach and developer that are specifically designed for use on dark hair. Mix the bleach and developer according to the instructions on the package. You may want to use a bowl and brush to mix the bleach to ensure even coverage.

Step 3: Apply the Bleach

Start at the back of your head and work in sections, applying the bleach to your hair with a brush. Be careful not to apply the bleach to your scalp, as it can cause irritation and damage. Work quickly to ensure that the bleach is applied evenly and saturates your hair completely.

Step 4: Check Your Hair Frequently

As your hair lightens, check it frequently to ensure that you're achieving the desired level of lightness. You may need to leave the bleach on for longer if your hair is particularly dark or thick. Be sure to follow the instructions on the package and don't leave the bleach on for too long, as this can cause damage.

Step 5: Rinse and Condition Your Hair

Once your hair has reached the desired level of lightness, rinse the bleach out with warm water. Be sure to thoroughly rinse your hair to remove all of the bleach. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair to help restore moisture and protect against damage.

Tips

  • Be patient and take your time when bleaching your hair.
  • Use a high-quality bleach and developer to minimize damage.
  • Protect your skin and clothing with gloves and a protective cape or towel.
  • Avoid applying bleach to your scalp to prevent irritation and damage.
  • Check your hair frequently to ensure that you're achieving the desired level of lightness.

FAQ

How many times do I need to bleach my hair to achieve a blonde look?

This depends on the natural color of your hair and the level of lightness you want to achieve. It may take multiple sessions to achieve the desired look, so be patient and take care of your hair between sessions.

Can I bleach my hair at home?

Yes, you can bleach your hair at home, but it's important to take the proper precautions to minimize damage to your hair and skin. Be sure to follow the instructions on the bleach and developer package, and consider seeking professional help if you're not confident in your ability to bleach your hair at home.

How can I take care of my hair after bleaching?

After bleaching, it's important to deep condition your hair regularly to restore moisture and protect against damage. Avoid using heat styling tools and harsh chemicals, and consider getting regular trims to prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After bleaching your hair, it's important to take care of it to prevent damage and promote healthy growth. Here are some tips for caring for bleached hair:

  • Deep condition your hair regularly to restore moisture.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools and harsh chemicals.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine.

With these tips and precautions, you can safely achieve a bright, bleach blonde look for your Korean hair. Remember to take care of your hair before and after bleaching to minimize damage and promote healthy growth.
