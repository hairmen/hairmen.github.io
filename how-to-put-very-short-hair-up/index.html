How To Put Very Short Hair Up A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Short hair can be stylish and easy to manage, but it can also limit your styling options. However, with the right techniques, you can still put your very short hair up in a variety of styles. In this post, we’ll walk you through how to put very short hair up with a step-by-step guide, tips, and FAQs.

Table of Contents

  • How to Put Very Short Hair Up
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Putting Very Short Hair Up
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Very Short Hair

How to Put Very Short Hair Up

First, you will need to gather some materials to put your very short hair up. You will need bobby pins, hair elastics, and hairspray. If you want to add some volume to your hair, use a volumizing spray or dry shampoo.

Next, decide which style you want to try. There are a few options for very short hair:

  • A half-up style with a small ponytail or twist at the crown of your head
  • A French twist
  • A messy bun or chignon
  • A braided crown or halo braid
  • A slicked-back look

Once you’ve chosen your style, it’s time to get started.

Step-by-Step Guide

Half-Up Style

  1. Start by taking a small section of hair from the top of your head, near your forehead.
  2. Twist the section of hair and pull it back, securing it with a bobby pin or hair elastic at the crown of your head.
  3. You can leave the rest of your hair down or add some texture by using a curling iron or adding some dry shampoo.

French Twist

  1. Brush your hair back and gather it at the nape of your neck.
  2. Twist your hair up and tuck the ends under, securing the twist with bobby pins.
  3. Add some hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Messy Bun or Chignon

  1. Gather your hair at the crown of your head and twist it into a bun or chignon.
  2. Secure the bun with bobby pins or a hair elastic.
  3. Pull out a few pieces of hair to create a messy, effortless look.
  4. Add hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Braided Crown or Halo Braid

  1. Start by creating a deep side part.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the front of your head and braid it.
  3. Wrap the braid around your head, securing it with bobby pins.
  4. Continue braiding small sections of hair and wrapping them around your head until you’ve created a braided crown.
  5. Add some hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Slicked-Back Look

  1. Start by applying some gel or pomade to your hair.
  2. Brush your hair back and away from your face.
  3. Smooth down any flyaways or baby hairs with a toothbrush or small comb.
  4. Add some hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Tips for Putting Very Short Hair Up

Here are some additional tips to help you put your very short hair up:

  • Use the right products for your hair type. If you have fine hair, use a volumizing spray to add some texture. If you have thick hair, use a pomade or gel to help tame flyaways.
  • Don’t be afraid to experiment with different styles. Just because you have short hair doesn’t mean you can’t have fun with it!
  • Invest in some high-quality bobby pins and hair elastics. They will hold your hair in place better and prevent any slipping or falling out.

FAQs

Can I put very short hair up in a ponytail?

Yes, you can put very short hair up in a small ponytail or twist at the crown of your head. Just make sure to use a hair elastic that won’t slip out.

What products should I use to put very short hair up?

You will need bobby pins, hair elastics, and hairspray. If you want to add some volume or texture, use a volumizing spray or dry shampoo.

What are some easy styles for very short hair?

Some easy styles for very short hair include a half-up style, French twist, messy bun or chignon, braided crown or halo braid, and a slicked-back look.

Hair Care for Very Short Hair

Even if you have very short hair, it’s still important to take care of it. Here are some hair care tips for very short hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • If you have oily hair, use a dry shampoo in between washes.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and nourished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can put your very short hair up in a variety of styles. Whether you want a sleek and polished look or a messy and effortless style, there’s a technique that will work for you. Have fun experimenting with different styles and finding what works best for your hair type and face shape!
