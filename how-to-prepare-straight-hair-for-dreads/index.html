How To Prepare Straight Hair For Dreads

Written by Eveline Sep 09, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have straight hair and want to get dreadlocks, it's important to prepare your hair properly to ensure that the locks form correctly and stay intact. In this article, we'll go over how to prepare straight hair for dreads, step by step. We'll also cover some tips and frequently asked questions about hair care and maintenance for dreadlocks.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prepare Straight Hair for Dreads
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Dreadlocks
  • FAQs about Dreadlocks
  • Hair Care for Dreadlocks

How to Prepare Straight Hair for Dreads

Before you start the process of creating dreadlocks, you need to prepare your hair properly. Here are the steps you should follow to prepare straight hair for dreads:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step is to wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or residue. This will ensure that your hair is clean and free of any oils or other substances that could interfere with the locking process. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, you'll need to section your hair into small, equal-sized sections. Use a rat-tail comb to create partings from the front to the back of your head. The sections should be no wider than the width of your finger and no thicker than a pencil. Use hair clips to secure each section once you've created it.

Step 3: Apply Dreadlock Wax or Gel

Apply a small amount of dreadlock wax or gel to each section of hair. This will help the hair to lock together and stay in place. Be careful not to use too much product, as this can cause buildup and prevent the locks from forming properly.

Step 4: Use a Comb to Backcomb Each Section

Starting at the roots, use a fine-toothed comb to backcomb each section of hair. This involves combing the hair in the opposite direction to its natural growth, which creates a knotted effect. Keep backcombing each section until it starts to feel tight and compacted. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 5: Twist Each Section into a Dreadlock

Once you've backcombed each section of hair, it's time to twist them into dreadlocks. Take each section and twist it in a clockwise direction, working from the roots to the tips. Use your fingers to smooth down any loose hairs and make sure the lock is tight and secure. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step by Step Guide

If you're still not sure how to prepare straight hair for dreads, here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo.
  2. Section your hair into small, equal-sized sections using a rat-tail comb.
  3. Apply a small amount of dreadlock wax or gel to each section of hair.
  4. Use a fine-toothed comb to backcomb each section of hair, starting at the roots.
  5. Twist each section of hair in a clockwise direction, working from the roots to the tips.
  6. Smooth down any loose hairs and make sure the lock is tight and secure.
  7. Repeat steps 3-6 for each section of hair.
  8. Allow your hair to dry completely before washing it again.

Tips for Maintaining Dreadlocks

Once you've created your dreadlocks, it's important to take good care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for maintaining your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup.
  • Use a natural bristle brush to keep your locks neat and free of loose hairs.
  • Protect your locks from sun damage by wearing a hat or scarf when you're outside.
  • Consider getting your locks retwisted every 4-6 weeks to keep them tight and well-formed.

FAQs about Dreadlocks

Here are some frequently asked questions about dreadlocks:

Q: Can I create dreadlocks with straight hair?

A: Yes, you can create dreadlocks with straight hair. However, it may be more difficult to get them to lock together and stay in place, so it's important to follow the proper preparation and maintenance steps.

Q: How long does it take to create dreadlocks?

A: The length of time it takes to create dreadlocks depends on several factors, including the length and thickness of your hair, and the method you use to create the locks. It can take anywhere from a few hours to several days to create a full head of dreadlocks.

Q: Can I undo my dreadlocks if I change my mind?

A: Yes, you can undo your dreadlocks if you change your mind. However, this can be a long and difficult process, as it involves combing out each lock and removing any buildup or residue.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your dreadlocks healthy and looking their best. Here are some tips for caring for your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup.
  • Use a natural bristle brush to keep your locks neat and free of loose hairs.
  • Protect your locks from sun damage by wearing a hat or scarf when you're outside.
  • Consider getting your locks retwisted every 4-6 weeks to keep them tight and well-formed.

By following these steps and tips, you can prepare your straight hair for dreadlocks and keep them looking great for years to come.
