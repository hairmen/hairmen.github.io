What Short Hair Would Suit Me

Written by Oliver Aug 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Short hair can be a liberating change for many women who are looking to switch up their look. However, choosing the right short hairstyle can be daunting. With so many styles to choose from, it can be overwhelming. In this article, we’ll guide you in finding the best short hair that will suit your face shape, hair type, and personal style.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Short hair can be a fantastic way to showcase your features and add a touch of sophistication to your look. However, with so many styles to choose from, it can be challenging to find one that suits you best. Finding the perfect short hairstyle for you will depend on several factors, including face shape, hair type, and personal style.

How to

The best way to find out what short hair would suit you is by taking your face shape into consideration. Your face shape will determine which haircut will flatter your features the most. There are four primary face shapes: round, square, heart, and oval.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, you should opt for a short hairstyle that adds height and volume to the crown of your head. A layered pixie cut or a short bob with side-swept bangs would be perfect. The goal is to elongate your face and draw attention away from the roundness of your cheeks.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, you should choose a short hairstyle that softens your angular jawline. A textured bob or a layered pixie cut with side-swept bangs would work best for you. Avoid blunt cuts, as they can make your face look boxy.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, you should go for a short hairstyle that balances your forehead and jawline. A layered pixie cut or a bob with side-swept bangs would be perfect. Avoid volume on the top of your head, as it can make your forehead appear wider.

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, you’re in luck! You can pull off almost any short hairstyle. A chic bob or a sleek pixie cut would look great on you. Just make sure to keep your face shape in mind when choosing a style.

Step by Step

Now that you know your face shape and which short hairstyles would suit you best, it’s time to take the plunge and cut your hair. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to get the perfect short haircut:

  1. Choose a style that suits your face shape and hair type.
  2. Bring a photo of the hairstyle you want to your hair appointment.
  3. Consult with your hairstylist to ensure that the style you want will work with your hair type and face shape.
  4. Enjoy your new short haircut!

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when choosing a short hairstyle:

  • Consider your lifestyle. If you’re always on the go, you may want to opt for a low-maintenance style.
  • Think about your personal style. Choose a hairstyle that reflects your personality and makes you feel confident.
  • Be open to trying new things. You never know, you may love a style you never thought would suit you.

FAQ

Q: Will a short hairstyle suit me if I have curly hair?

A: Absolutely! There are many short hairstyles that look great on curly hair. Just make sure to choose a style that works with your curl pattern.

Q: Can I still style my short hair?

A: Yes! Short hair can be styled in many ways, from sleek and straight to curly and textured.

Hair Care

Short hair is generally low-maintenance, but that doesn’t mean you should neglect it. Here are some tips on how to take care of your short hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage it.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Visit your hairstylist every six to eight weeks for a trim to keep your hair looking its best.

Choosing a short hairstyle can be a big decision, but with the right guidance, you’ll be able to find a style that suits you best. Keep in mind your face shape, hair type, and personal style when choosing a short haircut, and don’t be afraid to try something new. Happy hairstyling!
