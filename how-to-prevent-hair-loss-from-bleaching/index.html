How To Prevent Hair Loss From Bleaching Your Ultimate Guide

Written by Petter Aug 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you are a fan of trendy hair colors, you might be familiar with the process of bleaching. Bleaching is a chemical process that lightens the hair color, making it easier for you to achieve the desired shade. However, this process can cause hair damage and even hair loss if not done correctly. In this article, we will guide you on how to prevent hair loss from bleaching.

Description

Bleaching involves using a mixture of chemicals to strip your hair of its natural pigment. The process can cause hair damage and make your hair brittle, leading to hair breakage and hair loss. If you are planning to bleach your hair or have already done so, it is essential to take steps to prevent hair loss.

How to Prevent Hair Loss from Bleaching

The following are some measures you can take to prevent hair loss from bleaching:

1. Avoid Overlapping

Do not overlap the bleach onto previously bleached hair. Overlapping can cause excessive damage to your hair, making it brittle and prone to breakage.

2. Use a Lower Volume Developer

Using a lower volume developer can reduce the intensity of the chemical process, thus minimizing hair damage. A 20-volume developer is ideal for most hair types. However, if your hair is fragile, a 10-volume developer is better for you.

3. Perform a Strand Test

Perform a strand test before applying the bleach to your hair. A strand test will help you determine how your hair will react to the bleach, allowing you to adjust the process accordingly.

4. Limit Bleaching Frequency

Limit the frequency of bleaching to prevent hair damage. Ideally, you should wait for at least six weeks before bleaching your hair again.

5. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning can help to restore moisture to your hair and reduce hair breakage. Use a moisturizing deep conditioner at least once a week after bleaching your hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to bleach your hair without causing hair loss:

Step 1: Prepare the Bleaching Mixture

Prepare the bleach mixture according to the manufacturer's instructions. Wear gloves and protective clothing to avoid skin irritation.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts using hair clips. This will help you apply the bleach evenly.

Step 3: Apply the Bleach

Apply the bleach mixture to your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots. Avoid overlapping the bleach onto previously bleached hair.

Step 4: Check the Processing Time

Check the processing time according to the manufacturer's instructions. Do not exceed the recommended processing time, as this can cause excessive hair damage.

Step 5: Rinse Your Hair

Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water to remove the bleach. Apply a moisturizing conditioner and leave it on for five minutes before rinsing it off.

Tips to Prevent Hair Loss from Bleaching

The following are some additional tips to prevent hair loss from bleaching:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as blow dryers and flat irons, after bleaching your hair. Heat styling can cause further hair damage and make your hair prone to breakage.

2. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a protective hair spray. The sun's UV rays can cause hair damage and make your hair brittle.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends and prevent further hair damage. Trimming your hair can also give it a healthier appearance.

FAQs

Q: Can bleaching cause hair loss?

A: Yes, bleaching can cause hair loss if not done correctly. The chemicals in bleach can damage the hair shaft, making it brittle and prone to breakage.

Q: How can I prevent hair loss from bleaching?

A: You can prevent hair loss from bleaching by avoiding overlapping, using a lower volume developer, performing a strand test, limiting bleaching frequency, and deep conditioning regularly.

Hair Care after Bleaching

After bleaching your hair, it is essential to take extra care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are some tips for hair care after bleaching:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to prevent further hair damage. Look for products that contain ingredients such as keratin, argan oil, and shea butter, which can help to restore moisture to your hair.

2. Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as hair dyes and relaxers, after bleaching your hair. These chemicals can cause further hair damage and make your hair prone to breakage.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Protect your hair from heat styling by using a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools. Also, use a lower heat setting to prevent hair damage.

In conclusion, taking the necessary precautions can help you prevent hair loss from bleaching. Follow the tips and steps outlined in this article to protect your hair and keep it healthy.
