How To Thin Your Fringe With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning your fringe can give your hair a new look and texture. However, it can be daunting to do it yourself. In this article, we'll guide you on how to thin your fringe with scissors in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

How to Thin Your Fringe with Scissors

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to thin your fringe with scissors:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo that suits your hair type. Rinse it well and towel dry.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to comb your hair, ensuring that there are no knots or tangles.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into two equal parts by parting it down the middle. Clip one part of the hair to the side.

Step 4: Start Thinning

Take a small section of hair from the unclipped side and hold it between your fingers. Hold the scissors vertically and cut into the hair, taking off small sections at a time.

Be careful not to cut too much hair at once. Always start with a little and adjust accordingly.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you've thinned out a section of hair, let it fall naturally and check your progress in the mirror.

If you're happy with the result, move on to the next section of hair. If not, continue thinning until you achieve the desired result.

Step 6: Repeat on the Other Side

Repeat the same process on the other side of your hair.

Step 7: Blend the Fringe

Once you've finished thinning out both sides of your hair, take a section of hair from the middle and blend it into the thinned-out sections.

Step 8: Finish with a Trim

Trim the ends of your fringe to give it a neat and polished look.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired, and you're ready to go!

Tips for Thinning Your Fringe with Scissors

Here are some tips and tricks to keep in mind when thinning your fringe with scissors:

  • Use sharp scissors to make clean and precise cuts.
  • Always cut vertically to avoid creating blunt lines.
  • Take your time and work in small sections to avoid mistakes.
  • Don't thin your fringe too much, as it can make your hair look wispy and thin.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from different angles.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Thin My Fringe with Regular Scissors?

Yes, but it's recommended to use scissors specifically designed for hair cutting to avoid damage or split ends.

How Often Should I Thin My Fringe?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, it's recommended to thin your fringe every 4-6 weeks.

Can Thinning My Fringe Make My Hair Look Thinner?

Yes, but it can also give your hair more texture and movement. It's important to find the right balance and not over-thin your fringe.

Hair Care Tips

To maintain healthy and beautiful hair, it's important to follow these tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo that suits your hair type.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and smooth.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as straighteners and curling irons too often.
  • Use hair masks and treatments to nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Thinning your fringe with scissors can be a fun and easy way to switch up your hairstyle. By following these simple steps and tips, you can achieve a new look that suits your style and personality.
