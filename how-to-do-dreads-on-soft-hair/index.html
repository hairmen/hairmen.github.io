How To Do Dreads On Soft Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of having soft, silky hair that won't hold a style? Dreadlocks might be the perfect solution for you! Dreads can give your hair texture and volume, and they're also low maintenance. In this article, we'll show you how to do dreads on soft hair, step-by-step.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Do Dreads on Soft Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Making Dreads on Soft Hair
  • FAQs About Dreads on Soft Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

How to Do Dreads on Soft Hair:

Before we get started, it's important to note that the process of dreading hair can take several hours, depending on the length and thickness of your hair. So, make sure you set aside enough time to complete the process.

Here's what you'll need:

  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • Dread wax or gel
  • Rubber bands

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup, and then use a moisturizing conditioner to soften your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, divide your hair into small, equal sections. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreads you want to create.

Step 3: Twist and Backcomb Your Hair

Using your fingers, twist each section of hair in one direction until it starts to coil on itself. Then, use your comb to backcomb the twisted hair, working from the ends up to the roots. This will create knots in your hair that will eventually form into dreads. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 4: Apply Dread Wax or Gel

Once you've backcombed all of your hair, apply dread wax or gel to each section. This will help your hair to stay in place and prevent frizz. Make sure to work the wax or gel into each section thoroughly.

Step 5: Secure with Rubber Bands

Using rubber bands, secure each section of hair at the base of the dread. This will help to keep the dread in place and prevent it from unraveling.

Step 6: Let Your Hair Dry

Allow your hair to dry completely. This may take several hours, so be patient. You can speed up the drying process by sitting under a hooded dryer or using a blow dryer on a low heat setting.

Step 7: Remove the Rubber Bands

Once your hair is completely dry, remove the rubber bands from each dread. Your dreads should now be firmly in place.

Tips for Making Dreads on Soft Hair:

  • Be patient. Creating dreads can take several hours, so make sure you have enough time to complete the process.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup before starting the dreading process.
  • Apply dread wax or gel thoroughly to each section of hair to prevent frizz.
  • Use rubber bands to secure each section of hair at the base of the dread.

FAQs About Dreads on Soft Hair:

Can you do dreads on soft hair?

Yes, you can! However, it may take a little more effort to get your hair to hold the dreads in place.

How long does it take to create dreads on soft hair?

The length of time it takes to create dreads on soft hair will depend on the length and thickness of your hair. It can take anywhere from 4-8 hours.

Do I need to use dread wax or gel?

Yes, dread wax or gel is important for helping your hair to stay in place and preventing frizz.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads:

  • Wash your dreads every 1-2 weeks with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using conditioner on your dreads, as it can cause buildup and make them slippery.
  • Use a dreadlock-specific wax or oil to keep your dreads moisturized and prevent them from drying out.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your dreads.

With these tips and techniques, you can create beautiful, low maintenance dreads on your soft hair. Happy dreading!
