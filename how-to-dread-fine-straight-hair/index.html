How To Dread Fine Straight Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 29, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have fine, straight hair and are wondering if it's possible to dread it, the answer is yes! Dreading fine straight hair is a bit more challenging than dreading other hair types, but with patience and the right technique, you can achieve beautiful, healthy dreads. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know about how to dread fine straight hair, including tips, hair care, and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents:

  • Section 1: How to Prepare Your Hair for Dreading
  • Section 2: How to Create Dreads in Fine Straight Hair
  • Section 3: Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads
  • Section 4: Frequently Asked Questions
  • Section 5: Hair Care Tips for Dreadlocks

Section 1: How to Prepare Your Hair for Dreading

Before you start dreading your hair, you need to prepare it properly. Here are the steps you should follow:

Clean Your Hair

The first step is to wash your hair thoroughly. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue from your hair. It’s important to have clean hair when you start dreading.

Condition Your Hair

Next, condition your hair to make it easier to work with. Use a conditioner that is lightweight and won’t weigh your hair down. Avoid using heavy products that can leave residue in your hair.

Detangle Your Hair

Before you start dreading your hair, make sure it’s completely detangled. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently remove any knots or tangles. If your hair is prone to tangling, you may want to consider using a detangling spray or leave-in conditioner.

Section 2: How to Create Dreads in Fine Straight Hair

Creating dreads in fine straight hair is more challenging than other hair types. Here are the steps you should follow:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

The first step is to section your hair. Use a comb to divide your hair into small sections. The size of the sections depends on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreads you want to create. For fine straight hair, smaller sections are better.

Step 2: Backcomb Your Hair

Once you’ve sectioned your hair, you can start backcombing. Hold a section of hair at the root and comb it backward towards your scalp. Repeat this process until the hair starts to knot up. Be gentle and avoid pulling too hard, as this can damage your hair.

Step 3: Twist Your Hair

Once you’ve backcombed a section of hair, twist it tightly. This will help the hair knot up and form a dread. Continue to twist the hair until it feels secure.

Step 4: Repeat the Process

Repeat the backcombing and twisting process on each section of hair until your entire head is dreaded. Be patient, as this process can take several hours or even days.

Section 3: Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Once you’ve created your dreads, it’s important to maintain them properly. Here are some tips to keep your dreads looking healthy:

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and free of buildup. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using heavy conditioners or styling products.

Re-Twist Your Dreads

Re-twist your dreads regularly to keep them tight and prevent them from unraveling. Use a small amount of dread wax or gel to help hold the twists in place.

Protect Your Dreads

Avoid exposing your dreads to extreme heat or humidity, as this can cause them to unravel or become frizzy. Wear a hat or headscarf to protect your dreads from the sun.

Section 4: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I dread my hair if it’s thin?

A: Yes, you can dread thin hair. However, it’s important to be gentle when backcombing and twisting your hair to avoid causing damage.

Q: How long does it take to dread fine straight hair?

A: The time it takes to dread fine straight hair depends on the length of your hair and the size of the dreads you want to create. It can take several hours or even days to create dreads in fine straight hair.

Q: Can I still brush my hair after I’ve dreaded it?

A: No, you should avoid brushing your hair once you’ve dreaded it. Instead, use your fingers to separate the dreads and keep them from matting together.

Section 5: Hair Care Tips for Dreadlocks

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your dreadlocks looking healthy:

Avoid Using Heavy Products

Avoid using heavy styling products or conditioners that can leave residue in your hair. Use lightweight products that won’t weigh your hair down.

Be Gentle When Washing Your Hair

Be gentle when washing your hair to avoid causing damage or unraveling your dreads. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid scrubbing your hair too vigorously.

Keep Your Scalp Clean

Keep your scalp clean and free of buildup by washing your hair regularly. Use a tea tree oil shampoo or apple cider vinegar rinse to help remove buildup.

With these tips and techniques, you can create beautiful, healthy dreads in fine straight hair. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair to keep your dreads looking their best.
