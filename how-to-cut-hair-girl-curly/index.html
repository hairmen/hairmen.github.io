How To Cut Girl s Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be challenging to cut, especially for those who are not familiar with its unique texture and properties. That's why it's essential to know the right techniques and tools to achieve a beautiful, bouncy cut that enhances your curls' natural beauty. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to cut girl's curly hair like a pro.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Girl's Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQ

How to Cut Girl's Curly Hair

The first step in cutting girl's curly hair is to choose the right technique. There are several ways to cut curly hair, such as the DevaCut method or the Ouidad technique. These methods are specifically designed for curly hair and involve cutting the hair while it's dry. This helps the stylist to see how the curls fall and how the hair will look after it's styled.

Another essential factor to consider when cutting curly hair is the type of curl pattern. There are three main types of curls: loose, medium, and tight. Each curl pattern requires a different approach to cutting to achieve the best results. For example, tight curls need to be cut carefully to avoid creating frizz, while loose curls can be cut more freely.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut girl's curly hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning the hair with products specifically designed for curly hair. This will help to detangle the hair and make it easier to cut.
  2. Divide the hair into sections and clip them to separate them. This will help you to work on one section at a time.
  3. Start cutting the hair from the bottom, using a pair of sharp scissors. Cut the hair in small sections, following the natural curl pattern. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once or to cut straight across, as this can create an uneven look.
  4. Continue cutting the hair section by section, moving towards the top of the head. Be sure to cut the hair at an angle, following the natural shape of the head.
  5. When you reach the top of the head, cut the hair in layers to add volume and texture. Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it at an angle, following the natural curl pattern.
  6. Once you've finished cutting the hair, style it as desired using products specifically designed for curly hair.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some tips for cutting girl's curly hair:

  • Use a pair of sharp scissors to avoid damaging the hair or creating split ends.
  • Cut the hair while it's dry, as this will help you to see how the curls fall and how the hair will look after it's styled.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can create an uneven look.
  • Be gentle when cutting tight curls, as they can easily become frizzy or damaged.
  • Don't cut straight across, as this can create a blunt look. Instead, cut the hair at an angle, following the natural shape of the head.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair:

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It's recommended to cut curly hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my own curly hair?

A: While it's possible to cut your own curly hair, it's recommended to seek the help of a professional stylist who is familiar with cutting curly hair.

Q: What products should I use to style my curly hair?

A: Use products specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl-enhancing creams, gels, and mousses. Avoid products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out the hair and create frizz.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair is essential to maintain its health and beauty. Here are some tips for taking care of girl's curly hair:

  • Use products specifically designed for curly hair, such as sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair, as this can create frizz and damage the hair. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle the hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to moisturize and nourish the hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage the hair and create frizz.
  • Air-dry curly hair whenever possible, or use a diffuser attachment on a low heat setting to dry the hair.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, bouncy cut that enhances your girl's curly hair's natural beauty.
