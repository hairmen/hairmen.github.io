How To Cover Postpartum Hair Loss Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Jul 06, 2023 · 5 min read

If you’ve recently given birth, you may have noticed that your hair is thinning or falling out. Don’t worry, this is completely normal and is known as postpartum hair loss. However, if you’re feeling self-conscious about your hair, there are ways to cover postpartum hair loss until your hair grows back. In this article, we’ll be sharing some tips and tricks on how to cover postpartum hair loss.

Description of Postpartum Hair Loss

Postpartum hair loss is a common condition that affects many new moms. It occurs due to the hormonal changes that take place during pregnancy. During pregnancy, the increased levels of estrogen in your body cause your hair to remain in the growth phase, resulting in thicker and fuller hair. However, after giving birth, your estrogen levels drop, causing hair to enter the resting phase and fall out. This can result in thinning hair or bald patches.

How to Cover Postpartum Hair Loss

1. Change Your Hairstyle

Changing your hairstyle can help to cover up postpartum hair loss. A shorter haircut or a style with layers can help to create the illusion of thicker hair. Speak to your hairstylist about the best cut for your hair type and face shape.

2. Use Hair Extensions

Hair extensions can be a quick and easy way to add volume and length to your hair. Clip-in hair extensions are a great option as they are easy to apply and remove. Make sure to choose extensions that match your hair color and texture.

3. Use Hair Fibers

Hair fibers are small fibers that attach to your hair to create the appearance of thicker hair. They come in a range of colors and can be easily applied at home. Simply sprinkle the fibers onto your hair and brush through to distribute.

4. Use Hair Accessories

Hair accessories can also be used to cover up postpartum hair loss. Headbands, scarves, and hats can be worn to cover up bald patches or thinning areas. Choose accessories that complement your outfit and personal style.

Step by Step Guide

Here’s a step by step guide on how to cover postpartum hair loss:

Step 1: Identify the Areas of Hair Loss

Identify the areas of your hair that are thinning or have bald patches. This will help you to choose the best method of coverage.

Step 2: Choose Your Method of Coverage

Choose the method of coverage that works best for you. This could be a change in hairstyle, hair extensions, hair fibers, or hair accessories.

Step 3: Practice Applying Your Method

Practice applying your chosen method of coverage. This will help you to get the best results and feel more confident in your appearance.

Step 4: Experiment with Different Styles

Experiment with different hairstyles and accessories to find the best look for you. Don’t be afraid to try something new!

Tips for Managing Postpartum Hair Loss

Here are some tips for managing postpartum hair loss:

1. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid using harsh chemicals, heat styling tools, and tight hairstyles that can damage your hair and exacerbate hair loss.

2. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help to promote hair growth and prevent hair loss.

3. Take Supplements

Taking supplements such as biotin, zinc, and iron can also help to promote healthy hair growth.

4. Speak to Your Doctor

If you’re experiencing severe hair loss, speak to your doctor. They may be able to recommend a treatment or medication to help with hair growth.

FAQs

Q: How long does postpartum hair loss last?

A: Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for around six months to a year. However, every woman is different, and it may take longer for your hair to grow back.

Q: Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

A: Postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, but you can take steps to promote healthy hair growth.

Q: Will my hair grow back to its pre-pregnancy state?

A: Yes, your hair will eventually grow back to its pre-pregnancy state. However, it may take some time for your hair to fully recover.

Hair Care for Postpartum Hair Loss

Here are some tips for caring for your hair during postpartum hair loss:

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

Washing your hair regularly can help to remove excess oil and debris that can clog hair follicles and cause hair loss.

2. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that is free from harsh chemicals and sulfates that can damage your hair and scalp.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as blow dryers, curling irons, and straighteners that can damage your hair and exacerbate hair loss.

4. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help to increase blood flow to your hair follicles, promoting healthy hair growth.

Conclusion

Postpartum hair loss is a common condition that affects many new moms. While it can be frustrating, there are ways to cover up postpartum hair loss until your hair grows back. By following these tips and tricks, you can feel more confident in your appearance and take steps to promote healthy hair growth.
