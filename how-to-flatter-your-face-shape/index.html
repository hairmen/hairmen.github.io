How To Flatter Your Face Shape A Complete Guide

Do you ever feel like your hairstyle doesn't quite suit your face shape? It's a common issue, but luckily there are plenty of ways to flatter your face shape and highlight your best features. Here's a complete guide on how to flatter your face shape and achieve the perfect hairstyle.

1. Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in flattering your face shape is to determine what your face shape actually is. There are six main face shapes: oval, round, square, heart-shaped, diamond, and long. To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back away from your face. Then, take a close look at the shape of your face, paying attention to the length and width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline.

2. How to Flatter an Oval Face Shape

Those with oval faces have balanced features and can pull off almost any hairstyle. However, to really flatter your face shape, try a layered cut that falls just below your chin. This will accentuate your cheekbones and make your face appear longer. Avoid blunt bangs or heavy layers that can overpower your face.

3. How to Flatter a Round Face Shape

Those with round faces should aim to create the illusion of length. To do this, try a long, layered cut that falls below your shoulders. Avoid blunt cuts or heavy bangs that can make your face appear wider. Side-swept bangs can also help to elongate your face.

4. How to Flatter a Square Face Shape

Those with square faces should aim to soften their strong jawline. Try a shoulder-length cut with long, soft layers that frame your face. Side-swept bangs can also help to soften your features. Avoid blunt cuts or heavy layers that can accentuate your square jawline.

5. How to Flatter a Heart-Shaped Face

Those with heart-shaped faces should aim to balance their features. Try a chin-length cut with long layers that start at your cheekbones. This will help to balance your forehead and jawline. Avoid heavy or blunt bangs that can make your forehead appear wider.

6. How to Flatter a Diamond Face Shape

Those with diamond faces should aim to soften their angular features. Try a chin-length or shoulder-length cut with long, soft layers that frame your face. Side-swept bangs can also help to soften your features. Avoid blunt cuts or heavy bangs that can accentuate your strong cheekbones.

7. Tips for Flattering Any Face Shape

No matter what your face shape is, there are a few general tips that can help to flatter any face shape. First, consider your hair texture and thickness when choosing a hairstyle. Fine hair may benefit from shorter cuts with layers, while thick hair may benefit from longer cuts with minimal layers. Second, consider your lifestyle and how much time you're willing to spend styling your hair. A low-maintenance style may be best for those with a busy schedule. Finally, don't be afraid to experiment with different styles until you find the one that works best for you.

8. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I still wear bangs if I have a round face?
A: Yes, you can still wear bangs if you have a round face. However, side-swept bangs are typically more flattering than blunt bangs.

Q: Is it better to have short or long hair for a heart-shaped face?
A: A chin-length or shoulder-length cut is typically the most flattering for a heart-shaped face.

Q: How often should I get my hair cut to maintain my hairstyle?
A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, a trim every 6-8 weeks is recommended to maintain your hairstyle.

9. Hair Care Tips for Flattering Your Face Shape

No matter what your face shape is, taking care of your hair is essential for achieving a flattering hairstyle. Here are a few tips to keep your hair looking its best:

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
- Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
- Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
- Use a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles to avoid damaging your hair while styling.
- Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and choosing the right hairstyle for your face shape, you can achieve a look that is both flattering and stylish. Experiment with different cuts and styles until you find the one that works best for you, and don't be afraid to ask your hairstylist for recommendations.
