Description

The 1960s was a decade of change and rebellion in many aspects of society, including fashion and hairstyles. Men's hairstyles in the 1960s were characterized by clean cuts, sleek styles, and a lot of hair products. The classic look of the decade was the "Mad Men" style, which was popularized by the hit TV show of the same name. This style is still popular today, and many men are still sporting the look over 50 years later.

How to Achieve the Look

To achieve the classic 1960s look, you'll need to start with the right cut. The most popular cut of the decade was the "ivy league" or "preppy" cut, which was short on the sides and back and left longer on top. This cut can be achieved by visiting a barber or stylist who is familiar with the style.

Once you have the right cut, you'll need to style your hair using the right products. The key to achieving the look is to use a lot of pomade or wax to create a slick, shiny finish. You can also use a comb to create a side part or slick your hair back. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to achieve the classic 1960s look:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a small amount of pomade or wax to your hands and rub them together to warm the product.
  3. Apply the product to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way up to the tips.
  4. Use a comb to create a side part or slick your hair back, depending on the style you're going for.
  5. Smooth out any bumps or flyaways with a bit more pomade or wax.
  6. Finish the look with a spritz of hairspray to hold everything in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks for achieving the perfect 1960s hairstyle:

  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair type.
  • If you have longer hair, consider using a hair dryer to help set the style and create more volume.
  • You can add a touch of color to your hair to really make the style pop. Try using a semi-permanent hair dye in a shade that complements your skin tone.
  • Accessorize your look with a pair of classic sunglasses or a fedora hat for a truly authentic 1960s vibe.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about 1960s hairstyles for men:

What was the most popular hairstyle for men in the 1960s?

The most popular hairstyle for men in the 1960s was the "ivy league" or "preppy" cut, which was short on the sides and back and left longer on top.

What products were used to style men's hair in the 1960s?

The most popular products used to style men's hair in the 1960s were pomade and wax, which were used to create a slick, shiny finish.

Can I achieve a 1960s hairstyle with longer hair?

Yes, it's possible to achieve a 1960s hairstyle with longer hair. You'll just need to use more product and possibly use a hair dryer to create more volume.

Hair Care for 1960 Hairstyles

To keep your 1960s hairstyle looking its best, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and in great shape:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to remove dirt and oils.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and smooth.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain your style.
  • Avoid using too many products, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve the classic 1960s look and keep your hair looking great for years to come!
