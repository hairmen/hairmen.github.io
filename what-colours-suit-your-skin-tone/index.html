What Colours Suit Your Skin Tone A Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

In this year 2023, it's important to know what colours suit your skin tone. Whether you're dressing up for an event or just want to update your wardrobe, wearing the right colours can make a huge difference. This guide will help you figure out which colours work best for your skin tone.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to Determine Your Skin Tone
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Choosing Colours
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Your Skin Tone

Description

Your skin tone is the natural colour of your skin. There are three main categories: cool, warm, and neutral. Determining your skin tone will help you choose colours that complement your natural complexion.

How to Determine Your Skin Tone

The easiest way to determine your skin tone is to look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear blue or purple, you have cool undertones. If they appear green or olive, you have warm undertones. If you can't tell if they're blue or green, you may have neutral undertones.

Another way to determine your skin tone is to look at the jewellery you wear. If you look best in silver, you likely have cool undertones. If you look best in gold, you likely have warm undertones. If you look good in both, you may have neutral undertones.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you choose colours that suit your skin tone:

  1. Determine your skin tone using the methods above.
  2. Choose colours that complement your skin tone. If you have cool undertones, choose colours that have a blue or purple base, such as jewel tones (emerald, sapphire, amethyst) or pastels (lavender, baby blue, mint green). If you have warm undertones, choose colours that have a yellow or orange base, such as earth tones (olive, rust, ochre) or bright hues (coral, peach, golden yellow). If you have neutral undertones, you can wear a wide range of colours.
  3. Avoid colours that clash with your skin tone. If you have cool undertones, avoid colours that have a yellow or orange base, such as mustard or pumpkin. If you have warm undertones, avoid colours that have a blue or purple base, such as royal blue or violet.
  4. Experiment with different shades of the colours that suit your skin tone. For example, if you have cool undertones and look good in jewel tones, try wearing emerald green or sapphire blue to see which shade works best for you.

Tips for Choosing Colours

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing colours:

  • Consider the occasion. Bright colours may be more appropriate for a summer wedding than a job interview.
  • Don't be afraid to mix and match colours. Just because you have warm undertones doesn't mean you can't wear cool colours.
  • Think about your hair and eye colour when choosing colours. If you have warm undertones and blue eyes, for example, you may look great in a navy blue dress.
  • Pay attention to texture and fabric. Some colours may look better in certain fabrics. A bright red silk blouse may look better than a bright red cotton t-shirt.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about choosing colours:

Can I wear black if I have warm undertones?

Yes, you can wear black if you have warm undertones. However, you may want to soften the look by pairing it with a warm-coloured accessory, such as a gold necklace or brown shoes.

What if I have a lot of redness in my skin?

If you have a lot of redness in your skin, you may want to avoid colours that make it look more prominent, such as bright red or orange. Instead, try wearing colours that have a blue or green base, such as navy or forest green.

Do I have to follow these guidelines?

No, these guidelines are just suggestions. If you feel confident in a certain colour, wear it!

Hair Care for Your Skin Tone

Your hair colour can also play a role in determining which colours suit your skin tone. Here are some general guidelines:

  • If you have cool undertones and blonde hair, try wearing pastels or jewel tones.
  • If you have warm undertones and blonde hair, try wearing earth tones or bright hues.
  • If you have cool undertones and brown hair, try wearing navy or emerald green.
  • If you have warm undertones and brown hair, try wearing rust or coral.
  • If you have cool undertones and black hair, try wearing jewel tones or pastels.
  • If you have warm undertones and black hair, try wearing bright hues or earth tones.

Remember, these are just guidelines. The most important thing is to wear colours that make you feel confident and comfortable.

Now that you know what colours suit your skin tone, it's time to update your wardrobe and start experimenting with different shades and textures. With a little bit of practice, you'll be able to choose colours that complement your natural complexion and make you feel great.
