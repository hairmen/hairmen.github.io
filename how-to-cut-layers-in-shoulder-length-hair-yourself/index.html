How To Cut Layers In Shoulder Length Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of the same old look and want to add some dimension and volume to your shoulder-length hair? Adding layers is a great way to achieve a fresh and new look without sacrificing length. And the best part? You can do it yourself! In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting layers in shoulder-length hair, step by step.

How to Cut Layers in Shoulder Length Hair Yourself

Step One: Gather Your Tools

Before you get started, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step Two: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before cutting. This will ensure that your hair is clean and free of tangles, making it easier to cut.

Step Three: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into three parts: the top, middle, and bottom. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step Four: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer. Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut it straight across with your scissors.

Repeat this process for the rest of the bottom layer, working your way from the back to the front.

Step Five: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the middle section of hair and comb it straight down. Take a small section of hair from the back and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at a slight angle, starting from the length of the bottom layer and working your way up to the top layer.

Repeat this process for the rest of the middle layer, working your way from the back to the front.

Step Six: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top section of hair and comb it straight down. Take a small section of hair from the back and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at a slight angle, starting from the length of the middle layer and working your way up to the ends.

Repeat this process for the rest of the top layer, working your way from the back to the front.

Step Seven: Blend the Layers

Once you have cut all the layers, blend them together by combing your hair straight down and cutting any uneven ends.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Shoulder Length Hair Yourself

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure accuracy and precision.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Keep your head straight and avoid tilting it to prevent uneven layers.
  • Take breaks if you feel tired or uncomfortable to avoid making mistakes.

FAQs

Q: How many layers should I cut in my shoulder-length hair?

A: The number of layers you cut depends on the thickness and texture of your hair. If your hair is thick, you may want to add more layers to create volume. If your hair is fine, fewer layers may be necessary to avoid thinning it out.

Q: Can I cut layers in my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. However, it is important to cut the layers when your hair is dry to avoid cutting too much length due to the natural shrinkage of curly hair when dry.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting layers in your shoulder-length hair, it is important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat tools regularly to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to avoid pulling and breaking your hair.

By following these tips, you can enjoy your new layered hairstyle and keep it looking its best!
