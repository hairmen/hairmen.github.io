How To Style Curly Hair After The Shower

Written by Daniel Jul 21, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Curly hair can be a tricky beast to tame. It has its own mind and can be prone to frizz, tangles, and unruliness. But with the right techniques, products, and tools, you can style your curly hair after the shower and keep it looking fabulous all day long. In this post, we'll show you step-by-step how to style your curly hair after the shower, along with some helpful tips, FAQs, and hair care advice. So, let's get started!

How to Style Curly Hair After the Shower

The key to styling curly hair after the shower is to work with your hair's natural texture and shape. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by gently squeezing the excess water out of your hair with a microfiber towel or t-shirt. Avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel, as it can cause frizz and breakage.
  2. Apply a curl-defining product to your hair, such as a curl cream, gel, or mousse. Be sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair, from roots to tips.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots, being gentle to avoid damaging your curls.
  4. If you have long hair, consider scrunching your curls with your hands to encourage definition and bounce.
  5. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting. Hold the diffuser close to your scalp and move it in a circular motion to dry your roots. Then, use the same motion to dry the mid-lengths and ends of your hair. Avoid using a high heat setting, as it can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  6. Once your hair is dry, you can use a curling iron or wand to touch up any curls that need it, but be careful not to overdo it. You want your curls to look natural and effortless.
  7. Finally, apply a light-hold hairspray or hair oil to set your style and add shine.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you prefer a more detailed guide, here's a step-by-step breakdown of how to style curly hair after the shower:

  1. Start with freshly washed hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner formulated for curly hair.
  2. Gently squeeze the excess water out of your hair with a microfiber towel or t-shirt. Avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel.
  3. Apply a curl-defining product to your hair, such as a curl cream, gel, or mousse. Be sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair, from roots to tips.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots, being gentle to avoid damaging your curls.
  5. Scrunch your curls with your hands to encourage definition and bounce.
  6. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting. Hold the diffuser close to your scalp and move it in a circular motion to dry your roots. Then, use the same motion to dry the mid-lengths and ends of your hair.
  7. Once your hair is dry, you can use a curling iron or wand to touch up any curls that need it, but be careful not to overdo it.
  8. Apply a light-hold hairspray or hair oil to set your style and add shine.

Tips for Styling Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you style your curly hair after the shower:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as it can cause frizz and breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls when they're wet.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent frizz and tangles.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day, as it can disrupt your curls and cause frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and cause breakage. If you must use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about styling curly hair after the shower:

  • Can I use a regular towel to dry my curly hair? No, it's best to use a microfiber towel or t-shirt to gently squeeze the water out of your hair. Rubbing your hair with a regular towel can cause frizz and breakage.
  • What's the best way to detangle curly hair? Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots, being gentle to avoid damaging your curls.
  • Should I use a diffuser attachment on my blow dryer? Yes, a diffuser attachment can help dry your curls without causing frizz. Hold the diffuser close to your scalp and move it in a circular motion to dry your roots, then use the same motion to dry the mid-lengths and ends of your hair.
  • What's the best way to touch up curls with a curling iron? Use a curling iron or wand to touch up any curls that need it, but be careful not to overdo it. You want your curls to look natural and effortless.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner formulated for curly hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when you wash your hair, as it can dry out your curls. Use warm or cool water instead.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and cause breakage. If you must use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent frizz and tangles.

With these tips and techniques, you can style your curly hair after the shower and keep it looking fabulous all day long. Remember to work with your hair's natural texture and shape, and avoid using harsh products or tools that can damage your curls. With a little patience and practice, you'll have beautiful, bouncy curls that you can be proud of!
