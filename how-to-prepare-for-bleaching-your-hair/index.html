How To Prepare For Bleaching Your Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 28, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're considering bleaching your hair, it's important to prepare properly to ensure the best results. Bleaching can be damaging to your hair, so taking the time to properly prepare can help minimize any potential damage. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know to prepare for bleaching your hair.

Table of Contents:

  1. What is Hair Bleaching?
  2. How to Prepare for Bleaching Your Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide to Bleaching Your Hair

  6. Hair Care After Bleaching

What is Hair Bleaching?

Hair bleaching is a process that lightens the color of your hair. It involves using a chemical mixture to strip the pigment from your hair strands, leaving them lighter in color. Bleaching can be done on all hair types and colors, but it's important to note that it can be damaging to your hair.

How to Prepare for Bleaching Your Hair

Before you bleach your hair, there are a few things you should do to prepare:

Gather Your Supplies

You'll need a few supplies to bleach your hair, including:

  • Bleach powder
  • Developer
  • Applicator brush
  • Gloves
  • Bowl
  • Old t-shirt or towel

Do a Strand Test

Before you bleach your hair, it's important to do a strand test to determine how your hair will react to the bleach. Take a small section of hair and apply the bleach mixture to it, following the instructions on the package. Leave the bleach on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse it out. Check the strand for any damage or breakage.

Prepare Your Hair

It's important to prepare your hair before you bleach it. This includes:

  • Washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup
  • Brushing your hair to remove any tangles
  • Trimming any split ends

Protect Your Skin

Bleach can be irritating to your skin, so it's important to protect your skin during the bleaching process. Put on gloves to protect your hands, and apply petroleum jelly or a thick conditioner along your hairline to prevent the bleach from touching your skin.

Step-by-Step Guide to Bleaching Your Hair

Now that you've prepared for bleaching your hair, here's a step-by-step guide to the actual bleaching process:

Step 1: Mix the Bleach

Follow the instructions on the bleach package to mix the bleach powder and the developer together in a bowl. Mix until you have a smooth, creamy consistency.

Step 2: Apply the Bleach

Using the applicator brush, apply the bleach mixture to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Make sure to saturate each section of hair evenly, and avoid getting bleach on your scalp or skin.

Step 3: Check the Color

After a few minutes, check the color of your hair. If it's not light enough, leave the bleach on for a few more minutes. Keep checking the color every 5-10 minutes until you've achieved your desired shade.

Step 4: Rinse Out the Bleach

Once you've achieved your desired shade, rinse out the bleach with cool water. Make sure to rinse thoroughly to remove all of the bleach from your hair.

Step 5: Condition Your Hair

Bleaching can be drying to your hair, so it's important to condition your hair after bleaching. Use a deep conditioner to help restore moisture to your hair, and leave it on for at least 10 minutes before rinsing it out.

Tips for Bleaching Your Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when bleaching your hair:

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Use a lower volume developer for less damage
  • Don't bleach damaged or over-processed hair
  • Avoid bleaching your hair too often
  • Use a toner to remove any brassy tones after bleaching

FAQs About Bleaching Your Hair

How often can I bleach my hair?

You should wait at least 4-6 weeks between bleaching sessions to prevent damage to your hair.

What should I do if my scalp starts to burn?

If your scalp starts to burn or feel irritated, immediately rinse out the bleach and apply a soothing scalp treatment.

Can I bleach my hair if I have color in it?

You can bleach your hair if it's been previously colored, but the process may take longer and require more than one session.

Hair Care After Bleaching

After bleaching your hair, it's important to take good care of it to minimize damage and maintain the color. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner
  • Deep condition your hair regularly
  • Avoid chlorine and salt water
  • Protect your hair from the sun

By following these tips and properly preparing for the bleaching process, you can achieve beautiful, lightened hair without causing too much damage. Remember to always take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong.
