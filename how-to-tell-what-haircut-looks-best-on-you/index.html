How to Tell What Haircut Looks Best on You: A Beginners Guide

Are you tired of getting a haircut that doesn't suit your face shape or personality? Choosing the right haircut can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. In this article, we will provide you with a beginner's guide on how to tell what haircut looks best on you. We will cover everything from how to determine your face shape to tips on how to find the right haircut for your personality.

Table of Contents

  1. How to determine your face shape
  2. Consider your hair texture and thickness
  3. Think about your lifestyle
  4. Consider your personality

  6. FAQs

Step 1: How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in determining what haircut looks best on you is to determine your face shape. There are six different face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back away from your face. Take a good look at your face and compare it to the following descriptions:

  • Oval: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a gently rounded hairline and jawline.
  • Round: Your face is as wide as it is long, with a rounded hairline and jawline.
  • Square: Your face is as wide as it is long, with a strong, square jawline.
  • Heart: Your face is wider at the temples and narrows at the jawline, with a pointed chin.
  • Diamond: Your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at the forehead and jawline.
  • Oblong: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a straight hairline and jawline.

Once you have determined your face shape, you can start looking for haircuts that will complement your features.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Texture and Thickness

When choosing a haircut, it's important to consider your hair texture and thickness. If you have fine, thin hair, you may want to opt for a shorter haircut that adds volume and body. If you have thick, curly hair, you may want to consider a longer haircut that will help to weigh down your curls and prevent frizz.

Step 3: Think About Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle should also be taken into consideration when choosing a haircut. If you lead an active lifestyle and enjoy outdoor activities, you may want to opt for a low-maintenance haircut that doesn't require a lot of styling. On the other hand, if you work in a professional setting, you may want to choose a more polished, sophisticated haircut.

Step 4: Consider Your Personality

Your personality should also be reflected in your haircut. If you're outgoing and adventurous, you may want to choose a more daring haircut that makes a statement. If you're more reserved and conservative, you may want to opt for a classic, timeless haircut.

Step 5: Tips for Finding the Right Haircut

Here are a few tips to help you find the right haircut:

  • Bring pictures to your stylist that show the haircut you're interested in.
  • Ask your stylist for their professional opinion.
  • Try on wigs or use virtual makeover tools to see what different haircuts would look like on you.

Step 6: FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about finding the right haircut:

  • Q: Can a haircut change the appearance of my face shape?
  • A: Yes, a haircut can help to create the illusion of a different face shape.
  • Q: How often should I get a haircut?
  • A: It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain your style.
  • Q: What should I do if I don't like my new haircut?
  • A: Communicate with your stylist and explain what you don't like about the haircut. They may be able to make adjustments to fix the problem.

Step 7: Hair Care Tips

Once you've found the perfect haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain your style. Here are a few hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on a daily basis.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your haircut.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray.

In conclusion, finding the right haircut doesn't have to be a daunting task. By determining your face shape, considering your hair texture and thickness, thinking about your lifestyle and personality, and following a few tips, you can find a haircut that looks best on you and suits your individual style.
