What Does Crown Balding Look Like

Written by Daniel Nov 05, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you noticing hair loss in the crown area of your head? This could be a sign of crown balding, a common type of male pattern baldness. In this article, we’ll discuss what crown balding looks like, how to identify it, and what you can do to prevent further hair loss.

Description

Crown balding, also known as vertex baldness or crown thinning, is when hair loss occurs at the top of the head, specifically in the crown area. This type of hair loss can create a bald spot or thinning hair in a circular pattern. Crown balding is most common in men, but it can also affect women.

How to Identify Crown Balding

If you’re unsure if you have crown balding, there are a few signs to look out for. One of the most noticeable signs is a circular or oval-shaped bald spot at the crown of the head. This area may also have thinner hair than the rest of the scalp. You may also notice that your hairline is receding at the temples or that your hair is becoming thinner overall.

Step by Step Guide to Identifying Crown Balding

Step 1: Check Your Crown

Stand in front of a mirror and check the crown area of your head. Look for any circular or oval-shaped bald spots, or areas where the hair is thinner than the rest of your scalp.

Step 2: Check Your Hairline

Check your hairline at the temples. If your hairline is receding, this could be a sign of crown balding.

Step 3: Assess Overall Hair Thickness

Assess the overall thickness of your hair. If you notice that your hair is becoming thinner, this could be a sign of crown balding or another type of hair loss.

Tips to Prevent Crown Balding

While there is no guaranteed way to prevent crown balding, there are a few things you can do to slow down or delay hair loss:

  • Eat a healthy diet
  • Exercise regularly
  • Stay hydrated
  • Reduce stress
  • Avoid tight hairstyles or hair products that may damage your hair
  • Consider using a hair growth treatment, such as minoxidil or finasteride

FAQs

Q: Is crown balding genetic?

A: Yes, crown balding is often hereditary and can be passed down through genes.

Q: Can crown balding be reversed?

A: While there is no guaranteed way to reverse crown balding, there are treatments available that can help slow down or delay further hair loss.

Q: Does wearing a hat cause crown balding?

A: Wearing a hat does not cause crown balding, but wearing a tight hat or a hat for extended periods of time could potentially damage your hair.

Hair Care for Crown Balding

If you have crown balding, it’s important to take care of the hair you still have. Here are a few tips:

  • Gently shampoo your hair and avoid using hot water or harsh chemicals.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, and use a wide-toothed comb instead of a brush.
  • Consider using a volumizing shampoo or conditioner to make your hair appear thicker.
  • Avoid exposing your scalp to direct sunlight for extended periods of time.
  • Consider using a scalp treatment or oil to nourish your scalp and promote hair growth.

By taking care of your scalp and hair, you can help slow down or delay further hair loss due to crown balding.
