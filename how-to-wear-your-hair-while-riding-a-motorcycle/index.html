How To Wear Your Hair While Riding A Motorcycle Tips And Tricks

Written by Luffy Sep 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Motorcycle riding is a thrilling experience, but it can be challenging to keep your hair in place while cruising down the highway. Whether you're a seasoned rider or a beginner, knowing how to wear your hair while riding a motorcycle can make all the difference in your comfort and style. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks to help you keep your hair in check while you ride.

How to Wear Your Hair While Riding a Motorcycle

When it comes to motorcycle riding, there are several ways to wear your hair that can help keep it secure and comfortable. Here are some popular options:

1. Braid Your Hair

Braiding your hair is a classic way to keep it in place while riding. This style is especially useful if you have long hair, as it prevents it from getting tangled or flying around in the wind. To create a braid, divide your hair into three sections and weave them together tightly. Secure the end of the braid with a hair tie, and you're good to go!

2. Wear a Bandana

A bandana is a versatile accessory that can help keep your hair out of your face while riding. Simply fold the bandana in half and tie it around your head, making sure to tuck in any loose strands of hair. This style is especially popular among vintage motorcycle enthusiasts and can add a touch of retro flair to your riding outfit.

3. Use a Hair Net

If you have shorter hair, a hair net can be an effective way to keep it in place while riding. Simply gather your hair into a low ponytail and secure it with a hair tie. Then, place the hair net over your head and tuck in any stray hairs. This option is also great for preventing helmet hair, as it helps keep your locks smooth and in place.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Braid Your Hair for Motorcycle Riding

If you're new to braiding your hair, here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

Step 1: Brush Your Hair

Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to weave into a braid.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into three equal sections. Hold one section in each hand, with the third section in the middle.

Step 3: Weave Your Hair

Begin weaving the sections of hair together, starting with the right-hand section. Cross it over the middle section, then repeat with the left-hand section. Continue weaving until you reach the end of your hair.

Step 4: Secure Your Braid

Once you've finished braiding, use a hair tie to secure the end of your braid. You can also use a decorative clip or bobby pin to add some flair.

Tips for Keeping Your Hair Healthy While Riding

While wearing your hair in a protective style can help prevent damage while riding, it's also essential to take care of your hair off the bike. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo

Riding in the wind and sun can dry out your hair, so it's essential to use a moisturizing shampoo to keep it hydrated. Look for products that contain natural oils like coconut or argan oil.

2. Condition Regularly

Conditioning your hair can help prevent breakage and keep it smooth and shiny. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to give your locks some extra TLC.

3. Protect Your Hair from UV Damage

The sun's UV rays can be damaging to your hair, so it's essential to protect it with a hat or scarf while riding. You can also use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to shield your locks from the sun's harmful rays.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions About Wearing Your Hair While Riding a Motorcycle

Here are some common questions riders have about keeping their hair in check while on the bike:

1. Can I wear my hair down while riding a motorcycle?

While it's possible to wear your hair down while riding, it's not recommended. Loose hair can get caught in the wind and tangle, causing discomfort and even damage to your hair. It's best to secure your hair in a protective style like a braid or bun.

2. Will wearing a helmet damage my hair?

Frequent helmet use can cause friction and breakage, but wearing a protective style like a braid or hairnet can help prevent damage. It's also essential to take care of your hair off the bike by using moisturizing products and deep conditioning treatments.

3. Can I ride a motorcycle with wet hair?

It's not recommended to ride a motorcycle with wet hair, as it can increase your risk of catching a cold or other respiratory illness. It's best to dry your hair thoroughly before heading out on the bike.

Conclusion

Keeping your hair in check while riding a motorcycle is essential for both comfort and style. Whether you prefer a braid, bandana, or hairnet, there are plenty of options to choose from. By following these tips and tricks, you can enjoy a safe and comfortable ride while looking your best.
