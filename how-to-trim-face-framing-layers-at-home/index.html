How To Trim Face Framing Layers At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Nov 16, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking to freshen up your hairstyle without visiting the salon? One simple way to do so is by trimming your face framing layers. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to do it at home.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to Trim Face Framing Layers at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Trimming Face Framing Layers at Home
  • FAQs

Description

Face framing layers are a trendy and versatile hairstyle that can complement any face shape. These layers are cut around your face, usually starting from your cheekbones or jawline, to frame and accentuate your features. However, visiting the salon for a trim can be costly and time-consuming. Trimming your face framing layers at home is an easy and affordable way to maintain your hairstyle.

How to Trim Face Framing Layers at Home

Before you start trimming your face framing layers at home, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It’s also a good idea to have a towel or hair clip to keep your hair out of the way.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to trim your face framing layers at home:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. This will help you see the natural shape of your face framing layers.
  2. Part your hair in the middle or on one side, depending on your preference.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the front of your face, around your cheekbones or jawline.
  4. Comb the section of hair straight down.
  5. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, about one inch away from the ends.
  6. Using your hair cutting scissors, trim the ends of the hair at an angle. Start by cutting small pieces and check your progress in the mirror.
  7. Repeat steps 3 to 6 on the other side of your face.
  8. Check both sides of your face in the mirror to make sure the length and angle of your face framing layers are even.

Tips for Trimming Face Framing Layers at Home

Here are some tips to help you achieve a salon-worthy trim:

  • Start by trimming a small amount of hair. You can always cut more, but you can’t put it back once it’s gone.
  • Cut your hair at an angle, not straight across. This will give you a more natural and flattering look.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can damage and split your hair.
  • Take breaks if you need them. Trimming your hair can be tiring, so give yourself a break if your arms start to ache.

FAQs

1. How often should I trim my face framing layers?

It’s recommended to trim your face framing layers every six to eight weeks to maintain your hairstyle.

2. Can I trim my face framing layers with regular scissors?

No, it’s best to use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair.

3. What if I make a mistake while trimming my face framing layers?

If you make a mistake, don’t panic. You can always visit a salon to fix it or wait for your hair to grow out.

Hair Care After Trimming Face Framing Layers at Home

After trimming your face framing layers at home, it’s important to take care of your hair. Use a nourishing hair mask or conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and protect your hair from the sun and chlorine if you go swimming. With the right care, your face framing layers will continue to look fresh and stylish.

Trimming your face framing layers at home is a simple and affordable way to maintain your hairstyle. With these easy steps and tips, you can achieve a salon-worthy trim in no time.
