How Do I Know What The Shape Of My Face Is

Description

Knowing the shape of your face is essential when it comes to choosing the right hairstyles, makeup, and accessories that flatter your features. Your face shape determines which styles will look good on you and which ones won't. But how do you figure out what your face shape is?

In this blog post, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to determine your face shape. We'll also give you tips and tricks for identifying your face shape, answer some frequently asked questions, and provide hair care tips for different face shapes. By the end of this article, you'll be able to confidently identify your face shape and choose styles that flatter your features.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

There are six main face shapes: round, oval, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, you'll need to measure the length and width of your face and jawline. You'll also need to take note of the shape of your forehead, cheekbones, and chin.

Step 1: Measure Your Face

Using a measuring tape, measure the length of your face from your hairline to your chin. Then, measure the width of your face at the widest point, which is usually your cheekbones. Write down these measurements.

Step 2: Measure Your Jawline

Measure the width of your jawline from one end to the other. Write down this measurement.

Step 3: Determine Your Forehead Shape

Look in the mirror and take note of the shape of your forehead. Is it wide and round, or narrow and angular? Write down your observations.

Step 4: Determine Your Cheekbone Shape

Take note of the shape of your cheekbones. Are they high and prominent, or low and flat? Write down your observations.

Step 5: Determine Your Chin Shape

Look at your chin and determine its shape. Is it pointed, round, or square? Write down your observations.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your measurements and observations, it's time to determine your face shape. Use the following guide to help you identify your face shape:

Round Face Shape

A round face shape has equal length and width, with full cheeks and a rounded jawline. Your forehead and chin are also rounded. If your face length and width measurements are similar, and your forehead and chin are curved, you likely have a round face shape.

Oval Face Shape

An oval face shape has a slightly longer face length than width, with a gently curved jawline. Your forehead is wider than your chin, and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face. If your face length is longer than your face width, and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, you likely have an oval face shape.

Square Face Shape

A square face shape has equal length and width, with a strong jawline and a square-shaped forehead. Your cheekbones are also prominent. If your face length and width measurements are similar, and your jawline is angular and strong, you likely have a square face shape.

Heart Face Shape

A heart face shape has a wider forehead that tapers down to a pointy chin. Your cheekbones are also prominent. If your forehead is wider than your chin, and your chin is pointed, you likely have a heart face shape.

Diamond Face Shape

A diamond face shape has a narrow forehead and chin, with wide cheekbones. Your jawline is also angular. If your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, and your forehead and chin are tapered, you likely have a diamond face shape.

Oblong Face Shape

An oblong face shape has a longer face length than width, with a gently curved jawline. Your forehead, cheekbones, and chin are all roughly the same width. If your face length is longer than your face width, and your forehead, cheekbones, and chin are roughly the same width, you likely have an oblong face shape.

Tips for Identifying Your Face Shape

If you're still having trouble identifying your face shape, try the following tips:

  • Take a photo of yourself and trace the outline of your face to see its shape more clearly.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you measure your face and take note of its features.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles and makeup looks to see which ones flatter your features.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can my face shape change over time?

A: No, your face shape is determined by your bone structure and is unlikely to change over time.

Q: Can I have a combination of face shapes?

A: Yes, it's possible to have a combination of face shapes. For example, you might have a heart-shaped face with a slightly angular jawline.

Q: Do face shapes determine what styles I can wear?

A: Yes, your face shape can help determine what hairstyles, makeup, and accessories will flatter your features.

Hair Care Tips for Different Face Shapes

Now that you've identified your face shape, use the following hair care tips to choose hairstyles that flatter your features:

Round Face Shape

Go for hairstyles that add height to your crown and length to your face, such as long layers and high ponytails. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your face, such as blunt bobs and full bangs.

Oval Face Shape

Most hairstyles look good on oval face shapes, so experiment with different lengths and styles. Avoid hairstyles that cover your forehead, as they can make your face appear shorter.

Square Face Shape

Choose hairstyles that soften your angular jawline, such as layered cuts and side-swept bangs. Avoid hairstyles that emphasize your square forehead, such as blunt bobs and straight-across bangs.

Heart Face Shape

Go for hairstyles that add width to your forehead and chin, such as side-swept bangs and chin-length bobs. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to the top of your head, such as high ponytails and slicked-back styles.

Diamond Face Shape

Choose hairstyles that balance your narrow forehead and chin, such as side-swept bangs and chin-length bobs. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to your cheeks, such as blunt bobs and full bangs.

Oblong Face Shape

Go for hairstyles that add width to your face, such as soft curls and layered cuts. Avoid hairstyles that elongate your face, such as straight, long hair and slicked-back styles.

Identifying your face shape is the first step to choosing hairstyles, makeup, and accessories that flatter your features. Use the tips and tricks in this blog post to determine your face shape, and experiment with different styles to see what works best for you.
