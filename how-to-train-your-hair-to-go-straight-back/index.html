How To Train Your Hair To Go Straight Back A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Oct 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a sleek and polished hairstyle, training your hair to go straight back might be just what you need. This style is great for both men and women and can be achieved with a little bit of patience and effort. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of training your hair to go straight back, as well as provide tips and advice for maintaining your new look.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Train Your Hair to Go Straight Back

Training your hair to go straight back involves using a combination of styling products and techniques. Here are the basic steps:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before you begin styling your hair, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to ensure that your hair is free of buildup and tangles.

Step 2: Apply a Styling Product

Next, apply a styling product to your hair. This can be a gel, mousse, or pomade, depending on your hair type and the level of hold you want. Work the product through your hair from root to tip, making sure to distribute it evenly.

Step 3: Use a Comb or Brush

Using a comb or brush, start at the front of your hairline and comb or brush your hair straight back. Work in small sections, making sure to smooth out any bumps or knots. It's important to do this while your hair is still wet and pliable, as this will help to train your hair to stay in place.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Once you've combed or brushed your hair straight back, use a blow dryer on a low heat setting to dry your hair. Use a brush or comb to keep your hair in place as you dry it, and be sure to keep the dryer moving to avoid overheating any one section.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

Once your hair is dry, finish with a spritz of hairspray to help hold your style in place. Choose a hairspray with a medium to strong hold, depending on the thickness of your hair and the level of hold you want.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide for training your hair to go straight back:

Step 1: Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will help to remove any buildup or oils that may be preventing your hair from lying flat.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

Use a towel to gently dry your hair, being careful not to rub it too vigorously. You want your hair to be damp, but not soaking wet.

Step 3: Apply a Styling Product

Apply a styling product to your hair, such as a gel, mousse, or pomade. Be sure to work the product through your hair from root to tip, making sure to distribute it evenly.

Step 4: Comb Your Hair Straight Back

Using a comb, start at the front of your hairline and comb your hair straight back. Work in small sections, making sure to smooth out any bumps or knots. It's important to do this while your hair is still wet and pliable, as this will help to train your hair to stay in place.

Step 5: Blow Dry Your Hair

Use a blow dryer on a low heat setting to dry your hair. Use a brush or comb to keep your hair in place as you dry it, and be sure to keep the dryer moving to avoid overheating any one section.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

Once your hair is dry, finish with a spritz of hairspray to help hold your style in place. Choose a hairspray with a medium to strong hold, depending on the thickness of your hair and the level of hold you want.

Tips for Maintaining Your Style

Here are some tips and tricks for maintaining your straight-back hairstyle:

  • Use a silk pillowcase: Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and keep your hair looking smooth.
  • Wear a hat: If you're going to be outside in windy conditions, wearing a hat can help to keep your hair in place.
  • Don't over-wash your hair: Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, which can make it harder to style.
  • Trim your hair regularly: Regular trims can help to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to train your hair to go straight back?

A: It can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to train your hair to go straight back, depending on your hair type and the level of hold you want.

Q: Can I use a hair straightener to train my hair to go straight back?

A: While you can use a hair straightener to smooth out any bumps or kinks in your hair, it's not recommended to use it as your primary styling tool for this look. This is because straighteners can be damaging to your hair over time.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some general hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner: Look for products that are free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals that can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling: If possible, try to avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers, straighteners, and curling irons. If you do use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Eat a healthy diet: Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Get regular trims: Trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks can help to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you should be well on your way to training your hair to go straight back. Remember to be patient and consistent, and don't be afraid to experiment with different products and styling methods until you find the perfect look for you.
