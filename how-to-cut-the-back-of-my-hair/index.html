How To Cut The Back Of My Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially when it comes to the back. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can give yourself a fresh trim without leaving your home. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut the back of your hair, along with some tips and FAQs to help you achieve a salon-worthy look.

  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair

  • Blending Your Hair

Tools You Will Need

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror

It's important to use sharp scissors and clippers to ensure a clean cut. If you don't have clippers, you can use scissors for the entire haircut, but it may take longer.

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Brush out any tangles and knots, and make sure your hair is completely dry. Wet hair can stretch, making it difficult to get an accurate cut.

Next, find a comfortable place to sit where you can easily see the back of your head in the mirror. It's a good idea to have a handheld mirror as well, so you can see the back of your head from multiple angles.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair will make the cutting process easier and more manageable. Divide your hair into three sections: top, middle, and bottom. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section of hair, leaving the bottom section loose.

Starting with the bottom section, comb your hair straight down and use your clippers or scissors to trim any split ends. It's important to only take off a small amount of hair at a time, as you can always go back and cut more if needed.

Cutting Your Hair

Once you've trimmed the bottom section, let down the middle section and comb it straight down. Use the bottom section as a guide to make sure you are cutting your hair evenly. Again, only take off a small amount of hair at a time.

Repeat this process with the top section of your hair, making sure to blend the layers as you go. It's important to be patient and take your time, as rushing the process can lead to uneven cuts.

Blending Your Hair

To blend your hair, comb it straight up and use your scissors to cut vertically into the hair, creating small snips. This will help blend the layers together and create a smooth transition.

Once you've finished blending, comb your hair straight down and check for any uneven spots or flyaways. Use your scissors or clippers to make any necessary adjustments.

Finishing Touches

When you're satisfied with the length and shape of your hair, it's time to add some finishing touches. Use your scissors to create layers or texturize your hair, if desired.

If you have bangs, trim them separately using the same techniques as above. Make sure to cut them slightly longer than you want, as they will shrink up when they dry.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Always start with clean, dry hair
  • Use sharp scissors and clippers
  • Only cut a small amount of hair at a time
  • Be patient and take your time
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head

FAQs

Can I cut my hair by myself?

Yes, with the right tools and techniques, you can cut your own hair at home. However, if you're not confident in your abilities, it's always best to seek the help of a professional.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

How do I know if I've cut too much hair?

If you're unsure about the length or shape of your hair, it's best to err on the side of caution and only cut a small amount at a time. You can always go back and cut more if needed.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. When you do use heat, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut the back of your hair at home. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll have a salon-worthy look in no time.
