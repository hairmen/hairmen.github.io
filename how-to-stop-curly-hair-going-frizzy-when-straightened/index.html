How To Stop Curly Hair Going Frizzy When Straightened

Written by Daniel Sep 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of straightening your curly hair only to have it turn into a frizzy mess? It can be frustrating to spend time and effort on styling your hair, only to have it look like you haven't even tried. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to prevent your curly hair from going frizzy when straightened. Read on to learn more.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Curly Hair Going Frizzy When Straightened
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

How to Stop Curly Hair Going Frizzy When Straightened

The key to preventing frizz when straightening curly hair is to keep it moisturized and protected. Here are some steps you can take:

1. Use Heat Protectant

Before straightening your hair, use a heat protectant spray or cream to prevent damage and frizz. Apply it evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair before straightening can help to smooth out the curls and reduce frizz. Use a round brush to help straighten your hair as you blow dry it.

3. Use the Right Straightener

Invest in a high-quality straightener with ceramic plates to prevent damage and reduce frizz. Make sure the temperature is appropriate for your hair type to avoid overheating and causing damage.

4. Straighten Small Sections

Instead of trying to straighten large sections of hair, work in small sections to ensure that each strand is fully straightened. This will help to prevent frizz and ensure a smooth finish.

5. Avoid Touching Your Hair

Once you've straightened your hair, try to avoid touching it as much as possible. The oils and dirt from your hands can transfer to your hair and cause it to become frizzy.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to straightening curly hair without causing frizz:

  1. Wash and condition your hair, then gently towel dry.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray or cream to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  3. Blow dry your hair with a round brush, aiming the nozzle downwards to smooth out the curls.
  4. Divide your hair into small sections.
  5. Starting with the bottom sections, use a high-quality straightener to straighten each section of hair.
  6. Work your way up to the top sections of hair, making sure to straighten each section fully.
  7. Avoid touching your hair as much as possible once you're finished.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent frizz when straightening curly hair:

  • Avoid straightening wet hair, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair excessively, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz while you sleep.

FAQ

Q: Can I straighten curly hair without causing damage?

A: Yes, as long as you use a high-quality straightener with ceramic plates and a heat protectant, you can straighten curly hair without causing damage.

Q: How often should I straighten my curly hair?

A: It's best to limit the amount of heat styling you do on your hair to prevent damage. Try to only straighten your hair once a week or less.

Q: What should I do if my hair still goes frizzy after straightening?

A: If you notice frizz after straightening, try using a small amount of hair oil or serum to smooth it down.

Hair Care

Keeping your hair healthy and moisturized is key to preventing frizz when straightening curly hair. Here are some tips for taking care of your hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these steps and taking good care of your hair, you can prevent frizz when straightening your curly locks. With a little patience and practice, you'll have smooth, straight hair in no time.
