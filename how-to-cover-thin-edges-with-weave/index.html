How To Cover Thin Edges With Weave

Written by Bardi Oct 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you struggling with thinning edges and looking for a solution? Weaves can be a great way to cover up thinning edges and give you a fuller look. In this article, we will guide you through the process of how to cover thin edges with weave. Here's what we will cover:

Table of Contents

  • How to Cover Thin Edges with Weave
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Covering Thin Edges with Weave
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Weave Wearers

How to Cover Thin Edges with Weave

The first step to covering thin edges with weave is to choose the right type of weave. You want to look for a style that will give you the coverage you need while still looking natural. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Lace Closure: A lace closure is a small piece of lace with hair attached that can be sewn or glued onto your scalp, giving you a natural-looking hairline.
  • Full Lace Wig: A full lace wig is made entirely of lace, allowing you to part the hair in any direction and giving you a natural-looking hairline.
  • Frontal: A frontal is a larger piece of lace that covers the entire front of your head, from ear to ear. This gives you more coverage than a lace closure but requires more maintenance.

Once you have chosen your weave, you will need to prepare your hair for installation. This includes washing, conditioning, and detangling your hair. You may also want to trim any split ends or damaged hair to ensure a smooth installation.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to covering thin edges with weave:

  1. Start by braiding your hair in cornrows, going from front to back. Make sure the braids are tight and secure.
  2. If you are using a lace closure or frontal, cut off any excess lace and apply a thin layer of adhesive along the hairline.
  3. Place the closure or frontal onto your scalp, making sure it is aligned with your natural hairline. Use a rat-tail comb to adjust the placement and secure it in place with bobby pins.
  4. Sew the weave onto the braids using a needle and thread. Be sure to sew the weave onto the braids and not your natural hair.
  5. Continue sewing the weave onto the braids, working from the back of your head to the front. Be sure to leave out enough hair to cover the closure or frontal.
  6. Once the weave is sewn in, use a flat iron or curling iron to style your hair as desired.
  7. Apply some oil or serum to your edges to help protect them and give them a healthy shine.

Tips for Covering Thin Edges with Weave

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when covering thin edges with weave:

  • Choose the right type of weave for your hair and the coverage you need.
  • Make sure your braids are tight and secure to prevent pulling and damage to your natural hair.
  • Be gentle when styling your hair to avoid pulling on your edges.
  • Use a silk or satin scarf or pillowcase to help protect your hair while you sleep.
  • Avoid wearing your weave for too long as it can cause damage to your natural hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about covering thin edges with weave:

How long can I wear a weave?

It is recommended to wear a weave for no more than 6-8 weeks to avoid damage to your natural hair.

Can I wash my hair while wearing a weave?

Yes, you can wash your hair while wearing a weave. However, be gentle when washing and avoid scrubbing your scalp too hard.

How do I remove a weave?

To remove a weave, simply cut the thread and carefully unravel the braids. Be sure to use scissors and not a razor to avoid cutting your natural hair.

Hair Care Tips for Weave Wearers

Here are some hair care tips for weave wearers:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can clog your pores and lead to hair loss.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Be gentle when combing or brushing your hair to avoid pulling and damage to your natural hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your edges and cause damage.

Covering thin edges with weave can be a great way to achieve a fuller look while protecting your natural hair. By following these tips and techniques, you can ensure a successful and healthy weave installation. Happy styling!
