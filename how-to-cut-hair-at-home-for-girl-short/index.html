How To Cut Hair At Home For Girl Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Oct 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon every month to get a haircut? Do you want to save money and time by cutting your hair at home? If yes, then this article is for you. Cutting hair at home might seem daunting, but it is not as difficult as you think. With the right tools, technique, and guidance, you can cut your hair at home and achieve salon-worthy results. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting hair at home for a girl short.

How to Cut Hair at Home for Girls Short – A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather the Right Tools

The first step to cutting hair at home is to gather the right tools. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp and specifically designed for cutting hair. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it look uneven.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, wash and dry it thoroughly. Wet hair is harder to cut and style, and it can also make your hair look shorter than it actually is. Use a towel to dry your hair, and make sure it is completely dry before you start cutting it.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to keep each section in place. This will make it easier for you to cut your hair evenly.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair from the back section. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Cut the hair to your desired length, using your first cut as a guide for the rest of your hair.

Work your way up to the top section, cutting each section of hair to the same length. Make sure to cut small sections of hair at a time to ensure evenness.

Step 5: Trim the Sides

Once you have cut the back and top sections, it’s time to trim the sides. Comb your hair forward and trim it to your desired length. Make sure to cut small sections of hair at a time to ensure evenness.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Once you have finished cutting your hair, check for evenness. Comb your hair and make sure it looks even from all angles. If you notice any unevenness, trim the hair again until you achieve your desired look.

Tips for Cutting Hair at Home for Girls Short

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting hair at home for girls short:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut your hair shorter. You can always cut more, but you can’t add hair back once it’s been cut.
  • Use a comb to guide your scissors and ensure evenness.
  • Don’t rush the process. Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time.
  • Trim your hair in a well-lit room so you can see what you’re doing.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. On average, you should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health.

Q: Can I cut my hair when it’s wet?

A: It’s best to cut your hair when it’s dry. Wet hair can shrink when it dries, making it shorter than you intended.

Hair Care After Cutting Hair at Home for Girls Short

After cutting your hair at home, it’s important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shape. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health.
  • Use a hair serum to add shine and reduce frizz.

Cutting hair at home for girls short is not as difficult as it seems. With the right tools, technique, and guidance, you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your home. Follow these steps and tips, and you’ll have a stylish new haircut in no time!
