How To Cut Your Own Long Hair Into Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 06, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to add some volume and movement to your long hair, layers are the way to go. And while going to a professional stylist may be the safest bet, cutting your own hair into layers is definitely doable with the right tools and technique. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own long hair into layers with ease.

How to Cut Your Own Long Hair into Layers: What You Need

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror

Once you have these, you're ready to start.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Own Long Hair into Layers

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair and let it completely dry before starting. This will help you get a better idea of how your hair falls naturally and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into two parts: the top and the bottom. Use hair ties or clips to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the middle of the hair and working your way down to the ends. Repeat this process for the rest of the bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, let down the top layer and repeat the same process as step 3. Make sure to cut the top layer slightly shorter than the bottom layer to create the layered effect.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Use your scissors to blend the two layers together by cutting diagonally into the hair. This will help create a more natural and seamless look.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Check your work in the mirror to make sure everything is even and blended. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and trim accordingly.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Long Hair into Layers

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair
  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will appear shorter once it dries
  • Take breaks and step away from the mirror to avoid over-cutting

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair into layers if I have curly hair?

Yes, but keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink up when dry, so make sure to cut your hair a bit longer than you want it to be.

How often should I cut my hair into layers?

It's recommended to cut your hair into layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain the style and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Layered Haircut

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and nourished
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and prevent split ends

With these tips and techniques, cutting your own long hair into layers can be a fun and easy DIY project. Just remember to take your time and be patient. Happy cutting!
