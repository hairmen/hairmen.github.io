Description

Medium length hair is a versatile length that can be styled in many different ways. Itâs long enough to create braids, twists, and updos, but short enough to wear down and show off your natural texture. How to

Before we dive into the hairstyles, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Use a heat protectant spray if using heat tools
  • Invest in some good quality hair ties and bobby pins
  • Experiment with different partings and textures to create different looks

Step by Step

1. Half-up Half-down

To create this simple hairstyle, start by taking a section of hair from each side of your head and bringing it to the back. Secure with a hair tie or clip. You can leave the rest of your hair down or create some loose waves with a curling iron for added texture.

2. Messy Bun

Pull your hair back into a low ponytail and twist it into a bun. Secure with bobby pins and pull out a few strands at the front for a relaxed, messy look. You can also create a high bun or a side bun for a different variation.

3. Side Braid

Start by creating a deep side parting and bringing all of your hair to one side. Begin braiding at the nape of your neck and work your way down, securing with a hair tie. You can leave the braid as is or gently pull it apart to create a fuller, more voluminous look.

4. Twisted Half-up

Take a section of hair from each side of your head and twist it towards the back. Secure with bobby pins and leave the rest of your hair down or create some loose waves for added texture.

5. Double Braids

Create two braids on either side of your head and secure with hair ties. You can leave the rest of your hair down or create some loose waves for added texture.

Tips

  • Use a texturizing spray to add volume and texture to your hair
  • Experiment with different hair accessories such as headbands, clips, and scrunchies
  • Create a messy look by pulling out a few strands of hair around your face
  • Use a hair serum to tame frizz and add shine

The best way to style medium length hair is to experiment with different textures and partings. Try out different hairstyles such as braids, updos, and half-up half-down styles to find what works best for you.
