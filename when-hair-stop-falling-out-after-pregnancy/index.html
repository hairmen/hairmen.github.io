When Does Hair Stop Falling Out After Pregnancy

Written by Daniel Sep 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a new mom who noticed that you're shedding more hair than usual? Don't worry. Hair loss after pregnancy is a common occurrence, and it's known as postpartum hair loss. In this article, we will discuss when hair stop falling out after pregnancy and how you can manage this temporary condition.

When Does Hair Stop Falling Out After Pregnancy?

Postpartum hair loss usually occurs three to six months after giving birth. During pregnancy, the increased levels of estrogen hormones prevent excessive hair shedding, resulting in thicker and fuller hair. However, after delivery, hormone levels start to normalize, leading to a sudden release of the retained hair, causing hair loss.

The shedding can be significant, and it can be alarming for new moms. Fortunately, postpartum hair loss is temporary and will resolve on its own within six to twelve months after delivery. After this period, hair growth will return to its normal cycle, and hair loss will stop.

How to Manage Postpartum Hair Loss?

While postpartum hair loss is part of the normal cycle, there are ways to minimize the hair loss and promote hair growth:

  • Be gentle with your hair. Avoid using harsh chemicals, hot styling tools, and tight hairstyles, as these can cause hair breakage and further hair loss.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet that is rich in protein, iron, and vitamins A and C, as these nutrients are essential for healthy hair growth.
  • Consider taking hair supplements that contain biotin, collagen, and other hair-healthy vitamins and minerals.
  • Manage stress levels through relaxation techniques, such as meditation and yoga, as stress can cause hair loss.

Step-by-Step Guide to Hair Care After Pregnancy

Here is a step-by-step guide to caring for your hair after pregnancy:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy. However, avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils, leading to dryness and breakage.

Step 2: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently. Start at the ends and work your way up to avoid pulling on your hair and causing breakage.

Step 3: Air Dry Your Hair

Avoid using hot styling tools, such as blow dryers, straighteners, and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause breakage. Instead, let your hair air dry naturally or use a cool setting on your blow dryer.

Step 4: Protect Your Hair at Night

Wrap your hair in a silk scarf or sleep on a silk pillowcase to protect your hair from friction and breakage while you sleep.

Tips to Promote Hair Growth After Pregnancy

Here are some additional tips to promote hair growth after pregnancy:

  • Massage your scalp regularly to improve blood circulation and stimulate hair growth.
  • Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, as these can cause tension and breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as hair dyes and relaxers, as these can damage your hair and cause breakage.

FAQs

Q: Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

A: No, postpartum hair loss is temporary and will resolve on its own within six to twelve months after delivery.

Q: Can breastfeeding cause hair loss?

A: No, breastfeeding does not cause hair loss. Postpartum hair loss is caused by hormonal changes after delivery.

Q: Can I take hair supplements while breastfeeding?

A: It's best to consult with your doctor before taking any supplements while breastfeeding.

In conclusion, postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition that affects many new moms. While it can be alarming, it's a normal part of the hair growth cycle. By following proper hair care and nutrition tips, you can minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth after pregnancy.
