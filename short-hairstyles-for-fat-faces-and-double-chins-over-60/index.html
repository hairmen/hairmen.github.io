Short Hairstyles For Fat Faces And Double Chins Over 60 Tips And Ideas

Written by Robby Jun 16, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're over 60 and have a round face or a double chin, finding the perfect hairstyle can be a challenge. But don't worry, there are plenty of options that can help you look your best. In this post, we'll share some tips and ideas for short hairstyles that flatter round faces and double chins.

How to Choose the Right Short Hairstyle

Before we dive into the different styles, it's important to understand what makes a good hairstyle for a round face or double chin. Generally, you want to look for styles that add height and volume to the top of your head, while keeping the sides and back shorter. This helps elongate your face and draw attention away from your chin.

When choosing a short hairstyle, it's also important to consider your hair texture and face shape. For example, if you have fine hair, you'll want to avoid styles that are too heavy or layered. And if you have a square jawline, you may want to opt for a style with soft layers or side-swept bangs.

Short Hairstyles for Fat Faces and Double Chins Over 60

1. Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a classic short hairstyle that can work well for round faces and double chins. This style features short layers on top and tapered sides, which help add height to your face and draw attention to your eyes. To make the style more modern, you can add texture with a choppy cut or piecey layers.

2. Bob Cut

The bob cut is another versatile style that can work well for round faces and double chins. This style features a blunt cut that ends at the chin or just above it, which helps elongate your face. To add volume and texture, you can opt for a layered or angled bob, which creates movement and dimension.

3. Shag Cut

The shag cut is a layered cut that features choppy layers and lots of texture. This style works well for round faces and double chins because it adds volume to the top of your head and creates a more angular shape. To make the style more modern, you can add side-swept bangs or highlights to create dimension.

Tips for Maintaining Short Hairstyles

Once you've chosen a short hairstyle, it's important to know how to maintain it. Here are some tips:

  • Visit your stylist regularly to keep your cut looking fresh.
  • Use a volumizing mousse or spray to add volume to your roots.
  • Avoid heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Consider using a dry shampoo to add texture and volume to your roots.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons too frequently, as they can damage your hair.

FAQs

What is the best hairstyle for a round face?

The best hairstyle for a round face is one that adds height and volume to the top of your head, while keeping the sides and back shorter. This helps elongate your face and draw attention away from your chin. Short hairstyles like the pixie cut, bob cut, and shag cut can all work well for round faces.

What is the best hairstyle for a double chin?

The best hairstyle for a double chin is one that draws attention away from your chin and towards your eyes. Short hairstyles like the pixie cut, bob cut, and shag cut can all work well for double chins. You can also consider adding side-swept bangs or layers to create movement and dimension.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Short hair requires a different set of hair care tips than long hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every two to three days to prevent it from looking greasy.
  • Use a lightweight conditioner to keep your hair soft and smooth.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as they can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, instead of a brush.

Conclusion

Short hairstyles can be a great option for women over 60 with round faces and double chins. By choosing the right style and following these tips for maintenance and care, you can look and feel your best. Whether you opt for a pixie cut, bob cut, or shag cut, the key is to find a style that flatters your unique features and complements your personal style.
