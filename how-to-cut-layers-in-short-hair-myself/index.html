How To Cut Layers In Short Hair Myself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 13, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to add some texture and volume to your short hair, cutting layers is a great way to do it. And while it may seem intimidating, with the right tools and techniques, you can actually cut layers in short hair yourself.

Description

In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting layers in short hair step-by-step. We'll also provide tips for achieving the best results, as well as answer some frequently asked questions about hair care.

How to Cut Layers in Short Hair Myself

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step to cutting layers in short hair is to gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is, making it difficult to achieve the desired length.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, use the comb to section off your hair. Start by parting your hair down the middle, then create a section at the crown of your head. Clip the remaining hair out of the way.

Step 4: Cut the Crown Layer

Begin cutting the crown layer by holding the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cutting vertically. Cut the hair to the desired length, keeping in mind that shorter layers will add more volume.

Step 5: Cut the Side Layers

Next, unclip the side section of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut vertically, following the length of the crown layer. Repeat on the other side.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut the crown and side layers, it's time to blend them together. Comb your hair straight down, and use the scissors to trim any uneven pieces until you achieve a seamless look.

Step 7: Check Your Layers

After you've finished cutting, check your layers in the mirror to make sure they're even. If there are any areas that need touching up, go back and make adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Short Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting layers in short hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure accuracy
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from different angles
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven layers
  • Consider consulting a professional stylist if you're unsure about cutting your hair yourself

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair care:

1. How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and the style you're trying to achieve. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

2. How can I prevent split ends?

To prevent split ends, it's important to keep your hair moisturized and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims can also help prevent split ends from forming.

3. What should I do if I don't like my haircut?

If you're unhappy with your haircut, it's best to speak to your stylist and explain your concerns. They may be able to make adjustments or suggest alternative styles that better suit your preferences.

Hair Care

Caring for your hair is important to maintain its health and appearance. Make sure to use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims can also help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking its best.
