How To Cut Face Framing Layers Yourself

Written by Petter Sep 15, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Do you want to refresh your look without spending a fortune at the salon? Cutting face framing layers yourself is a great way to achieve a new look without breaking the bank. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of cutting face framing layers yourself in relaxed English language.

How to

The first step is to gather all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's essential to have good lighting and a clear workspace.

Start by wetting your hair and combing it straight. Part your hair down the middle or on one side, depending on your preference.

Next, take a small section of hair from the front and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and use the scissors to cut it at an angle. Be sure to cut at a slight angle, so the layers blend in with the rest of your hair.

Repeat this process on the other side of your face, making sure to cut the same amount of hair.

It's best to cut a little bit of hair at a time, so you don't accidentally cut too much. You can always go back and cut more if needed.

Step by Step

1. Wet your hair and comb it straight.

2. Part your hair down the middle or on one side.

3. Take a small section of hair from the front and comb it straight down.

4. Hold the hair between your fingers and use the scissors to cut it at an angle.

5. Repeat on the other side of your face, making sure to cut the same amount of hair.

Tips

- Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.

- Cut at a slight angle to blend the layers in with the rest of your hair.

- Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.

- Don't cut too much at once; you can always go back and cut more if needed.

FAQ

Q: How much hair should I cut for face framing layers?

A: It depends on your hair length and thickness. Start with a small amount of hair and cut more if needed.

Q: Can I cut face framing layers on curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut face framing layers on curly hair. Just make sure to cut the hair when it's dry, so you can see the curls' natural shape.

Hair Care

After cutting your face framing layers, it's essential to take care of your hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny. Avoid using heat tools too often and use a heat protectant when you do.

Cutting face framing layers yourself is a great way to switch up your look without spending a lot of money. Follow these steps, and you'll have a fresh new look in no time.
