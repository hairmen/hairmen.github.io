How To Grow Out Short Thick Wavy Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short, thick, and wavy hair, you may find it difficult to grow it out without it becoming a frizzy mess. But don't worry, with the right approach and hair care routine, you can achieve long and luscious locks that you can be proud of. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to grow out short thick wavy hair.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Grow Out Short Thick Wavy Hair

The key to growing out short thick wavy hair is to be patient and consistent. It takes time for hair to grow, so don't expect overnight results. You'll also need to take care of your hair properly to prevent breakage and damage.

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and cause breakage. If you must use them, make sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low. However, it's best to avoid heat styling altogether and let your hair air-dry.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush can help prevent breakage and damage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, gently detangling as you go.

3. Get Regular Trims

While it may seem counterintuitive, getting regular trims can actually help your hair grow faster. Trimming the ends of your hair can prevent split ends and breakage, which can inhibit growth.

4. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote hair growth. Make sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, lean protein, and whole grains.

5. Be Gentle When Styling

When styling your hair, be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging. Use soft hair ties and avoid tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids, which can cause breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start with a Good Haircut

Before you start growing out your hair, get a good haircut from a professional stylist. This will help ensure that your hair looks neat and healthy as it grows out.

2. Be Patient

As mentioned earlier, growing out short thick wavy hair takes time. Be patient and don't expect overnight results. It can take several months to a year or more to achieve the length you want.

3. Use Hair Accessories

As your hair grows, you may experience awkward in-between stages where your hair is too short for certain hairstyles. Use hair accessories like headbands, clips, and bobby pins to keep your hair out of your face and experiment with new styles.

4. Experiment with Different Products

As your hair grows, you may find that the products you used on short hair no longer work. Experiment with different shampoos, conditioners, and styling products to find what works best for your hair type.

Tips for Growing Out Short Thick Wavy Hair

1. Be Consistent with Your Hair Care Routine

Consistency is key when it comes to growing out your hair. Stick to a regular hair care routine and be patient.

2. Protect Your Hair from Damage

Protect your hair from damage by avoiding heat styling, using a wide-tooth comb, and being gentle when styling.

3. Be Patient

As mentioned earlier, growing out short thick wavy hair takes time. Be patient and don't expect overnight results.

FAQs

1. How often should I get my hair trimmed?

You should get a trim every 8-12 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

2. Can I still style my hair while growing it out?

Absolutely! Experiment with different styles and hair accessories to find what works for you.

Hair Care Routine for Short Thick Wavy Hair

1. Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type. Shampoo your hair every 2-3 days and use conditioner every time you wash your hair.

2. Deep Conditioning Treatment

Once a week, use a deep conditioning treatment to nourish and moisturize your hair.

3. Oil Treatment

Once a month, use an oil treatment to nourish and strengthen your hair. Apply the oil to your hair and scalp, leave it on for 30 minutes, and then rinse it out.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid heat styling as much as possible to prevent damage and breakage.

5. Be Gentle When Styling

Be gentle when styling your hair to prevent breakage and damage. Use soft hair ties and avoid tight hairstyles.

In conclusion, growing out short thick wavy hair can be a challenge, but with the right approach and hair care routine, you can achieve the long and luscious locks you've always wanted. Be patient, consistent, and gentle with your hair, and you'll be rocking beautiful waves in no time.
