What s My Hair Type Male Understanding Your Hair Type

Written by Frank Jun 10, 2023 Â· 5 min read

As a male, it can be difficult to determine what your hair type is. There are many different hair types, each with their own unique characteristics and needs. In this article, we'll help you understand what your hair type is and how to care for it properly.

What is Hair Type?

Hair type is determined by the shape and size of the hair follicle. This, in turn, affects the texture, thickness, and curl pattern of the hair. There are four main hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Each of these types can be further categorized based on their subtypes.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

The easiest way to determine your hair type is to wet your hair and examine it in its natural state. Here's how you can determine your hair type:

Straight Hair:

Straight hair has no curl pattern and is generally very smooth and shiny. It tends to be thin and limp, lacking volume and body.

Wavy Hair:

Wavy hair has a slight curl pattern, forming an "S" shape. It can be frizzy and tends to be thicker than straight hair.

Curly Hair:

Curly hair has a tighter curl pattern, forming a spiral shape. It can be very thick and voluminous, prone to frizz and dryness.

Coily Hair:

Coily hair has a very tight curl pattern, forming a zigzag shape. It is the most fragile hair type and requires the most moisture and care.

Hair Type Subtypes

Each of the four hair types has several subtypes, which can further help you determine your hair type. Here are the subtypes for each hair type:

Straight Hair Subtypes:

  • Type 1A - Fine, thin, and very straight hair
  • Type 1B - Medium-thick, straight hair with some volume
  • Type 1C - Thick and straight hair, often with a slight wave

Wavy Hair Subtypes:

  • Type 2A - Loose, beachy waves
  • Type 2B - Defined waves with some frizz and volume
  • Type 2C - Tighter, more defined waves with more frizz and volume

Curly Hair Subtypes:

  • Type 3A - Loose curls with a defined "S" shape
  • Type 3B - Bouncy, springy curls with more volume and frizz
  • Type 3C - Tight, corkscrew curls that are prone to dryness and frizz

Coily Hair Subtypes:

  • Type 4A - Defined coils with a tight "S" shape
  • Type 4B - Z-shaped curls with less definition and more shrinkage
  • Type 4C - Tight, coily curls that are prone to dryness and breakage

How to Care for Your Hair Type

Once you've determined your hair type, it's important to care for it properly. Here are some tips for caring for each hair type:

Straight Hair:

Straight hair tends to get oily easily, so it's important to wash it regularly with a gentle shampoo. Avoid heavy conditioners, as they can weigh down your hair. Use a light leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated without weighing it down.

Wavy Hair:

Wavy hair needs moisture to prevent frizz and maintain its shape. Use a gentle shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.

Curly Hair:

Curly hair needs lots of moisture to keep its shape and prevent frizz. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use your fingers to style your hair when it's wet.

Coily Hair:

Coily hair needs lots of moisture to prevent breakage and maintain its shape. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage. Instead, use your fingers to style your hair when it's wet.

FAQ

How often should I wash my hair?

This depends on your hair type and personal preference. Straight hair can be washed daily, while wavy, curly, and coily hair should be washed every 2-3 days.

Should I use hot or cold water to wash my hair?

Use lukewarm water to wash your hair. Hot water can strip your hair of its natural oils, while cold water can make it harder to wash out shampoo and conditioner.

Can I use the same products on my hair as my girlfriend?

No, men's and women's hair have different needs. Men's hair tends to be thicker and coarser, while women's hair tends to be finer and more delicate. Use products specifically formulated for men's hair.

Hair Care Tips

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet
  • Avoid using hot styling tools, as they can damage your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Drink plenty of water and eat a healthy diet to promote healthy hair growth

Understanding your hair type is the first step in caring for your hair properly. With the right products and techniques, you can keep your hair looking healthy and stylish.
