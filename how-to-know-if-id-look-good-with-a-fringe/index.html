How To Know If You d Look Good With A Fringe

Written by Aprilia Oct 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering getting a fringe but unsure if it will suit you? A fringe can be a great way to change up your look and add some personality to your hairstyle. But it's important to make sure it suits your face shape and hair type before taking the plunge. In this article, we'll give you some tips and tricks to help you determine if a fringe is right for you.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Determine if a Fringe Will Suit You
  2. Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Fringe
  3. Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe
  4. FAQs About Getting a Fringe
  5. Hair Care Tips for Fringe Wearers

How to Determine if a Fringe Will Suit You

Firstly, it's important to understand that there are different types of fringes. There's the classic straight-across fringe, the side-swept fringe, the choppy fringe, and many more. Each type suits different face shapes and hair types.

If you have a round face, a straight-across fringe can help to elongate your face and make it appear more oval-shaped. If you have a long face, a side-swept fringe can help to shorten your face and balance out your features. If you have a heart-shaped face, a choppy fringe can help to soften your forehead and balance out your jawline.

Another factor to consider is your hair type. If you have thick, straight hair, a blunt fringe can work well. If you have fine hair, a wispy, feathered fringe can add some texture and movement. If you have curly hair, a side-swept fringe can help to show off your curls and add some definition to your face.

Ultimately, the best way to determine if a fringe will suit you is to consult with a hairstylist. They can take into account your face shape, hair type, and personal style to recommend a fringe that will work for you.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Fringe

If you've decided that a fringe is right for you, here's a step-by-step guide to getting it:

  1. Consult with a hairstylist to determine the best type of fringe for you.
  2. Choose a length and style that suits your face shape and personal style.
  3. Prepare your hair by washing and drying it. It's important to have clean, dry hair before getting a fringe.
  4. Section off the hair that will become your fringe. Use a comb to create a straight or side-swept parting, depending on your chosen style.
  5. Cut the fringe to your desired length. It's important to take it slow and cut a little at a time, as you can always cut more but can't put hair back.
  6. Style your fringe using a round brush and hairdryer. This will help to create volume and shape.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe

Once you have a fringe, it's important to maintain it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Regularly trim your fringe to keep it at the desired length.
  • Use dry shampoo to keep your fringe looking fresh between washes.
  • Blow-dry your fringe every day to keep it in shape.
  • Avoid touching or playing with your fringe, as this can cause it to become greasy or misshapen.
  • Consider using a hair serum or oil to add shine and reduce frizz.

FAQs About Getting a Fringe

Q: Will a fringe suit me if I wear glasses?

A: Yes, a fringe can look great with glasses. Just make sure to choose a length and style that doesn't interfere with your frames.

Q: How often should I trim my fringe?

A: It's recommended to trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it at the desired length.

Q: Can I still wear my fringe if I tie my hair back?

A: Yes, you can still wear your fringe if you tie your hair back. Just make sure to use bobby pins or a hair tie to keep it in place.

Hair Care Tips for Fringe Wearers

If you have a fringe, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's specifically designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much product on your fringe, as this can weigh it down and make it look greasy.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

Now that you know how to determine if a fringe will suit you, it's time to book a consultation with a hairstylist and try out a new look!
