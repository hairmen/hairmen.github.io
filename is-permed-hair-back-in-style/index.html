Description

Permed hair, also known as a perm, is a hairstyle that involves chemically altering the hair to create curls or waves. This hairstyle was popular in the 80s and 90s but has since fallen out of fashion. However, in recent years, permed hair has made a comeback and is now considered trendy once again.

Many celebrities have been spotted sporting permed hair, which has contributed to its rise in popularity. Permed hair can give your look a retro vibe while still remaining modern and stylish.

How To: Get Permed Hair

If you're considering getting a perm, it's important to find a hairstylist who is experienced in the process. The hairstylist will apply a chemical solution to your hair, which breaks down the bonds that hold your hair in its natural shape. Then, your hair will be wrapped around perm rods, which determine the size and shape of the curls. The perm solution will be left on your hair for a certain amount of time, depending on your hair's texture and the desired result.

Step By Step Guide

1. Find an experienced hairstylist who specializes in perms.

2. Consult with the hairstylist about the desired outcome and what kind of perm will work best for your hair type.

3. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.

4. Section your hair and wrap it around perm rods.

5. Apply the perm solution to your hair and leave it on for the recommended amount of time.

6. Rinse your hair thoroughly and apply a neutralizer to set the curls.

7. Remove the perm rods and style your hair as desired.

Tips for Permed Hair

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to protect your curls.

2. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and disrupt the curl pattern.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushing can cause frizz.

4. Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

5. Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will a perm damage my hair?

A: Perming your hair can cause damage if not done properly, but a skilled hairstylist can minimize the risk.

Q: How long will my perm last?

A: The longevity of your perm depends on your hair's texture and the aftercare. On average, a perm can last between 3-6 months.

Q: Can I color my permed hair?

A: Yes, you can color your permed hair, but it's important to wait at least two weeks after getting a perm to allow your hair to recover.

Hair Care for Permed Hair

Taking care of your permed hair is essential to maintaining the health and longevity of your curls. Here are some tips for hair care:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to protect your curls.

2. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and disrupt the curl pattern.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushing can cause frizz.

4. Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

5. Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Overall, permed hair is back in style and can give your look a fun and trendy twist. Consult with an experienced hairstylist to determine if a perm is right for you, and take care of your curls to keep them looking their best.
