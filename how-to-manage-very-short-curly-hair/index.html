How To Manage Very Short Curly Hair

Written by Petter Oct 31, 2023 · 4 min read

Curly hair can be difficult to manage, especially when it’s very short. But with the right techniques and products, you can style and maintain your curls to look their best. In this article, we’ll go over some tips and tricks for managing very short curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

The first step in managing short curly hair is to find the right haircut. Look for a stylist who specializes in curly hair and ask for a cut that works with your natural curl pattern. A good haircut will make it easier to style your hair and keep it looking neat.

Next, invest in some good hair products. Look for products designed specifically for curly hair, such as curl creams, gels, and mousses. These products will help define your curls and keep them from frizzing.

When styling your hair, start with damp hair and apply a small amount of product. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair and encourage your curls to form. If you have very short hair, you may not need to use a comb or brush at all.

Step by Step

Here’s a step-by-step guide to managing very short curly hair:

  1. Find a stylist who specializes in curly hair and get a good haircut.
  2. Invest in good hair products designed for curly hair.
  3. Start with damp hair and apply a small amount of product.
  4. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair and encourage your curls to form.
  5. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on a low heat setting.
  6. Avoid touching your hair too much as this can cause frizz.
  7. Refresh your curls throughout the day with a spritz of water or leave-in conditioner.
  8. Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  9. Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz while you sleep.
  10. Visit your stylist regularly for trims to keep your hair looking neat.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for managing very short curly hair:

  • Don’t shampoo your hair too often as this can strip it of natural oils. Instead, try co-washing with a conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid using too much product as this can weigh down your curls and make them look greasy.
  • Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair.
  • Consider getting a silk or satin bonnet or scarf to protect your hair while you sleep.
  • If you’re struggling to manage your curly hair, consider consulting with a stylist or curl specialist for advice.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about managing very short curly hair:

How often should I wash my curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and how much product you use. Some people with very dry hair may only need to wash once a week, while others may need to wash every other day. Experiment to find what works best for your hair.

How can I prevent frizz?

Avoid touching your hair too much, use products designed for curly hair, and protect your hair while you sleep with a silk or satin pillowcase or bonnet.

What’s the best way to style short curly hair?

Start with damp hair and use products designed for curly hair. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair and encourage your curls to form. Avoid using a brush or comb as this can break up your curls.

Hair Care

Here are some additional tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it looking neat and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using hair elastics or hair ties as these can cause breakage and damage to your hair. Instead, use a silk or satin scrunchie.

Managing very short curly hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a beautiful, defined curl pattern. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair, and don’t be afraid to consult with a stylist or curl specialist for advice.
