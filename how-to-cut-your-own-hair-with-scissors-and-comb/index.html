How To Cut Your Own Hair With Scissors And Comb A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Sep 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have never done it before. However, with the right tools and technique, it is possible to achieve a great haircut from the comfort of your home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your own hair with scissors and comb, including tips on how to achieve different styles and how to take care of your hair afterwards.

Description

Cutting your own hair can save you time and money, and it can also give you a sense of empowerment and creativity. However, it is important to have realistic expectations and to start with a simple style that you can easily maintain. Make sure to choose the right scissors and comb for your hair type and length, and to cut your hair when it is dry and clean. Take breaks and step back from the mirror to avoid overcutting and unevenness. Finally, be prepared to make mistakes and to learn from them.

How to

To cut your own hair with scissors and comb, you will need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • A wide-toothed comb
  • A smaller comb or brush
  • Clips or hair ties to section your hair
  • A mirror

Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner, and let it air dry or blow dry it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before cutting it, as wet hair tends to stretch and can result in uneven cuts.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using clips or hair ties, divide your hair into sections that you can easily manage. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up. For shorter hair, you may only need two or three sections, while for longer hair, you may need more.

Step 3: Comb your hair

Using a wide-toothed comb, detangle your hair and make sure it is smooth and even. This will help you see the natural fall and shape of your hair.

Step 4: Choose your cutting technique

There are different cutting techniques you can use depending on the style you want to achieve:

  • Point-cutting: This involves cutting into the hair at an angle with the scissors, creating a textured and choppy effect.
  • Straight-cutting: This involves cutting the hair straight across with the scissors, creating a blunt and even effect.
  • Layering: This involves cutting different lengths of hair within each section, creating a voluminous and layered effect.

Choose the technique that best suits your hair type and length, and practice it on a small section of hair before moving on to the rest.

Step 5: Cut your hair

Start cutting your hair from the bottom layer and work your way up. Take small sections of hair and comb them straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut into it at an angle or straight across, depending on your chosen technique. Make sure to cut only a small amount of hair at a time, and to use the comb to guide your cutting and to check for evenness. Repeat this process for each section of hair, using the mirror to check the back and sides.

Step 6: Style your hair

Once you have finished cutting your hair, style it as desired using a smaller comb or brush. You can blow dry, straighten, curl or leave it natural, depending on your preference.

Tips

  • Start with a simple style and work your way up to more complex styles.
  • Take breaks and step back from the mirror to avoid overcutting.
  • Use the right scissors and comb for your hair type and length.
  • Practice on a small section of hair before moving on to the rest.
  • Be patient and don't rush the process.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if you have curly hair. However, curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so make sure to cut it when it is dry and to cut only a small amount of hair at a time. Use the point-cutting technique for a textured and natural look, and avoid straight-cutting as it can create a triangle shape.

Do I need to use hairdressing scissors?

Yes, it is recommended to use sharp haircutting scissors for a clean and precise cut. Avoid using regular scissors or kitchen scissors, as they can damage your hair and create uneven cuts.

How often should I cut my own hair?

It depends on your hair type and length, as well as on the style you want to achieve. Generally, it is recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health. However, if you have short hair or a specific style, you may need to cut it more often.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it is important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner that suit your hair type, and avoid over-washing or over-styling your hair. Trim split ends regularly, and protect your hair from heat and environmental damage by using a heat protectant spray or wearing a hat. Finally, embrace your new haircut and enjoy the feeling of self-sufficiency and creativity!
