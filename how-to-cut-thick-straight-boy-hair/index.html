How To Cut Thick Straight Boy Hair Your Ultimate Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your son's thick straight hair getting in the way of his face? Do you want to learn how to cut his hair yourself? Look no further! We have created a step-by-step guide on how to cut thick straight boy hair. In this article, we will cover everything from how to prepare for the haircut, the actual cutting process, to hair care tips after the cut. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Preparing for the Haircut
  • Tools You'll Need
  • The Cutting Process
  • Tips for a Successful Haircut
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After the Cut

Preparing for the Haircut

Before you start cutting your son's hair, it's essential to prepare for the haircut to ensure a successful outcome. Here are a few things to consider:

1. Choose the Right Style

Before cutting your son's hair, decide on the style you want to achieve. Look for inspiration online, in magazines, or consult with a hairstylist. Once you have a style in mind, you can start preparing for the haircut.

2. Wash and Dry the Hair

Washing your son's hair before the haircut will make the hair more manageable and easier to cut. Make sure to dry the hair thoroughly before beginning the cutting process.

3. Find the Right Spot

Choose a spot where you and your son will be comfortable during the haircut. Make sure the area is well-lit and has enough space for you to move around.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your son's hair, you'll need to gather the necessary tools. Here's a list of tools you'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Hair spray
  • Cape or towel

The Cutting Process

Now that you have prepared for the haircut and gathered the necessary tools, it's time to start cutting your son's hair. Follow these steps:

1. Start with the Clippers

Use the clippers to trim the hair on the sides and back of the head. Choose the right guard size for the length you want to achieve. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up to the crown of the head.

2. Blend the Hair

Use the clippers without a guard to blend the hair on the sides and back with the hair on top of the head. Make sure to blend the hair evenly to avoid any harsh lines.

3. Cut the Top of the Hair

Use scissors to cut the hair on top of the head. Start at the front and work your way to the back, cutting small sections at a time. Use the comb to pull the hair up and cut it to the desired length.

4. Cut the Bangs

Use scissors to cut your son's bangs. Comb the hair forward and cut it to the desired length.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

To ensure a successful haircut, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with longer guards and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Take breaks if your son gets restless or uncomfortable during the haircut.
  • Use hair clips to keep the hair out of the way while cutting.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time to ensure an even cut.

FAQ

1. How often should I cut my son's hair?

It's recommended to cut your son's hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the desired length and style.

2. Should I cut wet or dry hair?

It's easier to cut wet hair, but if your son's hair is too thick, you can cut it dry. Just make sure to dampen the hair with water before cutting.

Hair Care After the Cut

After the haircut, it's essential to take care of your son's hair to maintain its health and style. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to wash the hair.
  • Comb the hair regularly to avoid tangles and knots.
  • Trim the hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the style.
  • Use hair products, such as gel or pomade, to style the hair.

Now that you know how to cut thick straight boy hair, you can save time and money by doing it yourself. Just remember to prepare for the haircut, gather the necessary tools, and follow the cutting process step-by-step. With a little practice, you'll become a pro at cutting your son's hair.
