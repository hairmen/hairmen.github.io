How To Do An Undercut Haircut Yourself

Written by Frank Aug 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a new haircut that is trendy and low-maintenance? Look no further than the undercut! This style has been popular for several years now and shows no signs of slowing down. The best part? You can easily do an undercut haircut yourself at home with a few simple tools and techniques. Keep reading to learn how.

Table of Contents

  • How to Do an Undercut Haircut Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Successful Undercut
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Your Undercut

Step-by-Step Guide

Before you start, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Spray bottle with water

Now, here are the steps to do an undercut haircut yourself:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section off the hair on the top of your head with a clip or hair tie. The length of this section is up to you, but it should be at least 2 inches long to have enough hair to play with.
  3. Decide how short you want your undercut to be. Use the clippers to buzz the hair on the sides and back of your head to the desired length. Start with a longer guard and work your way shorter if you're unsure how short you want to go.
  4. Use the scissors to blend the hair on the sides and back with the hair on top. You want a gradual, seamless transition between the two lengths. Cut small sections at a time and check your work frequently.
  5. Release the top section of your hair and comb it straight up. Use the clippers to trim the hair on the sides and back of your head to the same length as the top section. Be careful not to cut into the top section of hair.
  6. Blend the hair on the sides and back with the hair on top using the scissors. Again, work in small sections and check your work frequently.
  7. Use the clippers to create a clean, straight line along the bottom edge of the undercut. This will give it a polished look.
  8. Style your hair as desired and admire your new undercut!

Tips for a Successful Undercut

Here are a few tips to keep in mind while doing your undercut:

  • Start with a longer guard on your clippers and work your way shorter. You can always take more off, but you can't put it back on.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep your hair damp while cutting. This will make it easier to work with and give you a more accurate cut.
  • Check your work frequently. It's better to catch a mistake early on than to have to start over.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, start with a small section and see how it turns out. You can always go to a professional if you don't like the result.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does an undercut haircut last?

An undercut haircut can last anywhere from 4-8 weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows and how short you cut it.

Can I do an undercut on curly hair?

Yes, you can do an undercut on curly hair. Just be sure to cut the hair when it's dry and in its natural state so you can see how it will fall.

What if I mess up my undercut?

If you make a mistake while cutting your undercut, it's best to stop and assess the damage. Depending on how severe the mistake is, you may be able to fix it yourself or you may need to go to a professional stylist to have it fixed.

Hair Care Tips for Your Undercut

Now that you have an undercut, here are some tips for keeping it looking great:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a styling product to help hold the hair in place and create the style you want.
  • Visit a professional stylist every few weeks to have the undercut touched up and keep it looking fresh.
  • Consider using a hair oil or serum to keep the hair healthy and prevent breakage.

Now that you know how to do an undercut haircut yourself, it's time to give it a try! With a little practice and patience, you can have a stylish new haircut without ever leaving home.
