How To Taper A Beard To Make Your Face Look Thinner

Are you tired of having a round face? Do you want to achieve a more chiseled and defined look? One of the easiest ways to achieve this is by tapering your beard. Not only does tapering your beard make your face look thinner, but it also adds definition and style to your overall appearance. In this article, we will discuss the steps on how to taper a beard to make your face look thinner.

How to Taper a Beard

Tapering a beard involves gradually trimming the hair towards the jawline, creating a more defined and sharp look. Here are the basic steps on how to taper a beard:

Step 1: Define Your Neckline

Before tapering your beard, define your neckline. Use a trimmer or razor to create a straight line underneath your chin. This will give you a clear boundary to work on while trimming your beard.

Step 2: Trim Your Beard

Start trimming your beard using a trimmer with a guard. Begin at the top of your beard and work downwards, gradually decreasing the guard length as you move towards your jawline. This will create a tapered effect on your beard.

Step 3: Define Your Jawline

Once you have trimmed your beard, use a razor or trimmer without a guard to define your jawline. Create a sharp edge by trimming the hair around your jawline in a straight line. This will create a more defined and chiseled look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to taper a beard:

Step 1: Clean Your Beard

Wash your beard thoroughly with shampoo and conditioner. This will help soften the hair and make it easier to trim.

Step 2: Comb Your Beard

Comb your beard in the direction of hair growth. This will help you identify any knots or tangles that need to be trimmed.

Step 3: Trim the Top of Your Beard

Using a trimmer with a guard, trim the top of your beard to your desired length. Work in a downward motion, gradually decreasing the guard length as you move towards your jawline.

Step 4: Trim the Sides of Your Beard

Trim the sides of your beard using the same technique as the top. Work downwards, gradually decreasing the guard length as you move towards your jawline.

Step 5: Define Your Jawline

Use a trimmer or razor without a guard to define your jawline. Create a sharp edge by trimming the hair around your jawline in a straight line.

Tips for Tapering Your Beard

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect tapered beard:

  • Use a beard oil or balm to keep your beard soft and moisturized.
  • Trim your beard in a well-lit area to ensure accuracy.
  • Start with a longer guard and work downwards to prevent accidentally trimming too much hair.
  • Trim your beard regularly to maintain the tapered look.

FAQs

How often should I trim my beard?

It is recommended to trim your beard every 2-4 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

Can I taper my beard if I have a round face?

A tapered beard can help create a more defined and chiseled look, regardless of your face shape.

Can I taper my beard myself or should I go to a barber?

You can taper your beard yourself with the right tools and technique. However, if you are unsure or want a more professional look, it is recommended to go to a barber.

Hair Care for Your Beard

Proper hair care is essential in maintaining a healthy and handsome beard. Here are some tips for taking care of your beard:

  • Wash your beard regularly with shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a beard oil or balm to keep your beard soft and moisturized.
  • Comb your beard regularly to prevent tangles and knots.
  • Trim your beard regularly to maintain its shape and style.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a tapered beard that makes your face look thinner and more defined. Remember to take care of your beard and maintain its style to achieve the best results.
