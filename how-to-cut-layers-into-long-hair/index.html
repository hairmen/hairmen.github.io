How To Cut Layers Into Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Long hair can be beautiful, but sometimes it can feel heavy and lifeless. Adding layers can give your hair more movement, volume, and texture. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of cutting layers into long hair.

How to Cut Layers into Long Hair

Cutting layers into long hair can be intimidating, especially if you have never done it before. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a layered look that flatters your face shape and enhances your natural features.

Tools You Will Need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair.

Wash and dry your hair before cutting it. This will make it easier to see the natural fall of your hair and ensure a more accurate cut.

Step 2: Section your hair.

Use clips to divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then divide it into three sections: left, right, and back.

Step 3: Begin cutting.

Start with the back section and work your way forward. Hold a small section of hair between your fingers and cut into it at an angle, starting at the ends and working your way up towards the roots. Repeat this process on each section of hair, working from back to front and from bottom to top.

Step 4: Blend the layers.

Once you have cut all the sections, use your scissors to blend the layers together. Take small sections of hair and cut into them at a slight angle to create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 5: Check for symmetry.

Take a step back and check for symmetry. Make sure both sides of your hair are even and that the layers are blended seamlessly.

Tips for Cutting Layers into Long Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting layers into long hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a spray bottle to mist your hair with water if it begins to dry out while you are cutting.
  • Be patient and take your time. It is better to cut too little hair than too much.
  • Consider your face shape when deciding where to place the layers.
  • Get a friend to help you if you are nervous or unsure about cutting your hair.

FAQs

Q: How many layers should I cut into my long hair?

A: The number of layers you should cut into your long hair depends on your personal preference and face shape. Generally, people with round or square faces look best with longer layers, while people with oval or heart-shaped faces can pull off shorter layers.

Q: Can I cut layers into my own hair?

A: Yes, you can cut layers into your own hair, but it can be difficult to achieve a professional-looking result. If you are nervous or unsure, it may be best to visit a professional hair stylist.

Q: How often should I cut layers into my long hair?

A: How often you cut layers into your long hair depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much you want to maintain the look of your layers. Generally, getting a trim every 6-8 weeks is recommended.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting layers into your long hair, it is important to take care of your hair to ensure it stays healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for hair care after cutting layers:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and minimize breakage.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and keep it shiny.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

Cutting layers into long hair can be a fun and creative way to update your look. With the right tools, techniques, and care, you can achieve a beautiful layered hairstyle that flatters your face shape and enhances your natural beauty.
