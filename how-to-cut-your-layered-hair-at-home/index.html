How To Cut Your Layered Hair At Home A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to save money on haircuts or simply prefer the convenience of cutting your hair at home, you might be wondering if it's possible to cut your own layered hair. The good news is, with the right tools and techniques, cutting your layered hair at home can be easy and effective. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a layered haircut that looks salon-worthy.

How To

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a handheld mirror to check the back of your head.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. This will help you achieve an even cut and ensure that your hair is free of any tangles or knots.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, separate your hair into sections based on how many layers you want. For a basic layered haircut, you'll need two to three sections. Use hair clips to secure each section in place.

Step 4: Trim the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, use your comb to gather a small section of hair and hold it straight up. Use your scissors to trim the ends at an angle, starting with the outermost sections and working your way in. Repeat this process with each section of hair on the bottom layer.

Step 5: Move to the Next Layer

Once you've finished trimming the bottom layer, release the middle layer and repeat the process. Make sure to blend the ends of each layer so that they seamlessly transition into each other.

Step 6: Finish with the Top Layer

Finally, release the top layer and repeat the same process. Make sure to trim the ends at an angle to achieve a layered look. Once you've finished trimming, use your comb to blend the layers together and ensure that there are no harsh lines.

Tips

- Start with small sections of hair and trim gradually to avoid cutting off too much at once.

- Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head as you trim.

- If you're unsure about how much to cut, start with a small amount and work your way up.

- Always use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair if it's wet?

Yes, you can cut your hair if it's wet. However, keep in mind that wet hair is more prone to breakage, so be gentle when trimming.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every six to eight weeks to maintain healthy ends.

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if it's curly. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so be conservative when trimming to avoid cutting off too much.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and appearance. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner that's suited for your hair type, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Consider using a hair mask once a week to help nourish and moisturize your hair.

Cutting your own layered hair at home can be a cost-effective and convenient solution to maintaining your hairstyle. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy look in the comfort of your own home.
