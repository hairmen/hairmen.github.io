Will Bleached Damaged Hair Grow Back

Written by Bardi Nov 27, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're someone who loves to experiment with hair color, you may have experienced the damage that comes with bleaching your hair. Bleach can be harsh on hair, resulting in breakage, split ends, and overall hair damage. This can leave you wondering, will bleached damaged hair grow back? The short answer is yes, but there are steps you can take to help your hair grow back healthier and stronger.

Description

Bleaching your hair involves using chemicals to strip away the natural color. This process can damage the hair cuticle, which is the outer layer of the hair. When the cuticle is damaged, it can lead to hair breakage, split ends, and overall hair damage. While bleached damaged hair can grow back, it may take time and effort to restore it to its former health.

How to Help Your Hair Grow Back

1. Take a Break from Bleaching

If you want to help your hair grow back healthier, it's important to take a break from bleaching. This means avoiding further chemical treatments, such as coloring or perming, until your hair has had a chance to recover. This will give your hair time to strengthen and repair itself.

2. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help strengthen and repair damaged hair. These treatments work by penetrating the hair shaft and rebuilding the proteins that make up the hair. Look for protein treatments that contain ingredients like keratin or collagen, which can help strengthen the hair.

3. Moisturize Your Hair

Moisturizing your hair is essential for keeping it healthy and strong. When hair is bleached, it can become dry and brittle, making it more prone to breakage. To help your hair grow back, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a hair mask or oil treatment to add extra hydration.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

While it may seem counterintuitive, trimming your hair regularly can actually help it grow back faster. This is because split ends can cause hair to break off, which can slow down the growth process. By trimming your hair regularly, you can remove split ends and help your hair grow back stronger.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to help your bleached damaged hair grow back:

Step 1: Stop Bleaching Your Hair

The first step to restoring your hair is to stop bleaching it. This means avoiding further chemical treatments, such as coloring or perming, until your hair has had a chance to recover.

Step 2: Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help strengthen and repair damaged hair. Look for treatments that contain ingredients like keratin or collagen, which can help rebuild the proteins in your hair.

Step 3: Moisturize Your Hair

Moisturizing your hair is essential for keeping it healthy and strong. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a hair mask or oil treatment to add extra hydration.

Step 4: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can help remove split ends and promote healthy growth. Aim to trim your hair every six to eight weeks.

Tips for Healthy Hair

Here are some additional tips for keeping your hair healthy:

1. Use Heat Protectant

When using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling wands, always use a heat protectant spray to help prevent damage.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails, can pull on the hair and cause damage. Avoid wearing these styles too often, and never sleep with your hair tied back.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to eat foods that are rich in vitamins and minerals, such as leafy greens, nuts, and eggs.

FAQ

Q: Can bleached hair grow back?

A: Yes, bleached hair can grow back. However, it may take time and effort to restore it to its former health.

Q: How long does it take for bleached hair to grow back?

A: The rate at which hair grows varies from person to person. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month. However, if your hair is damaged, it may take longer to grow back.

Q: Can I dye my hair after bleaching it?

A: It's best to avoid further chemical treatments, such as coloring or perming, until your hair has had a chance to recover. If you must color your hair, wait at least four weeks after bleaching it.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential for keeping it healthy and strong. If you've bleached your hair and are experiencing damage, take a break from further chemical treatments and focus on restoring your hair's health. Use protein treatments, moisturize your hair, trim it regularly, and follow these tips for healthy hair. With time and effort, your bleached damaged hair can grow back healthier and stronger than ever.
