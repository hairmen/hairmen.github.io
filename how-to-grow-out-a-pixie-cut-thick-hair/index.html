How to Grow Out a Pixie Cut Thick Hair

Growing out a pixie cut with thick hair can seem daunting, but it is possible. Here are the things you need to do:

1. Be Patient

The first thing you need to do is to be patient. Growing out your hair will take time, and you need to be prepared for this. You may experience awkward hair stages, but don't worry, this is normal. Just keep in mind that the end result will be worth it.

2. Get Regular Trims

While growing out your hair, you still need to get regular trims to keep your hair healthy. Split ends and damaged hair can slow down the growth process, so make sure to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

3. Use Hair Growth Products

Invest in hair growth products that will help promote hair growth. Look for products that contain biotin, keratin, and other vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth.

4. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Include foods that are rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals such as salmon, eggs, spinach, and nuts.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to grow out your pixie cut thick hair:

Step 1: Determine Your Goal Length

Before you start growing out your hair, determine your goal length. This will help you stay motivated and focused on your end goal.

Step 2: Get Regular Trims

As mentioned earlier, regular trims are essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure to get a trim every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends and damaged hair.

Step 3: Use Hair Growth Products

Invest in hair growth products that will help promote hair growth. Look for products that contain biotin, keratin, and other vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth.

Step 4: Be Gentle with Your Hair

Be gentle with your hair. Avoid using heat styling tools frequently and opt for air-drying your hair instead. Use a wide-toothed comb when detangling your hair to prevent breakage.

Step 5: Experiment with Different Hairstyles

Experiment with different hairstyles as you grow out your hair. This will help you avoid the awkward hair stages and keep you motivated.

Tips for Growing Out a Pixie Cut Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips that will help you grow out your pixie cut thick hair:

1. Embrace Hair Accessories

Embrace hair accessories such as headbands, hair clips, and scarves. These accessories will help you style your hair during the awkward hair stages.

2. Change Your Parting

Changing your parting can make a significant difference in how your hair looks. Try a side part or a middle part to switch up your look.

3. Consider Hair Extensions

If you want to speed up the process, consider getting hair extensions. Hair extensions can add length and volume to your hair, making it easier to style.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to grow out a pixie cut?

A: It can take up to a year or more to grow out a pixie cut.

Q: Can I still color my hair while growing it out?

A: Yes, you can still color your hair while growing it out. Just make sure to use hair color products that are gentle on your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair every other day or every two days. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can slow down the growth process.

Hair Care for Growing Out Pixie Cut Thick Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep in mind while growing out your pixie cut thick hair:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

2. Apply Hair Masks

Apply hair masks once a week to nourish your hair and promote healthy hair growth.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

In conclusion, growing out a pixie cut thick hair may seem challenging, but with patience, regular trims, and proper hair care, it is possible. Follow the steps, tips, and hair care mentioned above, and you'll have beautiful, long locks in no time!
