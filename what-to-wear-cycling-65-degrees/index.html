What To Wear Cycling 65 Degrees A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Jul 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you planning to go on a cycling adventure in 65-degree weather? If yes, then you need to know what to wear to stay comfortable and safe. In this guide, we will discuss what to wear cycling 65 degrees, including the best clothes, shoes, and accessories you need to have on your next ride.

How to Dress for Cycling in 65 Degrees?

When cycling in 65-degree weather, you want to wear clothes that will keep you comfortable and dry. You don't want to wear too many clothes that will make you feel hot and sweaty, nor do you want to wear clothes that are too thin and won't protect you from the wind. Here is what you need to wear:

Clothing

The best clothing for cycling in 65-degree weather is a lightweight, breathable, and moisture-wicking fabric. Some of the items you can wear include:

  • A short-sleeved cycling jersey
  • Cycling shorts with a chamois pad
  • Arm warmers
  • A lightweight windbreaker jacket

These clothes will keep you cool and dry while providing enough protection from the wind. You can also wear a base layer underneath your jersey for added warmth.

Shoes

The best shoes for cycling in 65-degree weather are cycling shoes with breathable mesh panels. These shoes will keep your feet cool and dry while providing enough support for your feet. You can wear socks that are made of moisture-wicking fabric to keep your feet dry.

Accessories

When cycling in 65-degree weather, you need to protect your head and eyes from the sun and wind. Here are some accessories you can wear:

  • A cycling cap or headband to protect your head from the sun
  • Cycling sunglasses to protect your eyes from the sun and wind
  • Gloves to protect your hands from the wind and provide a better grip on the handlebars

Step-by-Step Guide on What to Wear Cycling 65 Degrees

Here is a step-by-step guide on what to wear cycling 65 degrees:

  1. Start with a short-sleeved cycling jersey made of breathable and moisture-wicking fabric.
  2. Wear cycling shorts with a chamois pad to keep your bottom comfortable while cycling.
  3. Put on arm warmers to keep your arms warm in the morning or when the wind picks up.
  4. Wear a lightweight windbreaker jacket to protect you from the wind and rain.
  5. Put on cycling shoes with breathable mesh panels to keep your feet cool and dry.
  6. Wear socks made of moisture-wicking fabric to keep your feet dry.
  7. Wear a cycling cap or headband to protect your head from the sun.
  8. Put on cycling sunglasses to protect your eyes from the sun and wind.
  9. Wear gloves to protect your hands from the wind and provide a better grip on the handlebars.
  10. Make sure to apply sunscreen to any exposed skin to protect yourself from the sun.

Tips for Cycling in 65 Degrees

Here are some tips for cycling in 65-degree weather:

  • Check the weather forecast before heading out to make sure you're dressed appropriately.
  • Start your ride with a warm-up to get your muscles ready for the ride.
  • Bring a water bottle to stay hydrated throughout the ride.
  • Wear clothes that are visible to motorists, especially if you're riding on the road.
  • Take breaks if you feel tired or overheated.

FAQs

What should I wear for cycling in 65-degree weather?

You should wear a short-sleeved cycling jersey, cycling shorts with a chamois pad, arm warmers, a lightweight windbreaker jacket, cycling shoes with breathable mesh panels, socks made of moisture-wicking fabric, a cycling cap or headband, cycling sunglasses, and gloves.

Do I need to wear a helmet when cycling in 65-degree weather?

Yes, it's always recommended to wear a helmet when cycling, regardless of the weather.

Hair Care for Cycling in 65 Degrees

When cycling in 65-degree weather, your hair can get sweaty and messy. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Tie your hair in a ponytail or bun to prevent it from getting tangled in the wind.
  • Wear a headband or cycling cap to keep your hair away from your face.
  • Bring a hairbrush or comb to fix your hair after the ride.

Now that you know what to wear cycling 65 degrees, you can enjoy your ride comfortably and safely. Remember to always check the weather forecast, wear visible clothes, and stay hydrated throughout the ride.
