How To Put Short Hair Up In A High Ponytail

Written by Daniel Aug 24, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have short hair, you might think that a high ponytail is out of reach. But fear not! With a few simple tricks, you can achieve the perfect high ponytail no matter how short your hair is. In this article, we’ll walk you through the steps to create a high ponytail on short hair. Follow along and get ready to rock this classic hairstyle.

Description

A high ponytail is a classic hairstyle that can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. This style is perfect for short hair because it elongates the neck and draws attention to the face. Plus, it’s super easy to create and can be done in just a few minutes. Whether you’re heading to work or out for a night on the town, a high ponytail is a versatile hairstyle that will always look great.

How to

Before we dive into the steps, it’s important to note that there are a few things you’ll need to create a high ponytail on short hair. These include:

  • Elastic hair ties
  • Bobby pins
  • Hairspray

Now that you have your tools ready, here’s how to put short hair up in a high ponytail:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before attempting to create a high ponytail. If your hair is too oily or damp, it won’t hold its shape and may slip out of the ponytail.

Step 2: Brush your hair

Use a brush or comb to smooth out any tangles or knots in your hair. Make sure your hair is completely detangled before moving on to the next step.

Step 3: Gather your hair at the crown of your head

Using your fingers, gather all of your hair at the crown of your head. If you have bangs or shorter pieces in the front, leave them out of the ponytail for now.

Step 4: Secure your hair with an elastic hair tie

Wrap an elastic hair tie around your hair at the crown of your head. Make sure the hair tie is tight enough to hold your hair in place, but not so tight that it’s uncomfortable.

Step 5: Tease your ponytail

Using a comb or brush, tease the hair in your ponytail to add volume and texture. This will give your ponytail a more dramatic look and help it stay in place.

Step 6: Wrap a piece of hair around the hair tie

Take a small section of hair from the underside of your ponytail and wrap it around the elastic hair tie. Use a bobby pin to secure the end of the hair to the underside of the ponytail. This will hide the hair tie and give your ponytail a more polished look.

Step 7: Add hairspray

Spray a light mist of hairspray over your ponytail to help it stay in place throughout the day or night.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect high ponytail:

  • Use a brush or comb to smooth out any bumps or lumps in your hair before securing it with the hair tie.
  • If you have shorter pieces of hair in the front, leave them out of the ponytail and style them separately.
  • Tease your hair in the ponytail to add volume and texture.
  • Use bobby pins to secure any loose pieces of hair or to create a more polished look.
  • Add hairspray to help your ponytail stay in place.

FAQ

Can I create a high ponytail on short hair?

Yes, you can! Follow the steps above to create a high ponytail on short hair.

Do I need any special tools to create a high ponytail on short hair?

No, you don’t. All you need is an elastic hair tie, bobby pins, and hairspray.

Will a high ponytail damage my short hair?

No, as long as you’re not pulling your hair too tight, a high ponytail shouldn’t damage your short hair.

Hair care

To keep your short hair healthy and strong, make sure to wash and condition it regularly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type. Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and flat irons too often, as they can damage your hair over time. If you do use hot tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage. Lastly, get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and to prevent split ends.
