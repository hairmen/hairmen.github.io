Is Asian Hair Difficult To Cut

Written by Aprilia Aug 17, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you are a hairstylist or just someone who loves to experiment with haircuts, you might have heard that Asian hair is difficult to cut. But is it really true? In this blog post, we will explore this topic and give you tips on how to cut Asian hair.

Description

Asian hair is known for its thickness, straightness, and resilience. It is often said that Asian hair is difficult to cut because it is so thick and straight, making it hard to create layers and texture. However, this is not entirely true. With the right tools and techniques, cutting Asian hair can be just as easy as cutting any other hair type.

How to

Before we dive into the tips on how to cut Asian hair, let's talk about the tools you'll need. It's important to have sharp scissors, thinning shears, and a comb. Once you have these tools ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

It's important to start with clean and dry hair. This will make it easier to see the natural texture and thickness of the hair.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Use the comb to section the hair into manageable sections. This will help you focus on one section at a time and prevent you from cutting too much hair at once.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Use the scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. If you want to create layers or texture, use the thinning shears to thin out the hair.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Once you have cut the hair, use the scissors to blend the hair together. This will create a seamless look and prevent any choppy lines.

Step 5: Finish the Look

Style the hair as desired using a blow-dryer, straightener, or curling iron.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting Asian hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent any damage to the hair.
  • Cut the hair when it is dry to see the natural texture and thickness.
  • Section the hair into manageable sections to prevent cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use thinning shears to create layers and texture.
  • Blend the hair together to create a seamless look.

FAQ

Q: Is it true that Asian hair is difficult to cut?

A: While Asian hair is known for its thickness and straightness, it can be cut with the right tools and techniques.

Q: Do I need to use thinning shears when cutting Asian hair?

A: It's not necessary to use thinning shears, but they can help create layers and texture in the hair.

Hair Care

To keep Asian hair looking healthy and shiny, it's important to use the right products. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type, and use a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant spray when you do.

Conclusion

Cutting Asian hair doesn't have to be difficult. With the right tools and techniques, you can create a beautiful haircut that your client will love. Remember to take your time, section the hair, and use the right scissors and thinning shears. And don't forget to finish the look with a blow-dryer, straightener, or curling iron.
