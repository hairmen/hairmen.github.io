How To Turn Afro Into Freeform Dreads

Written by Luffy Aug 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and try something new, freeform dreads might be the perfect choice for you. This style is inspired by the natural look of dreadlocks, but without the structured and uniform appearance. Instead, freeform dreads are all about embracing the unique texture and shape of your hair to create a one-of-a-kind look.

Description

Freeform dreads are a popular hairstyle among people with afro-textured hair. This style is achieved by allowing the hair to naturally lock together and form dreadlocks without the use of any products or techniques. The result is a unique and individualized look that celebrates the natural texture and shape of your hair.

How to

If you're interested in turning your afro into freeform dreads, there are a few steps you can follow to get started:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before you begin the process of creating freeform dreads, it's important to start with clean hair. This will ensure that your hair is free of any product buildup or debris that could interfere with the locking process. Use a clarifying shampoo to thoroughly cleanse your hair, and avoid using any conditioner or styling products.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is clean and dry, it's time to begin sectioning your hair. Use a comb to create small sections of hair, and use a rubber band or hair tie to secure each section. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreads you want to create.

Step 3: Let Your Hair Lock Together

With your hair sectioned, the next step is to let your hair naturally lock together to form dreadlocks. This process can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months, depending on your hair texture and the size of your sections. During this time, avoid using any products or tools that could interfere with the locking process.

Step 4: Maintenance

Once your hair has locked together to form freeform dreads, it's important to maintain them regularly. This includes washing your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo, and using a light oil or moisturizer to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when turning your afro into freeform dreads:

  • Be patient â€“ the locking process can take time
  • Avoid using any products or tools that could interfere with the locking process
  • Maintain your dreads regularly to keep them healthy and looking their best
  • Experiment with different sizes and shapes of sections to create a unique look

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about turning afro into freeform dreads:

Q: Can I speed up the locking process?

A: No, the locking process is a natural process that cannot be rushed. Be patient and let your hair do its thing.

Q: Can I use products on my dreads?

A: It's best to avoid using products on your dreads, as this can interfere with the locking process and potentially damage your hair.

Q: Can I undo my dreads?

A: Yes, you can undo your dreads by combing them out. However, this process can be time-consuming and may cause some damage to your hair.

Hair Care

Maintaining healthy hair is essential when it comes to turning afro into freeform dreads. Here are a few hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Use a light oil or moisturizer to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can achieve healthy and beautiful freeform dreads that showcase the natural texture and shape of your hair.
