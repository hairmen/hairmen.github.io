How To Fix Medium Length Layered Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Aug 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Medium length hair is versatile and can be styled in many different ways. Layered hair, in particular, can add volume and dimension to your hair, but it can also be tricky to style. Here are some tips and tricks on how to fix medium length layered hair.

Description

Medium length layered hair is hair that falls between the chin and shoulders and has layers cut into it to add volume and movement. Layered hair is a popular choice for those who want to add volume and texture to their hair without sacrificing length. However, styling layered hair can be tricky, as the layers may not always sit the way you want them to.

How to Fix Medium Length Layered Hair

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Before you begin styling your medium length layered hair, make sure it is clean and dry. You can wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, then blow dry it using a diffuser attachment to enhance your natural waves.

Step 2: Use a heat protectant

If you're using heat styling tools to fix your medium length layered hair, such as a curling iron or straightener, make sure to use a heat protectant spray or cream first. This will help protect your hair from heat damage and keep it looking healthy.

Step 3: Choose your styling tool

Depending on the look you want to achieve, you can use different styling tools to fix your medium length layered hair. For loose waves, use a curling wand or iron. For straight hair, use a straightening iron. For a messy, textured look, use a texturizing spray and scrunch your hair with your hands.

Step 4: Section your hair

Before you start styling your hair, it's a good idea to section it off into smaller sections. This will make it easier to style and ensure that all of your hair is evenly styled.

Step 5: Start styling

Once you've sectioned your hair, you can begin styling it. Take small sections of hair and curl or straighten them as desired. For a more natural look, alternate the direction of your curls. If you're using a texturizing spray, apply it to your hair and scrunch it with your hands to create a messy, textured look.

Tips for Fixing Medium Length Layered Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you fix your medium length layered hair:

  • Use a hair oil or serum to add shine and smoothness to your hair.
  • Invest in quality heat styling tools that won't damage your hair.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles, such as braids or updos, to switch up your look.
  • Use a volumizing mousse or spray at the roots of your hair to add volume and lift.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my medium length layered hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair or work out frequently, you may need to wash your hair more often. However, if you have dry hair or don't sweat much, you can wash your hair every other day or every few days.

Q: Can I cut my own layers into my medium length hair?

A: It's not recommended to cut your own layers into your hair, as it can be difficult to achieve an even, balanced look. It's best to leave layering to a professional stylist.

Hair Care for Medium Length Layered Hair

To keep your medium length layered hair looking its best, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and always use a heat protectant.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.

With these tips and tricks, you can easily fix and style your medium length layered hair to achieve a variety of looks. Remember to take care of your hair properly to keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
