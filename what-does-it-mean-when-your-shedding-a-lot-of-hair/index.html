What Does It Mean When You re Shedding A Lot Of Hair

Written by Luffy Aug 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Shedding hair is a natural process that happens to everyone. However, when you start noticing more hair than usual falling out, it can be a cause for concern. Hair shedding can be caused by various factors, including genetics, stress, hormonal changes, and medical conditions. In this article, we'll discuss what hair shedding means, what causes it, and how you can manage it.

Description

Hair shedding is a normal part of the hair growth cycle. It's estimated that we lose around 50 to 100 hairs per day. When hair falls out, it's replaced by a new hair follicle. However, when you start shedding more hair than usual, it's known as excessive hair shedding or telogen effluvium. This type of hair loss is temporary and can be triggered by stress, changes in hormones, or nutritional deficiencies.

How to Tell If You're Shedding Too Much Hair

If you're shedding more hair than usual, you may start to notice thinning hair, bald spots, or excessive shedding in the shower or on your brush. You may also notice a change in the texture of your hair or an increase in hair breakage. It's important to keep track of how much hair you're shedding to determine if it's excessive or normal.

Step by Step Guide: What to Do If You're Shedding Too Much Hair

1. Determine the Cause

The first step in managing excessive hair shedding is to determine the cause. If you're experiencing stress or hormonal changes, it's important to address these underlying issues. If you suspect a medical condition is causing your hair loss, consult with your doctor. They may recommend blood tests or refer you to a dermatologist.

2. Practice Good Hair Care Habits

Good hair care habits can help reduce hair shedding. Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle wet hair, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-washing your hair.

3. Improve Your Diet

Nutritional deficiencies can cause hair shedding. Eating a well-balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals can help improve hair health. You may also consider taking supplements to support hair growth, such as biotin, iron, or vitamin D.

4. Consider Hair Loss Treatments

If you're experiencing significant hair loss, there are various hair loss treatments available. These include topical treatments, such as minoxidil, or oral medications, such as finasteride. Hair transplant surgery is also an option for some people.

Tips for Managing Hair Shedding

Here are some additional tips for managing hair shedding:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Avoid wearing tight hats or headbands
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Avoid brushing or combing wet hair
  • Avoid using hair extensions or weaves

FAQ

Q: Is it normal to shed hair every day?

A: Yes, it's normal to shed around 50 to 100 hairs per day.

Q: Can stress cause hair shedding?

A: Yes, stress can trigger telogen effluvium, a type of hair loss that causes excessive shedding.

Q: Can nutritional deficiencies cause hair shedding?

A: Yes, nutritional deficiencies can cause hair shedding. Eating a well-balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals can help improve hair health.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle wet hair
  • Eat a well-balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals

Conclusion

Excessive hair shedding can be a cause for concern, but it's important to remember that it's often temporary and can be managed with the right care. By determining the cause of your hair loss, practicing good hair care habits, and improving your diet, you can help reduce hair shedding and promote healthy hair growth.
