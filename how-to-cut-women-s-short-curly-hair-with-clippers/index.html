How To Cut Women s Short Curly Hair With Clippers

Written by Robby Jul 14, 2023 · 4 min read

Short curly hair can be a challenge to cut, especially if you're using clippers. But if done correctly, it can result in a stylish and low-maintenance haircut that's perfect for busy women. In this article, we'll show you how to cut women's short curly hair with clippers, step by step.

Description

Clippers are a great tool for cutting women's short curly hair because they can quickly and evenly cut through thick hair. However, cutting curly hair with clippers requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. You need to take into account the natural curl pattern, texture, and density of the hair to create a flattering and manageable haircut.

How to Cut Women's Short Curly Hair with Clippers

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure the hair is clean, dry, and detangled. Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to separate the hair into sections. You can also use hair clips to keep the sections in place.

Step 2: Choose the Right Clipper Guard

The clipper guard determines the length of the hair you're cutting. For short curly hair, start with a #2 or #3 guard. You can always go shorter if needed, but it's harder to fix a cut that's too short.

Step 3: Start at the Nape

Begin at the nape of the neck and work your way up. Use the clipper to cut the hair against the grain, moving upward in small sections. Use a gentle touch and avoid pressing too hard, as this can cause uneven cutting and irritation to the scalp.

Step 4: Blend the Sides and Back

Once you've cut the hair at the nape, move to the sides and back of the head. Use an upward motion to blend the sections together, making sure to keep the same guard size. Use a comb or your fingers to check for any uneven spots and make adjustments as needed.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Use scissors to cut the hair on top of the head. Keep the length longer than the sides and back, as this will help to create a more balanced look. Use your fingers to create texture and movement, and make sure to blend the top with the sides and back.

Step 6: Refine the Cut

Once you've finished the initial cut, use scissors to refine the shape and remove any stray hairs. Check for any uneven spots and make adjustments as needed. Use a mirror to check the back of the head, as this area can be tricky to cut.

Tips

  • Use a spray bottle to mist the hair with water if it's too dry or difficult to cut.
  • Don't cut too close to the scalp, as this can cause irritation and ingrown hairs.
  • Use a comb or your fingers to lift the hair and create a more natural-looking cut.
  • Keep the clippers clean and oiled to ensure smooth cutting and longevity.
  • Experiment with different guard sizes and cutting techniques to find the best style for your hair.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair with clippers?

While it's possible to cut your own hair with clippers, it's recommended that you have a friend or professional assist you. Cutting your own hair can be difficult and may result in an uneven or unsatisfactory cut.

How often should I cut my short curly hair?

Short curly hair should be cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends. However, the frequency of haircuts may vary depending on the individual's hair type and growth rate.

Hair Care

To keep your short curly hair looking its best, it's important to follow a regular hair care routine. This includes washing with a sulfate-free shampoo, conditioning with a moisturizing conditioner, and using a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep the hair hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage and frizz. Trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of the haircut.

In conclusion, cutting women's short curly hair with clippers can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and low-maintenance haircut that's perfect for your lifestyle. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant.
