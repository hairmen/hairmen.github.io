How To Smooth Flyaways A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Jun 23, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of dealing with pesky flyaways every time you style your hair? Don't worry, you're not alone. Flyaways are a common hair woe that can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, dryness, and static. Fortunately, there are several methods you can try to smooth flyaways and achieve sleek, frizz-free locks. In this article, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about taming flyaways, from the causes to the solutions.

What Are Flyaways?

Flyaways are those small, frizzy strands of hair that stick out from your head, often in random directions. They can be caused by a variety of factors, including weather conditions, heat styling, and chemical treatments. Flyaways are particularly common in hair that is dry or damaged, as well as hair that is naturally curly or wavy.

How to Smooth Flyaways

There are several methods you can try to smooth flyaways and keep them under control. Here are some of the most effective techniques:

Use a Smoothing Serum

A smoothing serum is a great way to tame flyaways and add shine to your hair. Look for a serum that contains ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or silicone, which can help to smooth the hair cuticle and prevent frizz. Apply a small amount of serum to your palms, then run your hands through your hair, focusing on the ends and any areas with flyaways.

Try a Hair Oil

Like a smoothing serum, a hair oil can help to tame flyaways and add shine to your hair. Look for an oil that is lightweight and non-greasy, such as jojoba oil or grapeseed oil. Apply a few drops of oil to your palms, then run your hands through your hair, focusing on the ends and any areas with flyaways.

Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to smooth flyaways and add moisture to your hair. Look for a conditioner that is lightweight and non-greasy, and avoid applying too much, as this can weigh down your hair. Apply the conditioner to your hair while it is still damp, then comb through with a wide-tooth comb to distribute evenly.

Try a Hair Mask

A hair mask is a deep conditioning treatment that can help to tame flyaways and improve the overall health of your hair. Look for a mask that contains ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or keratin, which can help to smooth the hair cuticle and prevent frizz. Apply the mask to your hair once a week, focusing on the ends and any areas with flyaways, and leave it on for the recommended time before rinsing thoroughly.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Smooth Flyaways

Here's a step-by-step guide to smoothing flyaways using a smoothing serum:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a small amount of smoothing serum to your palms.
  3. Run your hands through your hair, focusing on the ends and any areas with flyaways.
  4. Use a comb or brush to distribute the serum evenly.
  5. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Smoothing Flyaways

Here are some additional tips for taming flyaways and achieving a sleek, frizz-free look:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and frizzy.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause flyaways.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools like a flat iron or curling iron.
  • Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent friction and reduce flyaways.

FAQs

Q: Can flyaways be prevented?

A: While it's not always possible to prevent flyaways, there are steps you can take to minimize them. This includes using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools, and using a smoothing serum or hair oil.

Q: What causes flyaways?

A: Flyaways can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, dryness, and static. They are particularly common in hair that is dry or damaged, as well as hair that is naturally curly or wavy.

Q: Can I use hairspray to smooth flyaways?

A: Hairspray can be used to tame flyaways, but it may not be the most effective solution. Hairspray can be drying and stiff, which can actually make flyaways worse. Instead, try using a smoothing serum or hair oil.

Hair Care for Flyaways

To keep your hair healthy and flyaway-free, it's important to take good care of it. This includes:

  • Using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoiding over-washing your hair
  • Using a heat protectant spray before using hot tools
  • Sleeping on a silk pillowcase
  • Using a hair mask once a week

By following these tips and techniques, you can smooth flyaways and achieve a sleek, frizz-free look.
