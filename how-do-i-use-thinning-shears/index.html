How Do I Use Thinning Shears A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning shears, also known as blending shears, are a popular tool used by hairstylists to create texture, volume, and movement in the hair. However, for those who are not professionals, using thinning shears can be a bit intimidating. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps on how to use thinning shears, provide tips and answer common questions about this tool.

Table of Contents

  • How to Use Thinning Shears
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Using Thinning Shears
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Use Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are designed to remove bulk from the hair. They work by cutting some of the hair strands while leaving others intact, creating a more textured look. Thinning shears are best used on thick or curly hair, as they can help to remove some of the excess weight and give the hair more bounce.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to use thinning shears:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Thinning shears work best on dry hair, as wet hair can stretch and make it difficult to achieve the desired look.
  2. Section your hair. Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work on one section at a time.
  3. Start cutting. Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and cut through the section of hair. Be sure to cut only a small amount at a time, as thinning shears can be quite powerful and can quickly remove too much hair.
  4. Blend the hair. After you have cut through the section, gently blend the hair using your fingers. This will help to create a more natural look and prevent any choppy or uneven ends.
  5. Repeat. Continue to work through each section of hair until you have achieved the desired look.

Remember, less is more when it comes to using thinning shears. It's better to start with a small amount of hair and work your way up, as you can always cut more, but you can't put the hair back once it's been cut.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears:

  • Start with dry hair. Wet hair can stretch and make it difficult to achieve the desired look.
  • Use a light touch. Thinning shears are powerful and can quickly remove too much hair. Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up.
  • Use the right shears. There are different types of thinning shears, so be sure to choose one that is appropriate for your hair type and desired look.
  • Be patient. Achieving the perfect look with thinning shears takes time and practice. Don't get discouraged if it doesn't look perfect the first time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about using thinning shears:

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

Thinning shears work best on thick or curly hair. If you have fine or thin hair, it's best to avoid using thinning shears as they can make the hair look even thinner.

How often should I use thinning shears?

It's best to use thinning shears sparingly. Using them too often can cause the hair to become too thin, making it difficult to style.

Can I use thinning shears on my own hair?

Thinning shears are best used by professionals. If you want to use thinning shears on your own hair, it's best to practice on a small section first and work your way up.

Hair Care Tips

After using thinning shears, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Trim your hair regularly. Keeping your hair trimmed will help to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioner. Thinning shears can make the hair more susceptible to damage, so it's important to use a deep conditioner regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid excessive heat. Using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners can cause damage to the hair. If you must use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray.
  • Eat a healthy diet. A diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help to keep your hair healthy and strong.

By following these tips and using thinning shears properly, you can achieve a beautiful, textured look that will turn heads.
