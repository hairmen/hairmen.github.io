How Can I Make My Scalp Less Oily A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who struggles with an oily scalp, you know how frustrating it can be. Not only does it make your hair look greasy, but it can also lead to other issues like dandruff and breakouts. But fear not, there are ways to combat an oily scalp and keep it under control. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks on how to make your scalp less oily.

  • How to Make Your Scalp Less Oily
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Make Your Scalp Less Oily

Before we dive into the details, let's first understand what causes an oily scalp. Our scalp produces natural oils called sebum that help keep our hair healthy and shiny. However, when our scalp produces too much sebum, it can lead to an oily scalp. Here are some ways to combat excess sebum production:

1. Use a Clarifying Shampoo

Clarifying shampoos are designed to remove buildup and residue from your scalp and hair. Using a clarifying shampoo once a week can help remove excess oils and keep your scalp clean.

2. Avoid Overwashing Your Hair

While it may be tempting to wash your hair every day to combat an oily scalp, overwashing can actually make it worse. Washing your hair too frequently can strip your scalp of its natural oils, causing it to produce even more oil to compensate.

3. Use Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo is a great way to soak up excess oil without having to wash your hair. It's also a good option for those days when you don't have time to wash your hair but still want it to look fresh and clean.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can cause your scalp to produce more oil. Try to limit your use of these tools or use them on a lower heat setting.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to make your scalp less oily:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo once a week
  2. Avoid overwashing your hair
  3. Use dry shampoo in between washes
  4. Avoid heat styling tools or use them on a lower heat setting
  5. Try rinsing your hair with cool water to close your hair cuticles and reduce oil production
  6. Avoid touching your hair and scalp throughout the day as this can transfer oil from your hands to your hair
  7. Consider using a scalp scrub to exfoliate and remove buildup
  8. Limit your use of hair products like hairspray and gel as they can contribute to an oily scalp
  9. Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair and scalp
  10. Consider consulting with a dermatologist if you're experiencing severe oiliness or other scalp issues

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to keep your scalp less oily:

  • Avoid wearing hats and other head coverings for extended periods of time as they can trap heat and moisture, leading to more oil production
  • Consider using a scalp toner or astringent to help balance your scalp's pH levels
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair as it can stimulate oil production
  • Try using a boar bristle brush to distribute your scalp's natural oils throughout your hair

FAQs

1. Can certain foods cause an oily scalp?

While there isn't a direct correlation between certain foods and oily scalp, a diet high in processed foods and sugar can contribute to overall skin and scalp health.

2. Can stress cause an oily scalp?

Yes, stress can cause an increase in sebum production, leading to an oily scalp.

3. How often should I wash my hair?

This varies from person to person, but generally, it's recommended to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

Hair Care for Oily Scalp

When it comes to hair care for an oily scalp, it's important to use products that are specifically designed for oily hair. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are labeled as "oil-free" or "clarifying." Additionally, avoid using heavy styling products like gels and mousses, as they can contribute to oil buildup on your scalp.

Overall, combating an oily scalp takes some trial and error to find what works best for you. But with patience and consistency, you can achieve a healthy, balanced scalp.
