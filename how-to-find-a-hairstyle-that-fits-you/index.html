How To Find A Hairstyle That Fits You The Ultimate Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a new hairstyle but don't know where to start, you're in the right place. The key to finding a hairstyle that fits you is to consider your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to finding the perfect hairstyle for you.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding a hairstyle that fits you is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, look in the mirror and measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Then, compare the measurements to the following guidelines:

  • Oval: Forehead is slightly wider than the chin, and the length of the face is about one and a half times the width.
  • Round: Prominent cheeks and a rounded chin, with the width and length of the face about equal.
  • Square: Prominent jaw and forehead, with the width and length of the face about equal.
  • Heart: Narrow at the jawline and wide at the forehead and temples.
  • Diamond: Narrow at the forehead and jawline, with wide cheekbones.
  • Oblong: Long and narrow, with a forehead, cheekbones, and jawline of similar width.

Once you've determined your face shape, you can use it as a guide to finding a hairstyle that flatters your features.

How to Determine Your Hair Texture

The next step in finding a hairstyle that fits you is to determine your hair texture. Hair texture refers to the thickness and coarseness of your hair. There are three basic hair textures: fine, medium, and thick. To determine your hair texture, take a single strand of hair and rub it between your fingers:

  • Fine: Hair feels thin and wispy, and is difficult to feel between your fingers.
  • Medium: Hair feels neither too thin nor too thick, and is easy to feel between your fingers.
  • Thick: Hair feels dense and heavy, and is easy to feel between your fingers.

Your hair texture will affect how your hairstyle looks and how it behaves, so it's important to take it into consideration when choosing a new hairstyle.

How to Determine Your Personal Style

The final step in finding a hairstyle that fits you is to determine your personal style. Your hairstyle should reflect your personality and lifestyle, so it's important to choose a style that you feel comfortable with. Consider the following questions:

  • Do you prefer a classic or trendy look?
  • Do you enjoy experimenting with your hair, or do you prefer a low-maintenance style?
  • Do you have any hair concerns, such as thinning hair or a receding hairline?

Answering these questions will help you determine your personal style and choose a hairstyle that fits you.

Step-by-Step Guide to Finding a Hairstyle That Fits You

Now that you've determined your face shape, hair texture, and personal style, it's time to find a hairstyle that fits you. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Research hairstyles that flatter your face shape. Look for pictures of celebrities or models with face shapes similar to yours, and take note of the hairstyles that look good on them.
  2. Consider your hair texture. Some hairstyles work better with fine hair, while others work better with thick hair. Look for hairstyles that are suited to your hair texture.
  3. Think about your personal style. Do you want a classic, timeless hairstyle, or do you prefer something more trendy and edgy?
  4. Consult with a hairstylist. A professional hairstylist can help you determine which hairstyles will work best for your face shape, hair texture, and personal style.
  5. Try on wigs or use virtual hairstyle apps. If you're not sure about a particular hairstyle, try it on virtually before committing to a cut.
  6. Consider your lifestyle. If you have a busy schedule, you may want a low-maintenance hairstyle that requires minimal styling.
  7. Be open to suggestions. Your hairstylist may have suggestions for hairstyles that you haven't considered, so keep an open mind.
  8. Choose a hairstyle that makes you feel confident and comfortable. Ultimately, the most important factor in finding a hairstyle that fits you is how it makes you feel.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hairstyle

Once you've found a hairstyle that fits you, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for maintaining your hairstyle:

  • Follow a regular hair care routine, including shampooing and conditioning your hair.
  • Use styling products that are suited to your hair type.
  • Visit your hairstylist regularly for trims and touch-ups.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors.

FAQ

What if I don't know my face shape?

If you're not sure of your face shape, try measuring your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline, and comparing the measurements to the guidelines in this guide. Alternatively, ask your hairstylist to help you determine your face shape.

What if I have thinning hair or a receding hairline?

If you have thinning hair or a receding hairline, consider a hairstyle that adds volume or texture, such as a layered cut or a buzz cut. Your hairstylist can help you choose a hairstyle that flatters your features and addresses your concerns.

What if I don't like my new hairstyle?

If you don't like your new hairstyle, don't panic. Hair grows back, and you can always try a different style. Talk to your hairstylist about your concerns, and they may be able to make adjustments to the cut or suggest a different style.

The Bottom Line

Finding a hairstyle that fits you is all about considering your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. By following the steps in this guide, you can find a hairstyle that flatters your features and makes you feel confident and comfortable. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best, and don't be afraid to try new styles and experiment with your look.
