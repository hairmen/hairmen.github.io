Do You Go Bald From Chemotherapy

Written by Daniel Jul 13, 2023 · 3 min read

Chemotherapy is a common treatment for cancer that involves the use of drugs to kill cancer cells. However, one of the most well-known side effects of chemotherapy is hair loss. If you or a loved one is undergoing chemotherapy, you may be wondering, do you go bald from chemotherapy? In this article, we’ll explore the answer to this question and provide tips for hair care during and after chemotherapy.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Chemotherapy drugs target rapidly dividing cells, which includes cancer cells but also hair follicle cells. This is why hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy. However, not all chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss and the severity of hair loss can vary depending on the drug and dosage used.

How to

If you’re undergoing chemotherapy and are concerned about hair loss, there are a few things you can do to prepare:

  • Research the chemotherapy drugs you’ll be receiving and their side effects, including hair loss.
  • Talk to your doctor about your concerns and ask if there are any options to minimize hair loss.
  • Consider cutting your hair short before starting chemotherapy to make the hair loss less noticeable.

Step by step

Unfortunately, there’s no way to completely prevent hair loss from chemotherapy. However, there are some steps you can take to make the process more manageable:

  • Invest in a wig, hat, or scarf to cover your head.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools.
  • Consider trying a cold cap, which can help reduce hair loss by cooling the scalp during chemotherapy treatment.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips for managing hair loss during chemotherapy:

  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair to avoid pulling out more hair.
  • Avoid coloring or perming your hair during chemotherapy treatment.
  • Protect your scalp from the sun with a hat or scarf.

FAQ

Does everyone lose their hair during chemotherapy?

No, not everyone loses their hair during chemotherapy. The likelihood and severity of hair loss can vary depending on the chemotherapy drugs and dosage used.

Does hair grow back after chemotherapy?

Yes, hair typically grows back after chemotherapy treatment is complete. However, it may take several months for hair to grow back fully and the texture and color of hair may be different than before.

Can I color or perm my hair during chemotherapy?

No, it’s best to avoid coloring or perming your hair during chemotherapy treatment, as these processes can be harsh on the scalp and hair follicles.

Hair Care

After chemotherapy treatment is complete, it’s important to take good care of your hair to promote healthy regrowth:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools and harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that support hair growth.

In conclusion, while hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy, it’s not a guarantee for everyone. If you’re concerned about hair loss, talk to your doctor and take steps to prepare and manage the process.
