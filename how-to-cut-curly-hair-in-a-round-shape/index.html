How To Cut Curly Hair In A Round Shape A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Oct 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Curly hair is beautiful and versatile, but it can be challenging to cut. If you want to create a round shape for your curly hair, you need to approach the process differently than you would for straight hair. In this guide, we'll show you step-by-step how to cut curly hair in a round shape. We'll cover everything from preparation to aftercare, so you can achieve the perfect round cut for your curly locks.

Description

Cutting curly hair in a round shape can be tricky, but it's worth it for the beautiful, bouncy results. The key is to work with the natural texture of your hair, rather than fighting against it. By using the right tools and techniques, you can create a round shape that complements your face shape and highlights your curls.

How to Cut Curly Hair in a Round Shape

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp, high-quality hair cutting scissors, a wide-tooth comb, a spray bottle filled with water, and a mirror.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair, and then towel-dry it gently. You want your hair to be damp but not dripping wet. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 3: Create a Rough Shape

Using your comb, create a rough shape for your hair by sectioning it into four parts: the top, sides, and back. Pull each section outwards and away from your head, and then use your scissors to trim any split ends or damaged hair.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair, working in small sections. Use your comb to separate a small section of hair, and then twist it slightly to create tension. Holding your scissors at a slight angle, trim the ends of the hair, cutting in a curved shape to create a rounded effect. Work your way around your head, repeating this process for each section of hair.

Step 5: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you've finished the bottom layer, move on to the middle layer. Use the same technique, twisting each section of hair and cutting in a curved shape. Be sure to blend the middle layer with the bottom layer for a seamless look.

Step 6: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, it's time to cut the top layer of your hair. This layer is the most visible, so take your time and be careful. Use the same technique as before, cutting in a curved shape and blending the top layer with the middle layer.

Step 7: Check and Adjust

Once you've finished cutting, check your hair in the mirror to make sure the shape is even and balanced. If you notice any uneven sections, use your scissors to make small adjustments as needed.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair in a Round Shape

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect round cut for your curly hair:

  • Start with small cuts, and work your way up. You can always trim more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting curly hair requires precision and attention to detail.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep your hair damp as you cut. This will help you control your curls and prevent frizz.

FAQs

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, you should aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own curly hair in a round shape?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair in a round shape, but it can be challenging. If you're not confident in your skills, it's best to visit a professional stylist.

Hair Care After Cutting

After you've finished cutting your curly hair in a round shape, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated and frizz-free.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and keep them bouncy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.

Conclusion

Cutting curly hair in a round shape requires patience, skill, and the right tools. By following these steps and tips, you can create a beautiful, bouncy round cut that highlights your natural curls. Remember to take care of your hair properly after cutting, and you'll enjoy healthy, luscious curls for years to come.
