How To Darken Facial Hair With Makeup

Written by Bardi Aug 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to darken your facial hair with makeup? Whether you're trying to fill in patchy spots, enhance your beard or mustache, or simply want to experiment with a new look, there are a few simple steps you can follow to achieve the desired effect. In this article, we'll provide you with a comprehensive guide on how to darken facial hair with makeup, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice. Let's get started!

Description

Darkening facial hair with makeup is a popular beauty trend that involves using makeup products to enhance the look of your beard, mustache, or sideburns. This technique is especially useful for men who have light or patchy facial hair, as it can help fill in the gaps and create a more even, polished appearance. Additionally, darkening facial hair with makeup is a great way to experiment with different styles and looks without committing to a permanent change.

How to Darken Facial Hair with Makeup

To darken your facial hair with makeup, you'll need a few key products and tools:

  • A matte eyeshadow or brow powder in a shade that matches your natural hair color
  • A small, angled brush
  • A spoolie brush
  • A setting spray or hairspray

Once you have these items, follow these simple steps:

Step 1: Prep your skin

Before applying any makeup, make sure your skin is clean and dry. If you have any moisturizer or beard oil on, wipe it off with a clean towel or tissue.

Step 2: Apply the eyeshadow or brow powder

Using a small, angled brush, dip it into the eyeshadow or brow powder and tap off any excess. Then, start applying the powder to your facial hair in short, light strokes, following the direction of your hair growth. Make sure to focus on any patchy areas, and blend the powder in well with the rest of your hair.

Step 3: Use a spoolie brush to blend

After you've applied the powder, use a spoolie brush to blend it in and remove any excess. This will help create a more natural, seamless look.

Step 4: Set the makeup

To make sure your makeup stays in place all day, spritz some setting spray or hairspray over your facial hair. This will also help prevent any smudging or fading.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when darkening your facial hair with makeup:

  • Choose a matte eyeshadow or brow powder, as shimmery or glittery products can look unnatural on facial hair.
  • Make sure to blend the product in well with the rest of your hair to avoid any harsh lines or obvious makeup.
  • Experiment with different shades and intensities to find the look that works best for you.
  • Remove the makeup at the end of the day with a gentle cleanser or makeup remover.

FAQ

Can I use regular hair dye to darken my facial hair?

While it's technically possible to use regular hair dye on your facial hair, it's not recommended. Hair dye is formulated for use on the scalp, which has a different pH level and sensitivity than the skin on your face. Additionally, applying hair dye to your facial hair can be messy and difficult to control, potentially leading to uneven or patchy results.

Can I use this technique to lighten my facial hair?

No, this technique is specifically designed to darken facial hair. If you're looking to lighten your hair, you may want to consider using a bleach or lightening product specifically formulated for facial hair, or consult with a professional stylist.

Hair Care

When darkening your facial hair with makeup, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your facial hair regularly with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Use a beard oil or moisturizer to keep your hair and skin hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers or straighteners on your facial hair, as this can damage the hair and cause breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and techniques, you can easily darken your facial hair with makeup and achieve the look you're going for. Whether you're filling in patchy spots or experimenting with a new style, this technique is a simple and effective way to enhance your facial hair and boost your confidence. Give it a try today!
