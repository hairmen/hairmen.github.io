Textured Crop

The textured crop is a low-maintenance and versatile hairstyle that works for most face shapes. It's all about adding texture and volume to the hair, creating a messy yet stylish look. To achieve this style, ask your barber for a short back and sides, leaving the hair on top longer. Use a styling product like a clay or wax to create texture and hold.

How to Style:

  1. Apply a small amount of styling product to damp hair
  2. Using your fingers, work the product through your hair, creating texture and volume
  3. Use a blow dryer to add more volume if necessary
  4. Finish with a light hold hairspray

Tips:

  • The textured crop works best on hair that is naturally straight or wavy
  • It's important to use a small amount of product to avoid a greasy or heavy look
  • This style can be worn with or without a fringe, depending on your preference

FAQ:

Q: Will the textured crop work for curly hair?

A: Yes, but it may require more styling product and effort to create the desired texture and volume.

Hair Care:

To maintain the textured crop, it's important to use a shampoo and conditioner that will add volume and texture to the hair. Avoid using heavy or oily products that can weigh the hair down.

Buzz Cut

The buzz cut is a classic and timeless hairstyle that never goes out of style. It's a low-maintenance and practical option for those who prefer a simple and clean look. To achieve this style, ask your barber for a uniform length all over the head.

How to Style:

There's no styling necessary for the buzz cut, but it's important to maintain the length with regular trims.

Tips:

  • The buzz cut works best on those with a well-defined jawline and facial features
  • It's important to choose a length that flatters your face shape
  • This style can be worn with or without a beard, depending on your preference

FAQ:

Q: Will the buzz cut work for those with receding hairlines?

A: Yes, the buzz cut can actually help to disguise a receding hairline and create a more uniform look.

Hair Care:

To maintain the buzz cut, it's important to trim the hair regularly to avoid an unkempt look. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is gentle on the scalp and won't cause irritation.

Short Quiff

The short quiff is a modern take on the classic hairstyle, combining texture and volume for a stylish and edgy look. To achieve this style, ask your barber for a short back and sides, leaving the hair on top longer. Use a styling product like a pomade or gel to create height and hold.

How to Style:

  1. Apply a small amount of styling product to damp hair
  2. Using a comb, create volume at the front of the hair by pushing it upwards and back
  3. Use your fingers to create texture and shape at the back and sides
  4. Finish with a high hold hairspray

Tips:

  • The short quiff works best on hair that is thick and straight or wavy
  • It's important to use a high hold styling product to create the desired height and volume
  • This style can be worn with or without a fringe, depending on your preference

FAQ:

Q: Will the short quiff work for those with thinning hair?

A: Yes, but it may require more styling product and effort to create the desired volume and texture.

Hair Care:

To maintain the short quiff, it's important to use a shampoo and conditioner that will add volume and texture to the hair. Avoid using heavy or oily products that can weigh the hair down.
