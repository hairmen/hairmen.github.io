How To Stop Back Of Baby s Head Balding A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you noticing a bald spot at the back of your baby's head? Don't worry; it's a common problem among infants. The good news is that it's preventable and reversible. In this complete guide, we will discuss how to stop back of baby's head balding with easy-to-follow steps, tips, and FAQs.

Description

Baby's head balding is also known as positional plagiocephaly, which is caused by the baby's head resting in the same position for an extended period. It can also be due to too much time spent lying on their back or using car seats, swings, or bouncers. Bald spots are more common on the back of the head, but they can also occur on the sides or the front. Balding is not a severe health issue, but it can be a cosmetic concern for some parents.

How to Stop Back of Baby's Head Balding

1. Tummy Time

Tummy time is essential for your baby's development and prevents balding. It helps strengthen their neck, back, and arms and encourages them to move their head in different directions. Start with a few minutes a day and gradually increase the time. You can also use toys or mirrors to make it more fun for your baby.

2. Change Positions

Change your baby's position regularly throughout the day to avoid pressure on one spot. Alternate between side-lying, sitting, and carrying your baby in different positions. You can also use a baby carrier or sling to keep your baby close to you while keeping their head supported.

3. Limit Time in Car Seats, Swings, and Bouncers

Avoid leaving your baby in car seats, swings, or bouncers for an extended period. These devices can put pressure on one spot and cause balding. Use them only when necessary and limit the time to 30 minutes or less.

4. Use a Special Pillow

You can use a special pillow designed to prevent positional plagiocephaly. These pillows are made of soft materials and have a concave shape to support your baby's head and neck. However, it's essential to use them correctly and only under supervision.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Start Tummy Time

Begin with a few minutes a day and gradually increase the time. You can use toys or mirrors to make it more fun for your baby.

Step 2: Change Positions Regularly

Alternate between side-lying, sitting, and carrying your baby in different positions. Use a baby carrier or sling to keep your baby close to you while keeping their head supported.

Step 3: Limit Time in Car Seats, Swings, and Bouncers

Use them only when necessary and limit the time to 30 minutes or less.

Step 4: Use a Special Pillow

Use a special pillow designed to prevent positional plagiocephaly. Make sure to use it correctly and supervise your baby.

Tips

  • Massage your baby's head and neck to relieve pressure and increase blood flow.
  • Encourage your baby to look in different directions while lying on their back.
  • Avoid tight hats or headbands that can put pressure on one spot.
  • Consult your pediatrician if you notice any unusual changes in your baby's head shape.

FAQ

Q: Is baby's head balding a serious issue?

A: No, it's not a severe health issue. But it can be a cosmetic concern for some parents.

Q: Can balding be reversed?

A: Yes, it's reversible with proper care and treatment.

Q: How long does it take for balding to go away?

A: It depends on the severity and the age of the baby. It can take a few weeks to several months to notice improvement.

Hair Care

It's essential to take care of your baby's hair as well. Use a gentle shampoo and avoid using hair products that can irritate their scalp. Brush their hair gently and avoid pulling or tugging.

In conclusion, balding at the back of the baby's head is preventable and reversible. Follow the steps, tips, and FAQs mentioned above to stop back of the baby's head balding. Remember to consult your pediatrician if you notice any unusual changes in your baby's head shape.
