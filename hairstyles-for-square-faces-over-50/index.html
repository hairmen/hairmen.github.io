Hairstyles For Square Faces Over 50 A Guide

Written by Frank Jun 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on hairstyles for square faces over 50! As we age, it's important to find a hairstyle that flatters our face shape and complements our features. For those with square faces, this can be a bit tricky, but fear not – we've got you covered. Keep reading for tips, tricks, and hairstyle ideas.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Square faces are characterized by a strong jawline and a broad forehead. The goal when choosing a hairstyle for a square face is to soften these features and create balance. Hairstyles that add height or volume to the top of the head, or that incorporate layers or curls, can be particularly flattering.

For those over 50, it's also important to consider any changes in hair texture or thickness that may have occurred with age. Thinning hair, for example, may benefit from a shorter cut that adds volume and depth.

How To

When choosing a hairstyle for a square face over 50, consider the following:

  • Length: Shorter cuts can add volume and depth to thinning hair, while longer styles can elongate the face. Medium-length hair can be particularly flattering on square faces.
  • Layers: Layers can add movement and texture to hair, and can help to soften angular features.
  • Bangs: Side-swept bangs can help to soften a strong jawline and draw attention to the eyes. However, blunt bangs can make the face appear more square.
  • Volume: Hairstyles that add volume to the top of the head can be particularly flattering on square faces. This can be achieved through styling techniques such as backcombing or using volumizing products.
  • Curls: Soft curls can help to add movement and texture to hair, and can also help to soften angular features.

Step By Step

Here are some hairstyle ideas for square faces over 50:

Short Bob

A short bob can be a great option for those with thinning hair. To achieve this look:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Part hair to the side.
  3. Cut hair to chin length, with the back slightly shorter than the front.
  4. Add layers to create movement and texture.
  5. Style with a round brush to add volume at the crown.

Medium-Length Layers

Medium-length hair with layers can be a flattering option for square faces. To achieve this look:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Part hair to the side.
  3. Cut hair to shoulder length, with layers throughout.
  4. Add soft curls to create movement and texture.
  5. Style with a volumizing product to add height at the crown.

Long and Straight

For those who prefer longer hair, a straight style can be elongating and flattering on square faces. To achieve this look:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Straighten hair using a flat iron.
  3. Add layers to the ends to create movement.
  4. Add volume at the crown using a volumizing product or backcombing.

Tips

  • Consider your hair texture and thickness when choosing a hairstyle.
  • Side-swept bangs can be a great option for square faces.
  • Hairstyles that add height at the crown can be particularly flattering.
  • Soft curls or waves can help to soften angular features.

FAQ

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a square face?

Avoid hairstyles that are blunt or angular, as well as styles that add width to the sides of the face (such as a bob that hits at the jawline). Blunt bangs can also make a square face appear more square.

Should I consider hair color when choosing a hairstyle?

Yes! Hair color can also play a role in creating balance and softening features. Highlights, for example, can add depth and dimension to hair, while darker shades can create a slimming effect.

Hair Care

When styling and caring for hair, it's important to choose products that are appropriate for your hair type and texture. For those with thinning hair, volumizing products can be particularly helpful. It's also important to protect hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray or serum when using hot tools.

In addition, regular trims can help to keep hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Final Thoughts

With the right hairstyle, those with square faces over 50 can look and feel their best. Remember to consider length, layers, and styling techniques when choosing a hairstyle, and don't be afraid to experiment with different looks until you find one that works for you.
