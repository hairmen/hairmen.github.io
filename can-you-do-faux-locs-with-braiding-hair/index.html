Can You Do Faux Locs With Braiding Hair

Written by Bardi Oct 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who loves to experiment with different hairstyles, then you've probably heard of faux locs. Faux locs are a great way to achieve the look of dreadlocks without the commitment of actually growing them. They're a popular protective style that can last for weeks or even months. But can you do faux locs with braiding hair? The answer is yes! In this blog post, we'll guide you through the steps of achieving faux locs with braiding hair.

Description

Faux locs are a type of hairstyle that imitates the look of dreadlocks. They're achieved by wrapping or twisting hair extensions around your own hair. Faux locs can be done with various types of hair extensions, including braiding hair, and they come in different lengths and widths.

How to

Here are the steps to achieve faux locs with braiding hair:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start, make sure your hair is clean and detangled. You can also add some leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into small parts. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the faux locs you want to achieve.

Step 3: Attach the Braiding Hair

Take a piece of braiding hair and fold it in half. Place the folded end of the hair under the section of your hair and create a loop. Take the ends of the hair and pull them through the loop. This will create a knot around your hair. Repeat this step until the entire section of your hair is covered with the braiding hair.

Step 4: Create the Faux Locs

Once the braiding hair is attached, twist it around your own hair to create the faux locs. You can use a hairpin or rubber band to secure the ends of the braiding hair so that they don't unravel. Repeat this process for each section of your hair until you've achieved the desired look.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when doing faux locs with braiding hair:

  • Choose the right type of braiding hair. Make sure it's soft and lightweight so that it doesn't put too much tension on your hair.
  • Use a crochet hook to attach the braiding hair to your hair. This will make the process faster and easier.
  • Use a hair oil or moisturizer to keep your scalp and hair hydrated while wearing faux locs.
  • Avoid using heavy products or hair tools that can damage your hair or the faux locs.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about faux locs with braiding hair:

Q: How long do faux locs with braiding hair last?

A: Faux locs can last anywhere from 4 to 8 weeks, depending on how well you take care of them and how fast your hair grows.

Q: Can you wash faux locs with braiding hair?

A: Yes, you can wash your faux locs with braiding hair, but make sure you're gentle and don't tug or pull on the hair too much.

Q: How do you remove faux locs with braiding hair?

A: To remove faux locs with braiding hair, simply unravel the twists and gently pull out the braiding hair. You can also use a pair of scissors to cut the braiding hair if it's difficult to remove.

Hair Care

While wearing faux locs with braiding hair, it's important to take care of your hair and scalp. Here are some tips:

  • Moisturize your scalp and hair regularly with a light oil or leave-in conditioner.
  • Avoid scratching your scalp or pulling on your hair too much, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Cover your hair with a satin scarf or bonnet at night to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • When it's time to remove your faux locs, be gentle and take your time to avoid damaging your hair.

Now that you know how to do faux locs with braiding hair, you can try this trendy hairstyle for yourself. Remember to take care of your hair and scalp while wearing faux locs and enjoy your new look!
