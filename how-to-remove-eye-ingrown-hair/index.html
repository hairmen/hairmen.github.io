How To Remove Eye Ingrown Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Sep 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've ever had an ingrown hair, you know how uncomfortable and unsightly they can be. And when it comes to ingrown hairs on the eye area, the situation can be even more complicated. In this article, we'll explore how to remove eye ingrown hair using simple and effective methods.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Remove Eye Ingrown Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Eye Ingrown Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Eye Area

How to Remove Eye Ingrown Hair

Before we dive into the specifics of how to remove eye ingrown hair, it's important to understand what causes them. Ingrown hairs occur when a hair grows back into the skin instead of out of it. This can happen when dead skin cells clog the hair follicle or when the hair is cut too short and curls back into the skin.

To remove eye ingrown hair, you'll need to carefully extract the hair from the skin. This can be done using a sterile needle or tweezers. It's important to be gentle and avoid pulling the hair out forcefully, as this can cause further irritation and potentially lead to infection.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water to prevent introducing bacteria to the area.
  2. Cleanse the area around the ingrown hair with a mild, fragrance-free cleanser.
  3. Apply a warm compress to the area for 5-10 minutes to help soften the skin and open up the hair follicle.
  4. Using a sterile needle or tweezers, carefully lift the hair out of the skin. Be sure to avoid pulling the hair out forcefully.
  5. If the hair is too deeply embedded in the skin, don't force it. Wait for it to grow out further before attempting to remove it.
  6. After removing the hair, apply a soothing, unscented moisturizer or aloe vera gel to the area to help reduce inflammation and prevent infection.

Tips for Preventing Eye Ingrown Hair:

While it's not always possible to prevent ingrown hairs, there are some steps you can take to reduce your risk:

  • Avoid shaving the area around your eyes, as this can increase your risk of ingrown hairs.
  • Use a sharp razor when shaving other areas of your face to prevent irritation and ingrown hairs.
  • Exfoliate regularly to help remove dead skin cells and prevent clogged hair follicles.
  • Moisturize the area around your eyes regularly to keep the skin soft and supple.

FAQs:

Can I use a depilatory cream to remove eye ingrown hairs?

No, it's not recommended to use depilatory creams around the eye area. These creams contain harsh chemicals that can irritate the delicate skin around your eyes and potentially cause more ingrown hairs.

Is it safe to remove ingrown hairs myself?

While it's generally safe to remove ingrown hairs yourself, it's important to do so carefully and hygienically to avoid infection or further irritation. If you're unsure about how to remove an ingrown hair or if you have a large number of ingrown hairs, it's best to consult a dermatologist.

Hair Care for Eye Area:

In addition to following the tips above to prevent ingrown hairs, it's important to take good care of the hair around your eyes. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid pulling or tugging on the hair around your eyes, as this can cause it to become ingrown.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair soft and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons near your eyes, as the heat can damage the hair and cause it to break.
  • Trim your eyebrows regularly to prevent hairs from becoming ingrown.

By following the tips and tricks outlined in this article, you can effectively remove eye ingrown hair and prevent them from occurring in the future. Remember to always be gentle and hygienic when removing ingrown hairs to avoid further irritation or infection.
