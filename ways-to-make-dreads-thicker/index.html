Ways To Make Dreads Thicker A Comprehensive Guide

Are you tired of your thin and lifeless dreadlocks? Do you want to give them more volume and thickness? Look no further! In this article, we will discuss various ways to make dreads thicker, including how to, step by step, tips, FAQ, and hair care.

How to Make Dreads Thicker

There are several ways to make your dreads thicker, including:

1. Backcombing

Backcombing is a popular technique used to create volume and thickness in dreadlocks. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by sectioning your hair into small, even parts.
  2. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up.
  3. Use a fine-toothed comb to backcomb the hair from the tip to the root.
  4. Repeat this process for each section of hair.
  5. Once all sections are backcombed, use a crochet hook or your fingers to tighten the dreadlocks.

Backcombing can be time-consuming, but the results are worth it.

2. Using a Thickening Spray

Another way to make your dreads thicker is by using a thickening spray. These sprays contain ingredients that coat the hair strands, giving them more volume and thickness. Here's how to use it:

  1. Wash your hair and towel dry it.
  2. Spray the thickening spray onto your damp hair, focusing on the roots.
  3. Use your fingers to work the spray into your hair.
  4. Blow-dry your hair on a low setting, scrunching your dreads as you go.

Thickening sprays are a quick and easy way to add volume to your dreads.

3. Using Hair Extensions

If you want to add more length and thickness to your dreads, consider using hair extensions. Here's how to do it:

  1. Choose a color and texture of extensions that match your natural hair.
  2. Section off a small part of your hair and braid it tightly.
  3. Attach the hair extension to the end of the braid.
  4. Twist the extension around your natural hair, creating a new dreadlock.
  5. Repeat the process for each section of hair.

Using hair extensions is a great way to add more thickness and length to your dreads.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to make your dreads thicker:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a residue-free shampoo to remove any build-up.
  2. Use a towel to dry your hair thoroughly.
  3. Section off your hair into small, even parts.
  4. Use one of the techniques mentioned above to make your dreads thicker.
  5. Once your dreads are thicker, use a crochet hook or your fingers to tighten them.
  6. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your dreads to keep them moisturized.
  7. Wrap your hair in a silk or satin scarf before bed to prevent frizz and breakage.
  8. Repeat the process as needed to maintain the thickness of your dreads.

Tips to Keep Your Dreads Thick

Here are some tips to help you keep your dreads thick:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid build-up.
  • Avoid using heavy or oily hair products that can weigh down your dreads.
  • Protect your dreads from damage by wearing a hat or scarf in the sun.
  • Regularly palm roll your dreads to keep them tight and neat.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about making dreads thicker:

1. How long does it take to make dreads thicker?

The time it takes to make your dreads thicker depends on the technique you use and the thickness you want to achieve. Backcombing can take several hours, while using a thickening spray can take minutes.

2. Can I make my dreads thicker without damaging them?

Yes, you can make your dreads thicker without damaging them. However, it's important to use gentle techniques and avoid over-manipulation of your hair.

3. How often should I make my dreads thicker?

You can make your dreads thicker as often as you like, but it's important to give your hair time to rest between sessions. Over-manipulation of your hair can lead to breakage and damage.

Hair Care for Thick Dreads

Here are some tips for caring for your thick dreads:

  • Wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo once a week.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your dreads to keep them moisturized.
  • Use a silk or satin scarf to wrap your hair at night to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid using heavy or oily hair products that can weigh down your dreads.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can make your dreads thicker and more voluminous. Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong.
