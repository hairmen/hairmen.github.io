How To Thin Curly Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how challenging it can be to manage its thickness. Thinning your curly hair can help you achieve a more manageable and stylish look. However, going to a salon can be expensive, so learning how to thin your curly hair yourself can save you some bucks. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to thin curly hair yourself.

How to Thin Curly Hair Yourself

There are different methods you can use to thin curly hair yourself. However, before you start, it's essential to have the right tools. You'll need a pair of thinning scissors, a regular pair of scissors, and a comb.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to thin your curly hair yourself:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to see the thickness of your hair and identify which areas need thinning.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using a comb. This will make it easier to work on each section at a time.

Step 3: Start Thinning

Take one section of your hair and comb it thoroughly. Hold the section of hair between your index and middle fingers, and then use the thinning scissors to cut the hair. Remember to cut only a small amount of hair at a time.

Repeat this process on each section of your hair.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

After thinning your curly hair, use a regular pair of scissors to blend the hair. This will create a more natural look and avoid any choppy, uneven ends.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished thinning and blending your hair, style it as desired.

Tips for Thinning Curly Hair

Here are some tips you can follow when thinning your curly hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Cut your hair while it's dry, so you can see the thickness and avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use thinning scissors instead of regular scissors to avoid creating blunt lines.
  • Be patient and take your time. Thinning curly hair takes time and precision.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can you thin curly hair with regular scissors?

While you can use regular scissors to thin curly hair, it's not recommended. Regular scissors can create blunt lines, resulting in uneven and choppy ends. Thinning scissors are designed to remove hair gradually, creating a more natural look.

How much hair should I thin at once?

You should only thin a small amount of hair at once, especially if you're new to thinning curly hair. Start with small sections and gradually thin more hair as you gain experience.

Hair Care

Thinning your curly hair can make it more manageable and stylish. However, it's essential to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips you can follow to take care of your hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently. If you must, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.

Thinning curly hair yourself can be a challenging task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a more manageable and stylish look. Follow these steps, tips, and FAQ to thin your curly hair yourself like a pro.
