Cat Going Bald Around Eyes Causes Treatments And Hair Care Tips

Written by Frank Sep 06, 2023 · 3 min read

If you notice your feline friend going bald around the eyes, it’s important to act quickly. This condition, called alopecia, can be caused by a number of factors, from allergies to infections to stress. In this article, we’ll explore the causes of cat alopecia, how to treat it, and hair care tips to keep your cat looking and feeling their best.

Causes of Cat Alopecia

There are several possible causes of cat alopecia, including:

  • Allergies: Cats can be allergic to food or environmental factors like pollen, dust, or mold. Allergies can cause itching and hair loss around the eyes.
  • Infections: Bacterial or fungal infections can cause hair loss and scaly skin around the eyes.
  • Stress: Just like humans, cats can experience stress that leads to hair loss. This can be caused by changes in their environment, such as moving or adding a new pet to the household.
  • Parasites: Fleas, mites, and other parasites can cause hair loss and skin irritation around the eyes.

Treatments for Cat Alopecia

The treatment for your cat’s alopecia will depend on the underlying cause. If your cat has allergies, your vet may recommend dietary changes or medication to control symptoms. If an infection is to blame, your cat may need antibiotics or antifungal medication. If parasites are the issue, your vet may recommend a topical or oral medication to kill the pests.

If stress is the cause of your cat’s alopecia, you may need to work with a behaviorist to identify and address the root of the problem. This may involve creating a more comfortable environment for your cat, providing more toys and playtime, or even prescribing medication to help alleviate anxiety.

How to Care for a Cat with Alopecia

Once you’ve identified the cause of your cat’s alopecia and begun treatment, you can take steps to care for their hair and skin to promote regrowth and prevent further hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Brush your cat regularly to remove dead hair and stimulate the skin.
  • Use a gentle, hypoallergenic shampoo to bathe your cat.
  • Ensure your cat is eating a healthy diet with plenty of protein.
  • Provide your cat with plenty of mental and physical stimulation to reduce stress.

Frequently Asked Questions about Cat Alopecia

Can alopecia be prevented?

Preventing alopecia entirely may not be possible, but you can reduce your cat’s risk by feeding them a healthy diet, keeping them clean and groomed, and addressing any potential stressors in their environment.

Will my cat’s hair grow back?

If the underlying cause of your cat’s alopecia is identified and treated, their hair should eventually grow back. However, it may take several months for hair to regrow fully.

Is cat alopecia contagious to humans?

No, cat alopecia is not contagious to humans.

Conclusion

If you notice your cat going bald around the eyes, it’s important to act quickly to identify the underlying cause and begin treatment. With proper care and attention, your cat’s hair should eventually regrow, leaving them looking and feeling their best.
