How To Cut Thick Hair Into A Layered Bob A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 10, 2023 · 5 min read

Cutting thick hair into a layered bob can be a challenge, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done with ease. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut thick hair into a layered bob step by step. We’ll also share some tips and hair care advice to help you maintain your new look. So, let’s get started!

How to Cut Thick Hair into a Layered Bob

A layered bob is a versatile hairstyle that looks great on women of all ages. It’s a low-maintenance style that can be worn straight or wavy and can be customized to suit your face shape and personal style. Here’s how to cut thick hair into a layered bob:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start, you’ll need to gather some tools. You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. You may also want to have a mirror to check your progress as you cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: the top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Start with the back section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers and cut it at an angle to create a layered effect. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you’ve finished the back section.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers and cut it at an angle to create a layered effect. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you’ve finished both side sections.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Finally, move on to the top section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers and cut it at an angle to create a layered effect. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you’ve finished the top section.

Step by Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed step by step guide on how to cut thick hair into a layered bob:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a good quality shampoo and conditioner. This will help to remove any dirt or product buildup from your hair.

Step 2: Blow Dry Your Hair

Next, blow dry your hair straight using a round brush. This will help to smooth out any kinks or waves in your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: the top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Start with the back section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers and cut it at an angle to create a layered effect. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you’ve finished the back section.

Step 5: Cut the Sides

Move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers and cut it at an angle to create a layered effect. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you’ve finished both side sections.

Step 6: Cut the Top

Finally, move on to the top section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers and cut it at an angle to create a layered effect. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you’ve finished the top section.

Step 7: Check Your Progress

Use a mirror to check your progress as you cut. Make sure that both sides are even and that the layers are blended.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, style it as desired. You can wear your layered bob straight or wavy, depending on your personal style.

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair into a Layered Bob

Here are some tips to help you cut thick hair into a layered bob:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Avoid cutting too much hair at once to prevent mistakes.
  • Check your progress as you cut to ensure that both sides are even.
  • Consider using hair thinning shears to remove bulk from thick hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting thick hair into a layered bob:

Is it easy to cut thick hair into a layered bob?

Cutting thick hair into a layered bob can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done with ease.

What tools do I need to cut thick hair into a layered bob?

You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. You may also want to have a mirror to check your progress as you cut.

How long does it take to cut thick hair into a layered bob?

The time it takes to cut thick hair into a layered bob depends on the length and thickness of your hair. It can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour or more.

Hair Care Advice

Here are some tips to help you care for your hair after cutting it into a layered bob:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair to prevent damage.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh.

Cutting thick hair into a layered bob is a great way to update your look and add some volume and movement to your hair. With the right tools and techniques, it can be done with ease. Just remember to take your time, start with small sections of hair, and check your progress as you cut. Happy styling!
