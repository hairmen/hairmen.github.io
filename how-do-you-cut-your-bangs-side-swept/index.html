How Do You Cut Your Bangs Side Swept A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 30, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of your regular bangs and want to switch things up with a side-swept look? Well, cutting your bangs side swept is easier than you think! In this article, we'll guide you through the process step by step and provide some helpful tips along the way. Let's get started.

How To

Before you start cutting your bangs, make sure your hair is clean and dry. You'll also need a pair of sharp scissors and a comb. Here's how to cut your bangs side swept:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Take a section of hair at the front of your head and comb it straight down. This section should be about the width of your forehead.

Step 2: Determine Your Bangs' Length

Hold your comb horizontally at the point where you want your bangs to end. Then, use your fingers to hold the hair below the comb, and cut the hair above it. Remember, it's better to cut a little bit at a time than to cut too much at once.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

Hold your scissors at an angle and cut your bangs diagonally. This will create a natural, side-swept look. Make sure to cut small sections at a time and check your progress as you go.

Step 4: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your bangs, comb them to the side and check for any uneven spots. If you notice any, trim them with your scissors.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your bangs side swept:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Always cut your bangs when they're dry, as wet hair can shrink when it dries.
  • Don't pull your hair too tightly when cutting. This can result in uneven cuts and an unnatural look.
  • When in doubt, go to a professional stylist.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting side-swept bangs:

How often should I cut my side-swept bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your bangs to be. On average, you should trim your bangs every four to six weeks to maintain their length and shape.

Can I cut my bangs myself?

Yes, you can cut your bangs yourself, but it's important to be careful and take your time. If you're unsure, it's always better to go to a professional stylist.

What if I mess up my bangs?

If you accidentally cut your bangs too short or unevenly, don't panic. You can try to even them out by taking small snips at a time or pinning them back until they grow out.

Hair Care

Now that you've cut your side-swept bangs, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some hair care tips for your new bangs:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your bangs clean and oil-free.
  • Blow-dry your bangs with a round brush to create volume and shape.
  • Avoid using too much hair product, as this can weigh down your bangs.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to maintain their shape.

And there you have it, a step-by-step guide on how to cut your bangs side swept. Follow these tips and tricks, and you'll have a chic and stylish new look in no time!
