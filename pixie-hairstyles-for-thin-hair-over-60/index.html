Pixie Hairstyles For Thin Hair Over 60 A Guide To Flattering Cuts

Written by Bardi Sep 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're over 60 and have thin hair, you might think that your hairstyle options are limited. However, there are plenty of pixie haircuts that can flatter your face shape and hair texture while also making you feel stylish and confident. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about pixie hairstyles for thin hair over 60, including how to choose the right cut, how to style your hair, and how to take care of it.

Choosing the Right Pixie Cut

When choosing a pixie cut for thin hair over 60, it's important to consider your face shape and hair texture. For example, if you have a round face, you might want to choose a pixie cut with longer layers that frame your face. If you have a square face, a shorter, more angular pixie cut can help balance out your features.

When it comes to hair texture, a layered pixie cut can help create the illusion of fuller hair. You might also want to consider a textured or choppy pixie cut, which can add volume and movement to thin hair.

Some popular pixie cuts for thin hair over 60 include:

  • The classic pixie cut, which features short, cropped layers all over
  • The layered pixie cut, which features longer layers on top and shorter layers on the sides and back
  • The textured pixie cut, which features choppy, uneven layers for a more edgy look
  • The asymmetrical pixie cut, which features longer layers on one side and shorter layers on the other

How to Style Your Pixie Cut

Styling a pixie cut is relatively simple, but it's important to use the right products and tools to achieve the look you want. Here are some tips for styling your pixie cut:

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots to add lift
  • Use a small round brush to blow dry your hair, focusing on the roots and crown
  • If you want more texture, use a texturizing spray or pomade on the ends of your hair
  • To add definition to your bangs or layers, use a small amount of wax or gel

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Pixie Cut

If you're new to styling a pixie cut, it can be helpful to follow a step-by-step guide. Here's how to style your pixie cut:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair
  2. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots
  3. Blow dry your hair using a small round brush
  4. Use a texturizing spray or pomade to add texture to the ends of your hair
  5. If you have bangs or layers, use a small amount of wax or gel to define them
  6. Finish with a light hairspray to hold your style in place

Tips for Maintaining Your Pixie Cut

Once you have a pixie cut, it's important to maintain it regularly to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for maintaining your pixie cut:

  • Get regular trims every 4-6 weeks to keep your hair looking neat and tidy
  • Use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, which can damage it and cause breakage
  • Consider getting a deep conditioning treatment once a month to keep your hair healthy and hydrated

FAQs About Pixie Hairstyles for Thin Hair Over 60

Q: Will a pixie cut make my thin hair look even thinner?

A: Not necessarily. A layered pixie cut can actually create the illusion of fuller hair by adding volume and movement.

Q: Can I wear a pixie cut if I have a round face?

A: Yes! A layered pixie cut with longer layers can actually be very flattering for round faces.

Q: How do I know if a pixie cut is right for me?

A: The best way to know if a pixie cut is right for you is to try it out! You can also consult with your stylist to see what styles and cuts would be most flattering for your face shape and hair texture.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

Even if you have a great haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some hair care tips for thin hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't weigh down your hair
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, which can cause damage and breakage
  • Consider using a volumizing mousse or spray to add lift to your roots
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking neat and tidy
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a month to keep your hair healthy and hydrated

With the right cut, styling techniques, and hair care routine, you can rock a pixie haircut at any age. Whether you prefer a classic, layered, or textured pixie cut, there's a style that will flatter your face shape and hair texture. So go ahead and embrace your inner pixie!
