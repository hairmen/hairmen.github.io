How To Fade Your Own Buzz Cut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 18, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re looking to give your buzz cut a fade, you might be wondering if it’s possible to do it yourself. The answer is yes! With a little bit of patience and the right tools, you can learn how to fade your own buzz cut at home. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to achieve a fade that will make your buzz cut look even better.

How to Fade Your Own Buzz Cut: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Choose the Right Clippers

The first step to achieving a great fade is to choose the right clippers. You’ll need a clipper with adjustable blades so you can change the length of the cut as you move up the head.

Step 2: Start with a Longer Guard

Start with a longer guard on the clippers and work your way down in length as you move up the head. This will help you achieve a gradual fade.

Step 3: Create a Guideline

Use the longest guard to create a guideline around the head. This will help you keep the fade even all the way around. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up.

Step 4: Work in Sections

Work in sections around the head, gradually decreasing the length of the guard as you move up. Blend each section into the previous one using a flicking motion with the clippers. Be sure to keep the clippers parallel to the head to achieve an even fade.

Step 5: Check Your Work

After each section, step back and check your work in a mirror. This will help you see if there are any areas that need more blending or if the fade is too abrupt.

Step 6: Use Scissors and Comb to Blend

Once you’ve achieved the desired length, use scissors and a comb to blend any areas that need extra attention. This will help you achieve a more natural-looking fade.

Step 7: Clean Up the Neckline

Use the clippers or a razor to clean up the neckline. You can create a straight line or a rounded line, depending on your preference.

Step 8: Wash and Style

After you’ve finished fading your buzz cut, wash your hair to remove any loose hairs. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Fading Your Own Buzz Cut

  • Start with a longer guard than you think you need. You can always go shorter, but it’s harder to go longer.
  • Take your time. Fading your own buzz cut can be a time-consuming process, but it’s worth it for the end result.
  • Use a mirror to check your work often. This will help you catch any mistakes before they become too noticeable.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help if you need it. It can be difficult to fade the back of your head on your own.

FAQ

Can I fade my own buzz cut with scissors?

It’s possible to fade your own buzz cut with scissors, but it can be more difficult to achieve an even fade. Clippers with adjustable blades are the best tool for fading a buzz cut.

How often should I fade my buzz cut?

How often you should fade your buzz cut depends on how quickly your hair grows and how short you prefer to keep it. As a general rule, you should aim to fade your buzz cut every 2-4 weeks.

Can I use hair products on my faded buzz cut?

Yes, you can use hair products on your faded buzz cut. However, be sure to choose products that won’t weigh your hair down or make it look greasy.

Hair Care for Faded Buzz Cuts

To keep your faded buzz cut looking its best, be sure to wash your hair regularly and use a moisturizing conditioner. Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers and flat irons, as they can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve a smooth fade.

Now that you know how to fade your own buzz cut, you can achieve a stylish look from the comfort of your own home. With a little bit of practice, you’ll be able to achieve a fade that looks like it was done by a professional.
