Should You Have Layers If You Have Curly Hair

Written by Daniel Jul 11, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have curly hair, you may be wondering whether or not layers are a good idea for your hair type. Some people swear by layers, while others avoid them like the plague. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of layers for curly hair, and help you decide whether or not they're right for you.

Benefits of Layers for Curly Hair

Layers can work wonders for curly hair. Here are some of the benefits:

  • More volume: Layers can add volume and body to curly hair, making it look fuller and more bouncy.
  • More movement: Layers can help your curls move more freely, giving your hair a more natural, fluid look.
  • More texture: Layers can enhance the texture of your curls, making them look more defined and distinct.
  • More styling options: With layers, you can experiment with different styles and looks, from messy buns to sleek updos.

Overall, layers can give your curly hair more dimension and personality, and help you achieve a more dynamic and versatile look.

Drawbacks of Layers for Curly Hair

While layers can be great for curly hair, they're not without their drawbacks. Here are some of the potential downsides:

  • More frizz: Layers can sometimes exacerbate frizziness, especially if they're cut too short or unevenly.
  • More tangles: Layers can create more tangles and knots, especially if you have long curly hair.
  • More styling time: Layers can require more time and effort to style and maintain, especially if you're not used to working with them.
  • More maintenance: Layers can require more frequent trims and touch-ups to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

Overall, layers can be a bit high-maintenance for some people, and may not be worth the extra effort if you prefer a simpler, more low-key look.

How to Get Layers in Curly Hair

If you're interested in getting layers in your curly hair, the first step is to find a stylist who specializes in curly hair. Not all stylists are created equal, and some may not be familiar with the unique needs and challenges of curly hair.

Once you've found a stylist you trust, you can discuss your options and decide on the best approach. There are several ways to layer curly hair, including:

  • Long layers: Long layers are typically cut in a V-shape, with the longest layers near the bottom of the hair. This can add volume and movement to curly hair without sacrificing length.
  • Short layers: Short layers are typically cut around the crown of the head, and can add texture and dimension to curly hair. However, they can also create more frizz and tangles.
  • Choppy layers: Choppy layers are a more edgy and modern look, with uneven lengths and jagged edges. They can work well for some people, but may not be suitable for all hair types and face shapes.

Your stylist can help you decide which type of layering will work best for your hair, and can tailor the cut to suit your individual needs and preferences.

Step-by-Step Guide to Layering Curly Hair

If you're curious about how the layering process works, here's a basic step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual, and let it air dry or diffuse it with a blow dryer.
  2. Section off your hair into manageable chunks, starting at the nape of your neck.
  3. Using sharp scissors, cut the desired length of the first layer. Depending on the type of layering you're doing, you may want to cut at an angle or curve.
  4. Repeat the process for each section of hair, working your way up to the crown of your head.
  5. Blend the layers together by gently combing through your hair with your fingers, or using a wide-toothed comb.
  6. Style your hair as desired, using products that are appropriate for your hair type and texture.

It's important to note that layering curly hair can be tricky, and may require some practice and patience. If you're not confident in your cutting skills, it's best to leave it to the professionals.

Tips for Maintaining Layered Curly Hair

Once you've got your layered curly hair, you'll want to take good care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can create frizz and breakage. Instead, detangle your hair with a wide-toothed comb when it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too frequently, as this can damage your curls and make them more prone to frizz and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your layers.

By following these simple tips, you can help your layered curly hair look and feel its best.

FAQs About Layers for Curly Hair

Here are some common questions and answers about layers for curly hair:

Q: Will layers make my hair look thinner?

A: Not necessarily. While some types of layers can make hair look thinner, others can actually add volume and body.

Q: Can I get layers if I have very fine or thin hair?

A: Yes, but you'll want to be careful not to overdo it, as too many layers can make fine or thin hair look even sparser. Talk to your stylist about the best approach for your hair type.

Q: Do layers work better for certain types of curls?

A: It depends on the individual. Some people with tighter curls may prefer longer layers, while those with looser curls may prefer shorter layers. Your stylist can help you determine the best approach for your particular curl pattern.

Q: Can I still wear my hair in a bun or ponytail if I have layers?

A: Yes, but you may need to experiment with different styles and techniques to find the best approach. Using hair elastics that are gentle on your hair, like scrunchies or spiral hair ties, can help minimize damage and breakage.

Hair Care for Layered Curly Hair

If you want to keep your layered curly hair looking and feeling its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair no more than twice a week, using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it more prone to damage.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and hydrate your curls.
  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry, rather than rubbing it vigorously with a regular towel.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too frequently, and always use a heat protectant spray when you do.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental stressors by wearing a hat or scarf when you're outside.

By following these simple hair care tips, you can help your layered curly hair stay healthy, shiny, and beautiful.
