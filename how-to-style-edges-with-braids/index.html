How To Style Edges With Braids Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Aug 20, 2023 Â· 2 min read

If you're a fan of braids, you know that styling your edges can be a bit tricky. But with the right techniques and tools, you can create a sleek and polished look that will last for days. In this post, we'll share our top tips for styling your edges with braids, including how to prep your hair, what products to use, and step-by-step instructions for creating different styles.

How to Prep Your Hair

Before you start braiding, it's important to prep your hair properly. This will help ensure that your braids stay in place and that your edges look neat and tidy. Here are a few tips:

  • Start with clean, detangled hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup, and then follow up with a moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair soft and hydrated.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your hair to help prevent breakage and frizz. This will also make it easier to detangle your hair and create smooth braids.
  • Gently blow-dry your hair on a low heat setting, using a diffuser attachment to help prevent heat damage. This will help stretch your hair and make it easier to braid.

What Products to Use

When it comes to styling your edges with braids, using the right products is key. Here are a few products that we recommend:

  • Edge control: This is a must-have product for creating sleek and defined edges. Look for a formula that is lightweight and non-greasy, and that won't leave residue on your hair.
  • Hair gel: Gel can be used to smooth down any flyaways or frizz, and can also help hold your braids in place.
  • Hair spray: A light hold hairspray can help keep your braids in place without weighing them down or making them feel stiff.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you know how to prep your hair and what products to use, it's time to start braiding! Here are step-by-step instructions for creating two different styles:

Style 1: Cornrows with Sleek Edges

  1. Part your hair into small sections, using a rat-tail comb. Apply edge control to your edges and use a toothbrush or edge brush to smooth them down.
  2. Create a small braid at the front of your hairline, using the three-strand method. Use hair gel to smooth down any flyaways or frizz.
  3. Create a cornrow using the same three-strand method, starting at the front of your hairline and working your way back. Use hair gel to smooth down any flyaways or frizz as you go.

FAQs

How long can I keep my braids in?

This depends on your hair type and how well you care for your braids. Generally, you can keep your braids in for up to six weeks.

Can I swim with my braids?

Yes, you can swim with your braids, but make sure to wear a swim cap to protect your hair from chlorine and saltwater. After swimming, rinse your hair with fresh water and apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your braids moisturized.

How do I remove my braids?

To remove your braids, use a pair of scissors to cut the hair ties or elastics at the ends of your braids. Then, gently unravel each braid, using a detangling spray or hair oil to help remove any knots or tangles.
