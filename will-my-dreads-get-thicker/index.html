Will My Dreads Get Thicker Find Out Here

Written by Daniel Jun 14, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle among people with natural hair, and they come with their own unique set of questions and concerns. One of the most common questions people have about their dreadlocks is whether they will get thicker over time. In this article, we will explore the answer to this question and provide you with tips on how to care for your dreadlocks to encourage thickness.

Table of Contents

  • What are dreadlocks?
  • Will my dreadlocks get thicker?
  • How can I make my dreadlocks thicker?
  • Tips for maintaining thick dreadlocks
  • Frequently asked questions
  • Final thoughts

What are dreadlocks?

Dreadlocks, or locs, are a hairstyle that involves matting and twisting sections of hair over time to create long, ropelike strands. This style is often associated with Rastafarianism and has been around for centuries. Dreadlocks can be created with any type of hair, but they are most commonly seen on people with natural, textured hair.

Will my dreadlocks get thicker?

The answer to this question is: it depends. Some people's dreadlocks will naturally become thicker over time as the hair continues to mat and lock together. However, this is not the case for everyone. Some people's dreadlocks may stay the same thickness or even become thinner over time due to breakage or damage.

If you are hoping to encourage your dreadlocks to become thicker, there are a few things you can do:

How can I make my dreadlocks thicker?

  • Be patient: It can take time for your dreadlocks to naturally thicken, so be patient and give them time to mature.
  • Avoid over-manipulation: Constantly retwisting and manipulating your hair can cause damage and breakage, which can lead to thinner dreadlocks.
  • Maintain a healthy diet: Eating a diet rich in vitamins and nutrients can promote healthy hair growth and thickness.
  • Use the right products: Using products specifically designed for dreadlocks, such as locking gels and oils, can help encourage locking and thickness.

Tips for maintaining thick dreadlocks

Once you have achieved the desired thickness for your dreadlocks, it is important to maintain them properly to prevent breakage and thinning. Here are some tips:

  • Maintain a regular washing routine: Keeping your dreadlocks clean is important for preventing buildup and maintaining their health.
  • Avoid using too much product: Overusing products can cause buildup and make your dreadlocks heavy, which can lead to breakage and thinning.
  • Be gentle: Avoid rough towel-drying or pulling your dreadlocks too tightly, as this can cause breakage.
  • Get regular trims: Trimming the ends of your dreadlocks regularly can prevent split ends and breakage.

Frequently asked questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about dreadlocks and thickness:

  • Can I make my dreadlocks thinner? Yes, you can make your dreadlocks thinner by combing them out or cutting them.
  • Can I wear my dreadlocks up? Yes, you can wear your dreadlocks up in a bun, ponytail, or other style. Just be sure not to pull them too tightly, as this can cause breakage.
  • Can I dye my dreadlocks? Yes, you can dye your dreadlocks, but it is important to use a dye specifically designed for dreadlocks and to be gentle during the process to avoid breakage.

Final thoughts

Whether or not your dreadlocks will get thicker over time is largely dependent on your hair type and how well you care for them. By being patient, using the right products, and maintaining a healthy routine, you can encourage your dreadlocks to become thicker and healthier over time. If you have any concerns or questions about your dreadlocks, be sure to consult with a professional stylist or loctician.
