Easy 60S Hairstyles How To Achieve The Retro Look

Written by Frank Jun 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for some easy and fun hairstyles to rock this year, why not take inspiration from the 60s? This decade was known for its iconic fashion and beauty trends, including the bouffant, beehive, and sleek ponytails. In this article, we'll show you how to do easy 60s hairstyles step by step, along with some tips and tricks to make them last all day.

How to do Easy 60s Hairstyles

Before we dive into the tutorials, it's important to prep your hair first. Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and texture. Blow dry your hair using a round brush to add volume at the roots. If you have fine hair, tease the crown area gently to create a bouffant effect.

Bouffant

The bouffant is a classic 60s hairstyle that's perfect for formal events or a night out. Here's how to do it:

  1. Section your hair into three parts: the top, middle, and bottom.
  2. Tease the crown area and backcomb the top section to add volume.
  3. Smooth the top section and secure it with bobby pins.
  4. Gather the middle section and backcomb it slightly.
  5. Smooth the middle section and secure it with bobby pins, tucking the ends under the top section.
  6. Gather the bottom section and curl the ends using a curling iron.
  7. Spray your hair with hairspray to set the style.

To add a modern twist to this classic hairstyle, you can leave some strands loose around your face or add a headband or ribbon.

Beehive

The beehive is another iconic 60s hairstyle that's perfect for special occasions. Here's how to do it:

  1. Tease the crown area and backcomb it to create volume.
  2. Smooth the top layer and secure it with bobby pins.
  3. Gather the sides of your hair and twist them towards the back.
  4. Pin the twisted sections to the back of your head, making sure to hide the bobby pins.
  5. Tease the back section of your hair and smooth it over the pinned sections.
  6. Pin the ends of your hair at the nape of your neck.
  7. Spray your hair with hairspray to set the style.

You can also add a headband or scarf to this hairstyle to add some color and texture.

Sleek Ponytail

The sleek ponytail is a simple yet chic 60s hairstyle that's perfect for everyday wear. Here's how to do it:

  1. Brush your hair back and gather it into a high ponytail.
  2. Secure the ponytail with a hair tie.
  3. Wrap a small section of hair around the hair tie and secure it with bobby pins.
  4. Smooth the front section of your hair and pin it back to create a sleek look.
  5. Spray your hair with hairspray to set the style.

You can also add some volume to the crown area by teasing it before gathering your hair into a ponytail.

Tips for Easy 60s Hairstyles

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect 60s hairstyle:

  • Use a volumizing mousse or spray to add body and texture to your hair.
  • Tease your hair gently to avoid damaging it.
  • Use bobby pins that match your hair color to hide them.
  • Wrap a scarf or headband around your hair to add some color and texture.
  • Use hairspray to set your hairstyle and prevent it from falling flat.

FAQ:

Q: Can I do these hairstyles on short hair?

A: Yes, you can modify these hairstyles to suit shorter hair lengths. For example, you can create a mini bouffant or beehive for a retro vibe.

Q: How can I make my 60s hairstyle last all day?

A: Use a strong hold hairspray and bobby pins to secure your hairstyle in place. You can also touch up your hairstyle throughout the day if needed.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and shiny, make sure to use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat on your hair. You can also use a hair mask once a week to deep condition your hair.

With these easy 60s hairstyles, you can channel your inner retro queen and rock a classic look that's both stylish and fun. Whether you're attending a wedding, a party, or just want to switch up your everyday look, these hairstyles are sure to turn heads.
