How Long Does It Take To Recover From Postpartum Hair Loss

Written by Eveline Jun 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Postpartum hair loss is a common phenomenon that occurs after giving birth. During pregnancy, a woman's hormone levels increase, which can lead to thicker, fuller hair. However, after giving birth, hormone levels drop, and many women experience hair loss. This can be a stressful and overwhelming experience for new mothers, but the good news is that postpartum hair loss is temporary. In this article, we will discuss how long it takes to recover from postpartum hair loss, and provide some tips on how to care for your hair during this time.

How to Deal with Postpartum Hair Loss

Postpartum hair loss can be a frustrating experience, but there are some things you can do to minimize hair loss and promote hair growth. One of the most important things you can do is to maintain a healthy diet. Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. You should also avoid harsh hair treatments, such as coloring or perming, as these can damage your hair and make hair loss worse.

Another way to deal with postpartum hair loss is to style your hair in a way that minimizes the appearance of hair loss. You can try wearing your hair in a ponytail or bun, or using hair accessories to create volume and thickness. You can also try using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to help give your hair some extra lift.

Step-by-Step Guide to Recovering from Postpartum Hair Loss

While postpartum hair loss is temporary, it can take some time for your hair to grow back fully. The amount of time it takes to recover from postpartum hair loss varies from person to person, but most women will start to see some regrowth within six months of giving birth. Here is a step-by-step guide to help you recover from postpartum hair loss:

  1. Be patient. Postpartum hair loss is temporary, and your hair will grow back.
  2. Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  3. Avoid harsh hair treatments, such as coloring or perming.
  4. Style your hair in a way that minimizes the appearance of hair loss.
  5. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to help give your hair some extra lift.
  6. Consider taking a hair growth supplement, such as biotin or folic acid.
  7. Get regular haircuts to help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  8. Be gentle when brushing and styling your hair to avoid further damage.
  9. Consider talking to your doctor if you are experiencing excessive hair loss or other hair-related issues.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair During Postpartum Hair Loss

While postpartum hair loss can be a frustrating experience, there are some things you can do to care for your hair during this time. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as curling irons or straighteners
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet, as this can cause breakage
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner to help nourish your hair

FAQs

How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for six to twelve months after giving birth. However, it can take up to two years for your hair to fully recover.

Is postpartum hair loss normal?

Yes, postpartum hair loss is a normal phenomenon that affects many women after giving birth. It is caused by hormonal changes in the body and is typically temporary.

Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

While postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, there are some things you can do to minimize hair loss and promote hair growth. Maintaining a healthy diet, avoiding harsh hair treatments, and using gentle hair care products can all help.
