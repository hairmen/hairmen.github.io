How To Thicken One Side Of Your Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Oct 02, 2023 · 5 min read

If you’ve noticed that one side of your hair looks thinner than the other, don’t worry – you’re not alone. Many people experience this issue, and it can be frustrating to deal with. But there are steps you can take to thicken one side of your hair and achieve a more balanced look.

How to Thicken One Side of Your Hair

Before we dive into the tips and tricks, it’s important to understand that hair thickness is largely determined by genetics. However, lifestyle factors such as diet and stress can also play a role. Here’s what you can do to thicken one side of your hair:

1. Change Your Parting

One of the easiest ways to make one side of your hair look thicker is to change your parting. If you usually part your hair on the thin side, try switching to the other side instead. This will give the illusion of more volume on the thin side.

2. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Look for hair products that are specifically designed to add volume and thickness. Volumizing shampoos and conditioners contain ingredients that coat the hair shaft, making it look thicker and fuller. Be sure to apply the products only to the roots and scalp, as applying them to the ends can weigh down your hair.

3. Try a Hair Thickening Treatment

There are several hair thickening treatments available, such as hair fibers and thickening sprays. These products work by attaching to the hair shaft, creating the illusion of thicker hair. Be sure to choose a product that matches your hair color for a natural look.

4. Get a Haircut

A haircut can also help to make one side of your hair look thicker. Ask your hairstylist for layers or textured cuts, which can add volume and body to your hair. Avoid blunt cuts, as they can make your hair look flat and lifeless.

5. Use Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools such as curling irons and straighteners can also help to add volume to your hair. Use a round brush when blow-drying your hair to create more lift at the roots. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to thicken one side of your hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid any product buildup.

Step 2: Change Your Parting

Switch your parting to the other side to create the illusion of thicker hair.

Step 3: Apply a Hair Thickening Treatment

If desired, apply a hair thickening treatment to the thin side of your hair. Follow the instructions on the product label.

Step 4: Blow-Dry Your Hair

Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to create lift at the roots. Focus on the thin side of your hair, using the brush to lift the hair away from the scalp.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Use heat styling tools such as curling irons or straighteners to create volume and texture. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

Tips for Thicker Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve thicker, fuller hair:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Make sure you’re getting enough vitamins and nutrients in your diet, as these can affect the health of your hair. Focus on foods that are high in protein, such as eggs and lean meats.

2. Reduce Stress

Stress can affect your hair health, so try to find ways to relax and reduce stress levels. Exercise, meditation, and deep breathing techniques can all be helpful.

3. Avoid Overstyling

Overstyling your hair with heat tools and chemicals can damage your hair and make it look thin and lifeless. Try to limit your use of these products and opt for natural styling methods when possible.

FAQs

Q: Can hair thickness change over time?

A: Yes, hair thickness can change over time due to a variety of factors such as aging, hormones, and lifestyle changes.

Q: Can I thicken my hair naturally?

A: Yes, there are several natural remedies that can help to thicken your hair. These include scalp massages, essential oils, and a healthy diet.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair or sweat a lot, you may need to wash your hair more frequently. However, washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, so aim for every other day or every few days if possible.

Hair Care for Thicker Hair

Once you’ve achieved thicker hair, it’s important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips for caring for thick hair:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as this will be gentler on your hair than a brush. Start at the ends and work your way up to prevent breakage.

2. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks can help to prevent split ends and breakage, keeping your hair looking healthy and thick.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools to prevent damage to your hair. Try to limit your use of these tools and opt for natural styling methods when possible.

4. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to prevent hair breakage and frizz, keeping your hair looking smooth and healthy.

Conclusion

Thick, voluminous hair is something many of us strive for, but achieving it can be a challenge. However, by following the tips and tricks outlined in this article, you can thicken one side of your hair and achieve a more balanced look. Remember to take care of your hair properly to maintain its health and appearance, and always consult with a hairstylist or dermatologist if you have concerns about your hair.
