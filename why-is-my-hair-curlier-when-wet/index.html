Why Is My Hair Curlier When Wet Explained

Written by Oliver Jul 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you one of those people who have noticed that their hair becomes curlier when wet? This is a common phenomenon that many people experience. In this article, we will explain why this happens, how to manage it, and some tips for taking care of your hair.

How to Recognize Curly Hair?

Curly hair can be identified by its texture, which ranges from tightly coiled to wavy. The curl pattern can also vary depending on the person's ethnicity, gender, and age. Curly hair is often dry and prone to frizz, which makes it challenging to manage.

What Causes Curly Hair?

Curly hair is caused by the shape of the hair follicles. The follicles of curly hair are more elliptical than straight hair, which makes the hair strand bend and curl. The amount of curl is determined by the number of disulfide bonds in the hair, which is determined by genetics.

Why is My Hair Curlier When Wet?

When hair is wet, it absorbs water and swells. This swelling causes the hair shaft to stretch and straighten out, which can make it appear less curly. However, as the hair dries, it returns to its original shape, which can make it appear curlier. The amount of curl that the hair has when wet depends on the person's curl pattern and the amount of water that the hair absorbs.

How to Manage Curly Hair?

Managing curly hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, it can be done. Here are some tips for managing curly hair:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Curly hair is prone to tangling, so it is best to use a wide-tooth comb to detangle it. This will help prevent breakage and damage to the hair.

2. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Curly hair is often dry, so it is essential to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Leave-in conditioner can help keep curly hair moisturized throughout the day. Apply a small amount to damp hair and distribute it evenly through the hair.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage curly hair, so it is best to avoid it. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair gently.

FAQ

Q: Can I Straighten My Curly Hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but it is essential to use heat protectant products to prevent damage to your hair.

Q: Can I Dye My Curly Hair?

A: Yes, you can dye your curly hair, but it is essential to use hair dye products that are designed for curly hair. Look for products that contain moisturizing ingredients to prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your curly hair:

1. Wash Your Hair Every Few Days

Curly hair should not be washed every day, as this can strip the hair of its natural oils. Instead, wash your hair every few days or as needed.

2. Sleep on a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help prevent frizz and breakage, as it is gentler on the hair than cotton.

3. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Conclusion

In conclusion, your hair appears curlier when wet because the hair shaft swells and stretches when it absorbs water. Managing curly hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, it can be done. Remember to take care of your curly hair by using moisturizing products and avoiding heat styling.
