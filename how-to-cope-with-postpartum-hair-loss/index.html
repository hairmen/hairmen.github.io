How To Cope With Postpartum Hair Loss

Written by Eveline Jun 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have recently given birth, you may have noticed that you are shedding more hair than usual. This is a common occurrence known as postpartum hair loss, and it can be frustrating and concerning for many new mothers. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to cope with postpartum hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. In this article, we will cover everything you need to know about postpartum hair loss, including how to cope with it and maintain healthy hair.

What is Postpartum Hair Loss?

Postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition that affects many new mothers. It usually occurs between three and six months after giving birth and can last up to a year. During this time, you may notice an increase in hair shedding, which can be alarming. However, it is important to remember that postpartum hair loss is a normal part of the postpartum experience, and it will eventually subside.

Why Does Postpartum Hair Loss Occur?

Postpartum hair loss occurs because of hormonal changes in your body. During pregnancy, your body produces higher levels of estrogen, which helps to keep your hair in the growing phase for longer. After giving birth, your estrogen levels drop dramatically, causing more hair to enter the resting phase and eventually fall out. This is a normal part of the hair growth cycle, but it can be more noticeable after pregnancy.

How to Cope with Postpartum Hair Loss

While postpartum hair loss can be frustrating, there are steps you can take to cope with it and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips:

  1. Be gentle with your hair. Avoid tight hairstyles, excessive heat styling, and harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.
  2. Eat a healthy diet. Make sure you are getting enough vitamins and minerals, such as iron, zinc, and biotin, to support healthy hair growth.
  3. Take care of your scalp. Keep your scalp clean and moisturized to promote healthy hair growth. You can try using a scalp treatment or massaging your scalp to increase blood flow.
  4. Consider using hair growth products. There are many hair growth products on the market that can help stimulate hair growth and reduce hair loss. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, caffeine, and minoxidil.
  5. Be patient. Postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition, and your hair will eventually grow back. It may take several months to a year for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy state, but it will happen.

Postpartum Hair Loss Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you cope with postpartum hair loss:

  • Avoid wearing hats or scarves that are too tight, as this can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Use a detangling brush or wide-tooth comb to gently remove tangles and avoid pulling on your hair.
  • Avoid using tight elastics or hair ties, as these can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to remove split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

Postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, but you can take steps to promote healthy hair growth and reduce hair loss.

How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss usually lasts between three and six months, but it can last up to a year.

Will my hair grow back after postpartum hair loss?

Yes, your hair will grow back after postpartum hair loss. It may take several months to a year for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy state, but it will happen.

Hair Care

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your hair during postpartum hair loss:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is free from sulfates and parabens.
  • Avoid using excessive heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling wands, which can damage your hair.
  • Try using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair and promote healthy growth.
  • Consider taking a hair growth supplement or vitamin to support healthy hair growth.

By following these tips, you can cope with postpartum hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and don't hesitate to reach out to a hair care professional if you have any concerns.
