How To Cut Short Hair With Cowlicks

Written by Robby Jul 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short hair with cowlicks, you may have experienced difficulty in finding the right haircut that works for you. Cowlicks are stubborn hair strands that grow in a different direction from the rest of your hair, which can make it challenging to achieve a polished look. In this article, we will show you how to cut short hair with cowlicks, including tips and tricks for maintaining a sleek hairstyle.

Description

Cowlicks are a common hair problem that affects both men and women. They are caused by the natural growth pattern of your hair, which can be influenced by genetics, hormones, and environmental factors. Cowlicks can be found anywhere on your head, but they are most noticeable in areas where your hair is short or styled in a certain way. Cowlicks can make it difficult to style your hair, but with the right haircut and styling techniques, you can achieve a sleek and stylish look.

How to Cut Short Hair with Cowlicks

Cutting short hair with cowlicks requires a bit of skill and patience. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Choose the Right Haircut

The first step to cutting short hair with cowlicks is to choose the right haircut. The best haircut for cowlicks is one that works with your natural hair growth pattern. For example, if you have a cowlick in the front of your hair, you may want to consider a side-swept fringe or a textured crop. If you have a cowlick on the crown of your head, you may want to choose a layered haircut that adds volume and movement to your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Once you have chosen the right haircut, you will need to section your hair. Use hair clips or elastics to divide your hair into manageable sections. This will help you to cut your hair evenly and avoid chopping off too much hair at once.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Start by cutting the back of your hair first. Use a pair of sharp scissors and cut small sections of hair at a time. Be sure to cut in a straight line and avoid cutting at an angle. Once the back of your hair is cut, move on to the sides and then the front. Take your time and be patient. It's better to cut a little bit at a time than to cut off too much hair.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

After you have finished cutting your hair, check your progress in the mirror. Look for any uneven spots or areas that need to be trimmed. Use your scissors to clean up any stray hairs or uneven spots.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for cutting short hair with cowlicks:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take your time and be patient.
  • Section your hair to make it easier to cut.
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time.
  • Check your progress in the mirror as you go.
  • Ask for help if you are unsure about cutting your hair.

FAQ

Q: Can you cut hair with cowlicks without going to a salon?

A: Yes, you can cut hair with cowlicks at home. However, it's important to have the right tools and to take your time. If you are unsure about cutting your hair, it may be best to go to a salon.

Q: What is the best haircut for cowlicks?

A: The best haircut for cowlicks is one that works with your natural hair growth pattern. A layered haircut with textured ends can help to add volume and movement to your hair while camouflaging cowlicks.

Q: How can I style my hair with cowlicks?

A: To style your hair with cowlicks, use a hair dryer and a round brush to smooth out your hair. You can also use a styling product, such as a wax or pomade, to help control your cowlicks.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair with cowlicks, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain a healthy and polished look. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as a hair straightener or curling iron, too often.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using too much styling product, which can weigh down your hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut short hair with cowlicks and achieve a sleek and stylish look. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With practice, you can become an expert at cutting short hair with cowlicks!
