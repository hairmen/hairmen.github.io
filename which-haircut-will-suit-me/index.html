Which Haircut Will Suit Me Tips And Tricks For Finding Your Perfect Style

Written by Oliver Jul 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your current hairstyle and looking to switch things up? Finding the right haircut can be a daunting task, but with a few tips and tricks, you can discover the perfect style that will complement your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. In this article, we will explore how to determine which haircut will suit you and offer some hair care tips to keep your new style looking fresh and vibrant.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before selecting a new haircut, it's essential to determine your face shape. Knowing your face shape will help you choose a style that enhances your features and flatters your natural beauty. There are six primary face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and examine the shape of your face in the mirror.

Oval: An oval face shape is longer than it is wide and has a slightly rounded jawline. This face shape is versatile and can pull off most hairstyles.

Round: A round face shape has equal width and length, with a softer jawline and fuller cheeks. Opt for hairstyles that create volume on top to elongate the face.

Square: A square face shape has a prominent jawline and forehead, with equal width and length. Look for hairstyles that soften the angles of the face.

Heart: A heart-shaped face has a wider forehead and a narrow jawline. Layered haircuts work well with this face shape.

Diamond: A diamond-shaped face has a narrow forehead and jawline, with prominent cheekbones. Hairstyles that create volume at the crown or chin-length bobs work well with this face shape.

Oblong: An oblong face shape is longer than it is wide, with a straight jawline. Look for hairstyles that add volume to the sides to give the illusion of width.

How to Determine Your Hair Texture

In addition to knowing your face shape, it's essential to determine your hair texture. Hair texture refers to the thickness and coarseness of your hair. There are three primary hair textures: fine, medium, and coarse.

Fine: Fine hair is delicate and thin, with a silky texture. Short hairstyles work well with this hair texture.

Medium: Medium hair is thicker than fine hair and has more body. Medium hair can pull off most hairstyles.

Coarse: Coarse hair is thick and heavy, with a rough texture. Layered haircuts work well with this hair texture.

Tips for Choosing the Right Haircut

Now that you know your face shape and hair texture, it's time to choose the right haircut. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose a style that complements your face shape and hair texture.
  • Consider your lifestyle and how much time you can dedicate to styling your hair.
  • Bring pictures of hairstyles you like to your hairstylist for inspiration.
  • Ask your hairstylist for their professional opinion on what will work best for you.

FAQ

Q: What if I don't like my new haircut?

A: If you're unhappy with your new haircut, communicate with your hairstylist and explain what you don't like. They may be able to make adjustments or suggest a new style that works better for you.

Q: Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail with a short haircut?

A: Yes! Short hair can still be styled in a ponytail or updo with the help of hair accessories and styling products.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've found the perfect haircut, it's essential to take care of your hair to keep it looking fresh and vibrant. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that is suited for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as it can cause damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can find the perfect haircut that suits your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. Remember to communicate with your hairstylist and take care of your hair to keep it looking fresh and vibrant. Happy hairstyling!
