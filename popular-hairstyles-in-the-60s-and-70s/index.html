Description

The 60s and 70s were a time of great change and experimentation, particularly in the world of fashion and beauty. This was reflected in the popular hairstyles of the time, which ranged from perfectly coiffed bouffants to long, unkempt locks.

Some of the most iconic hairstyles of the era are still popular today, such as the afro and the shag. Whether you're looking for inspiration for a retro-themed party or just want to switch up your look, here's everything you need to know about popular hairstyles in the 60s and 70s.

How to Achieve Popular Hairstyles in the 60s and 70s

If you're looking to achieve a retro look, there are a few key elements you'll need to consider:

  • The length of your hair
  • The texture of your hair
  • The tools and products you'll need

Depending on the style you're going for, you may need to invest in a good quality curling iron, hairspray, and perhaps even a wig or hair extensions.

Length

The length of your hair is one of the most important factors to consider when choosing a retro hairstyle. In the 60s, shorter styles were popular, such as the pixie cut made famous by Twiggy. In the 70s, longer hair was all the rage, with styles ranging from bohemian waves to straight, sleek locks.

Texture

The texture of your hair will also play a big role in determining which styles will work for you. If you have naturally curly hair, you may want to lean into that and go for a full, voluminous afro. If your hair is naturally straight, you may need to use a curling iron or hot rollers to achieve the desired look.

Tools and Products

To achieve popular hairstyles in the 60s and 70s, you may need to invest in some specialized tools and products. Here are a few essentials:

  • A good quality curling iron or hot rollers
  • Hairspray
  • Bobby pins and hair ties
  • Hair extensions or a wig, if needed

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to achieving some of the most popular hairstyles in the 60s and 70s:

Bouffant

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Tease the crown of your hair to create volume.
  3. Smooth out the top layer of hair with a brush.
  4. Gather your hair at the back of your head and secure it with bobby pins or a hair tie.
  5. Spray with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Afro

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section off your hair and use a curling iron or hot rollers to create tight curls.
  3. Use a pick to comb out the curls and create volume.
  4. Spray with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Shag

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Part your hair down the middle.
  3. Use a razor comb to create choppy layers throughout your hair.
  4. Flip your hair upside down and scrunch it with your hands to create texture.
  5. Spray with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Maintaining Popular Hairstyles in the 60s and 70s

Here are a few tips for keeping your retro hairstyle looking great:

  • Use a good quality hairspray to hold your style in place.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it harder to style.
  • If you're using heat tools to style your hair, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.
  • Consider investing in a silk or satin pillowcase, which can help prevent frizz and breakage.

FAQs

What were some of the most popular hairstyles in the 60s?

Some of the most popular hairstyles in the 60s included the bouffant, pixie cut, and beehive.

What were some of the most popular hairstyles in the 70s?

Some of the most popular hairstyles in the 70s included the shag, afro, and long, straight hair.

Can I achieve a retro hairstyle if I have short hair?

Absolutely! There are plenty of retro-inspired short hairstyles, such as the pixie cut and the bowl cut.

Hair Care in the 60s and 70s

Hair care in the 60s and 70s was all about achieving the perfect balance of volume and texture. Popular products included mousse, hairspray, and hot rollers.

Women in the 60s and 70s also tended to use fewer hair care products overall, as the focus was on achieving a more natural, effortless look.

Overall, popular hairstyles in the 60s and 70s were all about embracing individuality and self-expression. Whether you're looking to channel a specific decade or just want to try something new, there's a retro hairstyle out there for everyone.
