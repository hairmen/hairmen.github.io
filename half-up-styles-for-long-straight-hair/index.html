10 Half Up Styles For Long Straight Hair

Written by Robby Sep 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a hairstyle that will keep your long straight hair out of your face but still allow you to show off your gorgeous locks, then half up styles might be the perfect solution for you. In this article, we'll go over 10 different half up styles that are perfect for long straight hair. Whether you're going for a casual look or a more formal vibe, there's a half up style that will work for you.

Description

Half up styles are a great way to keep your hair out of your face while still showing off your length. They work well for a variety of occasions, from casual outings to formal events. With long straight hair, you have a lot of options when it comes to half up styles. You can choose to keep it simple with a basic half up ponytail or add some braids or twists for a more intricate look.

How To

Depending on the half up style you choose, the steps will vary. However, here is a basic guide to creating a half up style for long straight hair:

  1. Brush your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Section off the top half of your hair, from ear to ear.
  3. Gather the top section of your hair and secure it with a hair tie, leaving the bottom half of your hair down.
  4. For added volume, tease the crown of your hair before securing it with a hair tie.
  5. Optional: add braids, twists, or other embellishments to the top section of your hair.

That's it! With these simple steps, you can create a variety of half up styles for your long straight hair.

Step by Step

Let's take a closer look at some specific half up styles for long straight hair:

1. Half Up Top Knot

To create this stylish half up top knot:

  1. Follow the basic steps for creating a half up style (as outlined above).
  2. Twist the top section of your hair into a bun and secure it with bobby pins.
  3. Optional: add some hairspray or texturizing spray for added hold and volume.

This is a great option for a casual day out or a low-key event.

2. Half Up Fishtail Braid

If you want to add some texture and visual interest to your half up style, try a fishtail braid:

  1. Follow the basic steps for creating a half up style.
  2. Divide the top section of your hair into two equal parts.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the outside of one part and cross it over to the inside of the other part.
  4. Repeat this process on the other side, crossing a small section of hair from the outside of one part to the inside of the other.
  5. Continue braiding in this way until you reach the end of your hair.
  6. Secure the braid with a hair tie.

This half up style is perfect for a boho-chic look or a summer festival.

3. Half Up Dutch Braid

If you want a more intricate braid for your half up style, try a Dutch braid:

  1. Follow the basic steps for creating a half up style.
  2. Begin braiding at the crown of your head, taking three small sections of hair and crossing them over each other.
  3. As you continue braiding, add small sections of hair to the braid from each side.
  4. Continue braiding until you reach the end of your hair, then secure the braid with a hair tie.

This half up style is perfect for a more formal occasion, such as a wedding or prom.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when creating half up styles for your long straight hair:

  • Experiment with different braids and twists to find the style that works best for you.
  • Use bobby pins and hairspray to secure your half up style in place.
  • Consider adding accessories like hair clips or flower crowns to your half up style for a unique touch.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about half up styles for long straight hair:

What face shapes work best with half up styles?

Half up styles work well for a variety of face shapes, but they are particularly flattering for those with oval or heart-shaped faces.

Can you create a half up style with short hair?

While it may be more challenging to create a half up style with short hair, it is still possible. You may need to use more bobby pins and hairspray to keep your style in place.

Hair Care

When creating half up styles for your long straight hair, it's important to take care of your locks to keep them healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and nourished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons or straighteners too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a hair mask or treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

With these tips and tricks, you can create beautiful half up styles for your long straight hair that will turn heads wherever you go!
