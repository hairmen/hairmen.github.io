How To Cut Your Own High Top Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 24, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of going to the barbershop every few weeks to maintain your high top fade? With a little practice and patience, you can learn how to cut your own high top fade at home. Not only will you save money, but you'll also have complete control over the length and shape of your fade. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide, tips, and answers to frequently asked questions to help you achieve a fresh and clean high top fade.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Your Own High Top Fade

Cutting your own high top fade requires the right equipment, patience, and practice. Before you get started, make sure you have the following:

  • Clippers with guards
  • A comb
  • A mirror or two
  • Scissors
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A towel or cape to catch hair clippings

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow the steps below to cut your own high top fade:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner. Dry your hair with a towel or blow dryer until it's completely dry.

Step 2: Decide on the Length of Your Fade

Use the clippers with the appropriate guard to determine the length of your fade. Start with a larger guard, such as a #3, and work your way down to a smaller guard, such as a #1 or #0.5, for a more gradual fade.

Step 3: Create a Guideline

Use the clippers with the appropriate guard to create a guideline around your head. This line will help you to maintain a consistent length throughout your fade. Start at the bottom of your hairline and work your way up, using the comb to lift sections of hair as you go.

Step 4: Blend the Sides and Back

Using a smaller guard, such as a #1 or #0.5, blend the sides and back of your hair with the guideline you created in step 3. Use the clippers to create a smooth transition from the shorter hair at the bottom to the longer hair at the top.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Use scissors to cut the hair on top of your head to the desired length. The length will depend on your personal preference, but most high top fades have at least 2-3 inches of hair on top.

Step 6: Shape the Top

Use the comb and scissors to shape the top of your hair. Use the comb to lift sections of hair and the scissors to cut the hair to the desired shape.

Step 7: Clean Up the Edges

Use the clippers without a guard to clean up the edges around your hairline and ears. Be careful not to cut too close to your skin.

Step 8: Check for Any Uneven Spots

Use the mirror to check for any uneven spots or missed spots. Use the clippers or scissors to fix any issues.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

Use a comb or your fingers to style your hair. You can use hair products such as gel, pomade, or wax to hold the hair in place.

Step 10: Clean Up

Use a towel or cape to catch any hair clippings. Sweep or vacuum the area to clean up any remaining hair.

Tips for Cutting Your Own High Top Fade

  • Start with a larger guard and work your way down to a smaller guard for a more gradual fade.
  • Take your time and don't rush. It's better to take longer and get it right than to rush and make mistakes.
  • Use a mirror or two to check your work from different angles.
  • Watch videos or read tutorials to learn different techniques and styles.
  • Practice makes perfect. Don't be discouraged if your first attempt isn't perfect.

FAQs

Can I cut my own high top fade without clippers?

No, clippers are necessary for cutting a high top fade. You can purchase clippers at most beauty supply stores or online.

What length should I cut the hair on top of my head?

The length will depend on your personal preference, but most high top fades have at least 2-3 inches of hair on top.

How often should I cut my high top fade?

It's recommended to cut your high top fade every 2-4 weeks to maintain the shape and length.

Hair Care for High Top Fades

In addition to cutting your own high top fade, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Follow these tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers or straighteners.
  • Use hair products that are specifically designed for your hair type and style.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles or pulling your hair too tightly, as this can cause damage to your hair and scalp.

Cutting your own high top fade may seem intimidating at first, but with practice and the right tools, you can achieve a fresh and clean look from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, follow the steps, and don't be afraid to experiment with different techniques and styles.
