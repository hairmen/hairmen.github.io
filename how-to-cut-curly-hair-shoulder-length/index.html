How To Cut Curly Hair Shoulder Length A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Nov 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have curly hair and are considering cutting it shoulder length, you've come to the right place. Cutting curly hair can be tricky, but with the right technique and some tips and tricks, you can achieve a beautiful shoulder-length cut that will enhance your curls and frame your face. In this article, we'll take you through the steps needed to cut your curly hair shoulder length.

Description of Shoulder-Length Cut for Curly Hair

A shoulder-length cut for curly hair is a great way to show off your curls while keeping your hair manageable. This length allows for more styling options while still maintaining a healthy length. The key to a great shoulder-length cut for curly hair is to make sure the layers are cut in a way that enhances your curls and frames your face.

How to Cut Curly Hair Shoulder Length

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb or brush
  • Clips to hold sections of hair in place
  • Spray bottle with water

Step 2: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will ensure that your hair is clean and free from tangles. After you've washed and conditioned your hair, gently towel dry your hair to remove excess water.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle your hair. Be gentle and start at the ends of your hair, working your way up to the roots. Be sure to remove any knots or tangles to ensure an even cut.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use clips to hold each section in place.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Start with the back sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut straight across, making sure to cut at an angle so that the hair is longer in the front and shorter in the back. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you've cut all of the back sections of your hair.

Step 6: Cut the Front Sections

Once you've cut the back sections of your hair, move on to the front sections. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, making sure that it is longer in the front and shorter in the back. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you've cut all of the front sections of your hair.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

Once you've cut all of your hair, check for evenness. Look at your hair in a mirror and make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your hair is even.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut your hair, it's time to style it. Use a curl-enhancing product to enhance your curls and add definition. You can also blow-dry your hair with a diffuser attachment to enhance your curls.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair Shoulder Length

  • Start by cutting less hair than you think you need. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting curly hair requires more care and attention than cutting straight hair.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress. Check your hair from all angles to ensure an even cut.
  • Consider getting a professional haircut if you're not comfortable cutting your hair yourself.

FAQ About Cutting Curly Hair Shoulder Length

Can I cut my curly hair shoulder length myself?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair shoulder length yourself. However, if you're not comfortable cutting your own hair, it's best to seek the help of a professional.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended that you cut your curly hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I still straighten my curly hair after cutting it shoulder length?

Yes, you can still straighten your curly hair after cutting it shoulder length. However, keep in mind that straightening your hair can cause damage and should be done sparingly.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair requires some extra attention. Here are some tips for keeping your curls healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-enhancing product to your hair to enhance your curls.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can cause damage to your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve a beautiful shoulder-length cut for your curly hair. Remember to be patient and take your time, and don't be afraid to seek the help of a professional if you need it. Happy cutting!
