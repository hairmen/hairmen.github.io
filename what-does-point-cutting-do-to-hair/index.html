What Does Point Cutting Do To Hair

Written by Frank Jun 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your hair looking dull and lifeless? Do you want to add some texture and movement to your locks? If so, point cutting may be the solution you’ve been looking for. In this article, we’ll explore what point cutting does to hair, how it’s done, and share some tips and FAQs to help you achieve your desired hair look.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How To
  3. Step By Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Point cutting is a hair cutting technique where the stylist uses scissors to cut the hair at an angle, creating textured ends. This technique is also known as texturizing, feathering, or razor cutting. Point cutting is mostly used to add movement and texture to the hair, and it’s suitable for all hair types and lengths.

Point cutting works by creating different lengths of hair at the ends, resulting in a choppy and edgy look. The technique can also remove bulk and weight from the hair, making it easier to style and manage. The stylist may use different techniques such as freehand cutting or using a comb to guide the scissors, depending on the desired outcome.

How To Point Cut Hair

Point cutting can be done by a professional stylist or at home, but it’s recommended to seek professional help if you’re unsure of what to do. Here are some steps on how to point cut hair:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly, so it’s easier to work with. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Divide your hair into sections

Using a comb, divide your hair into sections, depending on how much hair you want to cut. It’s recommended to start with small sections, so you have better control over the cutting.

Step 3: Hold the hair at an angle

Hold the hair at an angle and point cut using scissors. The angle you hold the hair at will determine the length of the cut. It’s recommended to cut at a 45-degree angle for a subtle textured look.

Step 4: Repeat on all sections

Repeat the process on all sections of your hair until you achieve the desired look. Remember to cut slowly and progressively, so you don’t cut off too much hair.

Tips for Point Cutting Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when point cutting your hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and gradually work your way up to larger sections
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and damage to your hair
  • Don’t cut too much hair at once, as this can result in an uneven and choppy look
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head and make sure the length is even
  • Experiment with different angles and techniques to achieve your desired look

FAQs

What is the difference between point cutting and razor cutting?

Point cutting is done with scissors, while razor cutting is done with a razor blade. Razor cutting creates a softer and more feathered look, while point cutting creates a more edgy and choppy look.

Is point cutting suitable for all hair types?

Yes, point cutting is suitable for all hair types and lengths. However, it’s recommended to consult with a professional stylist to determine if point cutting is the best technique for your hair.

How often should you point cut your hair?

It’s recommended to point cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the desired look and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Point Cutting

After point cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain the desired look. Here are some tips for hair care after point cutting:

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized
  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently, as this can damage your hair
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain the desired length
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet, as this can cause breakage and damage
  • Apply a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to keep your hair nourished and hydrated

Point cutting is a versatile hair cutting technique that can add texture and movement to your locks. Whether you want a subtle or edgy look, point cutting can help you achieve your desired hair look. Remember to follow the tips and take care of your hair after point cutting to maintain the desired look and keep your hair healthy.
