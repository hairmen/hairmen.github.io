Should Dreads Be Soft A Comprehensive Guide To Dreadlock Maintenance

Written by Daniel Oct 22, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for ages. While some people prefer their dreads to be stiff and tightly coiled, others believe that they should be soft to the touch. This article will explore this topic and provide tips on how to maintain soft, healthy dreads.

What Are Dreadlocks?

Dreadlocks, or simply dreads, are a hairstyle that involves matted hair. The hair is usually sectioned off and left to grow naturally without being combed, brushed, or washed frequently. Over time, the hair will tangle and create thick, rope-like strands that are characteristic of dreadlocks.

How Are Dreadlocks Made?

There are several ways to create dreadlocks, including backcombing, twisting, and the neglect method. The neglect method involves simply letting the hair grow without any maintenance. The hair will eventually tangle and form dreadlocks on its own.

Should Dreads Be Soft?

There is no right or wrong answer to this question. Some people prefer their dreads to be stiff and tightly coiled, while others prefer them to be soft and pliable. Ultimately, it comes down to personal preference and the type of hairstyle you are trying to achieve.

How to Make Dreads Soft

If you prefer your dreads to be soft and pliable, there are several things you can do to achieve this. First, wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo. This will help to keep your hair clean and prevent buildup, which can make your dreads stiff and heavy.

Second, use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized. This will help to prevent dryness and breakage, which can make your dreads feel rough and brittle.

Third, consider using a dreadlock wax or oil to help keep your hair soft and pliable. These products can help to lock in moisture and prevent your hair from becoming too stiff or dry.

How to Maintain Soft Dreads

Once you have achieved soft, pliable dreads, it is important to maintain them properly. Here are some tips to help you keep your dreads looking and feeling their best:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat on your hair
  • Be gentle when styling your hair to prevent breakage
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

Frequently Asked Questions

How Often Should You Wash Your Dreads?

You should wash your dreads at least once a week with a residue-free shampoo. This will help to keep your hair clean and prevent buildup, which can make your dreads stiff and heavy.

Can You Use Regular Shampoo on Dreads?

No, you should not use regular shampoo on your dreads. Regular shampoo contains chemicals that can leave behind residue and make your dreads stiff and heavy. Instead, use a residue-free shampoo that is specifically designed for dreadlocks.

Can You Straighten Dreads?

No, you should not straighten your dreads. Straightening can damage your hair and cause it to become brittle and break off. If you want to change the look of your dreads, consider styling them in a different way or adding accessories like beads or wraps.

Conclusion

In conclusion, whether or not your dreads should be soft is a matter of personal preference. If you prefer soft, pliable dreads, be sure to wash your hair regularly, use a leave-in conditioner, and consider using a dreadlock wax or oil to help lock in moisture. With proper maintenance, you can enjoy healthy, beautiful dreads for years to come.
