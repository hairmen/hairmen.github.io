How To Tone Orange Hair After Bleaching A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have recently bleached your hair and ended up with orange tones, don't worry - it's a common problem. Bleaching can often leave your hair with unwanted orange, yellow or brassy tones. However, there are several ways to tone orange hair after bleaching. In this article, we will discuss various methods to help you achieve your desired hair color.

Table of Contents

  • How to Tone Orange Hair After Bleaching
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Toning Orange Hair
  • Tips for Toning Orange Hair

How to Tone Orange Hair After Bleaching

The first step to toning orange hair after bleaching is to use a toner. A toner is a semi-permanent hair color that helps to neutralize unwanted shades in your hair. When selecting a toner, choose one in the opposite color of the unwanted tones. For example, if your hair has orange tones, choose a toner with blue or purple undertones.

Another option is to use a hair dye with ash tones. Ash tones contain green and blue pigments that help to neutralize orange tones in your hair. Make sure to choose a dye with the right level of ash - too much can make your hair look gray.

If you don't want to use a toner or dye, you can try using a purple shampoo. Purple shampoo is a violet-tinted shampoo that helps to neutralize yellow tones in your hair. Apply the shampoo to wet hair, leave it on for a few minutes, then rinse it out.

Step-by-Step Guide to Toning Orange Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide to toning orange hair after bleaching:

  1. Choose a toner, hair dye, or purple shampoo with the right undertones.
  2. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup and oils.
  3. Apply the toner, hair dye, or purple shampoo to your hair, following the instructions on the package.
  4. Leave the toner, hair dye, or purple shampoo on your hair for the recommended time. Don't leave it on for too long, or it may damage your hair.
  5. Rinse out the toner, hair dye, or purple shampoo with cool water.
  6. Condition your hair to restore moisture.

Tips for Toning Orange Hair

Here are some tips to help you tone orange hair after bleaching:

  • Use a color-safe and sulfate-free shampoo to prevent fading and maintain your hair color.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, as heat can cause damage and fade your hair color.
  • Use a deep conditioner or hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid swimming in chlorinated pools, as chlorine can strip your hair color.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

FAQs

How long should I leave the toner in my hair?

You should follow the instructions on the package. Typically, you should leave the toner in your hair for around 10-20 minutes.

Can I tone my hair immediately after bleaching?

No, you should wait at least 24 hours after bleaching to tone your hair. This will allow your hair to rest and recover from the bleach.

What if my hair turns gray after using an ash-toned hair dye?

If your hair turns gray after using an ash-toned hair dye, it means that you used too much ash. To fix this, wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo, then apply a toner with a warmer undertone.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Orange Tones After Bleaching

Here are some tips to prevent orange tones after bleaching:

  • Deep condition your hair before bleaching to prevent damage and breakage.
  • Avoid using a high volume developer, as it can cause more damage and orange tones in your hair.
  • Use a toner immediately after bleaching to prevent orange tones from developing.
  • Wait at least 4-6 weeks between bleachings to give your hair time to recover.
  • Consult a professional hairstylist if you're unsure about how to bleach or tone your hair.

With these tips and methods, you can easily tone orange hair after bleaching and achieve your desired hair color. Remember to take care of your hair and protect it from damage to maintain your hair color and health.
