How To Cut Hair At Home With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Nov 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to save money and cut your hair at home, using clippers is a great option. With a little practice and some basic equipment, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut in the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting hair with clippers, from choosing the right equipment to finishing touches. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. Equipment Needed
  2. Preparing for the Haircut
  3. Cutting the Hair
  4. Finishing Touches
  5. Tips for Success
  6. FAQs
  7. Hair Care After the Haircut

Equipment Needed

To cut hair with clippers, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • A set of clippers with guards
  • A comb
  • Scissors
  • A cape or towel to protect clothing
  • Clipper oil and cleaning brush
  • A mirror

When choosing clippers, look for a set with adjustable guards to allow for varying lengths of hair. A cordless set may also be more convenient for maneuvering around the head.

Preparing for the Haircut

Before beginning the haircut, make sure the hair is clean and dry. It's also helpful to have the person receiving the haircut sit in a well-lit area and face a mirror.

Next, choose the appropriate guard size for the desired length of hair. Start with a longer guard and work your way down if needed. It's better to start with a longer length and make adjustments as you go, rather than cutting too much off at once.

Cutting the Hair

Begin by combing the hair to remove any tangles or knots. Then, use the clippers to cut the hair in the desired direction, starting at the nape of the neck and working your way up to the crown of the head. Use a steady, even motion to avoid uneven cuts.

When transitioning from one guard size to another, blend the hair using a comb or scissors to create a natural-looking fade. Pay attention to the hairline and make sure to blend it smoothly.

For the top of the head, use scissors to trim any excess length and create shape. Start with small cuts and work your way up, checking the mirror frequently to ensure evenness.

Finishing Touches

After the haircut is complete, use the clippers to trim around the ears and neck for a clean finish. Use scissors to clean up any stray hairs and create shape if needed.

Once finished, clean the clippers with oil and a brush to keep them in good condition for future use.

Tips for Success

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Use a steady, even motion to avoid uneven cuts.
  • Check the mirror frequently to ensure evenness.
  • Blend the hairline smoothly for a natural-looking fade.
  • Use scissors for the top of the head to create shape and remove excess length.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair with clippers?

Yes, with some practice and the right equipment, you can cut your own hair with clippers.

What guard size should I use for a fade?

A fade typically starts with a larger guard size and gradually gets shorter. Start with a #4 guard and work your way down to a #1 or #0 for a more dramatic fade.

How often should I clean my clippers?

Clean your clippers after each use to keep them in good condition. Use oil and a brush to remove any hair and debris.

Hair Care After the Haircut

After the haircut, it's important to care for the hair to maintain its shape and texture. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and moisturized. Avoid using too much heat styling and try to air dry when possible. Regular trims every 4-6 weeks will also help maintain the shape of the haircut.

Cutting hair with clippers at home can be a cost-effective and convenient option. With some basic equipment and practice, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut in the comfort of your own home. Follow these steps and tips for success, and don't be afraid to experiment and find your own style!
