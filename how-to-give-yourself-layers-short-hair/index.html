How To Give Yourself Layers In Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Sep 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your boring, one-length short hair? Layers are a great way to add texture, volume and movement to your hair. And the good news is, you can easily give yourself layers at home without having to go to a salon. In this article, we’ll show you how to give yourself layers in short hair in a few simple steps.

Description

Layers are a popular hairstyle that involves cutting different lengths of hair to create varied dimensions and movement. Short hair with layers is a great way to add volume and texture to fine, thin hair, or enhance the natural curls of thicker hair. Layers can also help to frame your face and highlight your best features.

How to Give Yourself Layers in Short Hair – Step by Step

Step 1: Wash and Prep Your Hair

The first step to giving yourself layers in short hair is to wash your hair and towel-dry it. You don’t want your hair to be too wet or too dry, so make sure it’s slightly damp. Then, comb your hair to remove any tangles and knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into three sections – top, middle and bottom. Use hair clips or ties to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Section

Starting with the bottom section, comb your hair straight down and cut off the desired length. Then, take small sections of hair and cut them at a slight angle, moving towards the front of your face. This will create the first layer.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Section

Once you’ve finished the bottom section, move on to the middle section. Comb the hair straight down and cut off the desired length. Then, repeat the same process as the bottom section, cutting small sections at a slight angle towards the front of your face.

Step 5: Cut the Top Section

Finally, move on to the top section. Comb the hair straight down and cut off the desired length. Then, repeat the same process as the middle section, cutting small sections at a slight angle towards the front of your face.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you’ve cut all three sections, blend the layers together by combing your hair and checking for any unevenness. Use scissors to trim any parts that need to be adjusted.

Tips for Giving Yourself Layers in Short Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when giving yourself layers in short hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends or jagged cuts.
  • Cut small sections at a time to avoid over-cutting.
  • Don’t cut too much hair at once – you can always cut more later.
  • Make sure your hair is evenly distributed in each section to avoid uneven layers.
  • Take your time and be patient – it’s better to have a gradual layering effect than a drastic one.

FAQs

Is it possible to give myself layers in short hair without cutting my hair too short?

Yes, it is possible to give yourself layers in short hair without cutting off too much length. You can start with small sections and gradually cut more if needed.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, it’s recommended to use hair-cutting scissors as they are sharper and designed for cutting hair. Regular scissors can cause split ends and damage your hair.

How often should I cut my hair to maintain the layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the layers grow out. It’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the layers and keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair with Layers

Here are some hair care tips to keep your short hair with layers looking healthy and stylish:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage and breakage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the layers and prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a protective spray.

Now that you know how to give yourself layers in short hair, it’s time to try it out and see the difference it can make to your hair. Remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and follow the steps carefully. With a little practice, you’ll be able to create beautiful layers in no time.
