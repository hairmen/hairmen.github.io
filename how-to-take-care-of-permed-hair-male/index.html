How To Take Care Of Permed Hair Male A Complete Guide

Perming is a popular hair styling technique that involves chemical treatments to create curls or waves in hair. While permed hair can give men a unique and stylish look, it requires some special care to maintain its shape and health. In this article, we will discuss how to take care of permed hair male, including step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and more.

How to Perm Hair Male

Before discussing how to take care of permed hair male, it's important to understand how to perm hair male. Here are the steps:

  • Wash the hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any dirt or product buildup.
  • Wrap the hair around perm rods, starting at the nape of the neck and working upwards.
  • Apply a perming solution to the hair and let it sit for the recommended time.
  • Rinse the hair with warm water and apply a neutralizer to set the curls.
  • Remove the perm rods and rinse the hair again with cool water.

Now that you have permed hair, let's discuss how to take care of permed hair male.

Step-by-Step Guide to Take Care of Permed Hair Male

Here is a step-by-step guide to take care of permed hair male:

Step 1: Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a shampoo that is specifically designed for permed hair. Avoid using harsh shampoos as they can strip away the natural oils and moisture from your hair, leaving it dry and brittle.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

Use a conditioner after shampooing your hair. It will help to moisturize and detangle your curls, making them more manageable. Leave the conditioner on for at least 2-3 minutes before rinsing it off.

Step 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers, straighteners, and curling irons. Heat can damage your permed hair and make it frizzy. If you must use these styling tools, use a heat protectant spray before styling your hair.

Step 4: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. Start from the ends and work your way up to the roots. Avoid using a brush as it can break your curls and make them frizzy.

Step 5: Protect Your Hair from Sun

Exposure to sunlight can damage your permed hair and make it dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen when you go out in the sun.

Step 6: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and maintain your hair's shape. Visit a professional hair stylist every 6-8 weeks for a trim.

Tips to Maintain Permed Hair Male

Here are some tips to maintain permed hair male:

  • Use a hair serum or oil to add shine and moisture to your hair.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as it can dry out your hair.
  • Avoid using tight hair accessories, as they can break your curls and make them frizzy.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid touching or playing with your curls too much, as it can cause frizz and breakage.

FAQs about Permed Hair Male

Q: How long does a perm last?

A: A perm can last anywhere from 2-6 months, depending on your hair type and how well you take care of it.

Q: Can I color my permed hair?

A: Yes, you can color your permed hair. However, it's best to wait at least 2 weeks after perming your hair before coloring it.

Q: How often should I wash my permed hair?

A: You can wash your permed hair every 2-3 days, depending on your hair type and how oily it gets.

Hair Care Products for Permed Hair Male

Here are some hair care products that are specifically designed for permed hair male:

  • Shampoo and conditioner for permed hair
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Hair serum or oil
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Hair sunscreen

Now that you know how to take care of permed hair male, you can enjoy your new look with confidence. Follow these tips and maintain your permed hair properly to keep it healthy, shiny, and bouncy!
