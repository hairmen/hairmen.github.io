How To Cover Weave Tracks A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of feeling self-conscious about your weave tracks showing? Whether you're new to wearing weaves or you're a seasoned pro, knowing how to cover your tracks can make a big difference in the final look of your hairstyle. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of covering your weave tracks for a flawless finish. Let's get started!

How to Cover Weave Tracks

Before we dive into the step-by-step process, it's important to note that the technique you use to cover your weave tracks will depend on the type of weave you have. If you have a sew-in weave, for example, you'll need to approach the process differently than you would with a glue-in weave. With that in mind, let's take a look at the general steps you'll need to follow:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

The first step is to section off the hair that you'll be working with. Use clips to separate the top half of your hair from the bottom half, and then create a horizontal part at the nape of your neck to create a section for your weave.

Step 2: Apply Your Weave

The next step is to apply your weave, whether that's a sew-in or glue-in weave. Follow the instructions that came with your weave, and be sure to take your time to ensure that it's applied securely and evenly.

Step 3: Cover Your Tracks

Now it's time to cover your tracks. This is where the technique will vary depending on the type of weave you have. If you have a sew-in weave, you can use a weaving needle and thread to create a closure or leave-out that covers your tracks. If you have a glue-in weave, you can use hair extensions or a hairpiece to cover your tracks.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once your tracks are covered, you can style your hair as desired. Whether you want to wear it straight or curly, up or down, the possibilities are endless!

Tips for Covering Weave Tracks

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you work to cover your weave tracks:

  • Always follow the instructions that came with your weave to ensure proper application.
  • Use high-quality hair extensions or a hairpiece to cover your tracks for the most natural look.
  • If you're using a hairpiece to cover your tracks, be sure to blend it seamlessly with your natural hair.
  • Take your time when sewing in your weave to ensure that it's secure and won't come loose over time.
  • Experiment with different closure or leave-out styles to find the one that works best for you.

FAQs About Covering Weave Tracks

How can I make my weave look more natural?

Using high-quality hair extensions or a hairpiece can make a big difference in the final look of your weave. Additionally, be sure to blend your weave seamlessly with your natural hair for a more natural appearance.

Can I cover my weave tracks without using a closure?

Yes! You can use a leave-out technique to cover your tracks without using a closure. Simply leave out a small section of your natural hair to cover the tracks and blend it with your weave.

How often do I need to cover my weave tracks?

You'll typically need to cover your weave tracks every 2-4 weeks, depending on how quickly your natural hair grows.

Hair Care for Weave Wearers

Proper hair care is essential when wearing a weave. Here are a few tips to keep your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Wash your hair regularly to avoid buildup and keep your scalp clean.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh your hair down.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your weave, as this can cause damage to your natural hair.
  • Take breaks between weaves to give your natural hair a chance to rest and recover.

Now that you know how to cover your weave tracks, you can rock your new hairstyle with confidence. Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful!
