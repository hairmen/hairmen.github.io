What Hairstyle Suits A Long Thin Face

Written by William Jun 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have a long, thin face, it can sometimes be challenging to find a hairstyle that flatters your features. But don't worry, with the right cut and style, you can enhance your natural beauty and look your best. In this article, we'll explore some of the best hairstyles for long, thin faces, and provide tips on how to care for your hair.

Description

A long, thin face is characterized by a narrow forehead, high cheekbones, and a pointed chin. If you have this face shape, you'll want to choose a hairstyle that adds width to your face and balances out your features. You'll also want to avoid styles that make your face look even longer and thinner.

How to Choose the Right Hairstyle

When choosing a hairstyle for a long, thin face, there are a few things to keep in mind:

1. Look for volume

Hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your face can help balance out your features. Look for styles with layers, curls, or waves that add movement and fullness.

2. Avoid styles that pull your hair back tightly

Slicked-back styles or tight ponytails can emphasize the length of your face. Instead, choose styles that allow your hair to fall naturally around your face.

3. Consider bangs

Bangs can be a great way to add width to your face. Look for styles with side-swept bangs or blunt bangs that hit just above your eyebrows.

Step by Step: Best Hairstyles for Long, Thin Faces

Here are some of the best hairstyles for long, thin faces:

1. Long Layers

Long layers can add movement and volume to your hair. Ask your stylist to cut long layers that start at your chin and frame your face. This style works well with straight or wavy hair.

2. Shoulder-Length Bob

A shoulder-length bob with subtle layers can add width to your face and create a flattering silhouette. This style works well with straight or slightly wavy hair.

3. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs can add width to your face and draw attention to your eyes. Ask your stylist to cut your bangs at an angle and blend them into the rest of your hair.

4. Textured Pixie Cut

A textured pixie cut can add volume and movement to your hair. Ask your stylist to add layers and texture to your hair, and consider adding side-swept bangs to balance out your features.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hairstyle

Once you've found the perfect hairstyle, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best:

1. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type, and avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates.

2. Protect your hair from heat

Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools like curling irons or straighteners, and avoid using them on a daily basis.

3. Get regular trims

To keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends, get regular trims every 6-8 weeks.

FAQ: Hairstyles for Long, Thin Faces

1. Can I wear my hair in a center part?

While a center part can elongate your face, you can still wear your hair in a center part if you prefer. Just make sure to add volume and width to your hair with layers or waves.

2. Should I avoid short hairstyles?

Not necessarily. Short hairstyles can work well for long, thin faces, as long as they add volume and width to your face. Consider a textured pixie cut or a bob with subtle layers.

3. Can I wear my hair in a ponytail?

A low ponytail or a messy bun can work well for long, thin faces, as long as you don't pull your hair back too tightly. Consider adding volume to your hair with waves or curls.

Hair Care for Long, Thin Faces

With the right hairstyle and proper hair care, you can enhance your natural beauty and feel confident in your appearance. Remember to choose a hairstyle that adds width to your face, and take care of your hair with quality products and regular trims. With these tips, you'll be sure to look your best.
