How To Hide A Haircut At School Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Jul 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Getting a new haircut can be exciting, but it can also be nerve-wracking if you're not sure how to hide it at school. Whether you're trying to avoid the teasing or just want to keep your new look a surprise, there are a few easy tips and tricks you can use to keep your haircut under wraps. Here's what you need to know.

How to Hide a Haircut at School

Step One: Plan Ahead

Before you even get your haircut, think about how you want to style it afterwards. If you're used to wearing your hair down, consider getting layers or bangs that can be easily pinned back or tucked behind your ears. If you usually wear your hair in a ponytail, ask your stylist to leave some longer pieces near the front that you can use to create a side part or a messy bun.

Step Two: Use Accessories

Accessories are your best friend when it comes to hiding a new haircut. Headbands, scarves, and hats can all be used to cover up any awkward layers or lengths. If you're not sure what to wear, try experimenting with different styles at home until you find something that works for you.

Step Three: Experiment with Hairstyles

Experimenting with different hairstyles can also help you hide a new haircut. Braids, buns, and updos can all be used to conceal any changes to your hair. If you're not sure how to create a certain style, try watching tutorials on YouTube or asking a friend for help.

Step Four: Be Confident

Finally, the most important thing you can do to hide a new haircut at school is to be confident. Remember that your haircut doesn't define you, and that it's okay to try something new. If anyone asks about your hair, just smile and say that you're trying out a new style. Chances are, they'll be impressed by your bravery.

Tips for Hiding a Haircut at School

Here are a few additional tips to help you hide your new haircut at school:

  • Invest in some dry shampoo to help keep your hair looking fresh and clean.
  • Wear a hoodie or jacket with a hood to help cover up any awkward lengths.
  • If you have bangs, try sweeping them to the side or pinning them back.
  • Consider wearing your hair in a low ponytail or bun to keep it out of your face.

Hair Care After a Haircut

It's important to take care of your hair after a haircut to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to help prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat tools like blow dryers and flat irons for at least a few days after your haircut.
  • Try using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair healthy.
  • Get regular trims to help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking fresh.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will people notice if I get a haircut?

A: It depends on how drastic the change is. If you just get a trim, most people probably won't notice. If you get a dramatic new style or cut off a significant amount of length, people will likely notice.

Q: What if I don't like my new haircut?

A: Don't panic! Remember that hair grows back, and you can always try a different style next time. If you're really unhappy with your new haircut, talk to your stylist about what you don't like and see if they can make any adjustments.

Q: How often should I get a haircut?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to maintain your style. Most people get a trim every six to eight weeks to help prevent split ends and keep their hair healthy.

Conclusion

Hiding a new haircut at school doesn't have to be stressful. By planning ahead, using accessories, experimenting with hairstyles, and being confident, you can rock your new look with ease. And remember, if anyone asks about your hair, just smile and own it!
