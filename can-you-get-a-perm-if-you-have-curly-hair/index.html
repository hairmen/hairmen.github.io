Description

Curly hair can be a challenge to manage, and many people opt for a perm to achieve a more uniform curl pattern. However, if you already have curly hair, you may be wondering if it's possible to get a perm. The answer is yes, but there are some things to consider before going ahead with the process.

How to Get a Perm If You Have Curly Hair

Getting a perm on curly hair is not much different than getting a perm on straight hair. The process involves applying a solution to break down the hair's natural structure, then wrapping it around rods to create a new curl pattern. However, there are some things to keep in mind to ensure the best results.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for getting a perm if you have curly hair:

  1. Choose a reputable salon and stylist who has experience with perming curly hair.
  2. Talk to your stylist about your hair goals and any concerns you have.
  3. Prepare your hair by washing it with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup.
  4. Section your hair and wrap it around perm rods, using a smaller size for tighter curls and a larger size for looser curls.
  5. Apply the perm solution, making sure to saturate each rod thoroughly.
  6. Leave the solution on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse it out with warm water.
  7. Apply a neutralizer to stop the perming process and set the new curl pattern.
  8. Rinse out the neutralizer and remove the rods, then style your hair as desired.

Tips for Getting a Perm If You Have Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Choose a stylist who has experience perming curly hair to ensure they understand how to work with your hair's natural texture.
  • Be prepared for some trial and error to find the right curl size and shape for your hair.
  • Avoid coloring or chemically treating your hair for at least two weeks before and after getting a perm.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment regularly to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid heat styling tools and opt for air-drying or diffusing your curls instead.

FAQs

Can You Get a Perm If You Have Naturally Curly Hair?

Yes, you can get a perm if you have naturally curly hair, but it may not be necessary or advisable, as it can damage your hair and change the texture in unpredictable ways.

Is It Better to Get a Perm on Curly or Straight Hair?

There's no definitive answer to this question, as it depends on your individual hair type and goals. However, if you already have curly hair, you may want to consider other styling options before getting a perm, as it can be damaging and may not give you the results you want.

How Long Does a Perm Last on Curly Hair?

A perm can last anywhere from three to six months, depending on your hair type, the size of the curl, and how well you take care of your hair afterward.

Hair Care After Getting a Perm

After getting a perm, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and maintain your new curl pattern. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 48 hours after getting a perm to allow the new curl pattern to set.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling tools and opt for air-drying or diffusing your curls instead.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Getting a perm if you have curly hair is possible, but it's important to choose a reputable salon and stylist who has experience with perming curly hair. Be prepared for some trial and error to find the right curl size and shape for your hair, and take good care of your hair afterward to keep it healthy and maintain your new look.
