How To Cut A Fohawk Fade Step By Step

Welcome to our guide on how to cut a fohawk fade step by step. If you're looking for a bold and stylish haircut that's perfect for any occasion, then a fohawk fade might be just what you need. This haircut is a combination of a mohawk and a fade, and it creates a stunning and edgy look that's perfect for men of all ages.

How to Prepare for a Fohawk Fade

Before you start cutting a fohawk fade, it's important to prepare the hair properly. Here are some steps you need to follow:

  1. Wash the hair with shampoo and conditioner to remove any dirt and oil.
  2. Blow-dry the hair or let it air dry naturally.
  3. Comb the hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  4. Decide on the length and style of the fohawk and make sure you have the right tools.

Once you have prepared the hair, you're ready to start cutting.

Step by Step Guide to Cutting a Fohawk Fade

Follow these steps to cut a fohawk fade:

  1. Start by cutting the sides and back of the head with a clipper. You can use a #1 or #2 guard, depending on the length you want.
  2. Create a gradual fade by using a higher guard towards the top of the head and a lower guard towards the bottom.
  3. Blend the fade using a clipper over comb technique.
  4. Use scissors to cut the top of the hair to the desired length. Make sure to leave enough hair to create the fohawk.
  5. Section the hair in the middle and comb it upwards.
  6. Use a clipper to create a line in the middle of the hair. This will be the starting point of the fohawk.
  7. Use scissors to cut the hair on either side of the clipper line, creating a triangular shape.
  8. Blend the sides of the fohawk using scissors or clippers.
  9. Finish by styling the fohawk using a hair product of your choice.

Congratulations, you have successfully cut a fohawk fade!

Tips for Cutting a Fohawk Fade

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a fohawk fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting a fohawk fade requires precision and attention to detail.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from different angles.
  • Experiment with different lengths and styles to find what works best for you.

FAQs on Fohawk Fade Haircut

1. How often do I need to get a fohawk fade?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain the style. Typically, fohawk fades need to be trimmed every 2-4 weeks.

2. Can I cut a fohawk fade at home?

Yes, you can cut a fohawk fade at home if you have the right tools and know the proper technique. However, if you're not confident in your skills, it's best to leave it to a professional.

3. What hair products should I use to style a fohawk fade?

You can use a variety of hair products to style a fohawk fade, including pomade, wax, gel, and hairspray. Choose a product that works best for your hair type and desired style.

Aftercare for Fohawk Fade Haircut

After cutting a fohawk fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style and keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow-drying or straightening.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep the style looking fresh.

We hope this guide on how to cut a fohawk fade step by step has been helpful. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun with your new haircut!
