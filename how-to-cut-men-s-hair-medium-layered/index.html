How To Cut Men s Hair Medium Layered A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to change up your hairstyle without going too drastic, a medium layered cut might be the perfect choice for you. This style adds texture and movement to your hair, making it look fuller and more dynamic. In this guide, we'll go through the steps to cutting men's hair medium layered, as well as some tips for maintaining your new look.

Description

A medium layered cut is a style where the hair is cut to a medium length (usually around 2-3 inches) and then layered throughout. This means that some sections of the hair will be shorter than others, creating a textured and layered look. This style works well for all hair types and can be customized to suit your face shape and personal style.

How to Cut Men's Hair Medium Layered

Before you start cutting, it's important to gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section off the hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror in front of you, so you can see what you're doing from different angles.

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

It's best to cut hair when it's clean and dry, as this will give you a better idea of how the hair will fall once it's cut. Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner, and then let it air dry or blow dry it on a low heat setting.

Step 2: Section Off the Hair

Using your comb, section off the hair into four parts: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use the hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Start Cutting the Bottom Layer

Starting with the back section, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at a slight angle, creating a soft, textured edge. Continue cutting small sections of hair in this way, working your way up to the crown of your head.

Step 4: Move on to the Sides

Once you've finished the back section, move on to the left and right sides. Take small sections of hair and cut them at the same slight angle as before, making sure to blend the layers into the back section.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, move on to the top section of your hair. Take small sections and cut them at a slightly steeper angle, so that the hair at the crown is slightly shorter than the hair at the front. This will create more volume and lift at the top of your head.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best results from your medium layered cut:

  • Start with a smaller cut and work your way up if you want more layers.
  • Use a texturizing spray or pomade to enhance the texture and movement of your hair.
  • Visit a professional stylist if you're unsure about cutting your own hair.

FAQ

How often should I get my medium layered cut trimmed?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and texture of your medium layered cut.

Can I style my medium layered cut in different ways?

Absolutely! This style is versatile and can be styled in many different ways, from messy and textured to sleek and polished.

Hair Care

To keep your medium layered cut looking its best, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat on your hair when styling. Regular trims will also help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting men's hair medium layered is a great way to update your look and add some texture and movement to your hair. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. Just remember to take care of your hair and maintain your new style with regular trims and gentle hair care.
