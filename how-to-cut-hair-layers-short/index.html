How To Cut Hair Layers Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Nov 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to switch up your hairstyle with some short layers? Maybe you want to add more volume and movement to your hair, or perhaps you just want a fresh new look. Whatever your reason, cutting hair layers short can be a bit intimidating if you've never done it before. But don't worry, we've got you covered with this easy-to-follow guide.

Description

In this article, we'll be discussing how to cut hair layers short. We'll walk you through the step-by-step process, share some tips and tricks, and answer some frequently asked questions. We'll also touch on hair care after your haircut, so you can keep your new style looking fresh.

How To

Before we get into the actual cutting process, there are a few things you'll need:

What You'll Need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle

Now, let's get started:

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Start by washing and towel-drying your hair. Make sure it's completely detangled.
  2. Separate your hair into sections using hair clips. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of one of the sections. This will be your guide section.
  4. Hold the guide section straight up and cut it to the desired length. This will be the length of your shortest layer.
  5. Take another small section of hair from the same section you just cut. Hold it at a 45-degree angle and cut it to the same length as your guide section. This will create your first layer.
  6. Continue taking small sections and cutting them at a 45-degree angle to create shorter layers. Make sure you're cutting each section to the same length as your guide section.
  7. Move on to the next section of hair and repeat steps 3-6.
  8. Once you've cut all of your sections, take a comb and run it through your hair to make sure everything is even.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you're cutting your hair layers short:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. It's easier to cut more hair than it is to fix a mistake.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can lead to split ends.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about cutting hair layers short:

How short should I cut my layers?

The length of your layers will depend on your personal preference and the overall look you're going for. Generally, the shortest layers should be around chin-length or higher.

Can I cut my layers with regular scissors?

You can, but it's not recommended. Regular scissors can cause split ends and make your hair look frayed.

How often should I cut my hair layers?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layers and keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair layers short, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and fresh. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your layers and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

And that's it! With this guide, you should be able to confidently cut your hair layers short and rock a new hairstyle. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don't be afraid to experiment and try new things.
