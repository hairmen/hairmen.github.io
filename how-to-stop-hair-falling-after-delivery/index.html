How To Stop Hair Falling After Delivery Tips And Tricks

Are you experiencing hair loss after giving birth? If so, you're not alone. Many women experience postpartum hair loss, which can be frustrating and discouraging. However, there are steps you can take to stop hair falling after delivery. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks to help you keep your locks healthy and vibrant.

How to Stop Hair Falling After Delivery

First, it's important to understand why postpartum hair loss occurs. During pregnancy, your body produces more hormones that keep your hair from falling out. After delivery, however, these hormones drop, which can cause hair loss. Additionally, the stress of childbirth and lack of sleep can also contribute to hair loss.

To prevent hair loss after delivery, you can try the following:

1. Take a Hair Supplement

One of the easiest things you can do to prevent hair loss is to take a hair supplement. Look for supplements that contain biotin, vitamin D, and iron, as these nutrients are essential for healthy hair growth. You can also try a postpartum hair supplement, which is specifically designed to support hair growth after childbirth.

2. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting plenty of fruits, vegetables, lean protein, and healthy fats. Avoid processed foods, sugar, and caffeine, as these can contribute to hair loss.

3. Manage Your Stress

Stress can be a major contributor to hair loss. To manage your stress, try practicing relaxation techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or yoga. You can also try getting regular exercise, which can help reduce stress levels.

4. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids, buns, and ponytails can put stress on your hair and cause it to break. Instead, try wearing your hair down or in loose, low-maintenance styles like a messy bun or a simple braid.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you stop hair falling after delivery:

Step 1: Take a Hair Supplement

Start by taking a hair supplement that contains biotin, vitamin D, and iron. You can also try a postpartum hair supplement, which is specifically designed to support hair growth after childbirth.

Step 2: Eat a Healthy Diet

Eat a healthy diet that's rich in fruits, vegetables, lean protein, and healthy fats. Avoid processed foods, sugar, and caffeine, as these can contribute to hair loss.

Step 3: Manage Your Stress

Manage your stress by practicing relaxation techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or yoga. You can also try getting regular exercise, which can help reduce stress levels.

Step 4: Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like braids, buns, and ponytails, as these can put stress on your hair and cause it to break. Instead, wear your hair down or in loose, low-maintenance styles like a messy bun or a simple braid.

Step 5: Use a Mild Shampoo

Use a mild shampoo that's gentle on your hair and scalp. Avoid shampoos that contain harsh chemicals like sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle.

Tips for Preventing Hair Loss

Here are some additional tips for preventing hair loss after delivery:

Tip 1: Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help improve blood flow to your hair follicles, which can promote healthy hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

Tip 2: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as this can help prevent breakage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

Tip 3: Don't Overheat Your Hair

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause it to break. If you must use heat styling tools, use them sparingly and always use a heat protectant spray.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about postpartum hair loss:

Q: How long does postpartum hair loss last?

A: Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for a few months, but can last up to a year.

Q: Will my hair grow back after postpartum hair loss?

A: Yes, your hair will eventually grow back after postpartum hair loss. However, it may take several months for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy thickness.

Q: Can breastfeeding cause hair loss?

A: Yes, breastfeeding can contribute to hair loss because it causes hormonal changes in your body. However, this is usually temporary and your hair will eventually grow back.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help you keep your locks healthy and vibrant:

Tip 1: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can contribute to hair loss.

Tip 2: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner can help keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage. Apply a small amount of conditioner to the ends of your hair after shampooing and leave it in.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product that contains SPF. Exposure to the sun can cause your hair to become dry and brittle, which can contribute to hair loss.

By following these tips and tricks, you can help prevent hair loss after delivery and keep your locks healthy and vibrant. Remember to be patient, as it may take several months for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy thickness. With a little TLC, you'll have beautiful, healthy hair in no time!
