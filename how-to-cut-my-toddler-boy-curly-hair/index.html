How To Cut My Toddler Boy Curly Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Nov 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're a parent of a toddler boy with curly hair, you might find it challenging to cut his hair. With the right tools and techniques, you can give your little one the perfect haircut without any hassle. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your toddler boy curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • Tools Required
  • Preparing Your Toddler
  • Sectioning the Hair
  • Cutting the Hair
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Tools Required

Before you start cutting your toddler boy's curly hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Scissors
  • Electric clipper
  • Comb
  • Hair clippers with different guard sizes
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Hair gel or pomade (optional)

Preparing Your Toddler

It's important to prepare your toddler before you start cutting his hair. Make sure he is well-fed, well-rested, and in a good mood. You can also distract him with his favorite toy or TV show while you cut his hair.

Step 1: Wash Your Toddler's Hair

Wash your toddler's hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will make it easier to work with his hair and will remove any dirt or oil.

Step 2: Comb Your Toddler's Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your toddler's hair. This will make it easier to section and cut his hair. Start at the ends of his hair and work your way up to the roots.

Sectioning the Hair

Sectioning the hair is important to ensure that you cut the hair evenly. Follow these steps to section your toddler's hair:

Step 1: Divide the Hair into Four Sections

Divide your toddler's hair into four sections: top, back, and two sides. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 2: Start with the Back Section

Start with the back section of your toddler's hair. Use a hair clipper with a guard size of 1 or 2 and trim the hair evenly. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up to the crown of the head.

Step 3: Move on to the Sides

After you've finished cutting the back section, move on to the sides. Use a hair clipper with a guard size of 3 or 4 and trim the hair evenly. Start at the bottom of the sideburns and work your way up to the temples.

Step 4: Trim the Top Section

Finally, move on to the top section of your toddler's hair. Use scissors to trim the hair evenly. Start at the front of the head and work your way to the back. Cut the hair to the desired length.

Cutting the Hair

Now that you've sectioned your toddler's hair, it's time to start cutting. Follow these steps to cut your toddler boy curly hair:

Step 1: Wet the Hair

Use a spray bottle with water to wet the hair. This will make it easier to work with and will prevent the hair from flying around.

Step 2: Comb the Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to comb the hair. This will separate the hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Use scissors to cut the hair. Start at the ends of the hair and work your way up to the roots. Cut the hair to the desired length.

Finishing Touches

After you've finished cutting your toddler boy curly hair, it's time to add some finishing touches. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Trim the Edges

Use scissors to trim the edges of the hair. This will give it a neater look.

Step 2: Apply Hair Gel or Pomade (Optional)

If you want to give your toddler's hair some hold and shine, you can apply hair gel or pomade. Use a small amount and apply it evenly throughout the hair.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once to avoid mistakes.
  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Don't pull the hair too tightly when cutting to avoid uneven cuts.

FAQs

How often should I cut my toddler's hair?

It's recommended to cut your toddler's hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the length and shape.

What should I do if my toddler won't sit still during the haircut?

You can distract your toddler with his favorite toy or TV show. You can also cut his hair while he is sleeping or hire a professional to do it.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

After you've cut your toddler's curly hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner made for curly hair.
  • Don't wash the hair too often to avoid drying it out.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep the hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools to prevent damage to the hair.

Cutting your toddler boy curly hair might seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, you can do it like a pro. Follow this step-by-step guide and you'll be able to give your little one the perfect haircut every time.
