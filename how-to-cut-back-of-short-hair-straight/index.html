How To Cut Back Of Short Hair Straight Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're not a professional. But with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut the back of short hair straight, step-by-step. Whether you're looking to trim your hair between salon visits or want to try something new, we've got you covered.

Description

The back of your hair can be tricky to cut, especially if you're working with short hair. But with our guide, you can learn how to achieve a straight cut that looks professional and polished. We'll go over the tools you need, the technique to use, and tips for maintaining your new cut.

How to Cut Back of Short Hair Straight

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A fine-tooth comb
  • A handheld mirror or a mirror that you can position behind you
  • Hair clips or elastics to section your hair

Once you have your tools, you're ready to begin.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Use the comb to create a horizontal parting across the back of your head, just above the nape of your neck. Clip the top section of your hair up and out of the way.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting. Take a small section of hair from the bottom section, and comb it straight down. Use your fingers to hold the hair taut, and cut straight across. Be sure to only cut a small amount at a time, so you can see what you're doing and avoid any mistakes.

Continue cutting small sections of hair in this way, working your way up from the bottom of your hairline to the crown of your head. Be sure to check your work in the mirror as you go, to ensure a straight and even cut.

Step 4: Blend Your Layers

Once you've cut the back of your hair, it's time to blend any layers. Take down the top section of your hair, and use the same technique to cut any layers that need trimming. Be careful not to cut too much, as you don't want to create any choppy or uneven layers.

Step 5: Check Your Work

When you're finished cutting, check your work in the mirror. Use the comb to make any final adjustments, and trim any stray hairs that need attention. Be sure to take your time and go slowly, to ensure a straight and even cut.

Tips for Cutting Back of Short Hair Straight

  • Always cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is.
  • For a more precise cut, use a pair of hair cutting shears rather than regular scissors.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're struggling to reach the back of your head.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, consider booking an appointment with a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair straight without a mirror?

While it's possible to cut your hair without a mirror, it's not recommended. Using a mirror will help you see what you're doing and ensure a straight and even cut.

How often should I cut the back of my short hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking healthy and maintained.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain your new cut. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner suited to your hair type, and avoid using too much heat on your hair when styling. Regular trims will also help to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

With our step-by-step guide, you can confidently cut the back of your short hair straight at home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and be patient. With a little practice, you'll be able to achieve a salon-worthy cut every time.
