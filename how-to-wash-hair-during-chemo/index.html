How To Wash Hair During Chemo A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Cancer is a deadly disease that affects millions of people worldwide. One of the most common treatments for cancer is chemotherapy, which involves the use of strong drugs to kill cancer cells. However, chemotherapy can also have some side effects, such as hair loss. If you are undergoing chemotherapy, you may be wondering how to wash your hair without causing any damage. In this article, we will discuss some helpful tips on how to wash your hair during chemo.

How to Wash Hair During Chemo

If you are undergoing chemotherapy, it is important to be gentle with your hair. Chemotherapy can make your hair brittle and prone to breakage, so you need to take extra care when washing it. Here are some tips to help you wash your hair during chemo:

1. Use a Mild Shampoo

Choose a mild shampoo that is free of harsh chemicals such as sulfates and parabens. Look for shampoos that are specifically designed for chemo patients, as these products are formulated to be gentle on the scalp.

2. Wash Hair Less Often

Chemotherapy can dry out your scalp, so you may not need to wash your hair as often as you did before. Try washing your hair every two or three days instead of every day.

3. Use Warm Water

Use warm water when washing your hair, as hot water can be too harsh on your scalp. Avoid using cold water, as this can make your hair even more brittle.

4. Be Gentle

Be gentle when washing your hair, and avoid scrubbing or rubbing your scalp too hard. Use your fingertips to massage the shampoo into your scalp, and then rinse it out thoroughly.

5. Use a Soft Towel

Use a soft towel to dry your hair, and avoid rubbing it vigorously. Instead, gently pat your hair dry, and then let it air-dry.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to wash your hair during chemo:

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start washing your hair, gather all the supplies you will need. This includes a mild shampoo, a soft towel, and a wide-tooth comb.

Step 2: Wet Your Hair

Wet your hair with warm water, and then apply a small amount of shampoo to your scalp.

Step 3: Massage Your Scalp

Use your fingertips to massage the shampoo into your scalp, being careful not to scrub too hard. Massage your scalp for a few minutes to help stimulate blood flow.

Step 4: Rinse Your Hair

Rinse the shampoo out of your hair thoroughly with warm water.

Step 5: Condition Your Hair

If you want to use conditioner, apply a small amount to the ends of your hair, avoiding your scalp. Leave the conditioner in your hair for a few minutes, and then rinse it out thoroughly.

Step 6: Dry Your Hair

Use a soft towel to gently pat your hair dry, and then let it air-dry. Avoid using a hair dryer, as the heat can be too harsh on your scalp.

Tips for Washing Hair During Chemo

Here are some additional tips to help you wash your hair during chemo:

1. Avoid Using Chemicals

Avoid using chemical treatments such as hair dyes, perms, and relaxers, as these products can be too harsh on your hair and scalp.

2. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Use a satin pillowcase when sleeping, as this can help reduce friction and prevent hair breakage.

3. Wear a Hat

If you are going outside, wear a hat to protect your scalp from the sun and wind.

4. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid using a brush, as this can be too harsh on your scalp.

FAQ

Q: Can I use my regular shampoo during chemo?

A: It is best to use a mild shampoo that is free of harsh chemicals during chemo. Look for shampoos that are specifically designed for chemo patients.

Q: Will washing my hair cause it to fall out?

A: Washing your hair will not cause it to fall out, but be gentle when washing it to avoid breakage.

Q: Can I use a hair dryer during chemo?

A: It is best to avoid using a hair dryer during chemo, as the heat can be too harsh on your scalp.

Hair Care During Chemo

Here are some additional tips to help you take care of your hair during chemo:

1. Cover Your Hair

If you are self-conscious about your hair loss, consider wearing a wig, hat, or scarf to cover your hair.

2. Be Gentle

Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on it.

3. Moisturize Your Scalp

Use a moisturizing cream or oil to help hydrate your scalp and prevent dryness.

4. Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water to help keep your hair and scalp hydrated.

By following these tips, you can help keep your hair healthy and strong during chemo. Remember to be gentle and take care of yourself, and your hair will thank you.
