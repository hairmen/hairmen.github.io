How To Make Wavy Hair With Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 16, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you have thick hair and long for those beachy waves? Well, you’re not alone. Many women with thick hair struggle with achieving the perfect waves without the curls falling out or looking too stiff. In this guide, we’ll break down the steps to creating those effortlessly chic waves that will last all day.

Description

Wavy hair is a popular hairstyle that many women desire. It adds texture, volume, and movement to your hair. However, if you have thick hair, creating waves can be a bit challenging. Thick hair tends to hold curls better, but it can also weigh them down, making them look less bouncy and voluminous. In this guide, we’ll show you how to make wavy hair with thick hair that will look natural, effortless, and beautiful.

How to

Step 1: Prep your hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair until it’s damp, but not dripping wet. Apply a heat protectant spray to your entire hair, focusing on the ends. This will protect your hair from heat damage and help the waves hold their shape.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into four equal sections: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section up and out of the way, except for one section.

Step 3: Curl your hair

Using a 1 ½ inch curling iron, take a small section of hair and wrap it around the barrel, away from your face. Hold the curl for 15-20 seconds, then release. Repeat this process on the rest of the sections of your hair, alternating the direction of the curls.

Step 4: Create waves

Once you’ve curled your entire head, use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to gently brush out the curls. This will create soft waves that look natural and effortless.

Step 5: Finish with hairspray

Finally, finish off the look with a light hold hairspray. Hold the can about 10 inches away from your head and spray lightly all over your hair. This will help the waves hold their shape without looking stiff or crunchy.

Tips

  • For a more natural look, curl your hair in different directions and vary the sizes of the sections you’re curling.
  • If your hair is particularly thick, you may want to use a smaller curling iron to help the waves hold their shape.
  • Don’t brush your hair too vigorously after curling, as this can cause the curls to fall out.
  • Always use a heat protectant spray to prevent heat damage to your hair.

FAQ

Q: How do I make my waves last longer?

A: To make your waves last longer, use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo on your roots to add volume and texture. You can also sleep with your hair in braids or buns to help the waves hold their shape overnight.

Q: Can I make waves without heat?

A: Yes, you can make waves without heat by braiding your hair or using a headband to create waves overnight. You can also use a sea salt spray to add texture and create beachy waves.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, it’s important to follow a good hair care routine. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that’s appropriate for your hair type, and avoid using hot tools too often. Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and nourished, and try to air dry your hair whenever possible.

Now that you know how to make wavy hair with thick hair, you can rock those beautiful, beachy waves all year round. Remember to use a heat protectant spray, vary the size of your curls, and finish with a light hold hairspray to achieve the perfect look.
