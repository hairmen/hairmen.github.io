How To Cut A Layered Haircut Everything You Need To Know

Written by Christine Oct 07, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your same old hairstyle and looking for something new? A layered haircut might be the answer you’re looking for. Layered haircuts are versatile, easy to maintain, and can suit almost any face shape. In this article, we’ll guide you through how to cut a layered haircut, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut a Layered Haircut
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting a Layered Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Layered Haircuts

How to Cut a Layered Haircut

Before we dive into the steps, it’s important to understand what a layered haircut is. A layered haircut is a haircut where the hair is cut at different lengths, creating layers. This can add texture, volume, and movement to the hair.

To cut a layered haircut, you’ll need to have the following tools:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle
  • Cutting cape or towel

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut a layered haircut:

  1. Start by washing and drying the hair.
  2. Section the hair into four parts: top, left, right, and back.
  3. Take the top section of the hair and comb it straight up. Hold it between your index and middle fingers, and cut it to the desired length. This will be your guide for the rest of the haircut.
  4. Release the left section of the hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut it to the length of the guide you just created.
  5. Repeat the previous step on the right section of the hair.
  6. Release the back section of the hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut it to the length of the guide.
  7. Release the top section of the hair and comb it straight up. Using the guide, cut the hair at an angle, creating layers.
  8. Repeat the previous step on the left, right, and back sections of the hair.
  9. Blend the layers together by combing the hair straight down and cutting any stray hairs.
  10. Style the hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting a Layered Haircut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a layered haircut:

  • Start with small sections and gradually work your way to larger sections.
  • Don’t cut too much at once. It’s better to cut less and make adjustments later.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Keep the hair wet throughout the haircut to ensure an even cut.
  • Use hair clips to keep the hair in place and prevent it from getting tangled.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about layered haircuts:

How often should I get a layered haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the layers lose their shape. Generally, it’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

What face shapes suit layered haircuts?

Layered haircuts can suit almost any face shape. They can add volume to thin faces, soften angular faces, and frame round faces.

Can I cut my own layered haircut?

It’s possible to cut your own layered haircut, but it can be tricky. It’s recommended to have a professional do it for best results.

Hair Care for Layered Haircuts

Once you’ve cut your layered haircut, it’s important to take care of it to maintain its shape and texture. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that’s suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut a layered haircut, you can try it out on yourself or your friends. Don’t forget to follow the tips and take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and stylish.
