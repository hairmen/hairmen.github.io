How To Layer Really Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 07, 2023 · 5 min read

Long hair is beautiful, but it can also be heavy and difficult to manage. One way to make long hair more manageable is by layering it. Layering adds texture, shape, and movement to your hair. In this article, we’ll show you how to layer really long hair in a relaxed English language. We’ll cover everything from why layering is important, to how to do it yourself, to tips for maintaining your layered locks.

Table of Contents:

  • Why Layering is Important
  • How to Layer Really Long Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Layered Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Why Layering is Important

Layering is important for a few reasons:

  • Shape: Layering gives your hair shape and removes any heavy, unflattering weight. It can also help balance out your face shape.
  • Texture: Layering adds texture to your hair, making it look more interesting and dynamic.
  • Movement: Layering allows your hair to move freely, making it look more natural and relaxed.

How to Layer Really Long Hair

If you have really long hair, you might be wondering how to layer it without losing too much length. Here are a few tips:

  • Consult a professional: If you’re not confident in your ability to layer your own hair, it’s best to consult a professional stylist. They can help you achieve the look you want while maintaining the health of your hair.
  • Use the right tools: You’ll need a sharp pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.
  • Work in small sections: It’s important to work in small sections so you can control the amount of hair you’re cutting. This will help you avoid any mistakes.
  • Start with dry hair: It’s best to layer your hair when it’s dry, as wet hair tends to shrink when it dries. This can make your layers shorter than you intended.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to layering really long hair:

  1. Start by parting your hair down the middle and combing it out.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the top of your head and clip the rest of your hair away.
  3. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and comb it straight down.
  4. Using your scissors, cut a small amount of hair at a time. Start by cutting off the ends to remove any split ends or damaged hair.
  5. Continue cutting small amounts of hair at a time, moving down the section of hair. As you move down, cut the hair at a slight angle to create layers.
  6. Repeat this process on the other side of your head.
  7. Once you’ve finished the top sections of your hair, move on to the sides and back. Take small sections of hair and cut at a slight angle to blend with the layers on top.
  8. Once you’ve finished all sections, check for any uneven spots and trim as needed.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Layered Hair

Now that you’ve layered your hair, here are a few tips for maintaining your new style:

  • Use the right products: Use products that are designed for your hair type and texture. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent damage.
  • Get regular trims: To keep your layers looking fresh, it’s important to get regular trims. This will help maintain the shape and prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair: Use heat protectant products when styling your hair with hot tools. This will help prevent damage and keep your hair healthy.
  • Be gentle: Don’t tug or pull on your hair, as this can cause breakage and damage. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, and be gentle when brushing or styling.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about layering long hair:

  • Will layering make my hair look thinner? Layering can make your hair look thinner, but it can also make it look more voluminous. It depends on the amount of hair you layer.
  • How often should I get my hair trimmed? It’s recommended to get your hair trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your layers.
  • Can I layer my own hair? It’s possible to layer your own hair, but it’s best to consult a professional stylist if you’re not confident in your ability.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is important for maintaining healthy, beautiful hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly: Wash your hair every 2-3 days to remove dirt, oil, and product buildup.
  • Use a conditioner: Use a conditioner after shampooing to keep your hair hydrated and prevent tangles.
  • Avoid heat: Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet: Eating a healthy diet that’s rich in vitamins and minerals can help keep your hair healthy and strong.

Layering really long hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it’s possible to achieve a beautiful, textured look. Use this step-by-step guide to layer your own hair or consult a professional stylist for help. Remember to maintain your layered locks with regular trims and proper hair care.
