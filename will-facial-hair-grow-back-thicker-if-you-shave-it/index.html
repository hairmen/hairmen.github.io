Will Facial Hair Grow Back Thicker If You Shave It

Written by William Oct 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who's been trying to grow out your facial hair, you've probably heard the old wives' tale that shaving will make it grow back thicker. But is there any truth to this myth?

Description:

Before we dive into the answer to this question, let's first take a look at what facial hair actually is.

Facial hair, like any other hair on your body, grows from hair follicles. These follicles are located just beneath the surface of your skin, and they contain the cells responsible for producing hair. The thickness, color, and growth rate of your facial hair are all determined by your genetics, hormones, and other factors.

Now, let's explore whether or not shaving your facial hair will make it grow back thicker.

How to:

The answer is no, shaving your facial hair will not make it grow back thicker. This myth has been around for a long time, but it's just that - a myth. Shaving does not affect the thickness or growth rate of your facial hair.

When you shave your facial hair, you're simply cutting the hair that's already above the surface of your skin. You're not affecting the hair follicles beneath your skin, which are responsible for producing new hair. As a result, your facial hair will continue to grow at the same rate and thickness as it did before you shaved.

Step by Step:

Here's a step-by-step breakdown of what happens when you shave your facial hair:

  1. You apply shaving cream or gel to your face to lubricate the skin and hair.
  2. You use a razor to cut the hair above the surface of your skin.
  3. The cut hair falls away from your skin, leaving the hair follicles beneath your skin untouched.
  4. Your hair follicles continue to produce new hair at the same rate and thickness as before.

Tips:

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when shaving your facial hair:

  • Use a sharp razor to avoid irritation and cuts.
  • Apply shaving cream or gel to lubricate the skin and hair.
  • Shave in the direction of hair growth to prevent ingrown hairs.
  • Rinse your face with cool water after shaving to close your pores.
  • Moisturize your skin after shaving to prevent dryness and irritation.

FAQ:

Here are a few frequently asked questions about shaving and facial hair:

1. Will shaving make my facial hair grow back darker?

No, shaving does not affect the color of your facial hair. The darkness or lightness of your facial hair is determined by your genetics.

2. Will shaving make my facial hair grow back faster?

No, shaving does not affect the growth rate of your facial hair. Your hair follicles will continue to produce new hair at the same rate as before you shaved.

3. Should I shave my facial hair?

Whether or not you should shave your facial hair is a personal choice. Some people prefer the look and feel of a clean-shaven face, while others prefer to grow out their facial hair. It's up to you to decide what works best for you.

Hair Care:

Regardless of whether you shave your facial hair or grow it out, taking care of your hair is important. Here are a few tips for keeping your facial hair healthy:

  • Wash your face and facial hair regularly to remove dirt and oil.
  • Use a beard oil or balm to moisturize your facial hair and skin.
  • Trim your facial hair regularly to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or hot tools on your facial hair, as they can cause damage.

So, now you know the truth - shaving your facial hair will not make it grow back thicker. If you prefer a clean-shaven look, go ahead and shave. If you prefer to grow out your facial hair, that's okay too. Just remember to take care of your hair and skin to keep it healthy.
