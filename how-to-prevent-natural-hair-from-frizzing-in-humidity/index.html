How To Prevent Natural Hair From Frizzing In Humidity Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Jun 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizzy natural hair during humid days? Do you struggle to maintain your hair's sleekness and smoothness? Don't worry; you're not alone. Frizz is a common problem for those with natural hair, especially during humid weather. But, with the right hair care routine and techniques, you can prevent frizz and keep your hair looking its best. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to prevent natural hair from frizzing in humidity.

  • How to Prevent Frizzy Hair in Humidity
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Prevent Frizzy Hair in Humidity

Humidity is the enemy of natural hair, as it causes the hair to absorb excess moisture from the air, making it swell and frizz. But, here's what you can do to prevent frizz:

1. Use a Humidity-Proof Hair Product

Using a humidity-proof hair product, such as a leave-in conditioner, serum, or oil, can help to seal the hair cuticles, preventing moisture from entering the hair shaft. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or argan oil, as they are known for their moisturizing and anti-frizz properties.

2. Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it more prone to frizz. Instead, try to wash your hair once or twice a week and use a sulfate-free shampoo that won't dry out your hair.

3. Use a Microfiber Towel or T-Shirt to Dry Your Hair

Avoid using regular towels to dry your hair, as they can cause friction and breakage, leading to frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry.

4. Avoid Using Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers can damage the hair, making it more prone to frizz. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using heatless styling techniques like bantu knots or twist-outs.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to prevent frizzy hair in humidity:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Rinse your hair with cool water to seal the cuticles.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a humidity-proof leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends and mid-shaft. Comb through your hair to distribute the product evenly.

Step 3: Dry Your Hair

Use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt to blot your hair dry. Avoid rubbing or twisting your hair, as this can cause frizz.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Air-dry your hair or use heatless styling techniques like bantu knots or twist-outs. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to prevent frizzy hair in humidity:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh your hair down and cause greasiness.

FAQs

Q: Can I still use heat styling tools if I want to prevent frizz?

A: While it's best to avoid heat styling tools, you can still use them if you take precautions. Use a heat protectant spray or cream to protect your hair from heat damage, and avoid using the highest heat setting.

Q: How often should I trim my hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Hair Care Routine

Here's a sample hair care routine to prevent frizzy hair in humidity:

  • Wash your hair once or twice a week with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing it, focusing on the ends and mid-shaft.
  • Dry your hair with a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt.
  • Air-dry your hair or use heatless styling techniques like bantu knots or twist-outs.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can prevent frizzy natural hair in humidity and keep your hair looking its best. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid over-washing and heat styling, and use the right hair care products for your hair type. With a little patience and effort, you can achieve smooth and sleek natural hair, even in humid weather.
