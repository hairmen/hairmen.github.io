What To Do For Severe Postpartum Hair Loss

Written by Bardi Nov 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Postpartum hair loss can be a difficult experience for many new mothers. It’s a common condition that affects up to 50% of women after giving birth, but it can still be overwhelming and stressful. If you’re experiencing severe postpartum hair loss, here’s what you can do to address it.

Description

Postpartum hair loss, also known as telogen effluvium, is a type of hair loss that occurs after pregnancy. During pregnancy, the increased levels of estrogen in the body prevent hair from falling out as it normally would. After giving birth, however, estrogen levels drop, causing hair to enter a resting phase and eventually fall out. This can result in excessive hair shedding and thinning.

How to Address Severe Postpartum Hair Loss

1. Consult a Doctor

If you’re experiencing severe postpartum hair loss, it’s important to consult a doctor. They can help determine if there are any underlying medical conditions contributing to your hair loss and recommend appropriate treatment options.

2. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet with plenty of protein, iron, and other essential vitamins and minerals can help promote hair growth and prevent further hair loss. Foods like eggs, spinach, and salmon are great options.

3. Take Supplements

Supplements like biotin, vitamin D, and zinc can also help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. However, it’s important to consult a doctor before taking any supplements to ensure they’re safe for you.

4. Use Gentle Hair Products

Using gentle hair products that won’t damage or irritate your scalp can help prevent further hair loss. Look for products that are specifically formulated for thinning hair.

5. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoiding tight hairstyles like braids, buns, and ponytails can help reduce hair breakage and prevent further hair loss. Opt for looser hairstyles instead.

Step by Step Guide to Addressing Severe Postpartum Hair Loss

Step 1: Consult a Doctor

If you’re experiencing severe postpartum hair loss, make an appointment with your doctor. They can help determine if there are any underlying medical conditions contributing to your hair loss and recommend appropriate treatment options.

Step 2: Eat a Healthy Diet

Eat a healthy diet with plenty of protein, iron, and other essential vitamins and minerals. Foods like eggs, spinach, and salmon are great options.

Step 3: Take Supplements

If recommended by your doctor, take supplements like biotin, vitamin D, and zinc to help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss.

Step 4: Use Gentle Hair Products

Use gentle hair products that won’t damage or irritate your scalp. Look for products that are specifically formulated for thinning hair.

Step 5: Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like braids, buns, and ponytails. Opt for looser hairstyles instead.

Tips for Addressing Severe Postpartum Hair Loss

1. Be Patient

Postpartum hair loss can take several months to resolve on its own. Be patient and give your hair time to grow back.

2. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid harsh brushing or combing, and be gentle when towel-drying your hair. This can help prevent further hair loss.

3. Practice Self-Care

Postpartum hair loss can be stressful, so it’s important to take care of yourself. Practice self-care activities like yoga, meditation, or taking a relaxing bath.

FAQs

Q: Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

A: No, postpartum hair loss is usually temporary and resolves on its own within several months.

Q: Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

A: While postpartum hair loss can’t be prevented, eating a healthy diet and taking supplements may help reduce its severity.

Q: Can postpartum hair loss affect breastfeeding?

A: No, postpartum hair loss does not affect breastfeeding.

Hair Care for Severe Postpartum Hair Loss

In addition to the steps outlined above, here are some hair care tips for addressing severe postpartum hair loss:

  • Wash your hair less frequently to prevent further damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners.
  • Consider using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Try using a hair growth serum or oil to promote hair growth.

Remember, postpartum hair loss is a common condition that many women experience. With the right care and treatment, your hair will grow back in no time.
