Why Is My Hair Sticking Up Outside Tips And Tricks To Keep Your Hair In Place

Written by Robby Jul 02, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of seeing your hair sticking up outside? You’re not alone. Many people experience this problem, especially during the summer months when the air is hot and humid. In this article, we’ll discuss the reasons why your hair is sticking up outside and give you some tips and tricks to keep your hair in place.

Table of Contents

  • Reasons Why Your Hair Is Sticking Up Outside
  • How to Keep Your Hair in Place
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Taming Flyaways
  • Tips and Tricks for Managing Frizzy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Routine for Frizzy Hair

Reasons Why Your Hair Is Sticking Up Outside

There are several reasons why your hair might be sticking up outside:

Dry Air

During the winter months, the air can become very dry, which can cause static electricity to build up in your hair. This can make your hair stand on end and stick up in all directions.

Humidity

In the summer months, humidity can wreak havoc on your hair. Humidity causes your hair to absorb moisture from the air, which can cause it to frizz and stick up.

Heat Damage

If you use heating tools like straighteners or curling irons on your hair, the heat can damage your hair and cause it to stick up. This is especially true if you use these tools on a daily basis.

Product Buildup

If you use too much hair product, it can build up on your hair and cause it to stick up. This is because the product weighs down your hair and makes it harder to manage.

How to Keep Your Hair in Place

If you want to keep your hair in place, there are a few things you can do:

Use a Good Hairbrush

Invest in a good hairbrush that won’t damage your hair. A boar bristle brush is a good choice because it will distribute your hair’s natural oils evenly and help to prevent breakage.

Use the Right Products

Use hair products that are designed for your hair type. If you have curly hair, use products that are specifically formulated for curly hair. If you have straight hair, use products that are designed for straight hair.

Avoid Heat Damage

Avoid using heating tools on your hair as much as possible. If you do need to use them, make sure you use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Keep Your Hair Moisturized

Use a good quality conditioner to keep your hair moisturized. If your hair is dry, it will be more prone to frizz and flyaways.

Step-by-Step Guide to Taming Flyaways

If you’re dealing with flyaways, here’s a step-by-step guide to help you tame them:

Step 1: Apply Hair Oil

Apply a few drops of hair oil to the palm of your hand and rub your hands together. Then, run your fingers through your hair, focusing on the areas where you have flyaways.

Step 2: Use a Toothbrush

Take an old toothbrush and spray it with hairspray. Use the toothbrush to gently brush down any flyaways.

Step 3: Use a Flat Iron

If you have stubborn flyaways, use a flat iron to smooth them down. Make sure you use a heat protectant spray first.

Tips and Tricks for Managing Frizzy Hair

If you’re dealing with frizzy hair, here are some tips and tricks to help you manage it:

Tip 1: Use a Microfiber Towel

Instead of using a regular towel to dry your hair, use a microfiber towel. Regular towels can cause friction, which can make your hair frizzy.

Tip 2: Don’t Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can make it more prone to frizz. Try to wash your hair every other day instead of every day.

Tip 3: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent frizz. Apply it after you shampoo and condition your hair.

FAQs

Q: How can I prevent my hair from sticking up outside?

A: Use a good quality hairbrush, avoid using heating tools, and keep your hair moisturized with a good conditioner.

Q: What causes hair to stick up in the first place?

A: Hair can stick up due to dry air, humidity, heat damage, and product buildup.

Hair Care Routine for Frizzy Hair

If you have frizzy hair, here’s a hair care routine you can follow:

Step 1: Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Use a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for frizzy hair. Rinse your hair thoroughly to remove any product buildup.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized and prevent frizz.

Step 3: Dry Your Hair

Use a microfiber towel to gently dry your hair. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as this can cause friction and make your hair frizzy.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Use a styling product that is designed for frizzy hair, such as a smoothing serum or cream. Style your hair as desired.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your hair in place and prevent it from sticking up outside. Remember to use the right products for your hair type and avoid using heating tools as much as possible. With a little bit of effort, you can have smooth and manageable hair all year round.
