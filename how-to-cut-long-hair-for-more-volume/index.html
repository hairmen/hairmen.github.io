How To Cut Long Hair For More Volume

Written by Aprilia Oct 05, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of your long hair looking limp and lifeless? Cutting your hair may seem counterintuitive, but with the right techniques, it can actually add volume and bounce to your locks. In this article, we'll show you how to cut long hair for more volume in a relaxed and easy-to-understand way.

How to

Step 1: Choose the Right Length

The first step in cutting your long hair for more volume is to choose the right length. If your hair is currently all one length, consider adding some layers. Layers can remove some of the weight from your hair and create movement. If you already have layers, consider cutting them shorter to create even more volume.

Step 2: Use Texturizing Scissors

Texturizing scissors are designed to remove weight from your hair without actually cutting it shorter. They have teeth on one or both blades that create texture and movement in your hair. Use these scissors to cut into your layers, removing some of the bulk and creating more volume.

Step 3: Focus on the Crown

The crown of your head is the area where your hair naturally lifts away from your scalp. By focusing on this area, you can create even more volume throughout your hair. Use your texturizing scissors to cut into the hair at the crown, removing weight and creating lift.

Step 4: Add Bangs

Adding bangs to your haircut can create the illusion of more volume. Bangs that are cut straight across or at an angle can add fullness to the front of your hair, making it appear thicker and more voluminous.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help you cut your long hair for more volume:

  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different lengths and layers.
  • Consider getting a professional haircut if you're unsure about cutting your hair yourself.
  • Always cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

FAQ

How often should I cut my hair to maintain volume?

It's recommended that you get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the health and shape of your hair. Regular trims can also help prevent split ends, which can weigh down your hair and make it appear less voluminous.

Will cutting my hair make it grow faster?

No, cutting your hair won't make it grow faster. However, regular haircuts can help keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage, which can make it appear longer and fuller over time.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair for more volume, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and shape. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add even more fullness to your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair and make it appear thinner.

By following these tips and techniques, you can cut your long hair for more volume and achieve the thick, luscious locks you've always wanted. Happy cutting!
