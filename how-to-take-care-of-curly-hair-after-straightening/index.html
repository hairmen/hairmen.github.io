How To Take Care Of Curly Hair After Straightening

Written by Robby Nov 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you know the struggle of maintaining it. But sometimes, you may want to switch things up and straighten your hair for a different look. However, after straightening, it's important to take special care of your hair to prevent damage and maintain its health. In this article, we'll show you how to take care of curly hair after straightening.

How to Straighten Curly Hair

Straightening curly hair can be a challenging task, but with the right techniques and tools, you can achieve a sleek, straight look. Here are the steps to follow:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the heat of the straightener.
  3. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to smooth out the curls and create a sleek base for straightening.
  4. Section your hair and use a straightener to straighten each section. Use a low heat setting to prevent damage.
  5. Finish with a serum or oil to add shine and prevent frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide to Taking Care of Straightened Curly Hair

After straightening your curly hair, it's important to take special care of it to prevent damage and maintain its health. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Don't Wash Your Hair Immediately

Wait at least 24-48 hours before washing your hair to allow the natural oils to nourish your hair.

Step 2: Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

When you do wash your hair, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.

Step 3: Apply a Hair Mask

Once a week, apply a deep conditioning hair mask to restore moisture and nourish your hair.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent further damage to your hair.

Step 5: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently and avoid breakage.

Step 6: Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can prevent frizz and reduce hair breakage.

Tips for Maintaining Straightened Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you maintain your straightened curly hair:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much to prevent oil buildup and frizz.
  • Avoid tying your hair tightly to prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal parts as they can cause breakage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized throughout the day.
  • Avoid using products with alcohol as they can dry out your hair.

FAQs

Q: How often should I straighten my curly hair?

A: It's best to avoid straightening your hair too often as it can cause damage. Aim to straighten your hair no more than once a week.

Q: Can I use a hair straightener on wet hair?

A: No, it's not recommended to use a hair straightener on wet hair as it can cause damage. Wait until your hair is completely dry before using a straightener.

Q: Can I use a hair straightener on damaged hair?

A: It's best to avoid using a hair straightener on damaged hair as it can cause further damage. Focus on repairing your hair before straightening it.

Hair Care Products for Straightened Curly Hair

Here are some recommended hair care products for maintaining straightened curly hair:

  • Moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Deep conditioning hair mask
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Serum or oil
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Silk pillowcase

By following these tips and using the right hair care products, you can maintain the health and beauty of your straightened curly hair. Remember to be gentle with your hair and avoid using too much heat to prevent damage.
