How To Take Care Of Men s Permed Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 24, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're a man who has recently gotten a perm, congratulations! You've just taken a big step towards achieving your desired hairstyle. However, with great style comes great responsibility. Permed hair requires a bit more maintenance than your average haircut, but don't worry - we've got you covered. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about taking care of men's permed hair, from washing to styling and everything in between.

Description

Perming is a chemical process that changes the texture of your hair, making it curly or wavy. Men's perms have become increasingly popular in recent years, with many men opting for this hairstyle to achieve a more voluminous, textured look. Perms can last for several months, but they require proper care to maintain their shape and prevent damage to your hair.

How to Take Care of Men's Permed Hair

1. Wash your hair regularly

Washing your hair is essential to maintain healthy, permed hair. However, avoid washing your hair every day as it can strip away natural oils and cause your curls to lose their shape. Instead, aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls moisturized and defined.

2. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons can damage your permed hair and cause it to lose its shape. Instead, let your hair air dry and use a diffuser attachment if necessary. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and set them to a low temperature to avoid damage.

3. Don't brush your hair when it's dry

Brushing your hair when it's dry can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet, and avoid brushing your hair altogether when it's dry.

4. Use a leave-in conditioner

Permed hair can be prone to dryness, so using a leave-in conditioner can help keep your curls moisturized and defined. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing it, and don't rinse it out.

5. Sleep on a silk pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can prevent tangling and frizz and help your curls maintain their shape. Silk pillowcases are also gentler on your hair than cotton pillowcases, which can cause breakage.

Step by Step Guide to Taking Care of Men's Permed Hair

1. Wash your hair

Wet your hair and apply a sulfate-free shampoo, massaging it into your scalp and hair. Rinse thoroughly, then apply a sulfate-free conditioner, focusing on the ends of your hair. Leave the conditioner in for a few minutes, then rinse thoroughly.

2. Apply leave-in conditioner

Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Don't rinse it out.

3. Let your hair air dry

Don't use a blow dryer or towel to dry your hair. Instead, let it air dry naturally or use a diffuser attachment to dry it on a low setting.

4. Style your hair

Use your fingers to style your hair, scrunching it to enhance your curls. Avoid using a brush or comb.

5. Sleep on a silk pillowcase

Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent tangling and frizz.

Tips for Taking Care of Men's Permed Hair

  • Avoid swimming in chlorinated pools or saltwater, as they can dry out your hair and cause damage to your perm.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and cause damage to your perm.
  • Don't touch your hair too much, as it can cause frizz and disrupt your curls.

FAQs

How often should I wash my permed hair?

Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.

Can I still use hair gel or wax with a perm?

Yes, but avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and cause damage to your perm.

How long does a men's perm last?

A perm can last for several months, but it depends on your hair type and how well you take care of it.

Hair Care for Men's Permed Hair

Permed hair requires more maintenance than your average haircut, but with the right care, you can keep your curls looking healthy and defined. Remember to wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, avoid heat styling tools, and use a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized. Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent tangling and frizz, and trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends. With these tips, you'll have healthy, permed hair that turns heads wherever you go.
