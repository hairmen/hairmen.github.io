Why Don t I Look Good With Dark Hair Reasons And Solutions

Written by Daniel Jun 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you wondering why you don't look good with dark hair? Have you ever tried to dye your hair a darker shade but ended up feeling like it didn't suit you? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people struggle to find the right hair color that complements their skin tone and features. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why dark hair may not be the best choice for you and provide some tips on what you can do to make it work.

Table of Contents

  • Reasons Why Dark Hair May Not Suit You
  • Solutions to Make Dark Hair Work for You

Reasons Why Dark Hair May Not Suit You

There are several reasons why dark hair may not be the best choice for you:

Your Skin Tone

Your skin tone plays a crucial role in determining the best hair color for you. If you have a warm skin tone, you may look better with hair colors that have warm undertones such as golden brown, honey blonde, or copper red. On the other hand, if you have a cool skin tone, you may look better with hair colors that have cool undertones such as ash blonde, platinum blonde, or cool brown. If you have a neutral skin tone, you can pull off both warm and cool hair colors.

Your Natural Hair Color

Another factor to consider is your natural hair color. If you have light hair, going too dark may make you look washed out. If you have dark hair, going too light may not suit you either. It's best to stick to shades that are not too far from your natural hair color.

Your Facial Features

Dark hair can sometimes overpower your facial features and make them look less prominent. If you have delicate features such as small eyes, nose, or lips, going too dark may not be the best choice for you.

Solutions to Make Dark Hair Work for You

If you're determined to try dark hair, there are some solutions to make it work:

Try a Different Shade

If you've tried a dark hair color before and it didn't suit you, it may be because you didn't choose the right shade. Try a different shade that has warm or cool undertones depending on your skin tone. You can also try highlights or lowlights to add dimension to your hair.

Experiment with Makeup

If you're not used to having dark hair, it may take some time to adjust to it. Experiment with makeup to enhance your facial features and make them more prominent. Use eyeliner, mascara, and lipstick to create a balanced look.

Consult with a Hairstylist

If you're not sure which hair color suits you best, consult with a hairstylist. They can help you choose the right shade and provide some advice on how to maintain your hair.

How to Dye Your Hair Dark and Look Good

If you're ready to dye your hair dark, here are some steps to follow:

Choose the Right Shade

Choose a shade that complements your skin tone and natural hair color. If you're not sure which shade to choose, consult with a hairstylist.

Prepare Your Hair

Before dyeing your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Apply petroleum jelly around your hairline to prevent the dye from staining your skin.

Apply the Dye

Follow the instructions on the dye package and apply the dye evenly to your hair. Leave it on for the recommended time and rinse it off with lukewarm water.

Condition Your Hair

After dyeing your hair, use a conditioner to moisturize and nourish your hair. Leave it on for a few minutes and rinse it off with cool water.

Tips for Maintaining Dark Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining your dark hair:

Use Color-Safe Products

Use shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for color-treated hair to prevent fading and damage.

Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons as they can damage your hair and cause the color to fade faster. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.

Avoid Sun Exposure

Exposure to the sun can cause your hair color to fade faster. Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray when you're out in the sun.

FAQs

Is it normal to feel self-conscious after dyeing your hair a different color?

Yes, it's normal to feel self-conscious after dyeing your hair a different color. It takes some time to adjust to the new look, and it's natural to feel a bit insecure at first. Give yourself some time to get used to it.

Can I dye my hair dark if I have naturally light hair?

Yes, you can dye your hair dark if you have naturally light hair. Just make sure to choose a shade that complements your skin tone and features.

How often should I touch up my dark hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the color fades. On average, you should touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks to maintain the color.

Hair Care Tips for Dark Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your dark hair looking healthy and shiny:

Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.

Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Avoid washing your hair too often as it can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle. Stick to washing your hair every 2-3 days.

In conclusion, if you're wondering why you don't look good with dark hair, it may be because of your skin tone, natural hair color, or facial features. However, there are solutions to make dark hair work for you, such as choosing the right shade, experimenting with makeup, and consulting with a hairstylist. Follow these tips for maintaining your dark hair and enjoy your new look!
