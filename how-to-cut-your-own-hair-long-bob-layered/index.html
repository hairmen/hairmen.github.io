How To Cut Your Own Hair Long Bob Layered

Written by Luffy Aug 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and easy-to-maintain hairstyle, a long bob with layers might be the perfect choice for you. While going to a salon for a haircut can be expensive and time-consuming, cutting your own hair at home can save you money and give you more control over your look. In this article, we'll share step-by-step instructions on how to cut your own hair long bob layered, as well as tips for achieving the best results.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Success

Step-by-Step Guide

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to work in an area with good lighting and a large mirror to see what you're doing.

1. Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture and movement of your hair, which will make it easier to cut. Once your hair is dry, comb it to remove any tangles.

2. Section Your Hair

Using hair clips, divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. The top front sections should start at the top of your head and go down to your temples. The back sections should start at the crown of your head and go down to the nape of your neck.

3. Cut the Back

Start with the back sections. Take one section at a time, comb it straight down, and cut it to the desired length. Use the previous section as a guide to keep the length consistent. Cut the hair at a slight angle to create layers, starting from the middle of the hair and working your way down to the ends.

4. Cut the Front

Move to the front sections. Comb the hair straight down and cut it to the desired length, using the back sections as a guide. Cut the hair at an angle to create layers, starting from the middle of the hair and working your way down to the ends. It's important to cut the front sections at an angle to frame your face and create a soft, layered look.

5. Blend the Layers

After you've cut all four sections, blend the layers together by combing your hair straight down and cutting any uneven pieces. You can also use thinning shears to remove bulk and create a more textured look.

Tips for Success

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting your own hair long bob layered:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to the desired length. It's easier to cut more hair than it is to fix a mistake.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and ragged edges.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can look longer than it actually is. Wait until it's dry to get an accurate length.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting your own hair can be a bit tricky, but with practice, you'll get the hang of it.

FAQs

1. Can I cut my own hair long bob layered if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can. However, keep in mind that curly hair can spring up when it's cut, so it's best to cut it a bit longer than you want it to be.

2. How often should I trim my long bob layered?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain it. As a general rule, you should aim to trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it looking healthy and fresh.

3. Should I use a specific type of scissors to cut my own hair?

Yes, it's best to use hairdressing scissors, which are specifically designed for cutting hair. They have sharp blades and are comfortable to hold, which will make the process easier and more precise.

Hair Care After Cutting

Once you've cut your own hair long bob layered, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and nourished.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your long bob layered.

By following these tips, you can enjoy your new long bob layered haircut and keep it looking great for weeks to come.
