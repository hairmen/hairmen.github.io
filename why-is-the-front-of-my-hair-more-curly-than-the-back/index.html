Why Is The Front Of My Hair More Curly Than The Back

Written by Oliver Sep 08, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have relaxed hair, you may have noticed that the front of your hair is more curly than the back. This can be frustrating, especially if you're trying to achieve a certain look or style. In this article, we'll explore why this happens and what you can do to prevent it from happening.

Description

The front of your hair is more curly than the back because it's exposed to more heat and manipulation. When you style your hair, you're more likely to use heat tools like flat irons and curling irons on the front of your hair, which can cause damage and breakage. Additionally, you may brush or comb the front of your hair more often than the back, which can also lead to damage and breakage.

How to Prevent the Front of Your Hair from Being More Curly Than the Back

If you want to prevent the front of your hair from being more curly than the back, there are a few things you can do:

1. Use Heat Protectant

When you use heat tools on your hair, it's important to use a heat protectant to prevent damage. Look for a product that's specifically designed to protect relaxed hair from heat damage.

2. Limit Heat Styling

Try to limit the amount of heat styling you do on your hair. Instead, opt for heat-free styles like twists and braids. If you do use heat tools, use them sparingly and at a low temperature.

3. Be Gentle When Brushing and Combing

Brush and comb your hair gently, especially in the front. Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles to prevent damage and breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide to Preventing the Front of Your Hair from Being More Curly Than the Back

Follow these steps to prevent the front of your hair from being more curly than the back:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Rinse thoroughly and towel dry.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant to your hair, focusing on the front sections. Use your fingers to distribute the product evenly.

Step 3: Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair on a low setting, using a round brush to smooth out the front sections. Be gentle and don't pull too hard.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, style it using heat-free methods like twists or braids. If you do use heat tools, use them sparingly and at a low temperature.

Tips for Preventing the Front of Your Hair from Being More Curly Than the Back

Here are a few additional tips to help prevent the front of your hair from being more curly than the back:

1. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleep on a satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage.

2. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

3. Moisturize Your Hair

Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage.

FAQ

Q. Can I straighten the front of my hair to match the back?

A. Yes, you can straighten the front of your hair to match the back. However, it's important to use a heat protectant and to limit heat styling to prevent damage and breakage.

Q. Will using a relaxer on the front of my hair make it less curly?

A. Using a relaxer on the front of your hair may make it less curly, but it can also cause damage and breakage. It's important to consult with a professional stylist before using any chemical treatments on your hair.

Q. How often should I wash my hair?

A. It's best to wash your hair every 7-10 days, or as needed based on your hair type and styling routine.

Hair Care for Relaxed Hair

If you have relaxed hair, it's important to take good care of it to prevent damage and breakage. Here are a few tips:

1. Use Gentle Products

Use gentle, sulfate-free products that are specifically designed for relaxed hair.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized and healthy.

3. Protect Your Hair at Night

Wrap your hair in a satin scarf at night to prevent friction and breakage.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can prevent the front of your hair from being more curly than the back. Remember to be gentle and patient, and don't be afraid to consult with a professional stylist if you need additional help.
