How To Give Thick Straight Hair Volume Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Aug 12, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of your thick straight hair looking flat and lifeless? Do you wish you had more volume and bounce in your locks? Well, you’re in luck! In this article, we’ll be sharing some tips and tricks on how to give your thick straight hair volume. From blow-drying techniques to hair care products, we’ve got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • How to Give Your Thick Straight Hair Volume
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thick Straight Hair

How to Give Your Thick Straight Hair Volume

Before we dive into the tips and tricks, it’s important to understand the science behind hair volume. Hair volume is created by lifting the roots away from the scalp, creating space between the strands. This can be achieved through various techniques, such as blow-drying, backcombing, and using volumizing products.

For thick straight hair, the key is to create volume without adding too much weight or making the hair look greasy. Here are some tips and tricks to achieve that perfect balance:

1. Blow-Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow-drying your hair upside down is a great way to create volume at the roots. Start by flipping your hair over and blow-dry the roots in an upward direction. Once the roots are dry, flip your hair back up and continue blow-drying the rest of your hair.

2. Use the Right Products

Choose hair care products that are specifically designed for adding volume to hair. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are labeled “volumizing” or “thickening.” You can also use mousses, sprays, and powders that are designed to add volume to hair.

3. Backcomb Your Hair

Backcombing, also known as teasing, is a technique that involves combing the hair backwards towards the scalp to create volume. Use a fine-toothed comb and start at the roots, gently combing backwards towards the scalp. Be careful not to damage your hair or create tangles.

4. Use Velcro Rollers

Velcro rollers are a great tool for adding volume to hair. Roll sections of hair onto the rollers and leave them in for 10-15 minutes. Once you remove the rollers, your hair will have a natural-looking bounce and volume.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the tips and tricks, here’s a step-by-step guide to achieving voluminous hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Rinse thoroughly and gently towel-dry your hair.

Step 2: Apply Volumizing Product

Apply a volumizing product, such as a mousse or spray, to your hair. Focus on the roots and work your way down to the ends. Use a comb to distribute the product evenly.

Step 3: Blow-Dry Your Hair

Flip your hair over and blow-dry the roots in an upward direction. Once the roots are dry, flip your hair back up and continue blow-drying the rest of your hair. Use a round brush to add volume to the ends.

Step 4: Backcomb Your Hair

Use a fine-toothed comb and backcomb the roots to create volume. Be gentle and avoid tangles.

Step 5: Use Velcro Rollers

Roll sections of hair onto Velcro rollers and leave them in for 10-15 minutes. Once you remove the rollers, use your fingers to gently tousle your hair and create a natural-looking bounce.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for giving your thick straight hair volume:

  • Use a volumizing dry shampoo between washes to add volume and reduce oiliness.
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush and use it to backcomb your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy hair products, such as oils and serums, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.
  • Consider getting layers or a haircut that adds texture and movement to your hair.

FAQs

1. Can I use a volumizing shampoo every day?

Yes, you can use a volumizing shampoo every day. However, be sure to use a gentle, sulfate-free formula to avoid drying out your hair.

2. Will backcombing damage my hair?

Backcombing can cause damage if done improperly or too frequently. Be gentle and avoid tangles.

3. Can I use Velcro rollers on wet hair?

No, it’s best to use Velcro rollers on dry or slightly damp hair. Wet hair is more prone to breakage and damage.

Hair Care for Thick Straight Hair

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, voluminous hair. Here are some tips for taking care of your thick straight hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, too frequently.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or using a hair protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the health of your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can give your thick straight hair the volume and bounce it deserves. Don’t be afraid to experiment with different techniques and products until you find what works best for you. Happy styling!
