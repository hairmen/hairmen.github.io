How to Make Permed Hair Curly Again: A Step by Step Guide

If you have permed hair that has lost its curl, don't despair. There are several ways to bring back those bouncy, spiral locks. This guide will take you through the steps to make your permed hair curly again.

How to Make Permed Hair Curly Again: Step by Step

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step to making permed hair curly again is to wash it thoroughly. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Rinse your hair with cool water to help lock in moisture.

Step 2: Apply a Curl Enhancer

After washing your hair, apply a curl enhancer to help define your curls. You can use a mousse, gel, or cream depending on your hair type. Apply the product to damp hair and distribute it evenly from roots to ends.

Step 3: Diffuse Your Hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair. This will help to enhance your curls and reduce frizz. Hold the diffuser close to your hair and use a low heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 4: Scrunch Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, use your fingers to scrunch your curls. This will help to add volume and definition to your curls. Avoid using a brush or comb, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your curls.

Step 5: Sleep with a Silk Scarf

Before going to bed, wrap your hair with a silk scarf to help preserve your curls. Silk is a gentle fabric that won't cause friction or tangles in your hair while you sleep. In the morning, remove the scarf and use your fingers to scrunch your curls back into shape.

Tips for Making Permed Hair Curly Again

Here are some additional tips to help you make your permed hair curly again:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your hair.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair when styling to prevent damage to your curls.

FAQ: How to Make Permed Hair Curly Again

Can I use a curling iron to make my permed hair curly again?

Avoid using a curling iron on permed hair, as the heat can damage your curls and cause them to become frizzy. Instead, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls.

How often should I wash my permed hair?

It's best to wash permed hair no more than twice a week to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help preserve your curls.

Hair Care for Permed Hair

Permed hair requires special care to keep your curls looking their best. Here are some tips to help you maintain your permed hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your curls.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair when styling to prevent damage to your curls.
  • Wrap your hair with a silk scarf at night to help preserve your curls.

With these tips and tricks, you can make your permed hair curly again and keep your curls looking their best. Remember to take care of your hair and avoid damaging it with heat styling or harsh products.
