Is A Fade And Taper The Same Thing

Written by Christine Nov 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to my blog where I will be discussing the differences between a fade and a taper haircut. As a hair stylist, I have encountered numerous clients who have asked me whether a fade and taper are the same things. It's a common question that deserves a detailed answer. So, let's dive into it.

Table of Content

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Description

A fade and a taper are two terms used to describe men's haircuts. While both styles involve cutting the hair shorter towards the bottom, there are some key differences between the two. A fade is a style where the hair is cut very short on the sides and back and gradually becomes longer towards the top. A taper, on the other hand, is a style where the hair is cut shorter towards the bottom, but not as drastically as a fade. The hair gradually becomes longer towards the top, but not as much as in a fade.

How to

Getting a fade or a taper haircut requires a skilled barber or stylist. The process involves using clippers and scissors to gradually cut the hair shorter towards the bottom. The key difference between the two is the length and angle of the cut. A fade involves cutting the hair very short on the sides and back and gradually blending it towards the top. A taper involves cutting the hair shorter towards the bottom, but not as drastically as a fade. The hair is gradually blended towards the top, but not as much as in a fade.

Step by step

Here are the steps involved in getting a fade or taper haircut:

  1. Choose a style that suits your face shape and hair type.
  2. Consult with your barber or stylist to determine the length and angle of the cut.
  3. Sit back and relax while your hair is washed and prepped for the cut.
  4. The barber or stylist will use clippers and scissors to gradually cut the hair shorter towards the bottom.
  5. The hair will be blended towards the top to create a seamless transition from short to long.
  6. The final touches will be made to ensure that the cut is even and balanced.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting a fade or taper haircut:

  • Choose a barber or stylist who specializes in these styles to ensure that you get the best results.
  • Bring a picture of the style you want to help your barber or stylist understand your vision.
  • Be open to suggestions from your barber or stylist as they may have ideas that will enhance your chosen style.
  • Maintain your haircut by getting regular trims every 4-6 weeks.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about fades and tapers:

  • Q: Is a fade the same thing as a buzz cut?
    A: No, a buzz cut involves cutting the hair to the same length all over, while a fade involves cutting the hair shorter towards the bottom and gradually blending it towards the top.
  • Q: What face shapes suit a fade or taper?
    A: Fades and tapers can suit any face shape, but the length and angle of the cut will vary depending on the individual's features.
  • Q: Can fades and tapers be done on any hair type?
    A: Yes, fades and tapers can be done on any hair type, but the length and angle of the cut will vary depending on the individual's hair texture and thickness.

Hair Care

After getting a fade or taper haircut, it's important to maintain it with proper hair care. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners, as they can damage the hair.
  • Apply a hair oil or serum to keep the hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Get regular trims every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and length of the cut.

So, there you have it. A fade and taper are two similar but distinct haircuts. If you're considering getting one of these styles, make sure to consult with a skilled barber or stylist to ensure that you get the best results.
