What Is The Best Perm For Fine Thin Hair

Written by Daniel Oct 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have fine and thin hair, you might be looking for a way to add some body and volume to your locks. A perm is a chemical treatment that can help you achieve those bouncy, curly tresses you've always wanted. But with so many different types of perms available, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one for your hair. In this blog post, we'll discuss what is the best perm for fine thin hair, and provide you with tips and advice on how to care for your permed hair.

Description

Perms are a popular way to add curl and volume to hair, but they can also cause damage if not done correctly. Before you decide to get a perm, it's important to understand the different types of perms and how they work. A perm is a chemical treatment that changes the structure of your hair, making it curly or wavy. The process involves applying a solution to your hair that breaks down the bonds that hold it straight. Once the bonds are broken, the hair is wrapped around a perm rod, and a neutralizer is applied to set the curl.

How to

When it comes to perming fine and thin hair, it's important to choose a gentle formula that won't cause damage or breakage. Look for a perm that is specifically designed for fine hair, and avoid formulas that are too harsh or strong. You should also choose a hairstylist who has experience perming fine hair, as they will know how to create a natural-looking curl without damaging your hair.

Step 1: Consultation

The first step in getting a perm is to schedule a consultation with your hairstylist. During this consultation, you should discuss your hair type, concerns, and goals. Your hairstylist will examine your hair, and recommend the best perm for your hair type.

Step 2: Preparation

Before your perm appointment, you should prepare your hair by washing it with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any product buildup or oils that might interfere with the perm solution. You should also avoid using any heat styling tools or products that can damage your hair.

Step 3: Perm Application

During the perm application process, your hairstylist will apply the perm solution to your hair, and wrap it around perm rods. The size of the rods will depend on the type of curl you want to achieve. Once the solution has been applied, it will be left on your hair for a specified amount of time, depending on your hair type and the formula used.

Step 4: Neutralizer Application

After the perm solution has been rinsed out, your hairstylist will apply a neutralizer to your hair. This will set the curl and prevent any further chemical processing. The neutralizer will be left on your hair for a few minutes before it is rinsed out.

Step 5: Final Styling

Once the perm is complete, your hairstylist will style your hair to your liking. You can choose to blow-dry your hair, or let it air-dry for a more natural look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you care for your permed hair:

  • Avoid heat styling tools and products that can damage your hair
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 48 hours after getting a perm
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

FAQ

Q: Can I perm my hair if it is already colored?

A: Yes, you can perm colored hair. However, it's important to choose a gentle formula that won't cause damage or breakage.

Q: How long does a perm last?

A: A perm can last anywhere from three to six months, depending on your hair type and how well you care for it.

Q: Can I still straighten my hair after getting a perm?

A: Yes, you can still straighten your hair after getting a perm. However, you should avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.

Hair Care

Perming your hair can be a great way to add volume and body to your locks. However, it's important to choose the right formula and take good care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. By following the tips and advice in this blog post, you can achieve beautiful, bouncy curls that will last for months to come.
