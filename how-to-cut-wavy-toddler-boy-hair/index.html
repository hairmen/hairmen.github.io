How To Cut Wavy Toddler Boy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting a toddler boy's hair can be a daunting task, especially if he has wavy hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can give your little one a stylish haircut in the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut wavy toddler boy hair, including tips and hair care advice to keep your child's hair looking its best.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • The Cut
  • Finishing Touches

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your toddler's hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Scissors
  • Clipper with guards
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Water spray bottle
  • Haircutting shears for trimming around the ears

Preparation

Before you begin cutting your toddler's hair, it's important to prepare both your child and the space where you'll be cutting. Here's what you need to do:

  • Make sure your child is calm and comfortable. You may want to distract them with a toy or a video to keep them occupied during the haircut.
  • Cover your child with a haircutting cape or a towel to protect their clothes from hair clippings.
  • Set up your tools and make sure they are within easy reach.
  • Choose a well-lit area with a comfortable chair or stool for your child to sit in.

The Cut

Now that you're ready to start cutting, here's a step-by-step guide to follow:

  1. Start by using the clipper with a guard to trim the sides and back of your child's head. Use a longer guard for more length and a shorter guard for a closer trim.
  2. Next, use the scissors to trim the top of your child's hair. Start at the front and work your way back, cutting small sections at a time.
  3. Use the comb to lift sections of hair and trim them to the desired length.
  4. Once you've trimmed the top, use the scissors to trim around your child's ears and neckline.
  5. Check your work by running your fingers through your child's hair to make sure the length is even.

Finishing Touches

After you've finished cutting, it's time to add some finishing touches:

  • Use the water spray bottle to wet your child's hair and comb it into place.
  • Check for any stray hairs and trim them with the scissors or shears.
  • If your child has longer hair, you can add some styling product to keep it in place.

Tips for Cutting Wavy Toddler Boy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the best possible haircut:

  • Start with a longer guard on the clipper and work your way down to a shorter guard if necessary.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to make several small cuts than one big one.
  • If your child is fidgety, consider cutting their hair while they are sleeping.
  • Trim around the ears and neckline carefully, as these areas can be more sensitive.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting wavy toddler boy hair:

How often should I cut my toddler's hair?

It depends on how fast your child's hair grows. Most toddlers need a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

What if my child won't sit still during the haircut?

Try distracting them with a toy or a video, or consider cutting their hair while they are sleeping.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my toddler's hair?

No, you should use haircutting shears, as regular scissors can cause split ends and damage the hair.

Hair Care for Wavy Toddler Boy Hair

After you've cut your toddler's hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for children's hair.
  • Comb your child's hair regularly to prevent tangles.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage wavy hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to keep your child's hair moisturized.

Cutting wavy toddler boy hair doesn't have to be a stressful experience. With the right tools, preparation, and technique, you can give your little one a great haircut that they'll love. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and follow these tips to get the best results.
