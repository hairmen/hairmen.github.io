How To Manage Curly Thick Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Jul 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it's unique and beautiful, but on the other, it can be challenging to manage, especially if it's thick. However, with the right techniques and products, you can keep your curls looking healthy, defined, and manageable. In this guide, we'll show you how to manage curly thick hair, step by step.

How to Manage Curly Thick Hair

The first step in managing curly thick hair is to find the right products. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as shampoos, conditioners, and styling products. These products will help to nourish and define your curls, while also reducing frizz and tangles. You may also want to consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and soft.

Another important step is to find the right haircut for your hair type. Curly hair can be prone to frizz and tangles, so a good haircut can make all the difference. Look for a stylist who specializes in curly hair and ask for a cut that will enhance your natural curls and reduce bulk. Layers can also help to add volume and definition to your curls.

Finally, it's important to develop a good hair care routine. This means washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls, and avoiding heat styling as much as possible. You can also experiment with different styling techniques, such as scrunching, plopping, or diffusing, to find the method that works best for your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to managing curly thick hair:

Step 1: Wash and condition your hair

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. These products will help to cleanse and nourish your hair without stripping away its natural oils. Massage the shampoo into your scalp and work it through your hair, then rinse thoroughly. Follow up with a conditioner, focusing on the ends of your hair, and leave it in for a few minutes before rinsing.

Step 2: Detangle your curls

After washing, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your curls. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up, being careful not to pull or tug on your curls. You can also use your fingers to gently separate and define your curls.

Step 3: Apply styling products

Apply a styling product, such as a curl cream, gel, or mousse, to your damp hair. Work the product through your hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends, and scrunch your curls to encourage definition. You can also twist or braid your hair to create tighter curls or waves.

Step 4: Dry your hair

There are several ways to dry your hair, depending on your personal preference. You can air-dry your hair by letting it hang loose or using a microfiber towel to gently scrunch out excess water. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls without causing frizz or tangles.

Step 5: Refresh your curls

Throughout the day, you may need to refresh your curls to keep them looking defined and bouncy. You can do this by spritzing your hair with a curl refresher spray or misting it with water and scrunching your curls.

Tips for Managing Curly Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing curly thick hair:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce frizz and tangles while you sleep.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles, such as braids, buns, or ponytails, to keep your hair out of your face and prevent tangles.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap.

FAQ

How often should I wash my curly thick hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference, but most people with curly hair can go 2-3 days between washes. If your hair tends to get oily or sweaty, you may need to wash it more often.

What's the best way to detangle curly thick hair?

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your curls, starting at the ends and working your way up. You can also use a detangling spray or conditioner to make the process easier.

Can I straighten my curly thick hair?

Yes, but it's important to use heat protectant spray and avoid heat styling too often, as this can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage and frizz.

Hair Care for Curly Thick Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for curly thick hair:

  • Avoid using hot water, as this can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your curls.

By following these tips and techniques, you can manage your curly thick hair with ease, keeping your curls looking healthy, defined, and beautiful.
