How To Thin Out Bangs At Home A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thick bangs, you may find that they can be difficult to style, especially if you have fine hair. Thinning out your bangs can help you achieve a more manageable look, but it can be intimidating to do it at home. Don't worry- we've got you covered! In this article, we'll show you how to thin out your bangs at home like a pro.

How to Thin Out Bangs at Home

Before you start, you'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. Here's how to thin out your bangs:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see how your hair falls naturally and to trim your bangs evenly.

Step 2: Comb Your Bangs

Using a comb, separate your bangs from the rest of your hair. Make sure your bangs are combed straight down and are free of any tangles.

Step 3: Identify the Areas to Thin Out

Look at your bangs in the mirror and identify the areas that need to be thinned out. You may want to thin out the entire length of your bangs or just certain sections.

Step 4: Use the Point Cutting Technique

Point cutting is a technique that involves cutting into the hair at an angle, rather than straight across. This creates a softer, more textured look. To thin out your bangs using the point cutting technique, hold your scissors vertically, and make small cuts into your bangs at an angle. Be sure to cut only a little bit at a time, as it's easy to overdo it.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After making a few cuts, step back and check your progress in the mirror. Make sure you're achieving the look you want. If you need to thin out more, continue cutting in small sections.

Step 6: Blend the Edges

To avoid any harsh lines or unevenness, use your scissors to blend the edges of your bangs. Hold your scissors horizontally and make small, quick snips along the edges.

Step 7: Style Your Bangs

Once you've thinned out your bangs, style them as you normally would. You may find that they're easier to manage and style after thinning them out.

Tips for Thinning Out Your Bangs at Home

Thinning out your bangs at home can be a little daunting, but these tips will help you get the best results:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair
  • Consider cutting your bangs while they're dry for more accuracy
  • Don't overdo it- it's better to err on the side of caution and cut less than you think you need to
  • Be patient- it may take some time to achieve the look you want

FAQ

Can I thin out my bangs if they're curly?

Yes, you can thin out curly bangs using the same technique as described above. However, you may want to be especially careful not to cut too much, as curly hair can be more unpredictable.

Can I thin out my bangs if they're already thin?

If your bangs are already thin, you may not need to thin them out any further. However, if you want to add more texture or movement, you can use the point cutting technique to create a more layered look.

How often should I thin out my bangs?

The frequency of thinning out your bangs will depend on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it thickens. For most people, once every 6-8 weeks should be enough to keep your bangs looking their best.

Hair Care After Thinning Out Your Bangs

After thinning out your bangs, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends

Thinning out your bangs at home can be a great way to achieve a more manageable, stylish look. With a little patience and the right technique, you can achieve salon-quality results from the comfort of your own home. Good luck!
