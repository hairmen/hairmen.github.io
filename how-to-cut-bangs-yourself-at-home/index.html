How To Cut Bangs Yourself At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own bangs can seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and technique, it can be easily done at home. Not only is it a money-saver, but it's also a way to experiment with your style on your own terms. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about how to cut bangs yourself at home.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • How to Cut Bangs Yourself at Home
  • Tips for Cutting Bangs
  • FAQs About Cutting Bangs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Bangs

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your bangs, you'll need to gather a few essential tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties to section off hair
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and frayed hair. You'll want to use a comb with fine teeth to help section off your hair, and clips or hair ties to keep the rest of your hair out of the way. The spray bottle filled with water will help to keep your hair damp and easier to cut.

How to Cut Bangs Yourself at Home

Now that you have your tools ready, it's time to get started. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut bangs yourself at home:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair straight down and decide how thick you want your bangs to be.
  2. Use your comb to section off the hair you want to cut. For straight across bangs, section off a triangle-shaped piece of hair that starts at the center of your forehead and goes down to the outer corners of your eyebrows.
  3. Use your fingers to hold the hair in place, then use your scissors to cut straight across. Start with small cuts and work your way up, as you can always cut more hair off but you can't put it back on!
  4. If you want side-swept bangs, section off the hair you want to cut at an angle. Start at the center of your forehead and comb the hair to one side, then cut at an angle downwards.
  5. After cutting one side, use it as a guide for the other side to make sure they're even.
  6. Trim any stray hairs or uneven bits with small snips.
  7. Check your work in the mirror, and make any additional small cuts if necessary.

And that's it! With a bit of patience and practice, you can cut your own bangs at home.

Tips for Cutting Bangs

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your own bangs:

  • Start with small cuts, as it's easier to cut more hair off than to add it back on.
  • Make sure your hair is damp, as dry hair can be harder to manage and cut evenly.
  • Use a comb to section off your hair, and clips or hair ties to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.
  • Be patient and take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be stressful, so don't be afraid to step away for a few minutes if you need to.

FAQs About Cutting Bangs

Here are a few commonly asked questions about cutting bangs:

How often should I trim my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look uneven. On average, every 4-6 weeks is a good time to trim your bangs.

What if I mess up and cut too much off?

Don't panic! Depending on how much hair you cut off, you may be able to style your bangs differently to hide the mistake. If it's really bad, you can always wait for your hair to grow back or visit a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care After Cutting Bangs

After cutting your own bangs, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners or curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your bangs too much, as this can cause breakage and split ends.

With these tips, you'll be able to cut your own bangs at home and keep them looking great. Happy cutting!
