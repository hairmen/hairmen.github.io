Description

There are seven basic face shapes: oval, round, square, diamond, heart, oblong, and triangle. Out of these seven face shapes, the second rarest is the diamond-shaped face.

Diamond-shaped faces are characterized by a narrow forehead and jawline with wide cheekbones. People with diamond-shaped faces also tend to have angular features and a pointed chin.

If you have a diamond-shaped face, you may find it challenging to find hairstyles that flatter your unique features. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll show you how to determine your face shape and provide tips on choosing the best hairstyles for diamond-shaped faces.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Determining your face shape is easy. All you need is a mirror and a tape measure.

Here are the steps:

Step 1: Measure Your Forehead

Place the tape measure at the widest point of your forehead, just above your eyebrows. Measure straight across to the other side of your forehead.

Step 2: Measure Your Cheekbones

Place the tape measure at the widest point of your cheekbones, just below the outer corner of your eyes. Measure straight across to the other side of your cheekbones.

Step 3: Measure Your Jawline

Place the tape measure at the widest point of your jawline, just below your ears. Measure straight across to the other side of your jawline.

Step 4: Measure Your Face Length

Place the tape measure at the center of your hairline and measure straight down to the tip of your chin.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know your face shape, it's time to choose a hairstyle that flatters your features. Here are some tips:

Step 1: Emphasize Your Cheekbones

Since diamond-shaped faces have wide cheekbones, you'll want to choose hairstyles that emphasize this feature. Try a bob or a layered haircut that falls just below your cheekbones.

Step 2: Add Volume to the Crown

Diamond-shaped faces tend to have a narrow forehead, so adding volume to the crown of your head can balance out your features. Try a high ponytail or a voluminous updo.

Step 3: Avoid Narrow Haircuts

Since diamond-shaped faces have a narrow forehead and jawline, you'll want to avoid hairstyles that are too narrow or too long. This can make your face appear even more angular. Instead, opt for hairstyles that add width to your face, like a side-swept bang or a layered haircut.

Tips for Choosing Hairstyles for Second Rarest Face Shape

Here are some additional tips for choosing hairstyles for diamond-shaped faces:

  • Avoid center parts, as they can make your face appear even more narrow.
  • Try side-swept bangs to balance out your features.
  • Choose hairstyles with volume at the crown of your head.
  • Emphasize your cheekbones with a bob or a layered haircut.
  • Avoid hairstyles that are too long or too narrow.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about diamond-shaped faces:

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a diamond-shaped face?

Avoid hairstyles that are too narrow or too long, as they can make your face appear even more angular. You'll also want to avoid center parts, as they can make your face look even more narrow.

Can I wear my hair in a bun if I have a diamond-shaped face?

A high bun can look great on diamond-shaped faces, as it adds volume to the crown of your head and balances out your features.

What is the best haircut for diamond-shaped faces?

A bob or a layered haircut that falls just below your cheekbones can emphasize your wide cheekbones and balance out your features.

Hair Care for Second Rarest Face Shape

When it comes to hair care for diamond-shaped faces, you'll want to keep your hair healthy and well-maintained. Use a good shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair soft and shiny, and avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it.

You may also want to consider using a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

Now that you know what the second rarest face shape is and how to choose hairstyles that flatter your features, you can confidently rock any look you choose.
