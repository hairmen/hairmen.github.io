How To Cut My Son s Long Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Sep 13, 2023 · 4 min read

As a parent, cutting your child's hair can be a daunting task, especially when dealing with long and curly hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut for your son in the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your son's long curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut My Son's Long Curly Hair
  • Tools You'll Need
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Great Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After the Haircut

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your son's hair, you will need to gather the necessary tools:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clippers (optional)
  • Spray bottle with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have the necessary tools, follow these steps to cut your son's long curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Hair

Start by washing your son's hair with shampoo and conditioner. Once the hair is clean, use a towel to dry it thoroughly.

Step 2: Comb Hair

Use a comb to detangle the hair and remove any knots or tangles. Start from the bottom and work your way up to avoid pulling on the hair.

Step 3: Decide on Length

Decide on the desired length for your son's hair. You can use hair clippers to trim the hair to a desired length or use scissors to trim the hair to the desired length.

Step 4: Cut Hair

Use scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Start from the back of the head and work your way to the front. Use the comb to hold the hair in place while cutting.

Step 5: Blend Hair

Use scissors to blend the hair and create a seamless transition between the different lengths. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut diagonally to blend.

Step 6: Trim Bangs

If your son has bangs, use scissors to trim them to the desired length. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

Check the hair to ensure that it is even on both sides. Use scissors to make any necessary adjustments.

Step 8: Style Hair

Use a comb and styling products to style your son's hair as desired.

Tips for a Great Haircut

Here are some tips to make sure you achieve a great haircut for your son:

  • Start with clean and dry hair
  • Use sharp scissors
  • Work slowly and carefully
  • Use a comb to hold the hair in place while cutting
  • Check for evenness frequently

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your son's long curly hair:

Can I use hair clippers to cut my son's hair?

Yes, hair clippers can be a great tool to use when cutting your son's hair. They can help achieve an even cut and make the process quicker.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

If you make a mistake while cutting, don't panic. You can always make adjustments and fix any unevenness with scissors.

How often should I cut my son's hair?

The frequency of haircuts depends on the desired length and style. As a general rule, you should aim to cut your son's hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care After the Haircut

After cutting your son's hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain the new style:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner when washing the hair
  • Avoid using hot tools such as hair dryers and straighteners
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep the hair moisturized

Cutting your son's long curly hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut for your son and save money on trips to the salon. Just remember to take your time and follow the steps carefully.
