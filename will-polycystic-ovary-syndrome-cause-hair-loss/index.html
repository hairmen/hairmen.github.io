Will Polycystic Ovary Syndrome Cause Hair Loss

Written by Bardi Jul 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a common health condition that affects women of reproductive age. It is characterized by hormonal imbalances that can result in a range of symptoms, including irregular periods, weight gain, and acne. One of the less well-known symptoms of PCOS is hair loss. In this blog post, we will explore whether PCOS can cause hair loss and provide some tips for managing this symptom.

Description

PCOS is a hormonal disorder that affects the ovaries. Women with PCOS have elevated levels of androgens, which are male hormones that are normally present in women at low levels. This hormonal imbalance can cause a range of symptoms, including acne, weight gain, and irregular periods. In some cases, it can also cause hair loss.

There are a few different ways that PCOS can contribute to hair loss. First, the hormonal imbalances can disrupt the hair growth cycle, causing hair to fall out prematurely. Second, PCOS can cause inflammation in the scalp, which can damage hair follicles and lead to hair loss. Finally, some women with PCOS may have an underlying autoimmune condition that is causing their hair loss.

How to Determine If PCOS is Causing Your Hair Loss

If you have noticed that you are losing hair and are concerned that it may be related to PCOS, there are a few steps you can take to determine the cause. The first step is to see your doctor or a dermatologist who specializes in hair loss. They can evaluate your hair loss and determine whether it is related to PCOS or another underlying condition.

If your doctor determines that your hair loss is related to PCOS, they may recommend additional testing to evaluate your hormone levels and rule out any other underlying conditions. They may also recommend medications or lifestyle changes to help manage your PCOS symptoms and prevent further hair loss.

Step by Step Guide to Managing Hair Loss Caused by PCOS

If you have been diagnosed with PCOS-related hair loss, there are several steps you can take to manage this symptom:

  1. Eat a balanced diet that is rich in protein, iron, and other nutrients that support hair growth.
  2. Take supplements that support hair health, such as biotin and vitamin D.
  3. Avoid harsh hair treatments, such as chemical relaxers and hot oil treatments, which can damage hair.
  4. Use gentle hair products that are free from harsh chemicals, sulfates, and parabens.
  5. Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on the hair and cause damage.
  6. Consider using hair loss treatments, such as minoxidil or finasteride, under the guidance of a dermatologist.
  7. Manage your PCOS symptoms with medications or lifestyle changes, as recommended by your doctor.

Tips for Managing Hair Loss Caused by PCOS

Here are a few additional tips for managing hair loss caused by PCOS:

  • Be patient. Hair loss caused by PCOS can take time to reverse, so it is important to be patient and consistent with your treatment.
  • Take care of your overall health. PCOS is often linked to other health conditions, such as insulin resistance and diabetes, so it is important to take care of your overall health to manage your PCOS symptoms and prevent further hair loss.
  • Consider talking to a therapist or counselor. Hair loss can be a difficult symptom to manage, and it can take a toll on your mental health. Talking to a therapist or counselor can help you cope with the emotional aspects of hair loss and PCOS.

FAQs

Can hair loss caused by PCOS be reversed?

Yes, hair loss caused by PCOS can often be reversed with the right treatment. This may include medications, lifestyle changes, and hair loss treatments.

Is hair loss a common symptom of PCOS?

Hair loss is a less common symptom of PCOS, but it can occur in some women with the condition.

Will hair loss caused by PCOS grow back?

If hair loss is caused by PCOS, it is possible for hair to grow back with the right treatment. However, it can take time for hair to regrow and for the hair growth cycle to return to normal.

Hair Care Tips for Women with PCOS

If you have PCOS, it is important to take extra care of your hair to prevent further damage and hair loss. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid harsh hair treatments, such as chemical relaxers and hot oil treatments, which can damage hair.
  • Use gentle hair products that are free from harsh chemicals, sulfates, and parabens.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on the hair and cause damage.
  • Eat a balanced diet that is rich in protein, iron, and other nutrients that support hair growth.
  • Take supplements that support hair health, such as biotin and vitamin D.

In conclusion, PCOS can cause hair loss in some women, but there are steps you can take to manage this symptom. If you are experiencing hair loss and are concerned that it may be related to PCOS, be sure to see your doctor or a dermatologist who specializes in hair loss. They can help you determine the cause of your hair loss and recommend a treatment plan that is right for you.
