Ways To Keep Your Hair During Chemo A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Nov 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Dealing with cancer is difficult enough, but losing your hair during chemotherapy can make the experience even harder. As a result, many people look for ways to keep their hair during chemo. In this article, we’ll explore some of the best ways to do just that. Here’s a table of contents to help you navigate:

Table of Contents

  • How to Keep Your Hair During Chemo
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Keeping Your Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care During Chemo

How to Keep Your Hair During Chemo

While there’s no guaranteed way to keep your hair during chemotherapy, there are a few things you can try that may help:

  • Use a cooling cap during chemotherapy
  • Take hair care supplements like biotin and folic acid
  • Avoid harsh hair treatments like dyeing or perming
  • Keep your scalp healthy with gentle shampoos and conditioners
  • Avoid excessive heat styling and tight hairstyles

Keep in mind that everyone’s experience with chemotherapy is different, so what works for one person may not work for another. It’s important to talk to your doctor and do your own research to find the best options for you.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you’re interested in trying a cooling cap to keep your hair during chemotherapy, here’s a step-by-step guide:

  1. Talk to your doctor about whether a cooling cap is right for you.
  2. Find a cooling cap provider in your area or online.
  3. Follow the provider’s instructions for using the cooling cap before, during, and after chemotherapy.
  4. Be prepared for some discomfort during the cooling cap process, as it can be cold and uncomfortable.
  5. Continue using the cooling cap for as long as you’re comfortable with it and for as long as your provider recommends.

Tips for Keeping Your Hair

In addition to the steps outlined above, here are a few more tips for keeping your hair during chemotherapy:

  • Be gentle with your hair when washing and styling it
  • Use a soft-bristled brush or a wide-toothed comb to avoid pulling out hair
  • Avoid using hair dryers, curling irons, and other heat styling tools
  • Try wearing a scarf, hat, or wig to protect your scalp from the sun and wind
  • Stay hydrated and eat a balanced diet to keep your hair healthy

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about keeping their hair during chemotherapy:

Can I dye my hair during chemotherapy?

It’s generally not recommended to dye or perm your hair during chemotherapy, as this can damage your hair and scalp even more. However, you should talk to your doctor if you have specific concerns or questions.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after chemotherapy?

It varies from person to person, but most people start to see hair growth within 3-6 months after chemotherapy ends. It may take up to a year for your hair to fully grow back.

Are there any medications that can help me keep my hair during chemotherapy?

There are some medications on the market that claim to help keep your hair during chemotherapy, but there’s no solid evidence that they work. Talk to your doctor before trying any of these medications.

Hair Care During Chemo

Even if you do end up losing your hair during chemotherapy, it’s important to take care of your scalp and any remaining hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use gentle shampoos and conditioners
  • Moisturize your scalp with a gentle lotion or oil
  • Avoid scratching or picking at your scalp
  • Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and wind

Remember, losing your hair during chemotherapy is a difficult experience, but it’s not the end of the world. There are many things you can do to take care of yourself and feel confident and beautiful, even without hair.
