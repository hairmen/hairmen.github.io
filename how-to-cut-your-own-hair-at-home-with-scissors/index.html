How To Cut Your Own Hair At Home With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts every few weeks? Do you want to try cutting your own hair at home with scissors? With the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a salon-worthy haircut on your own. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of how to cut your own hair at home with scissors.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Tips for a Successful DIY Haircut
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Your DIY Haircut

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips or hair ties
  • Mirror (preferably a full-length mirror and a handheld mirror)
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll want to make sure it's clean and dry. It's also a good idea to wear a shirt that you don't mind getting hair on.

Start by combing your hair and removing any tangles. Then, use the sectioning clips or hair ties to divide your hair into manageable sections.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair will make it easier to cut and ensure that you don't miss any sections. Here's how to section your hair:

  1. Start by dividing your hair into two sections: the top and the bottom. Use a comb to create a horizontal parting at the back of your head, just above your ears. Clip the top section of hair out of the way.
  2. Next, create a vertical parting down the center of your head, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. This will divide the bottom section of hair into two halves. Clip one half out of the way.
  3. Now, create a horizontal parting across the back of your head, just below the first parting. This will create a middle section of hair. Clip the middle section out of the way.
  4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 on the other side of your head.
  5. You should now have four sections of hair: the top section, the two side sections, and the middle section.

Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting! Here's how to do it:

  1. Start with the bottom section of hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of the hair between your fingers and cut them straight across.
  2. Continue cutting small sections of hair, moving from the back to the front of your head. Make sure to cut each section to the same length.
  3. Once you've finished the bottom section, unclip the middle section and repeat the process.
  4. Finally, unclip the top section and cut it to the desired length. You can use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head and make sure everything is even.

Tips for a Successful DIY Haircut

Here are some tips to help you achieve a successful DIY haircut:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut slowly. It's always better to cut too little than too much.
  • Use the comb to lift the hair up and away from your head before cutting. This will create a more even cut.
  • If you're cutting layers, start with longer layers and work your way up to shorter layers.
  • When in doubt, it's better to leave a section of hair uncut and come back to it later.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own hair with regular scissors?

While it's possible to cut your own hair with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Haircutting scissors are designed to create a clean, even cut and will give you better results.

How often should I cut my own hair?

How often you cut your hair will depend on your hair type and desired style. As a general rule, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Your DIY Haircut

After you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain your new style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • If you're not happy with your DIY haircut, don't panic! Hair grows back, and you can always visit a professional stylist to fix any mistakes.

By following these steps and tips, you can successfully cut your own hair at home with scissors. Happy cutting!
