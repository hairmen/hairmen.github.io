Most Flattering Haircuts For Fat Faces In 2023

Written by Bardi Oct 05, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have a round or chubby face, finding a flattering haircut can be a challenge. The right haircut can accentuate your best features, while also slimming and elongating your face. In this article, we will discuss the most flattering haircuts for fat faces in 2023. From short and sassy to long and wavy, we’ve got you covered. So, let’s get started!

How to Choose the Right Haircut for Your Face Shape

Before we dive into the best haircuts for fat faces, it’s important to understand how to choose the right haircut for your face shape. There are several face shapes, including round, oval, square, heart, and diamond. Each face shape has its own unique characteristics, and certain haircuts will work better for some shapes than others.

If you have a round face, the goal is to create angles and elongate your face. Look for haircuts that are longer on the top and shorter on the sides. This will create the illusion of length and balance out your face. Avoid blunt cuts and heavy bangs, as these can make your face appear even rounder.

Short Haircuts for Fat Faces

Short haircuts can be a great option for those with fat faces. A pixie cut or a bob can be a fantastic choice, as they both create angles and add height to your face. A layered bob can also work well, as the layers will frame your face and create movement.

If you’re feeling daring, a shaved head can also be a great option. This will draw attention away from your face and create a bold, edgy look.

Medium Length Haircuts for Fat Faces

Medium length haircuts can also be flattering for those with fat faces. A shoulder-length cut with layers can be a great option, as it will add volume and create movement. A long bob, or “lob,” is another great choice. This cut falls just above the shoulders and can be styled straight or wavy.

If you prefer a more layered look, a shag haircut can be a fantastic choice. This style features choppy layers and can be styled with a side part or messy waves.

Long Haircuts for Fat Faces

Long haircuts can also work well for those with fat faces. The key is to add layers and texture to create movement and dimension. A long, layered cut with side-swept bangs can be a great option. This style will frame your face and create a slimming effect.

If you prefer a straighter look, a blunt cut with subtle layers can be a great choice. This will add volume to your hair without overwhelming your face.

Tips for Maintaining Your Haircut

Once you’ve found the perfect haircut for your face shape, it’s important to maintain it properly. Regular trims are essential to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. You should also invest in quality products, such as shampoos and conditioners, to keep your hair looking its best.

If you’re struggling with frizzy or unruly hair, consider using a smoothing serum or oil. This will help tame your hair and create a sleek, polished look.

FAQs

What is the most flattering haircut for a round face?

A layered bob or a pixie cut can be a great choice for those with round faces. These cuts create angles and add height, which can help elongate your face.

Can I wear bangs with a fat face?

Yes, you can wear bangs with a fat face. However, it’s important to choose the right type of bangs. Side-swept bangs or curtain bangs can be a great option, as they will frame your face and create a slimming effect. Avoid heavy, blunt bangs, as these can make your face appear even rounder.

Is it better to have short or long hair with a fat face?

Both short and long haircuts can be flattering for those with fat faces. The key is to add layers and texture to create movement and dimension.

Hair Care Tips for Fat Faces

When it comes to hair care, there are a few tips that can help keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful. First, avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, too often. These can damage your hair and lead to split ends.

Second, be gentle when brushing and styling your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

Finally, be sure to protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors. Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF when spending time outdoors.
