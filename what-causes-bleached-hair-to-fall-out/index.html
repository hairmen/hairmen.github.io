What Causes Bleached Hair To Fall Out

Description

Bleached hair has been a popular trend for years, and many people opt for this look to achieve a lighter hair color. However, bleaching can have damaging effects on hair, causing it to become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. In some cases, bleached hair can even fall out. In this article, we will explore the causes of bleached hair falling out and discuss ways to prevent this from happening.

How to

To prevent bleached hair from falling out, it is important to understand what causes it in the first place. There are several factors that can contribute to hair loss after bleaching, including:

  • Overprocessing: If hair is bleached too often or for too long, it can become damaged and fall out.
  • Chemical damage: Bleaching can weaken the hair, making it more susceptible to damage from other chemical treatments.
  • Heat styling: Using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners on bleached hair can further damage it and cause it to break.
  • Poor nutrition: Hair needs nutrients like protein and vitamins to stay healthy. Without them, bleached hair can become weak and fall out.

Step by step

To prevent hair loss after bleaching, follow these steps:

  1. Avoid overprocessing: Do not bleach hair too often or for too long.
  2. Limit chemical treatments: Avoid using other chemical treatments on bleached hair, such as perms or relaxers.
  3. Avoid heat styling: Let hair air dry or use a low heat setting on hot tools to prevent further damage.
  4. Eat a healthy diet: Make sure to consume enough protein and vitamins to keep hair healthy and strong.
  5. Use a deep conditioning treatment: Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and strengthen bleached hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair loss after bleaching:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner specifically formulated for bleached hair to prevent further damage.
  • Avoid using hair elastics or clips that can pull on hair and cause it to break.
  • Avoid brushing wet hair, as it is more prone to breakage.
  • Consider getting regular trims to remove damaged ends and prevent split ends from traveling up the hair shaft.

FAQ

Can bleached hair grow back?

Yes, bleached hair can grow back. However, it may take some time for new, healthy hair to grow in.

Can hair loss from bleaching be reversed?

In some cases, hair loss from bleaching can be reversed by taking steps to nourish and strengthen the hair. This may include using a deep conditioning treatment, eating a healthy diet, and avoiding further damage from chemical treatments and heat styling.

Is it normal for bleached hair to fall out?

While some hair loss after bleaching is normal, excessive hair loss can be a sign of damage and should be addressed.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to preventing hair loss after bleaching. Make sure to use gentle, nourishing products and avoid overprocessing and heat styling. Eating a healthy diet and getting regular trims can also help keep hair strong and healthy.
