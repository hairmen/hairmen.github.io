How To Tell What Texture Your Hair Is

Written by Christine Nov 25, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you confused about what texture your hair is? Knowing your hair's texture is crucial to take care of it properly. In this article, we will guide you through the steps to determine your hair texture.

Table of Content

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips

Description

The texture of your hair refers to the diameter of each hair strand. There are generally three types of hair textures; fine, medium, and coarse.

Knowing your hair's texture is essential when choosing the right hair products, styling methods, and hair care routines. Fine hair is delicate, and using heavy products or tools can damage it. Coarse hair, on the other hand, can handle heavier products and requires more moisture to prevent breakage.

How to

To determine your hair's texture, you need to examine a single strand of hair. Here's how to do it:

Step by Step:

  1. Wash and condition your hair and let it air dry.
  2. Take a single strand of hair from the crown of your head.
  3. Hold the hair between your thumb and forefinger.
  4. Run your fingers up and down the strand, feeling for its thickness.
  5. If you can hardly feel the strand or can barely see it, your hair is fine.
  6. If you can feel the hair strand slightly, your hair is medium.
  7. If you can feel the strand distinctly and it looks thick, your hair is coarse.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you determine your hair texture accurately:

  • Don't confuse hair density with hair texture. Density refers to the number of hairs on your head, while texture refers to the diameter of each hair strand.
  • The texture of your hair can change over time. Aging, hormonal changes, and chemical treatments can affect your hair texture.
  • It's easier to determine your hair texture when it's clean and dry. Avoid using any hair products before you examine your hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I determine my hair texture by looking at it?

A: No, you can't determine your hair texture by just looking at it. You need to examine a single strand of hair to determine its thickness.

Q: How often should I determine my hair texture?

A: You only need to determine your hair texture once, as it generally remains constant throughout your life.

Hair Care

Once you know your hair texture, you can choose the right hair products and styling methods to take care of your hair.

For fine hair, use lightweight products and avoid heat styling tools. For medium hair, use a mix of lightweight and heavier products and avoid using too many heat styling tools. For coarse hair, use heavier products and regular deep conditioning treatments to keep your hair moisturized.

Knowing your hair texture is crucial to take care of your hair's health and appearance. Follow the steps mentioned above to determine your hair texture and choose the right hair care routine for your hair!
