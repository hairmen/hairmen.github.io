How To Cut The Back Of Your Own Hair Male A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 10, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts every month? Do you want to take matters into your own hands and learn how to cut the back of your own hair male? Look no further! In this article, we’ll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut the back of your own hair male, as well as some tips and FAQs.

How to Cut the Back of Your Own Hair Male

Before we get started, it’s important to note that cutting your own hair can be tricky, especially if you’re not used to it. It’s always better to start with a longer length and work your way shorter, as you can always trim more later. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • A clipper or trimmer
  • A mirror
  • A comb
  • Scissors
  • A cape or towel to protect your clothing

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by washing and towel-drying your hair.

2. Stand in front of a mirror and use a comb to create a part down the middle of your head.

3. Clip one side of your hair up and out of the way.

4. Using your clipper or trimmer, start at the nape of your neck and work your way up towards the part, being careful not to create a harsh line.

5. Repeat on the other side, making sure to keep the length even.

6. Once you’ve finished the back, unclip the top section of your hair and use the comb to blend the back and sides together.

7. Use your scissors to trim any long hairs that may be sticking out.

8. Finally, style your hair as desired.

Tips

- If you’re not sure how short to go, start with a longer guard on your clipper or trimmer and work your way down gradually.

- Don’t be afraid to ask for help if you’re struggling to see the back of your head.

- Invest in a good quality clipper or trimmer to make the process easier.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a regular pair of scissors to cut my hair?

A: It’s not recommended, as regular scissors can create uneven cuts and damage the hair.

Q: How often should I cut the back of my hair?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and your desired length. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good rule of thumb.

Q: I made a mistake and cut too much off. What should I do?

A: Don’t panic! Depending on the severity of the mistake, you may be able to salvage the haircut or you may need to wait for it to grow out. If you’re really unsure, it’s always best to seek the help of a professional.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair.

Now that you know how to cut the back of your own hair male, you can save time and money by doing it yourself. With a little practice and patience, you’ll be a pro in no time!
