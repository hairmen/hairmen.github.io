How To Cut My Curly Hair In Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. While your curls can be beautiful and voluminous, they can also be difficult to manage and style. One way to make your curly hair more manageable and stylish is by cutting it in layers. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your curly hair in layers step-by-step, and provide tips on how to care for your curly hair afterwards.

Description

Cutting curly hair in layers is a great way to enhance your curls and give your hair more volume and movement. Layers can also help to reduce bulk, making your hair easier to style and manage. However, cutting curly hair can be tricky, and if you're not careful, you can end up with uneven or frizzy hair. That's why it's important to follow the right steps and use the right techniques when cutting your curly hair in layers.

How to Cut My Curly Hair in Layers

Before you start cutting your curly hair in layers, it's important to have the right tools and products on hand. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have all the necessary tools and products, it's time to start cutting your curly hair in layers. Follow these steps:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help to remove any dirt or product buildup, and make your hair easier to work with.
  2. Once your hair is clean and damp, use a fine-toothed comb to detangle it. This will help to prevent any knots or tangles from getting in the way while you're cutting your hair.
  3. Section your hair into four parts: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use hair clips or elastics to keep each section separate.
  4. Starting with the back section, take a small section of hair and twist it tightly. Use your scissors to make a small cut at the bottom of the twist, about half an inch from the ends. This will create a subtle layer.
  5. Continue working through the back section, twisting small sections of hair and making small cuts at the bottom of each twist. As you work, make sure to keep the twists consistent in size and direction.
  6. Once you've finished the back section, move on to the left side, right side, and top sections. Use the same twisting and cutting technique to create layers throughout your hair.
  7. When you've finished cutting your hair, use a mirror to check for any uneven or frizzy sections. If you notice any areas that need touching up, use your scissors to make small adjustments.
  8. Gently shake out your hair to let the curls fall into place. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair in Layers

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting your curly hair in layers:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. It's easier to add more layers than to remove too much hair.
  • Make sure to keep your twists consistent in size and direction. This will help to create a more uniform look.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's dry. Curly hair shrinks when it dries, so if you cut it when it's dry, you may end up cutting too much.
  • Use a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp as you work. This will make it easier to twist and cut your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair in layers:

  • How often should I cut my curly hair? It's recommended to cut your curly hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.
  • Can I cut my curly hair myself? Yes, but it's important to be careful and follow the right steps to avoid any mistakes or damage to your hair.
  • What if I make a mistake? If you make a mistake while cutting your curly hair, don't panic. Wait a few days for your hair to settle, and then make any necessary adjustments.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

After cutting your curly hair in layers, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, which can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair to help define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Sleep with your hair in a satin or silk scarf or bonnet to prevent tangling and breakage.

Cutting your curly hair in layers can be a great way to enhance your curls and give your hair more volume and movement. By following the right steps and using the right techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, layered look that will make your curls the envy of all your friends. Just remember to take good care of your hair afterwards, and you'll be enjoying your new look for months to come!
