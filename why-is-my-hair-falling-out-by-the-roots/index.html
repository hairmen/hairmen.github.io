Why Is My Hair Falling Out By The Roots

Written by Aprilia Nov 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're experiencing hair loss, you're not alone. It's a common problem that affects millions of people around the world. Losing hair by the roots, however, can be particularly distressing because it suggests a more severe underlying issue. In this blog post, we'll explore the reasons why your hair might be falling out by the roots and offer tips on how to prevent it.

Description

Hair fall can occur to anyone regardless of age, gender, or ethnicity. Hair loss by the roots is when hair strands come out of the scalp rather than breaking off near the ends. This condition can happen due to various reasons such as lifestyle, genetics, or medical conditions. It is important to understand the cause of hair loss to determine the best course of treatment.

How to Identify Hair Loss by the Roots

If you notice clumps of hair coming out when you comb or brush your hair, you might have hair loss by the roots. You may also notice that your hair is thinning, and your scalp is becoming more visible. Be sure to consult with a medical professional if you're unsure about the cause of your hair loss.

Step by Step Guide: Preventing Hair Loss by the Roots

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

Your hair needs essential vitamins and minerals to stay healthy. Eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, and protein can help keep your hair strong and prevent hair loss.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles and Chemical Treatments

Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids, ponytails, and cornrows, which can pull on your hair and cause damage to the roots. Also, chemical treatments such as relaxers and perms can weaken your hair and make it more susceptible to breakage.

3. Use Gentle Hair Products

Choose hair products that are gentle on your hair and scalp. Look for products that are free of harsh chemicals such as sulfates and parabens. Also, avoid over-styling your hair with heat tools like flat irons and blow dryers, as they can cause damage to your hair.

4. Manage Stress

Stress can affect your body in many ways, including causing hair loss. Finding healthy ways to manage stress, such as practicing yoga or meditation, can help prevent hair loss and improve your overall health.

Tips for Hair Care

In addition to the steps above, there are a few tips you can follow to keep your hair healthy:

  • Don't wash your hair too often, as it can strip your hair of natural oils.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with a towel; instead, gently pat it dry.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner that contains SPF.

FAQ

Q: Can hair loss by the roots be reversed?

A: It depends on the cause of the hair loss. In some cases, such as hormonal changes or nutritional deficiencies, hair loss can be reversed with proper treatment. In other cases, such as genetic hair loss, it may not be possible to reverse the condition.

Q: Is hair loss by the roots a sign of a serious medical condition?

A: Not necessarily. Hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including stress, nutritional deficiencies, and genetics. However, if you're experiencing other symptoms along with hair loss, such as fatigue or weight loss, it's important to consult with a medical professional.

Q: Can hair loss by the roots be prevented?

A: Yes, there are steps you can take to prevent hair loss by the roots, such as eating a balanced diet, avoiding tight hairstyles and chemical treatments, and managing stress.

Conclusion

Hair loss can be a distressing condition, but there are steps you can take to prevent it. By eating a balanced diet, avoiding tight hairstyles and chemical treatments, and managing stress, you can keep your hair healthy and prevent hair loss by the roots. If you're experiencing hair loss, be sure to consult with a medical professional to determine the best course of treatment.
