How To Trim Your Own Shoulder Length Hair At Home

Written by William Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of paying for expensive haircuts every few months? With a few simple tools and some patience, you can trim your own shoulder length hair at home. In this blog post, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to trim your hair, as well as some tips and frequently asked questions.

Description

Trimming your own hair can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's a simple process. By learning how to trim your own hair, you can save money and time, and have greater control over the final result.

How to Trim Your Own Shoulder Length Hair at Home

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror or two (one for the front, one for the back)
  • A spray bottle with water

Once you have your tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it straight. This will make it easier to see the length and texture of your hair.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Start Trimming

Begin by trimming small sections of hair at a time. Hold the hair with your fingers or comb and cut the ends with your scissors. Be sure to cut straight across and not at an angle. It's better to trim less hair at first and work your way up to the desired length.

Step 4: Check the Length

After each section, check the length by comparing it to the other sections. You can also hold a mirror behind your head to see the back of your hair.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

If your hair has layers, use your scissors to blend them together by cutting at a slight angle. This will create a more natural look.

Step 6: Trim the Bangs

If you have bangs, trim them last. Comb them straight down and cut them at the desired length. Remember to cut less hair at first and work your way up.

Step 7: Clean Up

Once you're finished trimming, brush your hair to remove any loose hair. You can also wash your hair again if necessary.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when trimming your own hair:

  • Trim your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors, as using regular scissors can damage your hair.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Trimming your own hair can be time-consuming and tiring.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, start with small trims and work your way up to more significant changes.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about trimming your own hair:

Can I trim my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can trim your own curly hair. However, be sure to cut it when it's dry and use a comb to separate each curl before trimming.

How often should I trim my hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't worry. Hair grows back, and you can always visit a professional stylist to fix any issues.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and preserve its health. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.

By following these tips and techniques, you can trim your own shoulder length hair at home and maintain healthy, beautiful locks.
