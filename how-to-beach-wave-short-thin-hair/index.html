How to Beach Wave Short Thin Hair

Before we get started, itâs important to note that the key to beach waves on short, thin hair is to create volume and texture. This can be achieved through the use of products, tools, and techniques that add body and movement to your hair. Here are a few things youâll need:

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to create beach waves on short, thin hair:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair, then towel-dry it until itâs damp. Apply a sea salt spray throughout your hair, focusing on the roots and mid-lengths. This will help to create texture and volume.

Step 2: Divide and Conquer

Divide your hair into small sections, starting at the back of your head. Use a curling iron or wand to create small, loose waves, making sure to curl away from your face. Hold each curl for a few seconds before releasing it.

Step 3: Work Your Way Up

Continue curling small sections of hair, working your way up to the top of your head. Make sure to vary the size and direction of your curls to create a natural, tousled look.

Step 4: Shake It Out

Once youâve curled all of your hair, use your fingers to gently shake out the curls. This will create a more relaxed, beachy look.

Step 5: Add Texture

If you want even more texture and volume, you can use a texturizing spray or powder to create a matte, tousled finish. Spritz it throughout your hair, then use your fingers to scrunch and tousle your hair.

Step 6: Lock It In

Finally, use a light-hold hairspray to set your style in place. Hold the can a few inches away from your head and mist it all over your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Beach Waves

Here are a few tips and tricks for keeping your beach waves looking great:

  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too much, as this can break up the waves and create frizz.
  • Try sleeping with your hair in braids to create even more texture and volume.
