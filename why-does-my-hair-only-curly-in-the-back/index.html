Why Does My Hair Only Curl In The Back

Written by Daniel Aug 08, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're one of those people who have noticed that their hair only curls in the back, you're not alone. This is a common problem that many people face, and it can be frustrating to deal with. In this article, we'll explore the possible reasons why your hair only curls in the back and what you can do to fix it.

Description

When we talk about hair curling, we're referring to the natural way that our hair bends or twists. Some people have naturally curly hair, while others have straight hair that can be styled into curls. However, some people notice that their hair only curls in certain areas, with the back being a common location for this issue.

How to

If you're wondering why your hair only curls in the back, there could be several reasons. Here are some of the most common ones:

1. Genetics

Your hair curl pattern is largely determined by your genetics. If you have family members with curly hair, there's a good chance that you have inherited the same trait. However, if you notice that your hair curls only in the back, it could be due to a specific gene that is more active in that area of your scalp.

2. Hair Texture

The texture of your hair can also affect how it curls. If you have fine or straight hair, it may be harder for it to hold a curl. On the other hand, if you have thick or coarse hair, it may be easier for it to curl naturally. In some cases, the hair at the back of your head may be coarser or thicker than the rest of your hair, which can result in more pronounced curls.

3. Styling Habits

How you style your hair can also impact its curl pattern. If you frequently use heat styling tools like curling irons or hot rollers, your hair may be more prone to curling in certain areas. This is because heat can damage the hair shaft and alter its natural texture. Additionally, if you frequently tie your hair back in a tight ponytail or bun, this can cause the hair at the back of your head to become more tightly coiled.

Step by Step

Now that you know some of the possible reasons why your hair only curls in the back, let's look at some steps you can take to fix the issue:

1. Experiment with Different Products

If you have straight or fine hair, you may need to use specific products to help your hair hold a curl. Look for products like mousse or curl enhancers that are designed to add volume and hold to your hair.

2. Change Your Styling Routine

If you frequently use heat styling tools, try to limit their use and use a heat protectant spray. Additionally, try to avoid tight hairstyles that can cause your hair to become more tightly coiled.

3. Consider a Perm

If you're looking for a more permanent solution, consider getting a perm. A perm is a chemical treatment that can alter the texture of your hair, making it easier to curl. However, it's important to note that perms can be damaging to the hair, so it's important to have it done by a professional and to take good care of your hair afterwards.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect curls:

1. Use the Right Tools

If you're using heat styling tools, make sure to use the right size curling iron or hot rollers for your hair length and texture. Additionally, consider investing in a diffuser attachment for your hair dryer, which can help enhance your natural curls.

2. Don't Overdo It

While it's tempting to keep curling your hair until you achieve the perfect ringlets, be careful not to overdo it. Over-styling your hair can cause damage and breakage, which can make it harder for your hair to hold a curl in the future.

3. Take Care of Your Hair

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair to ensure that it stays healthy and strong. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Additionally, avoid using harsh chemicals or over-styling your hair, which can cause damage and breakage.

FAQ

Q: Can I train my hair to curl in other areas?

A: While you can't change your hair's natural curl pattern, you can encourage it to curl in other areas by using different styling techniques and products. Experiment with different styling methods to see what works best for you.

Q: Will a perm damage my hair?

A: Perms can be damaging to the hair, especially if they are done incorrectly or if the hair is already damaged. It's important to have your perm done by a professional and to take good care of your hair afterwards.

Q: How can I prevent my curls from falling flat?

A: To prevent your curls from falling flat, try using a strong-hold hairspray or a curl-defining cream. Additionally, avoid touching your hair too much and try to avoid humid or damp environments, which can cause your curls to lose their shape.

Hair Care

Overall, if you're wondering why your hair only curls in the back, there could be several reasons. It's important to experiment with different styling techniques and products to find what works best for you. Additionally, take good care of your hair to ensure that it stays healthy and strong. With a little patience and care, you can achieve the perfect curls that you've always wanted!
