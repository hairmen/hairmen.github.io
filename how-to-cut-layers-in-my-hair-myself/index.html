How To Cut Layers In My Hair Myself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 20, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and add some volume and texture, cutting layers in your hair can be a great option. While going to a salon can be pricey, you can learn how to cut layers in your hair yourself with a few simple steps. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting layers in your hair at home, from start to finish.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Layers in My Hair Myself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers in Your Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Layers

How to Cut Layers in My Hair Myself

Before we get started, it's important to gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair ties or clips to section your hair. Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Tie or clip each section separately to make it easier to cut.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start by cutting the bottom layer of your hair. Take one of the back sections and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Repeat this step for the other back section.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you've cut the bottom layer, it's time to move on to the middle layer. Take one of the front sections and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this step for the other front section.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, it's time to cut the top layer. Take the remaining hair and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. This will create the longest layers in your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a quick summary of the steps:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section your hair into four parts.
  3. Cut the bottom layer.
  4. Cut the middle layer.
  5. Cut the top layer.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Your Hair

Cutting layers in your hair can be tricky, so here are a few tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and cut gradually.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once to avoid uneven layers.
  • Make sure your hair is dry before cutting to avoid any surprises.
  • Take breaks if you need to, cutting your hair can be tiring.

FAQs

Can I cut layers in my curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. Make sure to cut your hair when it's dry and use a technique called "curl by curl" to avoid any surprises.

Can I cut layers in my short hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in short hair. Make sure to take small sections and use a comb to guide your cuts.

How often should I cut my layers?

It depends on your hair growth and the look you're going for. Generally, cutting your layers every 6-8 weeks can help maintain the shape and texture of your haircut.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting layers in your hair, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the shape and texture of your haircut. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends.

Now that you know how to cut layers in your hair yourself, you can experiment with different styles and textures. Remember to take your time and be patient, cutting your own hair can be challenging but rewarding.
