How To Cut An Undercut At Home Female A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a bold and edgy hairstyle that will make you stand out from the crowd, consider getting an undercut. This trendy haircut involves shaving the hair underneath the top layer, creating a contrast between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair underneath. While it may seem intimidating to try this haircut at home, it's actually quite doable with the right tools and techniques. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut an undercut at home female.

How to Cut an Undercut at Home Female: Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the necessary tools:

  • Clippers or trimmers with a #1 or #2 guard
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle with water

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Cut an Undercut at Home Female

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it. You want your hair to be clean and dry so you can see its natural texture and shape.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, part your hair horizontally at the point where you want the undercut to start. Clip the top layer of hair out of the way with hair clips or elastics.

Step 3: Choose the Length of Your Undercut

Decide on the length of your undercut. A #1 guard will leave about 1/8 inch of hair, while a #2 guard will leave about 1/4 inch of hair. You can also choose to shave a design or pattern into the undercut for added flair.

Step 4: Start Cutting

With the clippers or trimmers, start cutting the hair underneath the clipped section. Work in small sections, moving from the back to the front. Use the comb to lift each section of hair and cut it to the desired length.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you've finished cutting the hair underneath the top layer, remove the clips or elastics and blend the hair. Use scissors to trim any longer pieces of hair and create a seamless transition between the top layer and the undercut.

Step 6: Clean Up the Edges

Use the clippers or trimmers without a guard to clean up the edges of the undercut. Be careful not to cut into the longer hair on top.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired. You can leave the top layer down, tie it up in a ponytail, or style it in a Mohawk or other edgy look.

Tips for Cutting an Undercut at Home Female

  • Start with a longer guard and gradually work your way to a shorter length to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a spray bottle with water to keep the hair damp and easier to cut.
  • Take breaks and step back from the mirror to assess your progress and make sure you're cutting evenly.
  • Watch tutorials or ask a friend for help if you're unsure about any steps.

FAQ: How to Cut an Undercut at Home Female

Q: Can I cut an undercut with scissors instead of clippers?

A: While it's possible to cut an undercut with scissors, clippers or trimmers are recommended for a more precise and even cut.

Q: How often do I need to touch up my undercut?

A: The frequency of touch-ups will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how short you choose to cut the undercut. On average, most people will need to touch up their undercut every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I dye my undercut a different color from the rest of my hair?

A: Yes, you can dye your undercut a different color for a bold and creative look.

Hair Care Tips for an Undercut

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep the hair soft and hydrated.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your undercut.

Now that you know how to cut an undercut at home female, you can try this trendy haircut for yourself. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a bold and edgy look that will make you stand out from the crowd.
