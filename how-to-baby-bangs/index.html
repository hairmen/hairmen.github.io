How To Baby Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our blog post about how to baby bangs. In this article, we will guide you on how to create baby bangs, which are short, wispy bangs that have been popular for a few years now. If you're looking to shake up your hairstyle, baby bangs might be just the thing you need. Read on for our step-by-step guide on how to achieve this look!

Description

Baby bangs are a type of short bangs that typically fall just above the eyebrows. They are cut straight across and have a wispy, almost choppy look to them. This style has become popular in recent years, thanks in part to celebrities like Emma Watson and Rooney Mara who have sported the look on the red carpet.

How to Cut Baby Bangs

If you're ready to take the plunge and cut your own baby bangs, here's how to do it:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. You want to make sure your hair is clean and free of any tangles before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair by pulling all of your hair back into a ponytail, leaving out the front section that you want to cut into bangs. Use a comb to create a straight line from temple to temple, separating the front section of hair from the rest of your hair.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

Using a pair of sharp scissors, cut your bangs straight across. Take your time and make small cuts, rather than trying to cut too much off at once. Remember, you can always go back and cut more if needed, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut!

Step 4: Style Your Bangs

Once you've cut your bangs, it's time to style them. Use a flat iron to straighten them out, or use a curling iron to add some waves. You can also add some hairspray to keep them in place.

Tips for Cutting Baby Bangs

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting baby bangs:

  • Start with longer bangs and gradually cut them shorter. It's better to cut too little at first than too much.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.
  • Don't cut your bangs when your hair is wet. Hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you go. It's important to make sure your bangs are even on both sides.

Baby Bangs FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about baby bangs:

Q: Will baby bangs look good on me?

A: Baby bangs can look good on a variety of face shapes and hair types. However, it's always a good idea to consult with a hairstylist before making a drastic change to your hair.

Q: How often do I need to trim my baby bangs?

A: Baby bangs grow out quickly, so you'll need to trim them every few weeks to maintain the look.

Q: Can I style my baby bangs differently?

A: Yes! Baby bangs can be styled in a variety of ways, from straight and sleek to curly and voluminous. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

Hair Care for Baby Bangs

Here are some tips for caring for your baby bangs:

  • Use a heat protectant spray when styling your bangs with hot tools.
  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your bangs looking fresh.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can make them look greasy.
  • Keep a mini hairspray in your bag to touch up your bangs throughout the day.

Now that you know how to baby bangs, it's time to give it a try! Remember to take your time and be patient, as cutting your own hair can be tricky. Good luck!
