How To Do A Low Fade Haircut For Beginners

Written by Christine Oct 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

A low fade haircut is a popular hairstyle that is perfect for beginners who want to experiment with their hair. It is a classic and versatile hairstyle that can be worn in different ways, making it ideal for any occasion. In this article, we will guide you on how to do a low fade haircut for beginners, including the tools you need, step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools you'll need
  2. How to do a low fade haircut for beginners
  3. Step-by-step instructions
  4. Tips for a perfect low fade haircut
  5. FAQs about low fade haircut
  6. Haircare tips after a low fade haircut

Tools you'll need

Before starting, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Clipper with different guard sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Water spray bottle
  • Hair gel or wax (optional)

How to do a low fade haircut for beginners

A low fade haircut is a type of haircut where the hair gradually fades from the sides and the back towards the neckline, creating a seamless transition from short to long hair. Here are the basic steps:

Step-by-step instructions

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair.
  2. Attach the longest guard size to your clipper and start trimming the hair on the sides and the back. Make sure to trim in an upward motion to avoid cutting too much hair.
  3. Change to a shorter guard size and trim the hair again, leaving about an inch of hair from the top to create a contrast between the top and the sides.
  4. Blend the hair between the top and the sides using a clipper without a guard size. Start from the bottom and work your way up, making sure to blend the hair evenly.
  5. Use scissors to trim the hair on the top to your desired length. You can also use a comb to lift the hair and cut it in a straight line.
  6. Blend the hair between the top and the sides using scissors or a clipper with a guard size. Make sure to blend the hair evenly.
  7. Trim the hair on the neckline using a clipper or scissors. You can create a sharp line or a rounded line depending on your preference.
  8. Use a comb to check if the hair is even on both sides. Make any necessary adjustments.
  9. Finish with hair gel or wax to keep the hair in place.

Tips for a perfect low fade haircut

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it in a straight line.
  • Take your time and be patient. A low fade haircut requires precision and attention to detail.
  • Check the hair on both sides using a comb to ensure that it is even.
  • Experiment with different guard sizes and hair lengths to find the perfect style for you.

FAQs about low fade haircut

1. How often should I get a low fade haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain your haircut. A low fade haircut can last for 2-3 weeks, but it is recommended to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and length.

2. Can I do a low fade haircut at home?

Yes, you can do a low fade haircut at home, but it requires some practice and the right tools. It is recommended to start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair. Take your time and be patient.

3. What is the difference between a low fade and a high fade?

The main difference between a low fade and a high fade is the position of the fade. A low fade starts just above the ears and gradually fades towards the neckline, while a high fade starts at the temples and gradually fades towards the crown.

Haircare tips after a low fade haircut

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water and harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using hair gel or wax every day, as it can cause buildup and damage your hair.
  • Visit your hairstylist every 4-6 weeks to maintain your low fade haircut.

Now that you know how to do a low fade haircut for beginners, you can experiment with different styles and find the perfect look for you. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
