How To Cut Short Hair For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 22, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of paying for haircuts every few weeks? Do you want to learn how to cut your own hair but don't know where to start? Cutting short hair can be intimidating, especially if you're a beginner. But with the right tools and techniques, you can give yourself a great haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut short hair for beginners.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Blending Your Hair
  • Tips for a Great Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Your Haircut

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the right tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle
  • Mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp and your clippers have a variety of guards to choose from.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start cutting, wash and dry your hair. Make sure it's free of tangles and knots. If you have curly hair, consider cutting it when it's dry so you can see the natural shape of your curls.

Sectioning Your Hair

Section your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to keep each section in place. Start with the back sections first, as they're the hardest to reach.

Cutting Your Hair

Start with the longest length you want to cut. Use your scissors to cut a straight line across the back of your head. Move to the sides and cut your hair at an angle, following the shape of your head. Take your time and cut small sections at a time.

For the top section of your hair, use your clippers with a guard that's one size longer than the sides. Start from the back and move to the front, cutting your hair in a straight line. Use the comb to lift your hair up and make sure you're cutting it evenly.

Blending Your Hair

Blend the top and sides of your hair by using a smaller guard on your clippers. Start from the bottom and work your way up, using a flicking motion with the clippers. Avoid cutting too much hair at once, as you can't put it back once it's cut.

Tips for a Great Haircut

  • Start with small sections and work your way up.
  • Use a mirror to see the back of your head.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry for a more accurate cut.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once.
  • Take breaks if you need to.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

You can, but it's not recommended. Regular scissors aren't as sharp as hair scissors and can cause split ends.

Can I cut my hair with a razor?

You can, but it's not recommended for beginners. Razors can be difficult to control and can cause uneven cuts.

Hair Care After Your Haircut

After you've finished cutting your hair, wash and style it as usual. Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent split ends. Trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking its best.

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut from the comfort of your own home. Follow these steps and tips for a successful haircut every time.
