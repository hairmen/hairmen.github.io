How To Cut Back Of Pixie Cut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Nov 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have a pixie cut, you know how important it is to maintain the back of your hair. However, going to a salon every few weeks for a trim can be expensive and time-consuming. That's why learning how to cut the back of your pixie cut yourself can save you time and money. In this article, we'll go through the steps on how to cut the back of your pixie cut and provide some tips and FAQs to help you along the way.

How to Cut Back of Pixie Cut: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you need to make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror. You may also want to have some hair clips handy to help section off your hair while you're cutting.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair. This will help you see the natural shape of your hair and make it easier to cut. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 3: Section Off Your Hair

Using your comb, section off the back of your hair into two or three parts. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with the bottom section, use your comb to hold your hair straight out from your head. Use your scissors to make small, vertical cuts into your hair, following the shape of your head. Make sure to cut only a little bit at a time to avoid cutting too much off.

Once you've finished cutting the bottom section, move on to the next section and repeat the process. If you have a longer pixie cut, you may have more than three sections to cut.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you've finished cutting all the sections, take a step back and look in the mirror to check your progress. Make any necessary adjustments, but be careful not to overdo it. Remember, you can always cut more later, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.

Tips for Cutting the Back of Your Pixie Cut

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. It's easier to cut more hair off than it is to add it back on.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head as you cut. This will help you see any areas you may have missed.
  • Be patient. Cutting your own hair can be tricky, but with practice, you'll get better at it.
  • If you're unsure about cutting the back of your hair, ask a friend or family member for help.

FAQs

How often should I cut the back of my pixie cut?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how short you want to keep your hair. Generally, it's a good idea to trim the back of your hair every four to six weeks.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, you should always use sharp hair-cutting scissors. Regular scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.

Should I wet my hair before cutting it?

No, you should always cut your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can be misleading, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.

Hair Care for Pixie Cuts

Now that you know how to cut the back of your pixie cut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its shape. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and use a heat protectant spray if you do use heat tools. Lastly, get regular trims to keep your hair looking fresh and healthy.

Learning how to cut the back of your pixie cut can save you time and money, but it's important to take your time and be patient. With practice, you'll be able to master the art of cutting your own hair and keep your pixie cut looking fresh and stylish.
