How To Tie Long Curly Hair At Night

Written by Frank Sep 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have long, curly hair, you know that sleeping with it loose can lead to a tangled mess in the morning. Tying your hair at night is a great way to keep it manageable and looking great. In this article, we will discuss how to tie long curly hair at night to keep it healthy and beautiful.

Table of Contents

  • How to Tie Long Curly Hair at Night
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Tying Long Curly Hair at Night
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Long Curly Hair

How to Tie Long Curly Hair at Night

The first step to tying your long curly hair at night is to choose the right type of tie. Avoid using rubber bands or tight elastics as they can cause breakage and damage to your hair. Instead, opt for a soft scrunchie or a loose hair tie.

Next, you will want to gather your hair into a loose ponytail at the crown of your head. Do not pull your hair too tight as this can cause tension and pulling on your scalp. Once you have your hair gathered, twist it gently into a loose bun or wrap it around the base of your ponytail.

Finally, secure your hair with your chosen hair tie or scrunchie. Make sure it is not too tight and is comfortable to sleep in. You can also wrap a silk scarf around your head to protect your curls and keep them from getting frizzy.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Choose a soft scrunchie or loose hair tie.

2. Gather your hair into a loose ponytail at the crown of your head.

3. Twist your hair gently into a loose bun or wrap it around the base of your ponytail.

4. Secure your hair with your chosen hair tie or scrunchie.

5. Wrap a silk scarf around your head to protect your curls and keep them from getting frizzy.

Tips for Tying Long Curly Hair at Night

1. Avoid tying your hair too tightly as it can cause tension and pulling on your scalp.

2. Use a soft scrunchie or loose hair tie to avoid breakage and damage to your hair.

3. Wrap a silk scarf around your head to protect your curls and keep them from getting frizzy.

4. Consider using a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage while you sleep.

5. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair before tying it up to keep it moisturized and healthy.

FAQs

Q: Can I tie my hair with a regular elastic band?
A: No, regular elastic bands can cause breakage and damage to your hair. Use a soft scrunchie or loose hair tie instead.

Q: How tight should I tie my hair at night?
A: Tie your hair loosely to avoid tension and pulling on your scalp.

Hair Care for Long Curly Hair

1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your curls.

2. Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls moisturized and healthy.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair while it is wet to avoid breakage.

4. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair before styling to keep it moisturized and healthy.

5. Avoid using heat styling tools as they can cause damage to your hair. Instead, air-dry your hair or use a diffuser on your blow dryer.

By following these tips and tying your long curly hair at night, you can keep your hair healthy and beautiful. Remember to choose a soft scrunchie or loose hair tie, tie your hair loosely, and wrap a silk scarf around your head to protect your curls. With the right hair care routine, your long curly hair can look great every day.
