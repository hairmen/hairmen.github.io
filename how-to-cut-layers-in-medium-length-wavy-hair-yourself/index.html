How To Cut Layers In Medium Length Wavy Hair Yourself

Written by Eveline Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune to the hairdresser every time you need a haircut? Do you want to experiment with your hair and try cutting layers in your medium length wavy hair? You've come to the right place! In this article, we'll guide you step by step on how to cut layers in medium length wavy hair yourself.

Description

Layers can add volume, texture, and movement to your hair. They can also make your face look slimmer and more defined. Medium length wavy hair is the perfect canvas for layers because it's not too short or too long, and it has a natural wave that adds dimension to the layers.

How to Cut Layers in Medium Length Wavy Hair Yourself

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror in front of you and behind you, so you can see the back of your head.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow-drying it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, part your hair down the middle and clip one side away. Take a small section of hair from the top of the unclipped side and clip the rest of the hair away. This will be your guide section for the rest of the layers.

Step 3: Cut the Guide Section

Hold the guide section straight up with your fingers and cut it straight across with your scissors. This will be the shortest layer of your haircut.

Step 4: Cut the Other Sections

Using the guide section as a reference, take another small section of hair next to it and hold it straight up. Cut it to the same length as the guide section. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you've cut all the layers on one side of your head.

Step 5: Repeat on the Other Side

Unclip the other side of your hair and repeat the same process to cut the layers on that side. Make sure you're cutting the same amount of hair on both sides, so your layers are even.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, use your scissors to blend the ends of each layer. This will create a seamless transition between the layers and give your hair a natural look.

Step 7: Check Your Layers

Check your layers in the mirror to make sure they're even and symmetrical. If you notice any unevenness, go back and make small adjustments.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut your layers, it's time to style your hair. You can leave it natural and let the waves do their thing, or you can use a curling iron or straightener to create a different look.

Tips

  • Start by cutting small sections of hair. You can always go back and cut more if you want.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • If you're not confident in cutting your own hair, ask a friend or family member to help.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.

FAQ

Can I cut layers in my hair if it's not wavy?

Yes, you can cut layers in any hair texture. However, the layers may look different depending on your hair type.

How often should I cut my layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how much you want to maintain your layers. Generally, getting a haircut every 6-8 weeks is recommended to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Now that you've cut your layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your layers and prevent split ends.

With these tips and steps, you're ready to cut layers in your medium length wavy hair yourself. Good luck and have fun!
