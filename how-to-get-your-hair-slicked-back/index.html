How To Get Your Hair Slicked Back A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of your hair getting in your face all the time? Do you want a sleek, sophisticated look that will turn heads? Look no further than the slicked-back hairstyle. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to achieve this look, from start to finish.

How to Get Your Hair Slicked Back

The slicked-back hairstyle is all about creating a smooth, polished look that keeps your hair out of your face. Here are the basics:

  • Start with freshly washed and conditioned hair
  • Apply a styling product, such as gel or pomade
  • Comb your hair straight back, from your forehead to the crown of your head
  • Smooth out any bumps or flyaways with your comb or your fingers
  • Add more product as needed to keep your hair in place

Of course, the devil is in the details. Read on for a step-by-step guide to achieving the perfect slicked-back hairstyle.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

The first step to achieving any hairstyle is to start with clean, healthy hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that work well for your hair type, and be sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid any buildup that could interfere with styling later on.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

Before you start applying any styling products, you want to get rid of as much excess moisture as possible. Use a towel to gently blot your hair dry, being careful not to rub too vigorously and cause damage or frizz.

Step 3: Apply Styling Product

For a slicked-back hairstyle, you want a product that will provide hold and shine without weighing your hair down too much. Pomade or gel are both good options. Start with a small amount and work it through your hair, focusing on the roots and middle sections.

Step 4: Comb Your Hair Straight Back

Using a fine-toothed comb, start at your forehead and comb your hair straight back. Make sure to get all the way to the crown of your head, and aim for a smooth, even finish.

Step 5: Smooth Out Any Bumps or Flyaways

Once you've got your hair combed back, take a closer look and see if there are any bumps or flyaways that need attention. You can use your comb or your fingers to smooth out any problem areas, being careful not to disrupt the overall shape you've created.

Step 6: Add More Product as Needed

If you find that your hair is starting to lose its shape, or if you want a little extra shine, feel free to add more product as needed. Just be careful not to overdo it, or you'll end up with a greasy, weighed-down look.

Tips for Slicking Back Your Hair

Now that you know the basics of how to slick back your hair, here are a few tips to help you get the most out of this classic hairstyle:

  • Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair
  • Don't be afraid to play around with the shape of your slicked-back hair - you can try a more voluminous look, or go for a tighter, flatter finish
  • Be gentle when combing or smoothing your hair, as rough handling can cause breakage and damage
  • Consider using a hair dryer on a low setting to help set the style in place
  • Remember that this style works best on medium to long hair - if your hair is too short, you may not be able to achieve the desired effect

FAQs

Q: Can I slick back my hair if it's curly or wavy?

A: Absolutely! In fact, the slicked-back look can be a great way to show off your natural texture while still keeping your hair out of your face. Just be sure to use a product that will provide enough hold to keep your curls or waves in place.

Q: How often should I wash my hair if I'm wearing it slicked back?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much product you're using. Some people find that they can go a few days between washes, while others prefer to wash their hair daily. Just be sure to pay attention to how your hair feels and looks, and adjust your washing schedule accordingly.

Hair Care for Slicked-Back Hair

While the slicked-back hairstyle can be a great look, it's important to take care of your hair to avoid damage or breakage. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it more prone to damage
  • Be gentle when combing or brushing your hair, and use a wide-toothed comb or brush to avoid tangles and snarls
  • Consider using a deep-conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons too often, as this can cause damage and breakage over time

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve a sleek, polished look that's sure to turn heads. So go ahead and try out the slicked-back hairstyle for yourself - you might just discover a new favorite look!
