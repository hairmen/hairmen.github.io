How To Cut Medium Length Boy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to cut your son's medium length hair at home? It can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut that your child will love. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps on how to cut medium length boy hair, including tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You Will Need
  2. Preparation
  3. Sectioning the Hair
  4. Trimming the Hair
  5. Blending the Hair
  6. Finishing Touches

  9. Hair Care after the Cut

Tools You Will Need

Before you start cutting your son's hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clippers
  • Clipper guards
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Hair clips

Preparation

Make sure your son's hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be harder to cut, and you don't want to accidentally cut more than you intended. It's also a good idea to have your son wear a shirt that you don't mind getting hair on, or place a towel around his neck to catch any loose hairs.


Sectioning the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair. Use a comb to create a center part from the forehead to the nape of the neck. Then, create two more sections on each side of the head, dividing the hair into four equal parts. Use hair clips to secure each section out of the way, leaving one section at a time to work on.


Trimming the Hair

Start by using scissors to trim the hair on the top section of the head. Take small sections of hair and hold them between your fingers, then cut the hair at an angle to create layers. Continue this process until you have trimmed all the hair on the top section.

Next, use the hair clippers with a guard to trim the hair on the sides and back of the head. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard until you reach the desired length. Use the comb to lift the hair and trim any stray hairs that the clippers may have missed.


Blending the Hair

Blend the hair between the top section and the sides using the scissors. Take small sections of hair and use a technique called point cutting, where you cut the hair at an angle to blend the two sections together. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can create choppy layers.


Finishing Touches

Once you have trimmed and blended the hair, use the hair clippers without a guard to clean up the hairline around the ears and neckline. You can also use scissors to trim any longer hairs that may be sticking out.


Tips and Tricks

  • Start with a longer guard on the clippers and work your way down to a shorter guard. This will help you avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • If you're not sure how short to cut the hair, start with a longer length and trim a little at a time until you reach the desired length.
  • Use the comb to lift the hair and trim any stray hairs that the clippers may have missed.
  • Trim the hair when it's dry. Wet hair can be harder to cut, and you don't want to accidentally cut more than you intended.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I cut my son's hair?

It depends on how fast your son's hair grows and how short you want his hair to be. On average, most boys need a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my son's hair?

Don't worry if you make a mistake. Hair grows back, and mistakes can be fixed. If you're not sure how to fix a mistake, take your son to a professional hair stylist for help.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my son's hair?

No, it's not recommended to use regular scissors to cut hair. Hair scissors are designed with a sharper blade that creates a clean cut, while regular scissors can damage the hair and create split ends.


Hair Care after the Cut

After you've finished cutting your son's hair, it's important to take care of his hair to maintain the style. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep his hair clean and healthy, and use a leave-in conditioner to prevent tangles. You can also use a styling product, such as gel or pomade, to keep his hair in place.


Cutting your son's medium length hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut that your child will love. Use this step-by-step guide to get started and don't be afraid to experiment with different styles. Remember, hair grows back, so have fun with it!
