Low Taper Fade Haircut For Straight Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a trendy and stylish haircut for your straight hair? Look no further than the low taper fade haircut. This haircut is perfect for men who want a clean and sharp look, while still keeping their hair relatively short. In this blog post, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to achieve the low taper fade haircut, as well as some tips and FAQs to help you maintain your new style.

Description

The low taper fade haircut is a popular style that involves gradually tapering the hair from short to shorter at the back and sides of the head. This creates a clean and neat look, while still keeping some length on top of the head. The haircut can be customized to suit your personal style, with the option of leaving more length on top or going for a shorter, more dramatic look.

How to

To achieve the low taper fade haircut, you will need to follow these steps:

Step 1: Choose the Right Clippers

Before you start cutting your hair, you will need to choose the right clippers. Look for a high-quality pair that will last you a long time and make sure they have adjustable blades so you can customize the length of your hair.

Step 2: Decide on the Length of Your Hair

Next, you will need to decide on the length of your hair. This will depend on your personal preference, but generally, the hair on top should be longer than the hair on the sides and back.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair with the longest length setting on your clippers. Work your way up from the nape of the neck to the crown of the head. Use a comb to help guide your clippers and create a clean and even line.

As you move up the back and sides of your head, gradually decrease the length setting on your clippers. This will create a taper effect, where the hair gradually gets shorter towards the bottom.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Once you have finished cutting the sides and back of your head, it's time to blend the hair. Use a comb and scissors to blend the hair from the sides and back into the longer hair on top. This will create a seamless transition between the different lengths of hair.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair using your preferred styling products. You can opt for a slicked-back look, a messy style, or any other look that suits your personal style.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you maintain your low taper fade haircut:

- Visit your barber regularly to maintain your style and keep your hair looking neat and tidy. - Use hair products, such as pomade or wax, to help style your hair and keep it in place. - Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type to keep your hair healthy and shiny. - Consider using a hair oil to nourish and moisturize your hair, especially if you have dry or damaged hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about the low taper fade haircut:

Is the low taper fade haircut suitable for all hair types?

Yes, this haircut can be customized to suit different hair types and textures. However, it may not be suitable for very curly or kinky hair, as it can be difficult to achieve a smooth and even taper effect.

How often should I visit the barber to maintain my low taper fade haircut?

You should visit your barber every 4-6 weeks to maintain your style and keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Can I style my low taper fade haircut in different ways?

Yes, you can style your hair in different ways depending on your personal preference. You can use different styling products, such as pomade, wax, or gel, to create different looks.

Hair Care

Maintaining your low taper fade haircut requires proper hair care. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and shiny:

- Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type. - Avoid using hot tools, such as hair straighteners or curling irons, as they can damage your hair. - Use a hair oil or serum to nourish and moisturize your hair, especially if you have dry or damaged hair. - Avoid using too much product in your hair, as this can weigh it down and make it look greasy.
