How To Layer Long Straight Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 01, 2023 · 3 min read

Long straight hair can be beautiful, but it can also be challenging to style. One way to add some dimension and movement to your hair is by layering it. In this post, we’ll walk you through how to layer long straight hair, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To Layer Long Straight Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Layering Long Straight Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Description

Layering long straight hair involves cutting the hair at different lengths to add texture and movement. This can make your hair look less flat and more voluminous. Layering is a great option for those with long hair who want a change without losing length.

How to Layer Long Straight Hair

Layering long straight hair is best done by a professional hairstylist, but if you’re feeling brave, you can try it at home. Follow these steps to layer your long straight hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back.
  3. Starting with the back sections, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle. Repeat this process, working your way up the back sections of your hair.
  4. Move on to the front sections of your hair, cutting the hair at the same angle.
  5. Once all the sections are cut, check for any unevenness and trim as needed.
  6. Style your new layered hair as desired!

Tips for Layering Long Straight Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering your long straight hair:

  • Start with smaller sections of hair to ensure a more precise cut.
  • Always cut at an angle to create a softer, more natural look.
  • Use sharp hair-cutting scissors for a clean cut.
  • If you’re not confident in your cutting skills, it’s best to leave it to a professional stylist.

FAQs

Can I layer my own long straight hair?

While it is possible to layer your own hair, it’s best to leave it to a professional stylist to avoid any mistakes.

How often should I get my layered long straight hair trimmed?

You should get your hair trimmed every six to eight weeks to maintain your layered look and keep your hair healthy.

Will layering my long straight hair make it look thinner?

Layering can make your hair look thinner, but it can also add volume and movement. It’s important to communicate your hair goals with your stylist to achieve the desired look.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Now that you’ve layered your long straight hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for your hair type.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any hot tools on your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair to prevent damage.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your layered look.
  • Consider using hair masks or oils to keep your hair moisturized.

Layering long straight hair is a great way to add some dimension and movement to your hair without losing length. Whether you choose to do it yourself or leave it to a professional, remember to communicate your hair goals and take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great!
