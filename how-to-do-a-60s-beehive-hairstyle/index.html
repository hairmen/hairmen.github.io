How To Do A 60S Beehive Hairstyle A Step By Step Guide

Are you looking for a retro hairstyle that will make you stand out from the crowd? Look no further than the iconic 60s beehive hairstyle! This voluminous and elegant hairdo was a staple of the mod scene and is still popular today. In this guide, we'll show you how to do a 60s beehive hairstyle step-by-step, including tips and hair care advice.

How to Do a 60s Beehive Hairstyle

A 60s beehive hairstyle is all about volume and height. To achieve this retro look, you'll need some basic hair tools and products:

  • Teasing comb
  • Hairspray
  • Bobby pins
  • Hair elastics
  • Hot rollers or curling iron

With these tools and products in hand, you're ready to create your beehive hairstyle!

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to create your own 60s beehive hairstyle:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is naturally curly, straighten it with a flat iron.
  2. Section off the top layer of your hair and clip it out of the way.
  3. Tease the hair at the crown of your head with a teasing comb. Backcomb the hair by holding a section of hair straight up and combing it downwards towards the scalp. Repeat this process until you have a good amount of volume.
  4. Gently smooth the teased hair with a soft-bristle brush to create a rounded shape.
  5. Pull the top layer of your hair back over the teased section and secure it with bobby pins.
  6. Take a small section of hair from the front of your head and tease it lightly. Smooth it over the top of the beehive and secure it with bobby pins.
  7. Roll the sides of your hair back towards the beehive and secure them with bobby pins.
  8. Curl the ends of your hair with hot rollers or a curling iron to create loose waves.
  9. Spray your hair with a generous amount of hairspray to hold the style in place.
  10. Finish the look with a hair accessory, such as a headband or scarf.

Voila! You now have a perfectly retro 60s beehive hairstyle.

Tips for a Perfect Beehive

Follow these tips to ensure your beehive hairstyle looks its best:

  • Use a teasing comb with narrow teeth for the best results.
  • Be gentle when smoothing out the teased hair to avoid flattening it.
  • Use bobby pins that match your hair color to camouflage them in the style.
  • For an extra-secure hold, insert bobby pins in a criss-cross pattern.
  • Use hairspray liberally to hold the style in place, but avoid using too much at once to prevent your hair from looking stiff.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about the 60s beehive hairstyle:

Q: Can I do a beehive with short hair?

A: It's possible to create a mini beehive with short hair, but it will require more teasing and pinning. You may also need to use hair extensions or a hairpiece to achieve the height and volume of a traditional beehive.

Q: How long does a beehive hairstyle last?

A: With the right products and techniques, a beehive hairstyle can last up to a week. However, you may need to touch up the teased sections and add more hairspray as needed.

Hair Care for Beehive Hairstyles

Teasing and backcombing your hair can cause damage, so it's important to take care of your hair before and after creating a beehive hairstyle:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on a daily basis to prevent further damage.
  • Wrap your hair in a silk scarf at night to prevent tangling and breakage.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • When removing bobby pins and teasing, be gentle to avoid pulling out hair or causing breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to create a stunning 60s beehive hairstyle that will turn heads wherever you go!
