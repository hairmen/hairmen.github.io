Is A Taper A Fade Understanding The Difference

Description

When it comes to men's haircuts, there are many different styles to choose from. Two popular options are the taper and the fade. While these terms are often used interchangeably, they actually refer to slightly different styles. Understanding the difference between a taper and a fade can help you get the exact look you want.

A taper is a gradual decrease in length from the top of the head to the bottom. This can be done with scissors or clippers, and the end result is hair that is longer on top and shorter on the sides and back. A fade, on the other hand, is a more dramatic decrease in length. It involves cutting the hair very short at the bottom and gradually getting longer as you move up the head. The end result is hair that is very short or even shaved at the sides and back, with longer hair on top.

How to

If you're interested in getting a taper or a fade, it's important to find a skilled barber or stylist. They can help you determine which style will work best for your face shape and hair type, and they can also ensure that the cut is done correctly.

When you arrive at the salon or barber shop, be sure to bring a picture of the style you want. This will help your stylist understand your vision and give them a clear idea of what you're looking for. You can also ask for their advice on which style would be best for you.

Step by Step

The exact process of getting a taper or a fade will depend on the style you choose and the tools your stylist uses. However, here is a general overview of the steps involved:

  1. Your stylist will start by washing and drying your hair.
  2. They will then use scissors or clippers to trim the hair on top of your head to the desired length.
  3. Next, they will use clippers to trim the hair on the sides and back of your head. If you're getting a taper, they will gradually decrease the length as they move down the head. If you're getting a fade, they will start with a very short length at the bottom and gradually increase the length as they move up the head.
  4. Your stylist will then blend the hair on the sides and back with the hair on top. This will create a seamless transition between the different lengths of hair.
  5. Finally, they will style your hair using gel or pomade, if desired.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you get the most out of your taper or fade:

  • Choose a style that works well with your face shape and hair type.
  • Find a skilled stylist who has experience with these cuts.
  • Bring a picture of the style you want to ensure clear communication.
  • Be prepared to style your hair on your own between salon visits.

FAQ

Here are some common questions people have about tapers and fades:

Can I get a taper or a fade if I have curly hair?

Yes! In fact, tapers and fades can look especially good on curly hair. Just be sure to find a stylist who has experience cutting curly hair.

How often do I need to get a taper or a fade?

This will depend on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the style starts to lose its shape. Most people need to get a haircut every 2-4 weeks to maintain their style.

Do I need to use special hair products for a taper or a fade?

Not necessarily, but using a styling product like gel or pomade can help you achieve the look you want and keep your hair in place throughout the day.

Hair Care

After getting a taper or a fade, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners, as these can damage your hair.
  • Visit your stylist regularly for trims to maintain your style.

Overall, a taper and a fade are both great options for men's haircuts. By understanding the difference between the two and following these tips, you can achieve a stylish and polished look that suits your individual style.
