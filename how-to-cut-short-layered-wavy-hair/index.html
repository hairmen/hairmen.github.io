How To Cut Short Layered Wavy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Oct 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your short layered wavy hair feeling limp and lifeless? It may be time for a fresh cut! Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with a few simple steps, you can achieve a stylish and textured look that complements your natural waves. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting short layered wavy hair, from sectioning to styling. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting the Bottom Layer
  • Cutting the Middle Layer
  • Cutting the Top Layer
  • Styling Your Hair

Sectioning Your Hair

Before you begin cutting, it's important to section your hair properly to ensure an even and balanced cut. Start by parting your hair down the middle, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, using a comb, create a horizontal part about an inch above your ears. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way, and focus on the bottom layer.

Cutting the Bottom Layer

Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Use a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors to trim the ends of the hair, using your fingers as a guide. Continue working your way around the bottom layer of your hair, taking small sections and trimming the ends until you've cut all of the hair in this layer.

Tip: If you have thick hair, you may want to use thinning shears to help remove some of the bulk.

Cutting the Middle Layer

Once you've finished with the bottom layer, unclip the top section of your hair and let it fall down. Create another horizontal part, about an inch above the first one, and clip the top layer out of the way. Take a small section of hair from the middle layer and repeat the same process of trimming the ends with your scissors. Work your way around the middle layer until you've trimmed all of the hair in this section.

Cutting the Top Layer

Finally, unclip the top layer of your hair and create a horizontal part, about an inch above the second one. Take small sections of hair and trim the ends, using your fingers as a guide. Be sure to blend the top layer with the middle and bottom layers to create a seamless and layered look.

Styling Your Hair

Now that you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to style it. Apply a small amount of texturizing spray or mousse to your hair, focusing on the roots and mid-lengths. Use your hands to scrunch your hair and create definition in your waves. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural waves and add volume.

Tips

  • Invest in a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut.
  • Take your time and work in small sections to ensure an even and balanced cut.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, consider booking an appointment with a professional stylist.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my short layered wavy hair?

A: It depends on your hair growth and desired style, but most people find that cutting their hair every 6-8 weeks helps to maintain its shape and texture.

Q: Can I cut my short layered wavy hair when it's wet or dry?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to work with and may result in an uneven cut.

Hair Care

Now that you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and texture. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils, and consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your strands. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant spray when you do. With the right care and maintenance, your short layered wavy hair will look and feel its best!
