How To Wear Your Hair When Riding A Motorcycle

Written by Frank Sep 21, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to riding a motorcycle, there are plenty of things to think about, from proper gear to safety precautions. However, one thing that often gets overlooked is what to do with your hair. Whether you have long hair that's prone to tangling or short hair that's easily blown around by the wind, it's important to take steps to keep your hair safe and comfortable while you're riding. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to wear your hair when riding a motorcycle.

Description

Motorcycle riding can be a lot of fun, but it can also be tough on your hair. The wind and the elements can cause tangles, frizz, and damage, and if you don't take steps to protect your hair, you could end up with a mess on your hands. In this article, we'll give you tips and tricks for keeping your hair safe and comfortable while you're riding your motorcycle.

How to

First things first, you'll want to make sure your hair is clean and dry before you head out on your motorcycle. If your hair is wet or damp, it will be more prone to tangling and damage from the wind. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to clean and hydrate your hair, and let it air dry or use a blow dryer on a low heat setting.

Next, you'll want to choose a hairstyle that will keep your hair out of your face and minimize tangling. For long hair, a braid or ponytail is a great option, as it will keep your hair secure and prevent it from getting tangled in your helmet. For shorter hair, consider using hair clips or bobby pins to keep your hair in place.

When it comes to choosing a helmet, opt for a full-face helmet if possible, as this will provide the most protection for your hair. Make sure the helmet fits snugly but comfortably, and adjust the straps to keep it secure. If you have long hair, tuck it into the helmet before fastening the chin strap to prevent it from getting caught in the wind.

Step by Step

1. Clean and dry your hair before riding.
2. Choose a hairstyle that will keep your hair out of your face and minimize tangling.
3. Opt for a full-face helmet if possible.
4. Tuck long hair into the helmet before fastening the chin strap.
5. Adjust the helmet straps to keep it secure.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when it comes to wearing your hair while riding a motorcycle:

- Use a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to help protect your hair from the wind and elements.
- Keep a hairbrush or comb in your motorcycle bag in case you need to touch up your hair after a ride.
- If you have bangs, consider using hair gel or hairspray to keep them in place and prevent them from getting in your eyes.
- If you're going on a long ride, consider bringing a hair tie or extra hair clips in case your hairstyle needs a touch-up.

FAQ

Q: Can I wear a helmet with a bun?
A: It's possible to wear a helmet with a bun, but it may be more comfortable and secure to wear a braid or ponytail instead.

Q: Will wearing a helmet damage my hair?
A: Wearing a helmet can cause some damage to your hair, but taking steps to protect your hair and choosing a helmet that fits properly can minimize this damage.

Q: What should I do if my hair gets tangled while riding?
A: If your hair gets tangled while riding, pull over to a safe spot and use a hairbrush or comb to gently detangle it. Avoid using harsh pulling or tugging motions, as this can cause damage to your hair.

Hair Care

While wearing a helmet can help protect your hair from the wind and elements, it's still important to take care of your hair on a regular basis. Make sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair, and use a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to help protect your hair from the elements.

Overall, wearing your hair while riding a motorcycle is all about finding a style that works for you and taking steps to protect your hair from the wind and elements. By following the tips and tricks in this article, you can keep your hair safe and comfortable while you're out on the road.
