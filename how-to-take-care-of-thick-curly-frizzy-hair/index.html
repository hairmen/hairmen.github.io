How To Take Care Of Thick Curly Frizzy Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 24, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have thick curly frizzy hair, you know that it can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, you have a voluminous and eye-catching mane that can turn heads. On the other hand, you have to deal with tangles, dryness, and frizz that can make you want to pull your hair out. In this article, we'll show you how to take care of your thick curly frizzy hair so that you can flaunt your locks with confidence.

Description

Before we dive into the nitty-gritty of hair care, it's important to understand what makes thick curly frizzy hair unique. Thick hair has a larger circumference than fine hair, which means that it can hold more water and be more prone to dryness. Curly hair has a natural curl pattern that makes it more susceptible to tangles and breakage. Frizzy hair is caused by a lack of moisture and can be exacerbated by humidity and heat styling.

How to Take Care of Thick Curly Frizzy Hair

The key to taking care of thick curly frizzy hair is to keep it hydrated and nourished. Here are some tips to help you do just that:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Look for a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for curly hair and contain moisturizing ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or argan oil. These ingredients will help to hydrate your hair and reduce frizz.

2. Detangle Gently

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently, starting from the ends and working your way up. Avoid using a brush, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

3. Deep Condition Regularly

Use a deep conditioner once a week to give your hair an extra boost of moisture. Apply the conditioner to your hair, cover it with a shower cap, and leave it on for 20-30 minutes before rinsing it out.

4. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing and conditioning it. This will help to keep your hair hydrated throughout the day and reduce frizz.

5. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause damage to your hair and make it more prone to frizz. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature on the lowest setting possible.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to taking care of your thick curly frizzy hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wet your hair and apply a moisturizing shampoo, massaging it into your scalp and hair. Rinse out the shampoo and apply a moisturizing conditioner, focusing on the ends of your hair. Rinse out the conditioner thoroughly.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently, starting from the ends and working your way up.

Step 3: Deep Condition

Apply a deep conditioner to your hair, cover it with a shower cap, and leave it on for 20-30 minutes. Rinse out the conditioner thoroughly.

Step 4: Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 5: Air Dry Your Hair

Allow your hair to air dry, avoiding heat styling tools.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips to help you take care of your thick curly frizzy hair:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce frizz
  • Sleep with your hair in a loose bun or braid to prevent tangles
  • Use a hair mask once a week to give your hair an extra boost of moisture
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as these can dry out your hair

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my thick curly frizzy hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Q: Can I use a hair oil on my thick curly frizzy hair?

A: Yes, hair oils like argan oil or coconut oil can help to hydrate your hair and reduce frizz. Apply a small amount to the ends of your hair after washing and conditioning it.

Q: Can I brush my thick curly frizzy hair?

A: It's best to avoid using a brush on your curly hair, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently.
