How To Cut Toddler Boy Hair With Clippers

Written by Bardi Aug 04, 2023 · 3 min read

Cutting your toddler’s hair can be quite a challenge, especially if they are always on the move. However, with the right tools and techniques, it is possible to give your child a neat and tidy haircut right from the comfort of your home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting toddler boy hair with clippers.

How to

Before you start, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of clippers, a comb, a spray bottle filled with water, a towel, and a small brush. Once you have all the tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Preparation

Start by preparing your child for the haircut. Place a towel around their shoulders to protect their clothes from hair clippings. You can also distract them by giving them a toy or a snack to keep them occupied during the process.

Step 2: Choose the Right Clippers

When it comes to choosing the right clippers, it is important to consider the size of your child’s head and hair texture. You can use a smaller set of clippers for younger children or those with finer hair. Choose a larger set of clippers if your child has thicker hair.

Step 3: Start Trimming

Begin by combing your child’s hair in the direction it naturally falls. Use the clippers to trim the hair around the ears and the nape of the neck. Use a smaller guard for a shorter cut or a larger guard for a longer cut. Repeat this process on the sides and the top of the head.

Step 4: Clean Up

Once you have finished trimming, use the comb to remove any loose hairs. You can also use the small brush to remove any hair clippings that may have fallen onto your child’s clothing.

Tips

Here are some tips to make the haircutting process easier:

  • Use a spray bottle filled with water to dampen the hair before trimming. This will make it easier to cut through the hair.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you want a shorter cut.
  • Be gentle when trimming around the ears and the nape of the neck.
  • Take breaks if your child becomes fussy or restless.

FAQ

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting toddler boy hair with clippers:

Do I need to use scissors?

No, you can use clippers alone to give your child a neat and tidy haircut.

What if my child moves around too much?

You can use distractions such as toys or snacks to keep your child occupied during the process. You can also take breaks if your child becomes too restless.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don’t worry. Hair grows back quickly, and you can always take your child to a professional stylist to fix any mistakes.

Hair Care

To keep your child’s hair looking neat and tidy between haircuts, you can use a comb and a small amount of hair gel or pomade. Use the comb to style the hair in the direction it naturally falls, and apply a small amount of gel or pomade to hold the style in place.

Cutting toddler boy hair with clippers can be a fun and rewarding experience for both you and your child. With the right tools and techniques, you can give your child a neat and tidy haircut right from the comfort of your home.
