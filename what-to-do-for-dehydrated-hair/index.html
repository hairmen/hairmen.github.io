What To Do For Dehydrated Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Jun 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Dehydrated hair is a common problem faced by many people. It can be caused by a variety of factors such as excessive heat styling, overuse of chemical treatments, and environmental factors like sun exposure and hard water. If you are dealing with dehydrated hair, don't worry! In this article, we will discuss what to do for dehydrated hair and how to restore its health and vitality.

What is Dehydrated Hair?

Dehydrated hair is hair that lacks moisture and essential oils. This can make your hair look dull, dry, and lifeless. It can also lead to hair breakage, split ends, and hair loss. Dehydration can be caused by various factors such as overuse of hair styling tools, exposure to hard water, and environmental factors like sun exposure and harsh weather conditions.

How To Determine If You Have Dehydrated Hair?

If you are not sure whether you have dehydrated hair, here are some signs that you can look out for:

  • Dull and lifeless hair
  • Dry and brittle hair
  • Split ends
  • Hair breakage
  • Itchy and flaky scalp
  • Tangled hair

How To Treat Dehydrated Hair?

Now that you know what dehydrated hair is and how to determine if you have it, let's discuss how to treat it. Here are some steps that you can follow:

Step 1: Use Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

The first step in treating dehydrated hair is to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that contain natural oils like coconut oil, argan oil, and jojoba oil. These oils can help to nourish your hair and restore its moisture balance. Avoid using products that contain sulfates and alcohol as they can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Step 2: Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning your hair once a week can help to restore its moisture balance and improve its overall health. You can use a store-bought deep conditioner or make your own using natural ingredients like avocado, honey, and olive oil. Apply the conditioner to your hair and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it off.

Step 3: Reduce Heat Styling

Excessive heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to dehydration. Try to limit your use of hair styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and hair dryers. If you must use these tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize heat damage.

Step 4: Protect Your Hair From Sun Exposure

Exposure to the sun can cause your hair to become dehydrated and damaged. If you are going to be spending time outdoors, make sure to wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your hair from the sun's harmful UV rays.

Step 5: Drink Plenty of Water

Drinking plenty of water can help to hydrate your body and improve the health of your hair. Aim to drink at least 8 glasses of water per day to keep your body and hair hydrated.

Tips For Preventing Dehydrated Hair

Prevention is always better than cure, and the same goes for dehydrated hair. Here are some tips that can help to prevent dehydrated hair:

  • Avoid overuse of chemical treatments like hair dyes and relaxers
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair
  • Eat a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Avoid using tight hair ties and braids that can pull on your hair
  • Avoid using harsh hair products that contain sulfates and alcohol

FAQ About Dehydrated Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about dehydrated hair:

Q: Can dehydration cause hair loss?

A: Yes, dehydration can cause hair loss as it weakens the hair follicles and makes your hair more prone to breakage.

Q: Is it safe to use oil on dehydrated hair?

A: Yes, using natural oils like coconut oil, argan oil, and jojoba oil can help to nourish and hydrate your hair.

Q: Can I use a hair mask to treat dehydrated hair?

A: Yes, using a hair mask once a week can help to restore your hair's moisture balance and improve its overall health.

Hair Care Routine For Dehydrated Hair

If you have dehydrated hair, here is a hair care routine that you can follow:

  • Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Deep condition your hair once a week
  • Avoid overuse of hair styling tools
  • Protect your hair from sun exposure
  • Drink plenty of water
  • Eat a balanced diet

Dealing with dehydrated hair can be frustrating, but with the right care and treatment, you can restore its health and vitality. Follow the steps and tips outlined in this article, and you'll be on your way to having healthy, hydrated hair in no time!
