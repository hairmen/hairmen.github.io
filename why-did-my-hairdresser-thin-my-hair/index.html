Why Did My Hairdresser Thin My Hair

Written by Luffy Nov 02, 2023 · 3 min read

Have you ever walked out of a salon wondering why your hair feels thinner than it did before? You’re not alone! Hair thinning is a common technique used by hairdressers to create a more voluminous and manageable hairstyle. In this post, we’ll explore the reasons behind why your hairdresser may have chosen to thin your hair and how to take care of your new hairstyle.

What is Hair Thinning?

Hair thinning is a hairdressing technique that involves removing some of the hair’s thickness to create a more manageable hairstyle. This technique is often used on thick or curly hair to make it easier to style and to reduce its weight.

Why Did My Hairdresser Thin My Hair?

There are several reasons why your hairdresser may have chosen to thin your hair:

To Create Volume

Thinning the hair can create volume in your hairstyle. By removing some of the hair’s thickness, your hairdresser can create a more voluminous look that is easier to style.

To Reduce Weight

Thick hair can often feel heavy and difficult to manage. By thinning the hair, your hairdresser can reduce the weight of your hair, making it easier to style and more comfortable to wear.

To Create Texture

Thinning the hair can also create texture in your hairstyle. By removing some of the hair’s thickness, your hairdresser can create a more textured look that is modern and on-trend.

To Fix Uneven Layers

If your hair has uneven layers, thinning the hair can help to create a more even look. By removing some of the thickness from the longer sections of your hair, your hairdresser can create a more uniform appearance.

How to Take Care of Thinned Hair

Once your hair has been thinned, it’s important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that is designed for thinning hair. Look for shampoos that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and collagen, which can help to strengthen and thicken your hair.

Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like hair straighteners and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it look thinner. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from damage.

Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help to keep your thinned hair looking its best. Your hairdresser can remove any split ends and damaged hair, which can make your hair look thinner.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid wearing tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids, as these can pull on your hair and make it look thinner. Instead, opt for looser styles that are gentle on your hair.

FAQ

Is hair thinning permanent?

No, hair thinning is not permanent. Your hair will grow back over time.

Does hair thinning damage hair?

Hair thinning does not damage hair if done correctly. However, if too much hair is thinned or if the technique is not done properly, it can cause damage to the hair.

Can hair thinning be done on any hair type?

Hair thinning can be done on most hair types, but it is most effective on thick or curly hair.

Conclusion

Hair thinning is a common hairdressing technique that can create a more voluminous and manageable hairstyle. Your hairdresser may have chosen to thin your hair to create volume, reduce weight, create texture, or fix uneven layers. To take care of your thinned hair, use a gentle shampoo, avoid heat styling, get regular trims, and avoid tight hairstyles.
