How To Look Good In Photos With A Round Face

Written by Petter Jul 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have a round face and struggle to look good in photos? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people with round faces find it challenging to take flattering photos. However, with a few tips and tricks, you can enhance your features and look your best in every photo.

Table of Contents:

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to:

The first step to looking good in photos is to understand your face shape. If you have a round face, your cheekbones and jawline are likely to be of similar widths, and your face may be more prominent in the middle. To balance out your features and create a more flattering look, you can try the following:

1. Angle your face

Instead of facing the camera head-on, try angling your face slightly to the side. This will create more depth and definition, making your face appear slimmer. Remember to keep your neck straight and elongated to avoid any double chin.

2. Find your best side

Everyone has a better side for photos, and it's usually the one where your features are more prominent. Experiment with different angles to find your best side and stick to it for your photos. This will give you consistency and a more confident look.

Step by Step:

Now that you know the basics, here's a step-by-step guide on how to look good in photos with a round face:

Step 1: Stand in front of a neutral background

Avoid busy backgrounds that can distract from your face. Instead, choose a neutral background like a plain wall or a natural landscape.

Step 2: Angle your face

Turn your head slightly to the side, away from the camera. This will create more definition and make your face appear slimmer. Remember to keep your neck straight and elongated.

Step 3: Find your best side

Experiment with different angles to find your best side. This is usually the one where your features are more prominent.

Step 4: Relax your face

Relax your face and avoid any tension in your jaw or forehead. A relaxed expression will make you look more natural and confident.

Step 5: Smile naturally

A natural smile will make you look more approachable and friendly. Avoid forced or exaggerated smiles, as they can make you look fake.

Tips:

Here are some additional tips to help you look good in photos:

1. Use natural light

Natural light is the most flattering for photos. Avoid harsh overhead lighting or direct sunlight, as they can create unflattering shadows on your face. Instead, try to take photos during the golden hour, which is the hour after sunrise or before sunset.

2. Wear flattering clothing

Choose clothing that flatters your body shape and enhances your features. Avoid baggy or loose clothing that can make you look shapeless.

3. Use makeup to enhance your features

Makeup can be a powerful tool to enhance your features and create a more polished look. Experiment with different techniques to find what works best for you.

FAQ:

Q: How do I avoid double chin in photos?

A: To avoid a double chin, keep your neck straight and elongated. Stick your chin out slightly and tilt your head down. This will create a more defined jawline and minimize any double chin.

Q: Should I smile with my teeth or without?

A: It depends on your preference and the occasion. A natural smile with teeth can make you look more friendly and approachable. However, if you're not comfortable showing your teeth, a closed-lip smile can still look great.

Hair Care:

Your hairstyle can also play a significant role in how you look in photos. Here are some tips to help you choose the best hairstyle for your round face:

1. Add volume at the crown

Avoid flat hairstyles that can make your face appear even rounder. Instead, try adding volume at the crown of your head to create the illusion of a longer face.

2. Avoid blunt cuts

Blunt cuts can accentuate the roundness of your face. Instead, try layers or angled cuts that create more movement and definition.

3. Experiment with different partings

Changing your parting can create a different look and enhance your features. Try a side parting to create more asymmetry and balance out your face shape.

By following these tips and tricks, you can enhance your features and look your best in every photo. Remember to experiment with different angles, lighting, and poses to find what works best for you. With a little practice and confidence, you'll be taking stunning photos in no time!
