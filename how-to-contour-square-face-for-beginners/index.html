How To Contour Square Face For Beginners

Written by Christine Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Contouring is no longer just a makeup artist's secret. Anyone can learn to contour and highlight their face to achieve a more defined and sculpted look. However, contouring a square face shape may seem daunting to beginners. But with the right techniques and tools, you can easily contour your square face to create a more oval and balanced appearance.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to Contour Square Face for Beginners
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Contouring Square Faces
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Square Faces

Description:

A square face shape is characterized by a strong jawline, wide forehead, and angular features. Contouring can help soften these features and create a more balanced and symmetrical appearance. By using darker shades to create shadows and lighter shades to highlight, you can create the illusion of a more oval-shaped face.

How to Contour Square Face for Beginners:

The first step in contouring a square face is to determine your face shape. Once you know your face shape, you can determine which areas to contour and which areas to highlight. For a square face, you will want to contour the jawline, temples, and forehead, and highlight the cheekbones and under-eye area.

Here are the basic steps to contour a square face:

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Apply your foundation as usual, making sure to blend it into your hairline and jawline.
  2. Use a matte bronzer or contour powder that is one or two shades darker than your skin tone to contour the jawline. Starting at the ear, apply the contour powder along the jawline and blend it downwards towards your chin. This will create a shadow that will help to define your jawline and make it appear more narrow.
  3. Next, use the same contour powder to contour your temples. Apply the powder to the sides of your forehead, starting at your hairline and blending downwards towards your temples. This will help to soften the angular shape of your forehead.
  4. Apply a highlighter to your cheekbones and under-eye area. Use a shade that is one or two shades lighter than your skin tone. This will help to bring out your cheekbones and make your face appear more lifted.
  5. Finally, blend everything together using a blending brush or sponge. Make sure to blend the contour powder and highlighter together to create a seamless look.

Tips for Contouring Square Faces:

Here are some additional tips to help you contour your square face:

  • Choose a matte bronzer or contour powder to avoid shimmer or glitter, which can make your face appear wider.
  • Use a small, angled brush to apply the contour powder precisely and avoid creating a harsh line.
  • When applying highlighter, use a light hand and build up the product gradually to avoid a harsh contrast with the contour powder.
  • Blend, blend, blend! Blending is key to creating a natural-looking contour and highlight.

FAQs:

Here are some frequently asked questions about contouring square faces:

Q: Can contouring make my face look too thin?

A: It is possible to overdo contouring and make your face appear too thin. To avoid this, start with a light hand and build up the product gradually. Remember, less is more!

Q: Can I contour my nose as well?

A: Yes, you can contour your nose as well. Use a small, fluffy brush to apply a darker shade of contour powder to the sides of your nose and blend it outwards towards your cheekbones. This will help to create the illusion of a more narrow nose.

Hair Care for Square Faces:

In addition to contouring, choosing the right hairstyle can also help balance out a square face. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Choose a hairstyle that adds volume to the top of your head, such as a messy bun or voluminous curls.
  • Avoid hairstyles that are too short or too long, as these can make your face appear wider.
  • Consider adding layers to your hair to soften your features.

By following these tips and techniques, you can easily contour your square face for a more defined and balanced appearance. Remember to start with a light hand and build up the product gradually, and don't forget to blend, blend, blend!
