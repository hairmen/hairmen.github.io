How To Change Your Hair Type From 4C To 4A

Written by Robby Oct 18, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you have 4c hair, you know that it can be difficult to manage and style. 4a hair, on the other hand, is more defined and easier to style. If you're looking to change your hair type from 4c to 4a, there are a few things you can do to achieve the look you want. Keep reading to find out how.

Description

4c hair is tightly coiled and has a zigzag pattern. This hair type is the most fragile and prone to breakage. It also tends to be dry and lacks definition. 4a hair, on the other hand, has a more defined curl pattern and is easier to style. If you're looking to change your hair type from 4c to 4a, you'll need to focus on moisturizing and defining your curls.

How to Change Your Hair Type from 4c to 4a

Step 1: Moisturize Your Hair

The first step to changing your hair type is to moisturize your hair. 4c hair tends to be dry, so it's important to keep your hair well moisturized. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and follow up with a leave-in conditioner. You can also use a hair oil to seal in moisture.

Step 2: Use a Styling Product

To define your curls, you'll need to use a styling product. Choose a product that works well with your hair type, such as a curl defining cream or gel. Apply the product to your hair in small sections, making sure to distribute it evenly. Use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to define your curls.

Step 3: Avoid Heat

Heat can damage your hair and cause it to break. If you're looking to change your hair type, it's important to avoid heat as much as possible. Instead of using a flat iron or a curling iron, try using flexi rods or perm rods. These tools will help define your curls without damaging your hair.

Step 4: Trim Your Ends

Trimming your ends is important for maintaining healthy hair. If your ends are damaged, they can cause your hair to break and make it difficult to achieve the style you want. Trim your ends every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Tips

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a silk or satin bonnet.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.

FAQ

Can you really change your hair type?

No, you can't change your hair type. However, you can change the way your hair looks and behaves by using the right products and techniques.

How long does it take to see results?

It can take several weeks or even months to see results. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

Hair Care

Changing your hair type requires a lot of care and attention. Be gentle with your hair, and avoid using products or techniques that can cause damage. Focus on keeping your hair healthy and moisturized, and use styling products that work well with your hair type.

With these tips and techniques, you can change your hair type from 4c to 4a and achieve the style you want.
