Easiest Big Dogs To Train

Written by Petter Aug 05, 2023 · 6 min read

Welcome to our guide on the easiest big dogs to train! Training a big dog can be a challenging task, but with the right breed and approach, it can be a rewarding experience for both the dog and the owner. In this article, we will discuss the best big dog breeds for training, how to train them, and some tips for success. Let’s get started!

Best Big Dog Breeds for Training

When it comes to training big dogs, some breeds are easier to train than others. Here are some of the best big dog breeds for training:

1. Labrador Retriever

The Labrador Retriever is one of the most popular dog breeds in the world, and for good reason. They are intelligent, loyal, and eager to please, which makes them easy to train. They are also great with children and make wonderful family pets.

2. Golden Retriever

The Golden Retriever is another popular breed that is easy to train. They are friendly, intelligent, and eager to please, making them great for families with children. They are also good hunting dogs and excel in obedience competitions.

3. German Shepherd

The German Shepherd is a highly intelligent and obedient breed that is often used as a police or military dog. They are loyal and protective of their families, and excel in obedience and agility competitions.

4. Standard Poodle

The Standard Poodle is a highly intelligent and trainable breed that is often used in circus acts and other performances. They are also great family pets and are hypoallergenic, making them a good choice for people with allergies.

5. Boxer

The Boxer is a playful and energetic breed that is easy to train. They are great with children and make wonderful family pets. They are also good guard dogs and excel in obedience competitions.

How to Train a Big Dog

Training a big dog requires patience, consistency, and positive reinforcement. Here are some tips for training your big dog:

1. Start Early

It’s important to start training your big dog as early as possible. Puppies are like sponges and can absorb information quickly. The earlier you start training, the easier it will be to teach your dog good habits.

2. Be Consistent

Consistency is key when it comes to training your big dog. Make sure everyone in your household is using the same commands and rewards. This will help your dog understand what is expected of them.

3. Use Positive Reinforcement

Positive reinforcement is the best way to train your big dog. This means rewarding good behavior with treats or praise. Avoid using punishment or negative reinforcement, as this can lead to fear and aggression.

4. Keep Training Sessions Short

Big dogs can get easily distracted, so it’s important to keep training sessions short and focused. Aim for 10-15 minute sessions, and end on a positive note.

Step-by-Step Guide for Training a Big Dog

Here’s a step-by-step guide for training your big dog:

1. Start with Basic Commands

Begin by teaching your dog basic commands such as sit, stay, and come. Use positive reinforcement to reward good behavior.

2. Practice in Different Environments

Once your dog has mastered basic commands, practice in different environments such as the park or the beach. This will help your dog generalize the behavior.

3. Teach Advanced Commands

Once your dog has mastered basic commands, teach them advanced commands such as heel, fetch, and roll over. Use positive reinforcement to reward good behavior.

4. Practice Regularly

Training your dog is an ongoing process, so make sure to practice regularly. This will help reinforce good behavior and prevent bad habits from forming.

Tips for Success

Here are some additional tips for training your big dog:

1. Be Patient

Training a big dog takes time, so be patient and don’t get frustrated. Focus on the progress your dog is making, and celebrate their successes.

2. Be Firm but Gentle

Big dogs can be strong-willed, so it’s important to be firm but gentle when training. Avoid using physical force or punishment, as this can lead to fear and aggression.

3. Use High-Value Treats

When training your big dog, use high-value treats such as chicken or cheese. This will make training more fun and rewarding for your dog.

4. Make Training Fun

Training should be a fun and enjoyable experience for both you and your dog. Use toys and games to make training more engaging and entertaining.

FAQs

Q: What is the easiest big dog breed to train?

A: Labrador Retrievers and Golden Retrievers are two of the easiest big dog breeds to train.

Q: How long does it take to train a big dog?

A: Training a big dog is an ongoing process, and can take several months to a year or more depending on the dog’s age, breed, and temperament.

Q: Can you train an older big dog?

A: Yes, you can train an older big dog. It may take longer than training a younger dog, but with patience and consistency, you can still see results.

Hair Care for Big Dogs

Big dogs often require special hair care to keep their coats healthy and shiny. Here are some tips for hair care:

1. Brush Regularly

Big dogs often have thick and dense coats, so it’s important to brush them regularly to prevent matting and tangling. Use a slicker brush or comb to remove loose hair and debris.

2. Bathe Occasionally

Big dogs don’t need to be bathed as often as small dogs, but they should still be bathed occasionally to keep their coats clean and healthy. Use a gentle dog shampoo and rinse thoroughly.

3. Trim Nails

Big dogs can have long nails that can cause discomfort and pain if not trimmed regularly. Use a nail clipper or grinder to trim the nails, being careful not to cut the quick.

That’s all for our guide on the easiest big dogs to train. Remember, training a big dog takes patience, consistency, and positive reinforcement. With the right approach, you can have a well-behaved and happy big dog as a loyal companion. Good luck!
