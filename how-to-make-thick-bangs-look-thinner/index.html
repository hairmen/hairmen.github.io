How To Make Thick Bangs Look Thinner A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Nov 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of thick bangs that make your forehead look bulky? Don't worry, you're not alone. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to make thick bangs look thinner.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Make Thick Bangs Look Thinner
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Thick bangs can be a nightmare to work with, especially if you have a small forehead. They tend to create a bulky look that can be unflattering. Fortunately, there are a few ways to make your thick bangs look thinner without having to resort to cutting them off entirely.

How to Make Thick Bangs Look Thinner

The first thing you need to do is determine the cause of your bulky bangs. Is it because you have too much hair, or is it because your bangs are cut too short? Once you figure that out, you can proceed with the appropriate solution.

Option 1: Thin out your bangs

If you have too much hair in your bangs, you can thin them out to reduce the bulk. Here's how:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Comb your bangs straight down and clip the rest of your hair back.
  2. Take a small section of your bangs and hold it between your fingers.
  3. Slide a pair of thinning shears down the section of hair, starting from the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  4. Repeat this process until you have thinned out all of your bangs.
  5. Style your bangs as desired.

Thinning shears are a great tool for reducing bulk in your bangs. However, be careful not to overdo it, as you don't want to end up with thinning hair.

Option 2: Grow out your bangs

If your bangs are cut too short, they may appear bulky. In this case, the best solution is to grow them out. This will allow your hair to blend in better with the rest of your hair, creating a more natural look.

Tips

  • Don't try to thin out your bangs with regular scissors, as they can create a choppy and uneven look.
  • If you're not confident in your ability to thin out your bangs, consider visiting a hairstylist.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products on your bangs, as they can add to the bulk.
  • If you have a cowlick in your bangs, try blow-drying them in the opposite direction to help them lay flat.
  • Consider styling your bangs to the side instead of straight down, as this can help reduce the bulk.

FAQ

Q: How often should I thin out my bangs?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much you want to thin them out. Generally, thinning out your bangs every 6-8 weeks is a good idea.

Q: Can I thin out my bangs myself?

A: Yes, you can. However, it's important to be careful not to overdo it. If you're not confident in your ability to thin out your bangs, consider visiting a hairstylist.

Q: Will thinning out my bangs make them look stringy?

A: Not necessarily. If you use thinning shears correctly, your bangs should still look full but with less bulk.

Hair Care

To keep your bangs looking their best, it's important to take good care of them. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent product buildup.
  • Use a lightweight conditioner on your bangs to keep them soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your bangs too often, as this can cause damage.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to keep them looking neat.

By following these tips and techniques, you can make your thick bangs look thinner and more manageable. With a little patience and practice, you'll be able to achieve the perfect look for your face shape and hair type.
