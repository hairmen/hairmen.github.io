How To Cut Long Curly Boy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 02, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Long curly hair can be a challenge to manage, especially for young boys who are constantly on the move. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can give your little one a stylish haircut that he will love. In this article, we will provide you with a step by step guide on how to cut long curly boy hair in a relaxed English language. We will also share some tips and tricks to make the process easier and answer some frequently asked questions.

How to Cut Long Curly Boy Hair

Cutting long curly boy hair can be a daunting task, but with the right approach, it can be a fun and rewarding experience. Here are some things you need to keep in mind:

  • Choose the right style: Before you start cutting, decide on the style you want to achieve. Look for inspiration online or in magazines and show it to your barber or hairdresser.
  • Use the right tools: Make sure you have a good pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clippers if you want to give your little one a fade or a buzz cut.
  • Start slow: Don't rush the process. Start by cutting a little at a time and check your progress frequently.
  • Ask for help: If you feel overwhelmed, don't hesitate to ask for help from a professional or a friend who has experience cutting hair.

Step by Step Guide

Now that you know the basics, it's time to get started. Here is a step by step guide on how to cut long curly boy hair:

  1. Wash your child's hair and towel dry it until it is damp but not too wet.
  2. Comb your child's hair to remove any tangles and knots.
  3. Section the hair: Use clips or elastics to divide the hair into sections. Start at the back of the head and work your way around.
  4. Cut the hair: Start cutting the hair at the nape of the neck and work upwards. Use the scissors to trim off the ends of the hair. As you work your way up, use the comb to lift the hair and cut it to the desired length.
  5. Blend the hair: Use the scissors to blend the hair between the different sections. This will create a seamless transition between the longer and shorter parts of the hair.
  6. Trim the sides: If you want to give your child a fade or a buzz cut, use the hair clippers to trim the sides and back of the head. Start with the longest guard and work your way down to the shortest.
  7. Define the edges: Use the scissors or clippers to define the edges of the hairline and the sideburns. Be careful not to cut too much hair.
  8. Check for symmetry: Once you have finished cutting, check for symmetry by comparing the left and right sides of the head.
  9. Style the hair: Use a styling product like gel or mousse to define the curls and give the hair a finished look.
  10. Clean up: Use a brush or a vacuum to clean up any loose hair that may have fallen on the floor.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to make the process of cutting long curly boy hair easier:

  • Use a spray bottle filled with water to dampen the hair if it starts to dry out during the cutting process.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of the head and make sure it is even.
  • Be patient and take breaks if your child gets restless or uncomfortable.
  • Use a towel or a cape to protect your child's clothes from hair clippings.
  • Invest in a good quality pair of scissors and hair clippers. They will make the process easier and give you better results.

FAQs

How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on the style you want to achieve and how fast your child's hair grows. As a general rule, you should cut your child's hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

How can I prevent tangling and knots in my child's curly hair?

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and comb the hair gently with a wide-toothed comb. Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage the hair.

Can I cut my child's hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your child's hair yourself if you have the right tools and techniques. However, if you feel unsure or overwhelmed, it's best to seek the help of a professional.

Hair Care

After you have cut your child's hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot water or harsh chemicals that can damage the hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles to detangle the hair gently.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on the hair, as this can cause breakage and split ends.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or a hair oil to add shine and definition to the curls.

Cutting long curly boy hair can be a fun and rewarding experience if you approach it with patience and the right tools. Use this guide to help you achieve the perfect haircut for your little one.
