How To Cut Your Own Hair Back Of Neck A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 12, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking for an easy way to save money on haircuts and keep your hair looking fresh, cutting your own hair at home is a great option. While it can be intimidating to cut your own hair, with a little bit of practice and the right tools, you can create a professional-looking cut. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut your own hair back of neck, providing tips and tricks along the way.

Table of Contents:

  1. How to Cut Your Own Hair Back of Neck
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips and Tricks
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care After Cutting

How to Cut Your Own Hair Back of Neck

The first step in cutting your own hair is to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror. It's also helpful to have a second mirror or someone to help you check the back of your head.

Before you begin cutting, wash and dry your hair. This will help you see any uneven spots and ensure that your hair is clean and easy to work with.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own hair back of neck:

  1. Start by combing your hair straight down.
  2. Use your handheld mirror to check the back of your head for any uneven spots or areas that need trimming.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the back of your head, and comb it straight up.
  4. Hold the section of hair between your fingers, and use your scissors to trim the ends. Start with small cuts to avoid cutting too much at once.
  5. Repeat this process, working your way around the back of your head.
  6. Check your progress using your handheld mirror and the second mirror, if available.
  7. Once you're satisfied with the length, use your scissors to add any layers or texture that you desire.
  8. Comb your hair back down, and check for any uneven spots or areas that need additional trimming.
  9. Make any final adjustments until you're happy with the overall look.
  10. Finish by cleaning up any stray hairs and washing your hair to remove any loose strands.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some helpful tips and tricks to keep in mind when cutting your own hair back of neck:

  • Start with small cuts, and work your way up to larger ones. It's easier to cut more hair later than to try to fix a cut that's too short.
  • Use your fingers to measure the length of your hair. This will help ensure that you're cutting evenly.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. It's easier to cut someone else's hair than your own, so consider asking a family member or friend for assistance.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it difficult to achieve a clean cut.
  • Take breaks as needed. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so make sure to rest your arms and hands as needed.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about cutting your own hair back of neck:

  • Is it difficult to cut your own hair? Cutting your own hair can be challenging, but with practice and the right tools, it's possible to achieve a professional-looking cut.
  • What if I make a mistake? If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. Depending on the severity of the mistake, you may be able to fix it by trimming the surrounding hair or using hair accessories to hide the mistake.
  • How often should I cut my hair? The frequency of haircuts depends on your hair type and style. As a general rule, it's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to ensure that it stays healthy and looks great. Here are some tips for hair care after cutting:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask to keep your hair soft and shiny.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the length and shape of your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you can confidently cut your own hair back of neck at home. Remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and use the right tools to achieve a professional-looking cut. Happy cutting!
