Are You Allowed Long Hair In The Army

Written by Daniel Sep 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are considering joining the army and have long hair, you might be wondering if it is allowed. In this article, we will answer the question "Are you allowed long hair in the army?" and provide you with some tips on how to maintain your hair while serving in the military.

Description

The army has strict grooming standards that all soldiers must adhere to. These standards are in place to ensure that soldiers have a neat and professional appearance at all times. However, the rules regarding hair length are not set in stone, and they can vary depending on the branch of the military and the specific unit you are assigned to.

How to

If you are considering joining the army and have long hair, you should do some research to find out what the specific grooming standards are for the branch of the military you are interested in. You can also talk to a recruiter who can provide you with more information.

If you are already in the army and have long hair, you should speak to your commanding officer to find out what the rules are for your specific unit. In some cases, you may be allowed to keep your hair long as long as it is kept neat and out of your face.

Step by Step

Step 1: Research the grooming standards for the branch of the military you are interested in.

Step 2: Talk to a recruiter for more information.

Step 3: Speak to your commanding officer if you are already in the army and have long hair.

Tips

If you are allowed to keep your hair long, it is important to maintain it properly. This means washing it regularly, using conditioner to keep it soft and manageable, and keeping it tied back or in a bun to prevent it from getting in your face while you are on duty.

You should also invest in a good quality hairbrush or comb to keep your hair looking neat and tidy. If you are unsure about how to care for your hair, you can always ask a hairdresser or barber for advice.

FAQ

Can women have long hair in the army?

Yes, women in the army are allowed to have long hair as long as it is kept neat and out of their face.

Can men have long hair in the army?

Men in the army are generally not allowed to have long hair. However, there are some exceptions to this rule, and it can vary depending on the branch of the military and the specific unit you are assigned to.

What happens if you violate the grooming standards?

If you violate the grooming standards, you may be subject to disciplinary action, which can include being fined, being assigned extra duties, or even being discharged from the military.

Hair Care

Maintaining good hair care is important for soldiers in the army. Here are some tips to help you keep your hair looking neat and tidy:

- Wash your hair regularly with a good quality shampoo.

- Use conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.

- Keep your hair tied back or in a bun to prevent it from getting in your face while on duty.

- Invest in a good quality hairbrush or comb to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

- Ask a hairdresser or barber for advice on how to care for your hair.

In conclusion, the rules regarding hair length in the army can vary depending on the branch of the military and the specific unit you are assigned to. It is important to do your research and speak to your commanding officer to find out what the rules are for your specific situation. If you are allowed to keep your hair long, it is important to maintain it properly to ensure that you have a neat and professional appearance at all times.
