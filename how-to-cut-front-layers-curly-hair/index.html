How To Cut Front Layers For Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 15, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that finding the right haircut can be a challenge. One popular style for curly hair is front layers, which add shape and movement to your locks. But how do you cut front layers for curly hair? In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process step by step, as well as provide tips and advice for caring for your curly hair.

How to Cut Front Layers for Curly Hair

Cutting front layers for curly hair is a delicate process that requires patience and precision. Here’s an overview of the steps involved:

1. Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, you’ll need a few tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

2. Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you begin cutting.

3. Section Your Hair

Next, use your comb to section your hair. Create a part in the middle of your head and then create two more parts on either side of the middle part. Use your sectioning clips to secure each section of hair.

4. Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, comb out a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, creating a soft, rounded shape. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you’ve finished the bottom layer.

5. Cut the Top Layer

Once you’ve finished the bottom layer, move on to the top layer. Comb out a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, creating a soft, rounded shape. Be sure to keep the length of the top layer longer than the bottom layer to create the layered effect.

6. Blend the Layers

Finally, use your scissors to blend the layers together. Comb out small sections of hair and cut at an angle to create a seamless transition between the layers.

Tips for Cutting Front Layers for Curly Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting front layers for curly hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Cut hair at an angle to create a more natural, soft shape.
  • Keep the top layer longer than the bottom layer to create the layered effect.
  • Blend the layers together to create a seamless transition.

FAQ

How often should I cut my curly hair?

Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so it’s important to trim your locks every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Can I cut my own curly hair?

It’s possible to cut your own curly hair, but it can be challenging. We recommend visiting a professional stylist who specializes in curly hair for the best results.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

After cutting your front layers, it’s important to take care of your curly hair to maintain its shape and health. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry to prevent frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it’s wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and add moisture.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you’ll be able to cut front layers for curly hair with ease. Remember to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shape, and enjoy your new look!
