My 9 Year Old Son Has Underarm Hair A Guide To Hair Care

Written by Petter Oct 04, 2023 · 4 min read

As a parent, it can be surprising and even a bit concerning when you notice that your 9 year old son has underarm hair. However, it’s important to understand that this is a natural part of the growing up process and nothing to be alarmed about. In this article, we’ll provide you with a guide on how to handle this situation, including tips on hair care, how to talk to your child, and more.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Handle the Situation
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Hair Care
  • Tips for Parents
  • FAQ

Description

Underarm hair is a natural part of puberty and typically starts to grow in boys around the age of 9 or 10. It’s caused by an increase in testosterone, a hormone that plays a key role in male development. While it may be surprising to see your child developing underarm hair at such a young age, it’s important to remember that this is a normal part of the growing up process.

How to Handle the Situation

If you notice that your 9 year old son has underarm hair, it’s important to handle the situation with care and sensitivity. Here are some tips:

Talk to Your Child

It’s important to have an open and honest conversation with your child about the changes they are experiencing. Explain to them that underarm hair is a natural part of puberty and that it’s nothing to be embarrassed or ashamed about. Reassure them that these changes are normal and that you are there to support them.

Be Positive

Avoid making negative comments or teasing your child about their underarm hair. Instead, focus on the positive aspects of growing up and the changes that come with it. Encourage your child to embrace their new experiences and celebrate their milestones.

Step-by-Step Guide to Hair Care

Now that your child has underarm hair, it’s important to teach them how to take care of it. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash Regularly

Encourage your child to wash their underarms regularly with soap and water. This will help to keep the area clean and prevent any unpleasant odors.

Step 2: Use Deodorant

Deodorant can help to control sweat and odor. Choose a gentle, unscented deodorant that is designed for sensitive skin.

Step 3: Shave or Trim?

Some parents may wonder whether their child should shave or trim their underarm hair. There is no right or wrong answer to this question. Some boys prefer to shave or trim their hair, while others choose to leave it natural. Talk to your child about their preferences and help them make a decision that they are comfortable with.

Tips for Parents

Here are some additional tips for parents:

Be Supportive

Puberty can be a challenging time for children. It’s important to be supportive and understanding during this time. Encourage your child to ask questions and provide them with the resources they need to learn more about their changing bodies.

Set Boundaries

While it’s important to be supportive, it’s also important to set boundaries. Talk to your child about appropriate behavior and help them understand what is and isn’t acceptable.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal for a 9 year old boy to have underarm hair?

A: Yes, it’s normal for boys to start developing underarm hair around the age of 9 or 10.

Q: Should my child shave or trim their underarm hair?

A: There is no right or wrong answer to this question. Some boys prefer to shave or trim their hair, while others choose to leave it natural. Talk to your child about their preferences and help them make a decision that they are comfortable with.

Q: Will my child’s underarm hair continue to grow?

A: Yes, your child’s underarm hair will continue to grow and thicken as they go through puberty.

Q: Do girls also develop underarm hair?

A: Yes, girls also develop underarm hair as part of puberty.

Overall, it’s important to remember that underarm hair is a natural part of puberty and nothing to be alarmed about. Talk to your child about the changes they are experiencing and help them learn how to take care of their new hair. With care and support, you can help your child navigate this exciting time in their life.
