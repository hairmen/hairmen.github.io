How Do You Cut Your Hair With Thinning Scissors

Written by Frank Jun 28, 2023 · 6 min read

Do you want to add texture and volume to your hair without sacrificing its length? Then, thinning scissors are the perfect tool for you! With these scissors, you can create a natural-looking, layered haircut that flatters your face shape and highlights your best features. However, if you've never used thinning scissors before, you might feel intimidated by their sharp blades and complex technique. Don't worry, though – cutting your hair with thinning scissors is easier than you think! In this article, we'll show you how to do it step by step, along with some tips and FAQs to help you get the best results.

Description: What Are Thinning Scissors?

Thinning scissors, also called texturizing shears, are a type of hair-cutting scissors that have teeth-like blades instead of smooth ones. These teeth are designed to remove some of the hair's thickness and weight, without creating harsh lines or uneven layers. Thinning scissors come in different sizes and shapes, depending on their teeth count, spacing, and length. Some thinning scissors have only a few teeth, while others have dozens of them. The more teeth a scissor has, the more hair it will remove with each cut. The spacing between the teeth also affects the amount of hair that is cut, as well as the texture and shape of the haircut. Finally, the length of the blades and the handles determines how comfortable and stable the scissors are to use.

How To Cut Your Hair with Thinning Scissors

Before you start cutting your hair with thinning scissors, make sure you have the right tools and equipment:

  • Thinning scissors
  • Regular scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle or misting brush with water
  • Mirror or someone to help you

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start with clean, dry hair that is free of tangles and knots. If your hair is curly or wavy, you might want to straighten it first, as thinning scissors work best on straight hair. Use a heat protectant spray or serum to prevent damage from the heat.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections, depending on the thickness and length of your hair. For example, if you have long hair, you might want to section it into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Use clips or hair ties to secure each section.

Step 3: Start with the back

Start cutting your hair from the back, as this area is harder to see and reach. Use regular scissors to trim any split ends or damaged hair. Then, take a small section of hair from the back, about 1 inch wide, and hold it between your fingers. Slide the thinning scissors along the length of the hair, starting from the mid-shaft and moving towards the ends. Repeat this process along the section, making sure to blend the hair evenly. Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can create gaps or uneven layers. Keep the thinning scissors at a slight angle, about 45 degrees, to avoid cutting too deep into the hair.

Step 4: Move to the sides and front

After you finish the back, move to the sides and front of your hair. Use the same technique as before, taking small sections and thinning them out gradually. You can also create face-framing layers by cutting some hair shorter than others, or by cutting diagonally instead of straight. Don't forget to blend the sections together, to avoid any harsh lines or choppy edges.

Step 5: Check the balance and symmetry

Once you finish cutting your hair with thinning scissors, check the balance and symmetry of your haircut. Look in the mirror or ask someone to help you. Make sure the layers are even and smooth, and that the length and volume are consistent. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and fix them with the thinning scissors or regular scissors.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair with Thinning Scissors

  • Use professional-grade thinning scissors, as they are sharper and more precise than regular scissors. Avoid using kitchen or craft scissors, as they can damage your hair and create uneven layers.
  • Start with a small amount of hair, and thin it out gradually. You can always go back and thin more if needed, but you can't add hair back once it's cut.
  • Don't thin your hair too close to the roots or the ends, as this can create a spiky or frizzy look. Aim for the mid-shaft, where the hair is thickest and heaviest.
  • Comb your hair before and after each cut, to remove any tangles and to see the texture and shape more clearly.
  • Hold the scissors vertically or horizontally, depending on the effect you want to achieve. Vertical cuts create softer layers, while horizontal cuts create sharper layers.
  • Practice on a mannequin or a friend's hair, before cutting your own hair. This will help you get used to the scissors and to the technique.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions About Cutting Your Hair with Thinning Scissors

Q: Can I use thinning scissors on curly or thick hair?

A: Yes, but you need to be careful not to thin too much hair at once, as this can create frizz or thin spots. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls, and apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to prevent dryness and breakage.

Q: Can I use thinning scissors on short hair?

A: Yes, but you need to be more precise and careful, as short hair shows mistakes more easily. Use small, sharp scissors to trim the hair, and use thinning scissors sparingly, only to add texture and volume.

Q: Can I cut my own hair with thinning scissors?

A: Yes, but it's recommended to have someone help you, especially for the back and hard-to-reach areas. If you're cutting your own hair, use a three-way mirror to see all angles of your head, and take breaks to rest your arms and hands.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair with Thinning Scissors

After cutting your hair with thinning scissors, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo, and condition it with a moisturizing conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and create split ends. Use a heat protectant spray or serum before blow-drying or curling your hair.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, to prevent split ends and to maintain the shape and length of your haircut.
  • Apply a hair mask or treatment once a week, to nourish your hair and to restore its shine and softness.
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and pollution, by wearing a hat or a scarf, or by using a leave-in conditioner or oil with UV protection.

Cutting your hair with thinning scissors can be a fun and creative way to change your hairstyle, and to express your personality and style. By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut at home, and feel confident and beautiful in your own skin. Happy cutting!
