How To Give Yourself A Fade Buzz Cut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a low-maintenance haircut that's easy to maintain, then a fade buzz cut might be just what you need. In this article, we'll show you how to give yourself a fade buzz cut from the comfort of your own home. We'll cover everything from the tools you'll need to the step-by-step process of cutting your hair, so let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • How to Give Yourself a Fade Buzz Cut
  • Tips for a Perfect Fade Buzz Cut
  • FAQs

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Guard attachments
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror (preferably two)
  • Towel or cape
  • Hairbrush
  • Hair product (optional)

Make sure your clippers are fully charged or plugged in and your guard attachments are clean and ready to use. You don't want to run out of battery or have your clippers jam halfway through the cut.

How to Give Yourself a Fade Buzz Cut

Now that you have all the tools you need, it's time to start cutting your hair. Follow these steps to give yourself a fade buzz cut:

Step 1: Decide on the Length

Decide on the length of your hair. A fade buzz cut typically involves cutting the hair on the sides and back shorter than the hair on top.

Step 2: Attach the Guard

Attach the guard to your clippers. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to shorter guards if necessary.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair with the longest guard attachment. Begin at the nape of your neck and work your way up to the crown of your head. Make sure you're cutting against the direction of your hair growth for an even cut.

Step 4: Change the Guard

Change the guard attachment to a shorter length and repeat the process. Keep working your way up the back and sides of your head, gradually shortening the length of the guard attachment.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Blend the hair between the different lengths by using a comb or your fingers to pull the hair up and away from your head. Use scissors to remove any longer hairs that stick out.

Step 6: Cut the Top

Use scissors to cut the hair on top of your head to the desired length. You can use a comb to help guide you and make sure the cut is even.

Step 7: Clean up the Edges

Use your clippers or scissors to clean up the edges around your ears, neck, and hairline. Take your time and be careful not to cut too much hair.

Step 8: Check Your Work

Use multiple mirrors to check your work from different angles. Make any necessary adjustments to the length or shape of your hair.

Step 9: Style Your Hair (Optional)

If you want, you can use a hair product to style your hair. A little bit of wax or pomade can help give your hair some texture and hold.

Tips for a Perfect Fade Buzz Cut

Here are some tips to help you achieve a perfect fade buzz cut:

  • Start with a longer guard attachment and work your way down to shorter ones
  • Blend the hair between different lengths to avoid harsh lines
  • Take your time and don't rush
  • Use multiple mirrors to check your work from different angles
  • Be careful when cutting around your ears, neck, and hairline

FAQs

Can I give myself a fade buzz cut if I've never cut my own hair before?

While it might be a little challenging if you've never cut your own hair before, it's definitely possible. Just take your time and be patient.

Do I need to use scissors for a fade buzz cut?

Yes, you'll need scissors to cut the hair on top of your head and to blend the hair between different lengths.

Can I use a regular comb instead of a barber comb?

Yes, you can use a regular comb. However, a barber comb might be more helpful because it has finer teeth and is typically longer than a regular comb.

Hair Care After a Fade Buzz Cut

After you've given yourself a fade buzz cut, it's important to take care of your hair properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with shampoo and conditioner
  • Use a hair product if you want to style your hair
  • Get regular haircuts to maintain the shape and length of your hair

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you should be able to give yourself a perfect fade buzz cut. Good luck!
