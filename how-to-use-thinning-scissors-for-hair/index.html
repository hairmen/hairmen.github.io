How To Use Thinning Scissors For Hair

Written by Daniel Oct 24, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Thinning scissors are a great tool to have in your hair kit because they can help you achieve a variety of styles. They are designed to remove bulk from hair without changing the length, making it easier to style and manage. In this article, we'll take a look at how to use thinning scissors for hair, step by step, and give you some tips on how to get the best results.

Description

Thinning scissors are designed with two blades, one with teeth and one without. The teeth blade has evenly spaced teeth that remove hair as it slides through. The other blade is solid and acts as a guide for the teeth blade. Thinning scissors come in various lengths and tooth spacing, so you can choose the right one for your hair type and desired look.

How to Use Thinning Scissors for Hair

To use thinning scissors for hair, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and condition your hair as you normally would, and then let it air dry or blow dry it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before using thinning scissors.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using hair clips or ties. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 3: Hold the Thinning Scissors Correctly

Hold the thinning scissors at a 45-degree angle and use your thumb and middle finger to control the blades. Make sure the teeth blade is facing away from your scalp.

Step 4: Start Thinning Your Hair

Starting at the bottom of your hair, slide the thinning scissors through the section of hair you want to thin. Make sure to cut only a small amount of hair at a time. Continue moving up your head, section by section, until you've thinned all the hair you want to thin.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when using thinning scissors:

  • Choose the right thinning scissors for your hair type and desired look.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, or you'll end up with uneven results.
  • Use thinning scissors on dry hair to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Hold the scissors at a 45-degree angle to get the best results.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about using thinning scissors for hair:

Can I use thinning scissors on wet hair?

No, it's best to use thinning scissors on dry hair to avoid cutting too much hair.

What's the difference between thinning scissors and regular scissors?

Thinning scissors have a toothed blade that removes hair as it slides through, while regular scissors have two solid blades that cut hair all at once.

Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?

Yes, but you need to choose the right thinning scissors for your hair type and desired look.

Hair Care

Using thinning scissors can help you achieve a variety of styles and make your hair more manageable. However, it's essential to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Make sure to wash and condition your hair regularly, use a heat protectant when styling with heat tools, and get regular trims to prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can use thinning scissors for hair and create a variety of looks that suit your style and personality.
