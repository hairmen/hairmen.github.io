How To Cut Curly Hair Into Mullet A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Oct 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a bold and edgy hairstyle that will make you stand out from the crowd, a mullet might be just what you need. This iconic haircut has been making a comeback in recent years, and it's not hard to see why. With its short front and long back, the mullet is a perfect choice for those who want to add some personality to their look.

Description

A curly mullet is a unique hairstyle that combines the best of both worlds. It adds volume and texture to your hair while keeping it short and manageable at the front. However, cutting curly hair can be a challenging task, especially if you're not used to working with this hair type. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut curly hair into a mullet step by step.

How to Cut Curly Hair into Mullet

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type. After washing, gently towel dry your hair until it's slightly damp. Don't use a hairdryer, as it can make your hair frizzy.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Using your comb, separate your hair into two sections. Comb the front section of your hair forward and the back section backward. Make sure the line between the two sections is straight.

Step 4: Cut the Front Section

Starting with the front section, cut your hair to your desired length. Keep in mind that the front should be shorter than the back. To achieve a curly mullet, cut your hair in layers. Take small sections of your hair and cut them at an angle. This will create layers that will add volume to your hair.

Step 5: Cut the Back Section

Once you've finished cutting the front section, it's time to move on to the back. Start by combing the back section of your hair. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to your desired length. You can cut the hair straight across or at an angle.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Using your scissors, blend the layers in the front and back sections of your hair. This will create a seamless transition between the two sections.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to style it. Use a curling iron to create loose curls throughout your hair. Apply a hair gel or mousse to hold the curls in place. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting curly hair into a mullet:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep your hair damp while cutting it.
  • Be patient and take your time when cutting your hair.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're not confident in your cutting skills.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair into a mullet:

Can I cut my own hair into a curly mullet?

Yes, you can cut your own hair into a curly mullet. However, it's important to be patient and take your time. It's also a good idea to have someone else check your work to make sure it looks even.

How often should I get a haircut?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your haircut. On average, most people get a haircut every six to eight weeks.

How do I maintain my curly mullet?

To maintain your curly mullet, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Make sure to keep your hair hydrated by using a hair mask once a week. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Caring for curly hair can be challenging, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your hair healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like hair straighteners and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Don't wash your hair too often, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as it can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to cut your curly hair into a stylish mullet that's sure to turn heads. Just remember to be patient, take your time, and have fun!
