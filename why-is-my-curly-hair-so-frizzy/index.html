Why Is My Curly Hair So Frizzy

Written by Petter Oct 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you may be all too familiar with the struggle of frizz. No matter how much you try to tame your curls, they always seem to have a mind of their own. So, what causes this frizz, and how can you prevent it? In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind frizz and give you some tips on how to keep your curls looking their best.

  • What Causes Frizz?
  • How to Prevent Frizz

What Causes Frizz?

Frizz occurs when the cuticle layer of your hair is raised, allowing moisture to enter and swell the hair shaft. This can happen for several reasons:

  • Humidity: Moisture in the air can cause your hair to absorb water and swell, leading to frizz.
  • Dryness: Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, which can cause the cuticle to become rough and frizzy.
  • Damaged Hair: Chemical treatments, heat styling, and rough handling can all damage the hair cuticle, making it prone to frizz.

It's important to understand what's causing your frizz so that you can take the appropriate steps to prevent it.

How to Prevent Frizz

Preventing frizz starts with proper hair care. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner formulated for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and always use a heat protectant spray when you do.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl defining cream to help keep your curls hydrated and defined.

Step-by-Step Guide to Taming Frizz

If you're already dealing with frizz, don't worry â there are steps you can take to tame it:

  1. Start with clean, wet hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl defining cream to your hair, focusing on the ends and avoiding the roots.
  3. Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze excess water from your hair.
  4. Flip your head upside down and scrunch your hair with your hands to help define your curls.
  5. Blow dry your hair on a low or medium heat setting, using a diffuser attachment to help distribute the heat evenly.
  6. Once your hair is dry, use a small amount of hair oil or serum to help smooth down any remaining frizz.

Tips for Managing Frizz

Here are a few additional tips for keeping frizz under control:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Sleep on a satin or silk pillowcase to help prevent friction and breakage.
  • Avoid wearing hats or tight headbands that can flatten your curls and cause frizz.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and prevent further damage.

FAQs About Frizzy Curly Hair

Q: Can I brush curly hair?

A: It's generally best to avoid brushing curly hair, as this can cause breakage and frizz. If you need to detangle your hair, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers when your hair is wet.

Q: What's the best way to dry curly hair?

A: The best way to dry curly hair is to let it air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel, as this can cause frizz.

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting to avoid causing damage and frizz.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner formulated for curly hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot water when you wash your hair, as this can cause frizz.
  • Consider using a silk or satin hair wrap or bonnet to protect your curls while you sleep.

By following these tips and taking good care of your curls, you can keep frizz at bay and enjoy beautiful, healthy hair.
