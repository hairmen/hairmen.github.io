Why Is My Dog s Hair Falling Out And Skin Turning Black

Written by Petter Oct 28, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are a dog owner, you may have noticed that your furry companion’s hair is falling out and their skin is turning black. This can be concerning and may indicate an underlying health issue. In this article, we will discuss the possible reasons why your dog's hair is falling out and their skin is turning black. We will also give you tips on how to take care of your dog's hair and prevent further hair loss.

Table of Contents

  • Possible Reasons for Hair Loss and Black Skin
  • How to Take Care of Your Dog's Hair
  • Tips to Prevent Hair Loss
  • FAQs

Possible Reasons for Hair Loss and Black Skin

There are several reasons why your dog's hair may be falling out and their skin turning black. Some of the most common causes include:

Hormonal Imbalance

Hormonal imbalances can cause hair loss and blackening of the skin. This can be due to an underactive thyroid gland, which can cause a condition called hypothyroidism. Dogs with hypothyroidism may also experience weight gain, lethargy, and a dull coat.

Allergies

Allergies can also cause hair loss and black skin in dogs. This can be due to a reaction to certain foods, environmental allergens like pollen, or flea bites. Dogs with allergies may also experience itching, redness, and inflammation of the skin.

Fungal or Bacterial Infections

Fungal or bacterial infections can also cause hair loss and blackening of the skin. These infections can be caused by a variety of pathogens, including ringworm, staphylococcus, and yeast. Dogs with infections may also experience itching, redness, and scaling of the skin.

Cushing's Disease

Cushing's disease is a hormonal disorder that can cause hair loss and blackening of the skin. This condition is caused by an overproduction of cortisol, a hormone produced by the adrenal gland. Dogs with Cushing's disease may also experience increased thirst and urination, weight gain, and a pot-bellied appearance.

How to Take Care of Your Dog's Hair

To take care of your dog's hair, you should:

Brush Your Dog's Hair Regularly

Brushing your dog's hair regularly can help to remove loose hair and prevent matting. This can also help to distribute the natural oils in your dog's coat and keep it healthy.

Bathe Your Dog as Needed

Bathing your dog as needed can help to keep their coat clean and healthy. However, you should be careful not to over-bathe your dog, as this can strip their skin of its natural oils and cause dryness.

Feed Your Dog a Healthy Diet

Feeding your dog a healthy diet can help to keep their coat healthy and shiny. You should choose a high-quality dog food that is rich in protein and nutrients.

Tips to Prevent Hair Loss

To prevent hair loss in your dog, you should:

Keep Your Dog's Environment Clean

Keeping your dog's environment clean can help to prevent infections and allergies that can lead to hair loss. You should regularly clean your dog's bedding, toys, and food and water bowls.

Use Flea and Tick Prevention

Using flea and tick prevention can help to prevent infestations that can cause itching and hair loss. You should talk to your veterinarian about the best flea and tick prevention products for your dog.

Visit Your Veterinarian Regularly

Visiting your veterinarian regularly can help to catch health issues early and prevent hair loss. Your veterinarian can also recommend treatments for hair loss and black skin.

FAQs

Can stress cause hair loss in dogs?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss in dogs. This can be due to the release of cortisol, a hormone produced by the adrenal gland, in response to stress.

Can a poor diet cause hair loss in dogs?

Yes, a poor diet can cause hair loss in dogs. Dogs need a balanced diet that is rich in protein and nutrients to maintain a healthy coat.

Can hair loss in dogs be treated?

Yes, hair loss in dogs can be treated depending on the underlying cause. Your veterinarian can recommend treatments based on the cause of your dog's hair loss.



By following these tips and visiting your veterinarian regularly, you can help to prevent hair loss and black skin in your furry companion. Remember, a healthy coat is a sign of a healthy dog!
