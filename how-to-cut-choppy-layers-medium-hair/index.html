How To Cut Choppy Layers On Medium Hair

Written by Petter Oct 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for a fresh new hairstyle that adds movement and texture to your medium-length hair? Choppy layers may be just what you need! This trendy look is perfect for adding volume and dimension to any hair type. In this article, we’ll show you how to cut choppy layers on medium hair in easy-to-follow steps. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Choppy Layers on Medium Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Choppy Layers
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Description

Choppy layers are a versatile haircut that works well on medium-length hair. They involve cutting the hair into different lengths to create a textured, messy look. Choppy layers can be cut in various ways, depending on your preferences and hair type. This style is perfect for those who want a low-maintenance look that still looks chic and on-trend.

How to Cut Choppy Layers on Medium Hair

Cutting choppy layers on medium hair can be done at home, but it’s best to get professional help if you’re unsure of what to do. However, if you’re feeling confident, here’s a step-by-step guide on how to cut choppy layers on medium hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it’s completely dry before you begin cutting.
  2. Part your hair down the middle and comb it straight.
  3. Take a section of hair from the back of your head, starting at the nape of your neck.
  4. Hold the section of hair straight up and cut it at an angle, using your scissors to create a choppy effect. Repeat this process until you’ve cut all the hair in this section.
  5. Move on to the next section of hair and repeat the cutting process.
  6. Make sure to cut the hair at different lengths to create a textured look.
  7. Continue cutting sections of hair until you’ve cut all the hair on your head.
  8. Use a mirror to check the back of your head to make sure the layers are even.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Choppy Layers

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting choppy layers on medium hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start by cutting small sections of hair, and gradually increase the size of the sections as you go.
  • Cut the hair at different lengths to create a choppy effect.
  • Take your time and be patient – cutting choppy layers can take some practice.
  • When cutting the hair at the back of your head, use a mirror to check that the layers are even.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut choppy layers on my own?

While it’s possible to cut choppy layers on your own, it’s best to get professional help if you’re unsure of what to do. A hairstylist can help you achieve the look you want while ensuring that your hair is cut evenly and at the right lengths.

What hair types work best for choppy layers?

Choppy layers work best on medium-length hair that’s straight or wavy. If you have curly hair, you may need to straighten it before cutting the layers to ensure an even cut.

How often should I get my choppy layers trimmed?

It’s recommended to get your choppy layers trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and texture of the cut.

Hair Care Tips for Choppy Layers

Here are some hair care tips to keep your choppy layers looking their best:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add volume to your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • When using heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your choppy layers looking fresh.

Now that you know how to cut choppy layers on medium hair, it’s time to try it out for yourself! Remember to take your time and be patient, and always seek professional help if you’re unsure of what to do. With the right care and maintenance, your choppy layers will look stylish and on-trend for months to come.
