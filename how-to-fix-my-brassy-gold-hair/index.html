How To Fix My Brassy Gold Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your brassy gold hair? Do you want to achieve a cooler, ashier tone? Look no further! In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to fix your brassy gold hair.

Description: What is Brassy Gold Hair?

Brassy gold hair is a common problem experienced by individuals who have bleached or lightened their hair. It occurs when the hair turns a warm, yellow or orange tone instead of the desired cooler, ashier tone. This can be caused by a variety of factors, such as the type of bleach or toner used, the condition of the hair, or even the water quality.

How to Fix My Brassy Gold Hair: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Assess Your Hair

The first step in fixing your brassy gold hair is to assess its current condition. Look at the overall tone of your hair and determine whether it has a warm or cool undertone. This will help you choose the right products and techniques to achieve your desired result.

Step 2: Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a must-have for anyone with brassy gold hair. It works by depositing violet pigments onto the hair to neutralize the warm tones. Apply the purple shampoo to wet hair and leave it on for 5-10 minutes before rinsing it out. Repeat this process once or twice a week until you achieve your desired tone.

Step 3: Try a Toner

If the purple shampoo alone doesn't fix your brassy gold hair, you may want to try a toner. Toners are specifically designed to neutralize unwanted tones in the hair and can be used in conjunction with a purple shampoo. Choose a toner that is the opposite of your hair's current undertone. For example, if your hair is warm, choose a toner with a cool, ashier tone.

Step 4: Consider a Professional Treatment

If your hair is severely brassy or damaged, you may want to consider a professional treatment. A hair stylist can use a variety of techniques, such as a color correction or a gloss, to help fix your brassy gold hair. While this option may be more expensive, it can provide you with the best results.

Tips for Fixing Brassy Gold Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you fix your brassy gold hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help maintain your new tone.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause additional damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help repair and nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can cause additional damage and fade your new tone.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I fix my brassy gold hair at home?

A: Yes, you can fix your brassy gold hair at home using a purple shampoo or toner. However, if your hair is severely damaged or you are unsure how to properly fix it, it may be best to seek the help of a professional stylist.

Q: How long will it take to fix my brassy gold hair?

A: The time it takes to fix your brassy gold hair depends on the severity of the problem and the products and techniques you use. It may take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to achieve your desired tone.

Q: Will fixing my brassy gold hair cause additional damage?

A: Fixing your brassy gold hair can cause additional damage if not done properly. It is important to use high-quality products and follow the recommended techniques to minimize damage.

Hair Care: Maintaining Your New Tone

Once you have fixed your brassy gold hair, it is important to take care of it to maintain your new tone. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help maintain your new tone.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause additional damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help repair and nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can cause additional damage and fade your new tone.

By following these tips and techniques, you can fix your brassy gold hair and achieve a cooler, ashier tone. Remember to take care of your hair to maintain your new tone and keep it looking healthy and vibrant.
