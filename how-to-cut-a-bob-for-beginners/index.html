How To Cut A Bob For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a fun and rewarding experience. If you're looking to try a new hairstyle, a bob is a classic look that's easy to maintain and style. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut a bob for beginners.

How to Cut a Bob for Beginners: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the tools you need. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. A handheld mirror can also be helpful to see the back of your head.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry. Shampoo and condition your hair, then use a blow dryer to dry it completely. This will help you see the natural shape of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to section your hair into four parts: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Start with the back section of your hair. Use your comb to separate a small section of hair at the nape of your neck. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, then cut straight across. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way up to the crown of your head. Be sure to cut in a straight line.

Step 5: Cut the Sides

Now it's time to cut the sides of your hair. Unclip the left side section and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle, following the shape of your jawline. Repeat on the right side.

Step 6: Cut the Top

Finally, it's time to cut the top section of your hair. Unclip the top section and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting, use your comb to check for any uneven areas. If you notice any, trim them with your scissors.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut your hair, it's time to style it. Use a straightener or curling iron to create your desired look. You can also add some volumizing mousse or hairspray to give your hair some extra hold.

Tips for Cutting a Bob

  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter. You can always cut more, but you can't add hair back once it's cut.
  • Use hair clips to keep sections of hair out of the way while you're cutting.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can shrink up when it dries.
  • Take your time and don't rush. It's better to make small cuts than to cut off too much at once.

FAQ About Cutting a Bob

Can I cut a bob with curly hair?

Yes! You can cut a bob with any hair texture. However, keep in mind that curly hair will shrink up when it dries, so you may want to cut it a little longer than you would if you had straight hair.

How often should I cut my bob?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your bob.

Can I cut a bob with bangs?

Yes! You can add bangs to your bob by cutting a section of hair in the front and trimming it to your desired length.

Hair Care Tips for Your Bob

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet to prevent breakage.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Cutting a bob can be a fun and easy way to switch up your look. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time and have fun!
