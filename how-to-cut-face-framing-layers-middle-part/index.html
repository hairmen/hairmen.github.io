How To Cut Face Framing Layers Middle Part

Written by Eveline Sep 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to give your hair a new look? One way to freshen up your style is by adding face framing layers to your middle part. This technique can help accentuate your features and add volume to your hair. In this blog post, we will walk you through step by step on how to cut face framing layers middle part.

Description

Face framing layers are a popular technique in hair cutting that can help highlight your best features. Adding layers to your hair can create depth and texture, and framing your face can help accentuate your cheekbones and jawline. Cutting face framing layers in a middle part is a great way to give your hair a new look without drastically changing its length.

How to Cut Face Framing Layers Middle Part

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it. It is important to start with clean hair so that you can see the natural fall of your hair.

Step 2: Part Your Hair

Part your hair down the middle using a comb. Make sure the part is straight and even.

Step 3: Section Off Your Hair

Clip the hair on one side of your part to keep it out of the way. Start with the other side and take a small section of hair near your face. This section should be about the width of your comb.

Step 4: Cut the First Layer

Hold the section of hair with your fingers and cut it at an angle. Start at the middle of your cheekbone and angle your scissors towards your chin. Cut the hair at a slight angle to create a soft, blended look.

Step 5: Repeat on the Other Side

Unclip the hair on the other side of your part and repeat steps 3 and 4. Make sure the length and angle of the layers on both sides are even.

Step 6: Check for Balance

After cutting both sides, check to make sure the layers are even and balanced. If you notice any unevenness, go back and trim the hair until it is even.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

After you have cut both sides, take a small section of hair from each side and bring them together. Cut the hair at a slight angle to blend the layers together.

Step 8: Finish with Styling

Style your hair as desired, using a round brush to create volume and smoothness.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting face framing layers in a middle part:

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure evenness
  • Cut the hair at an angle for a blended look
  • Double check for balance after cutting both sides
  • Use a round brush to create volume and smoothness when styling

FAQ

1. Can I do this myself or should I go to a salon?

You can do this yourself if you have experience cutting hair. If you are not confident in your skills, it is best to go to a salon.

2. How often should I cut my face framing layers?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain it. On average, you should cut your face framing layers every 6-8 weeks.

3. Will this style work on all hair types?

Yes, this style can work on all hair types. However, the length and angle of the layers may vary depending on your hair type and texture.

Hair Care

It is important to take care of your hair after cutting face framing layers. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools
  • Trim split ends regularly

Cutting face framing layers in a middle part can be a great way to update your hairstyle. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a fresh, new look that highlights your best features.
