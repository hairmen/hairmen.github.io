Why Does The Hair On My Head Stick Up

Written by Robby Sep 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do you ever wake up in the morning and find that your hair is sticking up in all directions? Or have you ever tried to style your hair and found that it just won't cooperate? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with hair that just won't lay flat. In this article, we'll explore some of the reasons why your hair might be sticking up and what you can do to fix it.

Genetics

One of the most common reasons why hair sticks up is genetics. If your parents have naturally curly or wavy hair, chances are you have inherited the same hair type. Curly hair tends to be drier and more prone to frizz, which can cause hair to stick up. Additionally, the shape of the hair follicle can also contribute to hair that sticks up. If the follicle is flat or oval-shaped, the hair is more likely to be straight. However, if the follicle is round, the hair is more likely to be curly or wavy.

Humidity

Humidity can also cause hair to stick up. When the air is humid, there is more moisture in the air, which can cause hair to become frizzy and unruly. This is because the moisture in the air is absorbed into the hair, causing it to swell and become misshapen. If you live in a humid climate, you may need to adjust your hair care routine to combat the effects of humidity.

Product Buildup

Product buildup can also contribute to hair that sticks up. If you use too much styling product or don't wash your hair often enough, product can build up on the hair shaft, causing it to become heavy and weighed down. This can make hair more prone to sticking up and looking unruly. To combat product buildup, make sure you are using the right amount of product for your hair type and wash your hair regularly.

Dry Hair

Dry hair is more prone to sticking up than hair that is properly moisturized. When hair is dry, it becomes brittle and prone to breakage, which can cause it to stick up and look frizzy. To combat dry hair, make sure you are using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated.

How to Fix Hair That Sticks Up

If you're tired of dealing with hair that sticks up, there are several things you can do to fix it. First, make sure you are using the right hair care products for your hair type. If you have curly hair, use products that are specifically designed for curly hair. If you have straight hair, use products that are designed for straight hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Fixing Hair That Sticks Up

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair.

Step 2: Apply a styling product that is designed for your hair type.

Step 3: Use a blow dryer to dry your hair, using a diffuser attachment if you have curly hair.

Step 4: Use a flat iron or curling iron to style your hair, depending on the look you are trying to achieve.

Step 5: Finish with a light hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips for Preventing Hair That Sticks Up

Here are some tips for preventing hair that sticks up:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and make your hair more prone to frizz and sticking up.
  • Avoid using too much product on your hair, as this can cause buildup and make your hair more difficult to style.
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush, as this can help distribute natural oils throughout your hair and prevent it from becoming too dry.

FAQs About Hair That Sticks Up

Can diet affect hair that sticks up?

Yes, a diet that is lacking in essential vitamins and minerals can contribute to dry, brittle hair that is prone to sticking up. Make sure you are eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits and vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein.

Can stress cause hair that sticks up?

Yes, stress can cause hair to become dry and brittle, which can make it more prone to sticking up. Additionally, stress can cause hormonal changes that can affect the health of your hair.

Is it normal for hair to stick up?

Yes, it is normal for hair to stick up from time to time, especially if you have curly or wavy hair. However, if your hair is sticking up consistently and you are unable to style it, it may be a sign of an underlying hair or scalp condition.

Hair Care for Hair That Sticks Up

If you have hair that sticks up, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking its best. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot tools on your hair too often. Additionally, make sure you are using the right hair care products for your hair type, and wash your hair regularly to prevent product buildup.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can keep it looking healthy, shiny, and smooth, without any of the unruly sticking up that can be frustrating to deal with.
