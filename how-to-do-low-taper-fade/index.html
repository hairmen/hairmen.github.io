How To Do Low Taper Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Oct 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a fresh and stylish haircut that's easy to maintain, then the low taper fade might be just what you need. This haircut has been popular for years, and it's not going away anytime soon. In this guide, we'll show you how to do a low taper fade, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • How to prepare for a low taper fade
  • Step-by-step guide for doing a low taper fade
  • Tips for getting the perfect low taper fade
  • Frequently asked questions about low taper fades
  • Hair care tips for maintaining a low taper fade

How to Prepare for a Low Taper Fade

Before you start cutting your hair, there are a few things you need to do to prepare. First, make sure you have all the tools you need, including clippers with different guard sizes, scissors, and a comb. You should also have a cape or towel to protect your clothes from hair clippings.

Next, decide on the length of your hair. A low taper fade typically involves leaving the hair on top of the head longer and gradually cutting the hair shorter towards the back and sides. You should also decide on the fade length, which refers to how short the hair will be at the bottom.

Finally, take a shower and wash your hair to make it easier to cut. You can also style your hair beforehand to make it easier to visualize the final result.

Step-by-Step Guide for Doing a Low Taper Fade

Follow these steps to do a low taper fade:

Step 1: Start with the longest guard

Use the longest guard on your clippers to cut the hair on top of your head. Start at the front and work your way towards the back. Make sure to cut the hair evenly and use the comb to keep the hair straight.

Step 2: Switch to a shorter guard

Once you've cut the top of your hair, switch to a shorter guard and begin cutting the sides and back of your head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the comb to keep the hair straight.

Step 3: Blend the hair

Use a blending guard to blend the hair on the sides and back into the hair on top of your head. This will create a smooth transition between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back.

Step 4: Create the fade

Use a shorter guard to create the fade at the bottom of your hair. Start at the bottom and work your way up, gradually reducing the length of the hair. This will create a faded effect that blends the hair into your skin.

Step 5: Finish the cut

Use scissors to trim any stray hairs and make sure the cut is even. You can also use a straight razor to clean up the hairline and create a crisp finish.

Tips for Getting the Perfect Low Taper Fade

Here are some tips to help you get the perfect low taper fade:

  • Take your time and be patient. A good haircut takes time and practice.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you cut your hair.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard for a gradual fade.
  • Experiment with different fade lengths to find the one that works best for you.
  • If you're not sure how to cut your hair, ask a professional for help.

Frequently Asked Questions About Low Taper Fades

Here are some frequently asked questions about low taper fades:

What is a low taper fade?

A low taper fade is a type of haircut that involves gradually cutting the hair shorter towards the back and sides, creating a faded effect.

Who can get a low taper fade?

Anyone can get a low taper fade, although it works best for people with shorter hair.

How often do I need to get a low taper fade?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain your haircut. Typically, you should get a low taper fade every 2-3 weeks to maintain the style.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining a Low Taper Fade

Here are some hair care tips for maintaining a low taper fade:

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it harder to style.
  • Use a hairbrush or comb to style your hair and keep it looking neat.
  • Visit a professional barber every few weeks to maintain your haircut.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you should be able to do a low taper fade with ease. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun experimenting with different styles.
