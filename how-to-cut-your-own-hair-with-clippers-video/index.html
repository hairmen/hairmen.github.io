How To Cut Your Own Hair With Clippers Video A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Aug 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read
If you're looking for a way to save money on haircuts, or you simply prefer to do it yourself, cutting your own hair with clippers can be a great solution. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut without leaving your home. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide to cutting your own hair with clippers, along with tips and FAQs to help you get the best results. We'll also include a helpful video tutorial to guide you through the process.

Description

Cutting your own hair with clippers can seem intimidating at first, but with a little practice, it can be a simple and cost-effective way to maintain your hairstyle. Clippers are a specialized type of hair-cutting tool that use a series of sharp blades to trim hair to a desired length. They're commonly used by barbers and hair stylists, and they come in a variety of sizes and styles to suit different hair types and cutting needs.

How to Cut Your Own Hair with Clippers Video: Step by Step Guide

To get started with cutting your own hair with clippers, you'll need a few basic tools, including a set of clippers, a comb, and scissors. You may also want to invest in a mirror or two to help you see the back of your head more easily. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started: 1. Start by selecting the right attachment comb for your desired length. Clippers typically come with a variety of combs that can be attached to the blades to achieve different lengths. Choose the comb that corresponds to the length you want to cut your hair. 2. Use the comb to lift a section of hair away from your head, and place the clippers at the base of the hair, near the scalp. Turn on the clippers and move them upward through the hair, using a smooth and steady motion. 3. Continue to work your way around your head, using the comb to lift sections of hair and the clippers to trim them to the desired length. Be sure to work slowly and carefully, checking your progress in the mirror as you go. 4. Use scissors to trim any longer sections of hair that the clippers may have missed, especially around the ears and at the back of the head. Hold the hair between your fingers and use the scissors to snip away any excess length. 5. Once you've finished cutting your hair, use a comb and a mirror to check for any uneven areas or missed spots. Touch up any areas that need it, and clean up any stray hairs with a brush or towel.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair with Clippers

Cutting your own hair with clippers can take some practice to get right, but here are a few tips to help you get the best results: - Start with a longer comb attachment and work your way down to shorter lengths, to avoid cutting your hair too short. - Use a mirror or two to help you see the back of your head more easily. - Work slowly and carefully, checking your progress as you go. - Use scissors to trim any longer sections of hair that the clippers may have missed. - Clean your clippers after each use to keep them in good condition.

FAQ

Q: How much money can I save by cutting my own hair with clippers?
A: Depending on how often you get your hair cut, you could save anywhere from $50 to $500 per year by cutting your own hair with clippers. Q: How often should I cut my hair with clippers?
A: The frequency of haircuts depends on how quickly your hair grows and how short you want it to be. On average, most people cut their hair every 4-6 weeks. Q: Can I use clippers to cut other people's hair?
A: Yes, clippers can be used to cut other people's hair, but it's important to use the right techniques and tools to avoid injury or uneven cuts.

Hair Care

Cutting your own hair with clippers can be a great way to save money, but it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips to help you maintain your hair: - Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. - Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and flat irons, as this can damage the hair. - Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to help nourish and moisturize your hair. - Trim split ends regularly to prevent them from spreading and causing damage to the rest of your hair. With these tips and techniques, cutting your own hair with clippers can be a simple and cost-effective way to maintain your hairstyle. Whether you're looking to save money or simply prefer to do it yourself, give it a try and see how easy it can be!
