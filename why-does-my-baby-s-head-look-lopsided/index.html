Why Does My Baby s Head Look Lopsided

Written by Daniel Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a new parent, it can be alarming to notice that your baby's head looks lopsided. This can be caused by a condition known as plagiocephaly or flat head syndrome, which occurs when a baby's head develops a flat spot due to prolonged pressure in one area. In this article, we will discuss the causes of plagiocephaly, how to prevent it, and what to do if you notice that your baby's head looks lopsided.

Description

Plagiocephaly is a condition where a baby's head develops a flat spot on one side. This can cause the head to look lopsided or misshapen. The flat spot can occur on the back of the head or on one side. It is caused by prolonged pressure on the baby's skull, which can occur when a baby spends too much time lying in the same position.

How to Prevent Plagiocephaly

The best way to prevent plagiocephaly is to ensure that your baby spends plenty of time off their back. This means holding your baby, using a baby carrier, or placing them in a reclined position on their tummy while they are awake and supervised. Additionally, you can alternate the position of your baby's head when they are sleeping, so that they are not always lying on the same spot.

Steps to Treat Lopsided Head

1. Repositioning Therapy

Repositioning therapy involves changing the position of your baby's head to relieve pressure on the flat spot. This can be done by placing a rolled towel or blanket under the baby's shoulder to encourage them to turn their head to the opposite side. You can also change the position of your baby's crib or playpen so that they are not always lying on the same side.

2. Helmet Therapy

If repositioning therapy is not effective, your doctor may recommend helmet therapy. This involves placing a custom-made helmet on your baby's head to encourage proper growth and shape of the skull. The helmet is worn for several months, and your doctor will monitor your baby's progress.

Tips for Preventing Plagiocephaly

Here are some additional tips to prevent plagiocephaly:

  • Give your baby plenty of tummy time when they are awake and supervised
  • Change your baby's position frequently when they are sleeping
  • Avoid leaving your baby in car seats, strollers, or swings for extended periods of time
  • Use a baby carrier instead of a car seat when possible
  • Make sure your baby's head is supported when you are holding them

FAQ about Lopsided Head

1. Is lopsided head a serious condition?

While lopsided head is not typically a serious condition, it can lead to developmental delays if left untreated. It is important to talk to your doctor if you notice that your baby's head looks lopsided.

2. How long does helmet therapy take?

Helmet therapy typically takes several months, and your doctor will monitor your baby's progress throughout the treatment.

3. Can I prevent lopsided head?

Yes, lopsided head can be prevented by ensuring that your baby spends plenty of time off their back and changing their position frequently when they are sleeping.

Hair Care for Lopsided Head

If your baby is undergoing helmet therapy, it is important to take care of their hair. Make sure to keep the scalp clean and dry, and use a soft brush to gently comb their hair. Avoid using any hair products or accessories that could interfere with the helmet.

In conclusion, lopsided head can be a concerning condition for new parents. However, with proper prevention and treatment, it can be resolved. Talk to your doctor if you notice that your baby's head looks lopsided, and take steps to prevent it from occurring in the first place.
