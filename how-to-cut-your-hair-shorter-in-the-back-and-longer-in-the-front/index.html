How To Cut Your Hair Shorter In The Back And Longer In The Front

Written by Oliver Nov 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your current hairstyle and looking for a change? One popular trend is to cut your hair shorter in the back and longer in the front. This style is also known as an angled or graduated bob. In this article, we will give you step-by-step instructions on how to achieve this look.

Description

The shorter in the back and longer in the front hairstyle is a versatile look that can be customized to suit different hair types and face shapes. It typically involves cutting the hair at an angle, with the back being shorter and the front being longer. This creates a flattering shape that frames the face and adds volume to the hair.

How to Cut Your Hair Shorter in the Back and Longer in the Front

Before you begin, gather the necessary tools: sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It’s also helpful to have a friend or family member assist you, especially if you’re new to cutting hair.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and style. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are appropriate for your hair type.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip the front sections out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Starting at the nape of your neck, use your scissors to cut the hair in a straight line. Gradually angle your scissors upwards as you move towards the crown of your head. You want the back to be shorter than the front, so be sure to cut less hair as you near the crown.

Step 4: Cut the Front

Unclip one of the front sections and comb it forward. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle, following the line of your jaw. Repeat on the other side.

Step 5: Blend the Angles

Use your scissors to blend the angles between the back and front sections. You want a smooth transition between the two lengths.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, you can style it using a straightener, curling iron, or other styling tools. You can also add layers or bangs to customize the look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect shorter in the back and longer in the front style:

  • Start with a longer length than you want, as you can always cut more off later.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as it will appear shorter when it dries.
  • Keep your head straight and level when cutting to avoid uneven angles.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your hair shorter in the back and longer in the front:

Can I cut my own hair?

While it’s possible to cut your own hair, it’s recommended that you have a friend or family member assist you, especially if you’re new to cutting hair.

What face shapes does this style suit?

The shorter in the back and longer in the front style can be customized to suit different face shapes, but it’s particularly flattering on those with oval or heart-shaped faces.

How often should I trim my hair?

It’s recommended that you trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Use a conditioner to keep it moisturized, and avoid using too much heat when styling. You can also use hair masks or oils to nourish your hair.

Now that you know how to cut your hair shorter in the back and longer in the front, you can try this trendy style for yourself. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don’t be afraid to experiment with different variations of the style.
