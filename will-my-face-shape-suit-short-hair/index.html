Description

Short hair is a versatile and stylish choice for many people, but it's important to consider whether it will suit your face shape before making the chop. Your face shape can affect how a particular hair style looks on you, and not all short haircuts will flatter every face shape. In this article, we'll help you determine whether short hair will suit your face shape, and provide tips and advice for choosing the best short hair style for you.

How to Determine If Short Hair Suits Your Face Shape

The first step in determining whether short hair will suit your face shape is to identify your face shape. There are six basic face shapes:

  • Oval
  • Round
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond
  • Long

Each face shape has its own unique characteristics and proportions, and certain hair styles will flatter them more than others. To determine your face shape, use a measuring tape to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline, and the length of your face from your hairline to your chin. Then, use these measurements to identify your face shape.

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, you're in luck - almost any short hair style will suit you. Oval faces have balanced proportions and a gently rounded hairline, which means you can experiment with a wide range of styles, from pixie cuts to bobs to shags.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, you'll want to choose a short hair style that elongates your face and creates the illusion of more angles. A long bob, a layered pixie cut, or a side-swept fringe can all work well for round faces.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, you'll want to soften your strong jawline and create some curves. A layered bob, a wavy pixie cut, or a side-swept fringe can all work well for square faces.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, you'll want to balance your wide forehead with a narrow chin. A layered bob, a side-swept pixie cut, or a textured fringe can all work well for heart faces.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond face shape, you'll want to balance your narrow forehead with your wider cheekbones and jawline. A textured pixie cut, a shaggy bob, or a side-swept fringe can all work well for diamond faces.

Long Face Shape

If you have a long face shape, you'll want to add width and volume to your hair to balance your proportions. A layered bob, a curly pixie cut, or a textured fringe can all work well for long faces.

Step by Step Guide

Once you've determined your face shape and identified some short hair styles that will flatter you, it's time to take the plunge and go for the chop. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get the perfect short hair style:

  1. Choose a reputable hair stylist who has experience cutting short hair.
  2. Bring pictures of short hair styles that you like to your consultation, and discuss your face shape and hair texture with your stylist.
  3. Consider your lifestyle and how much time you're willing to spend styling your hair each day. Short hair can be low-maintenance, but some styles require more upkeep than others.
  4. Get a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your short hair looking fresh and healthy.
  5. Experiment with different styling products and techniques to find the look that works best for you.

Tips for Short Hair Styles

Here are some additional tips to help you rock your new short hair style:

  • Invest in high-quality styling tools, like a good blow dryer and flat iron, to achieve the best results.
  • Consider adding highlights or lowlights to your short hair to add dimension and texture.
  • If you have fine hair, choose a short hair style that adds volume and texture.
  • If you have thick hair, choose a short hair style that thins out the bulk and creates movement.
  • Accessorize your short hair with headbands, barrettes, and other hair accessories to add some flair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about short hair and face shapes:

Q: Will short hair make my face look rounder?

A: It depends on your face shape and the short hair style you choose. Some short hair styles can create the illusion of roundness, while others can elongate your face and create more angles.

Q: Can I still wear my hair up if it's short?

A: Yes! Even if your hair is too short for a ponytail, you can still wear it up with bobby pins, clips, or a headband.

Q: Will short hair make me look older?

A: Not necessarily. Short hair can be youthful and stylish, and can actually make you look more modern and sophisticated.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Short hair requires different care and maintenance than longer hair. Here are some tips to keep your short hair looking its best:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from becoming too oily.
  • Use a lightweight conditioner to keep your hair hydrated without weighing it down.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can make your hair look greasy or flat.
  • Consider using a dry shampoo between washes to refresh your hair and add volume.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before blow drying or using hot tools.

By following these tips and choosing a short hair style that flatters your face shape, you'll be sure to turn heads with your new look.
