How To Cut Your Own Hair Flip Over A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 20, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to save some money on haircuts, or just want to try something new, cutting your own hair flip over can be a great option. It may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a stylish and professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair flip over, as well as some tips and hair care advice to ensure a successful outcome.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Flip Over
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

How to Cut Your Own Hair Flip Over

Cutting your own hair flip over involves using gravity and a pair of scissors to create layers or a blunt cut. This technique is popular because it helps to create a natural-looking haircut without requiring any fancy tools or techniques. Here are the basic steps:

What You'll Need

  • A pair of scissors
  • A hair tie or clip
  • A mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Begin by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural shape and texture of your hair.
  2. Flip your head over so that your hair is hanging upside down.
  3. Using a hair tie or clip, gather your hair at the top of your head. Make sure it is secure and tight so that it doesn't fall out while you're cutting.
  4. Using a pair of scissors, start cutting the ends of your hair. Start with small snips and work your way up to larger sections. Remember that you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  5. Focus on the ends of your hair, rather than the middle or top. This will help create layers and texture.
  6. Once you are finished cutting, remove the hair tie or clip and shake out your hair.
  7. Use a mirror to check for any uneven spots or sections that need further trimming.
  8. Repeat the process as needed until you achieve your desired hairstyle.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Flip Over

Cutting your own hair can be a bit nerve-wracking, but with these tips, you can achieve a great result:

  • Start small and work your way up to larger sections. It's better to take your time and cut slowly than to rush and end up with a botched haircut.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair-cutting scissors. These will help create clean lines and prevent split ends.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and may require some rest breaks.
  • Remember that hair grows back. If you make a mistake, it's not the end of the world. You can always get a professional haircut if needed.

FAQs

Here are some common questions and answers about cutting your own hair flip over:

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, cutting your own curly hair flip over can be done. However, it may require more skill and patience to achieve a great result.

How often should I cut my own hair flip over?

This will depend on your hair type and desired style. Generally, cutting your hair every 6-8 weeks will help maintain healthy ends and prevent split ends.

Do I need to use any hair products after cutting my own hair?

Yes, it's important to use a conditioner or styling product to help protect and nourish your hair after cutting it.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it properly to ensure it stays healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to help nourish and repair your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain a healthy length.

By following these tips and techniques, cutting your own hair flip over can be a great way to save money and achieve a unique and stylish haircut. Remember to take your time, invest in good tools, and practice regularly to achieve the best results.
