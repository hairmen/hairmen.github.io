Types Of Bangs For Your Face Shape A Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to switch up your hairstyle with some bangs? Bangs can be a fun and easy way to switch up your look, but it's important to choose the right type of bangs for your face shape. In this guide, we'll go over the different types of bangs and which face shapes they work best with.

Table of Contents:

  • Bangs for Round Faces
  • Bangs for Oval Faces
  • Bangs for Square Faces
  • Bangs for Heart-Shaped Faces
  • Bangs for Diamond-Shaped Faces
  • Bangs for Long Faces
  • Hair Care Tips for Bangs
  • FAQs

Bangs for Round Faces

If you have a round face shape, you'll want to look for bangs that help elongate your face. Side-swept bangs that hit just below the brow bone can help create the illusion of a longer face. Avoid blunt bangs or heavy bangs that hit at the cheekbones, as these can make your face look rounder.

Bangs for Oval Faces

Lucky for those with oval faces, almost any type of bangs can work! Consider trying out blunt bangs for a bold look, or side-swept bangs for a more subtle change.

Bangs for Square Faces

If you have a square face shape, you'll want to look for bangs that soften your strong jawline. Side-swept bangs that hit just below the brow bone can help balance out your face shape. Avoid blunt bangs or heavy bangs that hit at the chin, as these can make your face look more square.

Bangs for Heart-Shaped Faces

If you have a heart-shaped face, you'll want to look for bangs that help balance out your forehead and chin. Side-swept bangs that hit just above the brow bone can help create the illusion of a more balanced face shape. Avoid heavy, blunt bangs or short bangs that hit at the forehead, as these can make your forehead look larger.

Bangs for Diamond-Shaped Faces

If you have a diamond-shaped face, you'll want to look for bangs that help balance out your cheekbones and chin. Side-swept bangs that hit just above the brow bone can help soften your cheekbones. Avoid heavy, blunt bangs or short bangs that hit at the cheekbones, as these can make your face look wider.

Bangs for Long Faces

If you have a long face shape, you'll want to look for bangs that help shorten your face. Blunt bangs that hit at the eyebrows can help create the illusion of a shorter face. Avoid side-swept bangs that hit below the brow bone, as these can make your face look longer.

Hair Care Tips for Bangs

If you're new to bangs, it's important to know that they require a bit of maintenance. Here are some tips for caring for your bangs:

  • Trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.
  • Blow dry your bangs with a round brush to give them volume and shape.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much throughout the day, as this can make them look greasy.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about bangs:

Q: Will bangs make my face look shorter or longer?

A: It depends on the type of bangs and your face shape. For example, blunt bangs can make a long face look shorter, while side-swept bangs can make a round face look longer.

Q: Can I still wear a ponytail with bangs?

A: Yes! You can still wear a ponytail with bangs. Consider pulling your bangs back with a bobby pin or hair clip for a chic and easy look.

Q: Can I cut my own bangs?

A: We recommend going to a professional hairstylist to get your bangs cut. However, if you're feeling confident and want to give it a try yourself, make sure to use sharp scissors and start with small snips.

Now that you know which type of bangs work best for your face shape, go ahead and give them a try! With a little maintenance and care, bangs can be a fun and easy way to switch up your look.
