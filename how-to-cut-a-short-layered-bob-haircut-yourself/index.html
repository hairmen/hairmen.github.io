How To Cut A Short Layered Bob Haircut Yourself

Written by Daniel Oct 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon every few weeks to get a haircut? Do you want to learn how to cut a short layered bob haircut yourself? Well, you're in luck! In this article, we'll teach you step by step how to cut a short layered bob haircut yourself. By the end of this article, you'll have the skills and confidence to give yourself a fresh new look.

Description

A short layered bob haircut is a trendy and stylish haircut that is perfect for those who want to have a low-maintenance hairstyle. It's a haircut that is versatile, easy to style, and can be worn by women of all ages. The layers in the haircut give volume and texture to the hair, making it look fuller and thicker.

How to

Step 1: Prepare your hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to make sure that your hair is clean and dry. Comb your hair and make sure there are no tangles or knots. Use a spray bottle to mist your hair with water if needed.

Step 2: Section your hair

Section your hair into four parts: the top, left, right, and back. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the back section

Start by cutting the back section of your hair. Use a comb to pick up a small section of hair from the back of your head. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut the hair straight across. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you've cut all of the hair in the back section.

Step 4: Cut the side sections

Next, move on to the side sections of your hair. Use your comb to pick up a small section of hair from the side of your head. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle. Start at the front of your head and work your way to the back.

Step 5: Cut the top section

Finally, move on to the top section of your hair. Use your comb to pick up a small section of hair from the top of your head. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle. Start at the front of your head and work your way to the back.

Step 6: Blend the layers

After you've cut all of the sections of your hair, it's time to blend the layers together. Use your scissors to cut into the ends of your hair, creating a textured look. This will help the layers blend together seamlessly.

Tips

- Always use sharp scissors to cut your hair.

- Cut your hair in a well-lit area.

- Take your time and don't rush the process.

FAQ

Is it easy to cut a short layered bob haircut yourself?

Yes, it is easy to cut a short layered bob haircut yourself if you follow the steps above and take your time.

Can I use any type of scissors to cut my hair?

No, you should always use sharp scissors that are meant for cutting hair. Using regular scissors can damage your hair and make it look uneven.

How often should I cut my hair?

You should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and length of your haircut.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage to your hair. Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. And finally, don't forget to trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and length of your haircut.
