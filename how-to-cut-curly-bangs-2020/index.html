How To Cut Curly Bangs 2020 A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Oct 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly bangs can be both fun and challenging to style. But before you get started, it's important to know the basics of cutting curly hair. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut curly bangs in 2020 with ease. We'll cover everything from the tools you need to the best techniques to use. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • How to Cut Curly Bangs: Step-by-Step
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Bangs

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the right tools on hand. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends. A wide-tooth comb is essential for detangling and separating your curls. Hair clips will help you section off your hair, and a spray bottle filled with water will help keep your hair damp as you cut it.

How to Cut Curly Bangs: Step-by-Step

Now that you have your tools, it's time to get started. Here's how to cut curly bangs:

Step 1: Wash and Detangle Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and detangled. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and then use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your curls.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Use hair clips to section off the hair that you don't want to cut. Make sure your bangs are the only section of hair that you are working on.

Step 3: Determine the Length of Your Bangs

Decide how long you want your bangs to be. You can use a ruler or measuring tape to get an exact measurement, or you can simply estimate.

Step 4: Cut Your Bangs

Starting in the middle of your forehead, use your scissors to cut your bangs to the desired length. Be sure to cut slowly and carefully, and remember that it's always easier to cut more hair off than it is to add it back on.

Step 5: Check Your Work

After you've finished cutting, check your work in the mirror. Make sure your bangs are even and that they are the length you want.

Tips for Cutting Curly Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind as you cut your curly bangs:

  • Cut your bangs when they are dry, as wet hair can shrink as it dries.
  • Start by cutting your bangs longer than you think you want them. You can always go back and trim them later.
  • Don't cut your bangs straight across. Instead, use a point-cutting technique to create a softer, more natural look.
  • If you're nervous about cutting your own bangs, consider going to a professional stylist instead.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my curly bangs?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs lose their shape. As a general rule of thumb, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking their best.

Q: Can I straighten my curly bangs?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly bangs if you want to. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.

Q: How can I style my curly bangs?

A: There are many ways to style curly bangs, including wearing them straight across, sweeping them to the side, or pinning them back. Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you.

Hair Care for Curly Bangs

Now that you know how to cut your curly bangs, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your bangs as much as possible.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your bangs to help define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Consider getting a regular trim to keep your bangs looking their best.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to cut your curly bangs with confidence. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and be patient with your curls. Happy cutting!
