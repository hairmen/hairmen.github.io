How To Get Your Hair Back To Its Natural Curl Pattern A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 09, 2023 · 5 min read

If you’re tired of straightening your hair or using chemicals to achieve a certain look, you might be wondering how to get your hair back to its natural curl pattern. With some patience, care, and a few tips and tricks, you can restore your hair’s natural texture and enjoy its full potential. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of getting your curls back, from understanding your hair type to choosing the right products and techniques.

Description: Understanding Your Hair Type

The first step in getting your hair back to its natural curl pattern is to understand your hair type. This will help you choose the right products and techniques for your hair, and avoid damaging it further. There are several hair types, ranging from straight to coily, and each one requires specific care and attention.

If you’re not sure about your hair type, try the “hair typing system” developed by Andre Walker, Oprah Winfrey’s hairstylist. The system categorizes hair into four types: straight (type 1), wavy (type 2), curly (type 3), and coily (type 4). Each type has subcategories based on the size and shape of the curls. Once you know your hair type, you can start exploring ways to enhance your natural curls.

How to: Restoring Your Hair’s Natural Curl Pattern

Now that you know your hair type, it’s time to start restoring your hair’s natural curl pattern. Here are some steps you can follow:

Step 1: Stop Using Chemicals

If you’re using relaxers, perms, or other chemical treatments to straighten or alter your hair’s texture, it’s time to stop. These treatments can damage your hair and prevent it from curling naturally. You might experience some “transitioning” symptoms, such as dryness, breakage, and frizz, but it’s a necessary step towards healthier hair.

Step 2: Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning your hair is essential to restore its natural moisture and elasticity. Use a hydrating hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish your hair from the inside out. You can also try a “hot oil treatment” by heating up some coconut or olive oil and applying it to your hair before shampooing.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair Gently

Detangling your hair can be tricky, especially if you have curly or coily hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to remove knots and tangles, and do it gently to avoid pulling or breaking your hair. You can also use your fingers to detangle your hair, especially when it’s wet and slippery.

Step 4: Use Curl-Enhancing Products

To enhance your natural curls, you can use a variety of products such as curl creams, gels, mousses, and sprays. Look for products that are designed for your hair type and contain natural ingredients such as shea butter, coconut oil, or aloe vera. Apply the product evenly to your hair, from roots to tips, and scrunch your hair gently to encourage curl formation.

Tips and Tricks: Maintaining Your Natural Curl Pattern

Once you’ve restored your hair’s natural curl pattern, you’ll want to maintain it and prevent damage or frizz. Here are some tips and tricks:

Tip 1: Protect Your Hair While Sleeping

Sleeping with your hair out can cause friction, tangling, and breakage. You can protect your hair by wearing a silk or satin bonnet, scarf, or pillowcase. These materials are gentler on your hair and reduce frizz and static.

Tip 2: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers can damage your hair and affect its natural curl pattern. Try to avoid using them or use them sparingly, and always apply a heat protectant spray or cream before styling.

Tip 3: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can prevent split ends and breakage, and help your hair grow healthier and stronger. Aim to trim your hair every 2-3 months, or whenever you notice dryness or damage.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does it take to get my natural curl pattern back?

A: It depends on your hair type, the extent of damage, and your hair care routine. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months to notice a difference, but with consistent care, you should start seeing improvements.

Q: Can I use heat to enhance my natural curls?

A: You can use heat to enhance your natural curls, but be careful not to damage your hair. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer, or a curling wand with a low heat setting, and always apply a heat protectant beforehand.

Q: Can I still dye or color my hair?

A: Yes, you can dye or color your hair, but choose products that are gentle and don’t contain harsh chemicals such as ammonia or bleach. Opt for semi-permanent or demi-permanent dyes, and always follow the instructions carefully.

Hair Care: Love Your Natural Curls

Getting your hair back to its natural curl pattern requires patience, care, and a positive attitude. Embrace your natural hair and experiment with different styles and products. Remember that every hair type is unique and beautiful, and that your hair deserves the best care possible. With these tips and tricks, you’ll be on your way to healthier, bouncier, and more radiant curls.
