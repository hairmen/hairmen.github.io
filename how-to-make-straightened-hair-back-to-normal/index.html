How To Make Straightened Hair Back To Normal

Written by Daniel Nov 09, 2023 · 3 min read

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Straightened hair is a popular hairstyle among women, but it can be damaging to your hair if not done properly. If you've been straightening your hair for a while and want to go back to your natural hair texture, you're in the right place. In this article, we'll show you how to make straightened hair back to normal.

How to

The process of making straightened hair back to normal involves two steps: washing and conditioning.

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step is to wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This type of shampoo is designed to remove buildup from your hair, including any leftover straightening products. Make sure to rinse your hair thoroughly.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

After washing your hair, apply a deep conditioner to your hair. This will help to restore moisture to your hair and prevent any damage that may have been caused by the straightening process. Leave the conditioner on for at least 15 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step by step

Here are the step-by-step instructions for making straightened hair back to normal:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo.
  2. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair.
  3. Leave the conditioner on for at least 15 minutes.
  4. Rinse the conditioner out.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when making straightened hair back to normal:

  • Be patient. It may take a few washes and conditioning treatments to fully remove the straightening products from your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair for a while. This will help to prevent any further damage to your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush. This will help to prevent breakage.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making straightened hair back to normal:

Q: How long does it take to make straightened hair back to normal?

A: It depends on how often you've been straightening your hair and how damaged your hair is. It may take a few weeks or even months to fully restore your hair to its natural texture.

Q: Can I use a regular conditioner instead of a deep conditioner?

A: A deep conditioner is recommended because it is designed to penetrate your hair more deeply than a regular conditioner. However, if you don't have a deep conditioner, a regular conditioner will still help to moisturize your hair.

Hair Care

Here are some tips for caring for your hair after making it back to normal:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair as much as possible.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of any split ends.

By following these tips, your hair will be back to its natural texture in no time.
