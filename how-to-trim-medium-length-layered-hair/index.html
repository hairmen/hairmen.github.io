How To Trim Medium Length Layered Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have medium length layered hair, you know how important it is to keep it looking neat and tidy. Trimming your hair regularly is one of the best ways to maintain its shape and prevent split ends. In this article, we will guide you through the process of trimming your medium length layered hair at home.

How to Trim Medium Length Layered Hair: Step by Step

Before we get started, make sure you have a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a mirror. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. This will make it easier to see the natural shape of your hair and to identify any split ends.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Use a comb to detangle your hair and to create a smooth surface for cutting. Make sure your hair is completely free of knots and tangles.

Step 3: Identify the Length You Want to Cut

Decide how much hair you want to cut off. If you are only trimming split ends, focus on the tips of your hair. If you want to create a new style, decide on the length you want to cut.

Step 4: Cut One Section at a Time

Start by cutting one small section of hair at a time. Hold the section between your fingers and cut off the desired length. Make sure you cut straight across and avoid cutting at an angle.

Step 5: Check Your Progress in the Mirror

After cutting each section, check your progress in the mirror. Make sure the length is even and that the layers are blending well.

Step 6: Repeat on the Other Side

Once you are satisfied with one side, repeat the process on the other side of your head.

Tips for Trimming Medium Length Layered Hair

Trimming your hair at home can be a bit intimidating, but with these tips, you can achieve great results:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Using regular scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Start by cutting less than you think you need. You can always go back and cut more if needed.
  • Use a comb to create clean sections of hair. This will help you keep your cuts even.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair is longer and can create uneven cuts.
  • Take your time and be patient. Rushing can lead to mistakes and uneven cuts.

FAQs

How often should I trim my medium length layered hair?

You should trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Can I trim my layered hair myself?

Yes, you can trim your hair at home with the right tools and techniques. However, if you are unsure about the process, it's best to visit a professional stylist.

Is it okay to use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, it's not advised to use regular scissors to cut your hair. Hair cutting scissors are specially designed to create clean cuts and prevent damage to your hair.

Caring for Your Medium Length Layered Hair

Trimming your hair regularly is just one part of maintaining healthy hair. Here are some tips for keeping your medium length layered hair looking its best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often. This can cause damage to your hair and create split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair protectant spray.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily trim your medium length layered hair at home and keep it looking its best between salon visits. Remember to take your time, be patient, and invest in quality hair cutting tools for the best results.
