How To Cut Taper Fade Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a new haircut to try? Look no further than the taper fade. This popular style is perfect for any hair type and can be customized to fit your personal style. In this blog post, we'll walk you through how to cut a taper fade haircut step by step. Let's get started!

What is a Taper Fade Haircut?

A taper fade haircut is a type of haircut where the hair gradually fades from long to short as it goes down the head. It's a popular style for men of all ages and hair types, and can be customized to fit your personal style. There are many different types of taper fades, including low, medium, and high fades, as well as skin fades.

How to Cut a Taper Fade Haircut: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Determine the Length and Style

Before you begin cutting, determine the length and style of the taper fade you want. Consult with your client to ensure that you both have a clear understanding of the style and length desired. You may want to use clippers to remove excess hair before beginning the taper fade.

Step 2: Start with the Top

Begin by cutting the hair on the top of the head to the desired length. Use scissors or clippers to achieve the desired length. If using clippers, start with a larger guard and work your way down to a smaller guard as you move towards the sides and back.

Step 3: Create a Guideline

Using your clippers, create a guideline around the head where the fade will begin. This will help you create a consistent taper throughout the haircut.

Step 4: Blend the Sides and Back

Using your clippers, blend the hair on the sides and back of the head. Start with a larger guard and work your way down to a smaller guard as you move towards the neckline. Use a comb to help blend the hair and ensure a smooth transition from the top to the sides and back.

Step 5: Create a Fade

Using your clippers, create a fade by gradually decreasing the length of the hair from the guideline to the neckline. Use a comb to help blend the hair and ensure a smooth transition from the longer hair on top to the shorter hair on the sides and back.

Step 6: Detail the Hairline

Use your clippers to detail the hairline around the ears and neckline. Use a comb to ensure a straight and clean line.

Step 7: Clean Up the Hair

Use scissors to trim any excess hair and clean up the haircut. Use a comb to check for any uneven areas and make adjustments as necessary.

Step 8: Style the Hair

Style the hair as desired using hair products such as pomade or gel. You can also use a blow dryer to add volume and texture to the hair.

Tips for Cutting a Taper Fade Haircut

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to ensure a gradual fade.
  • Use a comb to help blend the hair and ensure a smooth transition from the top to the sides and back.
  • Take your time and make small adjustments as necessary.
  • Consult with your client to ensure that you both have a clear understanding of the style and length desired.

FAQ about Taper Fade Haircuts

Q: How often should I get a taper fade haircut?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain the style. Generally, you should get a taper fade haircut every 2-4 weeks to maintain the shape and length.

Q: Can I cut a taper fade haircut at home?

A: Yes, you can cut a taper fade haircut at home if you have the proper tools and experience. However, it's always best to consult with a professional stylist to ensure that you achieve the desired look.

Hair Care Tips for Taper Fade Haircuts

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Use a hair product such as pomade or gel to style your hair and keep it in place.

Now that you know how to cut a taper fade haircut, it's time to give it a try! Whether you're a professional stylist or cutting your own hair at home, this versatile style is sure to turn heads. Remember to take your time, consult with your client, and have fun!
