Fringes To Suit Round Faces A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have a round face, you may feel like fringes are not for you, but that's not true! Fringes can actually complement and enhance your features if you choose the right style. In this guide, we will show you how to choose and style fringes that suit round faces.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose Fringes to Suit Round Faces
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Fringes for Round Faces
  • Tips for Maintaining Fringes for Round Faces
  • FAQs about Fringes for Round Faces
  • Hair Care Tips for Fringes and Round Faces

How to Choose Fringes to Suit Round Faces

When choosing fringes for round faces, the goal is to create angles and elongate the face. The following are some tips to consider:

1. Avoid blunt fringes

Blunt fringes can make your face appear even rounder. Instead, opt for layered or side-swept fringes that create angles and add texture to your hair.

2. Go for asymmetrical fringes

Asymmetrical fringes that are longer on one side can create the illusion of length and balance out a round face.

3. Consider face-framing layers

Face-framing layers that start from the chin and go down can also create angles and elongate the face.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Fringes for Round Faces

Now that you've chosen the right fringes, it's time to style them. Here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Wash and dry your hair before styling your fringes. This will help you see the natural texture and shape of your hair.

2. Use a round brush

Use a round brush to blow-dry your fringes. This will create volume and lift at the roots.

3. Style your fringes

If you have side-swept fringes, use a flat iron to create a subtle curve away from your face. If you have asymmetrical fringes, use a flat iron to smooth out any kinks or bumps.

4. Finish with hairspray

Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your fringes in place all day.

Tips for Maintaining Fringes for Round Faces

Here are some tips to keep your fringes looking fresh and stylish:

1. Trim your fringes every 4-6 weeks

To prevent your fringes from becoming too long and heavy, trim them every 4-6 weeks. This will also help maintain their shape.

2. Use dry shampoo

Dry shampoo can help absorb excess oil and keep your fringes looking fresh between washes.

3. Experiment with different styles

Don't be afraid to switch up your fringes and try different styles. You may discover a new look that you love.

FAQs about Fringes for Round Faces

1. Can fringes make a round face look even rounder?

Yes, blunt fringes can make a round face look even rounder. It's best to opt for layered or side-swept fringes that create angles and add texture to your hair.

2. What length should my fringes be?

The length of your fringes depends on your personal preference and face shape. As a general rule, side-swept fringes should be at least chin-length, while asymmetrical fringes can be longer on one side.

3. Can I style my fringes with a flat iron?

Yes, you can style your fringes with a flat iron. Use a low heat setting and be gentle to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Fringes and Round Faces

Here are some general hair care tips to keep in mind when styling fringes for round faces:

1. Use a heat protectant

Before using any heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

2. Don't over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of natural oils and make it dry and brittle. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

3. Use a deep conditioning treatment

Once a week, use a deep conditioning treatment to nourish and hydrate your hair. This will help prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you can confidently choose and style fringes that suit your round face. Experiment with different styles and have fun with your hair!
