How To Find Out What Colours Suit Me

Written by Aprilia Aug 02, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of constantly buying clothes that don't look quite right on you? Do you struggle to find colours that complement your skin tone? If so, you're not alone. Finding the right colours that suit you can be a real challenge, but it's worth the effort to ensure you always look your best. In this article, we'll give you some tips on how to find out what colours suit you, so you can shop with confidence.

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in finding the right colours for you is to determine your skin tone. There are two main categories: warm and cool. Warm skin tones have a yellow or golden undertone, while cool skin tones have a pink or blue undertone. To determine your skin tone, try the following:

  • Look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear green, you have warm undertones. If they appear blue, you have cool undertones.
  • Try holding up a piece of white paper to your face. If your skin appears yellowish, you have warm undertones. If it appears pinkish, you have cool undertones.
  • Consider the colours that typically flatter you. If you look better in warmer colours like reds, oranges, and yellows, you likely have warm undertones. If you look better in cooler colours like blues, purples, and greens, you likely have cool undertones.

Once you've determined your skin tone, you can move on to identifying your undertones.

Step 2: Identify Your Undertones

Now that you know your skin tone, it's time to identify your undertones. Undertones refer to the subtle colours that are present beneath the surface of your skin. They can be warm or cool, just like your skin tone. Here's how to identify your undertones:

  • If you have warm undertones, your skin may have a golden or peachy hue.
  • If you have cool undertones, your skin may have a pink or rosy hue.
  • Try holding up different colours of fabric to your face to see which ones make your skin look brighter and more vibrant. Warm undertones tend to look best in colours like red, orange, and yellow, while cool undertones tend to look best in colours like blue, purple, and green.

Once you've identified your undertones, you can start choosing colours that complement them.

Step 3: Choose Colours That Complement Your Skin Tone

Now that you know your skin tone and undertones, you can start choosing colours that will complement them. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Warm skin tones tend to look best in colours like red, orange, yellow, brown, and green.
  • Cool skin tones tend to look best in colours like blue, purple, pink, and gray.
  • Neutral skin tones can usually wear a wide range of colours.

Of course, these are just guidelines, and there are always exceptions. Experiment with different colours to see what looks best on you.

Step 4: Experiment with Different Colours

Now that you have a general idea of which colours will complement your skin tone and undertones, it's time to start experimenting. Try on different colours and see how they look on you. Don't be afraid to step out of your comfort zone and try something new. You might be surprised by what looks good on you!

Tips for Finding Your Best Colours

Here are some additional tips to help you find your best colours:

  • Consider your hair and eye colour. These can also play a role in which colours look best on you.
  • Don't forget about patterns. Some patterns can be overwhelming on certain skin tones, while others can be very flattering.
  • Try wearing different shades of the same colour. For example, if you know you look good in blue, experiment with different shades of blue to find your best match.
  • Think about the occasion. Some colours are more appropriate for certain events than others.

FAQs

Q: Can I still wear colours that don't complement my skin tone?

A: Of course! These guidelines are just suggestions. If you love a certain colour, wear it with confidence.

Q: What if I have a hard time determining my skin tone?

A: If you're having trouble determining your skin tone, consider visiting a makeup counter or consulting with a stylist.

Q: Do the same rules apply for makeup colours?

A: Yes, the same general guidelines apply for makeup colours. Consider your skin tone and undertones when choosing makeup shades.

Hair Care Tips for Complementing Your New Colours

Once you've found your best colours, you might want to consider updating your hair colour to complement them. Here are some tips:

  • Consider getting highlights or lowlights in shades that complement your skin tone.
  • Choose a hair colour that is within two shades of your natural colour to ensure a natural look.
  • Keep your hair healthy and shiny with regular conditioning treatments.
  • Consider consulting with a stylist to determine which hair colours will complement your skin tone and undertones.

By following these tips, you'll be well on your way to finding your best colours and always looking your best.
