How To Fix A Thinning Wig Tips And Tricks For Hair Care

Written by Robby Nov 02, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re experiencing thinning hair or hair loss, a wig can be a great option to help you feel confident and beautiful. However, over time, even the best wigs can start to thin out and lose their fullness. If you’re dealing with a thinning wig, don’t worry – there are plenty of ways to fix it and restore it to its former glory. In this article, we’ll go over some of the best tips and tricks for fixing a thinning wig, as well as some important hair care tips to keep your wig looking great for longer.

How to Fix a Thinning Wig: Step by Step Guide

If you’re dealing with a thinning wig, here are some steps you can take to fix it:

Step 1: Evaluate the Wig

The first step in fixing a thinning wig is to evaluate the wig itself. Take a close look at the wig and assess where the thinning is happening. Is it in one specific area, or is it spread out across the wig? This will help you determine the best course of action.

Step 2: Use a Wig Revitalizer Spray

If the thinning is minor and the wig still has good overall fullness, you may be able to use a wig revitalizer spray to add some volume and restore the wig’s fullness. These sprays can be found at most beauty supply stores and can be sprayed directly onto the wig to add fullness and shine.

Step 3: Add Hair Extensions or a Hairpiece

If the thinning is more severe, you may need to add hair extensions or a hairpiece to the wig to restore its fullness. Hair extensions can be clipped onto the wig to add volume, while a hairpiece can be sewn onto the wig to add fullness and length.

Step 4: Get a Professional Wig Styling

If you’re not comfortable adding extensions or a hairpiece to the wig yourself, or if the thinning is severe, you may need to take the wig to a professional wig stylist. A professional stylist can help you evaluate the wig and determine the best course of action to restore its fullness, whether that’s adding extensions, a hairpiece, or even restyling the wig entirely.

Tips for Hair Care to Keep Your Wig Looking Great

Even if your wig is currently in good shape, it’s important to take good care of it to keep it looking great for longer. Here are some tips for hair care:

Tip 1: Wash Your Wig Regularly

Just like your natural hair, your wig can benefit from regular washing. Be sure to use a wig-specific shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot water or excessive rubbing when washing to prevent damaging the wig.

Tip 2: Store Your Wig Carefully

When you’re not wearing your wig, be sure to store it carefully to prevent tangling or damage. Use a wig stand or mannequin head to help the wig maintain its shape, and avoid storing it in a place where it could be exposed to excessive heat or moisture.

Tip 3: Brush Your Wig Regularly

Regularly brushing your wig can help prevent tangling and keep it looking great. Be sure to use a wig-specific brush or comb, and avoid using excessive force or pulling when brushing to prevent damaging the wig.

FAQs

Q: Can I dye my wig to match my natural hair color?

A: Yes, you can dye your wig to match your natural hair color. However, it’s important to use a wig-specific hair dye and to follow the instructions carefully to avoid damaging the wig.

Q: How long will my wig last?

A: The lifespan of a wig depends on a variety of factors, including the quality of the wig, how well it’s cared for, and how often it’s worn. With proper care, a high-quality wig can last for several years.

Q: Can I wear my wig in the shower or while swimming?

A: It’s generally not recommended to wear your wig in the shower or while swimming, as exposure to water and moisture can damage the wig.

Final Thoughts

If you’re dealing with a thinning wig, don’t worry – there are plenty of ways to fix it and restore it to its former glory. Whether you opt for a wig revitalizer spray, hair extensions, or a professional wig styling, with a little bit of effort, you can get your wig looking great again. And by following the tips for hair care, you can keep it looking great for longer.
