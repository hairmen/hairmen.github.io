How To Fix Falling Out Bleached Hair

Written by Frank Sep 19, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Bleaching your hair can be an exciting and glamorous experience, but it can also cause your hair to become damaged and fall out. If you've noticed that your bleached hair is falling out, don't worry! There are steps you can take to prevent further damage and restore your hair's health. In this article, we'll discuss how to fix falling out bleached hair.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Fix Falling Out Bleached Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Restoring Bleached Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Routine for Bleached Hair

How to Fix Falling Out Bleached Hair

If your bleached hair is falling out, the first step you should take is to stop bleaching your hair. This will prevent further damage and give your hair the chance to recover. You should also avoid using heat styling tools and harsh chemicals on your hair.

Next, it's important to nourish your hair with the right products. Look for hair care products that are designed for damaged hair, such as shampoos and conditioners that contain protein and moisturizing ingredients. You can also use hair masks and leave-in conditioners to provide extra nourishment.

If your hair is severely damaged, you may want to consider getting a haircut to remove the damaged ends. This will help to prevent further breakage and split ends.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to fix falling out bleached hair:

  1. Stop bleaching your hair immediately.
  2. Avoid using heat styling tools and harsh chemicals.
  3. Switch to hair care products that are designed for damaged hair.
  4. Use hair masks and leave-in conditioners to provide extra nourishment.
  5. Consider getting a haircut to remove the damaged ends.
  6. Be patient! It may take some time for your hair to recover.

Tips for Restoring Bleached Hair

Here are some additional tips for restoring bleached hair:

  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated from the inside out.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead.
  • Avoid using hair ties that can cause damage and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing falling out bleached hair:

Q: How long does it take for bleached hair to recover?

A: It can take several months for bleached hair to fully recover. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

Q: Can I dye my hair after it's been bleached and fallen out?

A: It's best to wait until your hair has fully recovered before dyeing it again. Dyeing your hair can cause further damage and breakage.

Hair Care Routine for Bleached Hair

Here's a sample hair care routine for bleached hair:

  • Wash your hair with a shampoo designed for damaged hair.
  • Apply a moisturizing conditioner and leave it on for a few minutes.
  • Once a week, apply a hair mask and leave it on for at least 30 minutes.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to provide extra nourishment.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools and harsh chemicals.
  • Get regular haircuts to remove damaged ends.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can fix falling out bleached hair and restore your locks to their former glory. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and your hair will thank you!
