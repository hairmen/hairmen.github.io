How To Fix A Skirt That s Too Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Oct 14, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you have a favorite skirt that’s just a bit too short? Don’t worry, you don’t have to give up on it just yet! With a few simple steps, you can fix a skirt that's too short and make it wearable again. In this article, we'll show you how to do just that.

Description

Whether it's a skirt you’ve had for years or a new purchase that turned out to be shorter than you expected, a skirt that's too short can be frustrating. But don't worry, with a few simple tricks, you can make it longer and more comfortable to wear. This article will guide you through the steps to fix your skirt and make it wearable again.

How to Fix a Skirt That's Too Short

Here’s how to fix a skirt that's too short:

Step 1: Measure the Skirt

The first step to fixing a too-short skirt is to measure it. Measure the length of the skirt and determine how much longer you’d like it to be. This will help you decide which method to use to extend the length of the skirt.

Step 2: Add a Hem

If the skirt is only slightly too short, adding a hem can be a simple solution. To do this, measure and mark the desired length of the skirt. Then, fold the excess fabric up to create a new hemline. Pin the fabric in place and use a sewing machine or hand-stitch to create a new hemline.

Step 3: Add a Ruffle

If the skirt is too short and adding a hem won’t be enough, you can add a ruffle to the bottom of the skirt. To do this, measure and cut a strip of fabric that’s twice the length of the circumference of the skirt. Fold the fabric in half lengthwise and sew it to the bottom of the skirt, creating a ruffle. This will add length to the skirt while also giving it a cute, feminine touch.

Step 4: Add a Lace Trim

If you want to add a bit of extra length to your skirt without making it too heavy, adding a lace trim can be a great option. Measure and cut a strip of lace that’s slightly longer than the circumference of the skirt. Sew the lace to the bottom of the skirt, creating a delicate, feminine look.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when fixing a skirt that's too short:

  • Be sure to measure the skirt carefully before making any alterations.
  • When adding a hem or ruffle, be sure to fold the fabric evenly to create a neat, clean look.
  • Consider adding a lining to the skirt to make it more comfortable to wear.
  • When adding a lace trim, be sure to choose a delicate lace that complements the fabric of the skirt.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing a skirt that's too short:

Q: Can I use fabric glue to add a hem or ruffle?

A: While fabric glue can be used to make alterations to clothing, we recommend using a sewing machine or hand-stitching for a more secure and long-lasting fix.

Q: Will adding a ruffle make the skirt too heavy?

A: It depends on the fabric you use and the size of the ruffle. If you’re worried about the weight of the skirt, consider using a lightweight fabric for the ruffle or making the ruffle smaller.

Q: Can I use iron-on hem tape to add a hem?

A: Iron-on hem tape can be a quick and easy solution for a temporary fix, but we recommend sewing the hem for a more secure and long-lasting fix.

Hair Care

Now that you know how to fix a skirt that's too short, it's time to think about your hair! If you're looking for a new hairstyle to complement your longer skirt, consider trying a chic updo or a sleek ponytail. These styles are easy to create and will keep your hair looking neat and tidy all day long.

Remember, fixing a skirt that's too short doesn't have to be difficult. With a few simple steps, you can make your favorite skirt wearable again and enjoy it for years to come!
