How To Cut Layered Hairstyle At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a new hairstyle but don't want to spend a lot of money at the salon? Cutting your own hair might seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful layered hairstyle at home. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting your hair in layers, from start to finish. Let's get started with a few key tips.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step process of cutting your hair, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

Invest in Quality Tools

To achieve a professional-looking haircut, you'll need to invest in a few key tools, including sharp hair cutting scissors, hair clips, and a comb. Don't try to use regular scissors or kitchen shears, as they can damage your hair and create uneven cuts.

Start with Clean, Dry Hair

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and free of any products, as this will help you see the natural texture and movement of your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

Take Your Time

Cutting your own hair can be nerve-wracking, but it's important to take your time and work slowly. Don't rush the process, as this can lead to mistakes and uneven cuts. Take breaks if you need to, and don't hesitate to ask a friend or family member for help if you're feeling overwhelmed.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Layered Hair at Home

Now that you know the basics, let's dive into the step-by-step process of cutting layered hair at home:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to keep each section separate, and make sure they're all roughly the same size.

Step 2: Cut the Back Section

Starting with the back section, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and use your other hand to cut across the hair in a straight line. Work your way through each section of hair, cutting straight across and making sure to keep your fingers level.

Step 3: Cut the Side Sections

Once you've finished the back section, move on to the sides. Take a small section of hair from the front of your ear, and comb it straight down. Use the same technique as before, cutting across the hair in a straight line. Work your way up the side of your head, cutting each section at the same length.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Now that you've cut the basic layers, it's time to blend them together. Take a small section of hair from the top of your head, and comb it straight up. Use your scissors to cut into the ends of the hair, creating soft, blended layers. Work your way through each section of hair, blending the layers together to create a seamless look.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, take a few minutes to check your work. Look for any uneven sections or stray hairs, and trim them as needed. Use a mirror to check the back of your head, and make any final adjustments before you're done.

FAQs

How often should I cut my hair?

It's generally recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can still cut your hair if it's curly, but keep in mind that curly hair tends to spring up once it's cut. It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry and styled, so you can see how it will naturally fall.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always trim a little more off or try to blend the mistake into the rest of your hair. If you're really unhappy with the results, don't hesitate to visit a professional stylist to fix the problem.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you've cut your hair, here are a few tips to keep it looking healthy and beautiful:

Use a Quality Shampoo and Conditioner

Invest in a quality shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent damage.

Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Try to avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause split ends. If you do use these tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray first.

Trim Your Hair Regularly

To prevent split ends and maintain your style, it's important to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it's important to take your time and use the right tools and techniques. With this step-by-step guide, you'll be able to achieve a beautiful layered hairstyle at home. Good luck!
