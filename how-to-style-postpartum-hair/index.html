How To Style Postpartum Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by William Jul 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a new mom, you may find that your hair has gone through some changes after giving birth. Postpartum hair loss, thinning, and changes in texture can make styling your hair a challenge. But don't worry! With some simple tips and tricks, you can style your postpartum hair and feel confident and beautiful.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Style Postpartum Hair

Styling postpartum hair can be a bit tricky, but with the right products and techniques, you can create a beautiful hairstyle that suits your needs. Here are some tips to help you style your postpartum hair:

1. Embrace Your Natural Texture

If your hair has become curly or wavy after giving birth, embrace your natural texture. Use a curl-enhancing product to define your curls and add volume. If your hair has become straighter or thinner, use volumizing products to add body and texture.

2. Choose the Right Products

Choose hair products that are gentle and nourishing, as postpartum hair can be more fragile and prone to breakage. Look for products that are free from sulfates, parabens, and other harsh chemicals.

3. Keep it Simple

Opt for simple hairstyles that don't require a lot of heat styling or product. A messy bun or a simple braid can look chic and stylish while being easy to achieve.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to styling your postpartum hair:

1. Start with Clean Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel as this can cause breakage. Instead, gently squeeze out excess water with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle and nourish your hair. This will also help reduce frizz and flyaways.

3. Choose Your Style

Choose a hairstyle that suits your needs and your hair texture. This could be a messy bun, a braid, or a simple ponytail.

4. Use the Right Tools

Use the right tools to achieve your desired look. If you're using a curling iron or straightener, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

5. Finish with a Hairspray

Finish your hairstyle with a hairspray to hold it in place. Look for a hairspray that is lightweight and flexible, as this will help your hair look natural and not stiff.

Tips for Styling Postpartum Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you style your postpartum hair:

1. Be Gentle

Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, as postpartum hair can be more fragile and prone to breakage.

2. Use Hair Accessories

Use hair accessories like headbands, hair clips, and scarves to add some interest to your hairstyle.

3. Experiment with Different Styles

Don't be afraid to experiment with different hairstyles and products to find what works best for you.

FAQs

1. How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss can last for several months, but it typically resolves on its own within a year.

2. Can I dye my postpartum hair?

It's best to wait until your hair has fully recovered before dyeing it. This can take several months, depending on the extent of your postpartum hair loss.

3. Can I use heat styling tools on my postpartum hair?

Yes, you can use heat styling tools on your postpartum hair, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

Postpartum Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional tips to care for your postpartum hair:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth.

2. Take a Hair Supplement

Taking a hair supplement with biotin and other essential nutrients can help support hair growth and strength.

3. Be Patient

Postpartum hair loss and changes in texture can be frustrating, but be patient. With time and proper care, your hair will recover and you'll be able to style it with confidence.

In conclusion, styling postpartum hair can be a challenge, but with the right products, tools, and techniques, you can create beautiful and stylish hairstyles that suit your needs. Be gentle with your hair, experiment with different styles, and be patient as your hair recovers. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock your postpartum hair with confidence!
