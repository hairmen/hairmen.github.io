How To Cut Curly Hair On Top Of Head A Step By Step Guide

Cutting curly hair can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to the top of the head. But with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a great cut that showcases your natural curls. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting curly hair on top of the head and provide tips on how to care for your curls afterward.

How to Cut Curly Hair on Top of Head

Cutting curly hair on top of the head requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. Curly hair tends to shrink when dry, so it's important to cut it when it's wet and stretched to get an accurate length. Here are the basic steps to follow:

1. Start with clean, detangled hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and free of tangles. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair to keep your curls hydrated and healthy. Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to avoid damaging your curls.

2. Section your hair

Divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to secure each section in place. This will make it easier to cut each section evenly and avoid cutting too much or too little hair.

3. Cut your hair when wet

Curly hair should be cut when it's wet and stretched to avoid uneven lengths. After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner or a curl-defining cream to help define your curls. Use a wide-tooth comb to comb your hair straight, and then use hair clips to stretch out each section.

4. Cut one section at a time

Starting with one section at a time, comb your hair straight and hold it between your fingers. Use hair-cutting scissors to trim the ends of your hair. Start by cutting a small amount at a time and continue until you reach your desired length. Repeat the process with the other three sections of your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and detangle your hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to secure each section in place.

Step 3: Stretch your hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to comb your hair straight, and then use hair clips to stretch out each section. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 4: Cut your hair

Starting with one section at a time, comb your hair straight and hold it between your fingers. Use hair-cutting scissors to trim the ends of your hair. Start by cutting a small amount at a time and continue until you reach your desired length. Repeat the process with the other three sections of your hair.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting curly hair:

  • Use hair-cutting scissors, not regular scissors or shears
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to avoid cutting too much hair
  • Cut your hair when it's wet and stretched to avoid uneven lengths
  • Don't cut your hair too often to avoid damaging your curls
  • Consult a professional stylist if you're unsure about cutting your hair at home

FAQs

Can you cut curly hair with regular scissors?

No, it's best to use hair-cutting scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Regular scissors can damage your hair and cause split ends.

How often should you cut curly hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends. However, the frequency of haircuts depends on your hair type and personal preference.

Can you cut your own curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair as long as you have the right tools and technique. However, it's recommended to consult a professional stylist if you're unsure about cutting your hair at home.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair requires a little more effort than caring for straight hair. Here are a few tips to keep your curls healthy and beautiful:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair
  • Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to avoid damaging your curls
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or a curl-defining cream to help define your curls
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry to prevent frizz
  • Avoid using heat styling tools that can damage your curls
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or a swim cap

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a great cut that showcases your natural curls. Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful.
