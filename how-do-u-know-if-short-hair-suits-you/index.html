How Do You Know If Short Hair Suits You

Written by Oliver Jul 31, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Welcome to our hair care blog! If you're thinking about getting a short haircut, you might be wondering if it will suit you. Short hair can be a great way to refresh your look, but it's important to choose a style that flatters your face shape, hair type, and personal style. In this article, we'll share some tips for how to know if short hair suits you.

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in figuring out if short hair suits you is to determine your face shape. This will help you choose a style that flatters your features and balances out any asymmetry. The most common face shapes are oval, round, square, heart, and diamond. You can use a mirror or take a selfie and trace your face shape onto the photo to get a better idea of your proportions.

Some general guidelines for each face shape include:

  • Oval: most styles will suit this face shape
  • Round: avoid adding volume to the sides, opt for length and height on top
  • Square: soften angular features with a layered cut or side-swept bangs
  • Heart: balance a wide forehead with a textured, chin-length bob
  • Diamond: add width at the temples with a layered pixie or bob

Remember, these are just suggestions and you should choose a style that makes you feel confident and comfortable.

Considering Your Hair Type

Another factor to consider when choosing a short hairstyle is your hair type. If you have thick, coarse hair, you might prefer a layered cut that thins out the bulk. If you have fine, straight hair, you could opt for a blunt bob or pixie that adds volume and texture. Curly or wavy hair can be tricky to manage in short styles, so you might want to choose a longer length or embrace your natural texture with a shaggy cut.

Matching Your Personal Style

Your personal style is also an important factor in choosing a short haircut. If you prefer a classic, polished look, a sleek bob or pixie might be a good option. If you're more edgy or bohemian, a textured, messy cut could suit you better. Consider your wardrobe, makeup, and accessories to make sure your hair complements your overall style.

Choosing the Right Length

Short hair can mean anything from a buzz cut to a chin-length bob, so it's important to choose the right length for your face shape and hair type. A good rule of thumb is to keep the length proportional to your features and not too extreme. You can also start with a longer cut and gradually go shorter to get used to the change.

Understanding Hair Texture

The texture of your hair can also affect how short styles look on you. If you have straight hair, a blunt cut can look sleek and sophisticated. If you have wavy or curly hair, a layered cut can add movement and volume. If you have coarse or frizzy hair, a textured cut can help tame the flyaways.

Highlighting Your Best Features

Your short haircut should flatter your best features and draw attention away from any areas you're not as fond of. For example, if you have high cheekbones, a pixie cut with side-swept bangs can accentuate them. If you have a strong jawline, a bob with layers around the face can soften it.

Considering Maintenance

Short haircuts require more frequent maintenance than longer styles, so it's important to consider how much time and effort you're willing to put in. If you're not a morning person, a low-maintenance cut like a buzz or a messy pixie might be a better choice than a sleek bob that requires daily heat styling.

Feeling Confident with Short Hair

The most important factor in knowing if short hair suits you is feeling confident and comfortable with the style. It's normal to feel nervous about a big change, but remember that hair grows back and you can always try something different if it doesn't work out. Trust your instincts and choose a style that makes you feel like the best version of yourself.

FAQ

Q: Will a short haircut make my face look rounder?

A: It depends on the cut and your face shape. A round face can benefit from a longer, layered cut that adds height and volume on top. Avoid blunt cuts that end at the chin or cheekbones, as they can accentuate the roundness.

Q: Can I still wear my hair up with a short haircut?

A: Yes, you can still wear your hair up with a short cut. You might need to use bobby pins or hair clips to secure shorter pieces, or try a half-up style that leaves some length on top.

Q: How can I style my short hair for a special occasion?

A: Depending on the length and texture of your hair, you can try a sleek updo, a braided style, or add accessories like hairpins or headbands. Experiment with different products and techniques to find a style that works for you.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Short haircuts require different hair care than longer styles, so here are some tips to keep your new look fresh and healthy:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping natural oils from your scalp
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep ends hydrated and prevent frizz
  • Avoid using heat styling tools every day, and use a heat protectant spray when you do
  • Get regular trims every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends
  • Try different styling products like mousse, wax, or pomade to add texture and hold

We hope these tips have helped you figure out if short hair suits you. Remember, the most important thing is to feel confident and comfortable in your own skin. Good luck with your new look!
