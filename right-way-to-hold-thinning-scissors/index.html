Right Way To Hold Thinning Scissors A Beginner s Guide

Written by Christine Nov 15, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Thinning scissors are an essential tool for any hair stylist, but using them incorrectly can cause damage to your client's hair. In this guide, we will show you the right way to hold thinning scissors to ensure that you achieve the best results while keeping your client's hair healthy.

Description

Thinning scissors are used by hair stylists to remove bulk from thick hair. They have a unique blade design that has small teeth on one blade and a smooth blade on the other. These small teeth remove some of the hair while leaving the rest intact, creating a softer look. Holding thinning scissors correctly is crucial to achieving the desired result.

How to Hold Thinning Scissors

The right way to hold thinning scissors is to use your thumb and your ring finger. Place your thumb in the lower hole and your ring finger in the upper hole. Your index and middle fingers will rest on the blade. The smooth blade should be on top, and the teeth should be on the bottom. This grip will give you control and precision when using thinning scissors.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Place your thumb in the lower hole of the scissors

Step 2: Rest your ring finger in the upper hole of the scissors

Step 3: Place your index and middle fingers on the blade

Step 4: Ensure that the smooth blade is on top, and the teeth are on the bottom

Step 5: Adjust your grip as necessary to ensure comfort and control

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning scissors:

  • Use the right scissors for the job. Thinning scissors come in different sizes and are made for different hair types.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Move the scissors in a zigzag motion to avoid creating visible lines in the hair.
  • Keep the scissors at a 45-degree angle to the hair.

FAQ

What happens if I hold thinning scissors incorrectly?

If you hold thinning scissors incorrectly, you may damage your client's hair. Holding them too tightly or using the wrong grip can cause the scissors to cut too much hair or damage the cuticle, resulting in split ends and breakage.

How often should I replace my thinning scissors?

You should replace your thinning scissors every 6 to 12 months, depending on how often you use them. Dull scissors can cause damage to your client's hair.

Hair Care

Using thinning scissors can be a great way to achieve a softer look for your clients. However, it's important to remember that using any tool on your client's hair can cause damage if not used correctly. Always use the right grip and the right scissors for the job to ensure that you achieve the best results while keeping your client's hair healthy.

Remember to take care of your scissors by cleaning and oiling them regularly. Proper care will ensure that they last longer and perform better.
