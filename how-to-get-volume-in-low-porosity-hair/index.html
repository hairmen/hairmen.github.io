How To Get Volume In Low Porosity Hair

Written by Aprilia Sep 13, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have low porosity hair, you know how difficult it can be to get volume. Low porosity hair is hair that has tightly closed cuticles, making it difficult for moisture to penetrate. This can make your hair look flat and lifeless. But don't worry, there are ways to get volume in low porosity hair! In this article, we'll show you how.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Volume in Low Porosity Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Getting Volume in Low Porosity Hair
  • FAQs

How to Get Volume in Low Porosity Hair

The key to getting volume in low porosity hair is to use lightweight products that won't weigh your hair down. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Clarifying Shampoo

Low porosity hair can easily become weighed down with product buildup. Using a clarifying shampoo once a month can help remove any buildup and give your hair a fresh start.

2. Use a Lightweight Conditioner

Choose a lightweight conditioner that won't add extra weight to your hair. Apply it only to the ends of your hair, avoiding the roots.

3. Use a Volumizing Mousse

Volumizing mousse is a great way to add volume to your hair. Apply it to your roots and work it through your hair. Be sure to use a lightweight mousse that won't weigh your hair down.

4. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow drying your hair upside down can help create volume at the roots. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends.

5. Use a Root Lifter

A root lifter is a great way to add volume to your hair without adding extra weight. Apply it to your roots and work it through your hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide to getting volume in low porosity hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

Use a lightweight conditioner and apply it only to the ends of your hair.

Step 3: Apply Volumizing Mousse

Apply a lightweight volumizing mousse to your roots and work it through your hair.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow dry your hair upside down, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.

Step 5: Use a Root Lifter

Apply a root lifter to your roots and work it through your hair.

Tips for Getting Volume in Low Porosity Hair

Here are some additional tips for getting volume in low porosity hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid heavy oils and butters
  • Avoid using too much product
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis

FAQs

Q: Can I use a volumizing shampoo?

A: Yes, you can use a volumizing shampoo. Just be sure to choose a lightweight formula that won't weigh your hair down.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Q: Can I use a hair mask?

A: Yes, you can use a hair mask. Just be sure to choose a lightweight formula that won't weigh your hair down.

Hair Care for Low Porosity Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for low porosity hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo
  • Deep condition once a week
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis
  • Avoid heavy oils and butters

By following these tips, you can add volume to your low porosity hair and enjoy a fuller, more voluminous look!
