Why Does Hair Thin And Fall Out

Written by Petter Jun 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As we age, it's not uncommon for our hair to thin and fall out. There are many factors that can contribute to hair loss, including genetics, hormones, and your overall health. If you're wondering why your hair is thinning or falling out, keep reading for some possible explanations.

Genetics

One of the most common reasons for hair loss is genetics. If your parents or grandparents experienced hair loss, it's likely that you will too. This type of hair loss is called androgenetic alopecia, or male or female pattern baldness.

Androgenetic alopecia is caused by a combination of genetics, hormones, and age. It typically starts with a receding hairline or thinning at the crown of the head, and can progress to complete baldness.

Hormones

Hormonal changes can also contribute to hair loss. Women may experience hair loss during pregnancy or menopause due to changes in hormone levels. Men may experience hair loss due to an increase in dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone that can shrink hair follicles.

Overall Health

Your overall health can also play a role in hair loss. Certain medical conditions, such as thyroid problems or autoimmune diseases, can cause hair loss. Additionally, a poor diet or vitamin deficiency can also contribute to hair loss.

Stress

Stress can also cause hair loss. When your body is under stress, it produces cortisol, a hormone that can cause hair to fall out. Additionally, stress can cause you to pull or twist your hair, which can damage hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

Nutrition

A diet lacking in essential nutrients can also lead to hair loss. Your hair needs protein, iron, and vitamin B12 to grow and thrive. If you're not getting enough of these nutrients, your hair may become thin and brittle.

Medications

Certain medications can also cause hair loss as a side effect. Chemotherapy drugs, blood thinners, and some antidepressants are known to cause hair loss. If you're experiencing hair loss and are taking medication, talk to your doctor about potential side effects.

Hair Care

The way you care for your hair can also contribute to hair loss. Over-styling, using harsh chemicals, and wearing tight hairstyles can damage hair follicles and lead to hair loss. Additionally, using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can also damage hair.

Tips for Preventing Hair Loss

If you're concerned about hair loss, there are steps you can take to prevent it. Here are some tips:

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in protein, iron, and vitamin B12.
  • Avoid over-styling and using harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often.
  • Reduce stress through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques.

FAQs

Can hair loss be reversed?

In some cases, hair loss can be reversed. This depends on the cause of your hair loss. If your hair loss is due to a nutrient deficiency, improving your diet may help. If your hair loss is due to a medical condition, treating the underlying condition may help.

Is it normal to lose hair every day?

Yes, it's normal to lose some hair every day. On average, people lose 50-100 hairs per day. If you're losing more than that or notice thinning, it may be a sign of hair loss.

Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss. When your body is under stress, it produces cortisol, a hormone that can cause hair to fall out. Additionally, stress can cause you to pull or twist your hair, which can damage hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

In conclusion, there are many reasons why hair can thin and fall out, including genetics, hormones, and overall health. By taking care of your body and avoiding harsh hair care practices, you can help prevent hair loss and maintain healthy hair.
