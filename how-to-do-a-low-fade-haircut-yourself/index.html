How To Do A Low Fade Haircut Yourself A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking to give yourself a fresh new look, a low fade haircut can be a great way to do it. This style is versatile and can work with a variety of hair types and lengths. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a low fade haircut at home.

How to Do a Low Fade Haircut Yourself

A low fade haircut is a style where the hair gradually gets shorter as it goes down the head. The hair is typically longer on top and shorter on the sides and back. To achieve this style at home, you'll need some basic hair cutting tools:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve a low fade haircut:

Step 1: Choose Your Fade Length

The first step is to decide how short you want your fade to be. A low fade typically starts just above the ears and gradually gets shorter towards the neckline. You can choose a shorter or longer fade depending on your preference.

Step 2: Start with Longer Hair on Top

Using your scissors, trim the hair on top of your head to the desired length. This will be longer than the hair on the sides and back. Use your comb to make sure the hair on top is even.

Step 3: Clip the Sides and Back

Using your clippers, start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one as you move towards the neckline. Make sure to blend the hair so that there are no harsh lines.

Step 4: Create a Fade

Blend the hair on the sides and back into the longer hair on top using a clipper over comb technique. This will create a fade effect where the hair gradually gets shorter as it approaches the neckline.

Step 5: Clean Up the Neckline

Using your clippers or scissors, trim any hair on the neckline. You can create a straight line or a rounded one depending on your preference.

Tips for a Great Low Fade Haircut

  • Start with a longer guard on your clippers and work your way down gradually to avoid cutting off too much hair.
  • Use a comb to make sure the hair on top is even and to help guide your clippers when creating the fade.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process. It's better to take a little off at a time than to cut off too much.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from different angles.

FAQs

Can I do a low fade haircut on myself?

Yes, with the right tools and some practice, you can achieve a low fade haircut at home.

What's the difference between a low fade and a high fade?

A low fade starts just above the ears and gradually gets shorter towards the neckline. A high fade starts at the top of the head and gradually gets shorter towards the neckline.

Hair Care Tips for Low Fade Haircuts

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized.
  • Avoid using styling products that can weigh your hair down or make it look greasy.
  • Visit a professional stylist every few weeks to maintain your low fade haircut.

With these tips and a little practice, you can achieve a great low fade haircut at home. Remember to take your time and don't be afraid to make adjustments as needed. Happy cutting!
