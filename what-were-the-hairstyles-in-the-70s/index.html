What Were The Hairstyles In The 70S

Written by William Aug 02, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you curious about the hairstyles that were popular in the 70s? Look no further! In this article, we’ll take a trip down memory lane and explore the most iconic hairstyles from the era.

Table of Contents:

  • Disco Bangs
  • Shag Haircut
  • Afro
  • Feathered Hair
  • Long Locks

Disco Bangs

Disco Bangs were all the rage in the 70s. This hairstyle was characterized by thick, blunt bangs that were cut straight across the forehead. The rest of the hair was usually styled in loose waves or curls. This hairstyle was perfect for dancing the night away at disco clubs.

How to achieve Disco Bangs:

To achieve Disco Bangs, ask your hairstylist for a thick, straight-across fringe. Use a round brush to blow-dry your bangs and style the rest of your hair in loose waves or curls. Finish the look with hairspray for hold.

Tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before blow-drying and styling your hair to prevent damage.
  • Invest in a good hairspray for hold.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: Can I achieve Disco Bangs with naturally curly hair?

A: Yes, you can still achieve Disco Bangs with naturally curly hair. Just make sure to blow-dry your bangs with a round brush for a straighter look.

Shag Haircut

The Shag Haircut was a popular hairstyle in the 70s that featured layers and feathered ends. This hairstyle was often worn by rock stars and celebrities, and it exuded a cool, effortless vibe.

How to achieve a Shag Haircut:

To achieve a Shag Haircut, ask your hairstylist for layers and feathered ends. Style your hair with a curling iron or wand to create loose waves and give the hair texture.

Tips:

  • Use a texturizing spray for added texture and hold.
  • Experiment with different partings to switch up your look.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: Can I achieve a Shag Haircut with straight hair?

A: Yes, you can still achieve a Shag Haircut with straight hair. Just make sure to use a curling iron or wand to add texture and create loose waves.

Afro

The Afro was a bold and iconic hairstyle in the 70s. This hairstyle featured natural, voluminous curls that were often styled with picks and combs to create a rounded shape.

How to achieve an Afro:

To achieve an Afro, embrace your natural curls and use picks and combs to style the hair into a rounded shape. Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls hydrated and defined.

Tips:

  • Use a satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by avoiding hot tools.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: Can I achieve an Afro with straight hair?

A: No, an Afro is a natural hairstyle that requires curly or coily hair. However, you can achieve a similar look with a wig or hair extensions.

Feathered Hair

Feathered Hair was a popular hairstyle in the 70s that featured long layers and flipped-out ends. This hairstyle was often worn by Farrah Fawcett and other celebrities, and it exuded a feminine and glamorous vibe.

How to achieve Feathered Hair:

To achieve Feathered Hair, ask your hairstylist for long layers and flipped-out ends. Use a round brush to blow-dry your hair and flip out the ends. Finish the look with hairspray for hold.

Tips:

  • Use a volumizing mousse for added lift.
  • Experiment with different partings to switch up your look.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: Can I achieve Feathered Hair with short hair?

A: No, Feathered Hair requires long layers and flipped-out ends, which are not achievable with short hair. However, you can achieve a similar look with a shag haircut.

Long Locks

Long Locks were a popular hairstyle in the 70s, especially among hippies and bohemians. This hairstyle featured long, flowing hair that was often styled with braids and flowers.

How to achieve Long Locks:

To achieve Long Locks, grow out your hair and embrace your natural texture. Style your hair with braids and flowers for a bohemian look.

Tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Invest in a good detangling brush to prevent breakage.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: Can I achieve Long Locks with short hair?

A: No, Long Locks require long hair, which is not achievable with short hair. However, you can achieve a similar look with hair extensions.

And there you have it – the most iconic hairstyles from the 70s. Whether you’re feeling bold and daring or feminine and glamorous, there’s a hairstyle from the era that will suit you. Just remember to embrace your natural texture, invest in good hair care products, and experiment with different styles to find your perfect look.
