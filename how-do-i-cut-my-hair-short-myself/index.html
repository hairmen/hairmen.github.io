How Do I Cut My Hair Short Myself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can seem intimidating, especially if you're used to going to a salon or barber. However, with the right tools and a bit of patience, it's possible to achieve a stylish and flattering short haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own hair short, including tips and tricks to make it easier and more successful.

Table of Contents

  • How To Cut Your Hair Short
  • Step-by-Step Guide

  • FAQ

How To Cut Your Hair Short

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a sharp pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a handheld mirror and a larger mirror to see the back of your head.

If you have long hair, it's best to cut it in stages to avoid cutting off too much at once. Start with a longer length and gradually work your way shorter. For shorter hair, you can go straight to the desired length.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair short:

  1. Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it.
  2. Section your hair and clip it up, leaving the bottom layer down.
  3. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight.
  4. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length.
  5. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way around your head.
  6. When you're finished, use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head and make any necessary adjustments.
  7. Style your hair as desired.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help make cutting your own hair short easier and more successful:

  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter to avoid cutting off too much at once.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Section your hair and clip it up to make it easier to cut.
  • Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head and make any necessary adjustments.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, consider practicing on a mannequin head first.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair short:

  • Can I cut my hair short with regular scissors?
  • It's not recommended to use regular scissors to cut your hair, as they can cause split ends and an uneven cut. It's best to use hair cutting scissors for a clean, precise cut.
  • What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?
  • If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. Depending on the severity of the mistake, you may be able to make adjustments or style your hair differently to hide it. If it's a major mistake, you may need to seek help from a professional hair stylist.
  • How often should I cut my hair short?
  • The frequency of haircuts depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips for caring for short hair:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Cutting your own hair short can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and a bit of patience, it's possible to achieve a stylish and flattering haircut. By following this step-by-step guide and using the tips and tricks provided, you'll be able to cut your own hair with confidence and ease.
