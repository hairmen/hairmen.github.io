What Is The Best Hair To Buy For A Sew In Weave

Written by Bardi Jun 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering getting a sew in weave but don't know what type of hair to buy? Look no further! In this article, we'll discuss the best hair to buy for a sew in weave, including how to choose the right texture, length, and quality.

Description

A sew in weave is a hair extension method where hair wefts are sewn onto a braided base. This method is popular because it allows for a protective style while still giving you the option to switch up your look with different hair textures and lengths. However, choosing the right hair for your sew in can be overwhelming with so many options on the market.

How to Choose the Right Texture

The first step in choosing the best hair for your sew in weave is to select the right texture. Your sew in should blend seamlessly with your natural hair texture for a natural-looking finish. If you have straight or relaxed hair, straight bundles will work well. If you have wavy or curly hair, opt for textured bundles to match your hair's natural pattern.

It's important to note that if you plan to straighten your hair regularly, you may want to opt for a texture that can be straightened without losing its curl pattern.

Choosing the Right Length

The next step is to choose the right length for your sew in weave. Consider your lifestyle and the level of maintenance you're willing to commit to. If you're a busy person who wants a low-maintenance style, shorter lengths may be best for you. If you're willing to put in the time and effort to maintain longer hair, then longer lengths may be a good option.

Keep in mind that longer lengths may require more bundles to achieve a full look, which can increase the cost of your sew in.

Choosing the Right Quality

The quality of hair you choose is also important. You want hair that will last and look good throughout the duration of your sew in weave. Look for hair that is 100% human hair and has minimal shedding and tangling.

Virgin hair is a good option because it hasn't been chemically treated or processed, which can damage the hair. However, virgin hair can be more expensive than other types of hair.

Step by Step Guide to Installing a Sew In Weave

Now that you know what type of hair to buy for your sew in weave, here is a step by step guide to installing it:

  1. Wash and condition your hair to prepare for the braiding process.
  2. Braid your hair into a cornrow pattern that will serve as the base for your sew in weave.
  3. Sew in the hair wefts onto the braids using a needle and thread.
  4. Cut and style the hair to your desired length and texture.

Tips for Maintaining Your Sew In Weave

Here are some tips for maintaining your sew in weave:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to prevent product buildup and maintain the hair's natural shine.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and tangling while you sleep.
  • Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh down the hair and cause tangling.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling, as this can damage the hair and cause it to lose its natural curl pattern.

FAQs

Q: How long can I keep my sew in weave in?

A: You can keep your sew in weave in for 6-8 weeks, depending on how well you maintain it.

Q: Can I swim with a sew in weave?

A: Yes, you can swim with a sew in weave, but it's important to protect it with a swim cap or wrap it in a silk scarf to prevent tangling.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining the quality and longevity of your sew in weave. Be sure to wash and condition your hair regularly, use a silk or satin pillowcase, and avoid excessive heat styling. With the right care and maintenance, your sew in weave can last for several weeks and give you a flawless, natural-looking style.
