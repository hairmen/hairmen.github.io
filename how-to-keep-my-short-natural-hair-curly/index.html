How To Keep My Short Natural Hair Curly Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Sep 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Keeping short natural hair curly can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve defined, bouncy curls that last all day. In this post, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you keep your short natural hair curly, from wash day to styling.

Table of Contents

  • Wash Day
  • Moisturizing
  • Styling
  • Nighttime Routine
  • FAQ

Wash Day

The key to keeping your short natural hair curly starts with a good wash day routine. Start by using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils. After washing, apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair and keep it moisturized.

Once you've finished washing and conditioning your hair, it's time to style it. Use a styling cream or gel to define your curls and set them in place. Avoid using heavy products that weigh down your hair and make it look flat.

Moisturizing

Keeping your hair moisturized is essential for maintaining curly hair. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and follow up with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to lock in moisture. You can also use a hair steamer or hot oil treatment to deep condition your hair once a week.

When styling your hair, use products that contain moisturizing ingredients such as shea butter, coconut oil, or glycerin. These ingredients help to keep your hair soft and supple, preventing breakage and frizz.

Styling

When it comes to styling short natural hair, less is more. Avoid using too many products or applying too much pressure when styling your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, and avoid using a brush, which can cause breakage and frizz.

To create defined curls, use a curl defining cream or gel and work it through your hair from root to tip. Scrunch your hair with your hands and allow it to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry your hair gently.

Nighttime Routine

Your nighttime routine is just as important as your daytime routine when it comes to keeping your short natural hair curly. Before going to bed, wrap your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet to protect it from friction and breakage. You can also use a satin pillowcase to prevent your hair from getting tangled while you sleep.

In the morning, refresh your curls by spritzing your hair with a water-based leave-in conditioner or hair mist. Use your fingers to reshape your curls and avoid using a brush or comb, which can cause frizz and breakage.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my short natural hair?

A: It's best to wash your short natural hair once a week to avoid stripping it of natural oils. However, if you have a particularly oily scalp, you may need to wash it more frequently.

Q: How can I prevent my hair from frizzing?

A: To prevent frizz, use a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel, which can cause friction and breakage. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry your hair gently.

Q: What products should I use to keep my short natural hair curly?

A: Look for products that contain moisturizing ingredients such as shea butter, coconut oil, or glycerin. Use a curl defining cream or gel to create defined curls, and finish with a hair mist or leave-in conditioner to lock in moisture.

Hair Care Tips for Keeping Short Natural Hair Curly

Keeping your short natural hair curly requires a combination of good hair care practices and the right products. Here are some additional tips to help you achieve bouncy, defined curls:

  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using hot styling tools.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, and opt for air drying or using a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as tight braids or ponytails.
  • Drink plenty of water and eat a balanced diet to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your short natural hair curly and defined, no matter your hair type or texture. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid using harsh chemicals, and use products that nourish and moisturize your curls.
