Why Is It So Hard To Bleach Asian Hair

Written by Aprilia Oct 06, 2023 · 5 min read

Asians are known for their beautiful, smooth, and silky black hair. However, some want to try a different look and bleach their hair. Unfortunately, it’s not as easy as it sounds. Bleaching Asian hair can be a difficult and time-consuming process. In this article, we will discuss why it’s so hard to bleach Asian hair and what you can do to make it easier.

Why is it so hard to bleach Asian hair?

Asian hair is different from other hair types. It’s thicker, coarser, and has more cuticle layers. It also has more melanin, which is the pigment that gives hair its color. The more melanin a hair has, the harder it is to bleach. Asian hair also tends to be more resistant to chemical treatments, making it harder to lighten.

The color of Asian hair

Asian hair naturally comes in shades of black or dark brown. This means that to achieve a lighter color, you need to bleach it. Bleaching is a chemical process that breaks down the melanin in the hair, making it lighter. However, because Asian hair has more melanin, it’s harder to break down, which means it takes more time and effort to achieve the desired result.

The texture of Asian hair

Asian hair is also known for its texture. It tends to be thicker and coarser than other hair types, which makes it harder for the bleach to penetrate the hair shaft. This means that the bleach may not be able to lift the color as well as it would on other hair types. The texture of Asian hair also means that it’s more prone to damage from chemical treatments, so it’s important to use caution when bleaching.

The condition of Asian hair

The condition of your hair also plays a role in how hard it is to bleach. If your hair is already damaged or dry, it may not take well to bleach. Bleaching can further damage your hair, so it’s important to make sure your hair is in good condition before attempting to bleach it.

How to bleach Asian hair

If you’re determined to bleach your Asian hair, there are some steps you can take to make it easier. Here’s how:

Choose the right bleach

Choose a bleach that’s specifically designed for Asian hair. Look for a bleach that’s gentle and won’t damage your hair. Avoid using bleach that’s too strong, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Prepare your hair

Before you bleach your hair, make sure it’s clean and free of any products. Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair to help protect it from damage. You may also want to apply a protein treatment to strengthen your hair.

Apply the bleach

Apply the bleach to your hair using a brush or applicator. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots. Leave the bleach on for the recommended time, then rinse it out thoroughly with cool water.

Tone your hair

After you’ve bleached your hair, it may have a yellow or brassy tone. To neutralize this, you can use a toner. Choose a toner that’s specifically designed for bleached hair and follow the instructions carefully.

Tips for bleaching Asian hair

Here are some tips to help make the bleaching process easier:

  • Don’t bleach your hair too often. Bleaching can damage your hair, so it’s important to give your hair time to recover between treatments.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair after bleaching. Heat can further damage your hair.

FAQs about bleaching Asian hair

Can I bleach my hair at home?

Yes, you can bleach your hair at home. However, it’s important to follow the instructions carefully and choose a bleach that’s specifically designed for Asian hair. If you’re unsure, it’s best to consult a professional.

How long does it take to bleach Asian hair?

The time it takes to bleach Asian hair depends on the color you’re trying to achieve and the condition of your hair. It can take anywhere from 30 minutes to several hours.

Can I bleach my hair if it’s already been colored?

If your hair has already been colored, it may be more difficult to bleach. You may need to have your hair professionally stripped before attempting to bleach it.

Hair care after bleaching

After you’ve bleached your hair, it’s important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that’s specifically designed for bleached hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to chlorine or saltwater, as this can cause further damage.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

Conclusion

Bleaching Asian hair can be a difficult and time-consuming process. However, with the right products and techniques, it’s possible to achieve the desired result. Remember to take good care of your hair before and after bleaching to minimize damage and keep your hair healthy.
