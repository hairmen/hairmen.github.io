The hair is tapered on the sides and back, and the length gradually decreases as it gets closer to the neckline. The fade can be low, medium, or high, depending on how high up the head it starts. Step-by-Step Guide to Fade Haircut

Hereâs a step-by-step guide on how to get a fade haircut:

  1. Start by washing your hair, and towel-drying it until damp.
  2. Choose the type of fade you want, and how high up your head you want it to start.
  3. Use clippers to cut the hair on the sides and back to the desired length.
  4. Create a guide line around the ears and the neckline, using a trimmer or razor.
  5. Blend the hair from the top to the sides and back, using a clipper or scissors.
  6. Use a comb and scissors to cut the hair on top to the desired length.
  7. Blend the hair on top with the hair on the sides and back, using scissors or a clipper.
  8. Finish by styling your hair with your preferred product.

Tips for Maintaining a Fade Haircut

To maintain a fade haircut, follow these tips:

  • Get a trim every 2-4 weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy.
  • Use a hair product, such as pomade or wax, to style your hair and hold it in place.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage it and make it brittle.
  • Wear a hat or cap when exposed to the sun, as UV rays can damage your hair.

FAQs About Fade Haircuts

Here are some common questions about fade haircuts:

What face shapes look best with a fade?

A fade haircut can work for any face shape, but itâs especially flattering for those with square faces, as it creates a more balanced look. It can also add length to round faces, and soften strong jawlines.

Can I get a fade if I have curly hair?

Yes, a fade haircut can work for any hair type, including curly hair. A skilled stylist can create a fade that complements your curls and enhances your natural texture.

How high should the fade go?

The height of the fade depends on your personal preference, and the style you want to achieve. A low fade starts just above the ear, while a high fade goes up to the crown of the head. A medium fade falls somewhere in between.

Hair Care for Fade Haircuts

To keep your fade haircut looking sharp and stylish, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days, using a shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as it can strip away natural oils and leave your hair dry and brittle.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools, such as a blow dryer or flat iron.
  • Avoid using too much product on your hair, as it can weigh it down and make it greasy.
