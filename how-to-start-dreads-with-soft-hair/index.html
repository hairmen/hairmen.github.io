How To Start Dreads With Soft Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Are you someone with soft hair who has always wanted to have dreadlocks? You might think it's impossible to start dreads with soft hair, but that's not true! With the right technique and hair care, anyone can achieve beautiful dreadlocks, regardless of hair type. In this article, we'll guide you through how to start dreads with soft hair, step by step.

How to Start Dreads with Soft Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step process, let's discuss the different methods you can use to start dreads with soft hair.

Backcombing

Backcombing is a popular method for starting dreadlocks, especially for people with soft hair. It involves teasing the hair in the opposite direction of growth to create knots, which eventually form dreads. This method can be done on wet or dry hair.

Twist and Rip

Twist and rip is another method that works well for soft hair. It involves sectioning the hair, twisting each section, and then ripping it apart to create knots. This method can be done on wet or dry hair.

Crochet

Crochet is a method that involves using a crochet hook to pull hair through existing dreads. This method is ideal for people with soft hair who don't want to backcomb or twist and rip their hair.

Step by Step Guide

Now that you know the different methods for starting dreads with soft hair, let's go through a step-by-step guide for each method.

Backcombing

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry or use a blow dryer on a low heat setting.
  2. Section your hair into small, even sections using clips or hair ties.
  3. Take one section and backcomb it from the ends to the roots. Repeat this process until the entire section is knotted.
  4. Use a dread wax or gel to help the knots form and hold the shape of the dreads.
  5. Repeat steps 3-4 for each section of hair until you have created all the dreads you want.

Twist and Rip

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry or use a blow dryer on a low heat setting.
  2. Section your hair into small, even sections using clips or hair ties.
  3. Take one section and twist it from the roots to the ends.
  4. Hold the twisted section with one hand and use the other hand to rip the section apart, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  5. Repeat steps 3-4 for each section of hair until you have created all the dreads you want.

Crochet

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry or use a blow dryer on a low heat setting.
  2. Section your hair into small, even sections using clips or hair ties.
  3. Take a crochet hook and use it to pull hair through an existing dread, starting at the root and working your way to the tip.
  4. Repeat step 3 for each section of hair until you have created all the dreads you want.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Soft Hair

Starting dreadlocks with soft hair can be a challenge, but with these tips, you can make the process easier:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup from your hair before starting the process.
  • When backcombing or twist and ripping, be gentle to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Use a dread wax or gel to help the knots form and hold the shape of the dreads.
  • Be patient! It can take several months for your dreads to fully mature.

FAQs

How long does it take to start dreads with soft hair?

The time it takes to start dreads with soft hair varies depending on the method you choose and how often you maintain your dreads. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months for your dreads to fully mature.

Can I start dreads with soft hair without using any products?

While it's possible to start dreads without using any products, using a dread wax or gel can make the process easier and help the knots form.

Can I wash my hair with dreads?

Yes, you can wash your hair with dreads, but it's important to use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once you've started your dreads, it's important to take care of them to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some tips for dreadlock hair care:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners or oils on your dreads.
  • Use a dreadlock-friendly hair tie to keep your hair out of your face.
  • Retwist or palm roll your dreads regularly to maintain their shape.

Starting dreads with soft hair might seem daunting, but with the right technique and hair care, anyone can achieve beautiful dreadlocks. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips, and you'll have gorgeous dreads in no time!
