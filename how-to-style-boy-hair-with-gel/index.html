How To Style Boy Hair With Gel A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Aug 26, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking for a way to style your son's hair, gel can be a great option. Not only is it easy to use, but it can also provide a long-lasting hold that will keep your child's hair looking great all day long. In this guide, we'll show you how to style boy hair with gel using a simple step-by-step process. We'll also share some tips and tricks to help you get the best results, as well as answer some frequently asked questions about using gel on boy hair.

Description

Gel is a popular hair styling product that can be used to create a variety of hairstyles. It's especially useful for boys who have short to medium-length hair, as it can help to add definition and hold to their hair. Gel comes in a variety of strengths, from light hold to strong hold, so you can choose the right product for your child's hair type and desired style.

How to Style Boy Hair with Gel

Step 1: Start with clean, towel-dried hair

Before you begin styling your child's hair with gel, make sure that his hair is clean and towel-dried. You don't want to apply gel to wet hair, as this can dilute the product and make it less effective. Use a towel to gently pat your son's hair dry, then comb it to remove any tangles.

Step 2: Apply the gel

Squeeze a small amount of gel into your hand and rub your palms together to warm it up. Then, starting at the roots, apply the gel evenly throughout your child's hair. Be careful not to use too much gel, as this can make your son's hair look greasy and weighed down.

Step 3: Style the hair

Once the gel has been applied, use a comb or your fingers to style your child's hair into the desired shape. You can create a variety of styles with gel, from messy spikes to sleek side parts. Experiment with different techniques to find the look that works best for your son's hair type and face shape.

Step 4: Allow the hair to dry

Once you've styled your child's hair with gel, allow it to air dry or use a blow dryer on a low setting. Avoid touching or brushing your son's hair while it's drying, as this can disrupt the hold and create frizz.

Step 5: Finish with hairspray (optional)

If you want to add even more hold to your child's hair, you can finish the look with a light spray of hairspray. This will help to keep the style in place throughout the day.

Tips for Styling Boy Hair with Gel

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when styling your child's hair with gel:

  • Choose the right strength of gel for your child's hair type and desired style
  • Don't use too much gel, as this can make your son's hair look greasy and weighed down
  • Experiment with different techniques to find the look that works best for your child's hair
  • Avoid touching or brushing your child's hair while it's drying, as this can disrupt the hold and create frizz
  • If your child has dry or damaged hair, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help prevent breakage

FAQs

Can gel damage my child's hair?

Using gel on your child's hair is generally safe, as long as you don't use too much and you wash it out regularly. However, some gels can contain alcohol or other drying ingredients that can damage the hair over time. To avoid this, choose a gel that is alcohol-free and contains moisturizing ingredients.

How often should I wash my child's hair when using gel?

It's a good idea to wash your child's hair every day or every other day when using gel. This will help to prevent product buildup and keep your son's hair looking clean and healthy. Make sure to use a gentle shampoo that is designed for children's hair.

Can I use gel on my child's hair for special occasions only?

Yes, you can definitely use gel on your child's hair for special occasions only. Just make sure to wash it out thoroughly after the event to avoid product buildup.

Hair Care for Boys

When it comes to caring for your child's hair, it's important to use gentle, child-friendly products that won't irritate the scalp or damage the hair. Make sure to wash your child's hair regularly, and use a moisturizing conditioner to help prevent breakage. Avoid using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners, as these can damage the hair over time. Finally, be sure to trim your child's hair regularly to keep it looking neat and healthy.
