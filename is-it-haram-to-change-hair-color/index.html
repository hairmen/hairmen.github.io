Is It Haram To Change Hair Color A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Sep 23, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Changing hair color has become a common practice among many people, especially women. It's a way of expressing oneself and enhancing one's beauty. However, for Muslims, the question of whether it's permissible or haram to change hair color arises. In this article, we will explore this topic in detail, answering some of the most frequently asked questions.

Description

In Islam, there are certain rules and regulations that Muslims follow. These rules are based on the teachings of the Quran and the Hadith. Muslims are obligated to follow these rules and regulations, and any action that goes against them is considered haram or forbidden. Changing hair color is one such action that has been debated among Muslims for years.

How to Change Hair Color

Before we delve into the question of whether it's haram or permissible to change hair color, let's first look at how you can change your hair color. There are various methods of changing hair color, including:

  • Hair dye
  • Henna
  • Hair extensions

Step by Step Guide to Changing Hair Color

If you're planning to change your hair color, it's important to follow the right steps to ensure that you get the desired results. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Choose the right hair dye or henna
  2. Do a patch test to ensure that you're not allergic to the dye or henna
  3. Apply the dye or henna to your hair, following the instructions carefully
  4. Rinse your hair thoroughly with water
  5. Apply conditioner to your hair to keep it soft and shiny

Tips for Changing Hair Color

Here are some tips to keep in mind when changing your hair color:

  • Choose a color that complements your skin tone
  • Don't bleach your hair as it can damage your hair and scalp
  • Choose a dye or henna that is halal certified
  • Do a strand test before applying the dye or henna to your entire head

FAQ

Is it haram to change hair color?

There is no clear-cut answer to this question. Some scholars believe that changing hair color is haram as it involves altering the creation of Allah. Others believe that it's permissible as long as it's done for beautification purposes and not to deceive others.

Is henna permissible to use for changing hair color?

Yes, henna is permissible to use for changing hair color as it's a natural product and doesn't involve any harmful chemicals.

Can I change my hair color if I'm a Muslim?

Yes, you can change your hair color if you're a Muslim. However, it's important to follow the guidelines set by Islamic scholars to ensure that you're not doing anything that's considered haram.

Hair Care

Changing hair color can have an impact on the health of your hair. It's important to take care of your hair after changing its color to prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent color fading
  • Deep condition your hair weekly to keep it moisturized
  • Avoid excessive heat styling as it can damage your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

Conclusion

In conclusion, changing hair color is a personal choice, and whether it's haram or permissible is a matter of debate among Islamic scholars. However, if you do decide to change your hair color, it's important to follow the right steps and take care of your hair afterward to prevent damage.
