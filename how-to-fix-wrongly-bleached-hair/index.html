How To Fix Wrongly Bleached Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Luffy Oct 02, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’ve ever tried to bleach your hair at home, you know that things can easily go wrong. Bleaching can cause serious damage to your hair, especially if you don't do it correctly. If you’ve ended up with over-bleached or brassy hair, don't worry. In this guide, we will show you how to fix wrongly bleached hair and get your hair back to its original color and health.

Description

Bleaching your hair can be a fun way to change your look, but it's important to do it properly to avoid damaging your hair. When you bleach your hair, you remove the color by breaking down the pigment molecules. This process can be harsh on your hair, and if you don't do it right, you can end up with over-bleached or brassy hair.

Over-bleached hair is hair that has been bleached too much, resulting in an extremely light or white color. Brassy hair is hair that has turned yellow or orange due to the bleaching process. Both over-bleached and brassy hair can be fixed, but it's important to take the right steps to do so.

How to Fix Wrongly Bleached Hair

Step 1: Assess the Damage

The first step in fixing wrongly bleached hair is to assess the damage. Take a close look at your hair and determine how much damage has been done. If your hair is extremely damaged, it may be best to go to a professional stylist to fix it.

Step 2: Use a Toner

If your hair is brassy, you can use a toner to neutralize the yellow or orange tones. A toner is a semi-permanent hair color that can be used to neutralize unwanted tones in your hair. Choose a toner that is the opposite color of the unwanted tone in your hair. For example, if your hair is yellow, use a purple toner to neutralize it.

Step 3: Deep Condition

Bleaching can cause serious damage to your hair, so it's important to deep condition your hair to restore moisture and prevent further damage. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help repair your hair.

Step 4: Trim Your Hair

If your hair is extremely damaged, you may need to trim it to get rid of the damaged ends. This will help your hair look healthier and prevent further damage.

Step 5: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can further damage your hair, so it's important to avoid using heat styling tools while your hair is recovering. Instead, opt for air-drying your hair or using heatless styling methods.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you fix wrongly bleached hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to help prevent further damage to your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair.
  • Incorporate hair-healthy foods into your diet, such as salmon, avocados, and nuts.

FAQ

How long will it take to fix wrongly bleached hair?

The amount of time it takes to fix wrongly bleached hair depends on the severity of the damage. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months to fully repair your hair.

Can I fix my wrongly bleached hair at home?

Yes, you can fix your wrongly bleached hair at home, but it's important to take the right steps to prevent further damage. If your hair is extremely damaged, it may be best to go to a professional stylist to fix it.

How can I prevent over-bleaching my hair in the future?

To prevent over-bleaching your hair in the future, it's important to follow the instructions on the bleach carefully. Don't leave the bleach on for longer than recommended, and use a lower volume developer if you have thin or damaged hair.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your hair healthy and preventing damage. Here are some tips for taking care of your hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent further damage to your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help repair your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, or use them sparingly and with a heat protectant.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes hair-healthy foods, such as salmon, avocados, and nuts.

With these tips and steps, you can fix wrongly bleached hair and get your hair back to its original color and health. Remember, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent further damage and keep it looking its best.
