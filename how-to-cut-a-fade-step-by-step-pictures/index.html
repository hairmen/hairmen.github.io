How To Cut A Fade Step By Step Pictures

Written by Aprilia Sep 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Looking for a new hairstyle? Want to learn how to cut a fade? You've come to the right place! In this article, we will show you step by step pictures on how to cut a fade. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced barber, you can learn how to cut a fade with our easy-to-follow guide.

Description

A fade haircut is a popular style that involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back, while leaving the hair on top longer. The hair gradually fades from short to long, creating a clean and modern look. This style is versatile and can be customized to suit different hair types and face shapes.

How to Cut a Fade

Step 1: Choose the Right Clippers

The first step in cutting a fade is to choose the right clippers. You'll need a set of clippers with adjustable blades, as well as guards of varying lengths. Make sure your clippers are sharp and well-maintained.

Step 2: Choose the Fade Length

Next, decide on the length of the fade you want. This will depend on your personal style and the shape of your face. Generally, a short fade starts at the hairline and gradually gets longer towards the top of the head. A longer fade starts higher up on the head and gradually gets shorter towards the hairline.

Step 3: Start with the Highest Guard

Begin by using the highest guard on your clippers to trim the hair at the bottom of the head. Work your way around the head, using the clippers to create a line around the base of the hairline.

Step 4: Switch to a Lower Guard

Next, switch to a lower guard on your clippers and trim the hair above the first line. Use a fluid motion to blend the hair from the shorter length to the longer length.

Step 5: Continue Blending

Continue to switch to lower guards and blend the hair from shorter to longer. Use a mirror to check your work and make sure the fade is even on both sides of the head.

Step 6: Detail the Fade

Once you've achieved the desired fade length, use scissors or a razor to detail the haircut. Trim any stray hairs or uneven sections to create a clean and polished look.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard for a more natural-looking fade.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from all angles.
  • Blend the hair in small sections to create a smooth transition between lengths.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help or advice from a more experienced barber.

FAQ

What is the difference between a fade and a taper?

A fade gradually gets shorter towards the bottom of the head, while a taper gradually gets shorter towards the neckline. A fade is typically shorter and more dramatic than a taper.

Can I cut a fade on my own?

While it is possible to cut a fade on your own, it is recommended that you seek the help of a professional barber. Cutting hair can be difficult and requires practice and experience.

Hair Care

To keep your fade looking fresh, it's important to maintain a regular hair care routine. This includes washing your hair regularly, using conditioner to keep it soft and healthy, and trimming the hair as needed to maintain the length and shape of the fade.

Now that you know how to cut a fade step by step pictures, you can try it out for yourself or share this guide with a friend. Remember to take your time and practice to achieve the perfect fade. Good luck!
