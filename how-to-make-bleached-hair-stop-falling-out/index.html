How to Make Bleached Hair Stop Falling Out: Tips, Tricks, & Hair Care

Are you struggling with bleached hair that just won't stop falling out? If so, you're not alone. Bleaching your hair can be a great way to switch up your look, but it can also cause damage and breakage. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to make your bleached hair stop falling out. In this article, we'll go over some tips, tricks, and hair care advice to help keep your bleached locks healthy and strong.

  • How to Make Bleached Hair Stop Falling Out
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Keeping Bleached Hair Healthy

How to Make Bleached Hair Stop Falling Out

The first step in making your bleached hair stop falling out is to identify the cause of the breakage. Bleached hair is often more fragile and prone to damage than hair that hasn't been bleached, so it's important to take extra care when styling and caring for your hair.

Some common causes of breakage in bleached hair include:

  • Over-processing
  • Heat damage
  • Chemical damage
  • Poor hair care habits

Once you've identified the cause of your hair breakage, you can take steps to address the issue and help your hair recover.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are some steps you can take to make your bleached hair stop falling out:

  1. Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and prevent further breakage.
  2. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to restore moisture and strengthen your hair.
  3. Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, or use them on a low heat setting.
  4. Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  5. Avoid using harsh chemicals like hair dyes or relaxers on your hair until it has fully recovered.
  6. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair when styling or brushing.
  7. Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles to detangle your hair.
  8. Avoid wearing tight hairstyles like ponytails or braids, which can damage your hair and cause breakage.
  9. Wash your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  10. Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

Tips for Keeping Bleached Hair Healthy

Here are some additional tips for keeping your bleached hair healthy:

  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair and protect it from damage.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can strip your hair of moisture and cause further damage.
  • Take a break from heat styling and allow your hair to air dry whenever possible.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and health.
  • Consider taking a hair supplement like biotin or collagen to promote hair health.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about bleached hair and hair breakage:

Q: Can I bleach my hair again if it's falling out?

A: It's generally not recommended to bleach your hair again if it's already falling out or damaged. This can cause further breakage and damage to your hair.

Q: How long does it take for bleached hair to grow back?

A: The rate of hair growth varies from person to person, but on average, hair grows about half an inch per month. It may take several months or even years for bleached hair to fully grow back, depending on the extent of the damage.

Q: Can I use hair extensions or wigs to cover up my damaged hair?

A: Yes, hair extensions or wigs can be a great way to cover up damaged or thinning hair while you're waiting for it to grow back. Just be sure to take care of your natural hair underneath to prevent further damage.

Hair Care for Bleached Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for bleached hair:

  • Use a purple shampoo to help tone down any brassiness or yellow tones in your bleached hair.
  • Avoid using clarifying shampoos, which can be too harsh for bleached hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to help nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and curling irons on a daily basis.
  • Protect your hair from chlorine and saltwater by wearing a swim cap or using a leave-in conditioner before swimming.

By following these tips and taking good care of your bleached hair, you can help prevent further breakage and keep your hair healthy and strong.
