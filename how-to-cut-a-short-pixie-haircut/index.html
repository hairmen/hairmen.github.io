How To Cut A Short Pixie Haircut A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking for a low-maintenance, edgy hairstyle, a short pixie cut might be right up your alley. But before you head to the salon, it's important to know how to cut a short pixie haircut properly to ensure the best results. In this guide, we'll go over everything you need to know about cutting a short pixie haircut, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Cut a Short Pixie Haircut

A short pixie haircut is a versatile style that can be customized to suit any face shape or hair texture. Here are the basic steps for cutting a short pixie haircut:

1. Start with clean, dry hair.

Before you begin cutting, make sure your client's hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut, and it's hard to gauge the final length when the hair is wet. Use a blow dryer to dry the hair completely before starting.

2. Section the hair.

Divide the hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start by sectioning the top of the hair, then move on to the sides and back. This will help you to cut the hair evenly and avoid any uneven or choppy sections.

3. Cut the sides and back.

Using scissors or clippers, cut the sides and back of the hair to the desired length. For a short pixie cut, you'll typically want to cut the hair to around 1-2 inches in length. Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it straight across, working your way around the head.

4. Cut the top.

Once the sides and back are cut, move on to the top of the hair. Use scissors to cut the hair to the desired length, making sure to blend the top with the sides and back. You can also use a razor to add texture and movement to the hair.

5. Finish the cut.

Once the hair is cut to the desired length, go over the hair and make any necessary adjustments. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs, and make sure the hair is evenly blended all around the head.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cut a short pixie haircut:

Step 1: Wash and dry the hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. Make sure it is completely dry before you begin cutting.

Step 2: Section the hair

Divide the hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start with the top of the hair, then move on to the sides and back.

Step 3: Cut the sides and back

Using scissors or clippers, cut the sides and back of the hair to the desired length. Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it straight across, working your way around the head.

Step 4: Cut the top

Move on to the top of the hair, using scissors to cut it to the desired length. Blend the top with the sides and back, and use a razor to add texture and movement to the hair.

Step 5: Finish the cut

Once the hair is cut to the desired length, go over the hair and make any necessary adjustments. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs, and make sure the hair is evenly blended all around the head.

Tips for Cutting a Short Pixie Haircut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a short pixie haircut:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more off as needed. It's easier to cut more hair off than it is to add it back on.
  • Use a razor to add texture and movement to the hair.
  • Keep the hair damp while cutting to help prevent flyaways.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it straight across for an even, clean cut.

FAQs

How often should I get a short pixie haircut?

A short pixie cut typically needs to be trimmed every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

Will a short pixie haircut suit my face shape?

A short pixie cut can be customized to suit any face shape. Work with your stylist to find a style that flatters your features and hair texture.

Can I style a short pixie haircut?

A short pixie cut can be styled in a variety of ways, from sleek and smooth to messy and tousled. Use a lightweight styling product to add texture and hold to the hair.

Hair Care Tips for Short Pixie Haircuts

Here are some tips for caring for your short pixie haircut:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to prevent stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as they can damage the hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep the hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Visit your stylist regularly for trims and touch-ups to maintain the shape and style of your short pixie cut.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to cut a short pixie haircut like a pro. Whether you're looking for a bold new look or a low-maintenance style, a short pixie cut is a great option for anyone looking to switch up their hairstyle.
