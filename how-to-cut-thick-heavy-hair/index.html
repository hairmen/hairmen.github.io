How To Cut Thick Heavy Hair A Guide For Beginners

Written by Luffy Jul 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with thick and heavy hair that seems impossible to manage? Cutting thick hair can be a daunting task, especially if you are not familiar with the proper techniques. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we will guide you through the steps on how to cut thick heavy hair, along with some tips and tricks to make the process easier. So, let's get started!

Description

Thick and heavy hair can be a blessing and a curse at the same time. While it can look voluminous and gorgeous, it can also be difficult to style and maintain. Cutting thick hair requires specific techniques and tools to achieve the desired result. In this guide, we will cover everything you need to know to cut thick heavy hair like a pro.

How to

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure that you have the necessary tools and equipment. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to dampen the hair if needed.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut thick heavy hair:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow-drying it. You want to make sure that your hair is clean and free of any tangles before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. Start by sectioning your hair horizontally from ear to ear, then divide each section into smaller sections. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 3: Start cutting

Begin by cutting the hair on the back of your head. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers and cut the hair at an angle. Be sure to cut only a small amount of hair at a time to avoid making any mistakes.

Continue cutting the hair in sections, working your way around the head. Remember to cut the hair at an angle to create layers and avoid cutting it straight across, which can result in a blunt and uneven look.

Step 4: Check your work

After you have cut all the sections, check your work in the mirror. Make sure that the hair is even and that there are no uneven spots. If you notice any areas that need to be fixed, go back and trim them.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting thick heavy hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Start by cutting a small amount of hair at a time to avoid making any mistakes.
  • Cut the hair at an angle to create layers and avoid a blunt look.
  • Work in sections to ensure that the hair is evenly cut.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven cutting.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting thick heavy hair:

Can I cut thick heavy hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if you have the proper tools and knowledge. However, if you are not confident in your skills, it's best to seek the help of a professional hairdresser.

How often should I cut my thick heavy hair?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the health and shape of your hair.

What's the best haircut for thick heavy hair?

The best haircut for thick heavy hair is a layered haircut. This will help to add texture and movement to the hair, making it easier to manage.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for thick hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to moisturize and nourish the hair.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends.

Conclusion

Cutting thick heavy hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and tools, you can achieve a beautiful and manageable hairstyle. Remember to work in sections, cut the hair at an angle, and take your time to ensure that your hair is evenly cut. By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can maintain a healthy and gorgeous mane.
