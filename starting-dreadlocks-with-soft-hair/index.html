Starting Dreadlocks With Soft Hair How To Make It Work

Written by Petter Jul 17, 2023 · 5 min read

Dreadlocks, also known as locs, are a popular and stylish way to wear your hair. However, starting dreadlocks with soft hair can be a bit tricky. Soft hair tends to be more slippery and has less texture compared to other hair types, which can make it harder to lock. But don't worry, with the right technique and some patience, you can still achieve beautiful and healthy dreadlocks.

Description

Starting dreadlocks with soft hair requires a different approach than other hair types. Soft hair is usually more delicate and prone to damage, so it’s important to take care of it properly. This article will guide you through the steps to start your dreadlocks with soft hair, from preparation to maintenance. You’ll learn how to make your hair more textured, how to section it, how to twist and knot it, and how to keep it healthy throughout the process.

How to Start Dreadlocks with Soft Hair

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start your dreadlocks, you need to prepare your hair to make it more textured. You can do this by using a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils that can make your hair slippery. You can also use a sea salt spray or a locking gel to add texture and hold to your hair. Make sure to rinse your hair thoroughly and let it air dry or use a diffuser to avoid heat damage.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, you need to section it into the desired size and shape for your dreadlocks. You can use a rat tail comb or your fingers to create partings that are even and symmetrical. You can make your sections as big or as small as you want, but keep in mind that smaller sections will result in more dreads and a tighter look. Use hair ties or clips to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Twist and Knot Your Hair

Now it’s time to start twisting and knotting your hair to create the dreadlocks. You can use your fingers or a crochet hook to twist and tighten each section. Start at the roots and work your way down, making sure to twist in the same direction for each dread. You can also use a backcombing technique or a palm rolling technique to create more texture and shape. Be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging your hair too hard, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Step 4: Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Once your dreadlocks are formed, you need to maintain them properly to keep them healthy and neat. You can use a residue-free shampoo or a baking soda mixture to clean your scalp and hair, and avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your dreads. You can also use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid frizz and breakage, and cover your hair with a scarf or hat when sleeping or exposed to the sun or wind. Regularly retwist and tighten your dreads to prevent them from unraveling or becoming too loose.

Tips for Starting Dreadlocks with Soft Hair

Here are some tips to help you start your dreadlocks with soft hair:

  • Be patient and don’t rush the process. It can take several months or even years to fully mature your dreadlocks.
  • Use natural and gentle products to avoid damaging your hair and scalp.
  • Avoid using hair tools or accessories that can cause tension or friction, such as rubber bands or tight headbands.
  • Protect your hair from environmental factors that can affect its health and appearance.
  • Consult with a professional loctician or a trusted source for advice and guidance.

FAQ About Starting Dreadlocks with Soft Hair

Q: Can I start dreadlocks with relaxed hair?

A: Yes, you can start dreadlocks with relaxed hair, but you need to be extra careful not to damage your hair or scalp. It’s recommended to wait at least 6 months after your last relaxer before starting your dreadlocks, and to use natural and gentle methods to form and maintain them.

Q: How often should I wash my dreadlocks?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle, but usually once a week or every two weeks is enough. You can adjust the frequency based on how oily or dirty your hair feels, but avoid washing your dreads too often or with harsh products that can cause buildup or dryness.

Q: Can I color or dye my dreadlocks?

A: Yes, you can color or dye your dreadlocks, but you need to be careful not to damage your hair or alter its texture. It’s recommended to use natural or semi-permanent dyes that don’t contain harsh chemicals or bleach, and to consult with a professional colorist or loctician for advice and guidance.

Hair Care for Starting Dreadlocks with Soft Hair

Starting dreadlocks with soft hair requires proper hair care and maintenance to avoid damage and promote healthy growth. Here are some tips to take care of your hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Moisturize your hair and scalp with natural oils or water-based products.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools or exposing your hair to high temperatures.
  • Eat a balanced and nutritious diet to support your hair health from the inside out.

Starting dreadlocks with soft hair requires patience, care, and dedication, but the end result can be stunning and unique. Follow these tips and steps to make the most of your journey, and enjoy your new look with confidence and style.
