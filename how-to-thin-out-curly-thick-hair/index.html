How To Thin Out Curly Thick Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can be voluminous and full of life, it can also be overwhelming and difficult to manage. If you're looking for a way to tame your curly thick hair without losing its natural bounce and texture, thinning it out might be the solution you need. In this guide, we'll walk you through the step-by-step process of thinning out curly thick hair.

Description

Thinning out curly thick hair involves removing some of the hair's bulk or weight without sacrificing its natural shape or texture. This technique can help make your hair more manageable and easier to style, especially if you have a lot of hair or if your curls tend to get tangled and frizzy. Thinning out your hair can also help reduce the amount of time you spend blow-drying, straightening, or styling your hair every day.

How to Thin Out Curly Thick Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to thin out your curly thick hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for curly hair. This will help remove any product buildup, dirt, or oil that may be weighing your hair down.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

Gently towel dry your hair to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously, as this can cause frizz and breakage.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Using a wide-tooth comb, detangle your hair from the ends to the roots. This will help you identify any areas that may need thinning out.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips or elastics. This will help you work on one section at a time and avoid cutting too much hair in one go.

Step 5: Thin Out Your Hair

Using thinning shears or scissors, cut small sections of hair from the middle or ends of each section. Avoid cutting too close to the roots, as this can create uneven layers or gaps in your hair. Work slowly and carefully, checking your progress as you go.

Step 6: Blend Your Hair

Once you've thinned out your hair, use your fingers or a comb to blend any uneven areas or harsh lines. This will help create a seamless and natural-looking transition between the thinned and unthinned sections of your hair.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Finish off by styling your hair as desired. You can air dry it for a more natural look, or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out curly thick hair:

  • Start small and work your way up. You can always thin out more hair later, but you can't put it back once it's cut.
  • Avoid thinning out your hair too much, as this can make it look stringy or sparse.
  • Consider getting professional help if you're not confident in your hair cutting skills.
  • Use a heat protectant spray or serum before using heat styling tools to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

FAQ

How often should I thin out my curly thick hair?

It depends on your hair's growth rate and thickness, as well as your personal preference. Some people may need to thin out their hair every few months, while others may only do it once or twice a year.

Can I thin out my curly thick hair myself?

Yes, you can thin out your hair yourself, but it's important to be careful and go slow. If you're not confident in your hair cutting skills, consider getting professional help.

Will thinning out my hair make it look less curly?

Thinning out your hair should not affect its natural curl pattern, but it can make it look more defined and less frizzy.

Hair Care

Thinning out curly thick hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and easier to style. However, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. This includes using gentle hair care products that are suitable for curly hair, avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible, and getting regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve the perfect balance of volume, texture, and manageability for your curly thick hair. Give it a try and see the difference it can make!
