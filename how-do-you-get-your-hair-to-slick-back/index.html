How Do You Get Your Hair To Slick Back

Welcome to our blog post on how to get your hair to slick back! In this post, we'll be sharing tips and step-by-step instructions on how to achieve this classic hairstyle. Whether you're going for a sleek and professional look or just want to switch up your style, slicked-back hair is a great option. Read on for our guide on how to get your hair to slick back!

How to Get Your Hair to Slick Back

Slicked-back hair can be achieved with a variety of hair types and lengths. Here are the basic steps you'll need to take to get your hair to slick back:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a generous amount of styling product to your hair, focusing on the roots and working your way down to the ends. Use a comb to distribute the product evenly.
  3. Using a comb, slick your hair back starting from the front of your hairline and working your way back. Make sure to comb your hair in the direction that you want it to lay.
  4. Once you've combed your hair back, use your hands to smooth down any flyaways or bumps.
  5. Finish with a strong-hold hairspray to keep your hair in place.

That's it! With a little practice, you'll be able to slick back your hair like a pro.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here's a more detailed breakdown of the steps to get your hair to slick back:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue. You want your hair to be clean and free of any styling products before you start.

Step 2: Towel-Dry Your Hair

Use a towel to gently blot your hair dry. You don't want your hair to be dripping wet, but it should still be damp.

Step 3: Apply Styling Product

Take a dime-sized amount of styling product (gel, pomade, or wax) and rub it between your palms to warm it up. Apply the product to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Make sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Comb Your Hair Back

Using a fine-tooth comb, start at the front of your hairline and comb your hair back. Make sure to comb your hair in the direction that you want it to lay.

Step 5: Smooth Down Flyaways

Use your hands to smooth down any flyaways or bumps. You want your hair to look sleek and polished.

Step 6: Apply Hairspray

Finish with a strong-hold hairspray to keep your hair in place. Hold the can about 10 inches away from your head and spray evenly over your hair.

Tips for Slicking Back Your Hair

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results when slicking back your hair:

  • Use a styling product that works best for your hair type. If you have fine hair, use a lightweight gel or mousse. If you have thick hair, use a heavier wax or pomade.
  • Don't use too much product. A little goes a long way, and too much product can make your hair look greasy or weighed down.
  • Experiment with different parting styles. You can slick your hair straight back, or create a side part for a more classic look.
  • Use a comb with fine teeth to get a polished look. A wide-tooth comb can leave your hair looking messy or uneven.

FAQs

Q: Can I slick back my hair if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can slick back curly hair! However, you may need to use a stronger hold product to keep your curls in place.

Q: How often should I wash my hair if I'm slicking it back every day?

A: It's best to wash your hair every other day to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care for Slicked-Back Hair

When slicking back your hair, it's important to take good care of it to avoid damage or breakage. Here are a few tips for hair care:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.
  • Don't tug or pull on your hair too harshly when combing or styling. This can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to get your hair to slick back like a pro. Whether you're going for a classic or modern look, slicked-back hair is a versatile style that can work for anyone. Give it a try and see how it looks on you!
