How To Find Matching Hair Color Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Nov 12, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to change up your hair color, it's important to find a shade that complements your skin tone and enhances your natural beauty. But with so many different hair colors on the market, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one. Here are some tips and tricks for finding a matching hair color that will make you look and feel your best.

How to

Determine your skin tone

The first step in finding a matching hair color is to determine your skin tone. There are three main types of skin tone: warm, cool, and neutral. Warm skin tones have yellow or gold undertones, cool skin tones have pink or blue undertones, and neutral skin tones have a mix of both.

To determine your skin tone, look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear green, you have a warm skin tone. If they appear blue or purple, you have a cool skin tone. If you can't tell if they're blue or green, you have a neutral skin tone.

Choose a hair color

Once you know your skin tone, you can choose a hair color that complements it. If you have a warm skin tone, go for hair colors with golden or honey tones, such as caramel or copper. If you have a cool skin tone, go for hair colors with ash or platinum tones, such as ash blonde or silver. If you have a neutral skin tone, you can choose either warm or cool hair colors.

It's also important to consider your natural hair color when choosing a new shade. If you have dark hair, it may be best to stick with shades that are only a few shades lighter or darker than your natural color. If you have light hair, you can experiment with more dramatic changes.

Get professional help

If you're unsure about which hair color to choose, it's always best to seek the help of a professional stylist. They can help you choose a shade that complements your skin tone and works with your natural hair color. They can also give you advice on how to maintain your new hair color and keep it looking its best.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to finding a matching hair color:

  1. Determine your skin tone
  2. Choose a hair color
  3. Consider your natural hair color
  4. Seek professional help if needed

Tips

Here are some additional tips for finding a matching hair color:

  • Look at pictures of people with similar skin tones to see what hair colors look good on them
  • Consider the season – warmer shades may be more appropriate for summer, while cooler shades may work better for winter
  • Don't forget about your eyebrows – you may need to adjust their color to match your new hair color
  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to keep your new hair color looking vibrant

FAQ

What if I don't know my skin tone?

You can use the vein test to determine your skin tone. If you're still unsure, seek the help of a professional stylist.

Can I dye my hair a completely different color than my natural color?

Yes, but it's important to choose a shade that complements your skin tone and looks natural. It's also important to maintain your new hair color with regular touch-ups and proper hair care.

How often should I touch up my hair color?

This depends on the hair color you choose and how quickly your hair grows. Generally, you should touch up your hair color every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

To keep your new hair color looking its best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading and maintain vibrancy. Avoid using hot tools such as curling irons or straighteners, as they can damage your hair and cause your color to fade.

It's also important to protect your hair from the sun, as UV rays can cause your hair color to fade. Wear a hat or use a UV-protectant spray when spending time outdoors.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can find a matching hair color that makes you look and feel your best.
