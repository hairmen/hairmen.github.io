Why Is My Hair Less Curly

Written by William Nov 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As someone with naturally curly hair, you may find yourself wondering why your hair has become less curly over time. There are a number of reasons why this might happen, from genetics to environmental factors to hair care practices. In this article, we will explore some of the most common reasons why hair can become less curly, and offer some tips on how to care for your curls to keep them looking their best.

Table of Contents

  • Genetics
  • Environmental Factors
  • Hair Care Practices
  • FAQ
  • Tips for Maintaining Curls

Genetics

One of the most common reasons why hair can become less curly is simply due to genetics. Some people are born with extremely curly hair, while others have hair that is only slightly wavy. Over time, it is possible for hair to become less curly due to changes in the hair follicles. This is especially true as we age, as the hair follicles in our scalp can become less active over time.

While it is impossible to change your genetics, there are steps you can take to care for your hair to promote healthy, bouncy curls. This includes using products specifically designed for curly hair, avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools, and being gentle when brushing or combing your hair.

Environmental Factors

Another common reason why hair can become less curly is due to environmental factors. This includes exposure to sunlight, wind, and humidity, as well as exposure to harsh chemicals and pollutants in the air. Over time, these factors can damage the hair cuticle, causing hair to become less curly and more prone to breakage.

To protect your curls from environmental damage, it is important to use products that contain UV protection, avoid overexposure to the sun, and protect your hair with hats, scarves, or other coverings when outside. You should also avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools, which can further damage the hair cuticle and cause curls to become limp and lifeless.

Hair Care Practices

Finally, hair care practices can also play a role in how curly your hair is. This includes how often you wash your hair, how you style your hair, and what products you use. For example, if you wash your hair too often, you may be stripping your hair of its natural oils, which can cause it to become dry and brittle. This can lead to less curly hair, as dry hair is less likely to hold a curl.

To care for your curls, it is important to use products specifically designed for curly hair, such as shampoos and conditioners that are sulfate-free and contain nourishing ingredients like shea butter or coconut oil. You should also avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and instead opt for air-drying or diffusing your hair. And remember to be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, as aggressive brushing can cause hair to become frizzy and less curly over time.

FAQ

Q: Can hormones affect the curliness of my hair?

A: Yes, hormones can play a role in how curly your hair is. For example, hormonal changes during pregnancy or menopause can cause hair to become less curly or even straighter than usual.

Q: Can I train my hair to be more curly?

A: While you can't change your genetics, you can improve the health of your hair and promote healthy, bouncy curls by using products specifically designed for curly hair, avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools, and being gentle when brushing or combing your hair.

Q: Is it normal for hair to become less curly as I age?

A: Yes, it is common for hair to become less curly as we age due to changes in the hair follicles. However, with proper care and attention, you can maintain healthy, bouncy curls well into your senior years.

Tips for Maintaining Curls

To keep your curls looking their best, try following these tips:

  • Use products specifically designed for curly hair
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair
  • Avoid over-washing your hair
  • Protect your hair from environmental damage with UV protection and coverings
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair

With these tips and some TLC, you can keep your curls looking healthy, bouncy, and beautiful for years to come.
