How To Cut Long Curly Hair On A Boy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 10, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have a little boy with long curly hair, you may be wondering how to keep it looking neat and tidy. Cutting curly hair can be tricky, but with the right technique and tools, it can be done easily and with great results. In this article, we'll show you how to cut long curly hair on a boy in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language. With our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to give your little boy a stylish haircut that he'll love.

Description

Long curly hair can be beautiful, but it can also be challenging to maintain. It can be prone to tangles, frizz, and split ends, which can make it look messy and unkempt. Therefore, it is important to give your little boy's curly hair a regular trim to keep it looking healthy and stylish. But cutting curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. You need to take into account the hair's natural texture, bounce, and curl pattern to achieve the best results.

How to Cut Long Curly Hair on a Boy

Step 1: Gather the Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a good pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean, as dull scissors can damage the hair and create split ends.

Step 2: Wet the Hair

Wet the hair with the water spray bottle until it is damp but not soaking wet. This will make it easier to cut and prevent the hair from flying around while you're cutting.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Divide the hair into sections using the comb. Start by making a part down the middle of the head, then divide the hair into four sections: two at the back and two at the front. Clip each section with a hair clip to keep it out of the way while you're cutting.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Starting with the back sections, take a small section of hair between your fingers and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across, following the natural curl pattern. Be sure to cut a little at a time and check the length frequently to avoid cutting too much. Repeat this process with each section, working your way around the head.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you've cut all the sections, blend the hair by combing it with your fingers or a wide-tooth comb. This will give it a more natural, layered look.

Step 6: Check for Unevenness

Check the hair for any uneven spots or missed sections. If you find any, trim them as needed.

Step 7: Style the Hair

Style the hair as desired using your fingers or a styling product. Curly hair is best left to air dry, but you can use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to speed up the process.

Tips for Cutting Long Curly Hair on a Boy

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Never cut curly hair when it is dry, as this can cause the hair to shrink and create uneven layers.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of the head while you're cutting.
  • Don't use a razor or thinning shears on curly hair, as this can cause frizz and split ends.

FAQ

How often should I cut my little boy's curly hair?

It depends on how fast his hair grows and how long you want it to be. Generally, it's a good idea to trim curly hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking its best.

What is the best way to detangle curly hair?

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle curly hair while it's wet. Apply a leave-in conditioner or detangling spray to make it easier to comb through.

Hair Care for Long Curly Hair on a Boy

To keep your little boy's curly hair looking healthy and shiny, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair, as this can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to define the curls and prevent frizz.
  • Let curly hair air dry whenever possible to avoid heat damage.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to cut long curly hair on a boy like a pro. Remember to take your time, be patient, and work with the hair's natural texture to achieve the best results. Happy cutting!
