How To Cut Short Thick Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Aug 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have thick hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, it can be done successfully. In this guide, we will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to cut short thick hair yourself. We will also give you some tips and answer some frequently asked questions to help you achieve a salon-worthy haircut.

How to Cut Short Thick Hair Yourself

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's first talk about the tools you will need to cut your short thick hair:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Mirror
  • Hair ties or clips

Once you have gathered all the necessary tools, it's time to get started.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your short thick hair yourself:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections using hair ties or clips. Start at the back and work your way to the front, creating sections that are about an inch wide.
  3. Take one section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, about one inch from the ends.
  4. Using sharp scissors, cut the ends of the hair straight across. Take your time and make sure the cut is even.
  5. Repeat this process for all of the sections, working your way from the back to the front.
  6. If you want a layered look, take a small section of hair from the top of your head and pull it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers, about one inch from the roots. Use the scissors to cut the ends at an angle.
  7. Continue cutting small sections of hair at an angle to create layers.
  8. If you want to use clippers to create a fade, start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one. Use the clippers to create a gradual fade, blending the hair at the sides and back.
  9. Once you have finished cutting your hair, use the comb to check for any uneven spots. Trim any stray hairs that you may have missed.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Short Thick Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your short thick hair:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and be patient.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from all angles.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, start with a small trim and work your way up to a full haircut.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short thick hair:

Q: How often should I cut my short thick hair?
A: It depends on how fast your hair grows. On average, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style. Q: Can I cut my short thick hair when it's wet?
A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may result in an uneven cut. Q: Can I use regular household scissors to cut my hair?
A: No, it's important to use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut.

Hair Care after Cutting Short Thick Hair Yourself

After cutting your short thick hair yourself, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain the style and health of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and style.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and using the right tools and techniques, you can successfully cut your short thick hair yourself. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't hesitate to seek professional help if you're unsure about cutting your hair.
