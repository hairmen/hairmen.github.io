What Color Does Blonde Hair Turn When You Get Old

Written by Oliver Jun 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our blog post about what color does blonde hair turn when you get old. In this article, we will discuss the changes that happen to blonde hair as you age, why it happens, and tips on how to care for your hair as you go through this process. So, if you are a blonde babe, keep on reading to find out what's in store for your locks as you gracefully age.

Table of Content

  • Why it Happens
  • What to Expect
  • Hair Care Tips
  • FAQ

Why it Happens

Blondes have less melanin in their hair than brunettes, which makes their hair lighter in color. As you age, your body produces less melanin, which means that your hair will start to lose its pigment and turn gray or white. This process is natural and happens to everyone, regardless of hair color. However, because blonde hair is already lighter, the change in color can be more noticeable.

Additionally, environmental factors such as sun exposure and pollution can also cause damage to your hair and contribute to the aging process. This can result in your hair becoming more brittle, dry, and prone to breakage.

What to Expect

As mentioned earlier, blonde hair will turn gray or white as you age. The exact shade of gray or white can vary depending on the individual, as well as genetic and environmental factors. Some people may experience a gradual change in color, while others may experience a sudden change.

If you are currently coloring your hair blonde, you may need to adjust your hair color as you age to avoid a harsh contrast between your natural hair color and your dyed hair. You may also want to consider using a purple shampoo or toner to neutralize any yellow tones in your hair and keep it looking bright and vibrant.

Hair Care Tips

As you age, it's important to take extra care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some hair care tips for blonde hair:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Choose a shampoo that is gentle and nourishing to your hair. Avoid shampoos that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage.

2. Condition Regularly

Conditioning is essential for keeping your hair moisturized and healthy. Use a conditioner that is specifically formulated for blonde hair to help prevent brassiness and keep your hair looking bright.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a UV-protective spray to shield your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause damage to your hair, especially as you age. Try to limit your use of hot tools and use a heat protectant spray when you do use them.

FAQ

Q: Can I still dye my hair blonde as I age?

A: Yes, you can still dye your hair blonde as you age. However, you may need to adjust your hair color to avoid a harsh contrast with your natural hair color.

Q: Will using purple shampoo make my hair turn purple?

A: No, using purple shampoo will not make your hair turn purple. Purple shampoo is specifically formulated to neutralize yellow tones in blonde hair and keep it looking bright and vibrant.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Generally, it's recommended to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

We hope that this article has been helpful in answering your questions about what color does blonde hair turn when you get old. Remember, aging is a natural process, and taking care of your hair can help keep it healthy and looking its best.
