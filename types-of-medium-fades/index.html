Types Of Medium Fades A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a new hairstyle to try out? If so, a medium fade might be the perfect option for you. This versatile haircut can be customized to fit any style or personality. In this guide, we'll go over the different types of medium fades and how to achieve them.

Low Fade

The low fade is a popular option for those who want a subtle transition from longer hair to shorter hair. This type of fade starts at the bottom of the hairline and gradually fades out as it moves up the head. It's a great option for those who want a low-maintenance haircut that still looks stylish.

Mid Fade

The mid fade is a classic haircut that is perfect for those who want a stylish yet low-maintenance look. This type of fade starts at the middle of the hairline and gradually fades out as it moves up the head. It's a great option for those who want a medium-length haircut that is easy to style.

High Fade

The high fade is a bold and trendy haircut that is perfect for those who want a dramatic change. This type of fade starts at the top of the hairline and gradually fades out as it moves down the head. It's a great option for those who want a short haircut that is easy to style.

Skin Fade

The skin fade is a popular option for those who want a bold and edgy look. This type of fade is achieved by shaving the hair down to the skin, creating a dramatic contrast between the longer hair on top and the shaved sides. It's a great option for those who want a low-maintenance haircut that still looks stylish.

Drop Fade

The drop fade is a unique and trendy haircut that is perfect for those who want a bold and edgy look. This type of fade starts at the top of the hairline and drops down behind the ear, creating a dramatic contrast between the longer hair on top and the shaved sides. It's a great option for those who want a medium-length haircut that is easy to style.

Bald Fade

The bald fade is a bold and edgy haircut that is perfect for those who want a dramatic change. This type of fade is achieved by shaving the hair down to the skin, creating a bold contrast between the longer hair on top and the shaved sides. It's a great option for those who want a short haircut that is easy to style.

How to Achieve a Medium Fade

To achieve a medium fade, you will need to visit a barber or stylist who is experienced in creating fades. Here are the basic steps involved in achieving a medium fade:

  1. Start by washing and towel-drying your hair.
  2. Decide on the type of fade you want (low, mid, high, skin, drop, or bald).
  3. Show your barber or stylist a picture of the type of fade you want.
  4. The barber or stylist will use clippers to gradually fade the hair from longer to shorter.
  5. The barber or stylist will use scissors to blend the hair on top with the faded sides.
  6. The barber or stylist will finish the haircut by styling your hair with product.

Tips for Maintenance

To keep your medium fade looking fresh and stylish, here are some tips for maintenance:

  • Visit your barber or stylist every 4-6 weeks for touch-ups.
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and clean.
  • Use a pomade or wax to style your hair and keep it in place throughout the day.
  • Experiment with different styles and products to find the look that works best for you.

FAQ

Q: How long does a medium fade last?

A: A medium fade typically lasts between 4-6 weeks before it needs to be touched up.

Q: Can I achieve a medium fade at home?

A: While it is possible to achieve a medium fade at home, it is recommended that you visit a barber or stylist who is experienced in creating fades.

Q: What type of hair works best with a medium fade?

A: A medium fade can work with any type of hair, but it is best suited for those with thick hair that is easy to style.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair with a high-quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals like bleach or hair dye, as they can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.

With this guide, you should now have a better understanding of the different types of medium fades and how to achieve them. Whether you want a low-maintenance style or a bold and edgy look, a medium fade can be the perfect haircut for you.
