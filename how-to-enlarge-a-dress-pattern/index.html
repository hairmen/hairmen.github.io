How To Enlarge A Dress Pattern

Written by Aprilia Aug 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of not finding the perfect dress size for you? Well, the solution is to make your own dress pattern and enlarge it to fit your body size. Enlarging a dress pattern may seem daunting, but it is a simple process that anyone can do. Here is a step by step guide on how to enlarge a dress pattern.

Description

Enlarging a dress pattern means increasing the size of the pattern to fit your specific body measurements. This is done by adding extra inches to the original pattern in specific areas such as the waist, bust, and hips. Once the pattern is enlarged, you can then use it to make your own custom-made dress.

How to Enlarge a Dress Pattern

Step 1: Take Your Body Measurements

The first step in enlarging a dress pattern is to take your body measurements. Measure your bust, waist, and hips using a measuring tape. Write down these measurements and keep them handy as you will be using them to adjust the pattern.

Step 2: Choose a Dress Pattern

Find a dress pattern that you would like to use. Choose a pattern that is close to your body measurements. It is easier to adjust a pattern that is slightly too small than too large. Once you have chosen your pattern, make sure to read the instructions carefully before proceeding.

Step 3: Adjust the Pattern

To adjust the pattern, you will need to add extra inches to specific areas. For example, if your waist measurement is 32 inches and the pattern waist measurement is 28 inches, you will need to add 4 inches to the waist area. Use a ruler and mark the extra inches on the pattern. Make sure to add the same amount to both sides of the pattern to keep it symmetrical.

Step 4: Cut the Pattern

Once you have marked the extra inches on the pattern, cut along the lines you have drawn. Make sure to cut both sides of the pattern equally to keep it symmetrical. If you need to add more inches, repeat the process until you have the desired size.

Step 5: Test the Pattern

Before cutting your fabric, it is important to test the pattern by making a mock-up or muslin. This will allow you to make any necessary adjustments before cutting your fabric. Try on the mock-up and make sure it fits well. If it is too tight, you may need to add more inches to the pattern. If it is too loose, you may need to remove some inches.
