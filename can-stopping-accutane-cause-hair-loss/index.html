Can Stopping Accutane Cause Hair Loss

Written by Aprilia Oct 15, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're one of the many people who have taken Accutane to treat your severe acne, you may have wondered if stopping the medication could cause hair loss. It's a common concern, and one that's worth exploring.

Description

Accutane is a prescription medication that's used to treat severe acne. It's a form of vitamin A that works by reducing the amount of oil produced by the skin's oil glands. This, in turn, helps to prevent acne from forming. However, Accutane is also known for its side effects, which can be serious. One of those side effects is hair loss.

How to

If you're considering stopping Accutane or you've already stopped taking it, there are some things you can do to help prevent hair loss:

Step 1: Talk to your doctor

If you're concerned about hair loss, the first step is to talk to your doctor. They can help you determine if hair loss is a side effect of stopping Accutane or if there's another underlying issue that's causing your hair loss.

Step 2: Take care of your hair

Regardless of whether or not stopping Accutane is causing your hair loss, taking care of your hair is important. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and avoid using heat styling tools. Additionally, consider using a mild shampoo and conditioner that's designed to be gentle on hair.

Step 3: Eat a healthy diet

A healthy diet can help keep your hair healthy and strong. Make sure to eat plenty of protein, as well as fruits and vegetables that are rich in vitamins and minerals.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent hair loss:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as braids or ponytails
  • Avoid harsh chemical treatments, such as perms or coloring
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol
  • Consider taking a biotin supplement, which may help promote hair growth

FAQ

Q: Can stopping Accutane cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stopping Accutane can cause hair loss. However, it's not a common side effect.

Q: Will my hair grow back after stopping Accutane?

A: In most cases, hair loss caused by stopping Accutane is temporary. Your hair should start to grow back once your body adjusts to being off the medication.

Q: How long does hair loss from Accutane last?

A: Hair loss from stopping Accutane can last for several months. However, in most cases, hair should start to grow back within six months to a year.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important, especially if you're experiencing hair loss. Here are some additional tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can also strip it of oils
  • Avoid using tight hair accessories that can pull on your hair
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to help nourish your hair

Conclusion

If you're worried about hair loss from stopping Accutane, it's important to talk to your doctor. They can help you determine if hair loss is a side effect of stopping the medication, or if there's another underlying issue that's causing your hair loss. In the meantime, taking care of your hair and eating a healthy diet can help promote hair growth and keep your locks looking healthy and strong.
