Are Perms Coming Back In Style What You Need To Know

Written by Daniel Nov 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Perms were a popular hairstyle trend in the 1980s and 1990s, but in recent years, they have taken a backseat to straight and natural hair. However, in 2023, it seems that perms are making a comeback in the world of hair fashion. If you're considering getting a perm, keep reading to learn everything you need to know about this trend.

Description

Perms involve chemically altering the hair to create a permanent wave or curl pattern. The process typically involves applying a perming solution to the hair, wrapping it around rods, and then applying a neutralizer to set the curl. Perms can be done on all hair types and lengths, but the results may vary depending on your hair's texture and condition.

How to Get a Perm

If you're interested in getting a perm, the first step is to consult with a professional stylist. They can assess your hair's health and texture to determine if a perm is right for you. Once you have decided to move forward with the process, here are the steps you can expect:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Your stylist will start by washing your hair to remove any product buildup or dirt.

Step 2: Apply the Perm Solution

The perm solution will be applied to your hair using a brush or spray bottle. Your stylist will carefully apply the solution to each section of your hair, making sure it's evenly distributed.

Step 3: Wrap Your Hair

Your stylist will then wrap your hair around perm rods of different sizes, depending on the type of curl you want. They will secure the rods with clips to keep them in place.

Step 4: Apply the Neutralizer

After the perm solution has been left on your hair for the appropriate amount of time, your stylist will apply a neutralizer to stop the chemical process. This will help set the curl in place.

Step 5: Rinse and Style

Your stylist will rinse out the neutralizer and remove the perm rods. They will then style your hair using a diffuser or other tools to enhance the curl.

Tips for Maintaining Your Perm

Once you've gotten a perm, there are a few things you can do to keep your curls looking their best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls.
  • Invest in a good leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as a brush can disrupt the curl pattern.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Q: Are perms damaging to your hair?

A: Perms involve using chemicals to alter the structure of your hair, which can cause damage if not done properly. However, if you go to a professional stylist and take good care of your hair, you can minimize any potential damage.

Q: How long do perms last?

A: Perms can last anywhere from a few months to a year, depending on your hair's texture and how well you take care of it.

Q: Can you color your hair after getting a perm?

A: It's best to wait at least a week after getting a perm before coloring your hair. This will give your hair time to recover from the chemical process.

Hair Care Tips for Permed Hair

If you're considering getting a perm, it's important to take good care of your hair to maintain healthy, bouncy curls. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as a brush can disrupt the curl pattern.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

Conclusion

Perms may be making a comeback in 2023, but they're not for everyone. If you're interested in getting a perm, make sure to consult with a professional stylist and take good care of your hair to maintain healthy, bouncy curls. Remember to use sulfate-free shampoo, deep condition regularly, and avoid heat styling tools to keep your curls looking their best.
