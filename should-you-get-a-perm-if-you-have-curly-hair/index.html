Should You Get A Perm If You Have Curly Hair

Written by Daniel Sep 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can be beautiful and full of volume, it can also be difficult to manage and style. One solution that some people consider is getting a perm. But should you get a perm if you have curly hair already? In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of perms for curly hair, how to get a perm, and how to take care of your hair afterwards.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Get a Perm
  3. Step by Step Guide
  4. Tips for Getting a Perm
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care After Getting a Perm

Description

A perm is a chemical treatment that changes the structure of your hair. It is often used to add curls or waves to straight hair, but it can also be used to enhance the curls in naturally curly hair. However, getting a perm on already curly hair can be risky. The chemicals used in the perm can damage your hair, and the results can be unpredictable.

How to Get a Perm

If you have curly hair and want to get a perm, it's important to find a stylist who has experience with curly hair. You should also do your research to find a salon that uses high-quality products and has a good reputation. Before getting a perm, you should also have a consultation with your stylist to discuss your hair type, the desired result, and any concerns or questions you may have.

Step by Step Guide

The process of getting a perm can vary depending on the salon and the stylist, but here are the general steps:

  1. Your hair will be washed and dried.
  2. The stylist will apply a solution to your hair to break down the bonds that give your hair its shape.
  3. Your hair will be wrapped around rods of various sizes, depending on the desired result.
  4. The stylist will apply a neutralizer to your hair to reform the bonds and set the curls in place.
  5. Your hair will be rinsed and styled.

Tips for Getting a Perm

Here are some tips to keep in mind if you decide to get a perm:

  • Make sure your hair is in good condition before getting a perm. If your hair is damaged or dry, the perm can make it worse.
  • Be realistic about the results. Perms can be unpredictable, and the curls may not look exactly how you imagined.
  • Take care of your hair after getting a perm. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting a perm if you have curly hair:

Can a perm damage curly hair?

Yes, a perm can damage curly hair. The chemicals used in the perm can be harsh on your hair, and the process can weaken the hair shaft.

Will a perm make my curly hair more curly?

It depends on the type of perm you get and your hair type. A perm can enhance the curls in naturally curly hair, but it can also make the curls tighter and more defined.

How long does a perm last?

A perm can last anywhere from a few months to a year, depending on how fast your hair grows and how well you take care of it.

Hair Care After Getting a Perm

After getting a perm, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, as they can damage your hair and make the curls fall out faster.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can dry out your hair and make the curls frizzy.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, getting a perm if you have curly hair can be a good option if you want to enhance your curls or add more volume. However, it's important to find a stylist who has experience with curly hair and to take care of your hair afterwards to keep it healthy and looking its best.
