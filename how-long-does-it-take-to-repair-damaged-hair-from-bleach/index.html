How Long Does It Take To Repair Damaged Hair From Bleach

Written by Frank Sep 24, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you've recently bleached your hair, you may have noticed that it has become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. This is because bleach strips the hair of its natural oils and proteins, leaving it vulnerable to damage. But don't worry - with proper care, you can help repair your damaged hair and restore it to its former glory. In this article, we'll answer the question, "how long does it take to repair damaged hair from bleach?" and provide tips for healthier, stronger hair.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Repair Damaged Hair from Bleach
  2. Step-by-Step Guide to Repairing Damaged Hair
  3. Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Products for Damaged Hair

How to Repair Damaged Hair from Bleach

The amount of time it takes to repair your hair from bleach damage depends on the severity of the damage. If your hair is mildly damaged, you may see improvement in a few weeks. However, if your hair is severely damaged, it may take several months or longer to see significant improvement.

To repair your hair from bleach damage, you'll need to follow a few key steps:

Step 1: Stop Bleaching Your Hair

The first step in repairing your hair is to stop bleaching it. Give your hair a break from harsh chemicals and let it recover.

Step 2: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Deep conditioning treatments can help restore moisture and nutrients to your hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, collagen, or argan oil, which can help strengthen and repair damaged hair.

Step 3: Trim Your Hair

If your hair is severely damaged, you may need to trim off the damaged ends. This can help prevent further breakage and split ends.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can further damage your hair. Avoid using them until your hair has fully recovered.

Step-by-Step Guide to Repairing Damaged Hair

Here's a more detailed look at the steps you can take to repair your damaged hair:

Step 1: Assess the Damage

Take a good look at your hair and assess the extent of the damage. Is it just a little dry and frizzy, or are there split ends and breakage? Knowing the severity of the damage can help you determine the best course of action.

Step 2: Stop Bleaching Your Hair

We already covered this in the previous section, but it's worth repeating. If you want to repair your hair, you need to stop bleaching it.

Step 3: Wash Your Hair Less Often

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it even more dry and brittle. Try washing your hair every other day or every few days instead.

Step 4: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair once or twice a week. Leave it on for at least 10 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 5: Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons until your hair has fully recovered. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray first.

Step 6: Trim Your Hair

If your hair is severely damaged, you may need to trim off the damaged ends. This can help prevent further breakage and split ends.

Step 7: Be Patient

Repairing damaged hair takes time, so be patient. It may take several months or longer to see significant improvement.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, strong hair:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about repairing damaged hair:

Q: How long does it take to repair damaged hair from bleach?

A: The amount of time it takes to repair your hair from bleach damage depends on the severity of the damage. If your hair is mildly damaged, you may see improvement in a few weeks. However, if your hair is severely damaged, it may take several months or longer to see significant improvement.

Q: Can damaged hair be repaired?

A: Yes, damaged hair can be repaired. It may take time and patience, but with the right care, you can restore your hair to its former glory.

Q: What are some signs of damaged hair?

A: Signs of damaged hair can include dryness, brittleness, split ends, breakage, and a lack of shine.

Hair Care Products for Damaged Hair

Here are some hair care products that can help repair and strengthen damaged hair:

  • Deep conditioning treatments
  • Leave-in conditioners
  • Hair masks
  • Protein treatments
  • Hair oils

When choosing hair care products, look for those that contain ingredients like keratin, collagen, or argan oil, which can help strengthen and repair damaged hair.

Conclusion

Repairing damaged hair from bleach takes time and patience, but with the right care, you can restore your hair to its former glory. Be sure to stop bleaching your hair, use deep conditioning treatments, trim off damaged ends, avoid heat styling tools, and be patient. With a little TLC, your hair will be healthy, strong, and beautiful once again.
