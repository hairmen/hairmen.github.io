Description

Curtain bangs, also known as Bardot bangs, are a popular hairstyle trend that has been around since the 1960s. They are characterized by long, wispy bangs that are parted in the middle, framing the face on either side. Curtain bangs can be worn by anyone, but they are especially flattering on long faces because they help to balance out the length of the face and create the illusion of width.

If you have a long face shape, you may find that traditional blunt bangs can make your face look even longer. Curtain bangs are a great alternative because they soften the face and draw attention to the eyes and cheekbones, creating a more balanced and proportionate look.

How to Get Curtain Bangs for Long Faces

If you're interested in trying out curtain bangs on your long face, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, it's important to choose the right length and thickness for your face shape. Your stylist can help you determine the best length and thickness based on your face shape and hair type.

When it comes to styling, curtain bangs are very versatile. They can be worn straight and sleek, curled and tousled, or pinned back for a more polished look. The key is to find a style that works for your face shape and personal style.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting curtain bangs for long faces:

  1. Start by consulting with your stylist to determine the best length and thickness for your face shape.
  2. Once you've decided on the length and thickness, your stylist will section off your hair and trim the bangs to the desired length.
  3. The bangs will then be parted in the middle and styled to create the curtain effect.
  4. To style your curtain bangs at home, start by blow-drying them straight and then use a curling iron or wand to add some texture and volume.
  5. You can also experiment with different partings and styles to find the look that works best for you.

Tips for Styling Curtain Bangs on Long Faces

Here are some tips for styling curtain bangs on long faces:

  • Use a round brush when blow-drying your bangs to create volume and movement.
  • Experiment with different partings to find the best look for your face shape.
  • Use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your bangs.
  • Don't be afraid to pin your bangs back when you want a more polished look.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about curtain bangs for long faces:

Q: Will curtain bangs make my face look shorter?

A: Yes, curtain bangs can help to balance out the length of a long face and create the illusion of width.

Q: Can I wear curtain bangs if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, curtain bangs can be worn by anyone, regardless of hair type. Your stylist can help you determine the best length and thickness for your curly hair.

Q: How often do I need to trim my curtain bangs?

A: It's recommended to trim your curtain bangs every 6-8 weeks to maintain the length and shape.

Hair Care for Curtain Bangs on Long Faces

To keep your curtain bangs looking their best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when styling your bangs with heat tools.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, which can strip it of its natural oils and lead to dryness and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your bangs looking fresh and healthy.

In conclusion, if you have a long face shape and are looking for a flattering hairstyle, curtain bangs may be a great option for you. With the right length and styling, curtain bangs can help to balance out the length of your face and create a more proportionate look. Talk to your stylist to determine the best length and thickness for your face shape, and experiment with different styles to find the look that works best for you.
