What To Do For Broken Damaged Hair Tips And Tricks

When you have damaged hair, it can be frustrating and overwhelming. Fortunately, there are several things you can do to help repair and restore your hair to its former glory. In this article, we'll go over tips and tricks for what to do for broken damaged hair.

Description

Damaged hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including heat styling, chemical treatments (like coloring or perming), environmental factors (like sun and pollution), and even genetics. Common signs of damaged hair include split ends, dryness, brittleness, frizziness, and dullness. The good news is that with some TLC, you can help repair your damaged hair and prevent further damage from occurring.

How to Repair Damaged Hair

There are several things you can do to help repair damaged hair:

  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends.
  • Avoid heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners, or use them sparingly and always use a heat protectant.
  • Avoid chemical treatments like coloring or perming, or space them out as much as possible.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help hydrate and nourish your hair.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb or brush designed for detangling.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles like braids or ponytails that can pull on your hair and cause damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for what to do for broken damaged hair:

  1. Start by trimming your hair to get rid of split ends. This will help prevent further damage and make your hair look healthier.
  2. Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, or use them sparingly and always use a heat protectant.
  3. If you must color or perm your hair, space out the treatments as much as possible and always use a deep conditioning treatment afterwards.
  4. Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  5. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help hydrate and nourish your hair.
  6. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb or brush designed for detangling.
  7. Avoid tight hairstyles like braids or ponytails that can pull on your hair and cause damage.
  8. Consider using hair masks or oils to help repair and nourish your hair.
  9. Try not to wash your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils. Instead, aim to wash it every other day or every few days.
  10. Be patient! It can take time for your hair to repair itself, but with consistent care and attention, you can help it look and feel healthier.

Tips for Damaged Hair

Here are some additional tips for what to do for broken damaged hair:

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can dry it out even more. Instead, use lukewarm or cool water.
  • Consider using a hair serum or leave-in conditioner to help protect and nourish your hair throughout the day.
  • Try not to rub your hair dry with a towel, as this can cause breakage. Instead, gently squeeze out the excess water and let it air dry or use a blow dryer on a low heat setting.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that are good for your hair, like iron, biotin, and vitamin C.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about what to do for broken damaged hair:

How long does it take to repair damaged hair?

It can take several weeks or even months to repair damaged hair, depending on the severity of the damage and how well you take care of your hair.

Can damaged hair be repaired?

Yes, damaged hair can be repaired with the right care and attention. However, it's important to remember that damaged hair can't be fully restored to its original state, so it's important to continue taking good care of your hair to prevent further damage.

What's the best way to prevent hair damage?

The best way to prevent hair damage is to avoid heat styling tools, chemical treatments, and tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair. Additionally, it's important to protect your hair from the sun and to use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

Hair Care for Damaged Hair

If you have damaged hair, it's important to take extra care of it to help repair and restore it. Here are some tips for hair care for damaged hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for damaged hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, and instead use lukewarm or cool water.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help hydrate and nourish your hair.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb or brush designed for detangling.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, or use them sparingly and always use a heat protectant.
  • Avoid chemical treatments like coloring or perming, or space them out as much as possible.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that are good for your hair, like iron, biotin, and vitamin C.
  • Be patient! It can take time for your hair to repair itself, so don't expect overnight results.

With these tips and tricks, you can help repair and restore your damaged hair. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and your hair will thank you for it!
