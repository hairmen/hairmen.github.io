How To Thin Out Thick Coarse Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Aug 22, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have thick, coarse, curly hair, you may find it difficult to manage and style. While having voluminous hair is great, sometimes it can feel overwhelming, heavy, and unmanageable. Fortunately, there are ways to thin out thick hair without losing its natural texture and bounce. In this article, we’ll discuss how to thin out thick coarse curly hair and provide some tips and tricks to make styling easier and more manageable.

How to Thin Out Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Before we dive into the tips and tricks, let’s talk about how to thin out thick coarse curly hair. Here are some methods you can use:

1. Layered Haircut

A layered haircut is an excellent way to thin out thick hair. Your stylist can remove bulk and weight by strategically cutting your hair into layers. This will create movement and texture, making your hair more manageable and easier to style. If you have curly hair, make sure your stylist knows how to cut curly hair to avoid any unwanted frizz or unevenness.

2. Texturizing Shears

Texturizing shears are a type of scissors that have teeth-like notches on the blades. When used on thick hair, they can remove some of the bulk without sacrificing length. Your stylist can use texturizing shears to thin out your hair and create a more manageable look.

3. Razor Cut

A razor cut is another technique that can be used to thin out thick hair. Your stylist will use a razor blade to cut your hair, which will remove some of the bulk and create texture. This technique is best suited for straight or wavy hair, as it can cause frizz in curly hair.

Step by Step Guide: How to Thin Out Thick Coarse Curly Hair

If you’re looking to thin out your hair at home, here’s a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for your hair type. This will help to soften and detangle your hair, making it easier to comb through.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Using a wide-tooth comb, gently comb through your hair to remove any tangles. This will help to prevent breakage and make it easier to cut.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips or bands. This will help you to work on one section at a time, making the process easier and more manageable.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Using sharp hair cutting scissors, start by snipping off small sections of hair. Work your way from the bottom of your hair upwards, cutting at an angle to create layers. Make sure you don’t cut too much at once, as this can result in unevenness.

Step 5: Thin Out Your Hair

Once you’ve created your layers, you can use thinning shears to remove some of the bulk. Again, work in small sections, thinning out your hair gradually until you achieve the desired look.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you’ve thinned out your hair, you can style it as usual. Use products that are designed for your hair type to help enhance your curls and create a more polished look.

Tips for Managing Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Now that you know how to thin out your hair, here are some tips to help you manage your thick, coarse, curly hair:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb will help to prevent breakage and minimize frizz. It will also help to detangle your hair without causing damage.

2. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner will help to moisturize your hair and make it more manageable. Look for a product that’s designed for your hair type.

3. Don’t Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it dry and frizzy. Try to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or as needed.

4. Use a Diffuser

A diffuser attachment for your hairdryer can help to enhance your curls and minimize frizz. Use it on a low heat setting to avoid damage.

5. Embrace Your Natural Texture

Don’t try to fight your natural texture! Embrace your curls and work with them, rather than against them. Your natural texture is what makes your hair unique!

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Thin Out My Hair Without Cutting It?

Yes! You can use thinning shears or a razor comb to thin out your hair without cutting it. However, these methods may not be as effective as a layered haircut or razor cut.

How Often Should I Get My Hair Thinned Out?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, you should get your hair thinned out every 3-6 months to maintain its shape and style.

Can I Thin Out My Hair at Home?

Yes, you can! However, it’s best to leave the more complex techniques, such as razor cutting, to the professionals.

Hair Care for Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Now that you know how to thin out your hair and manage your natural texture, here are some hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and strong:

1. Use a Deep Conditioner

Using a deep conditioner once a week will help to moisturize and strengthen your hair. Look for a product that’s designed for thick, coarse, curly hair.

2. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Using heat styling tools too often can damage your hair, causing breakage and frizz. If you need to use them, make sure to use a heat protectant spray.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can cause tension and breakage. Try to wear your hair down or in loose styles to avoid damage.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims will help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

5. Use a Hair Mask

Using a hair mask once a week will help to nourish and strengthen your hair. Look for a product that’s designed for your hair type.

Conclusion

Thick, coarse, curly hair can be challenging to manage, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a beautiful, manageable style. Whether you choose to thin out your hair with a layered haircut, texturizing shears, or a razor cut, make sure you work with a stylist who understands your hair type. With a little care and attention, you can keep your hair healthy, strong, and looking its best!
