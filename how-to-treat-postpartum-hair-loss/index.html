How To Treat Postpartum Hair Loss A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Jun 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a new mom experiencing postpartum hair loss, you're not alone. Almost every new mom experiences this issue after giving birth. It's normal and usually temporary, but it can be frustrating and affect your self-esteem. In this guide, we'll show you how to treat postpartum hair loss and help you get your hair back to its pre-pregnancy glory.

Causes of Postpartum Hair Loss

Postpartum hair loss is a common condition that affects many new moms. It occurs due to hormonal changes after giving birth. During pregnancy, your body produces higher levels of estrogen, which can keep your hair in the growth phase for longer. This can result in thicker, fuller hair during pregnancy.

After giving birth, your estrogen levels drop dramatically, causing your hair to enter the resting phase of the hair growth cycle. This can lead to excessive hair shedding, which is often noticeable when you brush or wash your hair.

How to Treat Postpartum Hair Loss

There are several ways to treat postpartum hair loss, including:

  • Using hair growth products
  • Eating a healthy diet
  • Reducing stress
  • Getting enough sleep
  • Using gentle hair care products

These methods can help stimulate hair growth and prevent further hair loss. However, it's important to note that postpartum hair loss is usually temporary and will resolve on its own over time.

Step-by-Step Guide to Treating Postpartum Hair Loss

Follow these steps to treat postpartum hair loss:

  1. Consult with your doctor: Before starting any hair growth treatments, it's important to consult with your doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions that may be contributing to your hair loss.
  2. Use hair growth products: Look for hair growth products that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and collagen. These ingredients can help strengthen and nourish your hair, promoting growth.
  3. Eat a healthy diet: Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help support hair growth. Focus on eating foods that are high in protein, iron, and vitamin C.
  4. Reduce stress: Stress can contribute to hair loss, so it's important to find ways to manage your stress levels. Try practicing yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises.
  5. Get enough sleep: Getting enough sleep is essential for overall health and can also promote hair growth. Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep each night.
  6. Use gentle hair care products: Avoid using harsh hair care products that can damage your hair. Instead, use gentle products that are free of sulfates and parabens.

Tips for Preventing Postpartum Hair Loss

While postpartum hair loss is usually temporary and will resolve on its own, there are several things you can do to prevent further hair loss, including:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Avoid over-washing your hair
  • Avoid using harsh hair care products
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

FAQs

Q: How long does postpartum hair loss last?

A: Postpartum hair loss usually lasts for a few months, but it can take up to a year for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy state.

Q: Will postpartum hair loss cause permanent hair loss?

A: No, postpartum hair loss is usually temporary and will resolve on its own over time.

Q: Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

A: While postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, there are several things you can do to minimize hair loss and promote hair growth.

Hair Care Tips for Postpartum Hair Loss

Follow these hair care tips to help prevent postpartum hair loss:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair
  • Avoid using tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools

By following these tips and methods, you can help prevent postpartum hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient and kind to yourself, as postpartum hair loss is a common condition that will resolve on its own over time.
