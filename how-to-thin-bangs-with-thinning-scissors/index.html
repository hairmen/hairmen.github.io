How To Thin Bangs With Thinning Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 14, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have thick, heavy bangs that tend to fall in your face, you may be wondering how to thin them out without sacrificing their length. One effective way to do this is by using thinning scissors. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of thinning your bangs with scissors, including tips, tricks, and hair care advice.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Thin Bangs with Thinning Scissors

Thinning scissors are a type of hair cutting tool that has a serrated edge, which allows you to remove some of the hair without cutting it all off. They’re a great option for people with thick hair or bangs because they can help reduce the weight of the hair while maintaining its length. Here’s how to use them to thin your bangs:

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

To start, you’ll need a few things:

  • Thinning scissors
  • Regular scissors
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean, and that your hair is clean and dry.

Step 2: Section Your Bangs

Use your comb to section off your bangs from the rest of your hair. If you have longer bangs, you may need to separate them into smaller sections.

Step 3: Spray Your Hair

Spritz your bangs with water to dampen them. This will make them easier to work with and will help prevent any flyaways.

Step 4: Start Thinning

Hold your thinning scissors at a 45-degree angle and make small snips into your bangs. Start at the middle of your bangs and work your way out to the sides. Be careful not to take off too much hair at once, as this can create uneven spots.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After making a few snips, use your regular scissors to blend the hair that you’ve cut. This will help create a natural-looking finish.

Step 6: Repeat as Needed

Continue thinning your bangs until you’ve achieved the desired look. Remember to take breaks and step back to assess your progress as you go.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed breakdown of the steps involved in thinning your bangs with thinning scissors:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it’s completely dry before you begin cutting.

Step 2: Comb Your Bangs

Use a comb to separate your bangs from the rest of your hair. This will make it easier to work with them.

Step 3: Spray Your Hair

Spritz your bangs with water to make them damp. This will help prevent flyaways and make them easier to cut.

Step 4: Start Thinning

Hold your thinning scissors at a 45-degree angle and make small snips into your bangs. Work from the middle of your bangs out to the sides, taking care not to remove too much hair at once.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

Use your regular scissors to blend the hair that you’ve cut. This will help create a natural-looking finish.

Step 6: Repeat as Needed

Continue thinning your bangs until you’ve achieved the desired look. Step back and assess your progress as you go.

Tips for Success

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect thin bangs:

  • Start small: It’s better to take off too little hair at first than too much. You can always go back and cut more later.
  • Take breaks: Thinning your bangs can be tedious and time-consuming. Take breaks as needed to rest your hands and eyes.
  • Use sharp scissors: Blunt scissors can make your hair look jagged and uneven. Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean before you begin cutting.
  • Don’t cut too close to the roots: This can create a choppy, uneven look. Leave a little bit of hair near the roots to maintain volume.
  • Practice makes perfect: If you’re new to using thinning scissors, it may take a few tries to get the hang of it. Don’t get discouraged if your first attempt isn’t perfect.

FAQ About Thinning Scissors

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning scissors:

Q: Can I use regular scissors to thin my bangs?

A: While you can use regular scissors to thin your hair, thinning scissors are specifically designed for this purpose and will give you better results.

Q: How often should I thin my bangs?

A: This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, you should thin your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

Q: Can thinning scissors damage my hair?

A: If used improperly, any type of scissors can damage your hair. However, if you use thinning scissors correctly and take care not to remove too much hair at once, they should not cause any damage.

Hair Care After Thinning Bangs

After thinning your bangs, it’s important to take care of them properly to maintain their health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid heat styling tools
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle wet hair
  • Apply a hair mask once a week
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

By following these tips, you can keep your newly thinned bangs looking great for weeks to come.
