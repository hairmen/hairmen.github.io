How To Cut Your Own Hair Youtube Video A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Nov 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon every few months? Or maybe you just want to try something new with your hair? Whatever the reason, cutting your own hair at home can be a great way to save money and experiment with different styles. And thanks to the power of YouTube, you don't even need to leave your house to learn how to do it!

How to Find the Best YouTube Videos for Cutting Your Own Hair

Before you grab your scissors and start snipping away, it's important to find the right YouTube video to guide you through the process. Here are a few tips for finding the best ones:

Look for Videos from Professional Hairstylists

While there are plenty of DIY hair cutting videos out there, it's best to stick with ones from professional hairstylists. They'll have the experience and knowledge to give you the best advice and techniques.

Check the Video's Length

You don't want to choose a video that's too long or too short. A video that's too long might be overwhelming, while one that's too short might not cover all the essential steps. Aim for a video that's around 10-15 minutes long.

Read the Comments

Before you start watching a video, take a few moments to read through the comments. This will give you an idea of whether the video is helpful and if there are any common issues or concerns that people have.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Own Hair

Now that you've found the perfect YouTube video, it's time to get down to business. Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting your own hair:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Make sure you have all the necessary tools before you start. This includes scissors, a comb, and hair clips. You may also want to have a handheld mirror to help you see the back of your head.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry. This will make it easier to see the natural shape of your hair and ensure an even cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using hair clips, separate your hair into sections. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Follow the instructions in the YouTube video to start cutting your hair. Take your time and be careful not to cut too much off at once.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After each section, check your progress in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments before moving on to the next section.

Step 6: Trim the Ends

Once you've finished cutting all the sections, it's time to trim the ends. This will give your hair a polished look and help prevent split ends.

Step 7: Style as Desired

Once you're happy with your new haircut, style it as desired. You may want to use a hair straightener or curling iron to add some extra flair.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind as you cut your own hair:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to larger ones.
  • Don't cut too much off at once, as it's difficult to fix a mistake.
  • Be patient and take your time.
  • Use a comb to help guide your scissors and ensure an even cut.
  • Watch the YouTube video multiple times before you start cutting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about cutting their own hair:

Is it difficult to cut your own hair?

Cutting your own hair can be challenging, but it's definitely doable with the right tools and technique. Watching a YouTube video can help you feel more confident and prepared.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. Depending on the severity of the mistake, you may be able to fix it yourself or seek help from a professional hairstylist.

How often should I cut my own hair?

This will depend on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, most people should aim to cut their hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain healthy ends.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Once you've finished cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

With these tips and a helpful YouTube video, you'll be able to cut your own hair like a pro in no time!
