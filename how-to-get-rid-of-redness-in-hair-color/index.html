How To Get Rid Of Redness In Hair Color

Written by William Jul 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of seeing unwanted red tones in your hair color? Whether you dyed your hair at home or went to a salon, redness can be a common issue. Don't worry, there are ways to fix it! In this article, we'll share our tips on how to get rid of redness in hair color.

Description

Redness in hair color can happen for various reasons, including using the wrong type of color or not leaving the dye on long enough. It can also occur if you have naturally red undertones in your hair. Regardless of the cause, it can be frustrating to see red hues in your hair when you were aiming for a different shade. Luckily, there are several methods to reduce or eliminate the redness and achieve your desired hair color.

How to

Here are some steps you can take to get rid of redness in hair color:

Step 1: Use a Clarifying Shampoo

Start by using a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up or residue from your hair. This can help prepare your hair for the next steps and create a clean slate. Make sure to rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Apply a Toner

A toner can help neutralize the red tones in your hair. Choose a toner with an opposite shade on the color wheel, such as green or blue, to cancel out the red. Apply the toner according to the instructions on the package, and leave it on for the recommended time. Rinse thoroughly.

Step 3: Try a Color Corrector

If the toner doesn't fully remove the redness, you can try a color corrector. This product typically comes in a green or blue shade and can be applied to specific areas of your hair or all over. Follow the instructions on the package, and leave the color corrector on for the recommended time. Rinse thoroughly.

Step 4: Visit a Professional

If you're still struggling with redness in your hair color, it may be time to visit a professional stylist. They can assess your hair and recommend the best course of action, such as a color correction or a new hair color altogether.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Do a strand test before trying any new products or methods.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to help maintain your new hair color.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can cause the color to fade.
  • Consider using a color-depositing shampoo or conditioner to help maintain your new hair color.

FAQ

Can I use household products to remove redness from my hair color?

We do not recommend using household products to remove redness from your hair color. These products can be damaging to your hair and may not provide the desired results.

How long will it take to remove redness from my hair color?

The time it takes to remove redness from your hair color can vary depending on the severity of the redness and the method you use. It may take several applications of toner or color corrector to achieve your desired hair color.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair after removing redness from your hair color. Use a hydrating conditioner to keep your hair moisturized, and avoid using heat styling tools too frequently. Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, getting rid of redness in hair color is possible with the right products and techniques. Whether you choose to use a toner, color corrector, or visit a professional stylist, you can achieve your desired hair color and rock your new look with confidence.
