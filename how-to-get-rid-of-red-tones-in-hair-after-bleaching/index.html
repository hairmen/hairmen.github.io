How To Get Rid Of Red Tones In Hair After Bleaching

Written by Luffy Aug 20, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you dealing with unwanted red tones in your hair after bleaching? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people experience this issue, but thankfully, there are ways to fix it. In this article, we'll go over how to get rid of red tones in hair after bleaching, step by step. Here's what we'll cover:

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to Get Rid of Red Tones in Hair After Bleaching
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Bleaching your hair can be a fun and exciting way to change up your look. However, it can also come with some unexpected results, such as unwanted red tones. Red tones can occur when the bleaching process doesn't remove all of the natural pigment in your hair, leaving behind warm tones like red or orange. Fortunately, there are several steps you can take to get rid of these tones and achieve the look you want.

How to Get Rid of Red Tones in Hair After Bleaching

There are several methods you can use to get rid of red tones in your hair after bleaching:

1. Use a Toning Shampoo

A toning shampoo is a great way to neutralize red tones in your hair. Look for a purple shampoo, which will counteract the warm tones in your hair and leave you with a cooler, more neutral color. Use the shampoo once or twice a week, depending on how often you wash your hair.

2. Apply a Toning Treatment

If your hair is particularly stubborn and the toning shampoo isn't doing the trick, you can try a toning treatment. These are similar to toning shampoos, but they're more concentrated and can provide more intense results. Apply the treatment to your hair and leave it on for the recommended time, then rinse it out and style your hair as usual.

3. Try a Color Remover

If the red tones in your hair are very strong, you may need to use a color remover to get rid of them. Color removers work by breaking down the pigment in your hair and removing it. Be sure to follow the instructions carefully, as these products can be harsh on your hair if used incorrectly.

4. Visit a Professional

If all else fails, you can always visit a professional stylist or colorist. They'll be able to assess your hair and recommend the best course of action. They may also be able to use more advanced techniques, such as color correction or a bleach bath, to remove the red tones and give you the look you want.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting rid of red tones in your hair:

Step 1: Determine the Severity of the Red Tones

Take a close look at your hair to determine how severe the red tones are. If they're only faint, a toning shampoo or treatment may be enough to correct them. If they're very strong, you may need to use a color remover or visit a professional.

Step 2: Choose Your Method

Decide which method you want to use to get rid of the red tones. If you're not sure, start with a toning shampoo and see if that makes a difference.

Step 3: Follow the Instructions

Be sure to carefully follow the instructions for whatever method you choose. This will ensure that you get the best results and avoid damaging your hair.

Step 4: Rinse Thoroughly

After applying your toning shampoo, treatment, or color remover, be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly. Any leftover product can cause damage or further discoloration.

Step 5: Moisturize Your Hair

Bleaching and toning can be drying on your hair, so be sure to moisturize it regularly with a deep conditioning treatment or hair mask.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you get rid of red tones in your hair:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo before applying any toning products to ensure that your hair is clean and free of buildup.
  • Be patient – it may take a few weeks of consistent use to see results.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause further damage to your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help protect your hair from damage and keep it moisturized.

FAQ

What causes red tones in bleached hair?

Red tones can occur when the bleaching process doesn't remove all of the natural pigment in your hair, leaving behind warm tones like red or orange.

Can I fix red tones in my hair myself?

Yes, you can fix red tones in your hair yourself using a toning shampoo, treatment, or color remover. However, if the red tones are very strong or you're not sure what to do, it's best to visit a professional.

How long will it take to get rid of red tones in my hair?

It may take a few weeks of consistent use of toning products to see results, so be patient and stick with it.

Hair Care

After getting rid of red tones in your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can cause damage and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can get rid of red tones in your hair and achieve the beautiful, natural-looking color you want. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair, and you'll be sure to have gorgeous locks that turn heads.
