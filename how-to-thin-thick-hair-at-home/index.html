Having thick hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it looks stunning when styled properly, it can also be a hassle to manage and style on a daily basis. One way to make thick hair more manageable is to thin it out. While you can go to a salon to get your hair thinned, it can be quite expensive. How to Thin Thick Hair at Home

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start thinning it.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. This will make it easier for you to work on one section at a time.

Step 3: Start Thinning Your Hair

Take one section of your hair and comb it out. Hold the thinning shears vertically and start cutting into the section of hair. Cut small sections of hair at a time, and be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Repeat this process on all sections of your hair, making sure to cut evenly on both sides.

Step 4: Blend Your Hair

Once you have finished thinning your hair, comb through it to blend the layers. This will make your hair look more natural and less choppy.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. You will notice that your hair is now more manageable and easier to style.
