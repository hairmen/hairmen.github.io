How To Wear Curly Hair At The Beach Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Oct 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that it can be challenging to manage, especially at the beach. The combination of sun, saltwater, and wind can cause your curls to become frizzy and unruly. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to wear curly hair at the beach.

  • How to

How to Wear Curly Hair at the Beach

Before we get started, it's essential to understand that everyone's hair is unique, and what works for one person may not work for another. However, there are a few general tips that can help you manage your curly hair at the beach.

1. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Saltwater can be very drying to your hair, so it's essential to keep it moisturized. Apply a leave-in conditioner before you go to the beach to help protect your hair from the sun and saltwater.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

A wide-tooth comb is the best way to detangle your curly hair without causing damage. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots, gently detangling any knots. Avoid using a brush, as it can cause your curls to become frizzy.

3. Wear a Hat

A hat is an excellent way to protect your hair from the sun and keep it out of your face. Look for a wide-brimmed hat that will provide maximum coverage.

4. Embrace the Messy Look

Curly hair tends to have a mind of its own, especially at the beach. Instead of trying to fight it, embrace the messy look. Use a curl-enhancing product to define your curls, and let them do their thing.

Step by Step Guide to Wearing Curly Hair at the Beach

Now that you know some general tips let's go through a step-by-step guide on how to wear curly hair at the beach:

Step 1: Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Before you head to the beach, apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help protect it from the sun and saltwater.

Step 2: Detangle your Hair

Using a wide-tooth comb, gently detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 3: Style your Hair

Apply a curl-enhancing product to define your curls and style your hair as desired. If you want to keep your hair out of your face, consider pulling it back into a messy bun or braid.

Step 4: Wear a Hat

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a wide-brimmed hat.

Tips for Wearing Curly Hair at the Beach

Here are some additional tips to help you manage your curly hair at the beach:

1. Use a Hair Mask

After a day at the beach, your hair may feel dry and brittle. To help restore moisture, use a hair mask once a week.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can cause damage to your hair. Instead, let your curls air dry.

3. Be Gentle

When detangling your hair, be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging on your curls. Use a wide-tooth comb and start at the ends.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair at the beach?

A: It's best to wash your hair after every swim to remove any saltwater or chlorine. However, if you don't want to wash your hair every day, you can use a co-wash or a dry shampoo.

Q: Can I still straighten my curly hair at the beach?

A: We don't recommend using heat styling tools at the beach. Instead, embrace your curls and let them dry naturally.

Curly Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your curly hair:

1. Use a Microfiber Towel

Avoid using a regular towel to dry your hair, as it can cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water.

2. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help reduce frizz and keep your curls looking their best.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your curls looking healthy.

With these tips and tricks, you can wear your curly hair at the beach with confidence. Whether you embrace the messy look or pull it back into a bun, remember to protect your hair from the sun and saltwater. With proper care, your curls will look their best all summer long.
