Do I Suit Middle Or Side Parting

Written by Robby Sep 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of wearing the same hairstyle every day? Have you been considering whether a middle or side parting would suit your face shape more? Look no further, as we break down the differences between the two and help you determine which one will flatter you the most.

Description

The way you part your hair plays a significant role in framing your face and enhancing your features. A middle parting is a straight line that divides your hair into two equal sections, while a side parting is a diagonal or curved line that splits your hair to one side. Your face shape, hair texture, and personal style all play a part in deciding which one to choose.

How to

Before deciding on a parting, it's essential to determine your face shape. This will help you understand which parting will flatter your features the most. Here's a quick guide:

  • If your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width, you have a square face shape. A middle parting will balance out your features.
  • If your forehead is wider than your jawline, and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, you have a heart-shaped face. A side parting will soften your features.
  • If your face is longer than it is wide, with a pointed chin, you have an oval face shape. Both middle and side partings will suit you.
  • If your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at the forehead and jawline, you have a diamond face shape. A side parting will complement your features.
  • If your forehead is wider than your cheekbones and jawline, you have a round face shape. A side parting will elongate your face.

Once you've determined your face shape, it's time to experiment with different partings. Start by dividing your hair down the middle and then to one side. Take note of how each parting frames your face and enhances your features.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to achieve a middle or side parting:

Middle Parting:

  1. Comb your hair straight back.
  2. Use the end of a comb to create a straight line from your hairline to the back of your head.
  3. Use the comb to part your hair down the center.
  4. Style your hair as desired.

Side Parting:

  1. Comb your hair straight back.
  2. Use the end of a comb to create a diagonal or curved line from your hairline to the back of your head.
  3. Use the comb to part your hair to one side.
  4. Style your hair as desired.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Experiment with different partings to find what suits you best.
  • Consider your hair texture and thickness when deciding on a parting.
  • Use hair products, such as mousse or hairspray, to help hold your parting in place.
  • Try adding layers or bangs to complement your chosen parting.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about partings:

Is a middle or side parting better for fine hair?

A side parting is better for fine hair as it adds volume and texture.

Can I switch between middle and side partings?

Yes, you can switch between middle and side partings to change up your look.

Can a parting change the shape of my face?

Yes, a parting can make your face appear longer or shorter, wider or narrower, depending on your face shape.

Hair Care

Regardless of which parting you choose, it's essential to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often, as this can strip your hair of natural oils.
  • Use conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as straighteners or curling irons, too often.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

Now that you know the differences between a middle and side parting and how to determine which one suits you best, it's time to experiment and find your perfect look. Remember to have fun and embrace your unique features.
