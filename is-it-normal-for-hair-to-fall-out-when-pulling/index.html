Is It Normal For Hair To Fall Out When Pulling

Written by Frank Jun 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're experiencing hair loss, you're not alone. Millions of people worldwide have the same problem. Some people experience hair loss when they brush their hair, while others notice a significant amount of hair falling out when they pull it. In this article, we'll discuss whether it's normal for hair to fall out when pulling and what you can do about it.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to Check for Hair Loss
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Prevent Hair Loss
  • Tips to Prevent Hair Loss
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

Description:

It is normal to lose some hair every day. In fact, it is estimated that the average person loses about 50 to 100 hairs daily. However, if you notice an excessive amount of hair falling out when you pull it, this could be a sign of hair loss or alopecia.

There are different types of hair loss, and the most common type is androgenetic alopecia, which is inherited from your parents. Other types of hair loss include telogen effluvium, alopecia areata, and traction alopecia.

How to Check for Hair Loss:

If you're worried about hair loss, there are a few things you can do to check for it:

  • Look at your hairbrush or comb after brushing your hair to see if there's an excessive amount of hair.
  • Check your pillowcase for hair in the morning.
  • Pay attention to how much hair falls out when you wash your hair.
  • Look for bald spots or thinning hair on your scalp.

Step-by-Step Guide to Prevent Hair Loss:

If you're experiencing hair loss, there are a few things you can do to prevent it:

  1. Visit a dermatologist or a trichologist to determine the cause of your hair loss and get a proper diagnosis.
  2. Eat a healthy and balanced diet that includes protein, iron, and vitamins A, C, and D.
  3. Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as braids and ponytails.
  4. Use a wide-toothed comb to gently detangle your hair.
  5. Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach and relaxers.
  6. Don't overheat your hair with hot tools like flat irons and curling irons.
  7. Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.
  8. Manage your stress levels through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques.
  9. Get enough sleep every night.
  10. Consider using hair growth products, such as minoxidil or finasteride, under the guidance of a dermatologist or a trichologist.

Tips to Prevent Hair Loss:

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair loss:

  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption, which can contribute to hair loss.
  • Avoid wearing tight hats or helmets that can pull on your hair.
  • Avoid using too many hair products that can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth.
  • Consider taking a hair supplement, such as biotin or zinc, under the guidance of a dermatologist or a trichologist.

FAQ:

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair loss:

  • Is hair loss permanent? It depends on the cause of hair loss. Some types of hair loss, such as androgenetic alopecia, are permanent, while others, such as telogen effluvium, are temporary.
  • Can stress cause hair loss? Yes, stress can cause hair loss. This is because stress can disrupt the hair growth cycle and push hair follicles into the resting phase.
  • Can hair loss be reversed? It depends on the cause of hair loss. Some types of hair loss can be reversed with proper treatment, while others are permanent.
  • What should I do if I'm experiencing hair loss? Visit a dermatologist or a trichologist to determine the cause of your hair loss and get a proper diagnosis.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair:

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and soft.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as this can dry out your scalp and hair.
  • Let your hair air dry as much as possible, and avoid using hot tools like blow dryers.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from chlorine and saltwater by wearing a swim cap or rinsing your hair with fresh water after swimming.

In conclusion, it is normal to lose some hair every day, but if you're experiencing excessive hair loss when pulling, it could be a sign of hair loss. Visit a dermatologist or a trichologist to determine the cause of your hair loss and get a proper diagnosis. Follow the step-by-step guide and tips to prevent hair loss and take care of your hair for healthy and shiny locks.
