How To Cut Men s Hair Sides And Back

Looking for a way to save money on haircuts? Learning how to cut men's hair sides and back is a great way to do that. With the right tools and techniques, you can give a great haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will cover everything you need to know to get started.

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Description

Before we dive into the specifics, it's important to understand what we mean by "cutting men's hair sides and back". This refers to trimming the hair on the sides and back of the head, often using clippers to achieve a short, even length. This is a popular style for men and is often referred to as a "buzz cut" or "crew cut".

How to

So, how do you cut men's hair sides and back? Here are the basic steps:

1. Gather your tools

You will need a good pair of clippers, a comb, and scissors. You may also want to invest in hair-thinning shears if you want to create a more textured look.

2. Wash and dry the hair

Make sure the hair is clean and dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly.

3. Determine the length

Decide how short you want the hair to be. This will depend on personal preference and the style you are going for. Use the clippers to trim the hair to the desired length, starting at the sides and working your way around the back of the head.

4. Blend the hair

Use the scissors or hair-thinning shears to blend the hair between the sides and top of the head. This will create a more natural look and prevent any harsh lines.

5. Trim the neckline

Use the clippers or scissors to trim the hair at the neckline. You can create a straight line or a more rounded shape, depending on the desired look.

Step by step

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide:

1. Prepare the hair

Wash and dry the hair. Comb it straight and make sure there are no tangles.

2. Determine the length

Decide on the length you want for the sides and back of the hair. Use the clippers to trim the hair to that length, starting at the sides and working your way around the back of the head. Use a higher guard for longer hair and a lower guard for shorter hair.

3. Blend the hair

Use the scissors or hair-thinning shears to blend the hair between the sides and top of the head. This will create a more natural look and prevent any harsh lines. Use the comb as a guide and cut small sections at a time.

4. Trim the neckline

Use the clippers or scissors to trim the hair at the neckline. You can create a straight line or a more rounded shape, depending on the desired look.

5. Check for evenness

Use the comb to check for any uneven spots or long hairs that may have been missed. Make any necessary adjustments.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Invest in quality tools. Cheap clippers or scissors can lead to uneven cuts and frustration.
  • Start with a higher guard and work your way down to achieve the desired length.
  • Use the comb as a guide to help you cut evenly.
  • Take your time and make small adjustments as you go.
  • Practice makes perfect. Don't get discouraged if your first attempt isn't perfect.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: This will depend on how quickly your hair grows and the desired length. Generally, men with shorter hair will need to cut their hair more frequently than those with longer hair.

Q: Can I cut my own hair?

A: Yes, with the right tools and techniques, you can cut your own hair. Just be patient and take your time.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: Don't worry, hair grows back! If you make a mistake, try to fix it as best you can or wait for it to grow out.

Hair Care

After you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too many styling products. Regular trims will also help keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Cutting men's hair sides and back may seem intimidating at first, but with practice and patience, you can achieve great results. Remember to use the right tools, take your time, and follow these tips and techniques for the best outcome.
