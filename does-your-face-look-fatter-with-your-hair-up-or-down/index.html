Does Your Face Look Fatter With Your Hair Up Or Down

Written by Oliver Aug 18, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to hairstyles, one of the most common questions people ask is "does your face look fatter with your hair up or down?" The truth is, it really depends on a number of factors. In this article, we'll explore the answer to this question and provide some helpful tips for choosing the right hairstyle for your face shape.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description:

Many people believe that wearing your hair up makes your face look slimmer, while wearing it down makes your face look wider or "fatter". However, this is not always the case. The way your hair looks can depend on a variety of factors, such as your face shape, hair type, and the style of your hair.

How To:

The first step in determining whether your face looks fatter with your hair up or down is to identify your face shape. There are several different face shapes, including round, oval, square, heart-shaped, and diamond-shaped. Once you know your face shape, you can choose a hairstyle that flatters your features.

Step By Step:

Here are some general tips for choosing a hairstyle based on your face shape:

  • Round Face: If you have a round face, you'll want to choose a hairstyle that adds height and lengthens your face. A high ponytail or top knot can achieve this look.
  • Oval Face: If you have an oval face, you're in luck! This face shape is versatile and can wear many different hairstyles. Experiment with different lengths and styles to find what works best for you.
  • Square Face: If you have a square face, you'll want to soften your features with a style that has a lot of movement. Loose waves or curls can achieve this look.
  • Heart-Shaped Face: If you have a heart-shaped face, you'll want to balance your features by adding volume to the lower half of your face. A low bun or side ponytail can achieve this look.
  • Diamond-Shaped Face: If you have a diamond-shaped face, you'll want to create the illusion of width in your forehead and jawline. A half-up, half-down style or a side-swept bang can achieve this look.

Tips:

Here are some additional tips for choosing the right hairstyle:

  • Consider your hair type: Your hair type can also affect how your face looks. For example, if you have fine hair, a sleek ponytail may make your face look thinner. If you have thick hair, a messy updo can add volume and balance to your features.
  • Experiment with different styles: Don't be afraid to try different hairstyles to find what works best for you. You may be surprised at what flatters your features!
  • Use accessories: Hair accessories like headbands, clips, and scarves can also help balance your features and add some style to your look.

FAQ:

Here are some common questions people ask about hairstyles and face shape:

  • Can bangs make your face look slimmer? Yes, bangs can help balance your features and make your face look slimmer. However, the type of bangs you choose will depend on your face shape.
  • What if I have a combination of face shapes? If you have a combination of face shapes, focus on the dominant features of your face. For example, if you have a heart-shaped face with a strong jawline, you may want to choose a hairstyle that balances your jawline.
  • Can hair color affect how my face looks? Yes, hair color can also affect how your face looks. Lighter hair colors can make your face look softer, while darker colors can make your features more pronounced.

Hair Care:

Lastly, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure it looks its best. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly: Depending on your hair type, you may need to wash your hair every day or every other day. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair. Try to limit your use of these tools and use a heat protectant spray when you do use them.
  • Get regular trims: Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, whether your face looks fatter with your hair up or down really depends on your face shape, hair type, and the style of your hair. By following the tips in this article, you can choose a hairstyle that flatters your features and makes you feel confident.
