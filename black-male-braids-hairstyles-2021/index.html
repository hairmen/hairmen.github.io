Black Male Braids Hairstyles 2021 A Guide

If you are looking for a new hairstyle that is both stylish and practical, then you might want to consider black male braids hairstyles. Braids have been a popular hairstyle for centuries, and they have been worn by men and women of all ages and cultures. In 2021, black male braids hairstyles are more popular than ever, and there are countless styles to choose from. In this article, we'll take a look at some of the most popular black male braids hairstyles, how to create them, and how to take care of your hair.

  • How to Create Black Male Braids Hairstyles
  • Step by Step Guide

How to Create Black Male Braids Hairstyles

Before we dive into the different styles, let's talk about how to create black male braids hairstyles. The first step is to make sure your hair is clean and moisturized. You can use a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically designed for braided hair, or you can use a regular moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Once your hair is clean and moisturized, you'll want to brush it out to make sure there are no tangles or knots.

Next, you'll need to decide on the size and shape of your braids. Some popular styles include box braids, cornrows, and twists. Box braids are thicker braids that are created using synthetic hair extensions. Cornrows are small, tight braids that are created by weaving the hair close to the scalp. Twists are similar to braids, but they are created by twisting two strands of hair together instead of braiding them.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide on how to create black male braids hairstyles:

  1. Start by sectioning your hair. Use a comb to create a part down the middle of your head, then create two more parts on each side of the middle part. You should end up with four sections of hair.
  2. Take one of the sections and divide it into three smaller sections. Begin braiding the hair by crossing the right section over the middle section, then crossing the left section over the new middle section. Continue braiding until you reach the end of the hair.
  3. Repeat step two on the remaining sections of hair.
  4. Once all of your hair is braided, you can style it in different ways. You can wear your braids down, or you can pull them back into a ponytail or bun.

Tips for Maintaining Your Braids

Now that you know how to create black male braids hairstyles, let's talk about how to take care of them. Here are some tips:

  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent breakage and frizz.
  • Avoid using heavy products on your hair, as this can weigh down your braids and make them look dull.
  • Don't leave your braids in for too long. It's recommended that you take them out after 6-8 weeks to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Wash your braids regularly to keep them clean and fresh.

FAQs About Black Male Braids Hairstyles

Q: Can I get braids if I have short hair?

A: Yes, you can still get braids if you have short hair. However, the length of your hair will determine the size and shape of your braids.

Q: How long does it take to create black male braids hairstyles?

A: The time it takes to create black male braids hairstyles will depend on the length and thickness of your hair, as well as the style you choose. On average, it can take anywhere from 2 to 8 hours to create braids.

Q: Will braids damage my hair?

A: Braids can cause damage to your hair if they are too tight or if you leave them in for too long. It's important to take breaks between braiding sessions and to not leave them in for more than 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care for Braids

Taking care of your hair while it's in braids is important to prevent damage and keep your hair healthy. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your braids regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Don't use heavy products on your hair, as this can weigh down your braids and make them look dull.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as this can cause damage to your hair and your braids.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent breakage and frizz.

With these tips, you can create and maintain black male braids hairstyles that are stylish and healthy for your hair. Remember to take breaks between braiding sessions and to not leave your braids in for too long to prevent damage to your hair.
