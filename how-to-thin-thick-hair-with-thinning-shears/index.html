How To Thin Thick Hair With Thinning Shears

Written by Christine Oct 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thick hair can be difficult to manage, especially when it comes to styling. Many people with thick hair struggle with volume and bulk, making it hard to achieve the look they desire. One solution to this problem is to use thinning shears to reduce the amount of hair and create a more manageable style. In this article, we'll go over how to thin thick hair with thinning shears, step by step.

Description

Thinning shears are a type of scissors with teeth on one blade that are designed to remove hair without creating a blunt edge. This makes them ideal for thinning out thick hair, as they remove some of the bulk while maintaining a natural look. Thinning shears come in different sizes and styles, so it's important to choose the right one for your hair type and desired style.

How to Thin Thick Hair with Thinning Shears

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before using thinning shears, it's important to wash and dry your hair thoroughly. This will help you see the natural texture and thickness of your hair and determine which areas need thinning. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for your hair type and rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, use a comb to section it off into manageable sections. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any areas. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward, sectioning off small sections of hair at a time.

Step 3: Hold the Thinning Shears Correctly

Hold the thinning shears with the teeth pointing up and the smooth blade pointing down. This will ensure that you cut the hair evenly and don't create any choppy or uneven edges.

Step 4: Thin Out the Hair

Starting at the bottom of each section, use the thinning shears to remove small sections of hair. Work in a zigzag pattern, taking small sections of hair at a time. Be careful not to take too much hair at once, as this can create an uneven look.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you've thinned out each section, check your progress in a mirror. Make sure that the hair is evenly thinned out and that there are no choppy edges or uneven sections. If necessary, go back and thin out any areas that need it.

Tips for Thinning Thick Hair with Thinning Shears

  • Choose the right size and style of thinning shears for your hair type and desired style.
  • Take your time and work in small sections to ensure an even look.
  • Don't take too much hair at once, as this can create an uneven look.
  • Check your progress frequently in a mirror to ensure that the hair is evenly thinned out.

FAQ

Can thinning shears damage my hair?

If used correctly, thinning shears should not damage your hair. However, if you use them too often or take too much hair at once, you may create split ends or damage the hair cuticle.

How often should I thin out my hair with thinning shears?

This depends on your hair type and desired style. If you have very thick hair, you may need to thin it out every few months. If you have finer hair, you may only need to thin it out once or twice a year.

Hair Care after Thinning with Thinning Shears

After thinning out your hair with thinning shears, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and appearance. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for your hair type and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims can also help maintain a healthy and even appearance.

Thinning out thick hair with thinning shears can be a great way to achieve a more manageable style. By following these steps and tips, you can create a natural and even look while maintaining the health of your hair.
