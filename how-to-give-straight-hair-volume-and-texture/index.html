How To Give Straight Hair Volume And Texture

Written by William Oct 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Straight hair is often considered sleek and chic, but sometimes it can fall flat and lack volume or texture. Fortunately, there are several ways to add volume and texture to straight hair without having to use a curling iron or other heat styling tools. In this article, we'll explore how to give straight hair volume and texture, and provide tips and tricks for maintaining these styles.

Table of Contents

  • How to Give Straight Hair Volume and Texture
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs

How to Give Straight Hair Volume and Texture

There are several different techniques and products that can be used to give straight hair volume and texture. Some of the most effective methods include:

1. Teasing or Backcombing

Teasing or backcombing involves using a fine-tooth comb to comb hair backwards towards the roots, creating a teased effect. This technique can be used to add volume to the crown of the head, or to create a textured look throughout the hair.

2. Using a Volumizing Mousse

Volumizing mousse is a styling product that is designed to add volume and body to hair. Apply a golf ball-sized amount of volumizing mousse to damp hair, focusing on the roots and working your way down to the ends. Blow dry hair upside down to create even more volume.

3. Braiding or Twisting

Braiding or twisting hair while it's damp can create texture and waves. Leave the braids or twists in overnight, then remove them in the morning to reveal textured hair. For a more natural-looking wave, use larger sections of hair when braiding or twisting.

4. Using Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo is a powder or spray that can be applied to the roots of hair to absorb excess oil and add volume. Spray dry shampoo onto the roots of hair, then massage it in with your fingers. Brush hair to distribute the product evenly.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to giving straight hair volume and texture:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Begin with freshly washed and dried hair. Hair that is weighed down with oil or product will be more difficult to style.

Step 2: Apply Volumizing Mousse

Apply a golf ball-sized amount of volumizing mousse to damp hair, focusing on the roots and working your way down to the ends.

Step 3: Blow Dry Upside Down

Flip your head upside down and blow dry hair, using a round brush to lift hair at the roots and create volume.

Step 4: Tease or Backcomb

Use a fine-tooth comb to tease or backcomb hair at the crown of the head to create volume. You can also tease hair throughout to create a textured look.

Step 5: Braid or Twist

Braid or twist hair while it's damp to create texture and waves. Leave the braids or twists in overnight, then remove them in the morning to reveal textured hair.

Step 6: Apply Dry Shampoo

Apply dry shampoo to the roots of hair to absorb excess oil and add volume. Massage in with your fingers, then brush hair to distribute the product evenly.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks for giving straight hair volume and texture:

1. Use the Right Products

Choose volumizing products that are designed specifically for your hair type. Avoid heavy, oily products that can weigh hair down.

2. Don't Overdo It

Be careful not to over-tease or over-backcomb hair, as this can cause damage and breakage. Use a gentle touch and go slowly.

3. Experiment with Different Techniques

Try different techniques, such as braiding, twisting, or using different styling products, to find the best method for your hair type and texture.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people may need to wash their hair every day, while others can go several days without washing. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Q: Can I use heat styling tools to add volume and texture to straight hair?

A: While heat styling tools can be effective for creating waves and curls, they can also cause damage to hair. Try using non-heat styling techniques first, such as braiding or twisting, before resorting to heat styling.

Hair Care for Straight Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, voluminous straight hair:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that are gentle and moisturizing, to avoid stripping hair of its natural oils.

2. Avoid Over-washing

Washing hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and brittle. Try washing hair every other day, or even every few days, to maintain healthy hair.

3. Protect Hair from Heat Damage

When using heat styling tools, always use a heat protectant spray or serum to prevent damage to hair.

By following these tips and techniques, you can give straight hair the volume and texture it needs to look healthy and stylish.
