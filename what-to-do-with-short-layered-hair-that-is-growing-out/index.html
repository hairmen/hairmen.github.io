What To Do With Short Layered Hair That Is Growing Out

Written by Christine Sep 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short layered hair that is growing out, it can be a challenge to style it in a way that looks good and feels comfortable. However, with the right approach and a few tips, you can make the most of your hair and rock any look you want.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips

Description

Short layered hair can be a great way to add texture and volume to your hair, but when it starts to grow out, it can be a challenge to style it. The key to managing short layered hair that is growing out is to find a style that works for your hair type and texture. Whether you have fine or thick hair, curly or straight hair, there are plenty of options to choose from.

How to

When it comes to styling short layered hair that is growing out, the first step is to find a good stylist who can help you create a plan for your hair. This may include trimming the ends to help even out the layers or adding in some longer layers to create a more natural look.

Once you have a plan in place, you can start experimenting with different styles. One option is to add in some waves or curls to your hair to give it more volume and texture. This can be done using a curling iron or hot rollers, or you can try a more natural approach by braiding your hair overnight and letting it air dry in the morning.

If you prefer a sleeker look, you can try straightening your hair with a flat iron or blow dryer. This can help to smooth out any frizz or flyaways and create a more polished look. You can also experiment with different parts, such as a side part or a middle part, to find a style that works best for you.

Step by Step

To style short layered hair that is growing out, follow these steps:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Use a towel to gently dry your hair, being careful not to rub it too hard.
  3. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair to prevent damage from styling tools.
  4. Use a curling iron or hot rollers to add waves or curls to your hair, or use a flat iron or blow dryer to straighten it.
  5. Finish with a light hold hairspray to help your style last all day.

Tips

To make the most of your short layered hair that is growing out, try these tips:

  • Invest in good quality styling tools, such as a high-quality flat iron or curling iron, to help create the best possible look.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Experiment with different products, such as mousse or texturizing spray, to add volume and texture to your hair.
  • Try different parting styles to find the one that works best for your face shape.

FAQ

Q. Can I still wear my hair in a bob if it's growing out?

A. Yes, you can still wear your hair in a bob even if it's growing out. To make the most of your bob, try adding in some layers to create more texture and movement.

Q. Should I use a heat protectant spray when styling my hair?

A. Yes, it's important to use a heat protectant spray or serum when styling your hair with heat tools to prevent damage and breakage.

Q. Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail if it's growing out?

A. Yes, you can still wear your hair in a ponytail if it's growing out. To make the most of your ponytail, try using a hair tie that won't damage your hair, such as a scrunchie.

Hair Care

To keep your short layered hair that is growing out looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. This includes:

  • Using a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for your hair type
  • Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Trimming your hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can make the most of your short layered hair that is growing out and create a style that looks great and feels comfortable.
