What Is A Fade Vs Taper

Description

If you're someone who loves to experiment with different hairstyles, chances are you've come across the terms "fade" and "taper" at some point. While both of these styles involve gradually cutting the hair shorter, there are some key differences between the two.

A fade haircut involves cutting the hair very short on the sides and back of the head, gradually blending into longer hair on top. This creates a faded effect that looks modern and stylish.

A taper haircut, on the other hand, involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back of the head, but gradually leaving more length towards the top. The hair is typically blended using scissors or clippers, resulting in a more gradual transition than a fade.

How to

If you're interested in getting a fade or taper haircut, it's important to find a skilled barber who can help you achieve the look you want. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Do your research to find a barber with experience cutting fades and tapers.
  • Show your barber a picture of the style you're interested in to ensure they understand your vision.
  • Be willing to communicate with your barber throughout the cutting process to ensure you're happy with the results.

Step by step

If you're curious about how a fade or taper haircut is achieved, here's a general overview of the process:

  1. The barber will start by cutting the hair short on the sides and back of the head.
  2. They will then gradually blend the hair using scissors or clippers to create a tapered effect.
  3. If you're getting a fade, the barber will continue to gradually cut the hair shorter towards the bottom, resulting in a faded effect.
  4. The hair on top of the head is typically left longer and can be styled in a variety of ways.

Tips

If you're considering a fade or taper haircut, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Be sure to consider your face shape and hair texture when choosing a style.
  • Ask your barber for styling tips to ensure you can maintain your new look at home.
  • Consider using hair products like pomade or styling cream to keep your hair looking its best.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fade and taper haircuts:

How often do I need to get a fade or taper?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how often you want to maintain your style. Generally, most people get their hair cut every 4-6 weeks.

Can I get a fade or taper with curly hair?

Absolutely! Your barber can tailor the cut to your hair texture to ensure the best results.

What's the difference between a low fade and a high fade?

A low fade involves cutting the hair short around the ears and nape of the neck, gradually blending into longer hair towards the top. A high fade involves cutting the hair very short around the temples and leaving more length towards the top.

Hair Care

Whether you opt for a fade or taper haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair to prevent damage.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.

By following these tips and working with a skilled barber, you can achieve the perfect fade or taper haircut that flatters your face shape and complements your personal style.
