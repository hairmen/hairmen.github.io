Can You Revive A Synthetic Wig Tips Tricks And Faqs

Written by Oliver Nov 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who wears wigs regularly or even occasionally, you might have faced the problem of a synthetic wig losing its luster and becoming dull and lifeless over time. The good news is that you don't always have to replace your wig when this happens. In this article, we'll explore whether or not you can revive a synthetic wig and share tips, tricks, and FAQs to help you do so.

How to Revive a Synthetic Wig

Before we dive into the details, it's important to note that synthetic wigs have a lifespan of around 4-6 months with regular wear and proper care. After this time, the fibers start to break down, and the wig loses its original shape and style. However, with the right care and maintenance, you can extend the life of your wig and revive it when it starts to look dull and lifeless.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to reviving your synthetic wig:

  1. Brush the wig gently with a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Fill a sink or basin with cold water and add a tablespoon of wig shampoo.
  3. Place the wig in the water and swirl it around gently for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Rinse the wig thoroughly with cold water until all the shampoo is removed.
  5. Squeeze out the excess water gently with a towel.
  6. Spray a leave-in conditioner on the wig and comb it through gently with a wide-tooth comb.
  7. Hang the wig on a wig stand or mannequin head and allow it to air dry completely.
  8. Once the wig is dry, use a heat protectant spray and a low-heat setting on a flat iron or curling iron to style it as desired.

Following this routine every 2-3 weeks can help keep your wig looking fresh and new for longer.

Tips for Reviving a Synthetic Wig

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when reviving your synthetic wig:

  • Always use cold water when washing your wig. Hot water can damage the fibers and cause the wig to lose its shape.
  • Choose a wig shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for synthetic wigs. Regular hair products can damage the fibers and cause them to frizz or tangle.
  • Don't rub or twist your wig when washing or drying it. This can cause the fibers to break or become tangled.
  • Invest in a wig stand or mannequin head to help your wig maintain its shape when drying.
  • Avoid using high heat on your synthetic wig. This can melt the fibers and cause irreparable damage.

FAQs

Can You Use Heat on a Synthetic Wig?

Yes, you can use heat on a synthetic wig, but it's important to use a low-heat setting and a heat protectant spray to avoid damaging the fibers. You should also avoid using hot tools on curly or wavy synthetic wigs, as this can cause the waves or curls to fall out.

Can You Dye a Synthetic Wig?

No, you cannot dye a synthetic wig. Synthetic fibers are made from a type of plastic and cannot be dyed with regular hair dye. If you want a different color wig, you'll need to purchase a new one.

How Often Should You Wash a Synthetic Wig?

You should wash your synthetic wig every 2-3 weeks with a wig shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for synthetic wigs. Over-washing can cause the fibers to break down faster, so it's important to stick to a regular washing schedule.

Hair Care for Synthetic Wigs

In addition to following the steps above to revive your synthetic wig, here are a few hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to help it maintain its shape.
  • Avoid exposing your wig to direct sunlight or high heat, as this can cause the fibers to melt or frizz.
  • Use a wig cap to protect your natural hair and scalp from the wig's materials.
  • Brush your wig gently with a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to avoid tangles or knots.

By following these tips and tricks, you can extend the life of your synthetic wig and keep it looking fresh and new for longer. Remember to always use cold water, a wig-specific shampoo and conditioner, and a low-heat setting when styling your wig to avoid damaging the fibers.
