How To Fix A Non Lace Front Wig

Written by Oliver Oct 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you having trouble with your non lace front wig? Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to fix your non lace front wig. If you follow these tips, you can have your wig looking as good as new in no time.

Description

A non lace front wig is a wig that does not have a lace closure or frontal. It is typically made with a thicker material and can be more difficult to style. If your non lace front wig is looking a little rough around the edges, there are a few things you can do to fix it.

How to Fix a Non Lace Front Wig

Step 1: Detangle

Before you start fixing your wig, you need to detangle it. Use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to gently remove any knots or tangles. Work from the ends of the hair up to the roots to avoid damaging the wig.

Step 2: Wash and Condition

Once your wig is detangled, it's time to wash and condition it. Fill a sink or basin with cool water and add a small amount of wig shampoo. Gently swish the wig in the water for a few minutes, then rinse it thoroughly with cool water. Apply a wig conditioner and let it sit for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Air Dry

After washing and conditioning, gently squeeze out any excess water from the wig. Do not rub or twist the hair, as this can cause damage. Place the wig on a wig stand or mannequin head and allow it to air dry. Do not use a blow dryer or other heat source to dry the wig, as this can damage the fibers.

Step 4: Style

Once your wig is dry, it's time to style it. You can use a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to style the hair. If you want to add some volume, you can use a hair dryer on the cool setting to fluff up the hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you fix your non lace front wig:

  • Use a wig cap to keep your natural hair from showing through the wig.
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to help maintain its shape.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your wig, as this can damage the fibers.
  • If your wig is looking dull, you can use a wig spray to add shine.

FAQ

Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my wig?

No, you should use a wig shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for wigs. Regular shampoo and conditioner can damage the fibers and shorten the lifespan of your wig.

How often should I wash my wig?

This depends on how often you wear your wig and how much product you use. As a general rule, you should wash your wig every 6-8 wears or when it starts to look dirty or oily.

Can I color my non lace front wig?

We do not recommend coloring your non lace front wig, as this can damage the fibers and alter the texture of the hair. If you want a different color, consider purchasing a wig in the color you desire.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to maintaining the quality and longevity of your non lace front wig. Here are some tips to keep your wig looking its best:

  • Avoid exposing your wig to direct sunlight for extended periods of time, as this can cause the color to fade.
  • Do not sleep in your wig, as this can cause tangles and damage the fibers.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out the hair and cause it to become brittle.
  • Store your wig in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat sources.

By following these tips and steps, you can easily fix your non lace front wig and keep it looking its best for years to come.
