Can Stress And Anxiety Make Your Hair Fall Out

Written by Petter Jun 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our hair care blog where we will discuss a topic that many people are worried about: can stress and anxiety make your hair fall out? Hair loss can be a distressing issue for anyone, but it is more common than you might think. In this article, we will discuss the relationship between stress and hair loss, how to prevent it, and how to take care of your hair.

Table of Contents

  • What is stress-related hair loss?
  • How does stress cause hair loss?
  • Can anxiety cause hair loss?

What is stress-related hair loss?

Stress-related hair loss is a type of hair loss that is caused by physical or emotional stress. It can be temporary or permanent, depending on the underlying cause. Stress-related hair loss is also known as telogen effluvium, which is a condition that causes hair follicles to enter the resting phase prematurely. As a result, the hair falls out easily, and new hair growth is delayed.

How does stress cause hair loss?

Stress can cause hair loss in several ways. One of the most common ways is through the production of cortisol, a stress hormone. When cortisol levels are high, it can disrupt the normal hair growth cycle and cause hair follicles to enter the resting phase prematurely. Stress can also cause inflammation, which can damage hair follicles and lead to hair loss. In addition, stress can also affect the immune system, which can trigger autoimmune conditions like alopecia areata, a type of hair loss that causes bald patches on the scalp.

Can anxiety cause hair loss?

Yes, anxiety can cause hair loss. Anxiety is a type of stress that can trigger the production of cortisol and other stress hormones. It can also affect the immune system, which can lead to autoimmune conditions like alopecia areata. In addition, anxiety can also cause trichotillomania, a condition where a person pulls out their hair due to anxiety or stress.

How to prevent stress-related hair loss

The best way to prevent stress-related hair loss is to manage your stress levels. Here are some tips:

  • Practice relaxation techniques like yoga or meditation
  • Exercise regularly
  • Eat a healthy diet
  • Get enough sleep
  • Avoid excessive caffeine and alcohol

Hair care tips for stress-related hair loss

If you are experiencing stress-related hair loss, here are some hair care tips:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Gently massage your scalp to improve circulation
  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Consider using hair growth products like minoxidil

FAQs about stress-related hair loss

Q: Is stress-related hair loss permanent?

A: Stress-related hair loss can be temporary or permanent, depending on the underlying cause. In most cases, it is temporary and will resolve on its own once the stressor is removed.

Q: Can stress-related hair loss be reversed?

A: Yes, stress-related hair loss can be reversed. Once the underlying cause is addressed, hair growth will resume.

Q: How long does stress-related hair loss last?

A: Stress-related hair loss can last for several months, but it usually resolves within six months to a year.

Q: Can stress-related hair loss be prevented?

A: Yes, stress-related hair loss can be prevented by managing your stress levels and taking care of your hair.

In conclusion, stress and anxiety can make your hair fall out, but it is usually temporary and can be reversed with proper care and management. By following the tips in this article, you can prevent stress-related hair loss and keep your hair healthy and strong. If you are experiencing hair loss, it is important to talk to your doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions.
