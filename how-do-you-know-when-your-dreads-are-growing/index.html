How Do You Know When Your Dreads Are Growing

Written by Petter Jul 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you one of the many people who have chosen to embrace the beauty and uniqueness of dreadlocks? If so, you may be curious about how to tell when your dreads are growing. As your hair transforms into dreadlocks, it can be difficult to notice the subtle changes that are happening. In this article, we will explore the different ways to determine if your dreads are growing.

  2. Length
  3. New Growth

Changes in Texture

One of the first signs that your dreads are growing is a change in texture. As your hair begins to lock together, it will become rougher and less flexible. You may notice that your dreads are becoming more defined and less frizzy. This is a good indication that your dreads are maturing and growing in size.

Length

Another way to tell if your dreads are growing is by measuring the length. If you keep track of the length of your dreads over time, you will be able to see how much they have grown. However, it is important to note that the length of your dreads may not be the same as the length of your hair. This is because the hair in your dreads is tightly compressed and may appear shorter than it actually is.

New Growth

New growth is another sign that your dreads are growing. You may notice new hairs starting to grow around the base of your dreads. This is a good indication that your hair is continuing to lock together and grow. However, it is important to maintain your dreads by separating them and keeping them clean to prevent new growth from matting with the existing dreads.

Tightness

If your dreads are becoming tighter and more compact, this is a good indication that they are growing. Tighter dreads are a sign that the hair is becoming more matted and compressed, which is a natural part of the dreadlocking process. However, it is important to avoid over-tightening your dreads as this can cause damage to the hair and scalp.

Thickness

As your dreads grow, they will become thicker and more substantial. You may notice that your dreads are becoming more voluminous and taking up more space on your head. This is a good indication that your dreads are growing and maturing.

FAQs

How fast do dreads grow?

The rate at which your dreads grow can vary depending on a number of factors, including genetics, diet, and hair care. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month, but this can be slower or faster depending on the individual.

Can you speed up the growth of your dreads?

There is no guaranteed way to speed up the growth of your dreads, but there are some things you can do to promote healthy hair growth. Eating a healthy diet, staying hydrated, and getting enough sleep can all help to promote healthy hair growth.

Tips for Promoting Growth

If you want to encourage the growth of your dreads, there are some things you can do to promote healthy hair growth. Here are a few tips:

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Avoid over-washing your hair
  • Massage your scalp to increase blood flow
  • Avoid wearing hats or tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair

Haircare for Healthy Dreads

Maintaining healthy dreads requires proper hair care. Here are some tips for keeping your dreads healthy:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  • Avoid using conditioner on your dreads
  • Use a hair oil or moisturizer to keep your dreads hydrated
  • Separate your dreads regularly to prevent matting
  • Avoid over-tightening your dreads

Now that you know how to tell if your dreads are growing, you can better understand the process of dreadlocking and how to care for your hair. By following these tips and maintaining a healthy hair care routine, you can ensure that your dreads stay healthy, strong, and beautiful.
