How To Start Dreads With Short Curly Hair

Starting dreadlocks with short curly hair can be a bit of a challenge, but it's not impossible. In this article, we'll go through a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with short curly hair, as well as some tips and FAQ's to help you along the way.

How to Start Dreads with Short Curly Hair

One of the most important things to keep in mind when starting dreads with short curly hair is that it takes time and patience. You won't achieve the perfect dreadlocks overnight, but with some dedication and effort, you can get there.

The first thing you'll need to do is decide on the method you want to use to start your dreadlocks. There are several methods to choose from, including backcombing, twisting, and the neglect method. Each method has its pros and cons, so it's important to do your research and choose the one that's right for you.

Once you've decided on the method, you'll need to gather the necessary tools. This may include a comb or brush, hair ties, rubber bands, and possibly some dread wax or gel. You'll also need to have some basic knowledge of how to care for your hair and keep it healthy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with short curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils.
  2. Section your hair into small, even sections using hair ties or rubber bands.
  3. Choose your method (backcombing, twisting, or neglect) and start working on each section of hair.
  4. If using the backcombing method, use a comb to backcomb each section of hair until it begins to knot up and form a dread.
  5. If using the twisting method, twist each section of hair until it begins to form a dread.
  6. If using the neglect method, simply stop combing or brushing your hair and let it naturally form into dreads over time.
  7. Once your hair has begun to form dreads, use dread wax or gel to help hold them in place and encourage further knotting.
  8. Continue to maintain your dreads by washing them regularly, using a residue-free shampoo and avoiding heavy products that can weigh them down.
  9. Over time, your dreads will mature and become tighter and more defined.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Short Curly Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when starting dreads with short curly hair:

  • Be patient - it takes time for dreads to form and mature.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup in your hair.
  • Keep your hair moisturized with natural oils like coconut or jojoba oil.
  • Avoid heavy products that can weigh down your dreads.
  • Consider getting a professional to help you start your dreads if you're unsure.

FAQ's

Here are some common FAQ's about starting dreads with short curly hair:

  • Can I start dreads with short curly hair?

    • Yes, you can start dreads with short curly hair.

  • What method is best for starting dreads with short curly hair?

    • The best method for starting dreads with short curly hair depends on your personal preference. Backcombing and twisting are both popular methods, but the neglect method can also work well for some people.

  • How often should I wash my dreads?

    • You should wash your dreads about once a week or as needed, using a residue-free shampoo.

Hair Care for Dreads with Short Curly Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair while wearing dreadlocks:

  • Wash your dreads regularly, using a residue-free shampoo.
  • Keep your scalp moisturized with natural oils.
  • Avoid heavy products that can weigh down your dreads.
  • Be gentle when washing and drying your dreads to avoid damage.
  • Consider using a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.

Starting dreads with short curly hair may take some time and effort, but it's a rewarding process that can lead to beautiful, unique dreadlocks. With the right tools, knowledge, and patience, you can achieve the perfect set of dreads.
