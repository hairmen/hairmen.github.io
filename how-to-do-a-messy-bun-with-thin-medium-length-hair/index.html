How To Do A Messy Bun With Thin Medium Length Hair

Written by Aprilia Jul 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle? Do you want to try something new and trendy? Look no further than the messy bun! This effortless and chic hairstyle is perfect for any occasion, from a casual day out to a fancy night on the town. And the best part? It's easy to achieve, even if you have thin medium length hair. Follow these simple steps to master the messy bun.

Description

A messy bun is a hairstyle that can be achieved by pulling your hair back and securing it with a hair tie. The key to this style is to create a loose, messy look by leaving some hair out and teasing the hair that is pulled back. This style is perfect for those with thin medium length hair who want to add volume and texture to their hair.

How to

1. Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles or knots.

2. Apply some texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your hair.

3. Gather your hair into a high ponytail, leaving some hair out around your face.

4. Twist your ponytail and wrap it around the hair tie, securing it with bobby pins.

5. Pull out some pieces of hair around your face to create a messy, effortless look.

6. Tease the hair that is pulled back to add even more volume and texture.

7. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your messy bun in place.

Step by Step

Step 1

Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to create your messy bun.

Step 2

Apply some texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your hair. This will make it easier to tease your hair and create a messy look.

Step 3

Gather your hair into a high ponytail, leaving some hair out around your face. Use a hair tie to secure your ponytail in place.

Step 4

Twist your ponytail and wrap it around the hair tie, securing it with bobby pins. This will create the bun shape.

Step 5

Pull out some pieces of hair around your face to create a messy, effortless look. Use your fingers to gently pull on the pieces of hair to create more volume.

Step 6

Tease the hair that is pulled back to add even more volume and texture. Use a fine-tooth comb to gently tease the hair, starting at the roots and working your way down.

Step 7

Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your messy bun in place. Spray the hairspray over your entire head, focusing on the areas where you teased your hair.

Tips

- Use bobby pins that match your hair color to conceal them in your messy bun.

- Don't worry if your messy bun isn't perfect. The beauty of this style is in its imperfection.

- Experiment with different textures and products to find what works best for your hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I do a messy bun with short hair?

A: It may be more difficult to achieve a messy bun with short hair, but it's still possible. Try using a hair donut or hair extensions to add length and volume to your hair.

Q: Can I wear a messy bun to a formal event?

A: Absolutely! A messy bun can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. Add some statement earrings or a bold lip color to make your messy bun look more formal.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair to ensure it looks its best. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

- Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.

- Avoid using too much heat on your hair.

- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

- Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or peroxide.

Now that you know how to do a messy bun with thin medium length hair, go ahead and give it a try! This trendy and effortless hairstyle is perfect for any occasion and is sure to turn heads.
