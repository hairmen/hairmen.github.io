How To Keep My Short Hair Out Of My Face A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Short hair can be both stylish and practical, but it can also be a hassle when it constantly falls into your face. Whether you're working out, running errands, or simply trying to look your best, having hair in your face can be quite frustrating. In this article, we'll provide you with step-by-step instructions and tips on how to keep your short hair out of your face.

Table of Contents

  • How to Keep Your Short Hair Out of Your Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Keep Your Short Hair Out of Your Face

Before we dive into the specifics, let's take a look at some general tips on how to keep your short hair out of your face:

  • Use hair accessories like headbands, clips, and bobby pins
  • Consider getting a haircut that's specifically designed to keep hair out of your face
  • Use hair products like hairspray, gel, or mousse to keep your hair in place
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for you

Step-by-Step Guide

Now let's take a closer look at how to keep your short hair out of your face with our step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Determine the Best Hair Accessory for Your Hair Type

The first step in keeping your short hair out of your face is to determine which hair accessory works best for your hair type. Some people find that headbands work well, while others prefer clips or bobby pins. Experiment with different options to find what works best for you.

Step 2: Choose the Right Size and Style

Once you've determined which hair accessory works best for your hair type, it's important to choose the right size and style. Headbands come in a variety of widths and materials, while clips and bobby pins come in different sizes and colors. Choose an accessory that complements your hair color and style.

Step 3: Position the Hair Accessory

Once you've chosen the right hair accessory, it's time to position it properly. If you're using a headband, place it at the crown of your head and pull your hair back. If you're using clips or bobby pins, take small sections of hair and secure them at the sides of your head or at the back.

Step 4: Use Hair Products

If you're still having trouble keeping your short hair out of your face, consider using hair products like hairspray, gel, or mousse. These products can help keep your hair in place and prevent it from falling into your face. Be sure to choose a product that works well with your hair type and style.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for keeping your short hair out of your face:

  • Wear a hat or scarf to keep your hair in place
  • Use a hair tie to create a small ponytail at the back of your head
  • Try a messy bun or top knot
  • Experiment with different hair textures and lengths to find what works best for you

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I wash my hair to keep it from falling into my face?

A: It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to keep it clean and healthy. However, if you're having trouble keeping your short hair out of your face, you may want to consider washing it more frequently to prevent it from becoming too oily or greasy.

Q: Can I use hair accessories on wet hair?

A: Yes, you can use hair accessories on wet hair, but it's important to be gentle to avoid damaging your hair. Consider using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair before using a hair accessory.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, here are some general hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors
  • Eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated to keep your hair healthy from the inside out

By following these tips and tricks, you should be well on your way to keeping your short hair out of your face. Remember, the key is to experiment with different hairstyles and accessories until you find what works best for you.
