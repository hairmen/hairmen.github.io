How To Cut Mullet Fringe A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a bold and edgy hairstyle, the mullet fringe might just be the look for you. This unconventional cut features short hair in the front and longer strands in the back, creating a unique and eye-catching style. But before you grab your scissors, it's important to know how to properly cut mullet fringe to ensure you achieve the desired look. In this blog post, we'll walk you through the steps to cut mullet fringe at home, as well as provide tips for maintaining the style and caring for your hair.

What You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following tools on hand:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into two parts: the front and the back. Use a comb to create a straight part down the middle of your head, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Clip the back section out of the way.

Step 2: Cut the Front

Next, dampen the front section of your hair with a spray bottle. This will make it easier to cut and ensure a more even result. Using your scissors, cut the front section of your hair to your desired length. This can be anywhere from a few inches to just above your eyebrows, depending on your preference. Make sure to cut in a straight line across your forehead.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Release the back section of your hair and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to cut the back section to your desired length, making sure to blend it with the front section. You can either cut the back section straight across or create a slight angle, depending on the look you want.

Step 4: Add Texture

To add texture to your mullet fringe, use your scissors to create choppy layers throughout. This will give your hair a more edgy and layered look.

Tips for Maintaining Mullet Fringe

Now that you've cut your mullet fringe, here are some tips for maintaining the style:

  • Regular trims are essential to keep your fringe looking its best. Aim to trim it every 4-6 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows.
  • Use a texturizing spray or pomade to add definition and hold to your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it dry and brittle.
  • Consider investing in a quality hair mask or conditioner to keep your hair healthy and nourished.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut mullet fringe on curly hair?

Absolutely! Mullet fringe can look great on all hair types, including curly hair. Just make sure to cut it when your hair is dry, as cutting it when it's wet can result in a shorter length once it dries.

Do I need to go to a salon to get mullet fringe?

No, you can easily cut mullet fringe at home with the right tools and technique. However, if you're not confident in your hair cutting skills, it's always a good idea to seek the help of a professional stylist.

How do I style mullet fringe?

Mullet fringe looks great styled in a messy, textured way. Use a texturizing spray or pomade to add definition and hold to your hair, then use your fingers to create a tousled, effortless look.

Hair Care for Mullet Fringe

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep your mullet fringe looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can damage your hair and make it dry and brittle.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can cause damage and breakage.

Cutting mullet fringe is a bold and daring choice that can add a fun and edgy vibe to your look. By following these steps, maintaining your style, and taking good care of your hair, you can rock this unconventional cut with confidence.
