How To Thin Out Thick Straight Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 17, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have thick straight hair, you might feel like it's too heavy and unmanageable. Luckily, there are ways to thin out your hair without losing length or volume. In this article, we will guide you through the process of thinning out your hair, including how to do it yourself and what hair care tips to follow. By the end of this article, you will have a better understanding of how to achieve the perfect hairstyle for your thick straight hair.

Description

Thinning out your hair can make it more manageable and easier to style. It involves removing some of the hair's volume while keeping the length. This process can be done using different techniques, such as razor cutting, thinning shears, or texturizing scissors.

How to Thin Out Thick Straight Hair

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will make it easier to work with and remove any buildup that might be weighing it down.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using clips, divide your hair into sections, starting from the bottom and working your way up. This will help you work on one section at a time and prevent you from cutting too much hair at once.

Step 3: Choose your technique

There are different techniques you can use to thin out your hair, such as razor cutting, thinning shears, or texturizing scissors. Choose the technique that works best for your hair type and desired style.

Step 4: Start cutting

Start by cutting small sections of hair at a time, focusing on the middle and ends of your hair. Use the chosen technique to remove some of the hair's volume while keeping the length. Be careful not to cut too much or create uneven layers.

Step 5: Blend the sections

After cutting each section, use a comb to blend it with the rest of your hair. This will create a natural look and prevent any harsh lines or unevenness.

Step 6: Check your progress

Throughout the process, check your progress in a mirror to ensure that you're achieving the desired look. If you're unsure, it's best to cut less rather than more, as you can always go back and cut more later.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Straight Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair, as it's easier to cut more than to add back what you've cut.
  • Use sharp scissors or blades to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't thin out your hair too often, as it can damage the hair and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Consult a professional if you're unsure about how to thin out your hair or if you're looking for a more drastic change.

FAQs

Can you thin out thick straight hair without losing length?

Yes, you can thin out your hair without losing length. This process involves removing some of the hair's volume while keeping the length, which can be done using different techniques such as razor cutting, thinning shears, or texturizing scissors.

Is it better to thin out thick straight hair when it's wet or dry?

It's better to thin out your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be more stretchy and elastic, making it difficult to cut evenly. Dry hair, on the other hand, is easier to work with and allows for more control.

How often should you thin out thick straight hair?

You should avoid thinning out your hair too often, as it can damage the hair and make it more prone to breakage. It's best to consult a professional to determine how often you should thin out your hair based on your hair type and desired style.

Hair Care for Thinned Out Hair

After thinning out your hair, it's important to follow a hair care routine that promotes healthy hair growth and prevents breakage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's free of sulfates and parabens.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to moisturize and protect the hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Conclusion

Thinning out your thick straight hair can be a great way to achieve a more manageable and stylish look. By following the steps and tips outlined in this article, you can achieve the perfect hairstyle for your hair type and desired style. Remember to consult a professional if you're unsure about how to thin out your hair or if you're looking for a more drastic change.
