How To Cut Front Hair Male A Complete Guide

Written by William Aug 31, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be daunting, especially if you’re new to it. But with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a great haircut without leaving your home. In this article, we’ll teach you how to cut front hair male, step by step. We’ll cover everything from preparation to finishing touches, so you can look and feel your best.

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, it’s important to prepare your tools and your hair. Make sure you have everything you need before you get started. This will save you time and help you avoid mistakes.

Tools You’ll Need

Here are the tools you’ll need to cut your hair:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean. You don’t want to use dull scissors, as they can damage your hair and make your cut uneven. If you don’t have hair clips, you can use regular clips or bobby pins to hold your hair in place.

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

It’s best to cut your hair when it’s clean and dry. This will help you see the shape of your hair and make it easier to cut. So, start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner. Rinse it thoroughly and then towel dry it. Don’t blow dry your hair, as this can make it harder to cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, divide your hair into three sections: the top, the sides, and the back. Clip the top section out of the way, so you can focus on the sides and the back. Use your comb to make a straight part down the middle of your head, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. This will help you create an even cut.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

Start with your bangs. Take a small section of hair from the middle of your forehead and comb it straight down. Hold it between your fingers and cut it straight across. Don’t cut too much at once. You can always trim more later. Repeat this process on both sides, making sure your bangs are even.

Step 4: Blend the Sides

Now it’s time to blend the sides. Take a small section of hair from the side of your head, just above your ear. Comb it straight out from your head and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, from short to long. This will create a natural, blended look. Repeat this process on the other side.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, take a look in the mirror. Check to make sure your bangs are even and your sides are blended. If you see any mistakes, use your scissors to fix them. Don’t be afraid to trim a little more if needed.

Tips for a Great Haircut

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up. This will help you avoid mistakes.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so don’t rush.
  • Use your fingers to measure the length of your hair. This will help you achieve an even cut.
  • Don’t cut your hair too short. You can always trim more later.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you. They can give you a second opinion and help you reach the back of your head.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your own hair. Just make sure you have the right tools and technique.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and style. Generally, it’s best to cut your hair every 6 to 8 weeks.

Should I cut my hair wet or dry?

It’s best to cut your hair when it’s dry. This will help you see the shape of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Hair Care After Your Haircut

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner. Rinse it thoroughly.
  • Avoid using heating tools, such as hair dryers and straighteners, for a few days.
  • Apply a hair mask or oil to nourish your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut.

Now that you know how to cut front hair male, you can achieve a great haircut without leaving your home. Remember to take your time and follow the steps carefully. With practice, you’ll become a pro at cutting your own hair.
