Where In The Bible Does It Say A Man Should Not Cut His Hair

Written by William Nov 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Many people have heard the phrase "a man should not cut his hair" but aren't sure where it comes from. The answer can be found in the Bible, specifically in the book of Numbers. In this article, we'll explore the biblical origins of this phrase and what it means for men today.

What Does the Bible Say?

In the book of Numbers, chapter 6, verses 1-5, it says:

"The Lord said to Moses, 'Speak to the Israelites and say to them: "If a man or woman wants to make a special vow, a vow of dedication to the Lord as a Nazirite, they must abstain from wine and other fermented drink and must not drink vinegar made from wine or other fermented drink. They must not drink grape juice or eat grapes or raisins. As long as they remain under their Nazirite vow, they must not eat anything that comes from the grapevine, not even the seeds or skins.

"'During the entire period of their Nazirite vow, no razor may be used on their head. They must be holy until the period of their dedication to the Lord is over; they must let their hair grow long.'

How to Interpret This Passage

At first glance, this passage may seem confusing or even irrelevant to modern times. However, it's important to understand the context in which it was written. The Nazirite vow was a special commitment that someone could make to God, in which they would dedicate themselves to a period of special holiness. As part of this vow, they would refrain from certain foods and drink, and would not cut their hair.

Today, many people interpret this passage to mean that men should not cut their hair, as a sign of their dedication to God. However, it's important to note that this is not a requirement or expectation for all men. Rather, it's a personal choice that someone can make as part of their own spiritual journey.

Step by Step Guide

If you're interested in following this tradition and not cutting your hair, here are some steps you can take:

  1. Read and understand the passage in Numbers 6:1-5.
  2. Decide if this is a spiritual practice that aligns with your personal beliefs.
  3. If you choose to follow this tradition, commit to growing your hair long and not cutting it.
  4. Consider other aspects of the Nazirite vow, such as abstaining from certain foods and drink.
  5. Seek guidance and support from a spiritual leader or community.

Tips for Growing Long Hair

If you do decide to grow your hair long, here are some tips to help you care for it:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Brush your hair regularly to prevent tangles and breakage.
  • Avoid using heating tools like blow dryers or straighteners, as these can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

FAQs

Is it a sin to cut your hair?

No, it's not a sin to cut your hair. The Bible passage in Numbers 6:1-5 is a specific instruction for those who choose to make a Nazirite vow, but it's not a general rule for all people.

Do women have to follow the same hair rules as men?

No, the Nazirite vow applies to both men and women, but there are no specific rules about hair length or cutting for women.

Can I still style my hair if I don't cut it?

Yes, you can still style your hair while growing it out. There are many hairstyles that work well for long hair, such as braids, buns, and updos.

Hair Care for Men

Whether or not you choose to follow the tradition of not cutting your hair, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking good. Here are some basic hair care tips for men:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a comb or brush to style your hair, and avoid using too much product.
  • If you have a beard, keep it clean and trimmed.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can damage it.

Conclusion

The phrase "a man should not cut his hair" comes from the book of Numbers in the Bible, in which the Nazirite vow is described. While this tradition is still followed by some people today, it's important to understand that it's a personal choice rather than a requirement. Whether or not you choose to grow your hair long, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking good.
