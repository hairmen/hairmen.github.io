Should You Cut Curly Hair Straight Across

Written by Oliver Aug 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair is beautiful, but it can be tricky to maintain. One question that many people with curly hair have is whether they should cut their hair straight across or not. In this article, we'll explore this question and give you tips on how to care for your curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Curly Hair Straight Across
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs

Description

Cutting curly hair straight across can be a controversial topic. Some people swear by it, while others say it's a bad idea. So, what's the truth?

The answer is that it depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with curly hair find that cutting their hair straight across gives them a more even, uniform look. Others prefer to keep their curls natural and avoid any straight cuts.

How to Cut Curly Hair Straight Across

If you decide to cut your curly hair straight across, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, you'll need the right tools. A sharp pair of scissors is a must, as well as a comb or brush to help you section your hair.

Next, you'll want to make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut, and you may end up with uneven results.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to cut curly hair straight across:

  1. Start by sectioning your hair. Use a comb or brush to create a part down the middle of your head, then divide your hair into two sections.
  2. Take one section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers at the length you want to cut it.
  3. Use your scissors to cut straight across the hair. Be careful not to cut too much at once; it's better to take small snips and work your way across the section.
  4. Repeat the process on the other side of your head.
  5. Check your work in the mirror. If you notice any unevenness, you can go back and trim as needed.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting curly hair:

  • Use a mirror to check your work as you go. This will help you catch any mistakes before they become too noticeable.
  • Don't cut your hair too short. Curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so if you cut it too short, you may end up with a much shorter style than you intended.
  • Consider getting a professional haircut. If you're not confident in your cutting skills, it's always a good idea to consult with a professional stylist.

FAQs

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting curly hair straight across:

Q: Will cutting my hair straight across make it less curly?

A: No, cutting your hair straight across won't change its texture. However, it may affect the way your curls lay and how much volume you have.

Q: Is it better to cut curly hair wet or dry?

A: It's generally better to cut curly hair dry, as wet hair can shrink up as it dries and make it difficult to achieve an even cut.

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: This varies depending on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's a good idea to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Curly Hair Care Tips

Cutting your curly hair straight across is just one part of maintaining healthy, beautiful curls. Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Consider using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help enhance your curls.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

In conclusion, cutting curly hair straight across is a personal choice that depends on your hair type and preference. If you do decide to cut your hair this way, be sure to use the right tools and follow our step-by-step guide. And, as always, be sure to take good care of your curls to keep them healthy and beautiful.
