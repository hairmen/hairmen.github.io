How To Style Thinning Curly Hair Tips Tricks And Products

Written by Petter Jun 27, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can be beautiful and unique, it can also be difficult to manage, especially if you have thinning hair. But don't worry, with the right styling techniques and products, you can make your thinning curly hair look voluminous and gorgeous. In this blog post, we'll share some tips, tricks, and products to help you style your thinning curly hair.

How to Style Thin Curly Hair: Step by Step

Styling thin curly hair can be challenging, but with the right approach, you can create a beautiful, voluminous look. Here's a step-by-step guide to styling thin curly hair:

1. Start with clean, conditioned hair

Before you start styling your hair, make sure it is clean and conditioned. Use a gentle shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner to keep your curls hydrated and healthy. Avoid using products that contain sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it dry and brittle.

2. Apply a volumizing mousse to damp hair

To add volume and texture to your hair, apply a volumizing mousse to damp hair. Use a golf ball-sized amount and distribute it evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the roots. This will help lift your curls and create a fuller, more voluminous look.

3. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer

When blow-drying your hair, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. This will help distribute the heat evenly and prevent your curls from getting frizzy. Use a low heat setting and be gentle when drying your hair, as rough handling can cause breakage and damage.

4. Use a curling iron to define your curls

If you want to define your curls further, use a curling iron on low heat. Take small sections of hair and wrap them around the curling iron, holding for a few seconds before releasing. This will create bouncy, defined curls that will last all day.

5. Finish with a hairspray or finishing spray

To keep your curls in place, finish with a hairspray or finishing spray. Choose a lightweight, flexible hold spray that won't weigh your hair down or make it stiff. Spray lightly, holding the can about 10 inches away from your hair.

Tips for Styling Thin Curly Hair

Styling thin curly hair can be a challenge, but with these tips, you can make it easier:

1. Don't over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and make it dry and brittle. Instead, aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or use a dry shampoo in between washings.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush

When detangling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. This will help prevent breakage and damage, and keep your curls defined and bouncy.

3. Avoid using heat styling tools too often

Using heat styling tools too often can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Try to limit your use of curling irons and straighteners, and use a heat protectant spray when you do use them.

4. Choose lightweight, volumizing products

When choosing styling products, look for lightweight, volumizing formulas that won't weigh your hair down. Avoid heavy oils and creams, as these can make your hair look greasy and flat.

5. Experiment with different hairstyles

Don't be afraid to experiment with different hairstyles, such as braids, updos, and half-up, half-down styles. These can add variety to your look and help you hide any areas of thinning hair.

FAQs About Styling Thin Curly Hair

Q: What are the best products for styling thin curly hair?

A: The best products for styling thin curly hair are lightweight, volumizing formulas that won't weigh your hair down. Look for mousses, foams, and sprays that add volume and texture without making your hair greasy or flat.

Q: Can I use a straightener on thin curly hair?

A: Yes, you can use a straightener on thin curly hair, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting to prevent damage. It's best to limit your use of straighteners and other heat styling tools, as they can make your hair more prone to breakage.

Q: How can I make my thin curly hair look thicker?

A: To make your thin curly hair look thicker, try using volumizing products, such as mousses and sprays, and blow-drying your hair with a diffuser attachment. You can also experiment with different hairstyles, such as braids and updos, to add volume and texture to your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Curly Hair

In addition to styling your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some hair care tips for thin curly hair:

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

Choose a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals. These can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it dry and brittle.

2. Deep condition your hair once a week

To keep your hair hydrated and healthy, deep condition it once a week. Use a moisturizing hair mask or oil treatment, and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing.

3. Sleep on a silk pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent breakage and damage to your hair, as it creates less friction than a cotton pillowcase. It can also help prevent frizz and keep your curls defined.

4. Avoid tight hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles, such as ponytails and braids, that can pull on your hair and cause breakage. Instead, opt for loose, gentle hairstyles that won't damage your hair.

5. Eat a healthy diet

A healthy diet can help keep your hair strong and healthy. Eat plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals, and drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

With these tips, tricks, and products, you can style your thinning curly hair to perfection. Experiment with different techniques and products to find what works best for you, and don't be afraid to try new hairstyles and looks. With a little patience and practice, you can create a beautiful, voluminous look that you'll love.
