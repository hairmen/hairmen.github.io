Illnesses That Cause Extreme Hair Loss

Written by Christine Sep 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss at an alarming rate? Hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, stress, and certain medical conditions. In this article, we will focus on illnesses that cause extreme hair loss and provide you with tips on how to manage this condition.

What are Illnesses That Cause Extreme Hair Loss?

There are a number of illnesses that can cause hair loss. These include:

  • Alopecia areata
  • Thyroid disorders
  • Lupus
  • Cancer
  • Iron-deficiency anemia
  • Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)

If you are experiencing hair loss and are unsure of the cause, it is important to consult with a medical professional to determine the underlying issue.

How Do These Illnesses Cause Hair Loss?

The way in which illnesses cause hair loss can vary depending on the condition. For example:

  • Alopecia areata is an autoimmune disorder that causes hair loss when the body attacks hair follicles. This can result in bald patches on the scalp, as well as hair loss on other parts of the body.
  • Thyroid disorders can cause hair loss by disrupting the production of hormones that regulate hair growth. This can result in thinning hair or baldness.
  • Lupus is an autoimmune disorder that can cause hair loss as a result of inflammation in the body. This inflammation can damage hair follicles and cause hair to fall out.
  • Cancer treatments such as chemotherapy and radiation can cause hair loss due to damage to hair follicles. This can result in temporary or permanent hair loss.
  • Iron-deficiency anemia can cause hair loss because iron is necessary for healthy hair growth. Without sufficient iron, hair can become thin and brittle.
  • PCOS can cause hair loss as a result of hormonal imbalances in the body. These imbalances can lead to excess levels of androgens, which can cause hair loss on the scalp.

Diagnosis and Treatment of Illnesses That Cause Hair Loss

If you are experiencing hair loss, it is important to consult with a medical professional to determine the underlying condition. Your doctor may perform blood tests, scalp biopsies, or other diagnostic tests to determine the cause of your hair loss.

Treatment options for hair loss will depend on the underlying condition. For example:

  • Alopecia areata may be treated with corticosteroid injections, topical medications, or oral medications.
  • Thyroid disorders may be treated with medication to regulate hormone levels.
  • Lupus may be treated with medications to reduce inflammation in the body.
  • Cancer-related hair loss may be managed with scalp cooling or by wearing a wig or other hairpiece.
  • Iron-deficiency anemia may be treated with iron supplements or changes to the diet.
  • PCOS may be treated with medications to regulate hormone levels.

Tips for Managing Hair Loss due to Illness

If you are experiencing hair loss due to an underlying illness, there are steps you can take to manage this condition:

  • Be gentle with your hair. Avoid harsh chemicals, heat styling, and tight hairstyles that can further damage hair follicles.
  • Eat a healthy diet. Nutrients such as iron, protein, and biotin are essential for healthy hair growth.
  • Consider a wig or other hairpiece if you are uncomfortable with your hair loss.
  • Practice stress management techniques such as yoga or meditation, as stress can exacerbate hair loss.
  • Consult with a dermatologist or hair specialist for additional treatment options.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can hair loss due to illness be reversed?

The reversibility of hair loss due to illness will depend on the underlying condition. In some cases, hair loss may be temporary and will resolve once the underlying condition is treated. In other cases, hair loss may be permanent.

Can hair loss due to illness be prevented?

Prevention of hair loss due to illness will depend on the underlying condition. However, taking steps to maintain a healthy diet and manage stress can help promote healthy hair growth.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

Regardless of whether you are experiencing hair loss, it is important to take care of your hair to promote healthy growth. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Avoid heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in nutrients such as protein, biotin, and iron.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to prevent damage to hair.

By following these tips, you can promote healthy hair growth and maintain healthy hair, even if you are experiencing hair loss due to an underlying condition.
