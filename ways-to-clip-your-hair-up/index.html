Ways To Clip Your Hair Up A Guide To Easy And Stylish Hairdos

Written by William Nov 06, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle? Do you want to try something new and trendy? Clipping your hair up is a great way to change up your look while keeping your hair out of your face. Whether you have short hair or long locks, there are plenty of ways to clip your hair up that are both practical and stylish.

Table of Contents

  • How to Clip Your Hair Up
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Styling
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Clip Your Hair Up

Before we dive into the different ways to clip your hair up, let's talk about the tools you'll need. You'll want to have some hair clips, bobby pins, and a hair tie on hand. These items will help you create a variety of styles that can be dressed up or down.

1. The Half-Up, Half-Down Style

This is a classic style that looks great on all hair types. Start by sectioning off the top half of your hair and securing it with a hair tie. You can leave the bottom half of your hair down or create loose waves with a curling iron or straightener.

2. The Messy Bun

The messy bun is a go-to style for lazy days or when you're short on time. Start by pulling your hair back into a ponytail and securing it with a hair tie. Twist your hair around the hair tie and secure it in place with bobby pins.

3. The French Twist

This elegant style is perfect for a night out or a special occasion. Start by pulling your hair back into a low ponytail, leaving some hair out at the front. Twist the ponytail upwards and tuck it under itself at the nape of your neck. Secure the twist in place with bobby pins and tease the hair at the front for added volume.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have an idea of the different styles you can create, let's go through a step-by-step guide on how to clip your hair up.

1. Start by brushing your hair to remove any knots or tangles.

Use a wide-toothed comb or a detangling brush to gently work out any knots or tangles in your hair. This will make it easier to create your desired style.

2. Decide on the style you want to create and gather the necessary tools.

Take a look at the different styles outlined above and decide which one you want to try. Gather the necessary tools, including hair clips, bobby pins, and a hair tie.

3. Section off your hair if necessary.

For some styles, you may need to section off your hair. Use a comb to create a part or section off the hair with your fingers.

4. Secure your hair with a hair tie.

If your style requires a hair tie, secure your hair in place with one. You can choose to create a low ponytail or a higher one, depending on the style you want to create.

5. Use bobby pins to secure your hair in place.

For styles like the messy bun or French twist, use bobby pins to secure your hair in place. Twist your hair around the hair tie or create a bun, then secure the style in place with bobby pins.

6. Finish with hairspray or texturizing spray.

To keep your style in place, finish with a spritz of hairspray or texturizing spray. This will help hold your hair in place while adding some extra volume.

Tips for Styling

Here are some tips to keep in mind when styling your hair:

  • Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you.
  • Invest in high-quality hair accessories that will hold your hair in place without slipping or pulling.
  • Don't be afraid to add some texture to your hair before styling. This will help your hair hold its shape and add some volume.
  • Be gentle when taking your hair down to avoid pulling or damaging your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I clip my hair up if it's short?

Absolutely! There are plenty of styles you can create with short hair, including the half-up, half-down style and the messy bun.

Do I need to use hair products to clip my hair up?

No, you don't need to use any hair products to clip your hair up. However, using hairspray or texturizing spray can help your style stay in place for longer.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to clip your hair up, it's time to try out some new styles! Whether you're heading to the office or out for a night on the town, these easy and stylish hairdos are sure to turn heads.
